# Zambezi River

## summary

The *Zambezi* is southern Africa's most important river, flowing approximately 1,650 miles from northwestern Zambia across eight nations to the Indian Ocean. It is renowned for the spectacular Victoria Falls and serves as the lifeline for agriculture, power generation, and transportation across the region.

## you gotta know

- Creates *Victoria Falls*, one of the world's largest and most impressive waterfalls, forming the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe.
- Flows eastward across southern Africa through Zambia, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, and Botswana before emptying into the Indian Ocean.
- The Kariba Dam, completed in 1959, created Lake Kariba—the world's largest hydroelectric reservoir by volume—and provides power to multiple nations.
- The Zambezi Basin covers roughly 540,000 square kilometers and is home to diverse ecosystems ranging from woodland to floodplain savanna.
- Forms the boundary between Zambia and Zimbabwe for much of its middle course, a vital water source for both nations.
- Historically served as a barrier to European exploration and colonial expansion in southern Africa.
- Subject to dramatic seasonal variation; the flood season (November to April) makes navigation hazardous in many sections.
