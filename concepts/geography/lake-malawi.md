# Lake Malawi

## summary

*Lake Malawi* (also called *Lake Nyasa*) is an East African lake spanning approximately 11,400 square miles along the borders of Tanzania, Mozambique, and Malawi. The third-largest lake in Africa by surface area, it is renowned for its extraordinary diversity of endemic fish species and plays a vital role in the region's food security.

## you gotta know

- Stretches roughly 360 miles from north to south, with an average width of 40 miles, making it one of Africa's most elongated lakes.
- Contains more fish species (over 1,000) than any other lake in the world, most found nowhere else, supporting major artisanal fisheries.
- Located in the East African Rift Valley, sharing the same tectonic origin as Lakes Tanganyika and Victoria.
- Known locally as Lake Nyasa in Tanzania and Mozambique, though Malawi—the nation most associated with it—has adopted "Lake Malawi" as the standard English name.
- The lake's waters are among the clearest of Africa's great lakes, allowing sunlight to penetrate to remarkable depths.
- Supports over 4 million people who depend on its fisheries for protein and income; overfishing is a growing concern.
- The shoreline includes significant conservation areas and is increasingly a focus of ecotourism.
