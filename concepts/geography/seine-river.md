# Seine River

## summary

The *Seine* is France's second-longest river, flowing 485 miles from Burgundy through Paris and Normandy to the English Channel. Paris's rise as a power depended on the Seine as a trade and defensive asset, and the river remains iconic in French culture and art.

## you gotta know

- Flows through the heart of Paris, defining the city's geography; the Seine islands (Île de la Cité, Île Saint-Louis) were the original settlement cores.
- Medieval Paris depended on Seine commerce; barges carried grain, wine, timber, and stone to and from the city.
- The river's bends and islands made Paris defensible; control of Seine crossing points was critical to medieval warfare.
- Extensively depicted in Impressionist art, especially by Monet, who painted the same scenes at different times of day and seasons.
- Flows northwestward through Normandy and empties at Le Havre, a major Atlantic port connecting French inland waterways to global maritime trade.
- Paris's riverside districts (Left Bank, Right Bank) developed as distinct cultural and intellectual zones, particularly the Latin Quarter.
