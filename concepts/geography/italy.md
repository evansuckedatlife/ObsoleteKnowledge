# Italy

## summary

Italy is a Mediterranean country in south-central Europe, extending into the Tyrrhenian Sea as a distinctive peninsula shaped like a boot, with the Alps forming its northern boundary. Home to the Roman Empire, the epicenter of the Renaissance, and a major center of Western art, literature, and philosophy for over 2,500 years, Italy's cultural influence on the world is immeasurable. Modern Italy, unified only in 1861, is a founding member of the European Union and NATO.

## you gotta know

- Italy's geography is dominated by the *Apennine Mountains* running the length of the peninsula and the *Alps* to the north; it includes numerous islands, the largest being *Sicily* and *Sardinia*.
- The *Roman Empire*, centered in Italy and radiating from Rome, conquered and ruled the Mediterranean world for nearly 500 years (27 BCE–476 CE), establishing the template for Western law, government, and urban planning.
- The *Italian Renaissance* (14th–17th centuries), beginning in Florence, was the cultural rebirth of classical learning that birthed modern art, literature, science, and philosophy, fundamentally reshaping European civilization.
- *Vatican City*, an independent city-state entirely within Rome, is the center of Roman Catholicism and the seat of the *Pope*, making Italy the spiritual heart of Western Christianity.
- Italy is home to numerous volcanic regions, including *Mount Vesuvius*, which infamously erupted in 79 CE and preserved the Roman cities of *Pompeii* and *Herculaneum*.
- Italian literature includes *Dante Alighieri's* *Divine Comedy* (14th century), often considered the greatest poem in any language, and *Petrarch's* sonnets, which founded an entire poetic tradition.
- The *Italian Unification* under *Cavour* and *Garibaldi* transformed the peninsula from a collection of kingdoms and papal states into a unified nation-state in 1861.
- Italy's *Fascist regime* under *Benito Mussolini* (1922–1943) allied with Nazi Germany in World War II, leading to Italy's occupation and division.
