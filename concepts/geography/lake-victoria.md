# Lake Victoria

## summary

*Lake Victoria* is Africa's largest lake by surface area, spanning approximately 26,600 square miles across the borders of Tanzania, Uganda, and Kenya. It is the source of the White Nile and has been vital to the societies surrounding it for millennia, supporting fisheries, transportation, and agriculture.

## you gotta know

- The world's second-largest freshwater lake by surface area, after Lake Superior in North America, though shallower than most large lakes.
- Bordered by three nations: Tanzania to the south and west, Uganda to the north, and Kenya to the east.
- Fed by numerous rivers and seasonal rainfall; the White Nile originates here and flows northward through Sudan and Egypt.
- Named *Victoria* by British explorer John Hanning Speke in 1858 in honor of Queen Victoria.
- Supports over 30 million people who depend on it for drinking water, fishing, and transportation.
- The introduction of the Nile perch in the 1950s led to the extinction of hundreds of endemic fish species, one of history's worst ecological disasters.
- Divided among three nations by colonial boundaries, making water-sharing agreements critical for regional stability.
