# England

## summary

England is the largest and most populous constituent country of the United Kingdom, occupying the southern portion of the island of Great Britain. Its history spans over 1,500 years from Anglo-Saxon kingdoms through Norman conquest, feudal monarchy, civil war, empire, and industrial dominance. England shaped the English language, common law, parliamentary democracy, and imperial systems that influenced the world.

## you gotta know

- The *Norman Conquest* of 1066, when William the Conqueror invaded from Normandy, permanently altered English culture, language, and governance, introducing feudalism and French Norman nobility.
- The *Magna Carta*, signed in 1215, established the principle that even the king was subject to law; it became foundational to democracy and constitutional governance worldwide.
- *William Shakespeare* (1564–1616), the preeminent English playwright and poet, created works that defined literature and human understanding; his plays remain the most performed in the world.
- The *English Reformation* under *Henry VIII* (r. 1509–1547) broke with Rome, established the Church of England, and reshaped European religious and political history.
- The *British Empire*, built from English naval power and colonial expansion from the 16th century onward, became the largest empire in history, ruling a quarter of the world's population by 1914.
- The *Industrial Revolution* began in 18th-century England with steam power and textile manufacturing, transforming human society and global economics.
- England's *Parliament*, developing from medieval assemblies, became the model for representative democracy and constitutional monarchy worldwide.
