## summary

New York City is the most populous city in the United States, located at the mouth of the Hudson River on the Atlantic coast. It became a major colonial settlement in the 17th century and grew into a global financial, cultural, and artistic hub by the 20th century. The city has shaped American identity, literature, and popular culture more than any other single place.

## you gotta know

- The city was founded by the Dutch in 1624 as *New Amsterdam* and remained a Dutch colony until 1664, when the English seized it and renamed it *New York*.
- Ellis Island, in New York Harbor, was the primary immigration gateway for millions of Europeans between 1892 and 1954, making the city the doorway to America.
- The Statue of Liberty, a gift from France, was dedicated in 1886 and became the symbol of American freedom and hope for immigrants.
- Wall Street, in lower Manhattan, is the global center of stock trading and finance; the New York Stock Exchange was founded there in 1817.
- Manhattan's grid street system, laid out in 1811, revolutionized urban planning and influenced city design worldwide.
- Broadway has been the heart of American theater since the early 20th century, producing musicals and plays that define popular entertainment.
- The city hosted the first modern Olympic Games held in an American city (1920s and 1932), and hosts major cultural institutions like the Metropolitan Museum of Art.
