## summary

The **Indus River** is one of the world's major rivers, flowing approximately 3,180 kilometers (1,976 miles) from the Tibetan Plateau westward through India and Pakistan to the Arabian Sea. It is the lifeline of Pakistan's agriculture and economy, and is shared by India and Pakistan under the 1960 Indus Waters Treaty, one of the world's most successful international water-sharing agreements.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the *Tibetan Plateau* and flows southwest through the Himalayas, then across the *Thar Desert* and the Pakistani plains.
- The *Indus Valley Civilization* (c. 2600–1900 BCE) was one of the world's earliest urban cultures, with cities like *Harappa* and *Mohenjo-daro*.
- The *Indus Delta* in Pakistan is shrinking due to upstream dams blocking sediment flow and rising sea levels from climate change.
- The *Indus Waters Treaty* (1960) between India and Pakistan divides the river's waters; a landmark example of international cooperation over shared resources.
- Major cities along the river include *Lahore*, *Multan*, and *Hyderabad*, centers of Mughal and Islamic culture.
- Water diversion through irrigation systems has reduced the lower delta's flow; the river now rarely reaches the sea with significant volume.
- The river is sacred in *Sikhism* and *Hinduism*; Indus dolphins are endemic freshwater cetaceans found nowhere else.
