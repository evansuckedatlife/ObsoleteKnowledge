# Nile River

## summary

The *Nile* is Africa's longest river, flowing approximately 4,130 miles through eleven countries before emptying into the Mediterranean Sea near Alexandria, Egypt. It has been the lifeblood of Egyptian civilization for millennia, sustaining agriculture through its annual floods and connecting vast territories along its course.

## you gotta know

- Flows northward from the East African highlands through Sudan into Egypt, a reversal of most rivers' expected southerly flow on the continent.
- The annual flooding cycle, governed by monsoon rains in the Ethiopian highlands, deposits nutrient-rich silt that enables agriculture in the otherwise arid desert.
- Forms the Nile Delta in Lower Egypt, a vast fertile fan where the river branches into multiple channels before reaching the Mediterranean.
- Home to the Aswan High Dam (completed 1970), which harnesses hydroelectric power and allows year-round irrigation control.
- Lake Nasser, created by the Aswan Dam, is one of the world's largest artificial reservoirs by surface area.
- Originates in the highlands around Lake Victoria, though the most water comes from the Blue Nile in Ethiopia.
- Passes through Cairo, historically the gateway between Upper and Lower Egypt and still Africa's largest metropolitan area.
