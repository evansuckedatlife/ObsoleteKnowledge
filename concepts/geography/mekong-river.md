# Mekong River

## summary

The **Mekong River** is Southeast Asia's longest and most important river, flowing approximately 4,350 kilometers (2,703 miles) from its source in the Tibetan Plateau through six countries before emptying into the South China Sea via a vast delta in Vietnam. It is the economic and cultural artery of mainland Southeast Asia, supporting over 60 million people through fishing, agriculture, and trade.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Tibetan Plateau and flows southeast through *China*, *Myanmar*, *Laos*, *Cambodia*, *Thailand*, and *Vietnam*.
- The *Mekong Delta* in southern Vietnam is one of the world's most productive agricultural regions, supplying rice, fish, and coconut to global markets.
- *Tonle Sap Lake* in Cambodia is connected to the Mekong and experiences one of the world's largest seasonal water-level fluctuations.
- Home to the *Irrawaddy dolphin* (freshwater cetacean) and *Mekong giant catfish*, critically endangered species endemic to the river.
- The *Khone Falls* (in Laos) are the largest waterfall system in Southeast Asia, creating a natural barrier to navigation.
- Controversial dams (particularly in China and Laos) disrupt fish migration and alter seasonal flooding patterns that farmers depend on.
- The *Mekong River Commission* (est. 1995) manages shared resources among riparian nations.

## connections

- [[southeast-asia]] — the defining geographic feature and economic spine of the region.
- [[vietnam]] — the Mekong Delta in southern Vietnam is the nation's agricultural heartland.
- [[cambodia]] — the river passes through *Phnom Penh* and connects to *Tonle Sap Lake*.
- [[laos]] — flows through the country; the Khone Falls form a natural boundary.
- [[thailand]] — the river defines its border with Laos.
