# Dublin

## summary

Dublin is the capital and largest city of Ireland, situated on the River Liffey's mouth at the Irish Sea, with a population of over 1.2 million in the metropolitan area. A city of Georgian architecture, literary giants, and intellectual ferment, Dublin has been the center of Irish culture, politics, and commerce since medieval times. It is one of Europe's youngest and most vibrant cities, with a heritage stretching back over a thousand years.

## you gotta know

- Dublin was founded by Vikings in the 9th century at the confluence of the River Liffey and the Poddle; its name derives from "Dubh Linn" (black pool).
- *Trinity College Dublin*, founded in 1592, is Ireland's oldest university and remains one of Europe's most prestigious institutions; it houses the illuminated manuscript *Book of Kells*.
- Dublin was the center of the Irish independence movement; *James Joyce*, *Samuel Beckett*, *William Butler Yeats*, and *Oscar Wilde* all emerged from Dublin's literary scene and profoundly influenced world literature.
- The city's Georgian architecture, particularly around *Merrion Square* and the *Liffey Quays*, makes it one of the finest 18th-century urban landscapes in Europe.
- The *Irish Famine* (1845–1852) devastated Ireland, causing mass starvation and emigration; Dublin, as the capital, was a focal point of the crisis and its aftermath.
- Dublin was the seat of Irish Home Rule agitation in the late 19th century and the epicenter of the *Easter Rising* (1916), which sparked the Irish War of Independence and eventually Ireland's independence.
- The *Guinness Storehouse* and the city's famous pub culture reflect Dublin's role as a social and cultural hub; *Guinness* beer was first brewed there in 1759.
- *Joyce's* modernist novel *Ulysses*, set entirely in Dublin on June 16, 1904, is considered one of the greatest novels ever written, and the date is now commemorated as "Bloomsday."
