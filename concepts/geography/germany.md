# Germany

## summary

Germany is a major European nation at the crossroads of Central and Western Europe, whose history spans from tribal kingdoms through the fragmented Holy Roman Empire to modern statehood. Its culture has profoundly shaped Western philosophy, music, literature, and science. Politically, Germany's 20th-century history—the rise of Prussian militarism, the devastation of the World Wars, and Nazi tyranny—repeatedly altered the continent and world.

## you gotta know

- Germany was fragmented into hundreds of semi-independent states within the *Holy Roman Empire* (843–1806) until *Otto von Bismarck* unified Prussia and surrounding territories into a single nation-state between 1864 and 1871.
- The *Protestant Reformation*, initiated by *Martin Luther* in 1517 when he challenged papal authority, began in Germany and reshaped European Christianity, governance, and culture.
- Germany's culture produced philosophical giants (*Goethe*, *Schiller*, *Kant*, *Hegel*) and musical masters (*Bach*, *Mozart*, *Beethoven*) who defined Enlightenment and Romantic thought and sound.
- *Prussian militarism* under Bismarck and his successors made Germany an aggressive power; German imperial ambitions contributed to *World War I* (1914–1918), which devastated Europe.
- The Treaty of Versailles imposed harsh reparations and territorial losses on Germany after World War I, creating economic and political instability that fueled the rise of *Adolf Hitler* and the Nazi regime.
- *World War II* (1939–1945), initiated by Nazi Germany, resulted in the Holocaust and the deaths of over 70 million people; Germany's defeat divided the nation into East and West until 1990.
- Germany's cultural contributions to music, philosophy, and literature remain unmatched; it was also a center of scientific innovation (Einstein, Max Planck, Marie Curie worked there).
