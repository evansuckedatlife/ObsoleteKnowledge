# Denali

## summary

*Denali* (formerly Mount McKinley) is North America's highest peak at 20,310 feet, located in the Alaska Range of interior Alaska. Despite its lower altitude than many global mountains, Denali is considered exceptionally challenging due to its remote location, extreme cold, and the dramatic elevation gain from sea level, attracting elite mountaineers and aspiring Everest climbers.

## you gotta know

- Height of 20,310 feet (6,190 meters); highest peak in North America.
- Formerly called *Mount McKinley* after the 25th U.S. President; officially renamed Denali (Athabascan for "the great one") in 2015.
- Located in the Alaska Range approximately 130 miles north of Anchorage; remote and accessible primarily by bush plane or foot.
- Climbing season is brief (May-July); winter temperatures plummet to -40°F with wind chills far lower; climbers face crevasse fields, avalanche danger, and acute cold injury.
- First ascent in 1913 by Hudson Stuck and his party, though Indigenous peoples and earlier explorers had traversed the region.
- Considered a crucial training ground for Himalayan mountaineering; many Everest climbers summit Denali first to test high-altitude acclimatization and logistics.
- The "Rescue Bowl" phenomenon: weather can trap climbers for weeks; the mountain demands patience and respect.
