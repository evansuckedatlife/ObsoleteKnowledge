# Okavango River

## summary

The *Okavango* is a southern African river approximately 1,000 miles long that flows from Angola through Botswana, terminating not at the ocean but in an inland delta—one of the world's largest desert wetlands. This unique feature makes it an ecological wonder and a crucial water source in an otherwise arid region.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Angolan highlands and flows southeastward across the Kalahari Desert into Botswana.
- Rather than reaching the ocean, the Okavango spreads into the *Okavango Delta*, a vast inland wetland ecosystem covering roughly 6,000 square miles.
- The delta is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a major center of wildlife and tourism in southern Africa, supporting elephants, lions, and countless bird species.
- During flood season (May-July), seasonal flooding from Angola spreads water across the delta, sustaining diverse ecosystems in an otherwise drought-prone region.
- The river's unique hydrology—flooding in the Southern Hemisphere winter—is opposite to most African rivers and driven by Angola's rainy season.
- The delta supports subsistence fishing and hunting, as well as high-value tourism lodges that generate income for Botswana.
- Water extraction and damming upstream pose significant threats to the delta's future water supply and ecological balance.
