# Mount Elbrus

## summary

*Mount Elbrus* is the highest peak in the Caucasus Range at 18,510 feet, straddling the Russia-Georgia border in the southern part of the Caucasus Mountains. A dormant stratovolcano with twin peaks, Elbrus is the highest mountain in Europe (by some geographic definitions) and a popular climbing destination, featuring modern lift infrastructure and established mountaineering routes.

## you gotta know

- Height of 18,510 feet (5,642 meters); the highest point in the Caucasus Range.
- Double-peaked stratovolcano; the western summit (18,510 feet) is marginally higher than the eastern peak (18,356 feet).
- The highest mountain in Europe by most definitions; dispute arises over whether Europe extends to the Caucasus or ends at the Ural Mountains.
- A cable car system ascends to roughly 11,500 feet, making Elbrus far more accessible than comparable peaks; climbs from base camps take 2–3 days.
- First ascent in 1829 by a Russian military expedition led by General Emmanuel von Toll; earlier ascents by local mountaineers likely occurred.
- Major climbing routes (South Slope, North Slope) are non-technical but exposed to altitude sickness, extreme cold, and sudden weather changes.
- Elbrus attracts thousands of climbers annually; it is part of the "Seven Summits" achievement for those seeking the highest peak on each continent.
