## summary

*Mount Fuji* is Japan's highest peak at 12,388 feet and an iconic symbol of Japanese culture and landscape. A dormant stratovolcano, it has inspired centuries of art, poetry, and pilgrimage; its perfect conical form and visibility from distant Tokyo make it perhaps the world's most aesthetically recognizable mountain.

## you gotta know

- Height of 12,388 feet (3,776 meters); located on the island of Honshu, roughly 60 miles west of Tokyo.
- A dormant stratovolcano; last erupted in 1707–1708; the distinctive cone shape results from repeated eruptions building a symmetrical profile.
- Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site (2013); sacred in Shinto tradition as the home of a divine spirit (*kami*).
- Climbing season is brief: July–September, when snow melts and weather is most stable; thousands summit each day during peak season.
- First ascent records are uncertain (mythology and history blend), but climbing became a organized pilgrimage practice by the 11th century.
- Featured prominently in Japanese art, most famously in Katsushika Hokusai's 18th-century print series "Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji."
- The ascent takes one day for most climbers; altitude acclimatization is minimal due to its modest height, making it accessible to non-climbers.
