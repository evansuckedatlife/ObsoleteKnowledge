# Mississippi River

## summary

The *Mississippi River* is the longest river in North America and the second-longest on the continent after the Missouri, flowing roughly 2,350 miles southward from Minnesota to the Gulf of Mexico. It is the continental dividing line between the eastern and western United States and has served as a crucial artery for settlement, commerce, and literature throughout American history.

## you gotta know

- Originates in Minnesota at *Lake Itasca*; the source was a contested question among early explorers and geographers.
- Forms a major portion of the border between the eastern and western halves of the United States, separating states like Iowa and Illinois from Missouri and Arkansas.
- Fed by major tributaries including the *Missouri River* and *Ohio River*, which add enormous volume and create a powerful downstream flow.
- The *Mississippi Delta* near its mouth has shaped Louisiana's geography and ecology, creating wetlands and a unique cultural landscape shaped by sediment deposition.
- Named by French explorers from the Anishinaabe (*Ojibwe*) word *Misi-ziibi*, meaning "Great River."
- Served as the setting for *Mark Twain's* *The Adventures of Tom Sawyer* and *The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn*, cementing its place in American literature.
- Carries enormous sediment loads; the delta has been sinking and losing land mass due to levee construction reducing silt deposition, a major environmental crisis.
