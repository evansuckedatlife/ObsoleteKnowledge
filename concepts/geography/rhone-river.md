# Rhône River

## summary

The *Rhône* is a major western European river flowing 505 miles from the Swiss Alps through Lake Geneva and eastern France into the Mediterranean Sea. It is the fastest and most powerful river in France, draining the Alps and creating the fertile Rhône delta.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Swiss Alps and flows northwest through Lake Geneva (Lac Léman), the largest freshwater lake in western Europe.
- Exits Lake Geneva at Geneva and flows south through the Rhône valley, accelerating as it descends; its current is notably fast and dangerous.
- The Rhône delta in southern France (the Camargue) is a vast wetland and salt-marsh region known for wild horses, flamingos, and unique ecosystems.
- Empties into the Mediterranean near Marseille; in Roman times, the river was a crucial transport artery for north-south Mediterranean trade.
- Historically difficult to navigate due to its rapids and strong current; medieval and Renaissance river management attempted locks and channels.
- The Rhône valley is a major wine-growing region; towns like Avignon and Arles are built along its banks.

## connections

- [[switzerland]] — the river originates in the Swiss Alps and drains alpine region.
- [[france]] — the Rhône valley is central to southern French geography and culture.
- [[mediterranean-sea]] — the river is the Alps' primary drainage to the Mediterranean.
- [[avignon]] — a major city on the Rhône; seat of the papacy in the 14th century.
- [[camargue]] — the river's wetland delta is a unique European ecosystem.
