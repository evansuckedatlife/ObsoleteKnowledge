# Middle East

## summary

The *Middle East* is a transcontinental region spanning Western Asia, Northeast Africa, and Southeast Europe, encompassing territories from Egypt and Turkey to Iran and Arabia. A crossroads of continents with ancient Mesopotamian, Egyptian, Persian, and Greek civilizations, it is the birthplace of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. The modern Middle East is defined by the legacy of the *Ottoman Empire*, European colonialism, the establishment of Israel, and oil wealth, making it strategically and geopolitically crucial to global politics.

## you gotta know

- The *Middle East* (also called the *Levant* in antiquity) was the cradle of Western civilization: *Mesopotamia* (modern Iraq) saw the rise of the Sumer, Babylon, and Assyrian empires; Egypt dominated the *Nile Valley*; Persia unified vast territories under imperial rule.
- The region is home to the world's three *Abrahamic religions*—*Judaism*, *Christianity*, and *Islam*—all originating in the Middle East and shaping its culture, politics, and conflicts profoundly.
- The *Ottoman Empire* ruled much of the Middle East for nearly 400 years (1453–1923), establishing Islamic governance, Ottoman administrative systems, and Turkish cultural dominance that shaped the region's identity.
- European *colonialism* and *imperialism* parceled the Middle East into European spheres after World War I: Britain controlled Egypt, Transjordan, Iraq, and Palestine; France controlled Syria and Lebanon; Italy held Libya.
- The establishment of *Israel* in 1948 initiated the *Arab–Israeli Conflict*, a protracted dispute over territory, national self-determination, and refugee rights that remains the region's most intractable geopolitical crisis.
- The *Suez Canal*, connecting the Mediterranean to the Red Sea, is one of the world's most vital maritime chokepoints; control over the canal has been central to regional power and global trade.
- *Oil wealth*, particularly in *Saudi Arabia*, *Iraq*, *Iran*, *United Arab Emirates*, and *Kuwait*, has made the Middle East crucial to global energy supply and geopolitics, driving intervention by major powers.
- Major cities—*Cairo* (Egypt), *Istanbul* (Turkey), *Tehran* (Iran), *Jerusalem*, *Baghdad* (Iraq), *Damascus* (Syria), and *Beirut* (Lebanon)—are ancient cultural and economic centers.
- The *Islamic Golden Age* (8th–14th centuries) saw unprecedented advances in mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy centered in Middle Eastern cities like *Baghdad* and *Damascus*.
- Linguistic and cultural diversity: the region speaks Arabic, Turkish, Persian, Hebrew, Kurdish, and numerous other languages; it is home to Arabs, Turks, Persians, Jews, Kurds, and other ethnic groups with competing nationalisms.
