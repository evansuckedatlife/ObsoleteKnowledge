# Taklamakan Desert

## summary

The *Taklamakan* is a large hot desert in northwestern China, covering roughly 130,000 square miles in the Xinjiang region between the Tian Shan and Kunlun mountain ranges. Its name, derived from Uyghur, traditionally meant "go in and you will not come out," reflecting its extreme aridity, fierce winds, and historical danger to travelers; it is a critical section of the ancient Silk Road.

## you gotta know

- One of the world's largest sand deserts; the second-largest in the world after the Sahara, characterized by enormous dunes and complex sand patterns.
- Temperatures fluctuate wildly; summers exceed 45°C (113°F) while winter nights can fall well below freezing; annual rainfall is less than 1 inch in most areas.
- The *Taklamakan* is surrounded by oasis cities—*Kashgar*, *Samarkand*, *Bukhara*—which served as vital nodes on the Silk Road's transcontinental trade network.
- Traversed by merchants, pilgrims, and armies for over two millennia; the Silk Road routes skirted its edges via oases, avoiding the interior.
- Contains deposits of oil and natural gas; modern drilling and exploration have increased economic interest and environmental impact.
- Uyghur and Tajik peoples have inhabited oasis settlements around its margins; the desert itself was largely unmapped and unexplored until the 20th century.
- Hazardous winds called *black winds* can obliterate visibility and landmarks, making navigation without modern technology extremely dangerous.
