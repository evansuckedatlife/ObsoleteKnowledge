# Tigris River

## summary

The **Tigris River** is one of the two great rivers of ancient Mesopotamia, flowing approximately 1,900 kilometers (1,180 miles) from its source in southeastern Turkey through Iraq to the Persian Gulf. Along with the Euphrates, it cradled one of humanity's earliest civilizations and continues to be critical to Iraq's agriculture and water supply, though shared between Turkey, Syria, Iraq, and Iran.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Taurus Mountains of southeastern Turkey and flows southeast through Iraq, ultimately merging with the Euphrates near the Persian Gulf.
- Ancient *Assyria* flourished on the Tigris's banks; cities like *Ashur*, *Nineveh*, and *Babylon* rose to dominance through control of the river.
- The *Tigris-Euphrates system* supported the rise of writing, law codes, agriculture, and urban civilization in ancient Mesopotamia.
- The rivers merge near *Basra* in southern Iraq; their combined delta extends into the Persian Gulf and is ecologically significant.
- Water diversion for dams in Turkey and Syria has reduced the Tigris's flow to Iraq by up to 80% in some seasons.
- The river is shared by four nations; tensions over dam construction and water allocation remain a source of political friction.
- Archaeological sites like *Nineveh* and *Assur* line the riverbanks, preserving layers of human civilization.
