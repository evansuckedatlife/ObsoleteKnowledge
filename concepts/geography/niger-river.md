# Niger River

## summary

The *Niger* is West Africa's principal river, flowing approximately 2,600 miles in a distinctive boomerang arc through nine countries before emptying into the Atlantic Ocean via the Niger Delta. It has been central to West African trade, settlement, and civilization for thousands of years.

## you gotta know

- Rises in Guinea near the Sierra Leone border, flows northeastward into Mali, then curves dramatically southeastward through Niger and Nigeria.
- The Niger Delta, where the river enters the Atlantic, is one of the world's largest river deltas and a major center of oil production.
- The middle Niger valley, particularly around Timbuktu in Mali, was a center of medieval West African empires and Islamic learning.
- Forms a vast inland delta (the Niger Inland Delta) in Mali during flood season, creating extensive wetlands and grasslands.
- Historically connected trans-Saharan trade routes, serving as a major transport artery for goods, salt, and gold.
- The Kainji Dam, built on the Niger in Nigeria, provides hydroelectric power and water supply for much of the region.
- Subject to significant seasonal flooding that supports both agriculture and fisheries across West Africa.
