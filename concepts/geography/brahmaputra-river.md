## summary

The **Brahmaputra River** is a major river of South Asia flowing approximately 2,896 kilometers (1,800 miles) from its source in the Tibetan Plateau through the Indian state of Assam and into Bangladesh, where it merges with the Ganges River. Named after the Hindu god *Brahma*, it is the second-largest river in India by discharge and is known for devastating seasonal floods that reshape its course and landscape.

## you gotta know

- Originates on the Tibetan Plateau as the *Tsangpo*, flows eastward through Tibet, then turns sharply south and west into India.
- Flows through *Assam*, a state defined by the river—the valley is among India's most fertile and significant agricultural regions.
- Notorious for seasonal flooding that submerges vast areas; islands like *Majuli* have been eroding and shrinking for decades.
- The river's **braided channels**—multiple shifting streams across a wide floodplain—make navigation unpredictable and engineering difficult.
- Merges with the Ganges in Bangladesh to form the *Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta*, the world's largest river delta.
- The *Assam Valley* along its course is home to diverse wildlife: Indian rhinoceros, wild elephants, and Bengal tigers.
- Dams on the upper reaches (in Bhutan and India) are controversial, affecting downstream flow and seasonal flood patterns.
