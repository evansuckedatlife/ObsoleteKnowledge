# France

## summary

France is a major European nation in Western Europe whose cultural, political, and artistic influence on the world has been immense since the medieval period. From the seat of absolute monarchy at Versailles to the radical ideals of the Revolution, from the military conquests of Napoleon to its role in world wars and the modern European Union, France has repeatedly reshaped European civilization. It remains a center of art, philosophy, cuisine, and fashion.

## you gotta know

- Under *Louis XIV* (the Sun King, r. 1643–1715), France became the dominant European power and the model of absolute monarchy; Versailles symbolized royal grandeur.
- The *French Revolution* (1789–1799) overthrew the monarchy, introduced revolutionary ideals of liberty, equality, and fraternity, and triggered decades of European conflict.
- *Napoleon Bonaparte* rose from the Revolution's chaos to conquer much of Europe and, though defeated, permanently altered the political map and legal systems of Europe.
- France's classical culture—literature, philosophy, and art—shaped the Enlightenment; figures like Voltaire, Rousseau, and Descartes defined European thought.
- The *Louvre* in Paris, originally a royal palace, became the world's greatest art museum, housing masterworks like the *Mona Lisa* and *Venus de Milo*.
- French Romanticism and later *Impressionism* (Monet, Renoir, Degas) revolutionized visual art and established Paris as the artistic capital of the world in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
- France was a central battleground and power in both World Wars; the Vichy regime's collaboration with Nazi Germany and the subsequent liberation shaped modern French identity.
