# Atacama Desert

## summary

The *Atacama* is the driest non-polar desert on Earth, stretching roughly 600 miles along the western edge of the Andes in Chile, Peru, and Bolivia. Some meteorological stations in its core have recorded no measurable rainfall in recorded history, making it an extreme environment whose aridity supports virtually no vegetation outside of rare oases.

## you gotta know

- Located on South America's Pacific coast, wedged between the Andes Mountains to the east and the cold Pacific to the west.
- Some portions have not experienced rainfall for over 400 years; parts of the *Atacama Pampa* are so dry that human mummies naturally preserve.
- The *Humboldt Current*, a cold ocean current, prevents moisture-laden clouds from crossing inland, creating a permanent rain shadow.
- Rich in mineral deposits, including copper, nitrates (historically extracted for explosives and fertilizer), and lithium, making it economically vital to Chile.
- Contains the *Valle de la Luna* (Valley of the Moon), a striking landscape of colorful rock formations shaped by wind erosion.
- Indigenous Atacameño peoples adapted to the desert using underground water sources and cultivating hardy crops in scattered oases.
- The extreme dryness and thin atmosphere make it ideal for astronomical observation; major observatories are located here.
