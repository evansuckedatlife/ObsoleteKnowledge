# Atlantic Ocean

## summary

The Atlantic Ocean is the second-largest ocean on Earth, covering approximately 20% of its surface and separating the continents of Europe and Africa from North and South America. Formed during the breakup of the supercontinent Pangaea, it has been the primary highway for European exploration, colonization, and trade since the 15th century. The Atlantic has shaped human history more than any other body of water, serving as both barrier and bridge.

## you gotta know

- The Atlantic is bounded by the coasts of Europe and Africa to the east and the Americas to the west, with the Arctic Ocean to the north and the Southern Ocean to the south.
- *Christopher Columbus's* 1492 voyage across the Atlantic initiated sustained European contact with the Americas, fundamentally reshaping world history.
- The Atlantic's discovery of fossil fuels in continental shelves made it economically critical; the *North Sea* oil fields remain major global energy sources.
- The *Mid-Atlantic Ridge*, an underwater mountain range running the length of the ocean, marks the boundary between the North American and Eurasian plates and the South American and African plates.
- The Atlantic was the primary route for the *transatlantic slave trade*, which forcibly transported over 12 million Africans to the Americas between the 16th and 19th centuries.
- The Battle of the *Atlantic* during World War II was the longest battle of the war, in which German U-boats attacked Allied convoys supplying Britain.
- The *Titanic* sank in the Atlantic in 1912 after striking an iceberg, becoming one of the most famous maritime disasters.
- The Atlantic has an average depth of about 3,300 meters (11,000 feet) and contains major island chains including the *Caribbean*, *Azores*, and *Canary Islands*.

## connections

- [[columbus]] — his 1492 transatlantic voyage inaugurated the Age of Discovery and European colonization of the Americas.
- [[british-empire]] — Britain's naval dominance of the Atlantic was central to its imperial power from the 17th through early 20th centuries.
- [[slave-trade]] — the transatlantic slave trade was built on Atlantic shipping routes.
- [[world-war-ii]] — the Battle of the Atlantic was a critical naval campaign fought across the ocean.
- [[titanic]] — the ship's sinking in the Atlantic became a defining cultural tragedy of the early 20th century.
- [[europe]] — the Atlantic's eastern shores are the cradle of European civilization and colonial expansion.
- [[africa]] — the Atlantic's western African coast was the origin point for the slave trade and European colonial ventures.
- [[panama-canal]] — revolutionized Atlantic navigation by providing a shortcut to the Pacific without rounding Cape Horn.
