# Tagus River

## summary

The *Tagus* is the Iberian Peninsula's longest river, flowing 645 miles from central Spain through Portugal to the Atlantic Ocean. It forms a natural boundary between central Spain and Estremadura, and its estuary at Lisbon is one of Europe's finest harbors, making it central to Portuguese maritime power.

## you gotta know

- The longest river of the Iberian Peninsula; originates in the mountains of central Spain and flows generally westward.
- Forms a dramatic gorge in the Montes de Toledo region of Spain; historically difficult to cross, making it a natural defensive line.
- The Tagus estuary at Lisbon provides a deep natural harbor that enabled Portugal's Age of Discovery maritime expansion.
- Serves as part of the Spain-Portugal border in some reaches; water-sharing rights between the two countries remain politically sensitive.
- Tidal influence extends far inland, and Lisbon's position at the estuary's mouth made it a crucial port for Phoenician, Roman, and medieval traders.
- The Salazar Dam (Barragem do Salazar) is one of Europe's largest, creating a massive reservoir for hydropower and irrigation.
