# Rio Grande

## summary

The *Rio Grande* flows approximately 1,900 miles from the San Juan Mountains of Colorado southward through New Mexico and along the border between Texas and Mexico before emptying into the Gulf of Mexico. It is a defining river of the American Southwest and Mexico, serving as the international boundary between the two countries and a crucial water source in an arid region.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the San Juan Mountains of southwestern Colorado, at elevations exceeding 12,000 feet, flowing southward into New Mexico and then south-southeastward.
- Named by Spanish explorers from the Spanish *Rio Grande*, meaning "big river" or "great river."
- Forms the international boundary between the United States and Mexico for approximately 1,250 miles, from El Paso, Texas, to the Gulf of Mexico.
- Flows through a series of dramatic canyons in New Mexico, including the *Big Bend* region in Texas, a popular wilderness destination.
- Heavily dammed and diverted for irrigation throughout its course; by the time it reaches Mexico, much of its water is consumed, leading to disputes over water allocation.
- Named in numerous treaties between the United States and Mexico as the official border, particularly significant after the Mexican-American War (1846–1848).
- Supports a unique riparian ecosystem that has been heavily impacted by water diversion and invasive species; the river's mouth creates a delta in Mexico.
