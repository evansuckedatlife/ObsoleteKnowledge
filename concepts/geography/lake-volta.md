# Lake Volta

## summary

*Lake Volta* is the world's largest artificial reservoir by surface area, created by the Akosombo Dam on the Volta River in Ghana. Covering approximately 3,283 square miles, it has been central to Ghana's economic development, providing hydroelectric power, transportation, and fisheries since its completion in 1965.

## you gotta know

- Created by damming the White Volta River, a major West African waterway, in southeastern Ghana.
- The world's largest artificial lake by surface area, though Lake Baikal in Russia and some other natural lakes are vastly larger by volume.
- Supplies hydroelectric power for Ghana and neighboring nations, historically providing over 90 percent of Ghana's electricity generation.
- Created one of West Africa's most productive freshwater fisheries, supporting tens of thousands of fishermen.
- The dam's construction required the displacement of over 80,000 people from towns and villages flooded by the rising reservoir.
- The lake's creation had significant environmental effects, including altered downstream flow regimes that affected the Volta Delta.
- Tourism and recreation on Lake Volta have become increasingly important to Ghana's economy.
