# Limpopo River

## summary

The *Limpopo* is a major southern African river flowing approximately 1,100 miles from the highlands of South Africa and Botswana to the Indian Ocean in Mozambique. It forms the border between South Africa and Botswana, and between South Africa and Zimbabwe, making it a crucial political and environmental boundary in the region.

## you gotta know

- Rises in the highlands northwest of Johannesburg, South Africa, and flows northeastward across the subcontinent.
- Forms the international boundary between South Africa and Botswana for much of its course, and historically served as the frontier between British colonial territories.
- Flows through the Limpopo National Park, a major conservation area spanning Mozambique, Zimbabwe, and South Africa.
- Enters the Indian Ocean in southern Mozambique, though its mouth and estuary are poorly developed.
- Subject to severe droughts, with flow becoming intermittent in dry years—a major challenge for water security in the region.
- Historically crossed by traders and explorers traveling between southern and central Africa.
- The river's name comes from the Tsonga word for "strong" or "rapid," referencing its forceful flow during rainy seasons.
