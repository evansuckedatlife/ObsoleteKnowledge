# Columbia River

## summary

The *Columbia River* is the longest river in the Pacific Northwest, flowing approximately 1,240 miles from British Columbia through Washington and Oregon to the Pacific Ocean. It is a defining geographical feature of the Pacific Northwest and one of North America's most powerful rivers, generating enormous hydroelectric power and historically supporting a rich salmon fishery.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Canadian Rockies near Columbia Lake in British Columbia, flowing south and west into the United States.
- Forms the natural boundary between Washington and Oregon for much of its course.
- Dammed extensively by the *Grand Coulee Dam* and other major hydroelectric projects, creating a cascade of reservoirs that transformed the region's landscape and ecology.
- Named by American fur traders after the *Columbia*, a merchant ship that explored its mouth in the late 18th century.
- Once supported one of the world's richest salmon fisheries; dam construction severely depleted salmon populations by blocking migration routes.
- Used by Lewis and Clark as a crucial part of their expedition route to reach the Pacific Ocean in 1805–1806.
- Discharges more water into the Pacific Ocean than any other North American river.
