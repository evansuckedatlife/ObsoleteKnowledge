# K2

## summary

*K2* is the world's second-highest mountain at 28,251 feet, located in the Karakoram Range on the Pakistan-China border. It is considered more technically difficult and dangerous than Everest; climbers refer to it as the "Savage Mountain" due to its punishing conditions and high fatality rate—roughly one death for every four summits.

## you gotta know

- Elevation of 28,251 feet (8,611 meters); lies in the Karakoram Range, distinct from the Himalayas.
- Known locally as *Chogori* in Balti; the name "K2" derives from being the second peak in the Karakoram (K1 is another name).
- First summited in 1954 by Italian climbers *Ardito Desio* and *Marcello Lacedelli*, part of an Italian expedition.
- The "Savage Mountain" epithet reflects its extreme technical difficulty, unpredictable weather, and avalanche danger; the death-to-summit ratio is roughly 1 in 4.
- Summer attempts are concentrated in a narrow window (July-August) when crevasses are visible and weather marginally more stable.
- Notable ascents include women reaching the peak in 1986 (Wanda Rutkiewicz) and speed climbs becoming more common in recent decades.
- The mountain claims many lives each year; 2008 saw a particularly deadly climbing season with over 20 deaths.

## connections
