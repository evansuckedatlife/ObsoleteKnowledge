# India

## summary

India is the world's seventh-largest country by area and second-most populous, with a civilization spanning over 5,000 years. It is the birthplace of Hinduism and Buddhism, home to the Vedic philosophy that shaped world thought, and the site of the Taj Mahal. India's cultures, religions, and intellectual traditions have profoundly influenced Asia and the world; its history spans kingdoms, colonial subjugation under Britain, and emergence as an independent democracy.

## you gotta know

- The *Vedic civilization* (c. 1500–500 BCE) produced the *Vedas*, the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism, establishing philosophies of karma, dharma, and reincarnation that influenced billions.
- *Hinduism*, one of the world's major religions, emerged in India and encompasses diverse practices, deities, and philosophical schools unified by belief in eternal truth (*Satya*) and cyclical time.
- *Buddhism*, founded in India by the Buddha in the 5th century BCE, originated as a reaction to Vedic Brahmanism and spread across Asia, becoming one of the world's major religions.
- The *Taj Mahal*, built in the 17th century by Mughal emperor *Shah Jahan* as a mausoleum for his wife, is considered the world's greatest example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love.
- The *British Raj* (1757–1947) was British colonial rule that drained India's wealth, reshaped its governance and education, and unified its diverse regions under one administration.
- *Mahatma Gandhi* (1869–1948) led India's nonviolent independence movement against British rule, pioneering *satyagraha* (truth-force) and influencing civil rights movements globally.
- The *caste system*, an ancient Hindu social hierarchy, divided society by birth and occupation; though outlawed in modern India, its legacy persists in cultural practice.
