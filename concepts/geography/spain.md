# Spain

## summary

Spain is a country in southwestern Europe occupying most of the Iberian Peninsula, bordered by Portugal to the west and France to the northeast, with coasts on both the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea. Once the center of a global empire rivaling Britain's, Spain today is the birthplace of Spanish language and culture, a major center of art and literature, and a founding member of the European Union. Its history encompasses Roman occupation, Islamic rule, Christian reconquest, global imperial expansion, and civil conflict.

## you gotta know

- Spain's geography includes the *Pyrenees* mountains separating it from France, the sun-baked meseta (central plateau), and two major coastal regions: the Atlantic and the Mediterranean.
- The *Spanish Inquisition* (1478–1834), established to maintain Catholic orthodoxy, became notorious for torturing and executing heretics, Jews, and Muslims, becoming a byword for religious intolerance.
- The *Spanish Empire*, beginning with *Columbus's* 1492 voyage, grew to encompass vast territories in the Americas, making Spain one of the world's most powerful nations from the 16th through 18th centuries.
- The *Reconquista*, the centuries-long Christian campaign to reclaim the peninsula from Islamic rule, culminated in 1492 with the fall of Granada; this same year saw the expulsion of Jews and Muslims.
- *Moorish Spain* (711–1492 CE), particularly the *Caliphate of Córdoba* and the *Emirate of Granada*, was a center of Islamic civilization, science, and culture that rivaled any in the world.
- Spain's *Golden Age* (16th–17th centuries) produced literary masterpieces including *Don Quixote* by *Miguel de Cervantes* and plays by *Lope de Vega*.
- The *Spanish Civil War* (1936–1939) between Republican and Nationalist forces, won by *Francisco Franco*, served as a rehearsal for World War II and resulted in nearly 500,000 deaths.
- Spain's museums and architecture, from the *Sagrada Familia* basilica to the *Prado Museum*, contain some of Europe's greatest artistic and architectural treasures.
