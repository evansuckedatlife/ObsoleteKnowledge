# Euphrates River

## summary

The **Euphrates River** is the longest river in southwestern Asia, flowing approximately 2,760 kilometers (1,715 miles) from its source in eastern Turkey through Syria and Iraq to the Persian Gulf. It is the western boundary of ancient Mesopotamia and, along with the Tigris, sustained some of humanity's earliest and most consequential civilizations, from the *Sumerians* to the *Persians*.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Armenian highlands of eastern Turkey and flows through Syria and Iraq, merging with the Tigris near the Persian Gulf.
- The *Euphrates-Tigris system* enabled the rise of *Sumer*, where writing, law codes, irrigation, and urban civilization first emerged.
- *Babylon*, history's most famous ancient city, sat on the Euphrates's banks and was the capital of *Hammurabi's* empire.
- Dams and irrigation projects in Turkey (particularly the *Atatürk Dam*) have reduced the river's flow to downstream nations by half in some seasons.
- The *Euphrates Valley* was traversed by empires and trade routes for millennia; it remains strategically important today.
- Water scarcity is becoming critical: Iraq's share of water has dropped dramatically, threatening agriculture and drinking water supplies.
- Archaeological sites like *Uruk*, *Ur*, and *Mari* line the riverbanks, preserving evidence of the world's earliest urban centers.
