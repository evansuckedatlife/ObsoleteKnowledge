# Mount Everest

## summary

*Mount Everest* is the world's highest mountain at 29,032 feet, located in the Himalayas on the Nepal-Tibet border. First summited in 1953 by Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay, it remains the supreme test of mountaineering skill, endurance, and determination, drawing climbers from around the globe despite its deadly hazards.

## you gotta know

- Stands at 29,032 feet (8,849 meters) above sea level; the death zone above 26,000 feet has insufficient oxygen to sustain human life for extended periods.
- Known in Nepali as *Sagarmatha* and in Tibetan as *Chomolungma* (Goddess Mother of the World).
- First successful ascent in 1953 by British climber *Edmund Hillary* and Sherpa *Tenzing Norgay*, part of a British expedition led by John Hunt.
- Hundreds of climbers attempt the peak each year during the brief spring weather window (May); over 300 people have died on the mountain since records began.
- Primary routes are the South Col route from Nepal (more frequently climbed) and the North Col route from Tibet.
- Avalanches, falling ice, extreme cold, and altitude sickness are the principal killers; many bodies remain on the mountain.
- The Sherpas, indigenous to the region, provide essential climbing support and have become synonymous with high-altitude mountaineering.
