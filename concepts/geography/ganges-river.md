# Ganges River

## summary

The **Ganges River** is the holiest river in Hinduism and the lifeblood of northern India, flowing approximately 2,525 kilometers (1,560 miles) from the Himalayas southeast across the Indian subcontinent to the Bay of Bengal. Revered as a goddess in Hindu tradition, it sustains over 400 million people through its vast delta and has shaped Indian spirituality, culture, and civilization for millennia.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the *Himalayan glaciers* and flows southeast through the *Ganges Plain*, one of the world's most densely populated regions.
- Sacred to Hindus as the goddess *Ganga*; pilgrims bathe in its waters to cleanse sins and seek divine blessing.
- *Varanasi* (Benares), built along its banks, is Hinduism's holiest city—where the faithful come to die and be cremated.
- The *Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta* in *Bangladesh* is the world's largest delta, covering 70,000 square kilometers.
- Historically a major trade route and cultural artery; dynasties like the *Maurya* and *Gupta* rose to power along its course.
- The river delta is experiencing severe ecological stress from pollution, damming, and climate change—water levels fluctuate dangerously.
- Cremation of millions annually means human remains are routinely committed to the river in Hindu funeral practice.
