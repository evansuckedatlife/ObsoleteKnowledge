# Mount Kenya

## summary

*Mount Kenya* is Africa's second-highest peak at 17,057 feet, located in central Kenya near the equator. A dramatic glaciated mountain with twin peaks and extensive rock climbing terrain, Kenya is technically more challenging than Kilimanjaro and appeals to mountaineers seeking alpine rock skills in addition to high-altitude endurance.

## you gotta know

- Height of 17,057 feet (5,199 meters); the second-highest mountain on the African continent.
- Located in the Kenya Highlands, roughly 100 miles north of Nairobi; straddles the equator.
- Twin peaks: *Batian* (the higher summit) and *Nelion*, separated by a dramatic snow-filled gap; the final approach to Batian requires technical rock climbing.
- Perpetual glaciers and snow adorn the upper reaches, though glaciers have retreated significantly in recent decades due to climate change.
- First ascent by Europeans in 1899 (Halford Mackinder and his party); local populations had reached lower altitudes, though the technical peaks remained unclimbed.
- Climbing requires rock-climbing skill and experience; the standard Point Lenana route avoids the technical crux and offers views but doesn't summit the true peak.
- Acclimatization is variable; the mountain's equatorial location and rainfall patterns create unpredictable weather; climbing season peaks in February and August.
