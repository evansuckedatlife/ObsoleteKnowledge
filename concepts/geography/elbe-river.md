## summary

The *Elbe* is a 724-mile river flowing from the Czech Republic through eastern Germany to the North Sea, passing through major cities like Dresden and Hamburg. It has been central to German and Baltic trade, and the Elbe valley's beauty inspired Romantic-era painters and poets.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Czech Riesengebirge mountains and flows northwest through Saxony, Bohemia, and Hamburg before emptying into the North Sea.
- Hamburg, situated at the Elbe's mouth, became one of Europe's major ports; the Hanseatic League's power partly depended on Elbe commerce.
- The Saxon Switzerland region along the Elbe (near Dresden) features dramatic sandstone cliffs and gorges that inspired Romantic landscape painters.
- Served as a natural border dividing Cold War Germany; the Elbe was the meeting point of American and Soviet forces in 1945.
- The river has been heavily industrialized; pollution from lignite mining and industrial sites in East Germany left a polluted legacy lasting into the post-Cold War era.
- The Elbe valley near Meissen is famous for porcelain production; Meissen's position on the river enabled trade in this luxury good.

## connections

- [[germany]] — the Elbe flows through the heart of German territory and shaped its geography.
- [[czech-republic]] — the river originates in Czech mountains; a shared resource between nations.
- [[hamburg]] — one of Europe's greatest ports is built at the Elbe's mouth.
- [[hanseatic-league]] — the Elbe was a crucial trade route for northern European medieval commerce.
- [[cold-war]] — the Elbe marked the boundary between Soviet and Western forces in divided Germany.
