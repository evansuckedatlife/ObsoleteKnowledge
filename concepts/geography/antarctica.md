# Antarctica

## summary

*Antarctica* is the world's southernmost continent, an ice-bound polar desert surrounding the South Pole and covering roughly 5.5 million square miles. Though it appears alien and uninhabited, it hosts thriving marine ecosystems and unique fauna; it is also a frontier for scientific research and geopolitical claims, governed by the international Antarctic Treaty.

## you gotta know

- The coldest continent on Earth; temperatures plunge below −80°C (−112°F) in interior regions during winter, with wind chills making it one of the planet's most hostile environments.
- Contains roughly 90% of the world's ice and 70% of its fresh water, locked in the Antarctic Ice Sheet, which is 2–3 miles thick on average.
- Classified as a desert because it receives minimal precipitation (under 10 inches annually as snowfall in most areas); the interior is essentially a polar desert despite being covered in ice.
- Home to penguins, seals, whales, and krill; the Antarctic food web is anchored by krill, tiny crustaceans that support whales, fish, and marine birds.
- The Antarctic ozone hole, discovered in the 1980s, is a thinning of the ozone layer caused by chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs); it remains a major environmental concern.
- The *Antarctic Treaty* (1961) designates Antarctica as a scientific preserve, bans military activity, and shelves territorial claims; it remains one of history's most successful international agreements.
- Research stations operated by multiple nations study climate change, astronomy, paleontology, and biology; the continent is critical to understanding Earth's climate system.
