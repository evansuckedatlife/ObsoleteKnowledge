# Colorado River

## summary

The *Colorado River* is a major river of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, flowing approximately 1,450 miles from the Rocky Mountains through the Colorado Plateau and deserts to the Gulf of California. It is the lifeblood of the arid Southwest, supplying water for irrigation, municipal use, and hydropower across seven US states and Mexico.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the San Juan Mountains of Colorado, in the high Rocky Mountain region where snowmelt feeds it year-round.
- Carves through the *Grand Canyon* in Arizona, creating one of North America's most iconic landscapes and a testament to the power of river erosion over millions of years.
- Dammed by the *Hoover Dam* near Las Vegas, created in the 1930s to provide water storage, hydropower, and flood control; creates *Lake Mead*, the largest reservoir in the US by volume.
- Water from the Colorado is allocated by the Colorado River Compact (1922) among California, Arizona, Nevada, Utah, Colorado, Wyoming, and New Mexico, and Mexico receives a guaranteed share.
- Heavily diverted for irrigation in California's Imperial Valley and elsewhere, such that it often runs dry before reaching its mouth in the Gulf of California.
- Home to species found nowhere else, including the Colorado pikeminnow, now federally protected due to dam impacts.
- Named by Spanish explorers for the reddish (colorado) sediment that colours the water in certain seasons.
