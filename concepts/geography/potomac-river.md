# Potomac River

## summary

The *Potomac River* flows approximately 380 miles from the Appalachian Mountains of West Virginia to *Chesapeake Bay*, forming the boundary between Maryland and Virginia. It is the river upon which *Washington, DC* was built, and has been central to American political and cultural history as the waterway of the nation's capital.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Appalachian Mountains of West Virginia, formed by the confluence of two branches, and flows generally eastward toward the Chesapeake.
- Named from the Powhatan Amerindian word meaning "where the goods are brought in" or "something brought."
- Flows directly through Washington, DC, where it defines the capital's geography and recreational space; the Lincoln Memorial and many monuments overlook its waters.
- Forms much of the boundary between Maryland and Virginia, with Virginia's suburbs spreading along its banks.
- Historically important for early colonial commerce; *Alexandria, Virginia* and other river towns were major 18th-century ports.
- Subject to dramatic tidal influence as far upstream as Washington, DC, creating a tidal estuary that affects navigation and water quality.
- Famous for its cherry blossom festivals, planted along the Tidal Basin (a branch connected to the Potomac).
