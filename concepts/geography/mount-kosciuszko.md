# Mount Kosciuszko

## summary

*Mount Kosciuszko* is the highest peak in Australia at 7,310 feet, located in the Great Dividing Range in New South Wales. Though modest in elevation compared to mountains on other continents, its gentle slopes and relative accessibility make it the easiest and most-climbed summit on the "Seven Summits" climbing list, accessible to millions of visitors annually via hiking trails.

## you gotta know

- Height of 7,310 feet (2,228 meters); the highest mountain on the Australian continent.
- Located in the Snowy Mountains of New South Wales, part of the Great Dividing Range; a granite peak with sparse alpine vegetation.
- Named after Polish military hero and American Revolutionary War officer *Tadeusz Kosciuszko*.
- Climbs are non-technical hikes accessible to children and non-climbers; the most popular route from Thredbo takes roughly 3–4 hours round-trip.
- The peak receives hundreds of thousands of visitors annually, making it among the world's most-summited mountains by raw numbers.
- Part of the "Seven Summits" climbing achievement (highest peak on each continent); it is the easiest summit on the list.
- Perpetual snow is rare; alpine ecology comprises grasses, low shrubs, and cushion plants adapted to extremes of wind and cold.
