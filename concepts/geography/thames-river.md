# Thames River

## summary

The *Thames* is England's most important river, flowing 215 miles through south-central England to the North Sea. It is London's lifeline; the city was founded at a Thames ford, and the river shaped England's maritime power and colonial expansion.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Cotswold Hills and flows southeast through London before widening into a tidal estuary leading to the North Sea.
- London's position at the Thames's first tidal crossing made it a natural trade hub; Roman Londinium was established here as a port.
- The Thames provided London's water supply and power for mills; medieval London's prosperity was inseparable from the river.
- The tidal Thames historically froze solid during severe winters, supporting "Frost Fairs" where ice-bound festivals and markets occurred (last recorded 1813).
- The 13th-century Magna Carta was signed at Runnymede, a Thames-side meadow; the Thames valley hosted England's most important political moments.
- Greenwich and Hampton Court, royal residences on the Thames, showcased power and culture; the river was England's principal transport for royalty and goods.
