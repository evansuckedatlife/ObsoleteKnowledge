# Australia

## summary

Australia is an island continent in the southern hemisphere, the world's sixth-largest country by area and the smallest continent, with a population of over 26 million concentrated on its coasts. Originally inhabited for at least 65,000 years by Aboriginal Australians, Australia was colonized by Britain in 1788 and developed from a penal colony into a major developed nation. Modern Australia is a multicultural democracy, though its relationship with its Indigenous peoples remains contested.

## you gotta know

- Australia's interior, the *Outback*, is a vast arid region of deserts and grasslands; the most famous geographic features are *Uluru* (Ayers Rock) and the *Great Barrier Reef*, the world's largest coral reef system.
- *Aboriginal Australians* inhabited Australia for at least 65,000 years, developing complex cultures, languages, and spiritual traditions before European contact; they comprise over 3% of modern Australia's population.
- *Captain James Cook's* voyage along the eastern coast (1770) led to British claims on the continent; Britain established a penal colony at Port Jackson in 1788, which grew into Sydney.
- Australia's "White Australia Policy" (1901–1970s) restricted non-European immigration, reflecting racist attitudes that marginalized Aboriginal Australians and created homogeneous Anglo-European settlement.
- The *Great Barrier Reef*, stretching 2,300 kilometers along Queensland's coast, is the world's largest coral reef system and one of the seven natural wonders of the world; it is threatened by climate change.
- Australia played a crucial role in the *Pacific Theater* of World War II, serving as a base for the Allies' campaign against Japan and suffering attacks at Pearl Harbor's periphery.
- The *Sydney Opera House* (opened 1973), designed by Danish architect Jørn Utzon, is an iconic modernist building and one of the world's most recognizable structures.
- Aboriginal *Dreamtime* stories and *dot painting* art traditions represent one of humanity's oldest continuing artistic traditions, spanning tens of thousands of years.
