# Kalahari Desert

## summary

The *Kalahari* is a large semi-arid sandy savanna covering roughly 360,000 square miles across Botswana, Namibia, and South Africa. Unlike hot deserts, it receives enough seasonal rainfall to support scattered grassland, shrub, and hardy trees, and is home to diverse wildlife and the indigenous San people who have inhabited it for millennia.

## you gotta know

- Characterized by red sand dunes and sparse vegetation; considerably less barren than the Sahara or Atacama, with seasonal water sources and underground aquifers.
- The *Okavango Delta*, the world's largest inland delta, borders it to the north, creating a seasonal flood-fed ecosystem that supports massive wildlife concentrations.
- Home to lions, African wild dogs, meerkats, and other predators; the wildlife documentary *Planet Earth* and *The Life of Mammals* feature its ecosystems.
- The San people (also called *Bushmen*) have lived as hunter-gatherers in the Kalahari for at least 20,000 years, with deep ecological knowledge of its plants and animals.
- Named from the Khoi word *'kalhari'*, meaning "the great thirst," reflecting its low and erratic rainfall despite not being a true desert in technical terms.
- Contains the *Central Kalahari Game Reserve*, one of Africa's largest protected areas, established in 1961.
- Ground-nesting birds like the secretary bird and social weavers thrive in its open grassland habitat.
