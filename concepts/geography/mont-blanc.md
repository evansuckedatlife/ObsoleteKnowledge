# Mont Blanc

## summary

*Mont Blanc* is the highest peak in the Alps at 15,774 feet, straddling the border of France, Italy, and Switzerland in the Western Alps. Its proximity to European population centers, moderate altitude, and relative accessibility make it the most popular high-altitude destination in Europe; tens of thousands summit annually, yet the mountain claims dozens of lives each year.

## you gotta know

- Height of 15,774 feet (4,808 meters); the highest point in Western Europe.
- Located in the Mont Blanc Massif; borders France and Italy, with Switzerland nearby—all three nations dispute boundary claims on the peak itself.
- Accessible via multiple routes from the Chamonix valley (France); the standard route is non-technical but requires acclimatization and glacier travel.
- The "Goûter Route" is the most popular path, funneling thousands of climbers up a single corridor; rockfall and altitude sickness exact a steady toll.
- First ascent recorded in 1786 by Michel Paccard and Jacques Balmat, spurring the birth of modern mountaineering and Alpine tourism.
- Perpetual snow and extensive glaciers define the peak; glacier retreat has made routes progressively more rockfall-prone and hazardous.
- Summer conditions (July-August) draw the vast majority of climbers; winter ascents are rare and extremely dangerous.
