# Irrawaddy River

## summary

The **Irrawaddy River** (also spelled Ayeyarwady) is Myanmar's most important river, flowing approximately 2,170 kilometers (1,348 miles) from its source in the Himalayan foothills south through the length of the country to the Andaman Sea. It is the economic and spiritual lifeline of Myanmar, supporting agriculture, transportation, and trade, and flows through the heart of Burmese Buddhist civilization.

## you gotta know

- Flows southward from the mountains of northern Myanmar (Burma) through central plains to a broad delta on the Andaman Sea.
- The *Irrawaddy Delta* is one of Southeast Asia's most fertile regions, a major rice-growing area that feeds much of Myanmar.
- *Yangon* (Rangoon), Myanmar's largest city and former capital, sits at the apex of the delta and was the center of colonial Burma.
- The river was a crucial British colonial trade route; steamboats transported goods and administration deep into the interior.
- *Mandalay*, Myanmar's second-largest city, sits on the river at a culturally and historically significant location in upper Myanmar.
- The *Irrawaddy dolphin* (*Orcaella brevirostris*), a rare freshwater cetacean, is found in the river and critically endangered.
- Buddhist pilgrims venerate temples and monasteries along the river; the Irrawaddy is sacred in Burmese Buddhist tradition.
