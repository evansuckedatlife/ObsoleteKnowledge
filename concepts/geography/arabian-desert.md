# Arabian Desert

## summary

The *Arabian Desert* spans roughly 900,000 square miles across the Arabian Peninsula, encompassing parts of Saudi Arabia, Yemen, Oman, the United Arab Emirates, Jordan, Iraq, and Israel. It is one of the world's largest hot deserts, characterized by sand dunes, rocky plateaus, and sparse oases, and has been home to Bedouin pastoralists and the birthplace of Islam.

## you gotta know

- Extends from the Levant in the north to the Arabian Sea in the south; the *Rub' al-Khali* (Empty Quarter) comprises its largest and most barren section.
- Temperatures regularly exceed 50°C (122°F) in summer; the desert experiences dramatic temperature swings due to the thin atmosphere and minimal cloud cover.
- Ancient trade routes crossed the Arabian Desert; the *Frankincense Road* and *Spice Road* connected Arabia's ports to the Mediterranean and beyond.
- Bedouin communities adapted to desert life through pastoral nomadism, deep knowledge of water sources, and social structures suited to arid environments.
- The region was the origin and early center of *Islam*; Mecca and Medina, Islam's holiest cities, lie in the Arabian Desert.
- Vast oil and natural gas reserves underlie the desert, particularly in Saudi Arabia, making it one of the world's most economically significant regions.
- Oasis settlements like *Shibam* (the "Manhattan of the Desert") showcased sophisticated desert urbanism and architecture adapted to extreme climate.
