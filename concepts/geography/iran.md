# Iran

## summary

Iran is a transcontinental country in western Asia, historically known as Persia, spanning the Iranian Plateau between the Caspian Sea and the Persian Gulf. One of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, Iran gave rise to the first great multinational empire under the Achaemenid Persians (c. 550–330 BCE) and later became the center of Islamic civilization. Iran's influence on world culture—through its language, poetry, philosophy, and religious thought—extends far beyond its borders.

## you gotta know

- Iran's territory encompasses the vast Iranian Plateau, bounded by the Caspian Sea to the north, the Persian Gulf and Gulf of Oman to the south, and mountain ranges (Alborz and Zagros) that define much of its topography.
- The *Achaemenid Persian Empire* (c. 550–330 BCE), founded by *Cyrus the Great*, was the largest empire the world had yet seen, spanning from Egypt to India until conquered by *Alexander the Great*.
- *Zoroastrianism*, one of the world's oldest monotheistic religions, originated in ancient Iran and profoundly influenced later religions including Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.
- The *Silk Road* trade routes passed through Iran, making it a crucial hub linking the Mediterranean, East Asia, and India for nearly two millennia.
- Persian poetry and literature, including the works of *Rumi*, *Omar Khayyam*, and *Hafiz*, rank among humanity's greatest literary achievements and remain widely read today.
- The *Iranian Islamic Revolution* of 1979 established an Islamic theocracy under *Ayatollah Khomeini* and fundamentally reshaped Middle Eastern politics and Iran's international relations.
- Iran is home to some of the world's largest proven oil reserves and natural gas deposits, making it a major global energy producer.
- The Persian language (Farsi) is one of the world's oldest languages still in continuous use, with a literary tradition spanning over 2,500 years.
