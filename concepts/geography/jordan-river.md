## summary

The **Jordan River** is the world's lowest river, flowing approximately 251 kilometers (156 miles) southward from the mountains of Lebanon through Israel and the Palestinian territories to the Dead Sea. Though relatively modest in length and flow, it holds immense historical and religious significance as a geographic boundary, a baptismal site, and a focal point of Middle Eastern geopolitics.

## you gotta know

- Flows from the *Anti-Lebanon Mountains* south through the *Jordan Valley*, passing through *Lake Tiberias* (Sea of Galilee) before reaching the *Dead Sea*.
- The lowest point on Earth: its mouth at the Dead Sea lies 1,410 meters (4,626 feet) below sea level.
- In Christian tradition, *Jesus* was baptized by *John the Baptist* in the Jordan River, making it a pilgrimage destination for Christians worldwide.
- Forms the boundary between Israel and Jordan; historically defined the eastern frontier of ancient Israel.
- The water level is dropping drastically due to upstream dams and irrigation projects; the Dead Sea shrinks by about 1 meter per year.
- The river is heavily over-allocated: Israel, Jordan, Syria, and the Palestinian territories all depend on its water, leading to chronic conflict.
- Ancient cities like *Jericho*, the "City of Palms," sit near the river and feature prominently in biblical and archaeological records.
