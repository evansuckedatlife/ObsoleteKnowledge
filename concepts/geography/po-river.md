# Po River

## summary

The *Po* is northern Italy's longest river, flowing 405 miles from the Alps westward across the Padana plain to the Adriatic Sea. It created Italy's most fertile agricultural region and enabled the rise of medieval city-states like Venice and Milan.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Maritime Alps and flows eastward across the Padana (Po Valley), Italy's most productive agricultural and industrial region.
- The Po delta near Venice is a complex network of lagoons, marshes, and waterways; Venice's foundation depended on Po-valley grain supplies.
- Medieval northern Italian city-states (Milan, Padua, Ferrara) grew wealthy controlling sections of the Po and its tributaries.
- Historically prone to catastrophic flooding; the 1951 flood devastated northern Italy and prompted modern flood-control infrastructure.
- Subject to heavy pollution from industrial and agricultural runoff; modern water quality in the lower Po is a major environmental concern.
- The Po valley is Italy's breadbasket; wheat, rice, corn, and other crops flourish in its alluvial soils.
