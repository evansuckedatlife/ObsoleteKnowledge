# Egypt

## summary

Egypt is a transcontinental country spanning northeastern Africa and the Sinai Peninsula of southwestern Asia, centered on the valley and delta of the Nile River. Home to one of the world's earliest great civilizations, which flourished for over three millennia and left indelible marks on architecture, writing, and religion, Egypt remains a major cultural and geopolitical hub in the Middle East. The country is roughly 97% desert, making the Nile's narrow fertile corridor the lifeblood of its population.

## you gotta know

- Egypt is bounded by the Mediterranean Sea to the north and the Red Sea to the east; the Suez Canal, one of the world's most strategically vital waterways, connects these two seas and crosses Egyptian territory.
- *Ancient Egypt* emerged around 3100 BCE with the unification of Upper and Lower Egypt under the First Dynasty and lasted until Rome's conquest in 30 BCE, spanning roughly 3,000 years of continuous civilization.
- The *Egyptian pyramids*, particularly the *Great Pyramid of Giza*, were built as monumental tombs for *pharaohs* during the Old Kingdom (c. 2686–2181 BCE) and remain among the world's most recognizable structures.
- *Hieroglyphics*, the ancient Egyptian writing system combining logographic and alphabetic elements, was used for nearly 3,500 years until it was replaced by Coptic after Roman conquest.
- The Nile River is the world's longest river, flowing northward through Egypt for over 6,600 kilometers; ancient Egyptian civilization was entirely dependent on its annual flooding cycles, which the *Aswan High Dam* (completed 1970) now controls.
- *Cleopatra VII* (69–30 BCE), the last active pharaoh of Egypt, was renowned for her intelligence, her alliances with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony, and her linguistic abilities in as many as nine languages.
- Egypt is the most populous country in the Arab world with over 100 million inhabitants; Islam is the predominant religion, with Coptic Christianity as the largest minority.
- The *Suez Canal*, opened in 1869, redirected world maritime trade and made Egypt a strategic chokepoint; its seizure in 1956 by President *Gamal Abdel Nasser* triggered an international crisis.
