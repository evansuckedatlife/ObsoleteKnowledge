# China

## summary

China is one of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, spanning over 4,000 years of recorded history. From the earliest dynasties through imperial periods that ruled vast territories, to the modern communist state under Mao and beyond, China has been a center of philosophy, art, governance, and technological innovation. It is home to the Great Wall, the Silk Road, and profound philosophical traditions that shaped Asia.

## you gotta know

- China's written history begins with the *Shang dynasty* (c. 1600–1046 BCE), and its classical period culminated in the *Han dynasty* (206 BCE–220 CE), which established many institutions that lasted millennia.
- The *Great Wall of China*, built over centuries from the 7th century BCE onward, was intended to defend against invasions from the north and symbolizes Chinese engineering and ambition.
- The *Silk Road*, a network of trade routes from the 2nd century BCE onward, connected China to Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe, facilitating commerce, ideas, and religions (including Buddhism).
- *Confucianism* and *Taoism*, philosophical and spiritual systems developed in ancient China, profoundly shaped East Asian ethics, governance, and personal practice for millennia.
- The *Forbidden City* in Beijing, built in the 15th century, was the imperial palace for nearly 500 years and exemplifies classical Chinese architecture and court hierarchy.
- *Mao Zedong* (1893–1976) unified China as a communist state in 1949 and ruled with revolutionary (and devastating) policies that reshaped the nation.
- Chinese innovations—papermaking, printing, gunpowder, the compass—were exported globally and revolutionized technology and knowledge transmission.
