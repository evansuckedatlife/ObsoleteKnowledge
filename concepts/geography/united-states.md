# United States

## summary

The *United States* is a federal republic comprising 50 states across North America, stretching from the Atlantic to the Pacific Ocean. Founded through the *American Revolution* (1775–1783) as a rebellion against British colonial rule, the nation was established as a constitutional republic with principles of limited government, separation of powers, and individual rights. As the world's largest economy and a dominant military power, particularly after World War II, the United States has shaped global politics, economics, and culture over nearly 250 years.

## you gotta know

- The *American Colonies* were established by European powers, primarily Britain, beginning in the early seventeenth century; by the eighteenth century, thirteen British colonies stretched along the Atlantic coast with substantial population and economic development.
- The *American Revolution* (1775–1783) began as a colonial rebellion against British taxation and governance; the *Declaration of Independence* (1776) articulated principles of natural rights and popular sovereignty, founding the nation's ideological basis.
- The *United States Constitution* (1787), drafted at the *Constitutional Convention* in Philadelphia, established a federal system with separation of powers (legislative, executive, judicial), checks and balances, and protection of individual rights through the *Bill of Rights* (1791).
- *George Washington*, military leader of the Revolution and first president (1789–1797), set precedents for limited executive authority and peaceful transfer of power, establishing the nation's constitutional foundations.
- Western *expansion* driven by *Manifest Destiny*, the belief that American expansion across the continent was justified and inevitable, led to displacement of Native Americans and acquisition of territories from Mexico, Britain, and others.
- The *Civil War* (1861–1865) between the Union (North) and *Confederacy* (South) was fought over slavery's expansion and the Union's continued existence; the North's victory preserved the Union, ended slavery, and established federal authority over states.
- The *Civil Rights Movement* (1950s–1960s) challenged racial segregation and discrimination through nonviolent direct action and legislation, fundamentally transforming American society and law, though racial inequality persists.
- American *industrial power*, developed through the nineteenth and twentieth centuries, made the United States an economic powerhouse; mass production, transportation infrastructure, and technological innovation established American economic dominance.
- *World War II* established the United States as a global superpower; American military and economic capacity were decisive in defeating Nazi Germany and Japan; post-war, the U.S. emerged as leader of the Western alliance.
- The *Cold War* (1947–1991) between the United States and the *Soviet Union* defined geopolitics for nearly half a century, involving nuclear deterrence, proxy wars, ideological competition, and technological rivalry in space exploration and weapons development.
