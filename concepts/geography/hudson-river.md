# Hudson River

## summary

The *Hudson River* flows approximately 315 miles southward from the Adirondack Mountains in upstate New York to New York Harbor and the Atlantic Ocean. Though relatively short, it is one of the most historically and culturally significant rivers in North America, serving as New York City's principal waterfront and a major symbol of American commerce and industry.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Adirondack Mountains of upstate New York near Lake Tear of the Clouds, in a pristine wilderness region.
- Explored and named after the English navigator *Henry Hudson*, who sailed it in 1609 seeking the Northwest Passage.
- Forms the boundary between Manhattan and New Jersey for much of its course through New York City.
- Created the first major American settlement at New York Harbor, making it essential to colonial commerce and later becoming the busiest port in North America.
- Subject to significant tidal influence up to Troy, New York, making it partially a tidal estuary rather than a purely freshwater river.
- Historically heavily polluted from industrial development and PCB (polychlorinated biphenyl) contamination; ongoing restoration efforts seek to revive its ecology.
- Inspired American landscape painters of the *Hudson River School* in the 19th century, including Thomas Cole and Frederic Church.
