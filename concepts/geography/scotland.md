# Scotland

## summary

Scotland is a country that comprises the northern part of the island of Great Britain and over 790 adjacent islands, part of the United Kingdom since 1707. Historically distinct from England, Scotland has preserved its own legal system, educational institutions, and cultural traditions rooted in Gaelic and Norse heritage. A nation of rugged highlands, dramatic coastlines, and a long history of resistance to English dominion, Scotland has produced world-changing philosophers, scientists, and innovations.

## you gotta know

- Scotland's geography is divided into the Highlands (northwest), the Lowlands (south and central), and the Southern Uplands; it is home to *Loch Ness*, famous for legends of its monster.
- *William Wallace* and *Robert the Bruce* led Scottish resistance to English conquest in the late 13th and early 14th centuries, culminating in the Declaration of Arbroath (1320) asserting Scottish independence.
- The *Scottish Enlightenment* (18th century) was a flowering of intellectual achievement that produced philosophers like *David Hume* and *Adam Smith*, whose ideas shaped modern political economy and philosophy.
- *Gaelic* and *Scots* languages are indigenous to Scotland; though historically suppressed, they have experienced a cultural revival, and Gaelic is now taught in schools alongside English.
- The *Highland Clearances* (18th–19th centuries) forcibly displaced thousands of crofters from the Highlands to make way for sheep farming, fundamentally altering Scottish society and spurring emigration to North America.
- Scotland joined with England to form the *Kingdom of Great Britain* in 1707 through the Acts of Union, which many Scots saw as an end to independence; devolution in 1999 restored a Scottish Parliament.
- Scottish inventors and scientists contributed enormously to the Industrial Revolution: *James Watt* developed the steam engine, and *Alexander Graham Bell* invented the telephone.
- Scottish culture—including *tartan*, *bagpipes*, *whisky*, and the Highland games—has become iconic symbols recognized worldwide.
