# Japan

## summary

Japan is an island nation in East Asia whose civilization developed in relative isolation for much of its history, creating a distinct culture centered on emperor veneration, feudal warrior codes, Zen Buddhism, and aesthetic refinement. From feudal samurai kingdoms through the isolationist Edo period to rapid modernization and militarism in the 20th century, Japan has repeatedly reinvented itself while preserving deep cultural continuity. It is home to distinctive art forms, philosophy, and one of the world's most advanced economies.

## you gotta know

- Japanese civilization developed from indigenous *Shinto*, a polytheistic religion venerating nature spirits and ancestors, which remains central to Japanese culture despite *Buddhism*'s later integration.
- The *samurai*, the feudal warrior class bound by *bushido* (the way of the warrior), dominated Japan from the medieval period until the Meiji Restoration abolished them in 1868.
- The *shogunate*, a military government ruled by a generalissimo (*shogun*), developed in the 12th century and remained the dominant power structure until 1868; the *Tokugawa shogunate* (1603–1868) was the longest-lasting.
- During the *Edo period* (1603–1868), Japan closed itself to foreign contact, developing a unique isolated culture; this ended when Commodore Perry's American ships forced opening in 1853.
- *Mount Fuji*, Japan's sacred mountain and highest peak, has inspired centuries of art, poetry, and pilgrimage; it appears in countless works including Hokusai's famous print series.
- *Haiku*, a brief three-line poetic form with a 5-7-5 syllable structure, emerged in the 17th century and became the quintessential expression of Japanese aesthetics and Zen philosophy.
- Japan's rapid militarization led to invasion of China (1937) and alliance with Nazi Germany; its World War II defeat resulted in occupation and transformation into a pacifist democracy.
