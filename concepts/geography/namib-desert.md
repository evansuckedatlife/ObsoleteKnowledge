# Namib Desert

## summary

The *Namib* is one of Africa's most ancient deserts, extending roughly 1,200 miles along the southwestern coast of Namibia. Sustained by the cold *Benguela Current*, which suppresses rainfall, it is famous for towering rust-colored sand dunes, minimal vegetation, and spectacular landscapes that have inspired artists, scientists, and filmmakers.

## you gotta know

- One of the oldest deserts on Earth, with evidence of aridity dating back at least 55 million years, making it geologically stable and ecologically specialized.
- The *Namib Dunes* near *Sossussvlei* include some of the world's tallest sand dunes, reaching 800 feet or higher; their bright orange color comes from iron oxide in the sand.
- Extremely arid—most areas receive less than 1 inch of rain annually—yet supports endemic species found nowhere else, including the *welwitschia* plant and the *namib beetle*.
- The *Skeleton Coast* to its north is a graveyard of shipwrecks, where the cold waters and coastal fogs create a haunt of danger for mariners.
- Sustains a remarkable guild of desert-adapted animals: jackals, hyenas, snakes, and insects that extract moisture from sea fog rather than rainfall.
- Named from the Nama language word *'namib'*, meaning "place where there is nothing."
- Now a major tourist destination; the desolate beauty has been featured in films and photography exhibitions worldwide.
