# Tiber River

## summary

The *Tiber* is the river on which Rome was founded and grew into the capital of the ancient Mediterranean world. Flowing 252 miles from the Apennine Mountains through central Italy to the Tyrrhenian Sea, the Tiber was Rome's lifeline for trade, defense, and agriculture.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Apennine Mountains and flows through Tuscany and central Italy before reaching Rome and emptying into the Tyrrhenian Sea.
- Rome's position at a shallow ford of the Tiber made it a natural crossroads for trade between the Mediterranean and the interior of Italy.
- The Tiber provided Rome with water, fish, and grain from its flood plain; the river's valley was fertile and agriculturally rich.
- Roman legend traces the city's founding to 753 BCE and attributes Romulus and Remus's rescue (by a she-wolf) to the Tiber's banks.
- The Tiber was notoriously prone to flooding; severe floods were often interpreted as divine omens and attributed to the gods' displeasure.
- Popes and medieval Rome built bridges and ports along the Tiber; the river remained Rome's lifeline even after the empire's fall.
