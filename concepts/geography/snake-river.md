# Snake River

## summary

The *Snake River* flows approximately 1,040 miles through the Pacific Northwest, originating in the Teton Range of Wyoming and flowing westward through Idaho before joining the *Columbia River* in Washington. It is the Columbia's largest tributary and drains a vast plateau region of the American interior West.

## you gotta know

- Originates near Jackson Lake in the Teton Range on the Wyoming-Idaho border, flowing through mountains and then across the Snake River Plain.
- Receives its name from the distinctive S-shaped bends along portions of its course, though some sources attribute it to a Shoshone or Nez Perce word.
- Flows westward across Idaho, carving the *Hells Canyon*, a dramatic 7,000-foot gorge that is among North America's deepest river canyons.
- Extensively dammed with multiple hydroelectric projects including the *Brownlee*, *Oxbow*, and *Hells Canyon Dams*, primarily for power generation and irrigation.
- Joins the *Columbia River* in Washington; the union dramatically increases the Columbia's volume and power.
- Historically crucial for fur trading and exploration in the Pacific Northwest; followed by trappers and early settlement.
- Supports a complex ecosystem and fishery, though dam construction has significantly impacted salmon populations and ecological integrity.
