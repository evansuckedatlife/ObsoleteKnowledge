# Mount Kilimanjaro

## summary

*Mount Kilimanjaro* is Africa's highest peak at 19,341 feet, located in Tanzania near the Kenyan border. Though its elevation is modest compared to Himalayan giants, its prominence, dramatic volcanic form, and accessibility make it one of the world's most climbed mountains; climbers cite the striking contrast between snow-capped summit and equatorial savanna.

## you gotta know

- Height of 19,341 feet (5,895 meters) above sea level; the highest point in Africa.
- A dormant volcano (last eruption roughly 360,000 years ago); comprises three volcanic cones: *Kibo* (the highest), *Mawenzi*, and *Shira*.
- Perpetual snows and glaciers cover the crater rim, though the glaciers have shrunk dramatically over the last century due to climate change.
- Climbs typically take 5–9 days via multiple routes (Marangu, Machame, Rongai); no technical climbing required, making it accessible to non-mountaineers.
- Altitude acclimatization is the primary challenge; many climbers experience acute mountain sickness despite the "simplicity" of the route.
- The stark contrast between lush rainforest at lower elevations and barren, ice-draped summit exemplifies Kilimanjaro's ecological diversity.
- First summited by Europeans in 1889 (Hans Meyer and Ludwig Purtscheller), though local populations had likely reached the summit much earlier.

## connections
