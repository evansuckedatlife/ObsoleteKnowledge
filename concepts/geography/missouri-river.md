# Missouri River

## summary

The *Missouri River* flows approximately 2,540 miles from the Rocky Mountains in Montana to its confluence with the Mississippi River near St. Louis, Missouri. It is technically longer than the Mississippi, and their union creates the enormous downstream flow that characterizes the lower Mississippi valley, draining a vast portion of central North America.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Rocky Mountains of western Montana, at the confluence of the Gallatin, Jefferson, and Madison Rivers near Three Forks.
- Named from the *Missouri* or *Mahah* Amerindian people who lived along its banks, the term meaning "big muddy" or "big river."
- Carries enormous sediment loads that give it a distinctive murky brown colour, and this sediment has historically reshaped the landscape downstream as far as the Mississippi Delta.
- Follows a serpentine course eastward across the Great Plains, flowing through or bordering Montana, North Dakota, South Dakota, Nebraska, Iowa, and Missouri.
- Served as the primary route of the *Lewis and Clark Expedition* (1804–1806) as they explored western territories and reached the Pacific.
- Dammed in multiple locations for flood control, hydropower, and irrigation, including the large Fort Peck and Garrison Dams.
- Meets the Mississippi just north of St. Louis, roughly doubling its volume and determining the character of the lower Mississippi's flow.
