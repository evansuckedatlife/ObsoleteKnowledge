# Mount Aconcagua

## summary

*Mount Aconcagua* is the highest peak in South America at 22,838 feet, located in the Andes on the Argentina-Chile border. Though lower than Himalayan giants, its combination of high altitude, thin air, extreme cold, and remote location in the shadow of the Andes makes it a significant mountaineering objective and a frequent "third summit" for climbers pursuing the Seven Summits.

## you gotta know

- Height of 22,838 feet (6,961 meters); the highest mountain in the Americas.
- Located in the Andes Range near the Argentina-Chile border, approximately 60 miles from the city of Mendoza.
- Name derived from Quechua words meaning "stone sentinel" or similar meanings in various Indigenous languages of the region.
- The standard route (Ruta Normal) is non-technical but exposed; acclimatization is the primary challenge at extreme altitude.
- First ascent by Europeans in 1897 (Matthias Zurbriggen led the expedition); higher elevations had been reached on Chimborazo and other peaks, but Aconcagua stands higher.
- Climbing season is brief: December–February (Southern Hemisphere summer); severe weather, cold, and wind make climbing dangerous despite the non-technical routes.
- The mountain's prominence stems partly from its accessibility to South American populations and its place in the Seven Summits canon.
