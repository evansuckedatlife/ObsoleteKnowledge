# Loire River

## summary

The *Loire* is France's longest river, flowing 625 miles from the Massif Central through the Loire Valley to the Atlantic Ocean. The Loire valley is one of Europe's most elegant regions, dotted with Renaissance châteaux and historic towns that epitomize French culture and aristocratic refinement.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Massif Central mountains and flows generally northward, then westward toward the Atlantic; it is longer than the Seine but less commercially developed.
- The Loire Valley châteaux are Renaissance masterpieces; Chambord, Chenonceau, and Amboise reflect the wealth and taste of French nobility.
- Medieval Loire-valley cities like Orleans and Tours were major political and religious centers; Orleans was the site of Joan of Arc's greatest victories.
- The river is notable for its wide sandy beaches and islands; historically, navigation was difficult due to sandbars and shifting channels.
- The Loire valley's climate and soil make it one of France's primary wine regions; white wines from the valley are world-renowned.
- Less industrialized and urbanized than the Seine or Rhône; the Loire valley has retained more of its natural beauty and rural character.
