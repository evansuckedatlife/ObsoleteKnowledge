# Red River of the South

## summary

The *Red River of the South* (distinct from the Red River flowing into Hudson Bay) flows approximately 1,300 miles from the Texas Panhandle eastward through Texas, Oklahoma, Arkansas, and Louisiana to its confluence with the Mississippi River. It forms portions of state boundaries and drains a significant portion of the south-central United States.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Texas Panhandle, flowing eastward through semi-arid to subtropical regions before meeting the Mississippi in Louisiana.
- Named for the reddish clay and sediment that colours its waters, particularly visible during high-water periods.
- Forms the boundary between Texas and Oklahoma for much of its course, and between Texas and Arkansas in another section.
- Flows through a region historically contested between the United States and the Spanish colonial empire, with the river serving as a boundary in various treaties.
- Receives major tributaries including the *Washita River* and the *Ouachita River* in Louisiana.
- The river valley was crucial to early American frontier expansion and the Native American displacement of the 19th century.
- Subject to significant seasonal variation, with spring flooding common; the river is generally smaller and more erratic than the Mississippi or Missouri.
