# Yellow River

## summary

The **Yellow River**, or *Huang He*, is the second-longest river in Asia and historically the most dangerous, flowing approximately 5,464 kilometers (3,395 miles) from the Tibetan Plateau eastward across northern China to the Bohai Sea. Its distinctive yellow color—caused by loess sediment transported from the plateau—gave it its name and made it a symbol of Chinese civilization, though its catastrophic floods earned it the name "China's Sorrow."

## you gotta know

- Flows from the high Tibetan Plateau across the *Loess Plateau*, picking up fine silt that gives it a yellowish hue.
- Responsible for some of history's deadliest floods; the 1931 inundation killed an estimated 1–4 million people.
- The *Grand Canal*, built in the 7th century, connected the Yellow River to the Yangtze, enabling grain transport to the capital.
- Major cities include *Xi'an*, *Luoyang*, and *Kaifeng*, ancient centers of Chinese imperial power and culture.
- Its unpredictable course shift the ancient capital multiple times, as the river changed channel across the North China Plain.
- Dike construction and maintenance was a defining feature of Chinese governance and engineering for over 2,000 years.
- Water scarcity in modern times: the river now often runs dry before reaching the sea, threatening agriculture and cities.
