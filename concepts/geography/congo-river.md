# Congo River

## summary

The *Congo* is Africa's second-longest river and by far its largest by discharge, carrying more water to the ocean than any other African waterway. It flows through the heart of Central Africa for roughly 2,900 miles, draining the Congo Basin and serving as the lifeblood of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

## you gotta know

- Discharges approximately 41,000 cubic meters of water per second into the Atlantic Ocean, making it the world's second-highest-discharge river after the Amazon.
- Forms the Inga Falls and other spectacular cataracts that have historically prevented deep-water navigation but offer immense hydroelectric potential.
- Straddles the equator for much of its course, flowing through some of the world's most biodiverse rainforest ecosystems.
- Rises in the highlands of Zambia and Angola, then flows northward through the vast Congo Basin before curving westward to the Atlantic.
- The Congo Basin contains one-third of Africa's tropical forest cover, making the river central to the continent's climate and biodiversity.
- Forms the boundary between the Democratic Republic of the Congo and the Republic of the Congo for a significant stretch.
- Historically navigable in sections; used by traders and later colonizers, though cataracts have limited large-scale river commerce.
