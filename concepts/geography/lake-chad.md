# Lake Chad

## summary

*Lake Chad* is a shallow freshwater lake in the Sahel region of West-Central Africa, bordered by Nigeria, Niger, Chad, and Cameroon. Once one of Africa's largest lakes, it has shrunk dramatically over the past century due to drought, dam construction, and increasing water withdrawal, becoming a symbol of environmental degradation in the region.

## you gotta know

- Historic surface area exceeded 9,000 square miles in the 1960s; by 2020 it had shrunk to roughly 540 square miles—a loss of over 90 percent.
- The lake's decline is driven by a combination of natural drought cycles, the Sahara's southward expansion, and human water extraction upstream.
- Lies at the convergence of four African nations and has been a crucial water source, fishery, and transportation hub for the Sahel.
- Fed primarily by the Chari and Logone rivers, which originate in the southern highlands of Cameroon and Chad.
- Supports over 30 million people who depend on it for drinking water, fishing, livestock watering, and agriculture.
- The lake's shrinkage has intensified regional conflict over water rights and has destabilized communities dependent on its resources.
- Named *Chad*—from the Kanuri word "Tsade" meaning "large body of water"—which became the English name for the nation.
