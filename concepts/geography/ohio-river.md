# Ohio River

## summary

The *Ohio River* is a major tributary of the Mississippi River, flowing approximately 980 miles from Pennsylvania to Kentucky, draining the Appalachian region and industrial heartland of America. It formed the traditional boundary between the Ohio River Valley's northern settlement and the southern frontier during American expansion.

## you gotta know

- Originates at the confluence of the Allegheny and Monongahela Rivers in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, from tributaries flowing out of the Appalachian highlands.
- Historically marked the northern limit of southern slavery in pre-Civil War America, serving as a geographic and cultural boundary.
- Flows through or borders West Virginia, Kentucky, Indiana, and Illinois, serving major industrial cities including Pittsburgh, Cincinnati, and Louisville.
- Named from the Iroquoian word *O-hi-yo*, meaning "great river" or "great water."
- Provides hydroelectric power, barge transportation, and water supply for millions of people in the upper Mississippi Valley and Ohio Valley regions.
- Forms a major transportation corridor with a series of locks and dams that allow commercial navigation despite natural rapids and falls.
- The *Ohio River Flooding* of 1937 was one of the worst natural disasters in American history, inundating entire communities along its banks.
