# Delaware River

## summary

The *Delaware River* flows approximately 330 miles from the Catskill Mountains of New York to Delaware Bay and the Atlantic Ocean, forming the boundary between New York and Pennsylvania, and between Pennsylvania and New Jersey. Though modest in length, it has been historically significant for early American settlement, commerce, and the founding of the nation.

## you gotta know

- Originates in the Catskill Mountains of southeastern New York, from two main branches that converge near Hancock, New York.
- Flows generally southeastward, serving as the boundary between several northeastern states for most of its course.
- Named after *Thomas West, Lord De La Warr*, the early colonial governor of Virginia (though the river itself is in the Mid-Atlantic region).
- Historically crucial for shipping, mills, and early settlements; *Philadelphia* and *Trenton* developed on or near its banks as major colonial and early American cities.
- The site of *George Washington's* famous 1776 crossing during the American Revolutionary War, a turning point in the conflict.
- Forms a relatively narrow, scenic gorge through the Appalachian region known as the *Delaware Water Gap*, now a major recreational destination.
- Heavily polluted in the 20th century; significant restoration efforts have improved water quality and habitat, though industrial legacy persists.
