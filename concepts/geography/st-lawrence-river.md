# St. Lawrence River

## summary

The *St. Lawrence River* flows approximately 750 miles from Lake Ontario to the Gulf of St. Lawrence in eastern Canada, serving as a crucial shipping corridor linking the Great Lakes to the Atlantic Ocean. It is shared between Canada and the United States and forms a central artery for commerce and hydropower in eastern North America.

## you gotta know

- Originates at the outflow of *Lake Ontario* near Kingston, drawing water from all five Great Lakes.
- Named by early French explorers, as *Saint Laurent* after Saint Lawrence, the Christian martyr.
- Descends about 280 feet from Lake Ontario to sea level, with *Niagara Falls* being the most dramatic drop, shared with the shorter Niagara River to the west.
- Connected to the Atlantic Ocean via the *Saint Lawrence Seaway*, a system of locks built in the 1950s that allows ocean-going ships to travel deep into North America.
- Forms the boundary between Canada and the United States for much of its course, with several islands (including *Thousand Islands*) and hydroelectric dams.
- Historically crucial for fur trading and early European settlement in North America.
- Freezes in winter, requiring icebreakers to maintain year-round navigation.
