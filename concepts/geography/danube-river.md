# Danube River

## summary

The *Danube* is Europe's second-longest river, flowing 1,771 miles from Germany's Black Forest through ten countries to the Black Sea. It is the continent's most internationally important waterway, connecting central Europe to the Balkans and the east, and its strategic position shaped empires from Rome to the Austro-Hungarian realm.

## you gotta know

- Originates in Germany's Black Forest mountains and flows southeast, forming the border between several nations, including Hungary-Slovakia and Serbia-Romania.
- Served as Rome's northern frontier; the Danube limes (fortified border) marked the Roman Empire's limit against Germanic and later nomadic tribes.
- The Danube gorge between Serbia and Romania (the Iron Gate) is one of Europe's most dramatic landscapes and historically a major transportation bottleneck.
- Vienna, Budapest, and Belgrade are major cities built on the Danube's banks; medieval and early modern central European power depended on Danube control.
- The Danube delta in Romania is Europe's second-largest river delta, a vast wetland sanctuary for migratory birds and biodiversity.
- Subject to international water management; the 1948 Danube Convention and later treaties govern riparian rights and navigation among upstream and downstream nations.

## connections

- [[germany]] — the Danube originates in German territory and is central to German-Austrian relations.
- [[austria]] — Vienna's founding and power relied on Danube commerce and defensibility.
- [[hungary]] — Budapest and the Danube valley are inseparable from Hungarian history.
- [[ottoman-empire]] — controlled significant Danube territory; Danube crossings were sites of major Ottoman wars.
- [[balkans]] — the river separates and connects the Balkan peninsula to central Europe.
