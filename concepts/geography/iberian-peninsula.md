# Iberian Peninsula

## summary

The *Iberian Peninsula* is the southwestern corner of Europe, containing the modern nations of *Spain* and *Portugal* (plus a small part of France). Home to multiple civilizations—Lusitanians, Romans, Visigoths, Moors—the peninsula was the stage for centuries of cultural, religious, and military conflict, particularly the *Reconquista*, the Christian reconquest of territories ruled by Islamic *Moorish* kingdoms. The peninsula's unique blending of Christian and Islamic cultures produced distinctive architecture, literature, and traditions; it later became the base for European global exploration and empire.

## you gotta know

- The Iberian Peninsula was inhabited by indigenous *Lusitanians* and *Celtiberians* before Roman conquest in the second century BCE, becoming the Roman provinces of *Lusitania* (modern Portugal) and *Hispania* (modern Spain).
- Following Rome's collapse, the peninsula was conquered by the *Visigoths* (Germanic Christian kingdom) in the early fifth century, establishing kingdoms that ruled until the early eighth century.
- The *Umayyad Caliphate* invaded the peninsula in 711 CE, rapidly conquering Visigothic kingdoms and establishing *Islamic rule* in what became known as *Al-Andalus*, a sophisticated Islamic civilization enduring until the fifteenth century.
- *Al-Andalus* was renowned for cultural and scientific achievement: *Córdoba* became one of the world's greatest cities with libraries, philosophers, mathematicians, and poets; *Toledo* was a center of translation and intellectual exchange.
- The *Reconquista* (Christian reconquest) began in the ninth century as Christian kingdoms in the north gradually reconquered territory from Moorish (North African Islamic) kingdoms, a process lasting nearly 800 years.
- By the thirteenth century, only the kingdom of *Granada* remained under Islamic rule; the *Reconquista* culminated in 1492 when the *Catholic Monarchs* *Ferdinand* and *Isabella* conquered Granada, ending Islamic rule on the peninsula.
- The *Alhambra*, Granada's magnificent Islamic palace, exemplifies the architectural and artistic sophistication of Islamic Al-Andalus and remains one of the world's great monuments.
- The blending of Christian and Islamic cultures created distinctive *Iberian* or *Spanish* culture: architecture combined Moorish and Christian elements; language incorporated Arabic words; literature and philosophy synthesized Christian and Islamic traditions.
- In 1492, the same year Granada fell, *Christopher Columbus* sailed for the Americas under the sponsorship of Ferdinand and Isabella, initiating Spanish exploration and colonial empire across the Atlantic.
- Major cities—*Madrid* (Spain's capital), *Barcelona*, *Lisbon* (Portugal's capital), *Seville*, *Granada*—became centers of culture, commerce, and imperial power.
