# Lake Tanganyika

## summary

*Lake Tanganyika* is Africa's deepest and second-largest lake by volume, stretching approximately 420 miles along the border of Tanzania, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Zambia, and Burundi. Its unique ancient ecosystem and extraordinary depth make it one of the world's most important freshwater systems.

## you gotta know

- The world's longest freshwater lake and the second-deepest, after Lake Baikal in Siberia; the average depth is over 2,300 feet.
- Located in the East African Rift Valley, formed by tectonic forces that continue to deepen it at a rate of roughly one meter per century.
- Contains over 1,000 endemic fish species, many found nowhere else on Earth, making it a living laboratory for studying evolution and adaptation.
- Serves as a vital transportation route and food source for over 10 million people across four nations.
- The lake is unusually clear and oxygenated at depth due to specific thermal stratification, supporting life in its deep waters.
- First scientifically explored by European explorers Richard Burton and John Hanning Speke in 1858.
- Named *Tanganyika* from the Bantu word meaning "the spreading wide-spreading lake."
