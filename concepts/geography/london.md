## summary

London is the capital and largest city of the United Kingdom, situated on the River Thames in southeastern England. Founded by the Romans as *Londinium*, it grew into a medieval trading center and became the political and commercial heart of the British Empire. London has been the setting for countless literary works, is home to the Palace of Westminster and Big Ben, and remains one of the world's most influential cities culturally, financially, and politically.

## you gotta know

- London was founded by the Romans around 47 CE as *Londinium*; after Roman withdrawal, it developed into a major Anglo-Saxon and then medieval trading city on the Thames.
- The *Tower of London*, built by William the Conqueror in 1066, served as royal palace, fortress, and prison; it houses the Crown Jewels and remains a symbol of royal authority.
- *Big Ben*, officially the Great Bell and (now) Elizabeth Tower, is the iconic clock tower at the Palace of Westminster, completed in 1859 and recognized worldwide as a symbol of London and Britain.
- London became the center of the *British Empire* and grew explosively during the *Industrial Revolution*; by the 19th century it was the world's largest city and financial capital.
- *Charles Dickens* (1812–1870), the preeminent Victorian novelist, set much of his work in London and used the city to expose social inequality, poverty, and human resilience.
- *Arthur Conan Doyle's* fictional detective *Sherlock Holmes*, set in Victorian London, became one of literature's most iconic characters and defined the detective genre.
- London survived the *Blitz*, Nazi aerial bombardment during World War II (1940–1941), which destroyed large sections but demonstrated British resilience; the city rebuilt and remains a major cultural and financial center.
