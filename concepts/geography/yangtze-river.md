# Yangtze River

## summary

The **Yangtze River** is the longest river in Asia and the third longest in the world, flowing approximately 3,915 kilometers (2,432 miles) across central China from its source in the Tibetan Plateau to the East China Sea. It is the economic and cultural lifeline of China, supporting over one-third of the country's population and serving as a major transportation artery for millennia.

## you gotta know

- Asia's longest river, spanning nearly 4,000 km from the Tibetan highlands eastward across China to Shanghai.
- Forms a natural division between northern and southern China, historically shaping distinct regions, cultures, and cuisines.
- Home to the *Three Gorges*, a dramatic section of deep canyons that inspired classical Chinese poetry and painting.
- The *Three Gorges Dam*, completed in 2006, is the world's largest hydroelectric project, creating a 600 km reservoir.
- Major cities dot its course: *Chongqing*, *Wuhan*, *Nanjing*, and *Shanghai*, making it crucial for inland commerce.
- The *Yangtze dolphin* (baiji), a nearly-blind freshwater cetacean found only here, was functionally extinct by the 2000s.
- Subject to devastating floods that have shaped Chinese history; dike systems built and maintained for over 2,000 years.
