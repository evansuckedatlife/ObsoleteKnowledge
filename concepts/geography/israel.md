# Israel

## summary

Israel is a country in western Asia at the eastern shore of the Mediterranean Sea, occupying a narrow strip of land between Lebanon and Egypt known historically as the Levant. Established as a Jewish state in 1948 following the Holocaust and decades of Zionist migration, Israel is the only Jewish-majority nation in the world. Its creation displaced hundreds of thousands of Palestinians and led to a conflict that has defined Middle Eastern politics for over 75 years.

## you gotta know

- Israel's territory includes the coastal plains of the Mediterranean, the Jordan River valley to the east, and the Negev Desert to the south; it also occupies the *West Bank* and *Gaza Strip*, territories disputed since the 1967 war.
- *Judaism*, one of the world's oldest monotheistic religions, originated in ancient Israel over 3,000 years ago; the *Torah*, Judaism's central text, recounts the history of the Jewish people in the land.
- The *Zionist movement*, emerging in the late 19th century, advocated for a Jewish homeland in response to antisemitism in Europe; mass Jewish immigration to Palestine increased after the *Holocaust* (1939–1945).
- Israel was established on May 14, 1948, immediately after the British Mandate ended; the declaration of independence triggered the *Arab-Israeli War* (1948–1949), with over 700,000 Palestinians fleeing or being expelled.
- *Jerusalem*, the capital claimed by Israel, is one of the world's holiest cities, sacred to Judaism, Christianity, and Islam; its status remains the central point of Israeli-Palestinian dispute.
- Israel's economy is highly developed, with major strengths in technology, agriculture, and diamonds; it has the highest per-capita number of startups of any nation.
- The *Arab-Israeli conflict* has involved multiple wars (1948, 1956, 1967, 1973), decades of Palestinian uprisings (*intifadas*), and ongoing settlement expansion that the international community considers illegal.
- Israel is home to the *Dead Sea*, the world's lowest elevation on land at 430 meters below sea level, and the *Sea of Galilee*, significant to Christian and Jewish history.
