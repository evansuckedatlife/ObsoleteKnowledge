## summary

**Daniel** was an Israelite prophet and statesman who served foreign kings during the Babylonian exile. Known for his unwavering faith even in the face of death, he survived a night in a *lion's den* and interpreted visions and dreams for kings. His book is the only substantial prophetic work in Hebrew scripture written primarily in Aramaic, and it contains visions of future kingdoms and the end times.

## you gotta know

- He was taken captive to *Babylon* as a young man during the exile of Judah, along with other noble youths.
- He rose to prominence by interpreting dreams and visions for King *Nebuchadnezzar* and later for other Babylonian rulers.
- He refused to eat the royal food and wine, insisting on a vegetarian diet, yet he thrived and surpassed his peers in wisdom and beauty.
- He was thrown into a *lion's den* as punishment for praying to God rather than obeying a decree to pray to the king—God closed the lions' mouths and he emerged unharmed.
- His three companions—*Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego*—were cast into a fiery furnace for refusing to worship a golden image, yet they walked out unburned.
- He received visions of four great kingdoms that would rule the earth, using symbolic imagery of beasts and metals to depict future political powers.
- He spoke of the "*Son of Man*," a messianic figure who would come in glory—language that later influenced New Testament descriptions of Jesus.
- His life and prophecies are recorded in the book of *Daniel*.

## connections

- [[babylon]] — the kingdom where he lived and served as an exile.
- [[nebuchadnezzar]] — the king whose dreams and visions he interpreted.
- [[torah]] — the law and covenant he upheld even in foreign lands.
- [[judaism]] — his faithfulness became a model of resistance and fidelity to God under persecution.
- [[messianic-hope]] — his visions contributed to Jewish expectations of a coming deliverer.
