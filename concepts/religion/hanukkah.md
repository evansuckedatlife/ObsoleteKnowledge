## summary

Hanukkah, the *Festival of Lights*, is an eight-day celebration beginning on the 25th day of Kislev (November or December). It commemorates the *rededication of the Second Temple* following the Maccabean revolt against Greek-Syrian rule in the 2nd century BCE. The holiday celebrates Jewish religious freedom and the triumph of a small resistance movement against a powerful empire.

## you gotta know

- The *menorah* (eight-branched candelabrum) is lit each night, with one additional candle lit each successive evening, symbolizing increasing light and hope.
- A *hanukkiah* (Hanukkah menorah) has nine branches — eight for the holiday nights and one for the *shamash* (helper candle) used to light the others.
- The *miracle of the oil* — that one day's worth of oil burned for eight days in the rededicated Temple — is the spiritual core of the holiday.
- *Dreidels* (four-sided spinning tops) bearing Hebrew letters are played as games, a tradition originating from the period when Jewish study was forbidden.
- *Latkes* (fried potato pancakes) and *sufganiyot* (jelly-filled donuts) are traditional foods, celebrating the oil's role in both the miracle and the holiday's cuisine.
- The holiday is relatively minor in traditional Jewish law but has become culturally significant, partly due to its proximity to Christmas.
- Hanukkah is not a biblical holiday; it was established by the rabbis to commemorate events recorded in the Books of Maccabees.
- Unlike most Jewish holidays, there is no prohibition on work or school during Hanukkah in traditional observance.
