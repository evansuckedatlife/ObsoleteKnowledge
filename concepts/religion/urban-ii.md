## summary

Reigning as pope from 1088 to 1099, **Urban II** (originally Odo of Châtillon) was a key administrative reformer who continued the Gregorian Reforms. He is most famous for launching the First Crusade at the Council of Clermont, which mobilized Western European nobility to capture Jerusalem from Muslim rule. His pontificate established the papacy as a central political force capable of coordinating international military actions.

## you gotta know

- *Preached the sermon at the Council of Clermont* in 1095 that launched the First Crusade, calling upon Western European Christians to reclaim the Holy Land with the famous rallying cry *"Deus vult"* ("God wills it").
- *Centralized the papal administration* by establishing the Roman Curia, organizing the treasury and papal chancery into a highly efficient bureaucracy.
- *Responded to Byzantine Emperor Alexios I Komnenos*, who requested aid against the Seljuk Turks, aiming to restore relations and potentially reunite the Eastern and Western Churches.
- *Excommunicated King Philip I of France* for unlawful marriage after the king repudiated his wife Bertha of Holland to marry Bertrade de Montfort.
- Died in July 1099, just two weeks after crusader forces captured Jerusalem, but before news of the victory could reach Rome.

## connections

- [[peter]] — the apostle and first Bishop of Rome whose seat Urban II occupied.
- [[gregory-vii]] — his predecessor and mentor; Urban II was a staunch supporter of Hildebrand's reforms and sought to continue his policies.
- [[innocent-iii]] — a later pope who built upon Urban's crusading legacy by launching the Fourth Crusade.
- [[first-crusade]] — the military campaign launched by Urban II's Clermont sermon.
- [[byzantine-empire]] — which Urban II sought to defend and reconcile with the Latin Church.
