# Mahavira

## summary

Mahavira, meaning "great hero," was the 24th and final *Tirthankara* (ford-maker) of *Jainism*, who revitalized and systematized the ancient Jain tradition in 6th century BCE India. His ascetic teachings and emphasis on absolute non-violence and truth established Jainism as a major philosophical and religious force in India and beyond.

## you gotta know

- Mahavira was born as Vardhamana, a prince in what is now Bihar, India, around 599 BCE and renounced his princely life at age 30 to pursue spiritual asceticism.
- He spent 12 years in extreme self-denial and meditation, eventually achieving *kevala jnana* (omniscience), the perfect knowledge that defines a Tirthankara.
- His teachings emphasize *Mahatma* (the great soul) through the practice of the *Mahavratas* (five great vows): non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, chastity, and detachment.
- *Ahimsa* (non-violence toward all living beings) is the cornerstone of Jain ethics and distinguishes Jainism from other Indian religious traditions.
- Mahavira taught that every soul is eternal and capable of achieving liberation through rigorous asceticism and moral discipline, regardless of caste or birth.
- He established the Jain monastic community (*Sangha*) with strict codes of conduct that remain influential in Jain practice today.
- The *Jina* (conqueror) title applied to him reflects his spiritual victory over worldly attachments and ignorance.
- His life and teachings are recorded in Jain texts such as the *Bhagavati Sutra* and celebrated annually on *Mahavira Jayanti*.
