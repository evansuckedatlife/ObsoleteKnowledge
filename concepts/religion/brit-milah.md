# Brit milah


<!-- foundations -->
> [!note]+ Foundations — read these first
> [[judaism]]
<!-- /foundations -->

## summary

Brit milah is the *circumcision ceremony* that welcomes a newborn Jewish boy into the covenant community. Performed on the *eighth day* of life, it marks the child's formal entry into the Jewish tradition and fulfills the covenant made with Abraham.

## you gotta know

- The ceremony takes place on the *eighth day* after birth, even if that falls on *Shabbat* or a holiday, emphasizing its religious importance.
- A trained *mohel* (Jewish ritual circumciser) performs the procedure, followed by a blessing and a festive meal called a *seudah*.
- The ritual symbolizes the *Covenant of Abraham*, the foundational agreement between God and the Jewish people.
- Traditionally, the baby receives his Hebrew name during the brit milah ceremony, which may differ from his secular name.
- Guests are invited to witness and celebrate the *simchat bris* (joy of the covenant), treating attendance as a valued mitzvah.
- The procedure is considered obligatory in Orthodox and Conservative Judaism, though Reform Judaism offers alternatives like a naming ceremony without circumcision.
- A *kvater* (godparents) traditionally carry the baby to the ceremony, and the *sandek* (godfather) holds the child during the procedure.
- The Hebrew blessing recited after the circumcision expresses gratitude for God's commandments and the privilege of entering the covenant.
