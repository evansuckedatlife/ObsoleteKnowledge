# Torah

## summary

The **Torah** is the central and most sacred text of Judaism, comprising the first five books of the Hebrew Bible: Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy. Also known as the *Pentateuch* or the Five Books of Moses, it contains the foundational narrative of the Jewish people, cosmic origins and divine law, as well as the 613 commandments (*mitzvot*) that govern Jewish practice and ethics. Believed by traditional Judaism to have been revealed by God to *Moses* at Mount Sinai, it forms the bedrock of Jewish theology, law, and identity. The Torah's status as the most authoritative Jewish text—above even the *Talmud*—shapes its central role in Jewish ritual, study, and spiritual life. Its narratives and laws have profoundly influenced Western culture, law, and ethics through Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

## you gotta know

### Content and Structure
- Composed of five books: *Genesis* (creation and patriarchs), *Exodus* (slavery and liberation), *Leviticus* (priestly law), *Numbers* (wilderness wandering), *Deuteronomy* (Moses's final teachings).
- Contains 613 commandments (*mitzvot*) divided into positive (do this) and negative (do not do this) precepts, covering ritual, ethical, and civil law.
- The narrative arc spans from creation through *Abraham*, *Isaac*, *Jacob*, and *Moses*, concluding with Moses's death on the brink of the Promised Land.

### Transmission and Reading
- Transmitted orally for centuries before written transcription; in synagogue, read from a *Sefer Torah*, a scroll hand-written on kosher parchment by a trained scribe (*sofer*).
- The scroll is stored in the *Holy Ark* (Aron Kodesh) and handled with a pointer (*yad*) to avoid touching the sacred parchment.
- Divided into 54 weekly portions (*parashot*), completing the annual cycle on *Simchat Torah* ("Rejoicing in the Torah").

### Theology and Criticism
- Traditional Judaism holds that *Moses* received both the Written Torah and the *Oral Torah* (interpretive traditions later recorded in the *Talmud*) from God at Mount Sinai.
- The *Documentary Hypothesis*, a major school of biblical criticism, proposes that the Torah was compiled from four source texts (Jahwist, Elohist, Deuteronomist, Priestly), dated to the 9th–5th centuries BCE.

## context

The Torah's role in Jewish practice extends far beyond textual study: it is ceremonially elevated in ritual, read liturgically in cycles, and serves as the basis for all Jewish law and custom. The reverence for textual interpretation itself—the rabbinical tradition of *midrash* and *pilpul* (dialectical argument)—makes Torah study a spiritual act, not mere scholarship. The text's influence radiates outward: the *Ten Commandments* form the basis of Western legal codes; the narrative of the *Exodus* became a metaphor for liberation in political and literary tradition; and the Torah's ethical and monotheistic vision shaped both Christianity and Islam.
