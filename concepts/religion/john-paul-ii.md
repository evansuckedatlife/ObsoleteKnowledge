## summary

Reigning as pope from 1978 to 2005, **John Paul II** (born Karol Wojtyła) was the first non-Italian pope in 455 years and the second-longest reigning pope in history. One of the most traveled world leaders in history, he used his global influence to support human rights and speak out against political oppression. His support for the Solidarity trade union in his native Poland played a key role in the collapse of communism in Eastern Europe.

## you gotta know

- *Helped dismantle Soviet-bloc communism* in Eastern Europe, particularly in his native Poland, where his 1979 visit and public support energized the anti-communist *Solidarity* movement.
- *Survived a 1981 assassination attempt* in St. Peter's Square when he was shot by Turkish gunman *Mehmet Ali Ağca*, whom he later visited in prison and forgave.
- *Traveled extensively*, visiting 129 countries during his pontificate and establishing *World Youth Day* in 1985 to foster youth ministry.
- *Issued the Catechism of the Catholic Church* in 1992, the first comprehensive codification of Catholic belief in over four centuries, and delivered the series of lectures known as the *Theology of the Body*.
- *Apologized for the historical errors of the Catholic Church* during the Jubilee Year 2000, including the trial of Galileo, the Crusades, the Inquisition, and Christian involvement in the Holocaust.

## connections

- [[peter]] — the apostle and first Bishop of Rome, whose tomb John Paul II's grave lies near in the Vatican grottoes.
- [[innocent-iii]] — a predecessor who authorized the Crusades and the Inquisition, historical actions for which John Paul II formally apologized.
- [[gregory-i]] — a predecessor who, like John Paul II, sent out widespread missions to expand the Church's reach.
