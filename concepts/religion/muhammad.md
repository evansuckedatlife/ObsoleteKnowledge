# Muhammad

## summary

Muhammad was the founding prophet of *Islam*, a religion that emerged in 7th-century Arabia and became one of the world's largest faiths. Believed by Muslims to be the final messenger of God (*Allah*), his life, teachings, and spiritual experiences laid the foundation for a civilization, legal system, and moral framework that continues to guide over a billion people today.

## you gotta know

- Muhammad was born around 570 CE in Mecca to the Quraysh tribe and became a merchant known for his honesty before receiving his prophetic calling at age 40.
- He received revelations from the angel Gabriel (Jibril) over approximately 23 years, which his followers compiled into the *Qur'an*, Islam's central scripture.
- The *Qur'an* teaches strict monotheism (*Tawhid*), the unity of God, rejecting polytheism and idolatry, which was central to Arabian religious practice before Islam.
- His teachings emphasize the *Five Pillars*: the declaration of faith (*Shahada*), prayer (*Salah*), almsgiving (*Zakat*), fasting during Ramadan (*Sawm*), and pilgrimage to Mecca (*Hajj*).
- He initially preached in Mecca, facing persecution from the Quraysh, and migrated to Medina in 622 CE (*Hijra*), an event marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
- In Medina, he established the first Islamic community and state, creating the *Ummah* (community of believers) that transcended tribal and ethnic boundaries.
- He is known in Islamic tradition as *Khatam al-Nabiyyin* ("the Seal of the Prophets"), meaning the final messenger and completion of God's revelation through Moses, Jesus, and other prophets.
- After his death in 632 CE, his companions preserved his sayings and practices in the *Hadith*, forming the basis for Islamic law (*Sharia*) and ethics.
