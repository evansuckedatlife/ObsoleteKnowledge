# Good Samaritan

## summary

The Good Samaritan is a parable told by Jesus about a Samaritan who stops to help a wounded traveler, while others—a priest and a Levite—pass by. It teaches compassion, mercy, and kindness toward strangers and those in need, especially those considered "outsiders" or enemies.

## you gotta know

- Jesus tells this parable in *Luke 10:25–37* in response to a question about who qualifies as one's "neighbor."
- A man is beaten and left dying on a road; a priest and a Levite (both respectable religious figures) see him but pass by without helping.
- A Samaritan—a member of a people despised and hostile to Jews—stops, tends the man's wounds, pays for his care, and ensures his recovery.
- Jesus concludes by saying the Samaritan showed true neighborliness, implicitly criticizing the priest and Levite for their indifference.
- The parable's power lies in the *reversal*: the "good" person is an outsider and former enemy, not the religious establishment.
- A "Good Samaritan" today means someone who helps a stranger or person in need, often at personal cost or inconvenience; "Good Samaritan laws" protect people who render emergency aid.
