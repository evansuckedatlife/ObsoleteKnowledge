# the sweat of your brow


<!-- foundations -->
> [!note]+ Foundations — read these first
> [[hebrew-bible]]
<!-- /foundations -->

## summary

This phrase comes from *Genesis*, where God condemns Adam to labor as punishment for eating the forbidden fruit. It means that humans must work hard and struggle to earn their livelihood, that survival requires effort and toil.

## you gotta know

- Used today to emphasize the value of hard work and honest labor, often in contrast to unearned wealth or idleness.
- The phrase appears in *Genesis 3:19*, part of God's curse upon Adam after he ate from the Tree of Knowledge.
- The curse was literal: Adam would have to till the soil and work the earth to produce food, sweating in the process.
- It reflects the theological idea that labor itself became a human burden after the Fall, separating humanity from the ease of Eden.
- The phrase often carries a sense of *dignity* in hard work—many cultures and religions have elevated physical labor as morally necessary.
- "By the sweat of thy brow shalt thou eat bread" became a motto for work ethic across Western civilization.
