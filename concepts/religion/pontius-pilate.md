# Pontius Pilate

## summary

The Roman prefect of Judea who presided over Jesus's trial and ultimately authorized his *crucifixion*. Though ambivalent about Jesus's guilt, Pilate yielded to pressure from Jewish religious authorities and the crowd, becoming the figure legally responsible for Jesus's execution in the eyes of the New Testament.

## you gotta know

- *Roman prefect of Judea* during Jesus's ministry, holding civil and military authority.
- *Interrogated Jesus*, asking "What is truth?" in a moment that encapsulates philosophical distance.
- *Declared Jesus innocent three times* in John's Gospel, yet still condemned him to appease the crowd.
- *Washed his hands* of Jesus's blood in Matthew's account, a gesture symbolizing refusal of personal responsibility.
- His wife *warned him against condemning Jesus*, claiming a troubling dream, mentioned only in Matthew.
- *Authorized the Crucifixion* under pressure from Jewish leaders who threatened to report him to Caesar for harboring sedition.
- His *inscription on the cross*—"Jesus of Nazareth, King of the Jews"—became a permanent record of the charge against Jesus.
- Historical sources confirm his *governorship from 26–36 CE* and his reputation for brutal suppression of dissent.
