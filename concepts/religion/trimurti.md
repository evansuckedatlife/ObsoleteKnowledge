# Trimurti

## summary

The Trimurti is the triple deity of supreme divinity in Hinduism, in which the cosmic functions of creation, maintenance, and destruction are personified as a triad of deities. Typically, these are Brahma the creator, Vishnu the preserver, and Shiva the destroyer. Together, they represent the cyclical nature of the universe and are worshipped as different aspects of the same ultimate reality (Brahman).

## you gotta know

- The triad represents the three fundamental forces of the cosmos: *creation* (associated with Brahma), *preservation* (associated with Vishnu), and *destruction/transformation* (associated with Shiva).
- The concept is often depicted as a single three-headed figure representing the three gods united as one supreme being, notably in the colossal *Trimurti sculpture* at the Elephanta Caves near Mumbai.
- It is closely mirrored by the *Tridevi*, the trinity of supreme goddesses comprising Saraswati (creator), Lakshmi (preserver), and Parvati/Kali (destroyer), who act as the consorts and active energies (*shakti*) of the Trimurti.
- In Hindu philosophy, particularly Advaita Vedanta, the three gods are viewed as manifestations of the formless, absolute reality known as *Brahman*.
- The Puranic literature contains various stories explaining how the three deities emerged from a single primordial source or competed to establish their supremacy.
