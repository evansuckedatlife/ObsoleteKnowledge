## summary

**Samson** was an Israelite judge whose extraordinary physical strength made him a folk hero and champion against Philistine oppression. Born under a special *Nazirite vow* that prohibited him from cutting his hair, his strength lay in his uncut locks—a sacred symbol of his covenant with God. His tragic life illustrates the consequences of pride and betrayal, culminating in his dramatic final act of destruction.

## you gotta know

- His strength came from his uncut hair, a sign of his *Nazirite* dedication to God from birth—he could tear lions apart and carry away city gates.
- He was betrayed by *Delilah*, the woman he loved, who discovered the secret of his strength and handed him over to the Philistines.
- His eyes were gouged out by the Philistines, and he was enslaved to grind grain in their prison.
- In his final act, blind and weakened, he pushed apart two pillars supporting the temple of Dagon, bringing the building down and killing more Philistines than he had in his lifetime.
- He served as one of the judges of Israel, a period before monarchy when God raised up leaders to rescue the people from enemies.
- His story emphasizes the tension between God's empowerment and human weakness, strength coupled with moral failing.
- His life is recorded in the book of *Judges*, chapters 13–16.
