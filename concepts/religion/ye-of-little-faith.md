# ye of little faith

## summary

This phrase, spoken by Jesus to his followers, expresses disappointment or gentle rebuke for lack of trust in God. It means doubting God's power or providence, or failing to have confidence in divine care and protection during difficult times.

## you gotta know

- Jesus uses this phrase repeatedly in the Gospels (*Matthew* and *Mark*) when his disciples show fear or doubt during crises.
- In *Matthew 14:31*, when Peter begins to sink while walking on water toward Jesus, Jesus catches him and says "O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt?"
- In *Matthew 8:26*, when the disciples panic during a storm, Jesus calms the waters and rebukes them for their fear and lack of faith.
- The phrase highlights the contrast between Jesus' certainty in God's presence and the disciples' anxiety despite witnessing miracles.
- "Little faith" does not mean *no* faith, but insufficient faith—a partial trust that wavers when circumstances become frightening.
- Modern usage: "ye of little faith" gently mocks someone for doubting something that should be obvious or for worrying unnecessarily despite reassurance.
