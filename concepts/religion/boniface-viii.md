# Boniface VIII

## summary

Reigning as pope from 1294 to 1303, **Boniface VIII** (born Benedetto Caetani) is remembered for his extreme claims of papal supremacy and his disastrous conflicts with the rising nation-states of Europe, particularly France. His papacy marks the beginning of the decline of medieval papal political hegemony. His arrest by French forces at Anagni shortly before his death paved the way for the Avignon Papacy.

## you gotta know

- *Issued the papal bull Unam Sanctam* in 1302, which asserted absolute papal supremacy, declaring that both spiritual and temporal powers are under the Pope's authority and that submission to the Pope is necessary for salvation.
- *Instituted the first Jubilee or Holy Year* in 1300, offering a plenary indulgence to thousands of Christian pilgrims who visited Rome.
- *Engaged in a bitter taxation conflict with King Philip IV of France*, issuing the bull *Clericis Laicos* in 1296 to forbid secular rulers from taxing the clergy without papal permission.
- *Captured and assaulted at his palace in Anagni* in 1303 by French soldiers led by Guillaume de Nogaret and the Colonna family (the "Outrage of Anagni"), dying in Rome weeks later.
- *Placed in Dante's Inferno* before his death, with the poet depicting him in the eighth circle of Hell among the simoniacs (those who bought/sold church offices).
