## summary

Passover, known in Hebrew as *Pesach*, is an eight-day festival beginning on the 15th of Nisan (March or April). It commemorates the *Exodus from Egypt* and the *liberation of the Israelites* from slavery. The holiday centers on the eating of *matzah* (unleavened bread) and the retelling of the Exodus story, emphasizing themes of freedom, redemption, and divine providence.

## you gotta know

- The *seder* is a ceremonial meal featuring symbolic foods — bitter herbs (maror), charoset (a sweet paste), an egg, lamb shank, and matzah — each representing aspects of the Exodus story.
- *Matzah* (unleavened bread) is eaten throughout the holiday, recalling the haste with which the Israelites left Egypt and had no time for bread to rise.
- The *Haggadah* is the text recited during the seder, telling the Passover story in prayers, blessings, and songs, with traditions varying across different Jewish communities.
- *Chametz* (leavened products) is completely forbidden; the week before Passover involves a thorough removal of all leavened foods from the home.
- The holiday is one of the three pilgrimage festivals in biblical tradition, when Jews would journey to the Temple in Jerusalem.
- *Four cups of wine* are drunk during the seder, representing the four expressions of redemption in the biblical narrative.
- The *plague of the firstborn* is the final plague, after which Pharaoh releases the Israelites; Passover commemorates both the plague and the salvation of Jewish firstborns.
- Passover is deeply tied to concepts of freedom, making it a symbol of liberation movements and human dignity across cultures and centuries.
