## summary

A friend of Jesus from the village of Bethany who died and was *raised from the dead by Jesus* in one of the most dramatic miracles in the New Testament. His resurrection became a powerful sign of Jesus's authority over death and a pivotal moment in the Gospel narrative.

## you gotta know

- *Brother of Martha and Mary*, living in Bethany near Jerusalem.
- *Died and was buried* while Jesus was away, his body in a tomb for four days before Jesus's arrival.
- *Called from the dead by Jesus* in the famous moment where Jesus cried out "Lazarus, come out!"—one of scripture's most dramatic sentences.
- Emerged from the tomb *still bound in burial cloths*, requiring others to unbind him, symbolizing incomplete resurrection until the Resurrection itself.
- Jesus's *raising of Lazarus* was a defining sign of his divine power and the *fulfillment of resurrection themes*.
- This *public miracle* became a turning point in the Gospels, intensifying both belief and opposition to Jesus.
- The *chief priests plotted to kill both Jesus and Lazarus* after witnessing this extraordinary display of power.
- His story *appears in the Gospel of John* as a climactic sign before Jesus's own Passion and Resurrection.
