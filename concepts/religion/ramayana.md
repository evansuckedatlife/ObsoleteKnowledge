## summary

The Ramayana is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, alongside the Mahabharata. Traditionally attributed to the sage Valmiki, the epic consists of 24,000 verses divided into seven books (*kandas*). It narrates the life and adventures of Prince Rama, an avatar of Vishnu, whose wife Sita is abducted by the demon king Ravana, serving as a foundational text that explores the concepts of dharma, devotion, and ideal relationships.

## you gotta know

- The epic is traditionally authored by the sage *Valmiki*, who is revered in Hindu tradition as the *Adi Kavi* (the first poet).
- The central narrative begins with Prince Rama's exile to the Dandaka Forest for fourteen years, engineered by his stepmother Kaikeyi to place her son Bharata on the throne of Ayodhya.
- The inciting incident of the epic's conflict is the abduction of Rama's wife *Sita* by the ten-headed demon king *Ravana*, who carries her away to his island kingdom of Lanka.
- To rescue Sita, Rama forms an alliance with the monkey kingdom of Kishkindha, notably befriending the monkey god *Hanuman*, who leaps across the ocean to find Sita in captivity.
- The monkeys construct a floating bridge (*Rama Setu*) across the sea to Lanka, enabling Rama's army to invade the kingdom, slay Ravana, and rescue Sita.
- Upon returning to Ayodhya, Rama is crowned king, marking the beginning of the ideal era of righteous rule known as *Ramrajya*.
