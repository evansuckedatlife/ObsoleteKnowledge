# Peter

## summary

One of Jesus's most prominent apostles, originally a fisherman named **Simon** who became *the rock upon which Jesus built his church*. Known for his passionate personality, his temporary denial of Jesus, and his leadership of the early Christian community after the Resurrection.

## you gotta know

- *Chosen as the first apostle* by Jesus, who renamed him *Cephas* (Aramaic for "rock") or *Peter* (Greek equivalent).
- Jesus declared him *"the rock"* upon which he would build his church, making him the foundational apostle.
- *Denied knowing Jesus three times* the night of the Crucifixion, despite his earlier declaration of loyalty.
- Witness to the *Resurrection* and *Ascension*, becoming the leader of the apostles and the early Jerusalem church.
- *Performed miracles*, preached, and imprisoned multiple times in the Acts of the Apostles for spreading Jesus's message.
- In *Catholic tradition*, he is venerated as the first Pope and foundation of papal succession.
- Wrote *1 and 2 Peter*, epistles that address Christian suffering and faith.
- Traditional accounts describe his *execution by crucifixion* in Rome, reportedly upside-down at his own request.

## connections

- [[jesus]] — who called him and named him Peter.
- [[judas-iscariot]] — fellow apostle; Peter witnessed his betrayal and participated in the early church after Judas's death.
- [[paul]] — both became pillars of the early Christian movement and corresponded or met in Jerusalem.
- [[the-twelve-apostles]] — he was the acknowledged leader among the twelve.
