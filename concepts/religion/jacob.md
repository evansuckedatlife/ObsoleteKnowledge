# Jacob

## summary

The grandson of *Abraham* and son of *Isaac*, Jacob is remembered as the patriarch whose name was changed to **Israel**, meaning "he who strives with God." His twelve sons became the founders of the *twelve tribes of Israel*. A figure of cunning and transformation, Jacob's life story moves from deception to spiritual encounter, culminating in his covenant relationship with God.

## you gotta know

- *His name change to Israel* came after wrestling with a divine being at the Jabbok ford—a pivotal moment of spiritual wrestling and blessing.
- He tricked his twin brother *Esau* out of his birthright by trading lentil stew, and later deceived his father *Isaac* to steal Esau's blessing.
- His twelve sons (Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Dan, Naphtali, Gad, Asher, Issachar, Zebulun, Joseph, and Benjamin) became the twelve tribes that formed the nation of Israel.
- He worked fourteen years for his uncle Laban to marry his beloved wife Rachel, being tricked into first marrying Leah.
- His favorite son was *Joseph*, whose coat of many colors sparked jealousy among the other sons.
- Jacob's story is found primarily in the book of *Genesis*, chapters 25–50.
- He reconciled with his brother Esau after years of separation and conflict.

## connections

- [[abraham]] — his grandfather, the original patriarch and founder of the covenant.
- [[isaac]] — his father, whom he deceived for his blessing.
- [[esau]] — his twin brother, whose birthright and blessing he took.
- [[joseph]] — his favored son, whose exile set the stage for the family's journey to Egypt.
- [[moses]] — descended from Jacob through the Levite tribe.
- [[israel]] — the covenant name given to Jacob, forever linking his identity to the nation.
- [[torah]] — the primary source recording his life and spiritual journey.
