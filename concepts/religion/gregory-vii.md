## summary

Reigning as pope from 1073 to 1085, **Gregory VII** (originally named Hildebrand) was a zealous reformer who fought to establish the moral integrity and political independence of the papacy. He is best known for initiating the Gregorian Reforms and his epic struggle with Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV during the Investiture Controversy. His assertions of absolute papal authority permanently altered medieval European geopolitics.

## you gotta know

- *Issued the Dictatus Papae* in 1075, a compilation of 27 statements asserting absolute papal supremacy over both the Church and secular rulers, including the power to depose emperors.
- *Excommunicated Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV* over the right of secular rulers to appoint local church officials, initiating the decades-long *Investiture Controversy*.
- *Forced Henry IV to perform penance* by waiting barefoot in the snow for three days outside the gates of the Castle of *Canossa* in 1077 (the "Walk to Canossa") to have his excommunication lifted.
- *Died in exile* in Salerno in 1085 after Henry IV invaded Rome, famously uttering: "I have loved justice and hated iniquity; therefore, I die in exile."
- Campaigned aggressively against *simony* (the buying and selling of church offices) and strictly enforced clerical celibacy.
