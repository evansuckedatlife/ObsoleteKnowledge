## summary

One of the twelve apostles of Jesus, best known for his skepticism about the *Resurrection* until he encountered the risen Jesus himself. His story has made him an enduring symbol of doubt transformed into faith within Christian tradition.

## you gotta know

- *Doubted the Resurrection* when other apostles reported seeing the risen Jesus, demanding physical proof.
- Famous for declaring he would not believe *unless he saw Jesus himself and touched his wounds*.
- *Encountered the risen Jesus a week later* and immediately proclaimed absolute faith: "My Lord and my God!"
- Often called *"Doubting Thomas,"* though the Gospel emphasizes his transformation to complete conviction.
- Called *"Didymus"* in Greek (meaning "twin"), suggesting he had a twin, though the twin's identity is not mentioned.
- *Proposed going to Bethany to die with Jesus* when Jesus decided to raise Lazarus, showing his courage despite doubts.
- Early church tradition places him as a missionary to *India* or *Parthia*, establishing Christian communities far beyond the Mediterranean.
- Represents the *universal human struggle with doubt and faith*, making him relatable across cultures and centuries.

## connections

- [[jesus]] — whose Resurrection he witnessed and accepted.
- [[resurrection]] — the central event that transformed his doubt into conviction.
- [[lazarus]] — whom Jesus raised, an event Thomas witnessed before his own encounter with the Resurrection.
- [[the-twelve-apostles]] — he was one of the original twelve chosen disciples.
