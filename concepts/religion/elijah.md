## summary

**Elijah the Tishbite** was one of the most powerful prophets in Hebrew scripture, known for his dramatic confrontations with idolatry and his miraculous deeds. Living during a period of spiritual apostasy in the northern kingdom of Israel, he stood alone against the worship of the false god *Baal* and the influence of Queen *Jezebel*. According to tradition, he was taken up to heaven in a chariot of fire without experiencing death.

## you gotta know

- He confronted King Ahab and Queen *Jezebel* over their promotion of Baal worship and their persecution of God's prophets.
- He challenged the prophets of Baal to a test on Mount Carmel: each side would prepare a sacrifice, and the true god would consume it with fire—God's fire consumed Elijah's offering, vindicating him.
- He called down *fire from heaven* multiple times, including to consume a captain and his soldiers sent to arrest him.
- He declared a three-year drought over Israel as punishment for idolatry, then prayed for rain to end it.
- He was fed by ravens at the brook Cherith and sustained by a widow in Zarephath during the drought, demonstrating God's provision.
- He was taken up to heaven in a chariot of fire in a whirlwind, never experiencing death—a unique fate that made him a symbol of eschatological hope.
- He mentored *Elisha*, his successor, passing on his mantle and prophetic authority before his ascension.
- His ministry is recorded in *1 Kings* chapters 17–19 and *2 Kings* chapter 1–2.

## connections

- [[jezebel]] — the queen whose idolatry and cruelty he opposed throughout his ministry.
- [[baal]] — the false god whose worship he confronted and defeated.
- [[elisha]] — his protégé and successor in the prophetic line.
- [[ahab]] — the king of Israel whose reign was marked by spiritual decline.
- [[torah]] — the law and covenant Elijah defended against apostasy.
- [[judaism]] — his figure became central to messianic expectations and Jewish hope.
