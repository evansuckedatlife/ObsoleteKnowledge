## summary

The mother of **Jesus**, revered in Christian tradition as the *Virgin Mary* because she conceived Jesus while remaining a virgin. She appears prominently in the birth narratives of Matthew and Luke and is present at key moments in Jesus's ministry and crucifixion, becoming a central figure in Christian devotion and theology.

## you gotta know

- *The virgin mother of Jesus*, central to the Incarnation theology of Christianity.
- Conceived Jesus through divine intervention while engaged to **Joseph**, a carpenter.
- Visited by the *Archangel Gabriel* who announced her pregnancy, known as the Annunciation.
- Gave birth to Jesus in *Bethlehem* after traveling from Nazareth during a Roman census.
- Present at the *wedding at Cana*, where Jesus performed his first recorded miracle by turning water into wine.
- Stood at the foot of Jesus's cross during the *Crucifixion*, witnessing his death.
- In Catholic and Orthodox traditions, she ascended to heaven after her earthly life—the Assumption.
- Venerated with prayers, feasts (her birthday on September 8, Assumption on August 15), and artistic depictions across Christian denominations.
