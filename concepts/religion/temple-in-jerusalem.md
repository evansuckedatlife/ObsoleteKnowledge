## summary

The Temple in Jerusalem refers to successive sanctuaries built on the Temple Mount in Jerusalem that served as the primary center of Israelite and Jewish worship, sacrifice, and national identity. The First Temple, built by King Solomon, was destroyed by the Babylonians in 586 BC. The Second Temple, reconstructed under Zerubbabel and later heavily renovated by Herod the Great, was destroyed by the Romans in 70 AD, leaving only the Western Wall standing and initiating the transition to rabbinic Judaism.

## you gotta know

- The *First Temple* was constructed by King *Solomon* in the 10th century BC on Mount Moriah, housing the Ark of the Covenant in its innermost sanctuary, the Holy of Holies.
- The First Temple was sacked and completely destroyed by the Neo-Babylonian Empire under *Nebuchadnezzar II* in 586 BC, sending the Jewish people into the Babylonian Exile.
- The *Second Temple* was constructed on the same site after Cyrus the Great authorized the return of Jewish exiles, and was later massively expanded by *Herod the Great* to become one of the grandest structures of the Roman East.
- In the 2nd century BC, the Hellenistic king Antiochus IV Epiphanes desecrated the Second Temple by dedicating it to Zeus, sparking the *Maccabean Revolt* and leading to the temple's rededication, commemorated by the holiday of *Hanukkah*.
- The Second Temple was destroyed by Roman legions under the future emperor *Titus* in 70 AD during the First Jewish-Roman War, an event mourned annually on the fast of *Tisha B'Av*.
- The innermost chamber, the *Holy of Holies* (Kodesh HaKodashim), was entered only once a year by the High Priest on Yom Kippur, separated from the rest of the temple by a heavy veil that the Gospels record split in two at the moment of Jesus's crucifixion.

## connections

- [[solomon]] — the wise king who built the First Temple.
- [[david]] — the king who purchased the temple site on Mount Moriah and gathered materials.
- [[tisha-bav]] — the annual day of fasting and mourning that commemorates the destructions.
- [[hanukkah]] — the holiday celebrating the rededication of the Second Temple by the Maccabees.
- [[judaism]] — the religion that transitioned from temple sacrifices to synagogue prayer after the temple's fall.
- [[crucifixion]] — the event when the temple's inner veil was reportedly torn in two.
