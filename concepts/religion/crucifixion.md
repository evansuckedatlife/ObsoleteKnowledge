# Crucifixion

## summary

Crucifixion was an ancient method of painful execution in which the victim was tied or nailed to a large wooden cross and left to hang until death. While practiced by various ancient empires, it is most famous in history as the method by which Jesus was executed in Judaea under the Roman governor Pontius Pilate, an event that serves as the theological centerpiece of Christian salvation, atonement, and resurrection.

## you gotta know

- The execution of Jesus took place at *Golgotha* (Aramaic for "the place of the skull"), traditionally identified in Latin as Calvary, located just outside the walls of Jerusalem.
- Before being nailed to the cross, Jesus was forced to carry his own crossbar (*patibulum*), scourged by Roman soldiers, and crowned with thorns in mockery of his claim to kingship.
- An inscription called the *Titulus Crucis* reading "INRI" (Latin abbreviation for *Iesus Nazarenus, Rex Iudaeorum*, meaning "Jesus of Nazareth, King of the Jews") was ordered by Pontius Pilate to be placed above Jesus's head.
- According to the Gospels, Jesus hung on the cross for six hours, during which he uttered the "Seven Last Words," including *Eloi Eloi lama sabachthani* ("My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?").
- The death was accompanied by miraculous events, including a three-hour darkness over the land, an earthquake, and the tearing of the heavy curtain of the Temple in Jerusalem from top to bottom.
- Following his death, a Roman soldier pierced his side with a spear to confirm his death, producing a flow of blood and water, and his body was removed by Joseph of Arimathea for burial.
