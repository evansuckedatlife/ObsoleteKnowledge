# Jezebel

## summary

Jezebel was a wicked queen of Israel, wife of King Ahab, who opposed the prophet Elijah and persecuted God's followers. Her name has become synonymous with an immoral, scheming, or seductive woman who uses her power for selfish and destructive ends.

## you gotta know

- Jezebel appears in *1 Kings*, ruling Israel (circa 870 BCE) and actively promoting pagan worship and idolatry against Elijah's protests.
- She orchestrated the deaths of God's prophets and acquired property through deception and murder, including the vineyard of Naboth.
- Elijah famously confronted her about her wickedness, and she swore to kill him, forcing him to flee into the wilderness.
- Jezebel was eventually killed, thrown from a window by her own servants, with her body eaten by dogs—seen as divine judgment.
- Her defiance of God, her manipulation of her husband, and her use of sexuality and power made her the biblical archetype of the wicked woman.
- Modern usage: calling a woman a "Jezebel" implies she is seductive, manipulative, immoral, and a threat to righteousness or social order.
