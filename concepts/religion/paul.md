## summary

Originally **Saul of Tarsus**, a persecutor of early Christians who underwent a dramatic conversion on the *road to Damascus* after encountering the risen Jesus. He became Christianity's most prolific missionary and theologian, authoring more than half the New Testament epistles and establishing churches throughout the Mediterranean.

## you gotta know

- *Persecuted Christians* before his conversion, overseeing the execution of Stephen and jailing believers.
- *Experienced a transformative vision* on the road to Damascus, encountering the risen Jesus and hearing his voice.
- *Renamed Paul* as a symbol of his new mission, traveling throughout the Mediterranean world as an apostle.
- *Wrote thirteen epistles* addressing theology, church practice, and spiritual instruction—Romans, Corinthians, Galatians, Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians, Thessalonians, Timothy, Titus, and Philemon.
- *Preached to Gentiles*, arguing that salvation through Jesus was available to all peoples, not just Jews.
- *Suffered imprisonments, beatings, and shipwrecks* in service of his mission, enduring persecution he once inflicted on others.
- *Debated with Peter* at Antioch over the inclusion of Gentiles, representing a crucial moment in early Christian theology.
- Traditional accounts describe his *martyrdom by beheading in Rome*, where he had been imprisoned.

## connections

- [[jesus]] — the risen Lord who appeared to him and redirected his life.
- [[peter]] — fellow pillar of the early church, with whom he corresponded and occasionally disagreed.
- [[john-the-apostle]] — part of the Jerusalem council that affirmed Paul's apostolic ministry.
- [[epistles]] — he authored the majority of New Testament letters, shaping Christian doctrine.
