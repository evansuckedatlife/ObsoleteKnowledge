# Solomon

## summary

**King Solomon** was the son and successor of *King David*, renowned for his wisdom, wealth, and architectural grandeur. He presided over Israel at the height of its power and prosperity, building the First Temple in Jerusalem—the permanent sanctuary that would define Jewish worship for centuries. Though he inherited a united and stable kingdom, his heavy taxation and complex politics would sow seeds of division after his death.

## you gotta know

- He was born to *David* and *Bathsheba* after the death of their first child, representing redemption from their tragic sin.
- God offered him any blessing, and he chose *wisdom* above all else—insight and understanding to judge his people rightly—which pleased God.
- He built the First *Temple* in Jerusalem, a magnificent structure that housed the Ark of the Covenant and became the center of Israelite worship and sacrifice.
- He was famed for his wealth, receiving tribute from surrounding nations, and his extensive trade networks made Israel prosperous.
- He composed thousands of proverbs and songs, and traditions attribute the book of *Proverbs* and *Ecclesiastes* to his authorship.
- He had many wives (tradition says 700) and many children, though this multiplicity of relationships also drew him toward foreign gods and idolatry.
- His reign marked the apex of Israel's unified power; the kingdom would divide after his death into northern Israel and southern Judah.
- His life is recorded in *1 Kings* chapters 1–11 and *2 Chronicles* chapters 1–9.
