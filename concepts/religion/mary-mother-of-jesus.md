# Mary, Mother of Jesus

## summary

Mary is the mother of Jesus, revered in Christian tradition as the Virgin Mary because she conceived Jesus while remaining a virgin, a doctrine central to Christian theology of the Incarnation. She appears throughout the Gospel narratives, from her pregnancy to Jesus's crucifixion and resurrection, and became a central figure in Christian devotion, prayer, and art, with veneration practices varying significantly across Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant traditions.

## you gotta know

- The *Annunciation* — the visit by the *Archangel Gabriel* to Mary in Nazareth — announces her pregnancy with Jesus and invites her acceptance of this divine plan.
- She was a young woman engaged to *Joseph*, a carpenter, when she conceived Jesus through divine intervention, remaining a virgin according to Christian tradition, though this doctrine is contested by some Protestant scholars.
- She traveled with Joseph to Bethlehem for a Roman census and gave birth to Jesus in a manger, as recounted in the Gospels of Matthew and Luke.
- She was present at the *wedding at Cana*, where Jesus performed his first recorded miracle of turning water into wine.
- She stood at the foot of Jesus's cross during his *crucifixion* and received his care (in the Gospel of John) through the apostle John, who became her guardian.
- In Catholic and Orthodox theology, she was assumed bodily into heaven (*the Assumption*) after her earthly life, commemorated on August 15.
- She is venerated in Christian tradition through prayers like the *Ave Maria* and the *Rosary*, and is invoked as an intercessor and mother figure in Catholic and Orthodox piety.
