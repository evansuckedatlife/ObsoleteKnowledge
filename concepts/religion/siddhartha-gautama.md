# Siddhartha Gautama

## summary

Siddhartha Gautama, known as *the Buddha* ("the Awakened One"), was the founder of *Buddhism* and one of history's most influential spiritual teachers. Born a prince in ancient Nepal around 563 BCE, he abandoned his palace to seek enlightenment and developed a path to liberation that has shaped the lives of over a billion people across Asia and the world.

## you gotta know

- Siddhartha was born Prince Siddhartha of the Shakya clan in what is now Nepal, sheltered in luxury by his father until his encounter with human suffering at age 29.
- He renounced his princely life, wife, and child to become an ascetic wanderer, seeking to understand the nature of suffering and its cessation.
- After years of extreme austerity, he achieved *Bodhi* (awakening) beneath a Bodhi tree in Bodh Gaya, India, around 528 BCE, becoming the Buddha.
- His core teachings, the *Four Noble Truths*, explain that suffering exists, has a cause (craving), can end, and can be ended through the *Noble Eightfold Path*.
- The concept of *Dharma* (universal law) and *Karma* (action and consequence) are central to Buddhist philosophy, emphasizing personal responsibility for one's spiritual path.
- Unlike contemporary traditions, he rejected the authority of the *Vedas* and the Brahmin priesthood, teaching that enlightenment is accessible to all regardless of caste.
- He established the *Sangha* (monastic community) of monks and nuns, creating institutional structures that preserved his teachings across centuries.
- Buddhism spread from India across Asia, developing into multiple schools (*Theravada*, *Mahayana*, *Vajrayana*) and becoming one of the world's major religions.
