# Abraham

## summary

Abraham is the founding patriarch of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, revered across all three Abrahamic faiths as the father of monotheistic faith. Born in ancient Mesopotamia, his covenant with God and willingness to sacrifice his son Isaac established the spiritual foundation for billions of believers across centuries.

## you gotta know

- Abraham is the common ancestor claimed by Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, making him the patriarch of over half the world's religious traditions.
- In *Judaism*, he is the founder of the covenant with God and the father of the Jewish people through his son Isaac.
- In *Christianity*, he is viewed as the model of faith and the ancestor of Jesus through the line of David.
- In *Islam*, he is venerated as Khalil Allah (the Friend of God) and patriarch of the faith, with Muslims tracing descent through his son Ishmael.
- The *Binding of Isaac* (or *Akeidah*) represents his ultimate test of faith, willingness to obey God's command even to sacrifice his beloved son.
- He is believed to have lived in the Bronze Age, around 2000–1800 BCE, in the Fertile Crescent region.
- His name change from Abram ("exalted father") to Abraham ("father of multitudes") marked God's covenant with him to father nations.
- Circumcision, the *brit milah*, originates from God's command to Abraham and remains central to Jewish identity.
