# pearls before swine

## summary

This phrase comes from Jesus' teaching in the Sermon on the Mount, warning against sharing precious or sacred things with those who cannot appreciate them and will only reject or ridicule them. It means offering something valuable to those unfit or unwilling to receive it.

## you gotta know

- Jesus says in *Matthew 7:6*, "Do not give what is holy to the dogs; nor cast your pearls before swine, lest they trample them under their feet, and turn and tear you in pieces."
- Pearls represent something precious, valuable, and rare—spiritually, this means sacred knowledge, truth, or grace.
- Swine (pigs) cannot perceive the value of pearls and will destroy them out of indifference or contempt, not malice.
- The teaching is about discernment: don't waste precious truth on those who are hostile to it or incapable of understanding its value.
- Jesus is warning his followers against preaching to those hardened in disbelief or mockery—they will only attack and despise the message.
- Modern usage: "casting pearls before swine" means offering something valuable (advice, help, art, truth) to someone who lacks the capacity or willingness to appreciate it.
