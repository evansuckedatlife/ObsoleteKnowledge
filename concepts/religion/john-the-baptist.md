## summary

A Jewish preacher who baptized people as a sign of repentance and is best known for *baptizing Jesus* in the River Jordan. He was an ascetic figure who prepared the way for Jesus's ministry and was later executed by **Herod** for criticizing the king's marriage.

## you gotta know

- *Baptized Jesus* in the Jordan River, marking the beginning of Jesus's public ministry.
- Preached a message of *repentance and moral reformation* in the Judean wilderness.
- Wore distinctive clothing: *a coat of camel's hair with a leather belt*, eating locusts and wild honey.
- Known for his ritual practice of *baptism*, which became a defining act in early Christian tradition.
- *Beheaded by Herod Antipas*, the tetrarch of Galilee, after criticizing the king's marriage to his brother's wife.
- His head was *brought to Herod on a platter* at a feast—a dramatic moment captured in Christian and secular literature.
- Recognized by both Christian and Jewish sources as a significant religious figure of his time.
- In Christian theology, he is understood as the *forerunner* who prepared Israel for Jesus's arrival.

## connections

- [[crucifixion]] — his death prefigures Jesus's later execution.
- [[the-twelve-apostles]] — some of his disciples became Jesus's apostles.
