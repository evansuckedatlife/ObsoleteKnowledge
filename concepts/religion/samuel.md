## summary

**Samuel** was a prophet and judge who marked a pivotal transition in Israelite history from the era of judges to the era of kings. Born in answer to his mother Hannah's prayers, he served as God's spokesman and became the anointer of the first two kings of Israel. His unwavering devotion to God and resistance to human autonomy defined his prophetic mission.

## you gotta know

- He was born to Hannah, a woman who had been barren, after she made a vow to dedicate him to God's service in the tabernacle.
- As a young boy serving in the tabernacle, he received a direct divine calling in the night, beginning his prophetic ministry.
- He was the last of the judges and the first of the great prophets, bridging two eras of Israelite governance.
- He anointed *Saul* as the first king of Israel, though Saul later disobeyed God and fell out of favor.
- He anointed *David*, the young shepherd, to be king in Saul's place, recognizing God's choice of a man after God's own heart.
- He warned the people about the cost of having a human king—that they would lose their freedom and autonomy—but the people insisted on kingship anyway.
- His life and ministry are recorded in the books of *1 and 2 Samuel*.

## connections

- [[saul]] — the first king he anointed, whose disobedience he confronted.
- [[david]] — the second king he anointed, who would become Israel's greatest king.
- [[judges]] — the period of governance he represented and concluded.
- [[moses]] — an earlier prophetic figure whose role he inherited and expanded.
- [[torah]] — the divine law he upheld and to which he called the kings to submit.
