# Zoroaster

## summary

Zoroaster is the founding prophet of *Zoroastrianism*, one of the world's oldest monotheistic religions originating in ancient Persia. His teachings about the cosmic struggle between good and evil, and the ultimate triumph of Ahura Mazda, profoundly influenced the religious development of the Middle East and beyond.

## you gotta know

- Zoroaster is the prophet-founder of *Zoroastrianism*, which emerged as one of the first organized monotheistic faiths in human history.
- The exact dates of his life remain disputed among scholars, ranging from roughly 1500 BCE to 600 BCE, reflecting uncertainty about when he lived in ancient Persia.
- His name means "owner of old camels" in Persian; "Zarathustra" is the Greek rendering that entered Western usage.
- He taught of Ahura Mazda, the supreme god of wisdom and light, engaged in eternal struggle against Ahriman, the spirit of darkness and destruction.
- The *Avesta*, Zoroastrianism's sacred scripture, is the primary source for his teachings and was transmitted orally before being written down.
- His dualistic cosmology—the conflict of good and evil, light and darkness—influenced later religious traditions including *Judaism*, *Christianity*, and *Islam*.
- He emphasized ethical choice and moral responsibility, teaching that humans must choose sides in the cosmic battle between good and evil.
- Fire worship and fire temples became central to Zoroastrian practice, with fire symbolizing the divine presence of Ahura Mazda.
