## summary

Joseph Smith (1805–1844) was the founding prophet of the *Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints* (*Mormonism*), a uniquely American religious movement that emerged in early 19th-century New York. Through his claimed visions and revelations, he established a faith that combines Christian theology with new scriptural texts and distinctive practices.

## you gotta know

- Joseph Smith was born in Vermont and raised in upstate New York during a period of intense religious revival known as the "Second Great Awakening."
- At age 14, he reported a vision of God and Jesus Christ instructing him that existing Christian denominations had fallen into error, initiating his prophetic mission.
- He claimed to have found golden plates inscribed with ancient American history, which he translated as the *Book of Mormon*, published in 1830 as Mormonism's central scripture.
- The *Book of Mormon* claims to be a history of pre-Columbian American peoples and their encounters with Jesus Christ after his resurrection, fundamentally expanding Christian cosmology.
- He founded the *Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints* in 1830, attracting thousands of followers and establishing communities in Ohio, Missouri, and Illinois.
- Smith taught that God continues to reveal truth through living prophets, establishing *continuous revelation* as a core Latter-day Saint principle distinct from mainstream Christianity.
- He practiced *polygamy* (plural marriage), a teaching that became central to early Mormonism and caused both internal controversy and violent external opposition.
- He was killed by a mob in Carthage, Illinois, in 1844, becoming a martyr figure whose death cemented his prophetic authority in Latter-day Saint tradition.
