## summary

The Hebrew Bible, also known as the *Tanakh* (an acronym for its three divisions: Torah, Nevi'im, and Ketuvim), is the sacred scripture of Judaism and the basis of the Christian Old Testament. Composed over nearly a thousand years and written in Hebrew and Aramaic, it contains narratives of creation, covenant, law, prophecy, and wisdom that form the foundation of monotheistic religion and Western ethical thought.

## you gotta know

- The *Torah* (Five Books of Moses) — Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy — comprises the foundational narrative and legal code of Judaism.
- The *Nevi'im* (Prophets) include the Former Prophets (Joshua, Judges, Samuel, Kings) and the Latter Prophets (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, and the Twelve Minor Prophets).
- The *Ketuvim* (Writings) encompass the Psalms, Proverbs, Job, the Song of Songs, Ruth, Lamentations, Ecclesiastes, Esther, Daniel, Ezra-Nehemiah, and Chronicles.
- Key narratives include the creation of humanity, the *Flood*, the covenant with *Abraham*, the *Exodus* from Egypt under *Moses*, and the kingdom of *David*.
- Major themes include covenant and election, divine justice, prophecy as God's communication with humanity, and wisdom as moral insight.
- Christians adopted the Hebrew Bible as the Old Testament, reading it as prophetic of Jesus and the New Covenant, which fundamentally shaped Christian hermeneutics and theology.
