## summary

One of Jesus's twelve apostles and traditionally credited with authoring the *Gospel of John*, the Epistles of John, and the *Book of Revelation*. Known as "the beloved disciple," he was present at pivotal moments in Jesus's ministry and became a central figure in early Christian theology and literature.

## you gotta know

- Called "the *beloved disciple*" in the Gospel of John, noted for his closeness to Jesus.
- Present at the *Last Supper*, reclining next to Jesus, and entrusted with care of Jesus's mother *Mary*.
- *Witnessed the Crucifixion* and was at the empty tomb on the Resurrection morning.
- *Traditionally authored the Gospel of John*, which presents Jesus's ministry with theological depth and poetic language.
- Credited with writing *three epistles* (1, 2, and 3 John) addressing faith, love, and eternal life.
- Wrote or was the source of the *Book of Revelation*, a apocalyptic vision received on the island of *Patmos*.
- Early church tradition holds he lived to old age in *Ephesus*, making him the longest-lived apostle.
- Distinguished from *John the Baptist* by his role as an apostle and his literary legacy in shaping New Testament theology.

## connections

- [[jesus]] — his teacher and the subject of his Gospel.
- [[peter]] — fellow apostle and witness to the same events.
- [[mary-magdalene]] — another witness to the Resurrection.
- [[gospels]] — he authored the Gospel of John, the most theologically distinct Gospel account.
- [[revelation]] — traditionally attributed to him while in exile.
