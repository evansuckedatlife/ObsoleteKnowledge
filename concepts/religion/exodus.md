## summary

The Exodus is the founding myth-history of the Israelites, recounting their departure from slavery in Egypt under the leadership of Moses, their journey through the wilderness, and the covenant established with God at Mount Sinai. Detailed primarily in the biblical Book of Exodus (the second book of the Torah), it is the central narrative of Jewish identity, celebrated annually during Passover.

## you gotta know

- The narrative begins with the oppression of the Israelites in Egypt, leading God to commission *Moses* at the burning bush to demand their release from the reigning Pharaoh.
- When Pharaoh refuses, God inflicts *Ten Plagues* upon Egypt, culminating in the death of the firstborn, which the Israelites escape by painting their doorposts with lamb's blood, initiating the first *Passover*.
- The most famous miracle of the departure is the *Parting of the Red Sea* (or Sea of Reeds), allowing the fleeing Israelites to cross on dry land before the returning waters drown the pursuing Egyptian army.
- The destination of their journey is Canaan, the Promised Land, famously described as a *land of milk and honey*.
- At Mount Sinai, Moses receives the *Ten Commandments* and the law of the covenant, an event severely disrupted by the Israelites worshipping a *Golden Calf* in his absence.
- The event initiated forty years of wandering in the wilderness, during which the Israelites survived on *manna* from heaven and built the Tabernacle to house the Ark of the Covenant.
