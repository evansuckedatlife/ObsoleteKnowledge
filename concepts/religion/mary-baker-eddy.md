# Mary Baker Eddy

## summary

Mary Baker Eddy (1821–1910) was the founding figure of *Christian Science*, a uniquely American religion blending Christian theology with metaphysical philosophy and emphasizing spiritual healing. Born in New England, she developed a distinctive interpretation of Jesus's teachings centered on the power of consciousness and the illusory nature of material evil.

## you gotta know

- Mary Baker Eddy was born in rural New Hampshire and experienced chronic illness throughout her childhood, which profoundly shaped her spiritual quest for healing and truth.
- She claimed to experience a sudden healing in 1866 after a severe fall, attributing her recovery to spiritual insight and divine understanding rather than medical treatment.
- Her foundational text, *Science and Health with Key to the Scriptures* (1875), combines biblical interpretation with metaphysical philosophy and established the theological framework of Christian Science.
- Christian Science teaches that *God is Mind* (not anthropomorphic), that matter is illusory, and that sin, sickness, and death are the result of false consciousness rather than material reality.
- She taught that Jesus Christ was the supreme exemplar of understanding God's true nature and that his healings demonstrated the spiritual power available to all believers through proper understanding.
- Eddy founded the *Church of Christ, Scientist* in 1879, establishing a unique congregational structure with reading rooms and practicing Christian Science practitioners who provide spiritual healing.
- She emphasized the role of *divine Science*, a systematic understanding of God's laws, as superior to medical science, making Christian Science distinctive in its skepticism toward conventional medicine.
- Though controversial, her movement attracted thousands of followers and established institutions, publications, and a worldwide presence that continues to influence American spirituality.
