## summary

**King David** is arguably the most revered figure in the Hebrew Bible and Jewish tradition—a shepherd who became Israel's greatest king. Anointed by the prophet *Samuel* while still a boy, David defeated the giant *Goliath* and united the tribes of Israel into a powerful kingdom. His reign established Jerusalem as the capital and set the spiritual and political foundation for later Jewish hope and identity.

## you gotta know

- He famously slew the Philistine giant *Goliath* with a sling and stone while still a young shepherd, becoming an instant hero of Israel.
- He was anointed king by *Samuel* while his older brothers were overlooked, fulfilling a divine choice based on his heart rather than his appearance.
- He lived in hiding as a fugitive for years, pursued by the jealous King *Saul*, but remained loyal and refused to kill Saul when he had the opportunity.
- He brought the Ark of the Covenant to Jerusalem and established it as the religious and political center of Israel.
- He conquered surrounding nations and expanded Israel's territory to its greatest extent, making it a regional power.
- His relationship with *Bathsheba* resulted in their adultery and the death of her husband *Uriah*, a grave sin that haunted his later reign.
- He fathered *Solomon*, who would succeed him and build the First Temple, fulfilling David's desire to construct a permanent home for God's presence.
- His life spans the books of *1 and 2 Samuel* and is recorded in *1 Kings*.

## connections

- [[goliath]] — the giant he defeated, which made his reputation.
- [[saul]] — the jealous king he succeeded, and whom he spared in mercy.
- [[samuel]] — the prophet who anointed him king.

- [[solomon]] — his son and successor, whom he prepared to build the Temple.
- [[jerusalem]] — the capital city he conquered and sanctified as Israel's heart.
- [[torah]] — the law and covenant he upheld and sought to center in the Ark.
