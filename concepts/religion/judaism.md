# Judaism

## summary

Judaism is an ancient, monotheistic Abrahamic religion, representing the collective religious, cultural, and legal tradition of the Jewish people. Developed in the Middle East during the Bronze Age, it is characterized by a belief in a single transcendent God who established a covenant with the Israelites. The tradition is anchored in its sacred texts, most fundamentally the Torah, and emphasizes ethical conduct, study, and adherence to commandments (*mitzvot*).

## you gotta know

- It is the oldest of the three major *Abrahamic religions*, tracing its lineage back to the biblical patriarch *Abraham*, with whom God established the first covenant.
- The core of its religious law and narrative is the *Torah* (the first five books of the Hebrew Bible), traditionally believed to have been revealed by God to *Moses* on Mount Sinai.
- It transitioned from a temple-based sacrificial cult centered on the *Temple in Jerusalem* to a text- and rabbinic-focused tradition following the Roman destruction of the Second Temple in 70 AD.
- Rabbinic Judaism is structured around the study of the *Talmud*, a vast compilation of legal discussions, debates, and folklore interpreting the Hebrew Bible (*Tanakh*).
- Its central confession of faith is the *Shema Yisrael* ("Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one"), asserting absolute monotheism.
- It encompasses diverse modern branches, including Orthodox (which holds the Torah as immutable and binding), Conservative (which views Jewish law as historically evolving but binding), and Reform (which emphasizes ethical teachings and personal autonomy).
