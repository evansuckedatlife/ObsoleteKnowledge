## summary

Reigning as pope from 1523 to 1534, **Clement VII** (born Giulio de' Medici) had a tumultuous pontificate defined by the rapid spread of the Protestant Reformation and intense geopolitical conflicts. A cousin of Pope Leo X, he struggled to navigate the rivalry between France and the Holy Roman Empire, leading to the catastrophic Sack of Rome. His refusal to annul the marriage of King Henry VIII of England triggered the English Reformation.

## you gotta know

- *Refused to annul the marriage of King Henry VIII of England* to Catherine of Aragon, primarily because he was under the physical control of Catherine's nephew, Holy Roman Emperor Charles V, which led directly to the *English Reformation*.
- *Trapped in the Castel Sant'Angelo during the Sack of Rome* in 1527, escaping through the Passetto di Borgo when mutinous troops of Emperor Charles V pillaged the city.
- *Joined the League of Cognac* in 1526 with France, Venice, Florence, and Milan to challenge the expanding imperial power of Holy Roman Emperor Charles V in Italy.
- *Commissioned Michelangelo to paint The Last Judgment* on the altar wall of the Sistine Chapel shortly before his death.
- Was the illegitimate son of Giuliano de' Medici (assassinated in the Pazzi Conspiracy), later legitimized by his cousin Leo X.

## connections

- [[peter]] — the apostle and first Bishop of Rome whose seat Clement VII held during the Sack of Rome.
- [[leo-x]] — his cousin and predecessor; Clement VII served as Leo's chief political advisor and cardinal.
- [[julius-ii]] — predecessor who commissioned the Sistine Chapel frescoes, where Clement later commissioned the *Last Judgment*.
- [[henry-viii]] — the English monarch who broke away from Rome to form the Church of England after Clement VII refused his annulment.
- [[charles-v]] — the Holy Roman Emperor whose mutinous forces sacked Rome and kept Clement prisoner.
