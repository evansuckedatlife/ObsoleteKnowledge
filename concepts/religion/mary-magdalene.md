# Mary Magdalene

<!-- /foundations -->

## summary

A devoted follower of Jesus often identified as the *first witness to the Resurrection*. While medieval tradition conflated her with other biblical Marys and portrayed her as a repentant prostitute, modern scholarship treats her as a separate figure who held a significant place in Jesus's ministry and the early church.

## you gotta know

- *Witnessed the Crucifixion* and stood near the cross as Jesus died, showing unwavering devotion.
- *First to encounter the risen Jesus* in all four Gospel accounts, though the details vary.
- In John's Gospel, *mistook Jesus for a gardener* after the Resurrection before recognizing him.
- Jesus performed a *miraculous exorcism* on her, casting out seven demons according to Luke.
- *Traveled with Jesus and supported his ministry*, listed among women who followed him from Galilee.
- Medieval tradition (not scriptural) merged her with the *sinful woman who anointed Jesus's feet*, creating confusion about her background.
- In *Catholic and Orthodox traditions*, celebrated on different feast days reflecting various versions of her story.
- Her *role as the first Resurrection witness* was theologically significant in early Christianity, making her an apostolic figure despite being female.
