## summary

The *Qur'an* is the central religious text of *Islam*, believed by Muslims to be the literal word of God (*Allah*) revealed orally to the Prophet *Muhammad* over roughly 23 years (609–632 CE). It is the foundation of Islamic theology, law, and practice.

## you gotta know

- Composed of 114 chapters (*surahs*), each divided into verses (*ayahs*), totaling roughly 77,000 words in Arabic.
- Written in Classical Arabic with a rhythmic, poetic structure (*saj*) that makes it aesthetically distinct from prose and verse.
- *Muhammad* received revelations orally and communicated them to followers, who memorized and wrote them on various materials; the standardized written compilation occurred under the third Caliph *Uthman* (c. 650 CE).
- Organized roughly by length (longest chapters first), not chronology; scholars distinguish *Meccan* (spiritual, shorter) and *Medinan* (legal, longer) revelations.
- Central themes: monotheism (*tawhid*), divine justice, prophetic tradition, ethical conduct, social justice, and the Day of Judgment.
- Muslims believe the *Qur'an* is inimitable (*i'jaz*) and cannot be truly translated; recitation in Arabic is integral to Islamic ritual.
- Extensively commented upon (*tafsir*) by Islamic scholars across centuries, generating a vast body of interpretive tradition.

## connections

- [[muhammad]] — the prophet to whom the *Qur'an* was revealed by God.
- [[hadith]] — supplementary to the *Qur'an*, providing context, jurisprudence, and practical guidance.
- [[islam]] — the foundational scripture defining Islamic belief and practice.
- [[torah]] — recognized in the *Qur'an* as a previous revelation; *Muslims* honor *Abraham*, *Moses*, and *Jesus*.
