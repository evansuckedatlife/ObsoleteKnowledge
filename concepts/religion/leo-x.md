# Leo X

## summary

Reigning as pope from 1513 to 1521, **Leo X** (born Giovanni de' Medici) was a lavish patron of the arts and the son of Lorenzo the Magnificent, representing the peak of Medici influence in the Church. His extravagant lifestyle and extensive spending exhausted the papal treasury. His promotion of the sale of indulgences to fund the rebuilding of St. Peter's Basilica triggered the Protestant Reformation, which he failed to contain.

## you gotta know

- *Issued the papal bull Exsurge Domine* in 1520, which condemned 41 errors of Martin Luther and threatened him with excommunication, later carrying it out with *Decet Romanum Pontificem* in 1521.
- *Authorized the aggressive sale of plenary indulgences* throughout Germany (notably by the Dominican friar Johann Tetzel) to finance the rebuilding of St. Peter's Basilica, directly prompting Luther to post his 95 Theses.
- *Presided over the closing sessions of the Fifth Lateran Council* (1512–1517), which failed to enact the sweeping ecclesiastical reforms that could have prevented the Reformation.
- *Negotiated the Concordat of Bologna* in 1516 with King Francis I of France, granting the French monarchy the authority to appoint French bishops and clergy.
- Appointed as a cardinal at the exceptionally young age of 13 through his father Lorenzo the Magnificent's political influence.
