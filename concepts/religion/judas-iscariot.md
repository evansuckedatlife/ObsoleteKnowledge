## summary

One of the twelve apostles of Jesus who became notorious for *betraying Jesus to the religious authorities* in exchange for thirty pieces of silver. His act of betrayal led directly to Jesus's arrest and crucifixion, making him one of history's most infamous figures in Western tradition.

## you gotta know

- *Betrayed Jesus for thirty pieces of silver*, the price of a slave in ancient law.
- *Identified Jesus to soldiers and priests* with a kiss in the Garden of Gethsemane, enabling his arrest.
- *Treasurer of the apostolic group*, trusted to manage funds despite his eventual treachery.
- Expressed *remorse after Jesus's condemnation*, attempting to return the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests.
- *Died by suicide*, hanging himself after Jesus's betrayal, according to Matthew's account.
- In John's Gospel, *possessed by Satan*, emphasizing the spiritual evil behind his betrayal.
- His *act of betrayal was prophesied* in Psalm 41:9, which Jesus quoted, linking his betrayal to scripture.
- Became the *archetypal symbol of betrayal* in Western culture, his name synonymous with treachery.

## connections

- [[jesus]] — whom he betrayed to religious and civil authorities.
- [[peter]] — fellow apostle who denied Jesus but later repented, contrasting with Judas.
- [[crucifixion]] — his betrayal was the trigger event leading to Jesus's execution.
- [[the-twelve-apostles]] — originally chosen as one of the twelve but became the traitor.
