## summary

Reigning as pope from 440 to 461, **Leo I** was a key figure in establishing the authority of the bishop of Rome as the supreme head of the Christian Church. He is best known for his doctrinal contributions defining the nature of Christ and his historic negotiations with barbarian invaders to protect the city of Rome. He was the first pope to be officially styled *the Great*.

## you gotta know

- *Convinced Attila the Hun* to withdraw his forces and spare Rome from sacking during a face-to-face meeting in northern Italy in 452.
- Wrote the *Tome of Leo*, a letter defining the two natures of Jesus Christ (divine and human) united in one person, which was accepted as orthodox doctrine at the *Council of Chalcedon* in 451.
- Promoted the doctrine of *Petrine supremacy*, arguing that the Bishop of Rome inherits the authority given by Christ to Saint Peter, and secured a decree from Emperor Valentinian III confirming this primacy.
- *Interceded with Genseric*, leader of the Vandals, during the 455 sack of Rome, successfully pleading to prevent massacres, torture, and burning of the city.
- First pope to be buried in the portico of the old *St. Peter's Basilica*.

## connections

- [[peter]] — whose successor Leo claimed to be, providing the theological basis for papal primacy.
- [[jesus]] — whose dual natures were defined by Leo in his famous Tome.
