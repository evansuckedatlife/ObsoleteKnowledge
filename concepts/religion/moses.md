# Moses

## summary

The greatest prophet and lawgiver in Hebrew scripture, **Moses** is credited with leading the Israelites out of slavery in Egypt and receiving the *Ten Commandments* from God at Mount Sinai. Born as the son of Hebrew slaves but raised as an Egyptian prince, Moses became the liberator of his people and the mediator of the covenant that defined Judaism. His forty years of wandering in the wilderness with the Israelites established the foundational laws and spiritual identity of the nation.

## you gotta know

- He was hidden as an infant and raised by Pharaoh's daughter after his mother placed him in a basket on the Nile River.
- He killed an Egyptian taskmaster and fled to the wilderness, where God spoke to him through a *burning bush*, commanding him to free the Israelites.
- He led the people through the ten *plagues of Egypt*, culminating in the *Passover*, when the firstborn of Egypt were struck down.
- He parted the Red Sea, allowing the Israelites to escape Egypt's pursuing army, which was then swallowed by the returning waters.
- He received the *Ten Commandments* written on two stone tablets at the top of Mount Sinai while the people waited below.
- He wandered with the Israelites in the desert for forty years, managing their complaints, mediating God's will, and establishing the laws of the *Torah*.
- He did not enter the Promised Land himself, but glimpsed it from Mount Nebo before his death at age 120.
