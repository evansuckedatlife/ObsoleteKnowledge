## summary

Mecca is the holiest city in Islam, located in western Saudi Arabia, and the birthplace of the Prophet Muhammad. Every year, millions of Muslim pilgrims from around the world converge on Mecca to perform the *hajj* (pilgrimage), one of the Five Pillars of Islam. The city is home to the *Kaaba*, a cubic shrine that Muslims believe was originally built by Abraham and is the spiritual center of Islamic worship.

## you gotta know

- Muhammad was born in Mecca around 570 CE; he preached there initially but was forced to flee to Medina in 622 CE in the *Hijra* (migration), an event that marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
- The *Kaaba* — a cube-shaped structure covered in black cloth — stands at the center of the *al-Masjid al-Haram* (the Sacred Mosque) and is believed by Muslims to be the house of God on Earth, originally built by Abraham and his son Ishmael.
- The *Hajj*, performed in the Islamic month of Dhu al-Hijjah, is a pilgrimage that every adult Muslim able to do so is required to undertake at least once in their lifetime; it is one of the Five Pillars of Islam.
- The circumambulation of the Kaaba (*tawaf*) and other rituals of the hajj commemorate events in the life of Abraham and Ishmael and symbolize the unity and equality of all Muslims before God.
- Mecca is considered *haram* (sacred); non-Muslims are forbidden to enter the city, and Muslims consider it the spiritual heart of the Islamic world.
