## summary

Baháʼu'lláh (1817–1892), born Mirza Husayn-Ali, was the founder of the *Baháʼí Faith*, a modern religion emphasizing the unity of humanity, religious pluralism, and world peace. Emerging from 19th-century Persia amid religious upheaval, he taught that all major religions are divine revelations addressing different stages of human development.

## you gotta know

- Baháʼu'lláh was born into Persian nobility and initially became a follower of the Báb, a religious reformer whose execution profoundly shaped his spiritual mission.
- He is believed by Baháʼís to be a *Manifestation of God*, a figure through whom God reveals divine will to humanity, standing in a line with Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad.
- He spent most of his life imprisoned or exiled by Persian and Ottoman authorities for his religious teachings, enduring severe suffering that his followers view as redemptive.
- His core teachings teach the *oneness of God*, the *oneness of religion* (viewing all major faiths as expressions of eternal truth), and the *oneness of humanity*.
- The *Kitáb-i-Aqdas* (the Most Holy Book) is Baháʼu'lláh's primary written revelation, containing laws, practices, and spiritual guidance for the Baháʼí community.
- He established the *Covenant*, a spiritual and administrative framework ensuring unity and continuity in the faith after his death through designated successors.
- The Baháʼí Faith teaches progressive revelation, the idea that God continues to guide humanity through successive messengers as civilization develops and matures.
- His teachings address social issues including the elimination of prejudice, establishment of universal education, gender equality, and international peace—themes ahead of his era.
