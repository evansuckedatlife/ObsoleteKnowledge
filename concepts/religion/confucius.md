# Confucius

## summary

Confucius was a Chinese philosopher and teacher whose ideas about proper conduct, family relationships, and social harmony fundamentally shaped East Asian civilization for over 2,500 years. Rather than founding a "religion" in the Western sense, he established a comprehensive ethical and social philosophy (*Confucianism*) centered on virtue, ritual, and harmonious relationships.

## you gotta know

- Confucius lived from 551 to 479 BCE during the Zhou Dynasty's decline, a period of social chaos that motivated his search for moral and social order.
- His name, Kongzi in Chinese (Kong Qiu being his birth name), was Latinized as "Confucius" by Jesuit missionaries, and he is often referred to as the "Ultimate Sage" in tradition.
- His teachings emphasize *Li* (propriety/ritual), *Ren* (humaneness/benevolence), *Yi* (righteousness), and *Junzi* (the cultivated person or gentleman), forming the foundation of virtuous living.
- The *Analects* (*Lunyu*), compiled by his disciples, are the primary source for his teachings and have been studied and revered across East Asia for millennia.
- He taught that moral cultivation begins with filial piety (*Xiao*) and extends through proper relationships: ruler-subject, father-son, husband-wife, elder-younger brother, friend-friend.
- Confucius believed that good governance depends on virtuous rulers who serve as moral exemplars, rejecting the idea that rule depends solely on laws and punishments.
- Though not a religious founder in the traditional sense, Confucianism became deeply embedded in East Asian religious and philosophical practice, coexisting with Buddhism and Daoism.
- His teachings profoundly influenced the civil service examination systems across China, Vietnam, Japan, and Korea, shaping their political and intellectual cultures.
