# Jewish divorce


<!-- foundations -->
> [!note]+ Foundations — read these first
> [[judaism]]
<!-- /foundations -->

## summary

Jewish divorce (*gerushin*) is the legal dissolution of a Jewish marriage according to *halakha* (Jewish law). The dissolution requires a *get* (bill of divorce)—a formal document—written by a *sofer* (scribe) and delivered by the husband to the wife in front of two valid witnesses. The *get* must be accepted willingly by both parties for the divorce to be valid.

## you gotta know

- The *get* is a precise legal document written in Aramaic following a strict halakhic formula; any significant error renders it invalid.
- A *sofer* (trained Jewish scribe) writes the *get*, and a *beit din* (rabbinic court) supervises the process to ensure validity.
- The husband must *give* the *get* to the wife of his own free will; a *get* given under coercion or extracted through duress is halakhically invalid.
- Two valid witnesses must be present when the *get* is delivered and accepted; they sign the document to attest to the transaction.
- The wife must physically receive and accept the *get* in front of witnesses; without her acceptance, the marriage is not dissolved according to Jewish law.
- A woman cannot remarry according to Jewish law until she receives a valid *get*; civil divorce alone does not dissolve the Jewish marriage.
- In Orthodox Judaism, remarriage before obtaining a valid *get* may result in *mamzerut* (illegitimate status) for any children born to a new union.
- Modern Jewish movements vary in their approach: Reform and Reconstructionist rabbis may recognize civil divorce, while Conservative Judaism typically requires a valid *get*.
