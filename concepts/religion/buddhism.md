# Buddhism

## summary

Buddhism is a major world religion and philosophical system founded in ancient India by Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha) in the 6th–5th centuries BC. Focusing on spiritual development and the attainment of deep insight into the true nature of reality, it teaches that life is characterized by suffering (*dukkha*), which can be overcome by eliminating craving and ignorance to attain the ultimate state of liberation known as *Nirvana*.

## you gotta know

- Its core doctrine is formulated as the *Four Noble Truths*: the truth of suffering, the cause of suffering (craving/attachment), the end of suffering, and the path to the end of suffering.
- The path to end suffering is the *Noble Eightfold Path*, a guide divided into three sections: wisdom (right view and intention), ethical conduct (right speech, action, and livelihood), and mental discipline (right effort, mindfulness, and concentration).
- It rejects the Vedic caste system and the concept of a permanent, eternal soul (*Atman*), teaching instead the doctrine of *Anatta* (non-self or insubstantiality).
- The tradition is divided into three major branches: *Theravada* (the oldest surviving school, prominent in Sri Lanka and Southeast Asia), *Mahayana* (which emphasizes the ideal of the *bodhisattva* who seeks enlightenment for all beings, popular in East Asia), and *Vajrayana* (Tibetan Buddhism, incorporating esoteric practices).
- The sacred scriptures of Theravada Buddhism are collected in the *Pali Canon*, also known as the *Tripitaka* ("Three Baskets"), consisting of the Vinaya Pitaka (rules for monastics), Sutta Pitaka (discourses of the Buddha), and Abhidhamma Pitaka (philosophical analysis).
- Its rapid spread throughout Asia was heavily boosted by the patronage of the Mauryan Emperor *Ashoka the Great* in the 3rd century BC.
