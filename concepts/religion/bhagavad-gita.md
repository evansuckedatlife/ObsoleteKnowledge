## summary

The *Bhagavad Gita* is a 700-verse philosophical dialogue embedded within the epic *Mahabharata*, where the god *Krishna* counsels the warrior *Arjuna* on duty, righteousness, and spiritual liberation. Composed around the 2nd century BCE, it is *Hinduism's* most widely read scripture.

## you gotta know

- Set on a battlefield at Kurukshetra, where *Arjuna* faces moral crisis before fighting his cousins; *Krishna* serves as his charioteer and spiritual guide.
- Composed in Sanskrit as 18 chapters within the much larger *Mahabharata* epic (authored traditionally by *Vyasa*).
- Teaches three primary paths to liberation (*moksha*): *karma yoga* (action without attachment), *bhakti yoga* (devotion), and *jnana yoga* (knowledge).
- *Krishna's* revelation of his divine form (*Vishvarupa*) in Chapter 11 is one of Hinduism's most iconic mystical moments.
- Central to *bhakti* devotional movements and influenced Advaita Vedanta non-dualism, *Tantra*, and modern Hindu philosophy.
- Famous teaching: "You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled to the fruits of action" (*karma yoga* principle).
- Extends Hindu philosophy beyond the Vedas and Upanishads, making it accessible to all castes and classes.
