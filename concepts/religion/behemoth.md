# Behemoth

## summary

Behemoth is an enormous, powerful creature described in the Book of Job as an example of God's incomprehensible creation and creative power. The word has come to mean any massive or unstoppable force, often used to describe a large, powerful organization or entity.

## you gotta know

- Behemoth appears in *Job 40*, where God describes it to Job as one of His greatest creations, beyond human understanding or control.
- The creature is described with strength in its loins, bones like bronze tubes, and a tail like a cedar—possibly inspired by ancient Near Eastern creatures or animals like the hippopotamus or behemoth, a water buffalo.
- God presents Behemoth (alongside Leviathan, a sea creature) to humble Job and show him that the world contains forces far greater than himself.
- The point is theological: humans cannot tame or fully comprehend creation, so pride is foolish and humility is necessary before God.
- Behemoth is often paired with Leviathan in medieval and rabbinic theology, representing chaos, wildness, and the limits of human dominion.
- Modern usage: "behemoth" describes any large, powerful, seemingly unstoppable entity—a "corporate behemoth," a "bureaucratic behemoth," etc.
