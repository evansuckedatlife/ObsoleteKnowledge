## summary

The Mahabharata is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, the other being the Ramayana. Traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa, it is the longest epic poem ever written, consisting of over 100,000 shlokas (couplets). The epic chronicles the dynastic struggle and devastating Kurukshetra War between two branches of a royal family, the Pandavas and the Kauravas, while serving as a comprehensive text on ethics, dharma, and philosophy.

## you gotta know

- It is the *longest epic poem in the world*, roughly ten times the length of the *Iliad* and *Odyssey* combined, divided into 18 books called *parvas*.
- The central plot revolves around a bitter succession conflict between the five virtuous *Pandava* brothers and their one hundred malevolent cousins, the *Kauravas*.
- It contains the *Bhagavad Gita*, an 18-chapter philosophical dialogue between the Pandava warrior Arjuna and the god Krishna on the eve of the battle.
- The conflict culminates in the catastrophic 18-day *Kurukshetra War*, in which nearly all major kings and warriors of ancient India participate and perish.
- It is traditionally said to have been narrated by the sage *Vyasa* and written down by the elephant-headed god Ganesha, who used his own broken tusk as a stylus.
- The epic frames its stories as a history (*Itihasa*) that teaches the four goals of human life: *dharma* (duty), *artha* (wealth), *kama* (pleasure), and *moksha* (liberation).
