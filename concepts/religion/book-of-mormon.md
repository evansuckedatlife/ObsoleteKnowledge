## summary

The *Book of Mormon* is a sacred text of *The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints* (LDS), purportedly translated by *Joseph Smith* in 1829 from golden plates he claimed to discover. It describes the history of ancient peoples in the Americas and continues Christian revelation beyond the Bible.

## you gotta know

- *Joseph Smith* stated that an angel named Moroni revealed the location of golden plates inscribed with a history of pre-Columbian American peoples.
- The text describes Nephite and Lamanite civilizations from around 600 BCE to 421 CE, with *Jesus Christ* appearing to them after his resurrection.
- Organized into 15 books (Nephi, Jacob, Enos, etc.), each further subdivided into chapters and verses, mimicking biblical structure.
- Central narrative: the *Nephites* (righteous) and *Lamanites* (fallen) struggle over centuries; the *Lamanites* ultimately destroy the *Nephites*, becoming ancestors of Native Americans.
- The text emphasizes continuing revelation, rejecting the sufficiency of the Bible alone; *Jesus* appears as the central figure of all scriptural periods.
- *Joseph Smith's* translation methods remain debated; he reported using a *seer stone* and the golden plates (said to be returned to the angel).
- Fundamentally shaped *LDS* theology, including doctrines of premortal existence, eternal families, and temple ceremonies.
