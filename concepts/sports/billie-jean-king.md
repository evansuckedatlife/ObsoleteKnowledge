# Billie Jean King

## summary

*Billie Jean King* is an American tennis pioneer who won 12 Grand Slam singles titles and became a global icon for gender equality through her landmark "Battle of the Sexes" match in 1973. Her advocacy for equal pay and women's rights transcended tennis, making her one of sports's most influential figures.

## you gotta know

- Won 12 Grand Slam singles titles: one Australian Open, one French Open, six Wimbledons, and four US Opens.
- Won 39 Grand Slam titles across singles, doubles, and mixed doubles, including a record six Wimbledon doubles titles.
- Famous for the "Battle of the Sexes" exhibition match in 1973 where she defeated 55-year-old Bobby Riggs in straight sets, a watershed moment for women's sports and gender equality.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1966 and dominated the 1960s and 1970s; her six Wimbledon singles titles showcase her grass-court mastery.
- Co-founded the WTA (Women's Tennis Association) and fought tirelessly for equal prize money and tournament opportunities for women.
- Won 20 consecutive matches at Wimbledon (1966–1968) and appeared in 20 major finals, establishing her as one of tennis's all-time greats.
