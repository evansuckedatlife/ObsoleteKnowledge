# Arnold Palmer

## summary

*Arnold Palmer*, known as "The King," was an American professional golfer whose charismatic personality and attacking style made him the first golf superstar of the television era. Winning 7 major championships and nurturing an enormous fan base known as "Arnie's Army," he elevated golf from an elite sport to a mass-audience phenomenon from the 1950s through the 1970s.

## you gotta know

- Won 7 major championships: 4 Masters titles (1958, 1960, 1962, 1964), 2 US Opens (1960), and 1 Open Championship (1961).
- Famous for his aggressive, bold style and willingness to take risks—embodied by his "go for broke" attitude that endeared him to fans.
- Lost the 1962 US Open to Jack Nicklaus in a playoff, marking the generational transition from Palmer to Nicklaus dominance.
- Pioneered the concept of the modern athlete-celebrity through endorsements, particularly the "Arnold Palmer" drink (iced tea and lemonade).
- Won over 60 professional tournaments during his career, competing well into his 60s.
- Helped establish the Senior PGA Tour, remaining competitive and visible to fans decades into his career.
- Created a merchandising empire that made him one of the wealthiest athletes of his generation.
