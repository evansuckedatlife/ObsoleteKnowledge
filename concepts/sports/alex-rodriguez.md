# Alex Rodriguez

## summary

**Alex Rodriguez** was one of baseball's most talented shortstops and third basemen, joining the Yankees in 2004 and anchoring their offence for 12 seasons. His 696 career home runs (4th all-time), prolific postseason performances, and clutch hitting in the Jeter era made him a key figure in the Yankees' continued relevance, though his legacy was complicated by performance-enhancing drug allegations.

## you gotta know

- Hit 696 career home runs, 35 short of Barry Bonds' all-time record; his 2,086 RBIs rank among the all-time greats.
- Won the American League MVP award in 2007 with the Yankees, batting .314 with 54 home runs and 156 RBIs.
- Was a 14-time All-Star and won the 2009 World Series with the Yankees, his only championship.
- Hit .295 in postseason play with 6 home runs in the 2009 World Series championship run.
- Admitted to using performance-enhancing drugs from 2001–2003 and faced a 162-game suspension (later reduced) in 2013, damaging his reputation.
- Finished his career with the Yankees in 2016, providing veteran leadership during a rebuilding period.
