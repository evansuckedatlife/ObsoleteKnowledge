# Ben Hogan

## summary

*Ben Hogan* was an American professional golfer whose technical precision and fierce competitiveness made him one of the sport's greatest players of the mid-20th century. Despite a career nearly ended by a severe automobile accident in 1949, he won 9 major championships and became renowned for his mechanical perfection and mental toughness.

## you gotta know

- Won 9 major championships: 4 US Opens (1948, 1950, 1951, 1953), 2 Masters (1951, 1953), 2 PGA Championships (1946, 1948), and 1 Open Championship (1953).
- Suffered a nearly fatal car crash in February 1949 that left him with serious injuries; doctors doubted he would ever walk normally again.
- Returned to competition 11 months after the accident and won the US Open in 1950, demonstrating extraordinary physical and psychological resilience.
- Famous for his meticulous swing technique and obsessive practice habits; authored *Five Lessons: The Modern Fundamentals of Golf*, a foundational instructional text.
- Won three major championships in 1953—the Masters, the US Open, and The Open Championship—nearly capturing the Grand Slam (missing only the PGA Championship due to scheduling).
- Known as "Bantam Ben" for his small stature (5'8"), proving that size was irrelevant to dominance in golf.
- Pioneered the modern approach to golf instruction through technical analysis and filmed demonstrations.
