# Ben Roethlisberger

## summary

*Ben Roethlisberger* is an American football quarterback who spent his entire 18-season career with the Pittsburgh Steelers, earning two Super Bowl championships and becoming one of the franchise's most successful modern passers. Known for his physical toughness and large stature at the position, he combined arm strength with mobility to lead the Steelers through two decades of competitive excellence.

## you gotta know

- Drafted by the *Pittsburgh Steelers* in the first round (11th overall) of the 2004 NFL Draft out of Miami University, becoming the youngest starting quarterback to win a Super Bowl in his second season.
- Won two *Super Bowl* championships: *Super Bowl XL* (2005 season) and *Super Bowl XLIII* (2008 season), both with the Steelers.
- Named to six *Pro Bowl* teams (2007, 2008, 2010, 2011, 2014, 2018) and selected to two *All-Pro* rosters during his career.
- Threw for 64,088 career passing yards and 418 touchdowns, ranking among the franchise leaders and establishing himself as a consistent NFL performer.
- Known for his large frame (6'5", 240 lbs) and ability to extend plays outside the pocket through physical mobility, creating opportunities through improvisation.
- Served as the Steelers' starting quarterback for his entire 18-season career (2004–2021), unusual longevity with one franchise in the modern era.
- Retired as one of the most durable and stable quarterbacks in NFL history, providing consistent leadership throughout two decades with Pittsburgh.
