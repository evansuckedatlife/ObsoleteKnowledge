# Rafael Nadal

## summary

*Rafael Nadal* is a Spanish tennis icon who won 22 Grand Slam singles titles, surpassing Roger Federer's record as the greatest in tennis history. Renowned for his clay-court dominance and powerful topspin game, Nadal's 14 French Open titles are unmatched, and his relentless competitiveness defines the modern era.

## you gotta know

- Won 22 Grand Slam singles titles: 14 French Opens (2005–2006, 2007–2009, 2010–2014, 2017–2018, 2019–2022), four US Opens, two Wimbledons, and two Australian Opens.
- Fourteen French Open titles are the most by any player at a single Grand Slam tournament; his clay-court dominance is historically unparalleled.
- Known for his aggressive topspin forehand, muscular physique, and distinctive sleeveless shirts and tight shorts; his intensity and never-say-die attitude are iconic.
- Reached world No. 1 in 2008 and held it for 209 weeks; his peak years (2008–2013) overlapped with Federer and Djokovic, creating tennis's "Big Three."
- Competed at the Olympics and won gold medals in singles and doubles, adding to his legacy beyond the majors.
- Continued competing into his late 30s, retiring in 2024 after sustained success across three decades.

## connections

- [[roger-federer]] — his great rival; Nadal ultimately surpassed Federer's Grand Slam record.
- [[novak-djokovic]] — fellow member of the "Big Three" who also surpassed Federer.
- [[french-open]] — his signature tournament with 14 titles, the greatest single-tournament dominance in tennis history.
