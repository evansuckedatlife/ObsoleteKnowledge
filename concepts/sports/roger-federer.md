# Roger Federer

## summary

*Roger Federer* is a Swiss tennis legend who won 20 Grand Slam singles titles and dominated the 2000s and 2010s with an elegant all-court game and unmatched consistency. His record stood as the greatest in tennis history until Rafael Nadal surpassed him, but his influence and grace redefined modern tennis.

## you gotta know

- Won 20 Grand Slam singles titles across three decades (2003–2018): eight Australian Opens, six Wimbledons, five US Opens, and one French Open.
- Held world No. 1 ranking for a record 310 weeks and reached 27 consecutive Grand Slam semifinals (2004–2010).
- Won 103 career ATP titles, remarkable consistency; reached at least one Grand Slam final in 13 consecutive years (2003–2015).
- Known for his graceful, efficient serve-and-volley style and his ability to win on all surfaces, though Wimbledon (six titles) was his signature tournament.
- Reached age 36 still competing at the highest level; retired in 2022 after playing on the circuit into his late 30s.
- Rivaled and often lost to Rafael Nadal on clay; their epic 2008 Wimbledon final is considered one of tennis's greatest matches.
