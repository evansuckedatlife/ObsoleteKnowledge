# Mario Lemieux

## summary

*Mario Lemieux*, known as *The Magnificent One*, is a Canadian centre regarded as the second-greatest player in hockey history behind only Gretzky. He won two Stanley Cups with the *Pittsburgh Penguins* and achieved remarkable scoring consistency despite a career interrupted by injuries and illness.

## you gotta know

- Scored 690 goals and 1,033 assists for 1,723 career points, the third-highest total in NHL history.
- Won the Hart Trophy (MVP) three times and the Lester B. Pearson Award (then *Dodge Cup*) six times as the players' choice for best player.
- Led the *Pittsburgh Penguins* to back-to-back Stanley Cups in 1991 and 1992, transforming the franchise from league doormat to dynasty.
- Battled Hodgkin's lymphoma during his prime (1989–1990); remarkably, returned to play and was named team captain while recovering from cancer treatment.
- Known for extraordinary size (6'4"), reach, and skill; could score using either hand and possessed elite playmaking ability.
- Won the *Canada Cup* in 1987 in a legendary performance on the world stage, cementing his status as a national hero.
- Briefly served as owner of the Penguins (1999–2005), becoming the only player to own an NHL team.
