# John McEnroe

## summary

*John McEnroe* is an American tennis champion who won seven Grand Slam titles and dominated the 1980s with a serve-and-volley game and unprecedented volatility on court. His passionate intensity, legendary disputes with umpires, and rivalry with Björn Borg defined an era and made him one of tennis's most memorable figures.

## you gotta know

- Won seven Grand Slam singles titles: four US Opens (1979–1981, 1984) and three Wimbledons (1981, 1983, 1984).
- Known for his serve-and-volley game, superior court sense, and especially his left-handed serve, which was nearly unreturnable.
- Infamous for his on-court tantrums, arguments with umpires, and the phrase "You cannot be serious!" — which became his defining catchphrase.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1983 and held the ranking for 170 weeks; his competitive peak spanned the late 1970s through mid-1980s.
- Played doubles successfully, winning 16 Grand Slam titles in men's doubles and mixed doubles combined.
- The 1980 Wimbledon final against Björn Borg (lost 6–8, 5–7, 6–3, 7–6, 6–4) remains one of the most celebrated matches ever played.
