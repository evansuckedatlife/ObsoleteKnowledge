# Colin Kaepernick

## summary

*Colin Kaepernick* is an American football quarterback whose seven-season NFL career was defined by both athletic excellence as a mobile quarterback and his off-field activism centered on racial justice and police brutality. Drafted by the San Francisco 49ers in 2011, he led the team to a Super Bowl championship before his career was derailed following his decision to kneel during the national anthem.

## you gotta know

- Drafted by the *San Francisco 49ers* in the second round (36th overall) of the 2011 NFL Draft and developed into a starting quarterback in 2012.
- Led the 49ers to *Super Bowl XLVII* (2013 season), demonstrating exceptional performance in the playoffs and establishing himself as a dynamic dual-threat quarterback.
- Threw for 9,989 career passing yards and 88 touchdowns while rushing for 2,300+ yards and 13 rushing touchdowns, showcasing his mobile threat capability.
- Named to one *Pro Bowl* team (2013) and selected to the *All-Pro* roster (2013), his peak year before his activism and subsequent departure.
- Began kneeling during the national anthem before games in 2016 to protest police brutality and racial injustice, sparking nationwide debate about sports activism.
- Subsequently became a free agent and was effectively blackballed by NFL teams, transitioning out of professional football despite capable play and limited remaining age at quarterback.
- Remained a controversial and polarizing figure, with his activism influencing teams' decisions to not sign him despite his demonstrated NFL-caliber ability.
