# Arthur Ashe

## summary

*Arthur Ashe* was an American tennis pioneer and civil rights advocate who won three Grand Slam titles and broke the color barrier in professional tennis. Beyond his on-court achievements, his intelligence, grace, and activism made him one of sports's most influential and respected figures.

## you gotta know

- Won three Grand Slam singles titles: the Australian Open (1970), US Open (1968), and Wimbledon (1975).
- First African American man to win the US Open (1968) and Wimbledon (1975); broke through in an era when racial barriers in tennis were still significant.
- Known for his intelligent, all-court game; his versatility and tactical awareness set him apart from power-based contemporaries.
- Became a major figure in the civil rights movement and later a fierce advocate for AIDS awareness after contracting HIV from a blood transfusion in 1983.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1975 and won the Australian Open title at age 35, one of the oldest first-time majors winners.
- Died in 1993; his legacy extends far beyond tennis into humanitarianism, education, and social justice.
