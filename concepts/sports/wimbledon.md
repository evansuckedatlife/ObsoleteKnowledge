# Wimbledon

## summary

The Wimbledon Championships is the oldest Grand Slam tennis tournament in the world, held annually since 1877 at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club in London. Traditionally played on grass courts, it is renowned for its strict dress code requiring all-white clothing and its distinctive culture of strawberries and cream, establishing it as tennis's most prestigious and culturally significant event.

## you gotta know

- Held every June-July at the All England Club in the London suburb of Wimbledon, maintaining its original grass-court surface since inception.
- The only major played on grass, which produces distinctive playing characteristics—fast courts that favor serve-and-volley and require rapid reflexes.
- Enforces a strict all-white dress code for competitors, one of tennis's last formal traditions dating back to Victorian-era sensibilities.
- Roger Federer won a record eight Wimbledon titles (as of 2024), more than any other player in the tournament's history.
- The men's singles trophy is called the Gentlemen's Challenge Cup; women's singles winners receive the Venus Rosewater Dish.
- Associated with Wimbledon fortnight culture: traditional British traditions like royalty attendance, formal ceremony, and the consumption of strawberries and cream.
- Tournament traditions include the queue for general admission and the "Seeding Ceremony" where players are ranked and draw numbers.
- Many legendary champions have defined their careers partly through Wimbledon success, from Björn Borg and John McEnroe to Serena Williams and Novak Djokovic.

## connections

- [[roger-federer]] — eight-time champion and the most decorated player in Wimbledon history.
- [[serena-williams]] — won seven Wimbledon singles titles and dominated the tournament in the 2000s-2010s.
- [[rod-laver]] — won the calendar Grand Slam, including Wimbledon, and was one of the tournament's greatest grass-court players.
- [[arthur-ashe]] — broke color barriers in tennis and represented the tournament's evolution.
- [[billie-jean-king]] — won six Wimbledon singles titles and championed equal prize money between men's and women's tournaments.
- [[novak-djokovic]] — multiple-time Wimbledon champion who demonstrated the modern game's dominance on grass.
- [[tennis]] — the sport for which Wimbledon is the most culturally significant tournament.
