# Bill Tilden

## summary

*Bill Tilden* was an American tennis titan of the 1920s who won 10 Grand Slam singles titles and dominated the sport for over a decade with superior technical skill and mental toughness. Known as "Big Bill," Tilden elevated tennis to a major spectator sport and pioneered the modern approach to the game.

## you gotta know

- Won 13 Grand Slam singles titles (though record-keeping in the 1920s-30s makes exact count debated; generally cited as 10–13).
- Seven-time US Open champion and three-time Wimbledon champion; his consistency across surfaces was remarkable for the era.
- Introduced the modern tennis serve and topspin forehand, revolutionizing technique and setting a standard for future generations.
- Played professionally and semi-professionally throughout his career, unusual for tennis's amateur-dominated early era; turned fully professional later in life.
- Dominated the 1920s and early 1930s; at his peak, he was virtually unbeatable and defined the sport's golden age.
- Later became a renowned coach, writing influential instructional texts on tennis technique and strategy.

## connections

- [[wimbledon]] — won three titles; one of the era's dominant venues for him.
- [[us-open]] — his signature tournament with seven titles.
- [[rod-laver]] — later player who inherited Tilden's legacy of American/Commonwealth tennis dominance.
