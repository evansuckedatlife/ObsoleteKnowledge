# Walter Hagen

## summary

*Walter Hagen*, known as "The Haig," was an American professional golfer who won 11 major championships and became the first true professional superstar of golf. His charismatic personality, business acumen, and competitive dominance in the 1920s established the template for the modern professional golfer.

## you gotta know

- Won 11 major championships: 2 US Opens (1914, 1919), 5 PGA Championships (1921, 1924, 1925, 1926, 1927), and 4 Open Championships (1922, 1924, 1928, 1929).
- Dominated the 1920s, winning at least one major championship every year from 1921 to 1927—an extraordinary streak of dominance.
- First golfer to openly play as a professional without apology, elevating the status of professional golf and breaking down the amateur-only establishment.
- Known for his flamboyant lifestyle, showmanship, and ability to perform under pressure—qualities that made him a celebrity.
- Won 45 professional tournaments and established himself as a prolific winner across multiple decades.
- Pioneered the concept of the golf celebrity through endorsements, exhibitions, and world tours, establishing the business model for professional golfers.
- Reached the finals of the PGA Championship 5 consecutive times (1923–1927), demonstrating sustained excellence.
