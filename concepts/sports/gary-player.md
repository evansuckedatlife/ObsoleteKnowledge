# Gary Player

## summary

*Gary Player*, known as "The Black Knight" for his signature black attire, is a South African professional golfer who won 9 major championships and became the first player from the Southern Hemisphere to achieve dominance in international golf. His global prominence, competitive longevity, and emphasis on fitness transformed professional golf.

## you gotta know

- Won 9 major championships: 3 Masters (1961, 1974, 1978), 3 PGA Championships (1962, 1972, 1978), 2 Open Championships (1959, 1968), and 1 US Open (1965).
- Achieved the "Career Grand Slam"—winning all four major championships—in 1965, the third player to do so at that time.
- Won tournaments on six continents, establishing himself as the first truly global golf figure.
- Known for his extraordinary physical fitness and dedication to exercise, pioneering the athlete-as-fitness-model approach decades before it became standard.
- Competed professionally across multiple decades, winning a senior major championship at age 48.
- Won 23 PGA Tour events and numerous international tournaments, establishing himself as a worldwide ambassador for the sport.
- Broke down racial and geographic barriers in professional golf, representing South Africa during the apartheid era while becoming an international star.
