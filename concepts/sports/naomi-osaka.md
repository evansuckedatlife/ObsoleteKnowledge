# Naomi Osaka

## summary

*Naomi Osaka* is a Japanese-Haitian tennis champion who won four Grand Slam singles titles and became a global icon for her outspoken advocacy on mental health and social justice. Her powerful serve and baseline game established her as one of the leading players of the 2020s, with a legacy extending beyond tennis.

## you gotta know

- Won four Grand Slam singles titles: two Australian Opens (2019, 2021) and two US Opens (2018, 2020).
- Rose to world No. 1 in 2019 at age 21, the first player from Japan (men or women) to reach the top ranking and win a Grand Slam.
- Known for her powerful serve and forehand; her athletic ability and mental toughness enabled her to compete at the highest levels from an early age.
- Took a break from professional tennis in 2021 to focus on mental health, candidly discussing depression and anxiety—a pioneering move in normalizing mental health conversations in elite sports.
- Won the US Open final against Serena Williams (2018) at age 20, announcing herself as a future champion in a dramatic debut.
- Her activism extends to racial justice and social causes; she represents a new generation of athletes using their platform for advocacy.
