# Bobby Jones

## summary

*Bobby Jones* was an American amateur golfer whose dominance in the 1920s and early 1930s established golf as a major spectator sport and created the template for amateur excellence. His 13 major championship victories, all as an amateur, remain the gold standard for amateur achievement in a professional era.

## you gotta know

- Won 13 major championships as an amateur: 7 US Opens, 5 British Opens, and 1 US Amateur, a feat never matched by an amateur.
- Achieved the Grand Slam in 1930, winning all four major championships in a single calendar year—a record that stands unmatched.
- Retired from competitive golf at age 28 in 1930, at the height of his dominance, to pursue law and focus on his family.
- Co-founded the Masters Tournament in 1934 at Augusta National Golf Club, which remains one of golf's most prestigious events.
- Never played professional golf, remaining strictly amateur throughout his career, which was extraordinary for a player of his dominance.
- Inspired a national obsession with golf during the 1920s–1930s, making the sport a mainstream American pastime.
- Later became a golf course designer and was instrumental in the formation of the United States Golf Association.
