# Serena Williams

## summary

*Serena Williams* is an American tennis legend who won 23 Grand Slam singles titles, placing her among the greatest athletes in sports history. Her powerful baseline game, competitive ferocity, and dominance across four decades made her the most successful active player in tennis, with an unparalleled legacy of excellence.

## you gotta know

- Won 23 Grand Slam singles titles: seven Australian Opens, three French Opens, seven Wimbledons, and six US Opens.
- Achieved the "Serena Slam" (holding all four majors simultaneously, though not in the same calendar year) multiple times; dominated women's tennis from 2002 onward.
- Known for her exceptional serve, powerful groundstrokes, and mental toughness; her competitiveness and clutch performances are legendary.
- Won 14 Grand Slam titles in women's doubles (many with her sister Venus); their partnership won 14 majors together, dominating doubles tennis.
- Reached world No. 1 in 2002 and held it for 319 weeks; her sustained dominance across nearly two decades is unmatched in women's tennis.
- Returned to competitive play multiple times after pregnancy and injury, demonstrating extraordinary resilience and athleticism.

## connections

- [[venus-williams]] — her sister, partner, and rival; they shaped women's tennis together for decades.

- [[us-open]] — won six times; venue of multiple significant victories and comeback wins.
- [[australian-open]] — dominated with seven titles.
