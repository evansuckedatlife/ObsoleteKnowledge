# The New York Yankees

## summary

The New York Yankees are the most successful and storied franchise in Major League Baseball, based in the Bronx and playing at Yankee Stadium since 1923. Their 27 World Series championships (as of 2024) far exceed any other team, and they are known for their tradition of excellence, high payroll, and iconic players who have shaped the sport's history.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1901 as the Baltimore Orioles before relocating to New York in 1903 and joining the American League as the Highlanders, renamed the Yankees by 1913.
- Play their home games at Yankee Stadium in the Bronx, one of baseball's most iconic venues, opened in 1923 as "the House that Ruth Built."
- Have won 27 World Series titles and 40 American League pennants, more than any other franchise in baseball history.
- Dominated the sport under manager Joe McCarthy (1931–1946) and later under Casey Stengel (1949–1960), establishing a culture of winning.
- Babe Ruth transformed the franchise and the sport itself with his arrival in 1920, hitting the home run that changed baseball from a low-scoring, scientific game.
- Features numerous Hall of Famers and legendary players including Mickey Mantle, Joe DiMaggio, Lou Gehrig, Yogi Berra, Derek Jeter, and Mariano Rivera.
- Known for operating with consistently high payroll and aggressive player acquisitions, prioritizing winning over cost control.
- Their pinstriped uniforms and iconic interlocking "NY" logo, unchanged since 1909, are among baseball's most recognizable symbols.
