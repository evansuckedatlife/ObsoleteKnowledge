# Gordie Howe

## summary

*Gordie Howe*, nicknamed *Mr. Hockey*, is a legendary Canadian right-winger who dominated the sport over a 32-season career spanning from 1946 to 1980. His combination of scoring ability, physical toughness, and longevity made him the standard by which hockey greatness was measured before Gretzky rewrote the record book.

## you gotta know

- Scored 801 goals and 1,049 assists for 1,850 career points across regular season and playoffs combined, records that stood until Gretzky surpassed them.
- Won the Hart Trophy (MVP) six times and played on six Stanley Cup championship teams with the *Detroit Red Wings*.
- Played the majority of his career with the *Detroit Red Wings* (1946–1971), establishing the franchise's identity during its dominant era.
- Known for a ferocious physical game combined with finesse; his elbows and stick-work were legendary, earning him respect and fear in equal measure.
- Returned to professional hockey with the *Houston Aeros* of the WHA (World Hockey Association) at age 45, proving his durability and skill.
- Wore number 9, which became iconic in hockey culture.
- Played through a wrist injury for much of his career, demonstrating extraordinary toughness and pain tolerance.
