# Vladislav Tretiak

## summary

*Vladislav Tretiak* is a Soviet goaltender widely regarded as one of the greatest net minders of all time. His dominance during the Cold War era, including a legendary performance in the 1980 "Miracle on Ice" Olympics, made him an international icon and proof of Soviet hockey excellence.

## you gotta know

- Won three Olympic gold medals with the Soviet Union (1972, 1976, 1988), establishing himself as the premier goaltender of the Cold War hockey era.
- Posted a 0.0 goals-against average in the 1980 Winter Olympics when the U.S. defeated the Soviets in one of hockey's greatest upsets; despite the loss, Tretiak's performance was extraordinary.
- Won the World Championship gold medal ten times with the Soviet Union, dominating international competition throughout the 1970s and 1980s.
- Played exclusively in the Soviet league (CSKA Moscow) due to Cold War restrictions on player movement, despite being capable of competing in the NHL.
- Known for his acrobatic style, quick reflexes, and butterfly-influenced goaltending technique that presaged modern style.
- Wore number 20 and became a symbol of Soviet hockey prowess during a period of intense geopolitical rivalry.
- Finally allowed to sign in the NHL at age 39 (1989) with the *Chicago Blackhawks*, though his stay was brief; remained the only Soviet-trained goaltender to play at the highest American level for decades.
