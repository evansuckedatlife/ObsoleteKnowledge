## summary

**Don Mattingly** was a first baseman and the face of the Yankees during the lean years of the 1980s, known for consistent, powerful hitting and exemplary defence. Spending his entire 14-season career with the Yankees, he won the 1985 American League MVP award despite his teams' inability to reach the postseason.

## you gotta know

- Hit 222 home runs with 1,099 RBIs over his Yankees career; his .307 batting average and sweet left-handed swing made him a fan favourite.
- Won the American League MVP award in 1985, the first Yankee to win the award since 1962, and was a six-time All-Star.
- Led the league in doubles three times and holds the record for consecutive games with an extra-base hit (8 games in 1987).
- Nicknamed *The Hit Man*, embodying consistency and excellence at the plate despite playing on rebuilding Yankees teams.
- Spent his entire career with the Yankees but never reached the World Series, a career regret that defined his legacy.
- Suffered a back injury in his prime years that limited his power potential but he remained a professional hitter.
