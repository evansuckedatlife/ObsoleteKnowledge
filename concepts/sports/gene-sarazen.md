# Gene Sarazen

## summary

*Gene Sarazen* (born Eugenio Saraceni) was an Italian-American professional golfer who won 7 major championships and established himself as one of golf's foundational figures of the early 20th century. Known for inventing the sand wedge and for his longevity competing across five decades, he was instrumental in professionalizing the sport.

## you gotta know

- Won 7 major championships: 3 US Opens (1922, 1932, 1935), 2 PGA Championships (1922, 1923), 1 Masters (1935), and 1 Open Championship (1932).
- Invented the sand wedge in the 1930s, revolutionizing short-game technique and forever altering how golf is played.
- Hit one of golf's most famous shots—the "double eagle" (albatross) at the 15th hole during the 1935 Masters, helping him win that tournament.
- Competed professionally from 1921 until well into the 1970s, demonstrating extraordinary competitive longevity across five decades.
- Won 39 PGA Tour events and numerous international tournaments, establishing himself as a global competitor.
- Pioneered teaching and instruction, mentoring younger players and establishing the pedagogy of professional golf.
- Remained a visible and respected figure in golf throughout his life, attending tournaments and mentoring into his 90s.
