# Wayne Gretzky

## summary

*Wayne Gretzky*, known as *The Great One*, is widely regarded as the greatest ice hockey player of all time. A Canadian centre and winger who dominated the sport from the 1980s into the 1990s, he rewrote the NHL record book with unprecedented scoring prowess, winning four Stanley Cups and four Hart Trophies as league MVP.

## you gotta know

- Scored 894 NHL goals and 1,963 assists for 2,857 career points, records that stood unchallenged for decades and may never be broken.
- Won the Hart Trophy (MVP) four consecutive seasons (1980–1984), a feat unmatched before or since in the modern era.
- Led the *Edmonton Oilers* to four Stanley Cups (1984, 1985, 1987, 1988), establishing the franchise as a dynasty.
- Revolutionized play-making and positioning; often positioned himself in the "office" behind the goal line to create scoring chances rather than pursuing the puck directly.
- Traded to the *Los Angeles Kings* in 1988, transforming hockey's popularity on the West Coast and helping legitimize the sport in Southern California.
- Played centre and right wing with a low, slap-shot style that became iconic and was widely imitated.
- Holds records for most career assists (1,963) and most consecutive seasons with 40+ goals.
