# Rod Laver

## summary

*Rod Laver* is an Australian tennis legend who remains the only player to win the calendar-year Grand Slam twice — in 1962 as an amateur and in 1969 as a professional. His dominance in the 1960s redefined excellence in men's tennis and bridged the amateur-professional divide of the era.

## you gotta know

- Won all four Grand Slam tournaments in a single calendar year in 1962 (first player ever) and again in 1969 (only player to accomplish this twice).
- Left-handed player known for exceptional speed, court coverage, and a powerful forehand; won 11 Grand Slam singles titles overall.
- Turned professional in 1962, then spent seven years banned from Grand Slam competition due to the amateur-professional divide (reinstated in 1968).
- Dominated the Australian Open, winning it six times, and was competitive at Wimbledon, the US Open, and the French Open.
- His competitive era roughly spanned 1960–1974, overlapping with and ultimately overshadowing Ken Rosewall and other contemporaries.
- The Australian Open's main stadium, Rod Laver Arena, is named in his honour.
