# Björn Borg

## summary

*Björn Borg* is a Swedish tennis icon of the 1970s and early 1980s who won 11 Grand Slam titles and dominated clay courts with five French Open crowns. His calm demeanor, fierce competitiveness, and rivalry with John McEnroe created some of tennis's most iconic moments, including the legendary 1980 Wimbledon final.

## you gotta know

- Won 11 Grand Slam singles titles: five French Opens (1974–1978, 1981) and six Wimbledons (1976–1980, 1981), dominating both clay and grass.
- Reached the Wimbledon final four consecutive years (1976–1979), winning five of six appearances in the tournament.
- Known for his ice-cool temperament, muscular physique, and distinctive long hair and headband; his demeanor contrasted sharply with McEnroe's volatility.
- Retired abruptly in 1982 at age 26 at the height of his powers, citing burnout; attempted a comeback in the 1990s without significant success.
- The 1980 Wimbledon final against McEnroe (won by Borg 1–6, 7–5, 6–3, 6–7, 8–6) is considered one of the greatest matches ever played.
