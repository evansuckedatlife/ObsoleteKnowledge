# Lou Gehrig

## summary

**Lou Gehrig** was a first baseman and American League legend best known for his record 2,130 consecutive games played, earning the nickname *The Iron Horse*. Over 17 seasons with the Yankees, he was one of baseball's most consistent power hitters and a cornerstone of the franchise's 1920s–1930s dominance.

## you gotta know

- Played 2,130 consecutive games from 1925 to 1939, a record that stood for 56 years until Cal Ripken Jr. broke it in 1995.
- Hit 493 home runs and drove in 1,995 runs; his career .340 batting average ranks among the all-time greats.
- Won the Triple Crown in 1934 (leading the league in home runs, RBIs, and batting average), one of the sport's rarest feats.
- His career was cut short by amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), now often called *Lou Gehrig's Disease*.
- Delivered his iconic "luckiest man" farewell speech on July 4, 1939, to a sold-out Yankee Stadium crowd.
- Played alongside Babe Ruth during the Yankees' most dominant era, forming one of baseball's greatest duos.
