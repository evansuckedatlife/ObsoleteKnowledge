# Eddie Shore

## summary

*Eddie Shore* was a Canadian defenseman and one of hockey's earliest superstars, dominating the sport from the 1920s through the 1940s. His physical, aggressive play and legendary toughness made him the standard-bearer for defenseman excellence in the era before Bobby Orr revolutionized the position.

## you gotta know

- Won the Hart Trophy (MVP) four times (1933, 1935, 1936, 1938), the most by any defenseman and a record that stood for decades.
- Played the majority of his career with the *Boston Bruins* (1926–1940), establishing the franchise as a championship team during the original six-team era.
- Won the Stanley Cup twice with Boston (1929, 1939) and was a pioneer of the Bruins' dynasty during hockey's early professional era.
- Known for his ferocious physical play, frequent fights, and willingness to dominate opponents through intimidation; wore number 2 and accumulated scars that became legendary.
- Suffered multiple serious injuries including a fractured jaw, several concussions, and a separated shoulder, yet returned repeatedly to dominate play.
- Exceptional skater for his era, combining speed with power; revolutionized defensive play through aggressive checking and puck rushing.
- Later managed and coached, founding the Springfield Indians in the American Hockey League and serving as owner.
