# Martina Navratilova

## summary

*Martina Navratilova* is a Czech-American tennis icon who won 18 Grand Slam singles titles and became one of the sport's greatest players with an aggressive serve-and-volley game and unmatched athleticism. Her seven Wimbledon titles and 59 major titles across singles, doubles, and mixed doubles set standards of dominance unmatched in women's tennis.

## you gotta know

- Won 18 Grand Slam singles titles: three Australian Opens, four French Opens, seven Wimbledons, and four US Opens (1983–1984, 1986–1987).
- Won 59 Grand Slam titles across all categories (singles, doubles, mixed doubles), the highest total by any player; her dominance in doubles rivaled her singles record.
- Defected from Czechoslovakia to the United States in 1975, becoming a pioneering advocate for LGBTQ+ rights in sports; her openness about her identity was groundbreaking.
- Known for her serve-and-volley game, exceptional athleticism, and her fierce rivalry with Chris Evert across a 16-year span.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1978 and held it for a then-record 186 weeks; dominated the late 1970s and 1980s.
- Continued competing at high levels into her 40s; her longevity was unprecedented for women's tennis at the time.
