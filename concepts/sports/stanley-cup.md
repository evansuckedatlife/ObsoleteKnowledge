# Stanley Cup

## summary

The Stanley Cup is ice hockey's most prestigious championship trophy, awarded annually to the winner of the National Hockey League (NHL) playoff tournament. Donated in 1892 by Canadian Governor General Lord Stanley of Preston, it is one of the oldest trophies in professional sports and has become an enduring symbol of hockey excellence and tradition.

## you gotta know

- First awarded in 1893 and named after Lord Stanley of Preston, who donated it to recognize excellence in Canada's national sport.
- The physical trophy consists of a bowl (Permanent Bowl), bands engraved with team names, and a base that travels to winning teams for celebration.
- Each player on the Stanley Cup-winning team gets one day with the trophy in the off-season to celebrate with family and in their hometown—a cherished tradition.
- The Montreal Canadiens have won it a record 24 times, including five consecutive championships from 1956–1960.
- The New York Islanders won four consecutive Stanley Cups (1980–1983), an feat matched only by the Montreal Canadiens and Edmonton Oilers.
- Wayne Gretzky and the Edmonton Oilers won four consecutive Stanley Cups in the 1980s, establishing themselves as the decade's dominant team.
- The trophy is missing teeth from being bitten, dented from celebrations, and bears scars reflecting its role as hockey's most celebrated championship artifact.
- Teams must win a best-of-seven playoff series against three opponents (starting with Wild Card rounds in modern format) to hoist the Cup.

## connections

- [[wayne-gretzky]] — led the Edmonton Oilers to four consecutive Stanley Cups and is considered hockey's greatest player.
- [[bobby-orr]] — revolutionized the defenseman position and won the Cup twice with the Boston Bruins in the early 1970s.
- [[gordie-howe]] — won the Cup four times and helped establish it as hockey's most important trophy.
- [[mario-lemieux]] — won the Cup three times and revitalized the Pittsburgh Penguins franchise.
- [[ice-hockey]] — the sport for which the Stanley Cup is the premier championship.
