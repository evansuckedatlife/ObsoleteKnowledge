# Drew Brees

## summary

*Drew Brees* is an American football quarterback recognized as one of the most accurate and prolific passers in NFL history. Signed by the New Orleans Saints in 2006 after the team's relocation following Hurricane Katrina, he led the franchise to a Super Bowl championship in 2009 and spent his entire prime with New Orleans, becoming synonymous with the team's return to prominence.

## you gotta know

- Drafted by the *San Diego Chargers* in 2001 (second round) and spent his first five seasons there before signing with the *New Orleans Saints*.
- Won the *Super Bowl XLIV* championship (2009) with the *Saints*, leading the team to its first championship in franchise history just four years after relocating from San Diego.
- Threw for 80,357 career passing yards and 571 touchdowns, both among the all-time NFL leaders; was the first quarterback to eclipse 80,000 yards.
- Named to 13 *Pro Bowl* teams and won one *NFL MVP* award (2008), establishing himself as a model of consistency and durability.
- Renowned for his accuracy and decision-making, leading multiple playoff runs and defining an era of Saints football throughout the 2000s and 2010s.
- Played the majority of his career with one franchise (15 seasons with New Orleans), unusual among modern Hall-of-Fame caliber quarterbacks.
- Remained with the Saints through age 41, demonstrating sustained excellence before finishing his career with one season elsewhere.
