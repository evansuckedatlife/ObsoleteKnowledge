# Byron Nelson

## summary

*Byron Nelson* was an American professional golfer whose extraordinary dominance in the early 1940s, including an unmatched winning streak, established him as one of golf's all-time greats. His 5 major championships and calm demeanor made him an influential figure in establishing modern golf's competitive standards.

## you gotta know

- Won 5 major championships: 2 Masters (1937, 1942), 1 US Open (1939), 1 Open Championship (1955), and 1 PGA Championship (1940, 1945).
- In 1945, won 18 PGA Tour events in a single season and had a streak of 11 consecutive tournament victories—records that have never been matched.
- Competed professionally from 1932 until 1946, then semi-retired at age 34 to focus on ranch management and family, remaining competitive into his later years.
- Known for his smooth, rhythmic swing and unflappable demeanor under competitive pressure.
- Pioneered the modern golf swing technique, influencing generations of players through his style and teachings.
- Served as mentor and teacher to numerous professional golfers, including Jack Nicklaus and Tom Watson.
- His consistent performance across the 1930s–1940s established the template for professional excellence before the television era.
