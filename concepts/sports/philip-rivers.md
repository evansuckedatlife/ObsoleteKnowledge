# Philip Rivers

## summary

*Philip Rivers* is an American football quarterback who spent 16 of his 17 seasons with the San Diego/Los Angeles Chargers franchise, defining the team's identity through his competitive fire and prolific passing arm. Though he never won a Super Bowl despite reaching the championship game, he earned consistent Pro Bowl selections and became one of the era's most durable and productive passers.

## you gotta know

- Drafted by the *San Diego Chargers* in the first round (fourth overall) of the 2004 NFL Draft out of North Carolina State University.
- Named to eight *Pro Bowl* teams (2007, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2013, 2014) and selected to three *All-Pro* rosters during his career peak.
- Threw for 63,440 career passing yards and 421 touchdowns, ranking among the all-time leaders and establishing himself as the franchise's greatest passer.
- Led the Chargers to the AFC West division title multiple times and one *Super Bowl XLII* appearance (2006 season), though the team lost to the *New England Patriots*.
- Played with the Chargers through their relocation from San Diego to Los Angeles following the 2016 season, demonstrating remarkable consistency and franchise loyalty.
- Known for his intense competitiveness and clutch performances, frequently leading game-winning drives and comeback victories throughout his career.
- Finished his career with brief stints with the *New York Chargers* (Indianapolis) and remaining with the franchise through 2020 before retiring.
