# Ken Dryden

## summary

*Ken Dryden* is a Canadian goaltender and Harvard graduate who rose to dominance in the 1970s with the *Montreal Canadiens*, winning six Stanley Cups in an eight-year career marked by intellectual sophistication and exceptional play.

## you gotta know

- Won six Stanley Cups with the *Montreal Canadiens* (1971, 1973, 1976, 1977, 1978, 1979) in just eight seasons, more cups per season than nearly any goaltender in history.
- Won the Conn Smythe Trophy (playoff MVP) four times, appearing in six straight Finals and becoming the greatest playoff performer of his era.
- Recorded a 2.24 goals-against average over 397 games and a .922 save percentage, exceptionally low for the high-scoring 1970s.
- Won the Vezina Trophy (best goaltender) five times in six years (1973–1979), establishing goaltending dominance during the Canadiens dynasty.
- Unique among hockey players for his intellectual approach to the game; wrote extensively about hockey philosophy and later became a broadcaster and author.
- Pioneered the "stand up" goaltending style with excellent positioning and rebound control, influencing generations of net minders.
- Attended Cornell University on an academic scholarship and remained a Rhodes Scholar finalist, embodying the rare athlete-intellectual.
