# 1912 Summer Olympics

## summary

The *1912 Summer Olympics* in Stockholm, Sweden, marked a expansion of the Games' scale and included the rise of American athlete *Jim Thorpe*, whose dominance in track and field events—and his later loss of medals due to professionalism rules—became a defining Olympic narrative.

## you gotta know

- Held in Stockholm, Sweden; featured nearly 2,400 athletes from 28 nations—nearly ten times the size of the 1896 Games.
- *Jim Thorpe*, a Native American athlete, won gold in both the pentathlon and decathlon, displaying exceptional versatility and strength.
- Thorpe's medals were later rescinded (in 1913) when it was revealed he had played semi-professional baseball—then a violation of amateur status rules—though they were posthumously restored in 1983.
- The first time *women were permitted to compete*, though only in swimming and diving; track and field remained male-only for decades.
- The modern pentathlon (five military-derived events) was introduced, creating a test of all-around athleticism.
- Sweden hosted efficiently and the Games ran smoothly, establishing Stockholm as a well-organized Olympic city.
- The program included 102 events across 14 sports, setting a new standard for Olympic scope.
