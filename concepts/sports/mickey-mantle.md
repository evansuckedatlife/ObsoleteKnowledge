# Mickey Mantle

## summary

**Mickey Mantle** was a switch-hitting outfielder and the Yankees' centerpiece from the 1950s through the early 1960s, combining power from both sides of the plate with blazing speed and graceful defence. His 536 career home runs, World Series heroics, and magnetic personality made him the face of the Yankees' post-DiMaggio era.

## you gotta know

- Hit 536 home runs, including 18 home runs in 12 World Series (the most in postseason history); his power from both left and right sides was unmatched.
- Won the Triple Crown in 1956, batting .353 with 52 home runs and 130 RBIs — one of only seven players to achieve this feat in the modern era.
- Named American League MVP three times (1956, 1957, 1962) and was a 20-time All-Star.
- Played 18 seasons with the Yankees, winning 12 pennants and 7 World Series titles.
- Nicknamed *The Mick*, beloved for his raw talent and his struggle with injuries that might have made him even greater.
- Succeeded Joe DiMaggio as the Yankees' superstar and cultural icon, maintaining the franchise's dynasty through the 1950s–1960s.
