# Margaret Smith Court

## summary

*Margaret Smith Court* is an Australian tennis legend who holds the record for most Grand Slam singles titles with 24, surpassing all competitors until Rafael Nadal. Her extraordinary dominance across three decades and her 64 Grand Slam titles across all categories make her one of the sport's greatest and most underrated figures.

## you gotta know

- Won 24 Grand Slam singles titles (record at the time): 11 Australian Opens, five French Opens, three Wimbledons, and five US Opens.
- Won 64 Grand Slam titles across singles, doubles, and mixed doubles—the highest total in tennis history.
- Dominated the 1960s and early 1970s; her peak years saw her win multiple majors in a single calendar year, nearly achieving a Calendar Slam.
- Known for her powerful serve and baseline game; her athleticism and competitive intensity were exceptional for her era.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1963 and held it for 220 weeks; her consistency across surfaces was remarkable.
- Won the Australian Open a record 11 times, establishing it as her signature tournament, though her record has been approached by later champions.
