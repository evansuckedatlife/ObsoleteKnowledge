# Pete Sampras

## summary

*Pete Sampras* is an American tennis champion who dominated the 1990s with 14 Grand Slam titles and held the record for most Grand Slams for 12 years until Roger Federer surpassed him. Known for his powerful serve and all-court game, Sampras won seven Wimbledon titles and embodied American tennis excellence in the modern era.

## you gotta know

- Won 14 Grand Slam singles titles between 1990 and 2002, holding the all-time record until Federer broke it.
- Seven-time Wimbledon champion; the grass-court specialist whose serve was devastatingly effective on fast surfaces.
- Won the Australian Open, US Open, and French Open once each; his consistency at Wimbledon set him apart.
- Reached the world No. 1 ranking in 1993 and held it for a then-record 286 weeks.
- Retired in 2002 while still ranked in the top 10, unusual for his era; later married tennis player Bridgette Wilson.
- Notably defeated Andre Agassi in the 1990 US Open final and Ivan Lendl in the 1992 US Open in their respective breakthrough moments.
