# Venus Williams

## summary

*Venus Williams* is an American tennis champion who won seven Grand Slam singles titles and pioneered women's tennis in the modern era alongside her sister Serena. Her powerful serve and aggressive game revolutionized women's tennis, and her dominance in the 1990s and 2000s made her an icon of athleticism and grace.

## you gotta know

- Won seven Grand Slam singles titles: one Australian Open, two French Opens, five Wimbledons (1994–1998, 2005, 2007–2008, 2009), and two US Opens (1997, 2000).
- Reached world No. 1 in 2002 and held it for 11 weeks; her peak years saw her dominate alongside her sister Serena.
- Known for her powerful serve (consistently among the fastest), exceptional athleticism, and her distinctive beaded hair braids and colorful on-court outfits.
- Won 14 Grand Slam titles in women's doubles (many with her sister Serena), making the Williams sisters a formidable force in doubles tennis.
- Competed in seven Olympic Games (a remarkable longevity record) and won multiple gold medals across singles and doubles events.
- Inspired a generation of players with her powerful game and presence; her rivalry and partnership with Serena defined women's tennis.

## connections
