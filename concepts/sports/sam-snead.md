# Sam Snead

## summary

*Sam Snead*, nicknamed "Slammin' Sam," was an American professional golfer with one of the longest competitive careers in golf history, spanning over five decades. His 7 major championships and 82 professional tournament victories made him one of the most prolific winners in the sport, known for his graceful, powerful swing and accessibility to fans.

## you gotta know

- Won 7 major championships: 3 Masters (1949, 1952, 1954), 3 PGA Championships (1942, 1949, 1951), and 1 British Open (1946).
- Won 82 official PGA Tour events, tied with Jack Nicklaus for the most in tour history (by official count).
- Competed professionally from 1936 until 1974, demonstrating extraordinary longevity—he was competitive across six different decades.
- Famous for his natural, effortless swing and ability to adapt his game across different course conditions and styles.
- Never won the US Open despite finishing second four times, which remains one of golf's most notable "what-ifs."
- Pioneered the Senior PGA Tour upon its formation, continuing to win tournaments well into his 60s.
- Known for his folksy personality, colorful attire, and connection with working-class fans who saw him as accessible.
