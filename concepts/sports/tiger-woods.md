# Tiger Woods

## summary

*Tiger Woods* is an American professional golfer whose 15 major championship victories and revolutionary playing style transformed golf in the late 20th and early 21st centuries. Born in 1975, he dominated the sport from 1997 through the 2010s, becoming the most prominent athlete in golf history and the first player of multiracial descent to achieve such dominance in the sport.

## you gotta know

- Won the Masters at age 21 in 1997, his first professional major, defeating the field by 12 strokes—the largest margin in Masters history.
- Captured 15 major championships, closing within three of Jack Nicklaus's all-time record of 18.
- Went on an extraordinary streak without a major victory from 2008 to 2019 (11 years), then won the 2019 Masters to end the drought.
- Suffered a near-fatal car crash in February 2021 that left him with multiple serious injuries; recovered to compete professionally again.
- Known for his psychological intensity, aggressive style, and remarkable ability to come from behind in major tournaments.
- Won the PGA Championship (5 times), US Open (3 times), and The Open Championship (3 times), in addition to his Masters titles.
- Earned an estimated $2 billion in career earnings, making him one of the wealthiest athletes in history.
