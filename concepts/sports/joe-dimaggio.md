## summary

**Joe DiMaggio** was an outfielder and one of baseball's most complete players, starring for the Yankees from 1936 to 1951. His grace, consistency, and 56-game hitting streak in 1941 made him an icon of the sport's golden age, and his quiet, dignified demeanor off the field elevated his cultural standing beyond baseball.

## you gotta know

- Achieved a 56-game hitting streak in 1941, the longest in major league history by 12 games; it is considered one of baseball's most unbreakable records.
- Hit .325 lifetime and drove in 1,537 runs; a career .977 OPS places him among the all-time greats.
- Won three American League MVP awards (1939, 1947, 1948) and was a 13-time All-Star.
- Played 13 seasons with the Yankees and won 10 American League pennants and 9 World Series championships.
- Nicknamed *Joltin' Joe* and *The Yankee Clipper*, epitomising elegance and power in the same player.
- Succeeded Babe Ruth and Lou Gehrig as the franchise's marquee star, anchoring the Yankees through the 1940s.
