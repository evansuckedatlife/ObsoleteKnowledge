# Reggie Jackson

## summary

**Reggie Jackson** was an outfielder and one of baseball's most dominant sluggers, best remembered for his dramatic postseason heroics—particularly his three home runs in Game 6 of the 1977 World Series—that earned him the nickname *Mr. October*. His five-season stint with the Yankees (1977–1981) revitalised the franchise and proved his ability to deliver under pressure.

## you gotta know

- Hit three consecutive home runs in Game 6 of the 1977 World Series off three different pitchers, cementing his legend as a clutch performer.
- Career 563 home runs (5th all-time at his retirement) and 2,584 hits; his postseason performance was proportionally even more dominant.
- Known as *Mr. October* for his disproportionately great performances in October playoff baseball.
- Played for the Yankees during their 1977–1978 back-to-back World Series championship run after a 15-year drought.
- Had a combative but ultimately productive relationship with Yankees manager Billy Martin, creating tension and drama that captivated New York.
- Hall of Fame first-ballot in 1993, with his Yankees achievements central to his legend.
