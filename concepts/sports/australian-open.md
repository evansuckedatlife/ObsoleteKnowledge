# Australian Open

## summary

The Australian Open is one of tennis's four Grand Slam tournaments, held annually in January at Melbourne Park in Melbourne, Australia. It is the southernmost Grand Slam and the first in the calendar year, played on hard courts and known for attracting a diverse international field as one of the sport's most accessible major championships.

## you gotta know

- Held in January at Melbourne Park (formerly Flinders Park) on hard courts, making it the first Grand Slam of the calendar year.
- Originally called the Australasian Championships when first held in 1905, it evolved into the Australian Championships and then the Australian Open in 1987.
- Novak Djokovic has won it more times than any other player—ten men's singles titles, establishing his dominance in the modern era.
- Margaret Smith Court won the tournament a record 11 times across singles and doubles, holding the distinction of the most Australian Open titles of any player.
- Took its current form as the Australian Open in 1987 when it moved from grass to hard courts and began being held in January rather than December.
- The tournament is known for extreme heat, with the court temperature regularly exceeding 50 degrees Celsius (122 Fahrenheit), leading to heat regulations and player retirements.
- Features smaller crowds compared to Wimbledon but offers the most relaxed atmosphere of the four majors, with accessibility and casual dress encouraged.
- Rod Laver and Margaret Smith Court, both Australians, won the calendar Grand Slam (all four majors) in their respective eras.
