# Babe Ruth

## summary

**Babe Ruth** revolutionised baseball in the 1920s as the most dominant home-run hitter of his era, transforming the sport from a contact-hitting game into one centred on power. His charisma, larger-than-life personality, and prolific slugging made him the first genuine sports celebrity, and his 27 seasons with the Yankees established the franchise as baseball's most storied dynasty.

## you gotta know

- Hit a record 714 home runs in his career, a mark that stood until 1974; pioneered the modern power-hitting style that defines baseball today.
- Called his shot in Game 3 of the 1932 World Series, allegedly pointing to the outfield before hitting a home run to that exact spot.
- Played for the Boston Red Sox (1914–1919) before the Yankees acquired him in a trade that many credit with cursing the Red Sox for 86 years.
- Batted a career .342 with 2,873 hits; his OPS of 1.164 remains one of the highest ever recorded.
- Nicknamed *The Bambino* and *The Sultan of Swat*, embodying both artistry and raw power.
- Known off-field for his wild lifestyle, drawing fans as much for his celebrity as for his performance.
- Finished his career with 10 World Series appearances and 7 championships (mostly with the Yankees).

## connections

- [[lou-gehrig]] — his teammate and fellow Yankees titan, the pair anchoring the franchise's dynasty.
- [[joe-dimaggio]] — succeeded Ruth as the Yankees' marquee power hitter and franchise icon.
- [[whitey-ford]] — pitcher on Ruth's era Yankees who benefited from his team-building excellence.
- [[the-new-york-yankees]] — the franchise he transformed into a perennial championship contender.
