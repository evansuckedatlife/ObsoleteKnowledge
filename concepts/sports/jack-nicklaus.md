# Jack Nicklaus

## summary

*Jack Nicklaus*, known as "The Golden Bear," is an American professional golfer who won 18 major championships—the most by any player until Tiger Woods approached the record—establishing himself as perhaps the greatest player of golf's modern era. His 46-year career, spanning from 1962 to 2005, demonstrated unparalleled consistency and dominance across five decades.

## you gotta know

- Won 18 major championships across all four majors: 6 Masters, 5 US Opens, 3 Open Championships, and 4 PGA Championships.
- Finished second in major championships a record 19 times, showing remarkable consistency in contending for titles.
- Won his first professional major, the 1962 US Open at Oakmont, unseating Arnold Palmer in a playoff, signaling a generational shift.
- Captured the Masters at age 46 in 1986, making him the oldest winner of a major championship.
- Pioneered modern course management and strategic golf, emphasizing precision and course design knowledge.
- Designed over 300 golf courses worldwide, creating a second career as a golf architect.
- Achieved the "Grand Slam" (all four majors in a calendar year) in 1966 as an amateur golfer in spirit, though he was professional.
