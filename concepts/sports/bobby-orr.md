# Bobby Orr

## summary

*Bobby Orr* is a Canadian defenseman and arguably the greatest blue-liner in hockey history. His revolutionary skating ability and offensive contributions transformed how the position was played, winning two Stanley Cups with the *Boston Bruins* in the 1970s.

## you gotta know

- Scored 270 goals and 645 assists for 915 points as a defenseman, an unprecedented offensive totals for the position.
- Won the Hart Trophy (MVP) twice, the only defenseman to win it in the modern era before it became reserved for forwards.
- Led the *Boston Bruins* to Stanley Cups in 1970 and 1972, including an iconic overtime goal in the 1970 Finals (often considered the most famous goal in hockey history).
- Famous for his explosive stride and skating ability; revolutionized the defenseman role by joining the attack and carrying the puck into the offensive zone.
- Won the Norris Trophy (best defenseman) eight consecutive years (1968–1975), a record unlikely to be surpassed.
- Career cut short by serious knee injuries at age 30, resulting in early retirement; nonetheless remains one of sport's most influential figures.
- Wore number 4 and was a pioneer of the aggressive, attacking defenseman model that dominated hockey in the 1970s.
