# Whitey Ford

## summary

**Whitey Ford** was the Yankees' ace pitcher for 16 seasons (1950–1967), posting one of the most successful records in postseason history and anchoring the franchise's dynasty. His 236 regular-season wins, unparalleled clutch pitching, and leadership in the clubhouse made him *The Chairman of the Board*.

## you gotta know

- Won 236 games with the Yankees (regular season) and an additional 10 in the World Series—the most postseason wins ever by a pitcher.
- Had a 2.75 career ERA and led the American League in ERA in 1956 (2.47) and 1958 (2.01).
- Pitched for 11 American League pennant winners and 6 World Series champions, an unprecedented run of success.
- Started the 1961 All-Star Game and was a six-time All-Star, commanding respect across the league.
- Nicknamed *The Chairman of the Board* for his cool leadership and mastery of the pitcher's mound in high-pressure moments.
- Retired as a Yankee and remains the franchise's all-time postseason ace.
