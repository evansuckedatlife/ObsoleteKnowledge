# 1896 Summer Olympics

## summary

The first modern *Olympic Games*, held in Athens, Greece, revived the ancient Greek athletic competition as an international event. Organized by the International Olympic Committee founded by Baron Pierre de Coubertin, these Games established the format of modern Olympic competition and sparked a global revival of athletic competition that would grow throughout the twentieth century.

## you gotta know

- Held in Athens, Greece, in April 1896; about 240 athletes from 14 nations participated — a modest start by modern standards.
- The host nation Greece finished at the top of the medal count, winning 10 gold medals and benefiting from home advantage.
- Revived the ancient Games tradition, last held in 393 CE — de Coubertin believed athletic competition could foster peace and international understanding.
- Women were prohibited from competing; the Games were male-only, reflecting Victorian attitudes toward sport and gender.
- American athletes dominated in many events, particularly track and field, establishing the US as an Olympic power from the outset.
- The marathon was run for the first time, commemorating the legendary run of a Greek soldier from Marathon to Athens bearing news of victory.
- Gymnastics, weightlifting, wrestling, and shooting featured alongside track and field, creating a diverse program.
