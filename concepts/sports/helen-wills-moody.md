# Helen Wills Moody

## summary

*Helen Wills Moody* was an American tennis champion of the 1920s and 1930s who won 19 Grand Slam singles titles, dominating the era and establishing herself as one of the sport's greatest players. Her technical mastery, powerful game, and graceful demeanor made her an international icon and a pioneering female athlete.

## you gotta know

- Won 19 Grand Slam singles titles: one Australian Open, four French Opens, eight Wimbledons, and six US Opens.
- Won nine consecutive Wimbledon matches (1927–1938) without losing, and 19 consecutive US Open matches, establishing records of dominance that stood for decades.
- Known as "Little Miss Poker Face" for her calm demeanor and emotional control on court; her technical mastery and baseline game set the standard.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1927 (the era before ranking systems tracked precisely, but she was universally acknowledged as the best) and maintained supremacy for over a decade.
- Won all major tournaments across hard, clay, and grass surfaces; her versatility was exceptional for the era.
- Married tennis player Frederick Moody (1929) and later Edmund Leach; her name evolved through her marriages, complicating her record's recognition in later eras.
