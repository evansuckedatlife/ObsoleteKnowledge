# Terry Sawchuk

## summary

*Terry Sawchuk* was a Ukrainian-Canadian goaltender and one of the greatest netminders in hockey history. His 103 shutouts remain an NHL record, and his 447 wins defined excellence in goal during an era of monster workloads and minimal equipment protection.

## you gotta know

- Recorded 103 career shutouts in 971 games, the most in NHL history and unlikely to be approached in the modern era with today's scoring rates.
- Won four Stanley Cups with the *Detroit Red Wings* (1952, 1954, 1955, 1997) and one with the *Toronto Maple Leafs* (1967).
- Played roughly 40+ games per season at his peak, facing brutal workloads without modern backup systems; recorded multiple sub-2.00 goals-against averages.
- Won the Vezina Trophy (best goaltender) four times and the Hart Trophy (MVP) once, the only goaltender to win MVP in the post-1960 era.
- Known for his aggressive, acrobatic style and willingness to leave the crease to handle the puck; suffered multiple career-threatening injuries including a serious eye injury.
- Played with minimal mask protection for much of his career, enduring facial cuts, broken nose, and other trauma from ungloved shots.
- Won the Stanley Cup late in his career at age 36 with the *Toronto Maple Leafs* in 1967, proving his durability across eras.
