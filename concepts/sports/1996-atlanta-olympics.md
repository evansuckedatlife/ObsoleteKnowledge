# 1996 Summer Olympics

## summary

The *1996 Summer Olympics* in Atlanta, Georgia, marked the centennial of the modern Olympic Games and became a symbol of the post-Cold War world, with the Soviet Union competing as independent nations following the Soviet collapse. The opening ceremony featured *Muhammad Ali* lighting the Olympic flame—an iconic moment of athletic redemption and American cultural reconciliation.

## you gotta know

- Held in Atlanta, Georgia, during the centennial year of the 1896 Athens Games; the Games returned to the American South for the first time since 1904.
- *Muhammad Ali*, the legendary boxer whose political activism had made him controversial decades earlier, lit the Olympic cauldron—a moment that symbolized his rehabilitation in American public opinion.
- The Soviet Union no longer existed as a political entity; former Soviet republics competed independently, and many competed as "Unified Team" or as invited nations, representing the geopolitical transformation of the post-Cold War era.
- Gymnastics superstar *Shannon Miller* and swimmers like *Amy Van Dyken* became American icons; the Games showcased dominant U.S. athletic talent across multiple disciplines.
- The Games were marked by the Centennial Olympic Park bombing, a domestic terrorist attack that killed two people and wounded over 100, though the perpetrator was not caught until 2003.
- China emerged as a major athletic power, winning a significant medal count and signaling a shift in global Olympic dynamics.
- The 1996 Olympics occurred during a period of American economic growth and cultural confidence following the end of the Cold War rivalry.
