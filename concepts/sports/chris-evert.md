# Chris Evert

## summary

*Chris Evert* is an American tennis legend who won 18 Grand Slam singles titles with a dominant baseline game built on exceptional consistency and precision. Her rivalry with Martina Navratilova defined women's tennis in the 1970s and 1980s, and her seven French Open titles remain a testament to her clay-court mastery.

## you gotta know

- Won 18 Grand Slam singles titles: two Australian Opens, seven French Opens, three Wimbledons, and six US Opens.
- Known for her baseline game, exceptional consistency, and especially her forehand drive; her mental toughness and competitive spirit were legendary.
- Reached world No. 1 in 1975 and held it for 260 weeks; dominated women's tennis from the early 1970s through the 1980s.
- Won 1,309 professional matches (the most by any female player in history) with a 75% win rate, an extraordinary achievement of durability.
- Seven French Open titles showcase her clay-court dominance; she reached the French Open final 13 times.
- Played over 150 times against Martina Navratilova (their rivalry spanned 16 years), with each winning roughly half their encounters.
