# Yogi Berra

## summary

**Yogi Berra** was a Hall of Fame catcher and one of the Yankees' most beloved figures, combining exceptional hitting (.285 career average), clutch postseason performance, and wise, often paradoxical off-field observations that made him a cultural icon. His 19 World Series appearances and 10 championships with the Yankees made him baseball's winningest player.

## you gotta know

- Played 19 seasons with the Yankees as catcher and occasional outfielder, winning 10 World Series titles (more than any other player in history).
- Hit 358 career home runs despite being a catcher, a position traditionally dominated by pitching and defence.
- Was a 15-time All-Star and three-time American League MVP (1951, 1954, 1955).
- Known for wise, paradoxical sayings such as "It ain't over till it's over" and "Nobody goes to that restaurant anymore—it's too crowded."
- Managed the Yankees to the 1964 World Series and later served as a coach across multiple franchises, remaining a Yankees ambassador for decades.
- Caught two perfect games (Don Larsen's in the 1956 World Series and others), demonstrating his exceptional rapport with pitchers.
