# Lee Trevino

## summary

*Lee Trevino*, nicknamed "The Merry Mex," is a Mexican-American professional golfer who won 6 major championships and established himself as one of the most charismatic and entertaining competitors in golf. His folksy humor and exceptional ball-striking made him a fan favorite from the 1960s through the 1980s.

## you gotta know

- Won 6 major championships: 2 US Opens (1968, 1971), 2 PGA Championships (1974, 1984), and 2 Open Championships (1971, 1972).
- Won three major championships in five weeks during 1971—the US Open, The Open Championship, and the Canadian Open—a remarkable streak.
- Known for his remarkable consistency off the tee and an unorthodox swing that generated exceptional accuracy.
- Famous for his comedy and accessibility to fans, earning sponsorships and television popularity through his charismatic personality.
- Struck by lightning in 1975 during an event, suffering serious injuries but recovering to continue his career with undiminished competitiveness.
- Won 29 PGA Tour events and remained competitive on the Senior PGA Tour well into his later years.
- Pioneered the path for Latino players in professional golf, becoming a cultural icon and ambassador for the sport.
