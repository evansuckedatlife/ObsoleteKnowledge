# Andre Agassi

## summary

*Andre Agassi* is an American tennis legend who won eight Grand Slam titles and achieved the Career Golden Slam, completing all four majors and Olympic gold in his career. His aggressive baseline game, iconic yellow ball wear, and humble autobiography made him one of tennis's most recognizable and beloved figures.

## you gotta know

- Won eight Grand Slam singles titles: four Australian Opens, two US Opens, one French Open, and one Wimbledon (1995).
- Achieved the Career Golden Slam — winning all four Grand Slam tournaments and Olympic gold; one of only two men to do so.
- Known for his aggressive baseline game powered by exceptional groundstrokes and his characteristic long hair, colorful outfits, and competitive fire.
- Rose from world No. 3 to No. 1 in 1995 and dominated the Australian Open in the late 1990s and early 2000s, winning four titles there.
- Retired and returned to professional play twice; his autobiography *Open* (2009) became a bestseller and candidly discussed his struggles and triumphs.
- Defeated Pete Sampras multiple times, notably in the 1994 French Open.
