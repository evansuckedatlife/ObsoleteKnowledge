# Steffi Graf

## summary

*Steffi Graf* is a German tennis champion who won 22 Grand Slam singles titles and achieved the Calendar Year Golden Slam in 1988, winning all four majors and Olympic gold in a single year. Her aggressive baseline game and competitive dominance in the late 1980s and early 1990s made her one of tennis's greatest players.

## you gotta know

- Won 22 Grand Slam singles titles: four Australian Opens, six French Opens, seven Wimbledons, and four US Opens.
- Achieved the Calendar Year Golden Slam in 1988 (all four majors plus Olympic gold), the only player ever to accomplish this feat in singles tennis.
- Known for her powerful baseline game, exceptional footwork, and aggressive forehand; won titles across all three surface types (hard, clay, grass).
- Reached world No. 1 in 1987 and held it for 377 weeks; her peak years (1987–1990) saw her win four consecutive major championships.
- Her 1988 season is often considered the greatest single season in women's tennis history.
- Retired in 1997 at age 28 while still near her peak, unusual for her era but reflecting the physical toll of her dominance.
