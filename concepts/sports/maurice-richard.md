# Maurice Richard

## summary

*Maurice Richard*, nicknamed *The Rocket*, was a Canadian right-winger and icon of the *Montreal Canadiens* who dominated the NHL from the 1940s through the 1950s. The first player to score 50 goals in a season, he became a national hero in Quebec and a pioneer of modern hockey aggression and passion.

## you gotta know

- Scored 544 goals in 978 regular-season games, a goal-per-game ratio nearly unmatched in his era; added 82 playoff goals, many in crucial moments.
- First player to score 50 goals in a single season (1944–45), in just 50 games, a benchmark that defined excellence for generations.
- Won six Stanley Cups with the *Montreal Canadiens* (1943, 1944, 1946, 1953, 1956, 1957), establishing the franchise's early dominance.
- Known for his fierce competitive fire, intensity, and willingness to fight; his temper and stick-work drew criticism and suspensions.
- Served as captain of the Canadiens for much of his career; his retirement at age 34 was mourned as an end to an era.
- Wore number 9 and became the symbol of French-Canadian pride in professional hockey.
- Established the Richard Riot (1955) when his suspension sparked protests in Montreal, highlighting his cultural significance.
