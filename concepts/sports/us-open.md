# US Open

## summary

The US Open is one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments and one of golf's five major championships, held annually in the United States. In tennis, it is played on hard courts at the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in New York since 1978, while the golf tournament rotates among courses nationwide and is administered by the United States Golf Association (USGA).

## you gotta know

- The US Open Tennis tournament is held in late August-early September at the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in Flushing Meadows, Queens, New York.
- Played on DecoTurf hard courts, the fastest surface of the four Grand Slams, which produces the highest-scoring games and favors aggressive playing styles.
- Serena Williams has won the most US Open tennis titles with six singles championships, dominating the American major throughout her career.
- The tennis tournament moved to its current location in 1978 and has been held there continuously, making it the longest-running major at one venue.
- Billie Jean King's name was added to the USTA facility in 2006, honoring her contributions to tennis and equal opportunity in sports.
- The golf US Open is held in June and rotates among courses approved by the USGA, known for its difficult course setup and championship-level challenge.
- Tiger Woods has won the golf US Open three times and is one of the few golfers to capture multiple major championships at this event.
- Jack Nicklaus won the golf US Open four times and holds the record for most major championships overall in professional golf.
