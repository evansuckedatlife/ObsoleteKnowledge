# Bobby Hull

## summary

*Bobby Hull*, known as *The Golden Jet*, was a Canadian left-winger and one of hockey's most electrifying scorers. His blazing speed, powerful slap shot, and blonde hair made him a cultural icon in the 1960s and early 1970s with the *Chicago Blackhawks*.

## you gotta know

- Scored 610 goals and 549 assists for 1,159 career points, third-most goals in NHL history at the time of his retirement.
- First player to score 50 goals in a season in the modern era (1961–62), shattering Maurice Richard's record of 50 in 50 games with 50 in 84.
- Won the Hart Trophy (MVP) twice and the Art Ross Trophy (leading scorer) three times, establishing himself as an elite offensive force.
- Won the Stanley Cup with the *Chicago Blackhawks* in 1961 and played on multiple Finals teams; captained the Blackhawks for much of his career.
- Pioneered the modern slap shot with his physical strength and timing, making it a feared offensive weapon in the hands of future players.
- Jumped to the *Winnipeg Jets* of the WHA (World Hockey Association) in 1972, legitimizing the rival league and proving his marketability.
- Known for his speed, toughness, and willingness to fight; wore number 9 and later number 16 with the Jets.
