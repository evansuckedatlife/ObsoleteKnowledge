# Derek Jeter

## summary

**Derek Jeter** was the Yankees' shortstop and captain for 20 seasons (1995–2014), revitalising the franchise after 18 years without a championship. His elegant play, clutch hitting in the postseason, leadership off the field, and iconic "The Jump" play made him the greatest Yankee of the modern era and a legend of the game.

## you gotta know

- Hit .310 career with 3,465 hits (5th all-time), 260 home runs, and was a 14-time All-Star and five-time World Series champion.
- Led the Yankees to five championships (1996, 1998, 1999, 2000, 2009), anchoring every deep postseason run.
- Made iconic defensive plays including the "Flip Play" in the 2001 playoffs (leaping to cut off a ball in the outfield and flipping it underhanded to catcher Jorge Posada).
- Nicknamed *The Captain* for his leadership and "Mr. November" for his postseason heroics.
- Spent his entire 20-season career with the Yankees, the last of the franchise's iconic one-team legends.
- Inducted into the Hall of Fame in 2020, earning the highest percentage of votes of any first-ballot player at that time.
