# The Phantom of the Opera

## summary

*The Phantom of the Opera* is an Andrew Lloyd Webber musical adaptation of Gaston Leroux's gothic novel, which premiered in London in 1986 and became the longest-running musical in Broadway history. It tells the story of Christine Daaé, a young opera singer seduced and manipulated by the mysterious Phantom living beneath the Paris Opera House, creating a melodramatic love triangle with tragic consequences.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Andrew Lloyd Webber* with lyrics by *Charles Hart* and *Richard Stilgoe*, the musical emphasizes Webber's gift for soaring, memorable melodies.
- **"The Music of the Night"** is the Phantom's hypnotic serenade to Christine, one of the most famous musical theatre ballads, showcasing Webber's romantic orchestrations.
- The chandelier crash at the end of Act I is the show's most iconic visual moment—a feat of technical theatre that became the musical's signature.
- The show ran for 35 years on Broadway (1988–2023), holding the record for longest-running musical until *Chicago* was revived and surpassed it in 2022.
- Webber's score blends classical operatic tradition with pop sensibility, creating melodies that are simultaneously sophisticated and immediately catchy.
- The love triangle between Christine, the Phantom, and Raoul echoes gothic Romantic literature while exploring themes of obsession, passion, and self-sacrifice.
