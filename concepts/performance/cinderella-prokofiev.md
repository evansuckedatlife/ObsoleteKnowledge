# Cinderella (Prokofiev)

## summary

*Prokofiev's Cinderella* is a full-length fairy-tale ballet, choreographed first by Konstantin Sergeyev in 1945 for the Kirov Ballet and subsequently restaged by Frederick Ashton (1948) and other major choreographers. The score is one of Prokofiev's most lyrical and melodic, featuring memorable themes for each character, waltzes, and a magical transformation scene that brings the beloved fairy tale to theatrical life with warmth and spectacle.

## you gotta know

- *Sergei Prokofiev* composed the score in 1940–44, with a wealth of melodic invention including the famous *Waltz of the Flowers*, the Glass Slipper theme, and character variations for the Fairy Godmother and the stepsisters.
- *Frederick Ashton*'s 1948 Royal Ballet version became the most beloved; it emphasizes the fairy-tale magic and Cinderella's emotional journey from drudgery to triumph, with Ashton's characteristic lyricism and humor.
- The Fairy Godmother is a magician character, danced by a male dancer in some versions and a female by convention in others; her transformation of rags into a gown and pumpkin into a carriage is a technical showcase.
- The famous *Grand Waltz* at the ball is a virtuosic pas de deux where Cinderella and the Prince dance in sustained, swooping movements; the waltz tune is one of Prokofiev's most celebrated melodies.
- The midnight deadline, when Cinderella must flee and lose her glass slipper, is the dramatic turning point that propels the final act reunion and marriage.
- Unlike earlier versions (e.g., Petipa's), Prokofiev's score supports a full-length dramatic structure with continuous musical motivation and depth.
- The ballet has been staged by nearly every major company; Ashton's version remains the standard for many Western ensembles.
