## summary

*Pulcinella* is a one-act ballet choreographed by Léonide Massine in 1920 for the Ballets Russes, with music by Igor Stravinsky adapted from 18th-century Italian composers (primarily Pergolesi). The work is a lighthearted comedy rooted in *commedia dell'arte*, featuring the stock character Pulcinella (a resourceful servant) and his romantic entanglements, combining Stravinsky's witty neo-classical score with Massine's comic choreography and traditional masked-character conventions.

## you gotta know

- *Stravinsky* based his score on fragments by *Pergolesi* and other Italian masters, creating a neo-classical work that playfully reinvents Baroque material with 20th-century dissonance and humor.
- *Léonide Massine* choreographed the work and danced the title role, bringing slapstick comedy and detailed characterization to classical ballet form; Massine was famous for blending mime, character work, and virtuosity.
- The plot is a traditional *commedia dell'arte* scenario: Pulcinella, a cunning servant, engages in romantic intrigue with multiple characters while using disguises and tricks to escape trouble and win the day.
- The character of Pulcinella wears the iconic white costume and black mask of the traditional stock character, grounding the ballet in theatrical tradition.
- The ensemble features other *commedia* archetypes: lovers, soldiers, and servants engaged in farcical complications and synchronized ensemble dances that parody classical ballet forms.
- The score features moments of mock-heroic orchestration and abrupt tonal shifts, reflecting Stravinsky's neo-classical aesthetic of playful subversion.
- Premiered at the Paris Opéra on 15 May 1920, *Pulcinella* demonstrated Stravinsky's ability to reinvent historical material and established Massine as a master of character choreography.

## connections

- [[stravinsky]] — the composer who created the neo-classical, witty score.
- [[massine]] — the choreographer-dancer who established the character-dance tradition in ballet.
- [[commedia-dell-arte]] — the theatrical tradition and stock-character source material.
- [[ballets-russes]] — the impresario company that commissioned and premiered the work.
- [[the-firebird]] — another early Stravinsky ballet, though more modernist and less neo-classical than Pulcinella.
