# A Chorus Line

## summary

*A Chorus Line* is a 1975 Pulitzer Prize-winning musical that documents the lives of dancers auditioning for a Broadway chorus. Composed by *Marvin Hamlisch* with lyrics by *Edward Kleban*, it revolutionized musical theatre by making the everyday struggles of working performers the substance of serious drama, and its 15-year run made it Broadway's longest-running musical for decades.

## you gotta know

- Created by director-choreographer *Michael Bennett* in collaboration with a group of dancers, the show emerged from taped interviews with real Broadway dancers about their lives, hopes, and rejections.
- The musical has almost no plot: it consists entirely of dancers introducing themselves, sharing personal stories, and auditioning for a chorus line in a Broadway show.
- **"One Singular Sensation"** is the anthem of commitment to dance as a calling; **"What I Did for Love"** became a standard and is often sung at graduations and farewell events.
- **"At the Ballet"** presents three dancers recounting their first ballet experiences in childhoods defined by poverty, abuse, and yearning—a moment of collective vulnerability that became the show's emotional anchor.
- The score blends disco, rock, and pop styles with a unifying theatrical vocabulary; it is fundamentally integrated with Bennett's choreography, making music and movement inseparable.
- Opened on Broadway in 1975 and ran for 15 years with 3,389 performances, becoming the longest-running musical in history until *Les Misérables* surpassed it.

## connections

- [[marvin-hamlisch]] — Composer whose accessible, singable melodies matched the dancers' authentic emotional vulnerability.
- [[edward-kleban]] — Lyricist whose direct, non-poetic language gave the dancers' stories dignity and specificity.
- [[michael-bennett]] — Director-choreographer whose vision of dancers' lives as worthy material transformed musical theatre.
- [[broadway-theatre]] — The subject and setting of the musical; a recursive exploration of what it means to perform.
- [[les-miserables-musical]] — Successor in socially conscious, narrative-driven musicals addressing working-class experience.
