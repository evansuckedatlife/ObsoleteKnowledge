# Sweeney Todd

## summary

*Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street* is a 1979 musical by *Stephen Sondheim* that blends Grand Guignol horror with operatic ambition, telling the story of a wrongly imprisoned barber who returns to Victorian London to murder his customers and bake them into meat pies. It remains one of musical theatre's darkest and most sophisticated works, proving the form could tackle tragedy and moral horror with the depth of opera.

## you gotta know

- Composed and written by *Stephen Sondheim*, the musical transforms a penny-dreadful tale into a sophisticated exploration of vengeance, class resentment, and the corruption of justice.
- The narrative follows Benjamin Barker (Sweeney Todd) seeking revenge against the judge who destroyed his life; he partners with Mrs. Lovett, a baker who profits from disposing of his victims in her meat pies.
- **"The Ballad of Sweeney Todd"** frames the story as a cautionary tale sung by the Beggar Woman; **"Pretty Women"** is a seductive duet between Todd and the judge unaware of his true identity.
- Sondheim's score features intricate counterpoint, dark humor, and songs that advance both character development and plot with surgical precision—each lyric is densely layered.
- The 1979 production starred Len Cariou as Todd and Angela Lansbury as Mrs. Lovett; the 2023 revival with Josh Groban brought the show to a new generation.
- Thematically, the musical interrogates the ethics of revenge, exploring whether justice obtained outside the law can ever be redemptive, and whether desperation excuses complicity in murder.
