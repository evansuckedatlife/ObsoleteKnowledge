# Don Quixote

## summary

*Don Quixote* is a comic ballet choreographed by Marius Petipa in 1869, adapting Cervantes' novel of a delusional Spanish knight's misadventures. The ballet transforms the picaresque narrative into a spectacular showcase for virtuosic dancing, with bright humor, vivid character work, and a famous pas de deux between the hero and Dulcinea that stands among the most technically demanding in the classical canon.

## you gotta know

- *Marius Petipa* choreographed the original version, establishing its character-driven, comedy-forward tone and the technical requirements for the leading roles.
- The Don himself is a character role danced by a male soloist, involving pantomime, quirky movement, and comic timing rather than classical virtuosity.
- *Kitri* (Dulcinea's maid) is the female lead, danced in a vibrant, flirtatious style with rapid footwork and sparkling technique; the pas de deux features famous *promenade en attitude* and fouettés.
- The *tavern scene* and carnival sequences showcase ensemble work, character dances, and folk rhythms (Spanish-flavored steps and rhythms), adding color and narrative context.
- *Ludwig Minkus* composed a lively, accessible score that follows the action closely and includes memorable variations and character themes.
- The ballet climaxes in a wedding celebration where the Don's dreams (of Dulcinea) and reality (her engagement to Basilio) merge into spectacle and celebration.
- The work has been restaged hundreds of times; the Bolshoi version and George Balanchine's 1965 adaptation for New York City Ballet remain widely performed.
