# Hamilton

## summary

*Hamilton: An American Musical* is a 2015 hip-hop and pop musical by *Lin-Manuel Miranda* that reimagines the life of founding father Alexander Hamilton through contemporary rap, R&B, and musical theatre styles. It became a cultural phenomenon, breaking attendance records and earning a Pulitzer Prize while making American history thrillingly immediate and relevant to new generations.

## you gotta know

- Composer and lyricist *Lin-Manuel Miranda* spent years developing the show, inspired by Ron Chernow's biography of Hamilton and his own experience as a second-generation immigrant.
- The cast is deliberately diverse, with actors of color playing white historical figures, challenging traditional narratives of who belongs in American history.
- Features breakout songs like **"My Shot"** (Hamilton's youthful determination), **"Wait for It"** (Aaron Burr's obsession with legacy), and **"The Room Where It Happens"** (political intrigue).
- The climactic duel between Hamilton and Aaron Burr mirrors actual 1804 events but gains dramatic weight through the musical's exploration of legacy and mortality.
- Premiered Off-Broadway in 2015, then moved to Broadway where it won 11 Tony Awards and grossed over $300 million domestically.
- The music blends hip-hop with traditional Broadway orchestration, using rapid-fire lyrics and internal rhymes to convey the frenetic pace of political ambition.
