## summary

*Prokofiev's Romeo and Juliet* is a full-length ballet adapting Shakespeare's tragic play, choreographed in multiple versions starting with Leonid Lavrovsky's 1940 Soviet production. The score, one of the most lyrical in ballet, transforms the romance and feudal violence into a sweeping tragedy of star-crossed lovers, their families' hatred, and inevitable sorrow.

## you gotta know

- *Sergei Prokofiev* composed the score in 1935–36, creating one of his most celebrated works with rich orchestration and memorable themes for each character and scene.
- Leonid Lavrovsky's 1940 premiere at the Kirov Ballet in Leningrad established the most influential choreography, later adapted by companies worldwide.
- The ball scene at the Capulet house is the dramatic centerpiece, where Romeo and Juliet meet and fall in love amid a grand court celebration.
- Tybalt's aggressive, angular movements contrast sharply with Romeo's tender, flowing passages; the famous sword fight is a turning point in the drama.
- The tomb scene, where Juliet awakens as Romeo dies, is the emotional climax — a pas de deux of grief and desperate love as both lovers perish.
- Kenneth MacMillan choreographed a celebrated 1965 version for the Royal Ballet, emphasizing intimacy and teenage passion, and remains widely performed.
- The piece demonstrates how ballet can carry narrative weight equal to opera or spoken drama, with music and movement perfectly aligned.

## connections
