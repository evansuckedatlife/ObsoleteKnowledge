# George Balanchine

## summary

*George Balanchine* was a Russian-American choreographer who revolutionized ballet through speed, athleticism, and abstract movement, casting off the narrative constraints of classical ballet to create a purely American neoclassical style. He founded New York City Ballet and created over 400 works, establishing himself as the most influential choreographer of the twentieth century.

## you gotta know

- Born in St. Petersburg in 1904 as *Giorgi Balanchivadze*; studied at the Imperial Ballet Academy and danced with the Ballets Russes under Sergei Diaghilev.
- Pioneered *neoclassical ballet*, stripping away elaborate sets and costumes to focus on the human body and the music's architecture.
- Co-founded *New York City Ballet* in 1948 with Lincoln Kirstein; elevated it to a world-class company despite chronic underfunding.
- Collaborated intensively with composer *Igor Stravinsky*; created masterworks including *Apollo*, *Serenade*, and *The Nutcracker* (reimagined as a one-act ballet).
- Defined the *"Balanchine body"*—long, lean, high-extension lines; speed of footwork; emphasis on the upper spine and neck in a distinctly American aesthetic.
- Created abstract ballets with no plot, breaking the 19th-century mold of pantomime-heavy storytelling; his *Agon* (1957) remains among the most innovative ballets ever made.
- Worked with hundreds of dancers including *Suzanne Farrell*; his eye for talent and ability to extract new movement vocabularies from dancers shaped an entire generation.

## connections

- [[stravinsky]] — his greatest artistic partner; they created a revolutionary body of work together.
- [[new-york-city-ballet]] — the institution he built and directed for decades.
- [[the-nutcracker]] — his version, premiered 1954, became the most-performed ballet in the world.
- [[swan-lake]] — he inherited this classical legacy and reinterpreted it through a neoclassical lens.
- [[martha-graham]] — contemporary and rival; Graham's modern dance paralleled his ballet innovation.
