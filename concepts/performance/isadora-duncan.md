# Isadora Duncan

## summary

*Isadora Duncan* was an American dancer and choreographer who single-handedly created modern dance by rejecting the rigid constraints of classical ballet and returning to what she saw as the "natural" movement of the human body. Her barefoot dancing, flowing costumes, and emotional authenticity made her a revolutionary figure in the early twentieth century and the spiritual mother of modern dance as an art form.

## you gotta know

- Born in 1877 in San Francisco; began her career in classical ballet but found its conventions restrictive and artistically stifling.
- Famously discarded pointe shoes, corsets, and tutus in favor of bare feet, a simple tunic, and movement derived from the torso and center of gravity rather than turned-out positions.
- Drew inspiration from *Greek vases* and ancient sculpture, seeking what she believed was a return to "natural" human movement aligned with emotion and music.
- Performed across Europe to great acclaim, becoming an international symbol of artistic rebellion and personal freedom; her life was as controversial as her art.
- Created a movement vocabulary emphasizing fluidity, weight, gravity, and emotional authenticity—principles that became foundational to all modern dance that followed.
- Established schools in Berlin, Moscow, and Paris to preserve and transmit her technique and philosophy to future generations of dancers.
- Her life ended tragically in 1927, but her influence on dance—and on the liberation of the human body from Victorian constraints—remained profound and permanent.
