# Les Misérables

## summary

*Les Misérables* is an epic musical adaptation of *Victor Hugo*'s 1862 novel, composed by *Claude-Michel Schönberg* with lyrics by *Alain Boublil*. It premiered in Paris in 1980 and became an international phenomenon, telling the story of a man's redemption against the backdrop of French revolution and social injustice through sweeping orchestrations and emotionally charged songs.

## you gotta know

- Based on Victor Hugo's sprawling novel of the same name, following Jean Valjean's escape from prison and his pursuit by Inspector Javert across decades of French history.
- **"Bring Him Home"** is Valjean's heart-stopping solo about sacrificing his own happiness for his adopted daughter Cosette; universally recognized as the show's emotional apex.
- The barricade scene, centered on the doomed 1832 June Rebellion, transforms student revolutionaries into tragic heroes through songs like **"Do You Hear the People Sing?"** and **"One Day More."**
- Originally opened in Paris in 1980, then ran in London's West End and Broadway, where it became the longest-running musical in history at the time.
- The score blends classical operatic ambition with melodic, singable themes accessible to popular audiences—the show's global reach owes much to this balance.
- Themes of redemption, social justice, and the costs of idealism resonate across cultures and generations, explaining its enduring appeal.
