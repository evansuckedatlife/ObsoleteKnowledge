# Fiddler on the Roof

## summary

*Fiddler on the Roof* is a 1964 musical by Jerry Bock (music) and Sheldon Harnick (lyrics) set in the Jewish village of *Anatevka* in tsarist Russia. Centered on the patriarch *Tevye* and his five daughters, it explores tradition, love, social change, and displacement, ultimately capturing the dissolution of a way of life as pogroms and migration uproot the community.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Jerry Bock* with lyrics by *Sheldon Harnick* — their most enduring collaboration, earning nine Tony Awards (1965).
- The opening number "Tradition" immediately establishes both the rigidity and the vulnerability of Jewish communal life in the Pale of Settlement.
- Famous songs include "If I Were a Rich Man" (often performed as a comic patter song), "Matchmaker, Matchmaker," "Sunrise, Sunset," "To Life," and "Anatevka."
- *Tevye*, the protagonist, engages in comic and philosophical dialogue with God, and his debates with each daughter about marriage choices drive the plot's emotional arc.
- Set against the historical backdrop of Russian pogroms (1903–1906), the musical uses folk idiom and klezmer-influenced orchestration to ground the story in Jewish cultural authenticity.
- Ran on Broadway for 3,242 performances (1964–1972), the longest-running Broadway show at the time, and has become a canonical staple.
- The 1971 film, directed by *Norman Jewison*, won three Academy Awards and introduced the musical to global audiences; *Zero Mostel* originated the role of *Tevye*.
