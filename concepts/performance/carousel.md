# Carousel

## summary

*Carousel* is a 1945 musical by Richard Rodgers (music) and Oscar Hammerstein II (lyrics) adapted from Ferenc Molnár's play *Liliom*. Set in a New England coastal town, it tells the tragic love story between *Billy Bigelow*, a rough-edged carousel barker, and *Julie Jordan*, a young textile-mill worker, exploring themes of redemption, sacrifice, and the unending bond between parent and child across the boundary of death.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Richard Rodgers* with lyrics by *Oscar Hammerstein II* — their second major collaboration, deepening their artistic partnership beyond *Oklahoma!*
- Features "If I Loved You" (a ballad duet establishing the central romance), "June Is Bustin' Out All Over" (a joyful ensemble number), "Soliloquy" (an extended dramatic monologue revealing *Billy*'s interior struggle), and "You'll Never Walk Alone" (the closing hymn).
- The "Soliloquy" is one of musical theatre's most challenging arias: eight minutes of *Billy*'s uninterrupted consciousness as he grapples with fatherhood, shame, and desperation, culminating in his decision to commit suicide.
- Opens with an extended dance sequence (choreography by *Agnes de Mille*) rather than dialogue, establishing a visual vocabulary of character and emotion.
- The plot culminates in *Billy*'s death during a robbery attempt, yet the musical extends into the afterlife: *Billy* is given a chance to return and help his daughter at a crucial moment, reframing the entire narrative as an act of love.
- Opened on Broadway in 1945 and ran for 890 performances; the 1956 film, also directed by *Henry King*, starred *Gordon MacRae* and *Shirley Jones*.
- Often cited as the darkest and most emotionally profound of the R&H musicals, with a protagonist who is neither wholly good nor wholly redeemable.
