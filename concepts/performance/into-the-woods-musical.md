# Into the Woods

## summary

*Into the Woods* is a 1987 musical by *Stephen Sondheim* that interweaves and subverts classic Grimm fairy tales—Cinderella, Rapunzel, Jack and the Beanstalk, Little Red Riding Hood—to explore the moral complexities hidden beneath happy endings. It challenges the notion that wishes, once granted, lead to contentment, and interrogates the costs of desire with Sondheim's characteristically intricate wit and harmonic sophistication.

## you gotta know

- Composed and written by *Stephen Sondheim*, with book by *James Lapine*, the musical deconstructs fairy-tale tropes by following characters after their "happily ever after" is achieved.
- The central narrative follows a Baker and his Wife seeking a child; they enter the woods to gather magical ingredients, intersecting with other familiar fairy-tale characters pursuing their own wishes.
- **"Into the Woods"** is the sung prelude establishing the show's tone of adventure and moral uncertainty; **"Wishes"** is a delightful but darkly ironic ensemble number about the dangers of getting what you desire.
- The second act is the show's masterwork: traditional happy endings collapse into tragedy, loss, and moral reckoning, as characters discover that wishes satisfy immediate wants but cannot ensure happiness or resolve deeper problems.
- Sondheim's score blends playful, fairy-tale-like melodies with complex harmonic language; the lyrics employ intricate rhyme schemes and metrical sophistication, matching the show's intellectual depth.
- Themes include the tension between individual desire and collective good, parental responsibility, the seduction of power, and the recognition that life does not culminate in a single "ever after."
