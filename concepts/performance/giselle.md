# Giselle

## summary

A landmark Romantic ballet in two acts, *Giselle* features music by Adolphe Adam and choreography originally created by Jean Coralli and Jules Perrot. The story centers on a young peasant girl who dies of a broken heart after discovering her lover is an aristocrat betrothed to another. In the afterlife, she joins the Wilis, a group of vengeful spirits of jilted women, but ultimately saves her lover from their deadly dancing curse.

## you gotta know

- The title character, *Giselle*, is a frail peasant girl with a weak heart who loves dancing, despite her mother's warnings.
- Duke *Albrecht* disguises himself as a peasant named Loys to court Giselle, concealing his royal identity and his betrothal to Bathilde.
- Giselle suffers a mental breakdown and dies (the "mad scene") when Albrecht's true identity is revealed by the gamekeeper *Hilarion*.
- In Act II, the *Wilis*—spirits of women who died before their wedding day—force men to dance to death in the forest.
- Giselle's love is so pure that she protects Albrecht from *Myrtha*, the Queen of the Wilis, by dancing with him until dawn, when the spirits' power fades.

## connections

- [[swan-lake]] — uses a similar motif of a cursed woman and a tragic love story, heavily influenced by Giselle's romantic styling.
- [[the-nutcracker]] — Tchaikovsky's ballet which uses character motifs and narrative structure inherited from Romantic ballets like Giselle.
- [[coppelia]] — a nineteenth-century ballet that also features a peasant protagonist and romantic conflicts, but with a lighthearted comedic ending.
