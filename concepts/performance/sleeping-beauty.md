## summary

A three-act classical ballet composed by Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky, *Sleeping Beauty* is one of the most celebrated works in the classical repertoire. Based on Charles Perrault's classic fairy tale, it features a scenario and choreography originally designed by Marius Petipa. The ballet premiered in 1890 at the Mariinsky Theatre in St. Petersburg, showcasing the peak of Imperial Russian style and technique.

## you gotta know

- The wicked fairy *Carabosse* curses the infant Princess Aurora to die on her sixteenth birthday by pricking her finger on a spindle.
- The benevolent *Lilac Fairy* softens the curse so that Princess Aurora will not die, but instead fall into a hundred-year sleep.
- Features the famous "Rose Adagio" in Act I, a technically demanding dance where Princess Aurora balances on one leg while receiving roses from four suitors.
- Includes a grand wedding celebration in Act III that showcases guest appearances from other fairy tale characters, including Puss in Boots and Little Red Riding Hood.
- Composed by *Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky*, representing the second of his three major ballet scores.
