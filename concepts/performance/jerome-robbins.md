# Jerome Robbins

## summary

*Jerome Robbins* was an American choreographer and director who bridged classical ballet and Broadway, creating movement that was both technically precise and dramatically alive. He founded the New York City Ballet alongside George Balanchine and created some of the twentieth century's most iconic theatrical moments, from the Prologue of *West Side Story* to the entire vocabulary of jazz ballet.

## you gotta know

- Born in 1918 in New Jersey as *Jerome Rabinowitz*; studied under various teachers and danced with the Ballet Theatre (now American Ballet Theatre).
- Created *Fancy Free* (1944), a ballet about three sailors on shore leave that became an instant classic and launched the jazz-ballet idiom.
- Co-founded *New York City Ballet* with George Balanchine in 1948; served as associate artistic director and created ballets including *Dances at a Gathering* and *In the Night*.
- Choreographed the Broadway musicals *West Side Story* (1957) and *Fiddler on the Roof* (1964), both landmark achievements that transformed musical theater's relationship to dance.
- Won three Tony Awards and two Academy Awards; his choreography for the *West Side Story* film (1961) set a new standard for dance on screen.
- Developed a theatrical vocabulary that combined classical ballet technique, contemporary movement, and vernacular idioms into a seamlessly integrated storytelling language.
- Worked as a director as well as choreographer, understanding dance as part of the total theatrical experience rather than an interpolated specialty.

## connections

- [[west-side-story]] — his masterpiece of theatrical dance; revolutionized how movement tells story in musicals.
- [[new-york-city-ballet]] — he co-founded this company and created a ballet vocabulary that endures.
- [[george-balanchine]] — his collaborator and co-founder; they shaped modern ballet together.
- [[bob-fosse]] — contemporary musical theater choreographer; both expanded Broadway's dance vocabulary.
- [[agnes-de-mille]] — predecessor in integrating dance into musicals; Robbins advanced her legacy.
