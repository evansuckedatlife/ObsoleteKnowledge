# Coppelia

## summary

A lighthearted, comedic classical ballet in three acts, *Coppelia* features music by Léo Delibes and choreography originally by Arthur Saint-Léon. Based on E. T. A. Hoffmann's short story "The Sandman," it follows the romance of Franz and Swanhilda. The plot turns on Franz falling in love with a life-sized mechanical doll created by the eccentric Dr. Coppelius, forcing Swanhilda to impersonate the doll to win him back.

## you gotta know

- The eccentric, toy-making scientist *Dr. Coppelius* builds a realistic mechanical doll named *Coppelia* and places her on his balcony.
- *Franz*, betrothed to Swanhilda, becomes infatuated with the doll, believing her to be a real woman.
- *Swanhilda* sneaks into Dr. Coppelius's workshop with her friends, discovers that Coppelia is just a doll, and hides in her place when the doctor returns.
- Dr. Coppelius attempts a magic spell to transfer Franz's life force into the doll, but Swanhilda pretends the doll has come to life to trick him.
- The ballet ends with Franz and Swanhilda reconciling and celebrating their marriage with the townspeople.
