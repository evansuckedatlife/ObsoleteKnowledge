# Spartacus

## summary

*Spartacus* is a full-length Soviet ballet choreographed by Yuri Grigorovich in 1968, set to Aram Khachaturian's monumental score. The ballet dramatizes the historical slave revolt against Rome led by Spartacus, combining grand spectacle, athletic male virtuosity, and political themes of freedom and oppression into a work that became a signature piece of Soviet ballet and a vehicle for showcasing male dancing.

## you gotta know

- *Aram Khachaturian* composed the score in 1954; its bold, rhythmic themes and sweeping melodies provide a sonorous, epic backdrop to the military and romantic action.
- *Yuri Grigorovich* choreographed the work to emphasize male dancing: the role of *Spartacus* is a hero role featuring powerful jumps, turns, and sustained Classical technique combined with character work and mime.
- The *Adagio of Spartacus and Phrygia* (his lover) is a romantic pas de deux of extraordinary beauty and emotional depth, featuring slow, sustained partnering and a famous high lift.
- The battle scenes and ensemble sections employ large corps formations, athletic movement, and dynamic spatial compositions that convey the scale of the slave rebellion.
- *Crassus*, the Roman general, is a villain character danced with sharp, angular technique reflecting his cruelty and refinement; his encounter with Spartacus provides dramatic conflict.
- The ballet culminates in Spartacus's crucifixion and a famous *apotheosis* scene where his silhouette rises above the battlefield, an image of freedom and spiritual transcendence.
- The work became a Cold War cultural symbol for the Soviet Union, presenting a heroic narrative of class struggle that aligned with Soviet ideology.
