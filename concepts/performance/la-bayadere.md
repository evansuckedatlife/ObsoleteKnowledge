# La Bayadère

## summary

*La Bayadère* is a full-length ballet choreographed by Marius Petipa in 1877, set in ancient India and centered on a doomed romance between a warrior and a temple dancer. The work features one of ballet's most famous acts — the Kingdom of the Shades — a visually stunning corps de ballet sequence of identical poses and formations that exemplify the precision and unity of classical ensemble work.

## you gotta know

- Petipa created the role of *Nikiya*, the bayadère (sacred temple dancer), as a romantic heroine; her variations showcase both soft, flowing movement and demanding technical passages.
- The *Kingdom of the Shades* sequence in Act III is a parade of identical arabesques followed by a seemingly endless line of corps dancers descending a ramp in synchronized diagonal formations — an icon of classical ballet geometry and discipline.
- A fatal *snake in a basket* scene in Act II provides the drama: a cobra kills Nikiya, setting up the tragic ending and the ghostly encounters of Act III.
- *Minkus*' score employs orientalist themes and rhythms (the ballet is set in India, reflecting 19th-century European fascination with the exotic East).
- The warrior *Solor* is the male lead, danced with a classical, aristocratic quality; he performs variations that display technical prowess while expressing romantic longing and anguish.
- The mime sequences, particularly Nikiya's reactions to betrayal and the poison snake, remain a benchmark for classical ballet pantomime.
- The ballet is considered a milestone in ballet structure, combining character work, spectacle, pantomime, and pure dance into a unified five-act narrative.
