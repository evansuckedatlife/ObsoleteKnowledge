# Swan Lake

## summary

A canonical four-act ballet composed by Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky, *Swan Lake* is one of the most famous and frequently performed classical ballets in history. It tells the story of Prince Siegfried, who falls in love with Odette, a princess cursed by the evil sorcerer Von Rothbart to turn into a swan by day. The ballet's premiere in 1877 at Moscow's Bolshoi Theatre was initially a failure, but a subsequent revival choreographed by Marius Petipa and Lev Ivanov in 1895 established it as a masterpiece. It is celebrated for its demanding dual role of Odette and Odile (the white and black swans) and its iconic, evocative score. This work remains a cornerstone of classical dance technique and romantic musical theater.

## you gotta know

### plot & characters

- Prince *Siegfried* celebrates his birthday and is pressured to choose a bride, leading him to hunt swans in the forest.
- *Odette*, the Swan Queen, is cursed by the sorcerer *Von Rothbart* and can only take human form at night near a lake created by her mother's tears.
- *Odile*, Von Rothbart's daughter, disguises herself as the black swan to trick Siegfried into breaking his vow of fidelity to Odette.
- The ballet ends tragically in many versions, with Siegfried and Odette drowning themselves in the lake to break Von Rothbart's spell and unite in the afterlife.

### choreography & music

- Composed by *Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky*, representing his first score written specifically for the ballet medium.
- The dual role of *Odette/Odile* is famously demanding, traditionally requiring the ballerina to perform 32 consecutive *fouettés* in Act III.
- The 1895 revival at the *Mariinsky Theatre*, choreographed by *Marius Petipa* (Acts I & III) and *Lev Ivanov* (Acts II & IV), became the definitive version.
- Contains the famous "Dance of the Little Swans" (*Danse des petits cygnes*) in Act II, featuring four dancers holding hands and moving in unison.

## context

*Swan Lake* represents the peak of Russian classical ballet, combining symphonic music with dramatic narrative. It introduced the convention of the "white act" (ballet blanc), where the female corps de ballet is dressed in white tutus, symbolizing purity and tragedy. In popular culture and quiz bowl, the ballet is frequently referenced for its dual white swan/black swan casting, its tragic endings, and specific musical themes like the oboe-led "swan theme."
