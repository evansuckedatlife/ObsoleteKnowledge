# Cats

## summary

*Cats* is a 1981 musical by composer *Andrew Lloyd Webber*, based on T. S. Eliot's poetry collection *Old Possum's Book of Practical Cats*. It revolutionized musical theatre by eschewing linear narrative for a kaleidoscopic parade of feline characters, and its record-breaking 18-year Broadway run (1982–2000) made it a cultural touchstone despite its famously surreal book.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Andrew Lloyd Webber* with lyrics adapted from *T. S. Eliot*'s witty and whimsical 1939 poetry collection; no traditional plot or protagonist.
- **"Memory"** became a concert standard and one of the most-recorded musical theatre songs, sung by Grizabella the Glamour Cat as she recalls her faded past.
- The show is structured as a nighttime ball where various cats introduce themselves through stylized dance and song—a framework that prioritizes spectacle and character vignettes over story.
- Won seven Tony Awards including Best Musical (1983) and ran for 18 years on Broadway with 7,485 performances, making it the second-longest-running musical ever.
- The 2019 film adaptation, widely derided as grotesque, sparked renewed cultural conversation about the show's already surreal aesthetic and its durability in audience affection.
- Features an eclectic cast of oddball felines: Rum Tum Tugger (a pampered narcissist), Mungojerrie and Rumpleteazer (cat burglars), Old Deuteronomy (a patriarchal elder).
