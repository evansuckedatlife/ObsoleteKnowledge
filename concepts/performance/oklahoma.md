# Oklahoma!

## summary

*Oklahoma!* is a groundbreaking 1943 musical by Richard Rodgers and Oscar Hammerstein II that revolutionized American musical theatre by integrating dance, character development, and lyrical storytelling into a cohesive whole. Set in Indian Territory during the territorial days, it follows the romance between a cowboy and a farmer's daughter against a backdrop of American frontier settlement and community conflict.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Richard Rodgers* with lyrics by *Oscar Hammerstein II* — their first and most celebrated collaboration, which transformed musical theatre.
- Contains the iconic opening number "Oh, What a Beautiful Mornin'" — an unprecedented choice to begin a musical with a solo, quiet song that establishes mood rather than spectacle.
- Features other famous songs including "The Surrey with the Fringe on Top," "People Will Say We're in Love," and "Oklahoma" (the title anthem).
- The plot hinges on the love triangle between *Curly* (a cowboy), *Laurey* (a farmer's daughter), and *Jud* (a troubled farmhand), culminating in their resolution at a community dance.
- Won the Pulitzer Prize for Drama in 1944, the first musical to do so, recognizing it as serious art rather than mere entertainment.
- Introduced the *integrated musical* format — dance and songs emerge from character and story rather than interrupting them; choreography by *Agnes de Mille* was revolutionary.
- Ran on Broadway for an unprecedented 2,248 performances (1943–1948), establishing the model for long-running American musicals.
