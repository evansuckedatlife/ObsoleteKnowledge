# La Sylphide

## summary

*La Sylphide* is a two-act Romantic-era ballet choreographed by Filippo Taglioni in 1832, featuring his daughter Marie Taglioni in the title role. The ballet tells of a young Scottish man enchanted by a supernatural sylph (air spirit) he encounters on his wedding day, choosing to abandon his human bride to pursue the ethereal creature—a choice that destroys both love and life. It is the prototype of Romantic ballet, establishing the vocabulary and themes that define the era.

## you gotta know

- *Marie Taglioni* originated the role and became the definitive Sylphide, dancing en pointe and pioneering the technique of sustained elevation and ethereal quality that remains synonymous with the role.
- The Sylphide herself is a phantom, a fleeting supernatural presence danced in white tulle costume and pointe shoes, representing Romanticism's fascination with the otherworldly and unattainable.
- The *divertissement* of Act I features character dances (Scottish reels and folk-inspired steps) and the famous *pas de deux* between James and the Sylphide, establishing the human-supernatural contrast.
- James's betrayal of his human betrothed, Effie, for an ephemeral dream embodies the Romantic conflict between ideal love (the Sylphide) and human reality, ending in tragedy.
- The Sylphide's sisters perform a *corps de ballet* scene of ethereal, synchronized movement, establishing the visual signature of Romantic supernatural ballets.
- Act II set in the forest is the magical climax where a witch traps James into giving the Sylphide a scarf that strips her of her wings; she dies, and James realizes his fatal error too late.
- Premiered at the Théâtre de la Porte Saint-Martin in Paris on 12 March 1832, *La Sylphide* launched both the Romantic ballet era and Marie Taglioni to international stardom.
