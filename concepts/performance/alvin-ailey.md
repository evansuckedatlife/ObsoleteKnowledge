# Alvin Ailey

## summary

*Alvin Ailey* was an American modern dancer and choreographer who founded the Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater, the most racially integrated and economically successful dance company in the world. His vision of a dance company that welcomed dancers of all races, ethnicities, and body types—and his commitment to preserving the works of other choreographers—transformed modern dance into a truly democratic art form.

## you gotta know

- Born in 1931 in Texas to a Black mother and white father; grew up in a segregated South before studying dance in Los Angeles.
- Studied with *Martha Graham*, *Lester Horton*, and others, absorbing a wide range of contemporary and vernacular movement vocabularies.
- Founded the *Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater* in 1958; it became the largest and most acclaimed modern dance company in the world, breaking barriers of race and class in concert dance.
- Created *Revelations* (1960), set to African-American spirituals, which became the company's signature work and one of the most performed pieces of modern dance.
- Pioneered the practice of presenting works by other choreographers—including Balanchine, Graham, Cunningham, and Fosse—within a single company, creating a living library of modern dance.
- Expanded the company's training and outreach, particularly serving inner-city youth through the Alvin Ailey School, making dance accessible across economic barriers.
- His philosophy of the "dance for all people" fundamentally changed the demographics and mission of concert dance in America.
