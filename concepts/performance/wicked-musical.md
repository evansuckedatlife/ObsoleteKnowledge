# Wicked

## summary

*Wicked* is a 2003 musical by composer-lyricist *Stephen Schwartz*, based on Gregory Maguire's 1995 novel, that retells the story of the Wizard of Oz from the Wicked Witch of the West's perspective. It became a cultural juggernaut, exploring how villainy is constructed and innocence corrupted, while delivering soaring melodies and a powerful central friendship that resonates across generations.

## you gotta know

- Composed and written by *Stephen Schwartz* (who also wrote *Godspell* and *Pippin*), the musical transforms Maguire's revisionist novel into a story centered on the friendship between Elphaba (the future Wicked Witch) and Glinda (the Good Witch).
- **"Defying Gravity"** is Elphaba's anthem of defiant self-determination, featuring a technically demanding vocal performance; it became one of the most iconic musical theatre moments of the 21st century.
- **"For Good"** is the emotional finale in which Glinda and Elphaba reflect on how they've changed each other, providing catharsis and underscoring the show's thesis about the power of friendship and understanding.
- The musical asks fundamental questions: how do people become "wicked," and whose version of history gets told? It reframes the Wicked Witch as a victim of propaganda and unjust systems.
- Premiered in New York in 2003 and became the highest-grossing musical production of all time, with global success and a record-breaking 2024 film adaptation.
- The score blends contemporary pop sensibilities with classic musical theatre orchestration, with melodies designed to be singable yet emotionally complex.

## connections

- [[stephen-schwartz]] — Composer-lyricist whose sophisticated approach to melody and narrative defined the show's success.
- [[gregory-maguire]] — Original novelist whose revisionist approach to the Wizard of Oz mythology inspired the musical.
