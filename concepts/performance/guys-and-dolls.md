# Guys and Dolls

## summary

*Guys and Dolls* is a 1950 musical by Frank Loesser (music and lyrics) based on Damon Runyon's short stories about Broadway, gambling, and small-time criminals. The plot weaves together the romance between *Sky Masterson* (a gambler) and *Sister Sarah* (a Salvation Army officer), and the parallel courtship between *Nathan Detroit* (a perpetual small-time hustler) and *Adelaide* (a nightclub performer), set against the colorful underworld of Times Square.

## you gotta know

- Composed and written entirely by *Frank Loesser* (both music and lyrics) — a remarkable achievement of unified style and comic wit spanning comedy songs, jazz numbers, and romantic ballads.
- Contains some of the most quoted and performed musical-theatre songs: "Luck Be a Lady," "If I Were a Bell," "A Woman in Love," "Sit Down, You're Rockin' the Boat," and "Fugue for Tinhorns."
- The score won the Tony Award for Best Musical (1951); notable for its sophisticated rhyming, jaunty orchestration, and perfect integration of character voice into melody.
- Based on *Damon Runyon*'s short stories set in 1930s New York, the musical captures both the linguistic flavor (colorful slang, Runyonesque phrasing) and the moral universe (honor among thieves, impossible redemptions).
- The plot follows two romantic pairings: the morally suspect *Sky Masterson* and the pure-hearted *Sarah*, and the long-suffering *Nathan* (who needs a woman for a wedding bet) and the opportunistic *Adelaide*.
- Opened at the 46th Street Theatre in 1950 and ran for 1,200 performances; the 1955 film directed by *Joseph L. Mankiewicz* starred *Marlon Brando* and *Frank Sinatra*.
- The show is famous for its tonal balance: it is simultaneously a comedy about street-smart con men and a genuine exploration of faith, love, and personal transformation.
