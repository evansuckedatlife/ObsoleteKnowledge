# Hello, Dolly!

## summary

*Hello, Dolly!* is a 1964 musical by Jerry Herman (music and lyrics) based on Thornton Wilder's play *The Matchmaker*. Set in turn-of-the-century New York, it follows *Dolly Levi*, a widowed matchmaker and schemer, as she attempts to make matches between wealthy merchant *Horace Vandergelder* and various women, while secretly pursuing him herself. The musical celebrates transformation, second chances, and the indomitable human spirit through infectious, jubilant songs and physical comedy.

## you gotta know

- Composed and written entirely by *Jerry Herman* (both music and lyrics) — a tour-de-force that won 10 Tony Awards (1964), including Best Musical.
- The title song "Hello, Dolly!" is one of musical theatre's most recognizable numbers, sung by *Dolly* at a restaurant, and instantly became a cultural phenomenon and jazz standard.
- Other celebrated songs include "Put On Your Sunday Clothes," "It Only Takes a Moment," "Before the Parade Passes By," and "Elegance."
- The original production starred *Carol Channing* as *Dolly* (she won the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical); later productions featured *Pearl Bailey* and *Ethel Merman*, all iconic.
- The plot is a sophisticated farce: *Dolly* returns to New York after several years, inserts herself into *Horace*'s life, sabotages his planned marriage to another, and wins him for herself.
- Ran for 2,844 performances on Broadway (1964–1970), the longest-running show on Broadway at the time; the 1969 film featured *Barbra Streisand*.
- The musical is a celebration of theatrical spectacle and human resilience; its central message—that life's best chapters come after loss and hardship—remains its emotional core.
