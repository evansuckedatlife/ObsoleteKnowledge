# The King and I

## summary

*The King and I* is a 1951 musical by Richard Rodgers (music) and Oscar Hammerstein II (lyrics) based on Margaret Landon's novel *Anna and the King of Siam*. Set in 19th-century Bangkok, it follows the cultural and romantic tension between *Anna Leonowens*, a headstrong British widow hired as a tutor to the royal children, and *King Mongkut of Siam*, an autocrat attempting to modernize his kingdom while preserving its traditions.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Richard Rodgers* with lyrics by *Oscar Hammerstein II* — their third major collaboration, running for 1,246 performances on Broadway (1951–1954).
- The opening number "I Whistle a Happy Tune" establishes *Anna*'s courage and self-reliance; other major songs include "Shall We Dance," "Getting to Know You," "Something Wonderful," and "We Kiss in a Shadow."
- The "Shall We Dance" sequence, in which *Anna* and the *King* dance together in formal Western dress, becomes a symbol of mutual respect and cultural bridging across their differences.
- Based on Margaret Landon's semi-fictionalized biography of the real *Anna Leonowens*, who served as tutor to the Thai royal family in the 1860s.
- Won five Tony Awards (1952); the 1956 film, directed by *Walter Lang* and starring *Deborah Kerr* and *Yul Brynner*, won five Academy Awards and became a cultural touchstone.
- *Yul Brynner* originated the role of the *King* and became synonymous with it, famously reprising the role in the 1985 Broadway revival (his final performance).
- The musical deftly balances the romance between *Anna* and the *King* with the larger theme of East-West cross-cultural understanding, prefiguring later explorations of cultural friction and mutual recognition.
