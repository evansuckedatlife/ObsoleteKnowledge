# Appalachian Spring

## summary

*Appalachian Spring* is a one-act ballet choreographed by Martha Graham in 1944 to a new score by Aaron Copland, celebrating American pioneer life in the Appalachian region. The work, created for the 1944 Coolidge Festival, captures the hope, joy, and resilience of early American settlers through Graham's modernist movement vocabulary combined with folk material, making it one of the most purely American ballets and a cornerstone of American modern dance.

## you gotta know

- *Aaron Copland* composed the score, which incorporates the Shaker hymn *"Simple Gifts"* and employs cowboy songs and other Americana; the music won the 1945 Pulitzer Prize for Music.
- *Martha Graham* choreographed and performed the Bride role, using her revolutionary modern dance technique to express both Classical grace and raw emotional authenticity.
- The narrative centers on a young bride and groom and their community: a Revivalist Preacher and his congregation, a Pioneer Woman, and attendant settlers, all celebrating a wedding and communal life in the frontier.
- The famous *"Simple Gifts"* variations feature soloists and ensemble performing transformations of the hymn tune, with increasingly complex geometric patterns and rhythmic variations.
- Graham's movement vocabulary blends modern dance contractions, floor work, and angular shapes with Classical ballet vocabulary, creating a distinctly American modern aesthetic.
- The *Bride's solo* is a moment of tender introspection and vulnerability, capturing the emotional complexity beneath the community celebration.
- The ballet premiered at the Coolidge Festival in Washington, D.C., on 30 October 1944 and has since become a repertory staple of American ballet and modern dance companies.
