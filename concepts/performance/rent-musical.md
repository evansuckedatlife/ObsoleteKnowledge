# Rent

## summary

*Rent* is a 1996 musical by *Jonathan Larson* that reimagines *La Bohème* in contemporary New York, following bohemian artists struggling with poverty, addiction, and AIDS as they navigate love and mortality. It became a cultural landmark for its unflinching depiction of marginalized communities and its infectious rock score, winning the Pulitzer Prize and numerous Tony Awards.

## you gotta know

- Composed and written by *Jonathan Larson*, who died suddenly of an undiagnosed aortic aneurysm the night before the show's off-Broadway premiere, adding tragic irony to the musical's themes of mortality.
- Adapted from Puccini's opera *La Bohème*, transposing the bohemian struggle to 1990s New York with characters living in a loft on the Lower East Side, dealing with eviction and disease.
- Features an ensemble of eight protagonists: Mark and Roger (filmmaker and musician), Mimi and Roger (drug-addicted lovers), Angel and Collins (a transgender angel and philosophy professor), Tom and Benjamin (landlord and tenant).
- The opening song **"Rent"** sets the show's urgent, rhythmic energy; **"Seasons of Love"** (measuring love in minutes) became a wedding and event standard.
- Major themes include AIDS crisis activism, queer identity, creative survival, and the commodification of bohemian culture in gentrifying New York.
- The show's rock-driven score and frank depiction of drug use, sexuality, and illness broke conventions, bringing musical theatre closer to the lived experience of its audience.
