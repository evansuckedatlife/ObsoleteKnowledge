# The Sound of Music

## summary

*The Sound of Music* is a 1959 musical by Richard Rodgers (music) and Oscar Hammerstein II (lyrics) that tells the true story of the von Trapp family and their escape from Nazi-occupied Austria. Centering on the romance between a free-spirited novice nun and a widowed Naval officer, it blends coming-of-age, family warmth, and historical peril into the most successful musical of all time.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Richard Rodgers* with lyrics by *Oscar Hammerstein II* — their final and most commercially successful collaboration before Hammerstein's death.
- Features iconic songs including "My Favorite Things," "Do-Re-Mi" (the beloved musical-education sequence), "Sixteen Going on Seventeen," and the title song "The Sound of Music."
- *Oscar Hammerstein II*'s last show; the opening number "The Sound of Music" (sung by *Maria* to the nuns) was one of the last songs he wrote before his death in August 1960.
- The 1965 film adaptation, directed by *Robert Wise* and starring *Julie Andrews*, became the highest-grossing film of its era and remains the most-watched movie musical ever.
- Plot centers on *Maria* (a rebellious governess) caring for the *von Trapp* children (seven of them, named in ascending order) and the growing romance with their father, *Captain von Trapp*, set against the Nazi invasion of Austria.
- Won five Tony Awards on stage (1960); the film won ten Academy Awards, including Best Picture, and dominated the global box office for over a decade.
- The closing scene—the family's escape over the Alps into Switzerland—transcends the musical-theatre medium into pure cinematic symbol of freedom.
