# Paul Ehrlich

## summary

German physician and biochemist who pioneered chemotherapy—the use of synthetic chemicals to treat disease. He synthesized over 600 compounds before discovering arsphenamine (Salvarsan), the first effective treatment for syphilis, and coined the concept of the "*magic bullet*" — a drug that selectively kills pathogens while sparing host tissue. He shared the 1905 Nobel Prize with Robert Koch.

## you gotta know

- Developed arsphenamine (Salvarsan) in 1909 as a treatment for syphilis — the first truly effective synthetic antimicrobial drug and a paradigm shift in medicine.
- Articulated the concept of the "*magic bullet*" (*Zauberkugel*): the ideal of a drug molecule that destroys disease agents with minimal harm to the patient.
- Pioneered staining techniques for bacteria and cells; his work on differential staining helped bacteriology move from observation to classification.
- Postulated the existence of *receptors* on cell surfaces — not directly observable at his time, but theorized as mediators of drug action and natural immunity.
- Worked extensively on serology and immunity, particularly *complement* (proteins in blood that enhance immune response).
- Founded the science of chemotherapy itself: systematic chemical synthesis, testing, and refinement rather than serendipitous discovery.

## connections

- [[robert-koch]] — shared the 1905 Nobel Prize; Koch identified pathogens; Ehrlich gave medicine the tools to chemically attack them.
- [[alexander-fleming]] — extended Ehrlich's chemotherapy vision through antibiotics and natural antimicrobial compounds.
- [[karl-landsteiner]] — peer in immunology and serology; both clarified the molecular basis of immune recognition.
