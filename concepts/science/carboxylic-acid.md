## summary

A carboxylic acid is an organic functional group characterized by a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom and single-bonded to a hydroxyl group, represented by the chemical formula -COOH. Because of the polar nature of both the carbonyl and hydroxyl components, carboxylic acids can form strong intermolecular hydrogen bonds, leading to significantly higher boiling points than comparable hydrocarbons. They are weak acids that partially dissociate in aqueous solution to yield a resonance-stabilized carboxylate anion and a hydronium ion. Carboxylic acids are ubiquitous in biology, forming the building blocks of amino acids, fatty acids, and many metabolic intermediates.

## you gotta know

### structure & physical properties

- *Characterized by the carboxyl group* (-COOH), which combines a polar carbonyl group (C=O) and a polar hydroxyl group (-OH) on a single carbon atom.
- *Forms stable hydrogen-bonded dimers* in the gas phase and in nonpolar solvents, which substantially elevates their boiling points and melting points relative to their molecular weight.
- *Displays weak acidity* in water, typically exhibiting pKa values in the range of 3 to 5, due to the resonance stabilization of the negative charge across both oxygen atoms in the carboxylate conjugate base.

### synthesis & reactions

- *Prepared by the oxidation* of primary alcohols or aldehydes using strong oxidizing agents such as potassium permanganate (KMnO4) or chromic acid (the Jones reagent).
- *Reacts with alcohols in Fischer esterification* under acid catalysis to produce esters and water, which is a reversible equilibrium reaction.
- *Reacts with amines* under heat or using coupling reagents like DCC to synthesize amides, which constitute the peptide bonds in proteins.
- *Undergoes decarboxylation* when heated (especially beta-keto acids), releasing carbon dioxide gas and forming a simplified hydrocarbon.
- *Reduced to primary alcohols* using lithium aluminum hydride (LiAlH4), although they are resistant to milder reducing agents like sodium borohydride (NaBH4).

## connections

- [[alcohol]] — reacts with carboxylic acids to form esters; also, primary alcohols are oxidized to yield carboxylic acids.
- [[ester]] — the product of the condensation reaction between a carboxylic acid and an alcohol.
- [[amide]] — the product of the condensation reaction between a carboxylic acid and an amine.
- [[amino-acids]] — biological building blocks that contain both a carboxylic acid group and an amine group.
- [[carbon]] — forms the backbone of the carboxyl group, which is sp2 hybridized and planar.
- [[oxygen]] — the two oxygen atoms in the carboxyl group carry the partial charges that enable hydrogen bonding and resonance.

## context

Carboxylic acids are the primary acidic functional group in organic chemistry, serving as precursors to esters, amides, and acid anhydrides. They are central to cellular metabolism, appearing in the citric acid cycle as intermediates like citric, succinic, and malic acids. In quiz bowl, they are clued via their -COOH structure, hydrogen-bonded dimerization, pKa values, synthesis from primary alcohol oxidation, and reactions such as Fischer esterification, decarboxylation, and reduction by LiAlH4.
