## summary

**Leptons** are *fundamental fermions* that do not participate in the *strong nuclear force*. They include the *electron*, *muon*, *tau*, and their corresponding *neutrinos*. Leptons have spin-1/2 and form one of the two primary matter constituents in the Standard Model, distinguished from *quarks* by their lack of color charge.

## you gotta know

- Six flavors exist: electron, muon, tau (charged leptons) and electron neutrino, muon neutrino, tau neutrino
- Spin-1/2 *fermions*—obey the Pauli exclusion principle
- *Electrically charged leptons* couple to the electromagnetic force via photon exchange
- *Neutrinos* are nearly massless, interact only via the *weak force*, and carry lepton number
- Organized in three generations, each with a heavier charged lepton
- Lepton number is conserved in all Standard Model interactions
- Interact via *electroweak* and *weak nuclear forces*, but NOT the strong force

## context

Leptons occupy a unique place in the Standard Model as the only *fundamental* matter particles that do not carry *color charge*. They are essential to atomic structure (electrons) and provide evidence of the universe's matter-antimatter asymmetry. The discovery of lepton flavor violations and neutrino oscillations revealed that leptons are not as immutable as initially believed.

## connections
