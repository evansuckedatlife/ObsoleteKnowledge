# Callisto

## summary

**Callisto** is the outermost of Jupiter's four large Galilean moons and the third-largest moon in the solar system. Ancient and heavily cratered, Callisto is one of the most crater-scarred bodies in the solar system, suggesting a relatively inert geological history with little tidal heating. Unlike its inner Galilean siblings, Callisto preserves a record of ancient impacts and may harbor a subsurface ocean beneath its icy crust.

## you gotta know

- Diameter 4,821 km; third-largest moon in the solar system.
- Heavily cratered and geologically *ancient*; surface shows ancient multi-ring impact structures (like *Gilgamesh*) with rings extending hundreds of kilometers.
- *No apparent tidal heating*; far enough from Jupiter that gravitational stress is minimal (unlike [[io]] and [[europa]]).
- One of the four *Galilean moons* discovered by Galileo in 1610.
- Possible subsurface ocean detected through magnetometry; composition is roughly equal parts ice and rock.
- Dark surface suggests composition of dark ice mixed with rock dust.
- Named after a figure from Greek mythology, a priestess of Hera.
