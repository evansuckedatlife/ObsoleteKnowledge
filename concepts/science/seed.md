# Seed

## summary

A *seed* is the reproductive unit of a flowering plant (angiosperm) or conifer (gymnosperm), consisting of an embryonic plant (embryo), a nutrient store (endosperm or cotyledon), and a protective seed coat. Seeds are the product of double fertilisation in angiosperms and enable plants to disperse offspring far from the parent and survive unfavourable conditions.

## you gotta know

- A seed develops from a *fertilised ovule* after flowering; it packages the embryo, nutritive tissue, and protective layers into a dispersal unit.
- The *seed coat* (testa) is the hardened outer layer that provides protection against desiccation, physical damage, and pathogen infection.
- Seeds are *quiescent* or *dormant*—metabolically inactive until triggered by light, moisture, temperature, or chemical signals (stratification) to *germinate*.
- *Monocots* (like grass and corn) have a single *cotyledon* (seed leaf) that absorbs nutrients from the *endosperm*; *dicots* (like beans and peas) have two cotyledons that consume and store the endosperm internally.
- *Germination* begins with imbibition (water uptake), which reactivates enzyme systems and causes the radical (primary root) to emerge, followed by the shoot.
- *Seed banks* (persistent soil seed pools) allow plants to outlast drought, disturbance, or fire—seeds can remain viable for years or decades.
- Seeds are dispersed by wind (light, winged seeds), water (buoyant seeds), animals (fruit ingestion, burrs), or ballistically (explosive pods).

## connections

- [[plant]] — seeds are the dispersal and propagation unit of vascular plants.
- [[cotyledon]] — the seed leaf that supplies initial nutrition to the germinating embryo.
- [[embryo]] — the miniature plant contained within the seed.
- [[photosynthesis]] — once germinated, seedlings rely on photosynthesis to build biomass.
- [[plant-reproduction]] — seeds are the mature product of sexual plant reproduction.
