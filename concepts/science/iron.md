# Iron

## summary

Iron is a lustrous, ductile, and magnetic transition metal that is the most common element on Earth by mass, forming much of Earth's outer and inner core. It is the fourth most common element in the crust and has been smelted for thousands of years, defining the historical transition from the Bronze Age to the Iron Age. It is chemically active and oxidizes readily in damp air to form rust. In biology, iron is a critical micronutrient, forming the active core of hemoglobin and myoglobin to facilitate oxygen transport in blood and muscle tissues.

## you gotta know

### astrophysics & geological structure

- *Is the heaviest element produced by stellar nucleosynthesis* that does not require a supernova or neutron star merger, as the fusion of iron-56 absorbs rather than releases energy, leading to core collapse in massive stars.
- *Forms Earth's core* alongside nickel in a metallic alloy that generates the planet's magnetic field through dynamo action in the liquid outer core.

### chemistry & mineralogy

- *Exhibits ferromagnetism*, a strong magnetic property named after the element itself, which disappears when heated above its Curie temperature of approximately 1043 Kelvin.
- *Commonly displays oxidation states* of +2 (ferrous) and +3 (ferric), although states ranging from -2 to +6 are chemically achievable.
- *Found in major ores* including hematite (Fe2O3), magnetite (Fe3O4, which acts as natural lodestone), and pyrite (FeS2, commonly known as "fool's gold").

### metallurgy & biology

- *Alloyed with carbon to make steel*, where small amounts of carbon atoms disrupt the iron crystal lattice to drastically increase strength, hardness, and durability.
- *Binds oxygen in hemoglobin* via an iron-containing porphyrin ring called heme, which gives red blood cells their color and enables respiration.
- *Acts as the industrial catalyst in the Haber-Bosch process*, facilitating the synthesis of ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen gases.

## connections

- [[carbon]] — alloyed with carbon to produce steel, the backbone of modern structural engineering.
- [[oxygen]] — binds to the iron atom in heme groups for oxygen transport in respiration, and reacts with iron to form rust.
- [[nitrogen]] — iron catalysts are used to fix nitrogen in the Haber-Bosch process.
- [[copper]] — iron replaced copper alloys (bronze) as the dominant metal for tools and weapons during historical transitions.
- [[nickel]] — forms iron-nickel alloys in planetary cores and iron meteorites.
- [[potassium]] — iron ferrocyanide forms the pigment Prussian blue, which is used as an antidote for heavy metal poisoning.

## context

Iron is the metal that forged modern civilization and powers planetary magnetism. Its nuclear stability marks the death of stars, and its magnetic properties define the physical phenomenon of ferromagnetism. In quiz bowl, iron is clued through its stellar nucleosynthesis limit, its ores (hematite, magnetite, taconite), its +2 and +3 oxidation states, its role in hemoglobin, its magnetism and Curie temperature, and the Haber-Bosch iron catalyst.
