# ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay)

## summary

**ELISA** is a biochemical assay for detecting and quantifying specific proteins or antigens in a sample using antibody-antigen binding coupled to an enzyme reaction. The enzyme (e.g., horseradish peroxidase or alkaline phosphatase) catalyzes a color-producing substrate, making protein presence and concentration visible and measurable.

## you gotta know

- *Antibody sandwich* — in the most common format, a capture antibody is bound to a well; the sample (containing the target protein) is added, allowing the protein to bind the antibody; a detection antibody (linked to an enzyme) binds a different epitope on the protein.
- *Enzyme-substrate reaction* — the enzyme linked to the detection antibody catalyzes a reaction producing a colored or luminescent product.
- *Plate-based format* — 96-well or 384-well plates allow high-throughput testing of many samples in parallel.
- *Quantification* — the intensity of the color reaction (measured by optical density or luminescence) is proportional to the target protein concentration; a standard curve calibrates the measurement.
- *Sandwich vs. competitive* — sandwich ELISA (above) suits large proteins; competitive ELISA (antigen competes with enzyme-linked antigen for antibodies) works for small molecules.
- Widely used for COVID-19 antibody detection, hormone assays, food allergen testing, and diagnostic serology.
