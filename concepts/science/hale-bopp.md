# Hale-Bopp

## summary

**Comet Hale-Bopp** is one of the brightest and most spectacularly active comets of the modern era, discovered independently in 1995 and reaching perihelion on April 1, 1997. Its passage was visible to the naked eye for over a year, making it a signature astronomical event of the 1990s and sparking global public interest, though it also became associated with the Heaven's Gate mass suicide that occurred during its visibility.

## you gotta know

- *Discovered independently* by Thomas Bopp (amateur) and Alan Hale (professional) on 23 July 1995; named for both discoverers per convention.
- *Extremely active comet:* developed two distinct tails — one of dust and one of ionized gas — and brightened far more than models predicted, outgassing copiously as it approached the Sun.
- *Visible to naked eye* from mid-1996 through mid-1997, an unusually long naked-eye visibility window that made it a cultural phenomenon and inspired public observatory events worldwide.
- *Orbital period:* roughly 2,533 years; last perihelion ~4,385 BCE, placing previous visibility during early Egyptian civilization.
- *Nucleus dimensions* estimated at 40 km across, one of the largest comets on record; its immense size powered the exceptional outgassing and brightness.
- *Two tails:* the ion tail (straight, following the solar wind) reached 40+ million km in length, while the dust tail curved behind its orbital motion.

## connections

- [[comet]] — Hale-Bopp is the canonical example of a bright, long-period comet and an archetype for public interest in cometary astronomy.
- [[oort-cloud]] — likely an *Oort Cloud* immigrant, ejected inward by stellar perturbations, explaining its multi-millennia periodicity.
