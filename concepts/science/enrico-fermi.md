# Enrico Fermi

## summary

An Italian-American physicist, **Enrico Fermi** is widely known as the "architect of the nuclear age" for his work in both theoretical and experimental physics. He built the world's first nuclear reactor, developed the theory of beta decay, and co-developed the statistical mechanics governing fermions. He was awarded the 1938 Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on induced radioactivity.

## you gotta know

- *Constructed Chicago Pile-1*, the world's first artificial nuclear reactor, which achieved the first self-sustaining nuclear chain reaction under the stands of Stagg Field at the University of Chicago in 1942.
- *Formulated the Fermi paradox*, a famous question ("Where is everybody?") highlighting the contradiction between the high probability of extraterrestrial life and the lack of evidence or contact.
- *Co-developed Fermi-Dirac statistics*, which describe the distribution of fermions (such as electrons, protons, and neutrons, named *fermions* in Fermi's honor) over energy states.
- *Developed the theory of beta decay* in 1933, introducing the weak interaction (one of the four fundamental forces) and utilizing Wolfgang Pauli's hypothesized particle, which Fermi named the *neutrino* ("little neutral one").
- *Discovered that slow neutrons* are far more effective than fast neutrons at inducing nuclear reactions, which led to the discovery of nuclear fission.
- *Served as an associate director of Los Alamos Laboratory* during the Manhattan Project, playing a central role in the development and Trinity test of the first atomic bomb.

## connections

- [[albert-einstein]] — whose mass-energy equivalence equation provided the theoretical energy basis for Fermi's nuclear reactors.
