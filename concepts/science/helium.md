# Helium

## summary

Helium is a colorless, odorless, tasteless, and chemically inert monatomic gas that heads the noble gas group in the periodic table. It is the second lightest and second most abundant element in the observable universe, produced largely by stellar nucleosynthesis. On Earth, it is relatively rare, primarily extracted from natural gas deposits where it accumulates from the radioactive decay of heavy elements.

## you gotta know

- *Discovered spectroscopically* during a solar eclipse in 1868 by Pierre Janssen and Norman Lockyer, who named it after the Greek sun god *Helios*.
- *Liquid state displays superfluidity* below the Lambda point at approximately 2.17 Kelvin, where it flows with zero viscosity and forms a Rollin film that creeps up the sides of containers.
- *Possesses the lowest boiling point* (4.2 Kelvin) and melting point of any element, and cannot be solidified at standard pressure even at absolute zero.
- *Extracted commercially from natural gas* through fractional distillation, particularly from helium-rich fields such as the Cliffside Gas Field in Texas.
- *Utilized widely in cryogenics* to cool superconducting magnets in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) machines, and as a non-reactive lifting gas.

## connections

- [[hydrogen]] — the only element lighter than helium, with which it undergoes fusion in stellar cores.
- [[uranium]] — alpha decay of heavy elements like uranium and thorium in the Earth's crust is the primary source of terrestrial helium.
- [[neon]] — the noble gas directly below helium in the periodic table.
