## summary

A crewed lunar mission launched by *NASA* on April 11, 1970, that suffered a catastrophic *oxygen tank failure* two days into the flight. The crew—*James Lovell*, *Jack Swigert*, and *Fred Haise*—were forced to abort the Moon landing and return to Earth using the *lunar module* as a lifeboat. The mission became a triumph of problem-solving and crisis management, immortalized by the phrase "Houston, we've had a problem."

## you gotta know

- *Apollo 13* launched on *April 11, 1970*, with the goal of landing on the Moon near the *Fra Mauro* crater.
- On *April 13*, an *oxygen tank rupture* in the service module forced *mission control* to abort the lunar landing and work toward returning the crew safely to Earth.
- *Astronaut Jack Swigert* transmitted the now-famous understatement: "*Houston, we've had a problem*."
- The crew—*James Lovell*, *Jack Swigert*, and *Fred Haise*—sheltered in the *lunar module*, using it as a lifeboat to return home instead of as a landing vehicle.
- *Mission control* and the crew made a series of improvised fixes, including improvising a carbon-dioxide filter by adapting parts from the command module.
- The *three astronauts* splashed down safely in the *Pacific Ocean* on *April 17*, having survived extreme cold, power rationing, and critical life-support challenges.
- The mission demonstrated the effectiveness of *astronaut training*, *ground support*, and *collaborative problem-solving* under extreme pressure.
