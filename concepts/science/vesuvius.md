# Mount Vesuvius

## summary

A sommo-stratovolcano located on the Gulf of Naples in Campania, Italy, **Mount Vesuvius** is one of the world's most famous and dangerous volcanoes. It is best known for its catastrophic eruption in 79 AD, which buried the prosperous Roman cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum under thick layers of volcanic ash and pumice. It remains the only active volcano on the European mainland to have erupted in the last hundred years, and its proximity to the densely populated city of Naples makes it a major modern hazard.

## you gotta know

### history & roman eruptions

- *Catastrophically erupted in 79 AD*, burying and preserving the Roman towns of *Pompeii*, *Herculaneum*, Oplontis, and Stabiae.
- *Documented by Pliny the Younger*, who witnessed the 79 AD event from Misenum and wrote two letters to Tacitus describing the towering pine-tree-shaped ash column, creating the term *Plinian eruption*.
- *Caused the death of Pliny the Elder*, the famous Roman naturalist and admiral who died of suffocation at Stabiae while leading a naval rescue mission to save citizens.
- *Served as the hideout for Spartacus* and his rebelling gladiators in 73 BC, who camped in the volcano's crater before rappelling down using wild vines to defeat a besieging Roman force.

### geological features & modern history

- *Classified as a decade volcano*, one of sixteen volcanoes selected for intensive monitoring due to their destructive history and proximity to large cities.
- *Structured as a double volcano*, with the active cone of Vesuvius growing inside the collapsed semicircular caldera of the older, extinct *Mount Somma*.
- *Erupted most recently in 1944* during the Allied occupation of Italy in World War II, destroying several villages and damaging dozens of B-25 bombers stationed at a nearby airfield.

## context

Mount Vesuvius is a cornerstone of classical history, archaeology, and the science of volcanology. The preservation of Pompeii provided modern scholars with an unprecedented window into daily Roman life. In quiz bowl, Vesuvius is clued by the 79 AD date, the accounts of Pliny the Younger, the death of Pliny the Elder, Spartacus's gladiator revolt, and the Somma-caldera structure.
