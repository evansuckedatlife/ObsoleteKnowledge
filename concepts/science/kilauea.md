# Kilauea

## summary

An active shield volcano located on the southeastern part of the Big Island of Hawaii, **Kilauea** is one of the most active volcanoes on Earth. It has experienced frequent, near-continuous eruptions from its summit and rift zones, including a 35-year continuous flow at the Puʻu ʻŌʻō vent. In Hawaiian religion and oral tradition, it is the sacred home of the volcano goddess Pele.

## you gotta know

- *Regarded as the sacred home of Pele*, the Hawaiian goddess of fire and volcanoes, who is believed to reside within the Halemaʻumaʻu crater at the volcano's summit.
- *Experienced a near-continuous 35-year eruption* from 1983 to 2018 at the Puʻu ʻŌʻō vent, which sent vast flows of basaltic lava down the rift zone, creating new coastline and destroying several towns.
- *Underwent a major summit collapse in 2018*, which dramatically enlarged the Halemaʻumaʻu crater, drained the lava lake, and triggered destructive eruptions in the Lower East Rift Zone.
- *Name translates to "spewing" or "much spreading"* in the Hawaiian language, describing its frequent and fluid lava flows.
- *Hosts the Hawaiian Volcano Observatory*, established in 1912 by geologist Thomas Jaggar on the rim of the Kilauea caldera, making it one of the most heavily monitored volcanoes in the world.
