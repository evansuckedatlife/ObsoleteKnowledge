# Triassic Period

## summary

The **Triassic Period** (252–201 million years ago) marks the recovery from the Permian extinction and the rise of dinosaurs. Named for its three distinctive rock layers (tri-assic), the Triassic saw reptiles inherit the barren landscape left by the Great Dying. Mammals emerged as small, shrew-like creatures. Pangaea slowly began to rift apart.

## you gotta know

- *Dinosaurs* first appeared in the early Triassic and diversified steadily, beginning as bipedal predators like *Eoraptor*.
- *Synapsids* (the therapsids) were decimated by the Permian extinction; their survivors evolved into early *mammals*, which remained small and nocturnal.
- *Archosaurs*—the clade that includes dinosaurs and crocodilians—rose to dominance, with early forms like *Lagosuchus* anticipating dinosaurian body plans.
- *Conifers* and *cycads* became the dominant plants; flowering plants had not yet evolved.
- *Marine reptiles* like *ichthyosaurs* (fish-like predators) and *nothosaurs* colonised the recovering oceans.
- The *Triassic extinction* (~201 million years ago) cleared the way for dinosaurs to become Earth's dominant vertebrates.
