# SpaceX CRS-1

## summary

The first *Commercial Resupply Services* mission to the *International Space Station*, launched by *SpaceX* on May 22, 2012. The *Dragon* spacecraft successfully reached the ISS, delivered cargo, and returned to Earth intact—the first commercially developed spacecraft to visit the station. The mission marked a watershed moment in spaceflight: the beginning of private companies providing critical logistics for human space exploration.

## you gotta know

- *SpaceX CRS-1* launched on *May 22, 2012*, aboard a *Falcon 9* rocket carrying the *Dragon* cargo spacecraft.
- *Dragon* became the first *private spacecraft* to dock with the *International Space Station*, delivering over 400 kilograms of supplies and equipment.
- The mission was part of a *Commercial Resupply Services* contract with *NASA*, marking the transition of ISS logistics from government-operated to commercially operated spacecraft.
- *SpaceX* had lost its two previous *Falcon 9* flights and the *Dragon* demonstration missions, so *CRS-1's* success was redemptive after years of setbacks and skepticism from the aerospace industry.
- The *Dragon* capsule was *recovered* intact in the *Pacific Ocean* and reused for subsequent missions, pioneering the practice of capsule reusability.
- The mission demonstrated that *private companies* could achieve what previously only government space agencies could do, reducing costs and increasing access to space.
- *SpaceX* has since flown over 30 *CRS* missions to the ISS, becoming the primary resupply partner.
