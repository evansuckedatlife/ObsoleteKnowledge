# Higgs Boson

## summary

The **Higgs boson** is an elementary particle in the *Standard Model* of particle physics, the quantized excitation of the *Higgs field* — an energy field thought to permeate all of space. Its existence explains how other fundamental particles acquire *mass*: particles that interact strongly with the Higgs field behave as though they are heavier, while those that do not (like the *photon*) remain massless. Proposed in 1964 and long the last missing piece of the Standard Model, it was finally detected in 2012 at *CERN*'s Large Hadron Collider, confirming a half-century-old prediction.

## you gotta know

### the idea
- It is the particle associated with the *Higgs field*; particles gain mass through the *Higgs mechanism* (electroweak symmetry breaking) by coupling to that field.
- It was predicted in *1964*, most famously by *Peter Higgs*, alongside François Englert and Robert Brout (and others — Guralnik, Hagen, Kibble).
- It is a *scalar boson* — spin 0 — the only fundamental scalar particle in the Standard Model.

### the discovery
- It was discovered in *2012* by the *ATLAS* and *CMS* experiments at the *Large Hadron Collider* (LHC) at *CERN*, near Geneva.
- Its mass is about *125 GeV/c²* (roughly 133 times the mass of a proton).
- The 2013 *Nobel Prize in Physics* went to *Peter Higgs* and *François Englert* the year after confirmation.

### the nickname
- It is popularly (and misleadingly) called the *"God particle,"* a name from Leon Lederman's book that physicists generally dislike.

## context

The Higgs completed the Standard Model, the most successful theory of fundamental forces and particles, by supplying the long-sought mechanism for mass without breaking the theory's symmetries. Its discovery was one of the largest scientific collaborations in history and a triumph of the *LHC*, the world's biggest machine. Open questions remain — whether the Higgs is truly elementary, why its mass is what it is (the "hierarchy problem"), and how it connects to dark matter and physics beyond the Standard Model — keeping it central to ongoing research.

## connections

- [[standard-model]] — the Higgs is its final confirmed piece and the source of particle mass.
- [[photon]] — massless precisely because it does not couple to the Higgs field, unlike the W and Z bosons.
- [[quantum-mechanics]] — the framework in which the Higgs field and its excitation are described.
