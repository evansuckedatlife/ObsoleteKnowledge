## summary

An aldehyde is an organic compound containing a carbonyl functional group (C=O) where the carbonyl carbon is bonded to at least one hydrogen atom, represented by the general formula -CHO. Positioned at the terminal end of a carbon chain, aldehydes are highly reactive intermediates that are easily oxidized to carboxylic acids or reduced to primary alcohols.

## you gotta know

- *Detected qualitatively using Tollens' reagent* (the "silver mirror test"), where the aldehyde is oxidized to a carboxylate while reducing diamminesilver(I) ions to metallic silver.
- *Detected qualitatively using Fehling's solution*, which oxidizes the aldehyde and precipitates copper(II) ions as red copper(I) oxide (Cu2O).
- *Prepared by the mild oxidation of primary alcohols* using reagents like pyridinium chlorochromate (PCC) or via the Swern oxidation, avoiding further oxidation to carboxylic acids.
- *Synthesized via the ozonolysis of alkenes* containing terminal or monosubstituted double bonds, followed by a reductive workup.
- *Includes formaldehyde* (methanal), the simplest aldehyde, which is widely used in aqueous solution (formalin) as a biological preservative and disinfectant.

## connections

- [[alcohol]] — primary alcohols are oxidized to aldehydes, which can in turn be reduced back to primary alcohols using sodium borohydride.
- [[carboxylic-acid]] — oxidation of aldehydes with strong oxidizing agents (like chromic acid) yields carboxylic acids.
- [[copper]] — Fehling's test utilizes copper sulfate to distinguish aldehydes from ketones.
