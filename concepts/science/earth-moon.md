# Earth's Moon

## summary

**The Moon** is Earth's only natural satellite, orbiting at an average distance of 384,400 km. It is the fifth-largest moon in the solar system and the only celestial body beyond Earth to have been visited by humans. Its gravitational influence regulates Earth's tides and stabilizes the planet's axial tilt.

## you gotta know

- Only moon ever walked on by humans; *Apollo* missions 1969–1972 landed twelve astronauts.
- *Synchronous rotation*: the Moon always shows the same face to Earth because its rotation period equals its orbital period (~27.3 days).
- Formed ~4.5 billion years ago, likely from a giant impact between Earth and a Mars-sized body called *Theia*.
- Controls Earth's *tidal forces*; without it, ocean tides would be negligible and Earth's axial tilt would be unstable.
- Surface dominated by *highlands* (ancient, light-colored) and *maria* (darker, younger volcanic plains); heavily cratered.
- Discovery of *water ice* in permanently shadowed craters (detected by orbital spectroscopy in 2009) transformed lunar science.
- Average diameter 3,474 km; mass about 1/81st of Earth's; no atmosphere or magnetic field.
