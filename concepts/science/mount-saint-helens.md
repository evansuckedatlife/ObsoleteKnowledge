# Mount Saint Helens

## summary

An active stratovolcano located in Skamania County, Washington, **Mount Saint Helens** is part of the Cascade Volcanic Arc. It is famous for its catastrophic eruption on May 18, 1980, which stands as the deadliest and most economically destructive volcanic event in United States history. The eruption triggered the largest debris avalanche in recorded history and reduced the mountain's summit by over 1,300 feet.

## you gotta know

- *Catastrophically erupted on May 18, 1980*, triggered by a 5.1 magnitude earthquake that caused the volcano's entire unstable northern flank to slide away.
- *Experienced a massive lateral blast* that blew sideways out of the collapsing mountain, destroying 230 square miles of forest and creating a horseshoe-shaped crater.
- *Lethally trapped U.S. Geological Survey volcanologist David A. Johnston*, who famously radioed his final words, *"Vancouver! Vancouver! This is it!"*, from his observation post just before being swept away.
- *Part of the Cascade Volcanic Arc*, a segment of the Pacific Ring of Fire named by British explorer George Vancouver after diplomat Alleyne Fitzherbert, 1st Baron St Helens.
- *Developed a prominent northern bulge* (the "Goat Rocks bulge") prior to the 1980 eruption, which grew outward at a rate of up to five feet per day due to rising magma.
