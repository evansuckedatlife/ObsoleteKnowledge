# Porifera

## summary

An ancient phylum of aquatic multicellular organisms commonly known as sponges, **Porifera** are characterized by a lack of true tissues, organs, or body symmetry. They are sessile filter feeders that draw water through their porous bodies to trap microscopic food particles. They represent the most primitive group of animals in existence.

## you gotta know

- *Lack true tissues and organs*, making them the only major animal phylum classified as Parazoa rather than Eumetazoa.
- *Possess flagellated cells called choanocytes* (or collar cells), which line their internal chambers and beat their flagella to maintain a constant current of water through the sponge.
- *Maintain structural rigidity using spicules*, microscopic structures made of silica or calcium carbonate, or a flexible protein network called spongin.
- *Draw water through pores called ostia* into a central cavity (the spongocoel) and expel it through one or more large exit openings called *oscula*.
- *Capable of complete regeneration*, meaning that even if a living sponge is completely shredded or blended into individual cells, the cells can reassociate and reform a functional sponge.

## connections

- [[cnidaria]] — a slightly more advanced phylum that possesses true radial symmetry and tissues, contrasting with Porifera's lack thereof.
- [[ctenophora]] — another primitive diploblastic phylum that developed tissues and nervous systems.
