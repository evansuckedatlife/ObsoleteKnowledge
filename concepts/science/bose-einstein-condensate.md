# Bose–Einstein condensate

## summary

A Bose–Einstein condensate (BEC) is a state of matter in which a large fraction of *bosonic* particles occupy the same lowest-energy *quantum state* at very low temperatures. In this macroscopic quantum state, individual particles lose their identity and behave as a single coherent object, exhibiting remarkable phenomena like zero viscosity and quantum interference patterns.

## you gotta know

- **Condensation criterion**: occurs when thermal energy becomes comparable to the energy spacing between quantum states; particles "fall" into the ground state to minimize energy
- **Bosons only**: unlike fermions subject to the *Pauli exclusion principle*, bosons have no limit to how many can share a quantum state
- **Temperature scale**: BECs require extremely low temperatures (nanokelvin range) because thermal motion normally keeps particles excited across many states
- **Coherence**: all condensed particles share a single *wavefunction* and phase; they move and interfere as one collective entity
- **Zero viscosity**: superfluidity emerges; a BEC flowing through a tube experiences no friction from the container walls
- **Experimental creation**: achieved in labs via laser cooling of rubidium, lithium, or other alkali atoms; requires isolation from thermal environment
- **Macroscopic quantum effects**: displays quantum behavior at human scales—diffraction through gratings, persistent vortices, and density oscillations

## context

Albert Einstein and Satyendra Nath Bose predicted the possibility of this state in the 1920s using quantum statistics of indistinguishable particles. However, it remained theoretical until 1995, when experimenters first created a BEC in a trapped rubidium cloud. The condensate is not a phase transition in the classical sense (no latent heat); rather, it reflects a fundamental change in how quantum states are populated. When particles are indistinguishable (as bosons are), the probability distribution for occupancy becomes sharply peaked at the ground state below a critical temperature. The BEC is an extreme case of *superposition*—many particles literally in the same state at once—made possible only because bosons have no exclusion constraint.

## connections

- [[boson]] — only bosons can form BECs; photons, W/Z bosons, the Higgs boson all exhibit Bose-like statistics
- [[superposition]] — the condensate is a macroscopic superposition of identical particles in the ground state
- [[quantum-operator]] — creation and annihilation operators for the ground state mode become large; their commutation relations are crucial to BEC dynamics
- [[wavefunction]] — all particles share a single collective wavefunction ψ(r,t); coherence is encoded in the wavefunction's global phase
- [[uncertainty-principle]] — in a trap, particles are localized; momentum uncertainty is reduced as the ensemble cools, allowing ground-state occupation
- [[quantum-spin]] — integer spin (bosons) vs. half-integer spin (fermions) determines whether condensation is possible
- [[standard-model]] — Higgs boson condensate plays a role in electroweak symmetry breaking
