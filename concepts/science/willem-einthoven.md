# Willem Einthoven

## summary

Dutch physiologist who invented the string galvanometer and, through it, founded electrocardiography. His apparatus recorded the electrical activity of the beating heart, revealing normal and pathological rhythms. His technique became the standard diagnostic tool in cardiology and won him the 1924 Nobel Prize. The electrocardiogram remains the EKG abbreviation from his German name.

## you gotta know

- Invented the *string galvanometer* (1903): a sensitive detector of minute electrical currents that visualized the heart's electrical activity as deflections of a vibrating string.
- Recorded the first human electrocardiograms (EKGs), establishing characteristic waveforms for normal rhythm and identifying arrhythmias, ischaemia, and hypertrophy.
- Standardized the *Einthoven triangle*: the geometric framework for interpreting electrical vectors of the heart from three limb leads.
- Worked at the University of Leiden, where he spent his entire career refining and teaching electrocardiographic method.
- His technique was non-invasive and immediately reproducible — it transformed cardiac diagnosis from physical exam alone to instrumental objectivity.
- Named the distinctive waves of the EKG (P, QRS, T) — names still in use more than a century later.
