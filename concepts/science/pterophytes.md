# Pterophytes

## summary

**Pterophytes** (ferns and allies) are the first plants to evolve true *vascular tissue*, enabling them to grow tall and colonize drier habitats. They reproduce by *spores* released from leaf-like structures called *fronds*, and their complex life cycle alternates between a large, visible sporophyte (the fern plant itself) and a tiny, short-lived gametophyte.

## you gotta know

- Possess *xylem* and *phloem* — true vascular tissue for transporting water and nutrients, allowing erect growth and competition for light.
- The *sporophyte* (the familiar fern plant with fronds) is now the dominant generation; the *gametophyte* shrinks to a tiny, ground-hugging prothallus.
- Reproduce via *spores* found in clusters called *sori* (visible on the underside of fronds), not seeds; still require moisture for *flagellate sperm* to reach the egg.
- Include true ferns (the most diverse), *whisk ferns* (Psilotales), *clubmosses* (Lycopodiophyta), and *horsetails* (Equisetales).
- Flourished in the Carboniferous period (300+ million years ago) and dominated ancient forests; modern ferns are less dominant but still ecologically important.
- Fronds often *pinnately compound* (feather-shaped) or *dichotomously branched*; unfold from the *crozier*, a coiled young frond.

## connections

- [[bryophytes]] — pterophytes evolved from bryophytes by acquiring vascular tissue, allowing greater height and dry-habitat colonization.
- [[gymnosperms]] — the next major step: replacing spores with seeds, eliminating the need for free water in reproduction.
- [[vascular-plants]] — pterophytes are the earliest vascular plants in the evolutionary tree.
- [[xylem]] — essential to pterophyte function; allows tall growth.
- [[sporophyte]] — pterophytes flip the balance; the sporophyte is now the large, visible form.
