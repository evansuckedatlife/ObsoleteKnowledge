# George Gamow

## summary

A Soviet-American theoretical physicist and cosmologist, **George Gamow** was a key developer of the Big Bang theory. He made pioneering contributions to nuclear physics by explaining radioactive decay through quantum tunneling, and he later crossed into molecular biology, proposing that genetic code uses a triplet codon system.

## you gotta know

- *Co-authored the Alpher-Bethe-Gamow paper* ($\alpha\beta\gamma$ paper) in 1948, which explained the cosmic abundance of hydrogen and helium through nucleosynthesis during the early hot universe.
- *Predicted the existence of the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)* radiation in 1948 with Ralph Alpher and Robert Herman, describing it as the leftover heat of the Big Bang.
- *Explained alpha decay through quantum tunneling* in 1928, showing how alpha particles can escape the strong force potential barrier of the atomic nucleus despite lacking the classical energy to do so.
- *Proposed the triplet code hypothesis* in molecular biology, suggesting that combinations of three DNA bases code for one of the twenty amino acids, and founded the *RNA Tie Club* to research the genetic code.
- *Formulated the Gamow-Teller transition* in nuclear physics, describing a specific type of beta decay in which the spins of the emitted electron and antineutrino couple to a total spin of one.
- *Wrote the popular science book series Mr Tompkins*, which explained relativity and quantum physics through the dreams of a bank clerk.

## connections

- [[albert-einstein]] — who worked on the expanding universe solutions to general relativity that formed the mathematical basis for Gamow's Big Bang model.
- [[enrico-fermi]] — a contemporary who also worked on beta decay and nuclear physics.
