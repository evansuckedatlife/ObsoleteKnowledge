# Cenozoic Era

## summary

The **Cenozoic Era** (66 million years ago–present) is the "Age of Mammals," spanning the recovery from the K-Pg extinction through the rise of primates, ungulates, carnivores, and ultimately humans. The era includes three major periods: Paleogene, Neogene, and Quaternary. Climate shifted from greenhouse to icehouse conditions, and grasslands expanded, reshaping the course of evolution. The name means "recent life" in Greek.

## you gotta know

- *Mammals* radiated explosively after dinosaurs vanished, filling ecological niches left empty and diversifying into the major modern lineages—primates, carnivores, cetaceans, ungulates, and rodents.
- *Flowering plants* continued their dominance, including the evolution of *grasses*, which created vast savannas and prairies by the Neogene.
- *Primates* evolved during the early Cenozoic in tropical forests, eventually giving rise to apes and hominins in Africa.
- *Climate cooling* intensified through the Cenozoic, culminating in *ice ages* (glacial cycles) during the Quaternary Period—the last ~2.6 million years.
- The *Paleocene*, *Eocene*, and *Oligocene* epochs of the Paleogene saw early mammal diversification and the establishment of modern mammalian orders.
- *Humans* (genus *Homo*) emerged only in the last ~3 million years, representing a tiny fraction of the Cenozoic's span.
