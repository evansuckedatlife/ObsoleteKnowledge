# Mauna Loa

## summary

An active basaltic shield volcano on the Big Island of Hawaii, **Mauna Loa** is the largest subaerial volcano on Earth by both mass and volume. It covers over half of the island of Hawaii and has broad, gentle slopes built by successive layers of highly fluid lava flows. It is home to a world-famous atmospheric observatory that monitors global greenhouse gases.

## you gotta know

- *Largest active volcano on Earth by volume*, covering over 2,000 square miles (approximately 51% of the land area of the Big Island of Hawaii).
- *A classic basaltic shield volcano*, characterized by extremely broad, gentle slopes that contrast with the steep profiles of stratovolcanoes.
- *Hosts the Mauna Loa Observatory*, which has continuously measured atmospheric carbon dioxide concentrations since 1958, producing the famous *Keeling Curve* that documents global climate change.
- *Name translates to "Long Mountain"* in the Hawaiian language, referring to its massive, dome-like shape and long rift zones.
- *Erupted most recently in late 2022*, ending a 38-year period of quiet and sending lava flows within miles of the Daniel K. Inouye Highway.
