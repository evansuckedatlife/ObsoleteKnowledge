# Charon

## summary

**Charon** is Pluto's largest moon and so massive relative to its primary that the two bodies orbit around a shared center of gravity called a *barycenter*—making them functionally a binary system. Discovered in 1978, Charon is half the diameter of Pluto, and together they were reclassified as dwarf planets in 2006. The *New Horizons* spacecraft flyby in 2015 revealed Charon to be a geologically diverse, cratered world with evidence of past tectonic activity.

## you gotta know

- Diameter ~1,212 km; about 50% of Pluto's size; largest moon relative to its primary.
- *Binary system*: Pluto and Charon orbit a barycenter outside Pluto's surface; they face each other (tidally locked).
- Discovered by *James W. Christy* in 1978 using photographic plates of the United States Naval Observatory.
- *Geologically complex*: cratered highlands, smooth plains, and evidence of past volcanism and tectonics.
- Dark equatorial zone and bright polar caps of water ice suggest compositional variations.
- Orbits every 6.39 days at an inclination of 99.7°, tilting Pluto's rotation axis.
- *New Horizons* mission (2015) provided first high-resolution images; revealed Charon's intricate surface geology.
