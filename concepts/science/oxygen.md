# Oxygen

## summary

Oxygen is a highly reactive, electronegative chalcogen nonmetal that is the third most abundant element in the universe by mass and the most abundant element by mass in Earth's crust, oceans, and biosphere. It forms a colorless and odorless diatomic gas that is essential for aerobic cellular respiration in most living organisms. Because of its high reactivity, it forms oxides with almost all other elements, driving combustion and corrosion processes. It also exists as trioxygen or ozone, which forms a protective layer in the stratosphere that absorbs harmful ultraviolet solar radiation.

## you gotta know

### discovery & isolation

- *Discovered independently* by Carl Wilhelm Scheele in 1772 (who called it "fire air") and Joseph Priestley in 1774, who published his findings first after isolating it by heating mercuric oxide.
- *Named by Antoine Lavoisier* in 1777, who correctly identified it as a chemical element and disproved the phlogiston theory of combustion.

### chemistry & physical properties

- *Is the second most electronegative element* on the Pauling scale (3.44), surpassed only by fluorine, which makes it a highly powerful oxidizing agent.
- *Displays paramagnetism* in its liquid and solid states, a property that causes liquid oxygen to be attracted to and suspended between the poles of a strong magnet.
- *Exists as allotropes* including diatomic oxygen (O2) and ozone (O3), the latter of which is formed in the stratosphere by photolysis and absorbs UV-B radiation.

### biology & geology

- *Produced via photosynthesis*, where water molecules are split by light-dependent reactions in cyanobacteria, algae, and plants, releasing oxygen gas.
- *Accumulated during the Great Oxidation Event* approximately 2.4 billion years ago, which dramatically altered Earth's atmospheric composition and caused a mass extinction of anaerobic organisms.
- *Acts as the terminal electron acceptor* in the electron transport chain of aerobic cellular respiration, where it is reduced to form water.

## connections

- [[carbon]] — forms carbon dioxide, which is converted to glucose and oxygen during photosynthesis.
- [[hydrogen]] — combines with oxygen to form water, the universal solvent of life.
- [[iron]] — reacts with oxygen and water to form rust (hydrated iron(III) oxides).
- [[mercury]] — heating mercuric oxide was the historical method used by Joseph Priestley to isolate oxygen gas.
- [[helium]] — oxygen is synthesized in massive stars via helium-burning processes (the alpha process).
- [[ozone]] — the triatomic allotrope of oxygen that blocks ultraviolet light.

## context

Oxygen is the life-giving driver of aerobic respiration and chemical oxidation on Earth. Its high electronegativity and reactivity explain why it is central to both the combustion of fuels and the metabolism of living cells. In quiz bowl, oxygen is frequently clued via its co-discoverers Scheele and Priestley, its naming by Lavoisier, its paramagnetic liquid state, Stratospheric ozone depletion, the Great Oxidation Event, and its role as the final electron acceptor in the mitochondria.
