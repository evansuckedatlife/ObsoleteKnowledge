## summary

**Ceres** is a dwarf planet in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, and the largest body in that region. With a diameter of ~950 km and a mass comprising roughly one-third of the asteroid belt's total mass, it was classified as an asteroid after its discovery in 1801, then reclassified as a planet briefly in the 19th century, and finally as a dwarf planet in 2006. The *Dawn* space probe revealed it to be geologically complex, with cryovolcanism, subsurface water ice, and bright mineral deposits.

## you gotta know

- *Discovered* on 1 January 1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi; the naming follows the Roman goddess of agriculture, fitting for the first asteroid discovered.
- *Most massive asteroid:* constitutes ~38% of the asteroid belt's total mass, far exceeding Vesta or any other belt resident; this dominance enabled its classification as a dwarf planet.
- *Water-rich composition:* the surface and interior contain substantial water ice and hydrated minerals; spectroscopy indicates clay minerals and carbonates, suggesting aqueous alteration.
- *Bright spots (Occator impact):* the *Occator* crater contains unusually bright deposits of salt-mineral residue (sodium carbonate), thought to be brine evaporite left by cryovolcanic activity.
- *Cryovolcanism:* evidence suggests water-ice-rich magma erupted in the recent past; *Ahuna Mons*, a dome-shaped cryovolcanic feature, is geologically young (~200 million years).
- *Halo of dwarf planets:* Ceres may be the remnant core of a proto-planet that never fully assembled due to Jupiter's gravitational perturbations.

## connections

- [[asteroid-belt]] — Ceres is the gravitational center and mass anchor of the asteroid belt; all belt residents move in its shadow.
- [[dwarf-planet]] — Ceres shares reclassification history with Pluto; both were demoted in 2006 under the same IAU rule.
- [[vesta]] — Ceres's nearest massive rival in the belt; Vesta is drier and more geologically rigid, contrasting Ceres's icy, dynamic character.
- [[bright-spots]] — the Occator crater's mineral deposits are among the solar system's most reflective features.
