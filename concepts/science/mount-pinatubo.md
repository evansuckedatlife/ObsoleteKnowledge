# Mount Pinatubo

## summary

Located on the island of Luzon in the Philippines, **Mount Pinatubo** is an active stratovolcano. Its catastrophic eruption in June 1991 was the second-largest volcanic eruption of the 20th century, ejecting millions of tons of sulfur dioxide into the atmosphere. This resulted in a global aerosol cloud that caused a significant worldwide temperature drop over the following years.

## you gotta know

- *Erupted in June 1991* after a 600-year period of dormancy, representing the second-largest volcanic eruption of the 20th century (surpassed only by the 1912 eruption of Novarupta in Alaska).
- *Caused measurable global cooling* of approximately 0.5°C (0.9°F) between 1991 and 1993 by injecting 20 million tons of sulfur dioxide gas into the stratosphere, which formed a global haze of sulfuric acid aerosols.
- *Coincided with Typhoon Yunya*, whose torrential rains mixed with the freshly erupted volcanic ash to trigger massive, destructive mudflows called *lahars* across Central Luzon.
- *Forced the evacuation of Clark Air Base*, a major United States military facility located nearby, which was heavily damaged and subsequently abandoned by the U.S. military.
- *Home to the indigenous Aeta people*, who had resided on the slopes of the volcano for generations and were permanently displaced by the eruption.
