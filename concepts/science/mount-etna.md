# Mount Etna

## summary

Located on the east coast of Sicily, Italy, **Mount Etna** is the highest active volcano in Europe outside the Caucasus. One of the most active volcanoes in the world, it is in a state of near-constant activity and has a recorded history of eruptions dating back to 1500 BC. In ancient mythology, it was believed to be the prison of the giant Typhon and the site of Hephaestus's subterranean forge.

## you gotta know

- *Highest active volcano in Europe* (outside the Caucasus Mountains), dominating the skyline of eastern Sicily near the city of Catania.
- *Associated in Greek mythology with the monster Typhon*, who was said to have been defeated and buried under the mountain by Zeus, with Etna's eruptions representing his fiery struggles.
- *Mythological home of the forge of Hephaestus* (Vulcan), where the Cyclopes crafted thunderbolts for Zeus.
- *Linked to the death of the philosopher Empedocles*, who according to legend threw himself into the volcano's crater in 430 BC to prove his divinity, only for the volcano to spit back one of his bronze sandals.
- *Enjoys a long history of study*, with its 1669 eruption being one of the first in history where humans successfully attempted to divert lava flows by digging trenches.
