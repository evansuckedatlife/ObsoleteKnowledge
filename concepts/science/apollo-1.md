## summary

A crewed Apollo capsule test scheduled for launch on February 21, 1967, that ended in catastrophic fire during a ground test on January 27. All three crew members—*Gus Grissom*, *Ed White*, and *Roger Chaffee*—were killed in the *capsule fire*. The disaster prompted a major redesign of the Apollo spacecraft and crew safety systems, forcing a lengthy pause in the Space Race.

## you gotta know

- On *January 27, 1967*, a *cabin fire* broke out during a launch-pad test of the *Apollo* command module.
- The crew—*Gus Grissom*, *Ed White*, and *Roger Chaffee*—were overcome by *smoke and toxic fumes* and could not escape; all three were killed.
- *Gus Grissom* was a veteran *Mercury* and *Gemini* astronaut; *Ed White* had performed the *first American spacewalk*; *Roger Chaffee* was on his first spaceflight.
- The pure *oxygen atmosphere* in the command module, pressurized at higher than normal levels during the test, fed the fire and made escape impossible.
- *Investigations* revealed the Apollo capsule's design had *numerous flaws*: faulty wiring, flammable materials, and a hatch that could not be opened quickly from inside.
- The disaster halted the Apollo program for *21 months*, during which *NASA* undertook a complete redesign of the command module and crew safety protocols.
- The capsule's hatch was replaced with an outward-opening hatch that could be jettisoned in seconds.
