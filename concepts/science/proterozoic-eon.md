# Proterozoic Eon

## summary

The **Proterozoic Eon** (2.5 billion–541 million years ago) saw the rise of oxygen in the atmosphere, the evolution of multicellular life, and dramatic glaciation events. The name means "bearing early life" in Greek. This vast eon is divided into three major eras: Paleoproterozoic, Mesoproterozoic, and Neoproterozoic.

## you gotta know

- The *Great Oxidation Event* (~2.4 billion years ago) flooded the atmosphere with oxygen, poisoning anaerobic microbes and forcing life to adapt—one of Earth's most profound ecological shifts.
- *Snowball Earth* episodes (especially the *Neoproterozoic Snowball*) saw the entire planet freeze over multiple times, creating a white-world covered in ice thousands of feet thick.
- *Multicellular organisms* evolved near the end of the Proterozoic, including the *Ediacara fauna*—soft-bodied animals that predate the Cambrian by tens of millions of years.
- The *Proterozoic supercontinent Rodinia* broke apart during this eon, and later *Pannotia* assembled near the end.
- *Eukaryotic cells* (cells with a nucleus) arose during the Proterozoic, evolving through *endosymbiosis*—the engulfing of one cell by another.
- By the end of the Proterozoic, the stage was set for the *Cambrian Explosion*—the rapid diversification of animal phyla.
