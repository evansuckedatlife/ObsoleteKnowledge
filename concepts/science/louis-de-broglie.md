## summary

A French physicist and aristocrat, **Louis de Broglie** proposed the radical theory that all matter possesses wave-like properties. His wave-particle duality hypothesis for matter formed the foundation of wave mechanics and was experimentally confirmed by the diffraction of electrons. He was awarded the 1929 Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of the wave nature of electrons.

## you gotta know

- *Formulated the de Broglie hypothesis* in his 1924 doctoral thesis, proposing that moving particles have an associated wavelength ($\lambda = h/p$) determined by Planck's constant ($h$) divided by the particle's momentum ($p$).
- *Explained the quantized orbits of the Bohr model* by demonstrating that the stable electron orbits around a nucleus correspond to standing waves.
- *Developed the pilot wave theory* (or double solution theory), an early deterministic, non-local hidden-variable interpretation of quantum mechanics that was later expanded by David Bohm.
- *Confirmed experimentally by the Davisson-Germer experiment* (and independently by George Paget Thomson), which demonstrated that electrons undergo diffraction when scattered by a nickel crystal.
- Awarded the Kalinga Prize by UNESCO in 1952 for his efforts in popularizing scientific knowledge.

## connections

- [[albert-einstein]] — who endorsed de Broglie's doctoral thesis, which helped gain it acceptance among physicists.
- [[niels-bohr]] — whose quantized atomic model was physically explained by de Broglie's standing wave hypothesis.
- [[erwin-schrodinger]] — who used de Broglie's concept of matter waves to construct his famous quantum wave equation.
