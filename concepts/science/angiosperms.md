## summary

**Angiosperms** are the *flowering plants* — the most diverse and ecologically dominant plant group today — characterized by seeds enclosed in a fruit (the ripened ovary). They evolved in the Early Cretaceous and rapidly diversified, now accounting for ~90% of all plant species and nearly all staple food crops.

## you gotta know

- Seeds develop inside a *fruit* (the ripened *ovary*), protecting the seed and often aiding in dispersal via animals; this is the key distinction from *gymnasperms*.
- Possess *flowers* — specialized reproductive structures with petals, sepals, stamens, and carpels — which attract pollinators and facilitate diverse *pollination* strategies (insects, birds, wind, water).
- Include two major groups: *Monocots* (one cotyledon; grasses, lilies, orchids, palms) and *Dicots* (two cotyledons; broadleaves, legumes, composites).
- Have evolved *double fertilization* — a unique process where two sperm nuclei fertilize two egg cells, one producing the embryo and one producing the nutritive *endosperm* inside the seed.
- Dominate nearly every terrestrial and shallow-water habitat; include all major food crops (wheat, rice, corn, beans, fruits) and most wild flowering plants.
- Secondary growth (wood) is produced in some angiosperms via cambium, but many lack it (herbaceous plants); this flexibility allows both trees and short-lived herbs.

## connections

- [[gymnosperms]] — angiosperms are derived from gymnosperm ancestors; the *fruit* and *flower* are their major innovations.
- [[monocots]] — one of the two major angiosperm classes.
- [[dicots]] — the other major angiosperm class.
- [[seed]] — angiosperm seeds are enclosed in fruits, unlike gymnosperm seeds.
- [[flower]] — the defining reproductive structure of angiosperms.
- [[pollination]] — flowers are exquisitely adapted to diverse pollination vectors.
- [[photosynthesis]] — like all plants, angiosperms are photosyntheistic.
