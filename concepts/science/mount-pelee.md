# Mount Pelée

## summary

An active stratovolcano on the Caribbean island of Martinique, **Mount Pelée** is infamous for its devastating eruption in May 1902. The eruption destroyed the city of Saint-Pierre, killing approximately 30,000 people in minutes, making it the deadliest volcanic disaster of the 20th century. The event revolutionized modern volcanology by demonstrating the speed and destructive power of pyroclastic flows.

## you gotta know

- *Destroyed the city of Saint-Pierre* (known as the "Paris of the West Indies") in May 1902, killing nearly all of its 30,000 inhabitants within minutes.
- *Spared only a few survivors*, most famously *Ludger Sylbaris*, a prisoner who survived the superheated gas because he was locked in a poorly ventilated, semi-subterranean stone jail cell.
- *Popularized the study of pyroclastic flows*, which were described in detail by French mineralogist Alfred Lacroix under the name *nuée ardente* ("glowing cloud").
- *Grew a massive volcanic spine* (the "Spine of Pelée") from its crater floor following the eruption, which reached a height of over 1,000 feet before collapsing.
- *Occurred during a local election cycle*, which led the governor of Martinique to discourage evacuation to prevent panic and ensure residents cast their votes.
