# Devonian Period

## summary

The **Devonian Period** (419–359 million years ago) is called the *Age of Fishes* because jawed fish diversified explosively and dominated the oceans. Meanwhile, plants carpeted the land with forests, and the first four-legged vertebrates—*tetrapods*—crawled from the water. The period is named after Devon, a county in southwest England.

## you gotta know

- The *Age of Fishes* saw the rise of sharks, ray-finned fish, and lobe-finned fish—the latter being ancestors of all land vertebrates.
- *Tetrapods* (four-limbed vertebrates) evolved from lobe-finned fish, with *Tiktaalik* and *Acanthostega* showing the transition between aquatic and terrestrial life.
- Vast *forests* of early trees (ferns, club mosses, seed-ferns) covered the continents, producing the first significant oxygen surge from land plants.
- The *Devonian extinction* (~375 and 359 million years ago) came in pulses, wiping out reef-building corals and many fish species, possibly from ocean anoxia or asteroid impact.
- *Lobe-finned fish* had fleshy fins and lungs, giving them the anatomical toolkit to eventually colonise land.
- The first *insects* appeared, diversifying on the newly vegetated landscape.
