## summary

The **Silurian Period** (444–419 million years ago) witnessed the colonisation of land by plants for the first time—humble *vascular plants* like *Cooksonia* rooted themselves in barren soil. The period is named after the Silures, an ancient Welsh tribe. Marine life continued to flourish with the rise of jawed fishes, while the first scorpions crept onto land.

## you gotta know

- *Vascular plants* (with tubes for water transport) evolved, marking the first vegetation on land—initially small, without leaves or roots.
- *Jawed fish* (placoderms) dominated the seas, armored creatures with crude jaw-like structures that were the ancestors of all modern jawed vertebrates.
- *Coral reefs* expanded, and the oceans were cooler and more stable than after the Ordovician extinction.
- The first *arachnids* (scorpions) ventured onto land, breathing air and hunting the earliest arthropods.
- *Ozone layer* formation in the upper atmosphere made land more habitable by filtering ultraviolet radiation.
- No large animals colonised land yet; terrestrial life was still confined to small arthropods and primitive plants.
