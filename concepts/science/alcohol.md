# Alcohol

## summary

An alcohol is an organic compound in which a hydroxyl functional group (-OH) is covalently bonded to a saturated carbon atom. The presence of the highly polar hydroxyl group enables alcohols to participate in intermolecular hydrogen bonding, giving them significantly higher boiling points and greater water solubility than corresponding hydrocarbons. Alcohols are classified as primary, secondary, or tertiary based on the number of carbon atoms bonded to the carbon atom bearing the hydroxyl group. They are among the most versatile intermediates in synthetic organic chemistry, widely used as solvents, fuels, and chemical precursors.

## you gotta know

### structure & physical properties

- *Characterized by the hydroxyl group* (-OH), consisting of an oxygen atom bonded to a hydrogen atom, which imparts polar character and hydrogen-bonding capability.
- *Classified by carbon substitution* as primary (1°), secondary (2°), or tertiary (3°) depending on the number of alkyl groups attached to the C-OH carbon, which dictates their reactivity and oxidation pathways.
- *Exhibits water solubility* for short-chain members (like methanol and ethanol) due to hydrogen bonding, which decreases as the hydrophobic carbon chain length increases.

### synthesis & reactions

- *Synthesized via hydration of alkenes* using acid-catalyzed hydration or oxymercuration-demercuration (yielding Markovnikov products), or hydroboration-oxidation (yielding anti-Markovnikov products).
- *Prepared by the reduction of carbonyls*, where aldehydes and ketones are reduced using sodium borohydride (NaBH4) or lithium aluminum hydride (LiAlH4), and carboxylic acids and esters are reduced using LiAlH4.
- *Oxidized to carbonyl compounds*, where primary alcohols yield aldehydes (under mild oxidants like PCC) or carboxylic acids (under strong oxidants like Jones reagent), and secondary alcohols yield ketones.
- *Undergoes dehydration* in the presence of strong acid catalysts (like sulfuric acid) to form alkenes via an elimination reaction.
- *Reacts in Fischer esterification* with carboxylic acids under acid catalysis to produce esters.

## connections

- [[carboxylic-acid]] — condenses with alcohols to form esters; also, oxidation of primary alcohols yields carboxylic acids.
- [[ether]] — constitutional isomers of alcohols with the same molecular formula (such as ethanol and dimethyl ether).
- [[ester]] — formed by reacting an alcohol with a carboxylic acid.
- [[aldehyde]] — produced by the mild oxidation of primary alcohols.
- [[ketone]] — produced by the oxidation of secondary alcohols.
- [[water]] — shares structural similarity with alcohols, where an alcohol can be viewed as a water molecule with one hydrogen replaced by an alkyl group.

## context

Alcohols are central to organic synthesis, acting as both targets of reduction and sources of oxidation. The term alcohol commonly refers to ethanol, the active ingredient in alcoholic beverages and a major biofuel. In quiz bowl, alcohols are clued via their hydroxyl group, classification into primary/secondary/tertiary, synthesis (hydroboration-oxidation, oxymercuration-demercuration), oxidation pathways (using PCC, Swern, or chromic acid), and diagnostic tests like the Lucas test (which differentiates alcohol classes based on precipitation rate).
