# Distillation

## summary

Distillation is a widely used laboratory and industrial technique for separating the components of a liquid mixture based on differences in their volatile behaviors or boiling points. The process involves heating the liquid mixture to vaporize its more volatile components, which are then routed to a condenser where they cool and return to a liquid state, called the distillate. It is a fundamental technique in chemical engineering, essential for refining crude oil into gasoline and diesel, purifying water, and producing concentrated alcoholic beverages.

## you gotta know

### thermodynamic principles

- *Governed by Raoult's law* and Dalton's law, which describe how the composition of the vapor phase in equilibrium with a liquid mixture is enriched in the more volatile component.
- *Limited by azeotropes*, constant-boiling mixtures where the liquid and vapor phases have identical compositions, preventing separation beyond that point by simple or fractional distillation.
- *Analyzed using the McCabe-Thiele method*, a graphical technique that plots operating lines on a vapor-liquid equilibrium diagram to determine the number of theoretical plates needed for column separation.

### common methods & apparatus

- *Includes fractional distillation*, which uses a fractionating column filled with packing material to create multiple vaporization-condensation cycles, separating liquids with close boiling points.
- *Employs steam distillation* for temperature-sensitive organic compounds, bubbling steam through the mixture to lower their boiling points below their thermal decomposition temperatures.
- *Utilizes vacuum distillation* to lower the boiling points of heavy or non-volatile compounds by reducing the pressure inside the system.
- *Features the rotary evaporator* (rotovap), which uses a rotating flask under vacuum to rapidly and gently evaporate solvents from a sample.

## connections

- [[chromatography]] — a separation method based on differential phase affinity, whereas distillation relies on vapor-liquid equilibrium.
- [[water]] — simple distillation is widely used to produce high-purity distilled water by separating it from dissolved minerals.
- [[helium]] — fractional distillation of natural gas at cryogenic temperatures is the primary method used to extract helium.
- [[mercury]] — historical alchemists purified mercury through distillation using an apparatus called an alembic.
- [[alcohol]] — distillation is the key process for concentrating ethanol to produce spirits and industrial solvent.
- [[pressure]] — vacuum distillation directly exploits the relationship between ambient pressure and boiling point.

## context

Distillation is one of the oldest and most economically significant separation processes in history, transitioning from alchemical alembics to massive industrial refinery columns. In quiz bowl, distillation is frequently clued via Raoult's law, azeotropes (such as the 95.6% ethanol-water minimum-boiling azeotrope), fractionating columns (with theoretical plates and HETP), the McCabe-Thiele method, and apparatus components like the Liebig condenser and fractionating column.
