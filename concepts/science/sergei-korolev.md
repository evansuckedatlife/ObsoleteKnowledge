# Sergei Korolev

## summary

Soviet chief rocket designer (1906–1966) who led the Soviet space program from *Sputnik* through the *Vostok* manned missions. Though his name was withheld from the public during his lifetime for security reasons, Korolev was the architect of the Soviet space triumph and a visionary planner who envisioned the first lunar landing.

## you gotta know

- Chief designer of the *R-7 rocket* (the first *intercontinental ballistic missile*), which was adapted to launch *Sputnik 1* in 1957 — the first artificial satellite and the event that shocked the West into the Space Race.
- Designed the *Vostok spacecraft* and oversaw the first crewed spaceflight: *Yuri Gagarin* on April 12, 1961 — a propaganda victory that established Soviet superiority in space.
- Planned an ambitious *Soviet lunar program*, including the *N1 rocket* (intended to be a Soviet answer to the Saturn V); the N1 failed repeatedly, and the Soviet Union never reached the moon.
- Worked in secrecy under the Soviet system; his name was classified and not publicly revealed until after his death, making him an unknown giant to the West while he shaped history.
- Endured imprisonment during Stalin's purges (1938–1944) and survived wartime hardship, yet rebuilt Soviet rocket design from captured German V-2s and indigenous innovation.
- Died suddenly in January 1966 during surgery, at age 59, depriving the Soviet program of his leadership during its final push for the moon.

- [[konstantin-tsiolkovsky]] — Korolev revered Tsiolkovsky as a predecessor and grounded his program in Tsiolkovskian principles.
- [[wernher-von-braun]] — American rival; both inherited V-2 technology and diverged in competing space visions.
