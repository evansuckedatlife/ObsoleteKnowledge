## summary

A German-born theoretical physicist, **Albert Einstein** is widely considered one of the two pillars of modern physics alongside quantum mechanics. He developed the special and general theories of relativity, which revolutionized concepts of space, time, mass, and gravity. He also made foundational contributions to quantum theory and statistical mechanics, earning the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect.

## you gotta know

### relativity & mass-energy equivalence

- *Formulated the theory of special relativity* in 1905, postulating that the laws of physics are identical for all non-accelerating observers and that the speed of light in a vacuum is constant, leading to the famous mass-energy equivalence equation $E = mc^2$.
- *Developed the theory of general relativity* in 1915, describing gravity not as an attractive force but as the curvature of four-dimensional spacetime caused by the presence of mass and energy.
- *Predicted the existence of gravitational waves* and the bending of light by gravity (gravitational lensing), which was famously confirmed by Arthur Eddington during a solar eclipse in 1919.

### quantum theory & statistical mechanics

- *Explained the photoelectric effect* in 1905 by proposing that light consists of discrete wave-packets of energy called light quanta (photons), a key foundation for wave-particle duality.
- *Mathematically explained Brownian motion* in 1905, providing the first definitive physical proof of the existence of atoms and molecules.
- *Co-developed Bose-Einstein statistics* and predicted the existence of the Bose-Einstein condensate, a state of matter occurring near absolute zero.
- *Co-authored the EPR paradox paper* in 1935 with Boris Podolsky and Nathan Rosen to challenge the completeness of quantum mechanics, arguing against "spooky action at a distance" (quantum entanglement).
- Spend his later years at the *Institute for Advanced Study* in Princeton trying to formulate a Unified Field Theory, famously rejecting the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics by stating *"God does not play dice with the universe"*.
- *Wrote a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt* in 1939 warning of German nuclear weapons research, which catalyzed the creation of the Manhattan Project.

## connections

- [[max-planck]] — whose quantum constant Einstein extended to electromagnetic radiation to explain the photoelectric effect.
- [[niels-bohr]] — his chief philosophical rival with whom he engaged in the Bohr-Einstein debates over the interpretation of quantum mechanics.
- [[louis-de-broglie]] — whose hypothesis on matter waves Einstein endorsed, helping it gain widespread scientific acceptance.
- [[werner-heisenberg]] — whose uncertainty principle Einstein contested using various thought experiments.
- [[manhattan-project]] — the atomic bomb project that Einstein helped initiate through his warning letter to Roosevelt.
- [[photoelectric-effect]] — the phenomenon involving the emission of electrons when light shines on a material, which earned Einstein his Nobel Prize.

## context

Albert Einstein's work dismantled the classical Newtonian framework that had dominated physics for over two centuries. His name has become synonymous with genius in the popular imagination. In the quiz bowl canon, Einstein is a massive target clued by his four 1905 *Annus Mirabilis* papers, his debates at the Solvay Conferences, his work on the EPR paradox, and specific components of relativity like the equivalence principle and the Einstein field equations.
