# Europa

## summary

**Europa** is the fourth and smallest of Jupiter's four large Galilean moons, but its icy surface and compelling evidence of a subsurface liquid-water ocean make it one of the most intriguing worlds in the solar system. Discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610, Europa's smooth, fractured ice crust is thought to overlie a global ocean that may harbor conditions suitable for life. It is a primary target for astrobiology and future robotic exploration.

## you gotta know

- Diameter 3,122 km; smallest of the four Galilean moons but dynamically unique.
- *Subsurface ocean*: 50–200 km of liquid water beneath an icy crust; may contain more water than Earth's oceans.
- *Tidal heating*: orbital resonance with [[io]] and [[ganymede]] drives internal friction despite Europa's distance from Jupiter.
- Smooth icy surface crisscrossed by *linear features* (fractures and ridges) suggesting ocean dynamics and cryovolcanism.
- Few impact craters; young surface (100–200 million years old) implies active resurfacing.
- Spectroscopy suggests organic compounds on the surface; radiation may drive synthesis of prebiotic chemistry.
- One of the four *Galilean moons* discovered by Galileo in 1610.
