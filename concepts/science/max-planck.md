# Max Planck

## summary

A German theoretical physicist, **Max Planck** is widely regarded as the founder of quantum mechanics. He made the revolutionary proposal that energy is emitted or absorbed in discrete packets rather than a continuous wave, fundamentally altering the course of modern physics. He was awarded the 1918 Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of energy quanta.

## you gotta know

- *Solved the ultraviolet catastrophe* of black-body radiation by introducing the hypothesis that electromagnetic energy can only be emitted in quantized packets.
- *Defined the Planck constant* ($h \approx 6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ Joule-seconds), a fundamental physical constant that relates the energy of a photon to its frequency via the equation $E = hf$.
- *Formulated Planck's law* of black-body radiation, which mathematically describes the spectral density of electromagnetic radiation emitted by a black body in thermal equilibrium.
- *Established the Planck units*, a system of natural measurement units based on fundamental physical constants (such as the Planck length, Planck time, and Planck mass).
- *Served as president of the Kaiser Wilhelm Society* during the interwar and World War II years, which was later renamed the Max Planck Society in his honor.

## connections

- [[albert-einstein]] — who built on Planck's quantum hypothesis to explain the photoelectric effect.
- [[niels-bohr]] — who incorporated the Planck constant into his quantized model of the hydrogen atom.
