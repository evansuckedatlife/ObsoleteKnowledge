# Jurassic Period

## summary

The **Jurassic Period** (201–145 million years ago) was the golden age of dinosaurs, when *sauropods* reached colossal sizes—*Brachiosaurus*, *Diplodocus*—and *theropods* grew into fearsome predators like *Allosaurus*. Pangaea rifted into northern and southern continents; oceans flooded vast inland seas. *Birds* evolved from theropod dinosaurs. The period is named after the Jura Mountains in the Alps.

## you gotta know

- *Sauropods* dominated terrestrial ecosystems, quadrupedal herbivores with long necks and whip-like tails, some exceeding 30 metres in length.
- *Theropod predators* like *Allosaurus* and *Spinosaurus* hunted the sauropods; some theropods evolved *feathers* and eventually took flight as *birds*.
- *Pangaea* rifted, opening the *Atlantic Ocean* and creating separate northern (*Laurasia*) and southern (*Gondwana*) continents, altering climate and biodiversity.
- *Conifer forests* remained dominant; *flowering plants* (angiosperms) appeared late in the Jurassic but remained rare.
- *Ammonites* (spiral-shelled cephalopods) and other marine life flourished in the shallow inland seas that covered much of the continents.
- *Pterosaurs*—flying reptiles distinct from birds—soared overhead, some with wingspans exceeding 10 metres.
