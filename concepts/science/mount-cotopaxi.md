# Mount Cotopaxi

## summary

An active stratovolcano located in the Andes Mountains of Ecuador, **Mount Cotopaxi** is one of the highest active volcanoes in the world, rising to an elevation of 19,347 feet. It features a nearly symmetrical, snow-capped cone and is home to one of the world's few equatorial glaciers. Eruptions of Cotopaxi present a severe risk due to the rapid melting of its ice cap, which generates massive mudflows.

## you gotta know

- *One of the highest active volcanoes in the world*, reaching an elevation of 5,897 meters (19,347 feet) in the Cordillera Central of the Ecuadorian Andes.
- *Possesses a nearly symmetrical cone* covered by a prominent equatorial glacier, which is highly prone to melting during eruptions and causing devastating mudflows.
- *Threatens the nearby city of Latacunga*, which has been buried and rebuilt multiple times throughout history due to massive volcanic mudflows (*lahars*) racing down the mountain's valleys.
- *First climbed successfully* in 1872 by German geologist Wilhelm Reiss and Colombian explorer Angel Escobar.
- *Depicted in a famous 1862 landscape painting* by Frederic Edwin Church, a prominent member of the American Hudson River School of artists.
