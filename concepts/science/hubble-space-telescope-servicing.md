# Hubble Space Telescope Servicing Missions

## summary

A series of five *Space Shuttle* missions (1993–2009) to repair, upgrade, and maintain the *Hubble Space Telescope*. The first servicing mission, in December 1993, corrected a *spherical aberration* in Hubble's primary mirror, enabling the observatory to produce the sharp images it was designed for. Subsequent missions installed new instruments, replaced solar panels and batteries, and kept Hubble operational for over three decades, making it one of the most productive scientific instruments ever built.

## you gotta know

- *Hubble* launched on *April 24, 1990*, but was found to have a *flawed mirror* that caused blurred images—a devastating flaw in what was meant to be humanity's premier space observatory.
- The first *servicing mission* launched on *December 2, 1993*, carrying a corrective *optics module* called *COSTAR* and new instruments that compensated for the mirror defect.
- Astronauts performed *spacewalks* to install the repair hardware and new instruments during the mission, demonstrating the value of human spaceflight for complex maintenance tasks.
- Subsequent servicing missions (1997, 1999, 2002, 2009) upgraded Hubble's instruments, replaced *solar panels*, *batteries*, and *gyroscopes*, and extended its operational life well beyond its original design.
- The *final servicing mission* in *May 2009* was one of the most dangerous *Space Shuttle* missions ever flown, as the shuttle had to boost Hubble's orbit to a safe altitude while servicing it.
- Hubble has discovered *dark energy*, revealed the universe's age and expansion rate, imaged *deep fields* containing thousands of galaxies, and captured images of *exoplanet atmospheres*.
- The *Hubble Space Telescope Servicing Missions* remain exemplars of *human spaceflight's* unique value for on-orbit repair and adaptation of complex machinery.
