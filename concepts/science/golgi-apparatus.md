# Golgi apparatus

## summary

The Golgi apparatus is a membrane-bound organelle that modifies, packages, and ships proteins and lipids received from the endoplasmic reticulum. Consisting of stacked, flattened cisternae (sac-like compartments), the Golgi acts as a post-office for the cell, adding sugar groups (glycosylation), sorting cargo, and directing molecules to their final destinations. It is especially prominent in secretory cells such as those in the pancreas and immune system.

## you gotta know

- **Stack of cisternae**: typically 4–8 flattened membranous sacs arranged in a polarized manner
- **Cis, medial, trans**: three functional regions with distinct enzyme sets for progressive modification
- **Glycosylation**: adds and modifies carbohydrate chains on proteins (*N-glycans* and *O-glycans*)
- **Sulfation and phosphorylation**: adds chemical modifications that affect protein function and targeting
- **Cargo sorting**: packages proteins into vesicles destined for different cellular locations
- **Vesicular transport**: receives from ER via COPII vesicles; sends to lysosomes, plasma membrane, and other organelles
- **Enzyme compartmentalization**: each cisterna contains specific enzymes ensuring sequential modifications

## context

The Golgi apparatus represents a specialization of the secretory pathway, allowing eukaryotic cells to produce diverse protein variants. Post-translational modifications at the Golgi—particularly glycosylation—critically determine protein folding, stability, localization, and function. Defects in Golgi glycosylation cause severe genetic diseases. The organelle's prominence in secretory cells reflects the constant demand for protein export; non-secretory cells have smaller Golgi arrays. Vesicular budding and fusion at the Golgi remain active areas of cell biology research.

## connections

- [[endoplasmic-reticulum]] — receives newly synthesized proteins from ER
- [[lysosome]] — sorts proteins destined for lysosomal degradation
- [[protein-synthesis]] — modifies proteins after synthesis
- [[cell-membrane]] — packages proteins for membrane insertion and secretion
