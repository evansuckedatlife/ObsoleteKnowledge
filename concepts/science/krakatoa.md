# Krakatoa

## summary

Located in the Sunda Strait between Java and Sumatra in Indonesia, **Krakatoa** is famous for its cataclysmic 1883 eruption. The explosion destroyed the majority of the volcanic island, generated massive tsunamis that killed over 36,000 people, and produced the loudest sound recorded in modern history. The ongoing volcanic activity within the collapsed caldera led to the birth of a new active island, Anak Krakatau (Child of Krakatoa), in 1927.

## you gotta know

### the 1883 eruption & global effects

- *Erupted cataclysmically in August 1883*, releasing energy equivalent to 200 megatons of TNT and destroying over 70% of the island and its surrounding archipelago.
- *Generated the loudest sound in recorded history*, which was heard 3,000 miles away on Rodrigues Island in the Indian Ocean and ruptured the eardrums of sailors 40 miles away.
- *Triggered devastating tsunamis* up to 120 feet high that swept across the coasts of Java and Sumatra, destroying hundreds of villages and causing the vast majority of the eruption's 36,417 casualties.
- *Pioneered global telecommunications tracking*, as news of the disaster was among the first historical events spread instantaneously around the globe via the newly completed worldwide submarine telegraph network.
- *Produced global atmospheric phenomena*, injecting aerosols into the stratosphere that caused temperatures to drop worldwide and generated vivid red sunsets, which reportedly inspired Edvard Munch's painting *The Scream*.

### geographical context & anak krakatau

- *Located in the Sunda Strait* in Indonesia, situating it along the subduction zone where the Indo-Australian Plate slides beneath the Eurasian Plate.
- *Gave birth to Anak Krakatau* ("Child of Krakatoa"), a new volcanic cone that emerged from the underwater caldera in 1927 and continues to grow at a rapid rate.
- *Experienced a major collapse of Anak Krakatau in 2018*, which triggered a landslide-induced tsunami in the Sunda Strait that killed over 400 people.

## connections

## context

Krakatoa is the quintessential case study of a major volcanic explosion and its global geophysical and communications impact. In quiz bowl, Krakatoa is frequently tested through its 1883 date, its location in the Sunda Strait, the record-breaking sound propagation, the atmospheric cooling and red sunsets, and its active successor island Anak Krakatau.
