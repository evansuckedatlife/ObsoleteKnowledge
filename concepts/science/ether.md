# Ether

## summary

An ether is a class of organic compounds characterized by an oxygen atom bonded to two alkyl or aryl groups, represented by the general formula R-O-R'. Ethers are relatively unreactive and volatile, widely used as solvents in organic chemistry because they dissolve both polar and nonpolar substances and do not possess acidic protons.

## you gotta know

- *Synthesized via the Williamson ether synthesis*, an SN2 reaction where an alkoxide ion acts as a nucleophile to displace a halide leaving group from a primary alkyl halide.
- *Lacks the ability to form intermolecular hydrogen bonds* with itself, resulting in significantly lower boiling points than their constitutional isomer alcohols.
- *Forms explosive organic peroxides* upon prolonged exposure to air and light, requiring ethers like tetrahydrofuran (THF) and diethyl ether to be stored with stabilizers and tested regularly.
- *Includes crown ethers*, cyclic polyethers containing repeated ether units that can selectively complex alkali metal cations (like 18-crown-6 chelating potassium ions) in their central cavities.
- *Historically used as an inhalation anesthetic*, specifically diethyl ether, which revolutionized surgical medicine in the mid-19th century.

## connections

- [[alcohol]] — reacting an alcohol with a strong base (like sodium hydride) generates the alkoxide nucleophile required for ether synthesis.
- [[sodium]] — metallic sodium is often used to dry ethers for water-sensitive reactions, as it reacts with trace water and alcohols to form insoluble alkoxides.
- [[oxygen]] — the central ether linkage consists of an sp3 hybridized oxygen atom with a bent molecular geometry.
