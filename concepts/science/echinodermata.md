# Echinodermata

## summary

A phylum of marine deuterostomes including starfish, sea urchins, sand dollars, and sea cucumbers, **Echinodermata** are characterized by five-fold radial symmetry in adult forms. They possess a calcareous endoskeleton and a unique water vascular system that hydraulically operates their tube feet for movement, respiration, and feeding.

## you gotta know

- *Possess a water vascular system*, a network of fluid-filled canals that powers muscle-controlled *tube feet* used for locomotion, respiration, and grasping prey.
- *Exhibit pentaradial symmetry* (five-fold radial symmetry) as adults, although their free-swimming larvae are bilaterally symmetrical, indicating evolutionary descent from bilateral ancestors.
- *Possess a calcareous endoskeleton* composed of tiny plates or spines called ossicles, covered by a thin layer of living skin.
- *Feed using Aristotle's lantern* in sea urchins, a complex chewing apparatus composed of five calcareous jaws and teeth used to scrape algae from rocks.
- *Capable of evisceration* in sea cucumbers, a defense mechanism in which the animal ejects its internal organs to confuse predators, subsequently regenerating the lost structures.

## connections

- [[chordata]] — fellow deuterostomes that share similar embryonic development traits (where the blastopore becomes the anus), separating them from protostome phyla.
- [[arthropoda]] — the highly successful protostome phylum representing a contrasting evolutionary branch of bilateral symmetry and exoskeletons.
