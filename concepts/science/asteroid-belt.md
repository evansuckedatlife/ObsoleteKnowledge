# Asteroid Belt

## summary

The *Asteroid Belt* is a region of space between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter populated by millions of rocky objects ranging from dust particles to dwarf planets. Often imagined as a dense field of colliding rocks, the belt is actually sparse—spacecraft navigate it routinely without collision risk. It is thought to be a remnant of the solar system's formation, representing material that never coalesced into a planet, likely due to Jupiter's gravitational influence.

## you gotta know

- The asteroid belt contains approximately 1.9 million objects larger than 1 kilometer in diameter, with a combined mass less than Earth's Moon.
- The dwarf planet *Ceres*, located in the asteroid belt's inner region, is the largest object and comprises roughly one-third of the belt's total mass.
- The belt extends roughly from 2.2 to 3.2 astronomical units (AU) from the Sun; objects orbit with periods between 3 and 6 years.
- *Jupiter's gravity* is thought to have prevented asteroids from accreting into a planet; gravitational perturbations can eject objects into the inner solar system (creating meteorites) or outward.
- Notable asteroids include *Vesta* (large, bright, and geologically complex), *Eros* (an elongated, potato-shaped object visited by NASA's NEAR-Shoemaker), and *Apophis* (a near-Earth asteroid whose trajectory has been carefully monitored).
- Spectroscopic studies classify asteroids into types—*carbonaceous* (C-type, dark and carbon-rich), *silicate-rich* (S-type), and *metallic-rich* (M-type)—reflecting different solar-system formation environments.
- NASA's Dawn spacecraft orbited both Vesta and Ceres, revealing them as differentiated bodies with complex geological histories.

## connections

- [[jupiter]] — whose gravity shaped the asteroid belt and prevented planetary accretion.
- [[mars]] — orbits just inside the belt; affected by asteroid impacts throughout its history.
- [[comet]] — asteroids and comets are both small bodies, though asteroids are rocky and comets are icy.
- [[kuiper-belt]] — the outer analog, a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune.
- [[ceres]] — the dwarf planet and largest asteroid belt object.
- [[meteorite]] — asteroids that have fallen to Earth, providing direct samples of belt composition.
- [[impact-crater]] — evidence of asteroid collisions on planets and moons.
