# NASA

## summary

The *National Aeronautics and Space Administration* (NASA) is the United States space agency, founded in 1958 at the height of Cold War tensions following the Soviet Union's launch of Sputnik. NASA coordinated the American space program's response, most famously the Apollo program that landed humans on the Moon in 1969. It remains the primary civilian space exploration authority in the U.S.

## you gotta know

- Founded October 1958 by President Eisenhower in direct response to the perceived "missile gap" with the Soviet Union.
- Organized the *Mercury* program (1958–1963) to put the first American in space, culminating in John Glenn's orbital flight.
- Directed the *Gemini* program (1961–1966) to test techniques essential for the Moon landings: EVA, rendezvous, and docking.
- Orchestrated the *Apollo* program under Kennedy's mandate to land humans on the Moon before the Soviets—achieved in 1969 with Apollo 11.
- Developed the *Space Shuttle* program (1981–2011) for reusable spacecraft and built the International Space Station (ISS) in partnership with other nations.
- Led planetary exploration: Voyager probes, Viking Mars landers, Hubble Space Telescope, and rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance.
