## summary

The first spacecraft to carry a *multi-person crew*, launched by the *Soviet Union* on October 12, 1964. *Voskhod 1* carried three cosmonauts—*Vladimir Komarov*, *Boris Yegorov*, and *Konstantin Feoktistov*—in a cramped cabin with no spacesuits. *Voskhod 2*, flown five months later, achieved the first *spacewalk* by *Alexei Leonov*.

## you gotta know

- *Voskhod 1* launched on *October 12, 1964*, carrying *three cosmonauts*—*Vladimir Komarov*, *Boris Yegorov*, and *Konstantin Feoktistov*—the first multi-person crewed spaceflight.
- The three crew members flew *without spacesuits*, unable to separate because the capsule was too small; they relied on the cabin's atmosphere for survival.
- *Konstantin Feoktistov* was a *spacecraft designer*, not a traditional cosmonaut—a political move to show Soviet civilian involvement in space.
- *Voskhod 2*, launched on *March 18, 1965*, achieved the *first spacewalk* when *Alexei Leonov* exited the spacecraft, tethered to the cabin.
- *Leonov's* spacewalk lasted about *12 minutes*, during which his suit inflated in the vacuum, requiring him to reduce air pressure inside the suit to re-enter the airlock.
- Both *Voskhod* missions were rapid Soviet responses to American advances, competing for firsts in the Space Race.
- *Voskhod* was essentially a modified *Vostok* capsule, hastily redesigned to carry additional crew members.
