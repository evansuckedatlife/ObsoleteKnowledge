## summary

An American theoretical physicist, **Richard Feynman** was one of the most brilliant and colorful scientists of the 20th century. He co-developed quantum electrodynamics (QED), introducing the famous visual representations of subatomic particle interactions known as Feynman diagrams. He was awarded the 1965 Nobel Prize in Physics alongside Julian Schwinger and Shin'ichiro Tomonaga.

## you gotta know

- *Invented Feynman diagrams*, pictorial representations of mathematical expressions describing the behavior and interactions of subatomic particles, which revolutionized particle physics calculations.
- *Developed the path integral formulation* of quantum mechanics, describing quantum behavior as a sum over all possible histories/paths rather than a single trajectory.
- *Investigated the Challenger space shuttle disaster* in 1986 as part of the Rogers Commission, famously demonstrating that the shuttle's rubber O-rings lost elasticity in cold temperatures by compressing one and dipping it in a glass of ice water.
- *Worked on the Manhattan Project* at Los Alamos during World War II, where he assisted in developing the atomic bomb and became famous for safe-cracking and practical jokes.
- *Proposed the parton model* of hadron structure in 1969, which described protons and neutrons as composed of point-like constituents (later identified as quarks and gluons).
- *Delivered the Feynman Lectures on Physics*, a highly celebrated three-volume textbook series based on his introductory lectures at Caltech.
- *Wrote the popular autobiographical books* *Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!* and *What Do You Care What Other People Think?*.
