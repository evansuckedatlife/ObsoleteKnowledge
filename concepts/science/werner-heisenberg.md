# Werner Heisenberg

## summary

A German theoretical physicist, **Werner Heisenberg** was a key pioneer of quantum mechanics. He developed matrix mechanics, the first complete mathematical formulation of quantum mechanics, and formulated the uncertainty principle, which places fundamental limits on the measurement of physical systems. He was awarded the 1932 Nobel Prize in Physics for the creation of quantum mechanics.

## you gotta know

- *Formulated the uncertainty principle* in 1927, which states that certain pairs of physical properties, such as position ($x$) and momentum ($p$), cannot be simultaneously measured with arbitrary precision ($\Delta x \Delta p \ge \hbar/2$).
- *Developed matrix mechanics* in 1925 alongside Max Born and Pascual Jordan, which was the first mathematically rigorous formulation of quantum mechanics using non-commutative algebra.
- *Led the German nuclear weapons research program* (the *Uranverein*) during World War II, a highly controversial role that culminated in his tense, mysterious 1941 meeting with Niels Bohr in occupied Copenhagen.
- *Formulated the proton-neutron model* of the atomic nucleus following the discovery of the neutron in 1932, introducing the concept of isospin to describe the strong nuclear force.
- *Proposed the Heisenberg model of ferromagnetism*, a statistical mechanics model explaining magnetic ordering in materials through quantum exchange interactions.

## connections

- [[niels-bohr]] — his mentor and close collaborator, under whose guidance Heisenberg developed the uncertainty principle and Copenhagen interpretation.
- [[albert-einstein]] — who debated Heisenberg's uncertainty principle, arguing that physical systems must possess definite properties independent of measurement.
- [[erwin-schrodinger]] — whose wave mechanics was mathematically equivalent to Heisenberg's matrix mechanics, though the two initially clashed over the physical interpretation.
