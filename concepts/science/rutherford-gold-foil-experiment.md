# Rutherford Gold-Foil Experiment

## summary

In 1909, *Ernest Rutherford*, *Hans Geiger*, and *Ernest Marsden* bombarded thin gold foil with *alpha particles* (helium nuclei) and detected where they scattered. The shocking discovery that most particles passed straight through but a few bounced backward led *Rutherford* to conclude the atom contains a tiny, dense, positively charged *nucleus* — overturning the plum-pudding model of the atom.

## you gotta know

- *Rutherford's* team fired streams of *alpha particles* at a thin gold foil flanked by a detector screen, expecting the particles to scatter slightly if atoms were uniform.
- Most *alpha particles* penetrated the foil undeflected; some scattered at wide angles; a tiny fraction bounced nearly straight back — "as if firing artillery shells at tissue paper and having some bounce back."
- The backward bounces revealed a concentrated centre of positive charge: the *nucleus*, comprising virtually all the atom's mass in a region just 1/100,000 the size of the atom itself.
- The *scattering angle* distribution matched the prediction from *Coulomb's law* applied to head-on collisions with a point charge — confirming nuclear geometry.
- The nuclear model replaced the defunct *plum-pudding model* (a diffuse positive sphere studded with electrons) and became the foundation for *Bohr's* model and modern atomic structure.
- The experiment's elegance: a simple counting task revealing the cosmos at the atomic scale.
