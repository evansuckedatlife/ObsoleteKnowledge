# Ganymede

## summary

**Ganymede** is the largest moon in the solar system and the second-largest Galilean moon of Jupiter. Larger than the planet Mercury, Ganymede has a diverse geology featuring ancient impact basins, younger grooved terrain, and compelling evidence of a subsurface water ocean beneath its icy crust. It was discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610 and is unique among moons in possessing its own magnetic field.

## you gotta know

- Largest moon in the solar system; diameter 5,268 km (larger than Mercury).
- *Subsurface ocean*: evidence suggests a liquid-water layer 100+ km thick beneath an icy crust, containing more water than all Earth's oceans combined.
- *Magnetic field*: generates its own magnetosphere (weak but measurable), unlike other moons.
- Surface shows *grooved terrain* (younger, lighter rifts) and *dark terrain* (ancient, heavily cratered highlands).
- One of the four *Galilean moons* discovered by Galileo in 1610.
- Ice composition suggests a history of volcanism ("cryovolcanism"); lacks the tidal heating of neighboring [[io]].
- Named after the Trojan prince abducted by Zeus in the form of an eagle.

## connections
