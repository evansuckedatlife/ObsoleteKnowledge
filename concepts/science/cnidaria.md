## summary

A phylum of radially symmetrical aquatic animals including jellyfish, corals, sea anemones, and hydras, **Cnidaria** are characterized by specialized stinging cells called cnidocytes. They possess a simple tissue-level body plan with two germ layers and a gastrovascular cavity with a single opening that serves as both mouth and anus.

## you gotta know

- *Possess stinging cells called cnidocytes* (which contain explosive organelles called *nematocysts*) used to capture prey and defend against predators by injecting toxins.
- *Exhibit radial symmetry* and are diploblastic, developing from only two primary germ layers (ectoderm and endoderm) separated by a jelly-like layer called the mesoglea.
- *Alternate between two body plans*: the stationary, cylinder-shaped *polyp* and the free-swimming, umbrella-shaped *medusa*.
- *Feature a simple nerve net* without a centralized brain, using basic sensory receptors for light, gravity, and touch.
- Includes *siphonophores* (such as the Portuguese man o' war), which are not single organisms but colonies of specialized individual units called zooids.

## connections

- [[porifera]] — a simpler animal phylum that lacks the true tissues and symmetry found in Cnidarians.
- [[ctenophora]] — a similar phylum of gelatinous marine animals (comb jellies) that lacks cnidocytes.
