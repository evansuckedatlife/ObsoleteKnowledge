# Annelida

## summary

A phylum of invertebrate segmented worms including earthworms, leeches, and marine bristle worms, **Annelida** are coelomate protostomes. They are characterized by metamerism, where the body is divided into repeated segments containing identical organs. They possess a closed circulatory system and are found in marine, freshwater, and terrestrial habitats.

## you gotta know

- *Exhibit metamerism*, a body plan composed of repeated segments (metameres) separated internally by membranous walls called septa.
- *Possess chitinous bristles called setae* (or chaetae) projecting from their skin, which they use to anchor themselves and aid in locomotion.
- Includes *leeches* (hirudineans), blood-feeding ectoparasites that secrete the powerful anticoagulant chemical *hirudin* to keep host blood flowing while feeding.
- *Feature a clitellum*, a prominent glandular ring near the head of earthworms and leeches that secretes a sticky cocoon to protect fertilized eggs.
- *Maintain a closed circulatory system*, utilizing a system of blood vessels and five pairs of muscular loop vessels (aortic arches) that act as hearts.

## connections

- [[mollusca]] — fellow lophotrochozoans that share a similar larval stage (trochophore) but lack segmented bodies.
- [[nematoda]] — roundworms, which are unsegmented pseudocoelomates.
- [[platyhelminthes]] — flatworms, which are unsegmented acoelomates.
