## summary

**Bosons** are particles with integer spin (0, 1, 2, …) that obey *Bose-Einstein statistics*, allowing multiple identical particles to occupy the same quantum state. The Standard Model includes fundamental *gauge bosons* (photons, W/Z bosons, gluons) that mediate forces and the *Higgs boson* that generates mass. Composite bosons like *pions* and *mesons* emerge from quark interactions.

## you gotta know

- Integer spin: 0, 1, 2, … (includes zero)
- Obey *Bose-Einstein statistics*—symmetric under particle exchange
- No *Pauli exclusion principle*; unlimited particles in one state
- Fundamental bosons: *gauge bosons* (force mediators) and the *Higgs*
- Composite bosons: *mesons*, *glueballs*, Bose-Einstein condensates
- *Photons* are massless spin-1 bosons; *W/Z* are massive weak force carriers
- *Gluons* mediate the strong force; *Higgs* generates rest mass

## context

Bosons are the universe's force-carrying mediators. The photon—a massless boson—is responsible for electromagnetism and makes light itself. The discovery of massive *gauge bosons* validated the electroweak unification, while the *Higgs boson*, discovered in 2012, confirmed the mechanism by which particles acquire mass. Bose-Einstein condensation demonstrates quantum coherence at macroscopic scales.

## connections

- [[fermion]] — complementary to bosons; carry half-integer spin
