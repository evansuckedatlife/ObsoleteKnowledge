# Enceladus

## summary

**Enceladus** is a small moon of Saturn famous for its dramatic water-ice geysers that erupt from its south polar region. Beneath its icy crust lies a subsurface liquid-water ocean in contact with a rocky core, making Enceladus one of the most promising candidates for detecting extraterrestrial microbial life. The *Cassini* spacecraft discovered the geysers in 2005, revolutionizing understanding of Saturn's moon system.

## you gotta know

- Diameter 504 km; small compared to Titan and Ganymede but geologically spectacular.
- *Water-ice geysers*: erupt from the south pole at ~1.9 km/s, injecting 200 kg of water per second into space.
- *Subsurface ocean*: 10–40 km of liquid water beneath the icy crust; global in extent, in contact with rocky core.
- Ocean chemistry includes organic compounds and chemical energy sources supportive of microbial life.
- Discovered by *William Herschel* in 1789; geysers discovered by *Cassini* spacecraft in 2005.
- *Tidal heating*: resonance with Saturn's moon Dione drives internal friction and heat.
- Smooth, young terrain indicates recent resurfacing; oldest surfaces are heavily cratered.
