# Mach number

## summary

**Mach number** is a dimensionless ratio expressing the speed of an object relative to the speed of sound in the surrounding medium. Mach 1 equals the local speed of sound; Mach 2 is twice the speed of sound. It is the standard measure of extreme velocity in aeronautics, rocketry, and fluid dynamics, especially when compressibility effects become significant.

## you gotta know

- Named after physicist and philosopher *Ernst Mach*, who studied shock waves and the physics of fast-moving objects in the 19th century.
- Mach 1 is not a fixed speed: it depends on temperature and medium. At sea level and 15 °C, Mach 1 ≈ 343 m/s (761 mph) in air; at higher altitudes where air is colder, the speed of sound is lower.
- *Subsonic* flight is below Mach 1; *transonic* spans roughly Mach 0.8 to 1.2 (mixing subsonic and supersonic flow regions); *supersonic* is Mach 1 to about 5; *hypersonic* is Mach 5 and above.
- The scale is *dimensionless*, derived from the ratio v/a, where v is object velocity and a is the speed of sound.
- Compressibility effects—shock waves, pressure changes, heat generation—become dominant above Mach 1 and increase dramatically in the hypersonic regime.
- Used in aircraft design, ballistics, and testing because aerodynamic forces change dramatically across the Mach transition.
- Not logarithmic; it is a ratio scale, allowing meaningful multiplication and division.
