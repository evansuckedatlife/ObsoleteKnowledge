# Eris

## summary

**Eris** is a dwarf planet in the outer solar system, slightly more distant and marginally more massive than Pluto. Its discovery in 2005 by Mike Brown's team at Caltech triggered the International Astronomical Union's 2006 reclassification that demoted Pluto and created the dwarf-planet category. Eris orbits in the *scattered disk*, beyond the Kuiper Belt proper, and remains one of the solar system's least-well-understood bodies due to its remoteness and the scarcity of observational data.

## you gotta know

- *Discovered* on 5 January 2005 by Mike Brown, Chad Trujillo, and David Rabinowitz; initially designated a "tenth planet," sparking the IAU reclassification crisis.
- *More massive than Pluto:* precise mass ~27.8 × 10²⁰ kg vs. Pluto's 13 × 10²⁰ kg; Eris's discovery argued that Pluto could not hold unique status, forcing the definition question.
- *Named for the Greek goddess of discord:* fitting, given its role in sparking the planetary redefinition controversy.
- *Extreme orbit:* semi-major axis ~96 AU; perihelion ~38 AU (inside Pluto's), aphelion ~98 AU; orbital eccentricity 0.44, the highest of any known dwarf planet.
- *One known moon:* **Dysnomia** (goddess of lawlessness), discovered in 2005, orbits at ~37,350 km distance; used to refine Eris's mass through orbital mechanics.
- *Reflective surface:* among the brightest known solar-system bodies per unit area; likely covered in frozen methane, nitrogen, or ethane; albedo ~0.96.
- *Scattered-disk resident:* unlike Kuiper Belt bodies in stable orbits, Eris and others were scattered outward by early solar-system gravitational chaos, leaving low-density, high-eccentricity orbits.
