# Deimos

## summary

**Deimos** is the smaller and more distant of Mars's two natural satellites, discovered in 1877 by Asaph Hall. Named after the Greek god of panic and dread, Deimos orbits Mars at a distance of approximately 23,460 km—farther than the synchronous orbit radius—and moves so slowly that it takes nearly three Martian days to orbit the planet.

## you gotta know

- Diameter ~12 km; smallest of the four large Galilean and Martian moons.
- Very low density and porous composition; likely a captured C-type asteroid.
- Orbits Mars every 30.3 hours—slower than Mars rotates; from the Martian surface, Deimos appears nearly stationary.
- *Retrograde* orbital studies suggest it may have been captured from the asteroid belt rather than forming with Mars.
- Surface is smoother and less cratered than Phobos; covered in regolith (dust layer).
- Discovered by *Asaph Hall* in 1877, nine days after his discovery of Phobos.
- Named from the Greek *Δεῖμος* (dread); along with Phobos represents the two aspects of fear.
