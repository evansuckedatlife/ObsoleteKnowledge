# Nitrogen

## summary

Nitrogen is a nonmetallic element that forms a colorless, odorless, tasteless, and relatively inert diatomic gas making up about 78% of Earth's atmosphere. It is an essential element for life, serving as a building block for amino acids, proteins, and nucleic acids. Because atmospheric nitrogen consists of diatomic molecules held together by a strong triple covalent bond, it must undergo biological or industrial nitrogen fixation to be converted into usable chemical forms.

## you gotta know

- *Discovered by Daniel Rutherford* in 1772, who isolated it and called it "noxious air" or "phlogisticated air" because it did not support combustion or respiration.
- *Converted to ammonia biologically by nitrogenase*, a complex iron-sulfur/molybdenum enzyme found in diazotrophic bacteria like *Rhizobium* that live symbiotically in legume root nodules.
- *Fixed industrially via the Haber-Bosch process*, which combines nitrogen gas with hydrogen gas under high temperature and pressure over an iron catalyst to mass-produce ammonia.
- *Acts as a widely used cryogen* in its liquid state, which boils at 77.36 Kelvin and is utilized to freeze biological samples, cool electronics, and perform low-temperature science.
- *Forms nitrous oxide* (N2O), commonly known as "laughing gas," which serves as an anesthetic, a greenhouse gas, and a propellant oxidizer.

## connections

- [[carbon]] — works with nitrogen in the CNO cycle to catalyze hydrogen fusion in massive stars.
- [[oxygen]] — combines with nitrogen in the atmosphere and in various nitrogen oxides (like NO and NO2) which are key environmental pollutants.
- [[hydrogen]] — combines with nitrogen to form ammonia, hydrazine, and ammonium ions.
