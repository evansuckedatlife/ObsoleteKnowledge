# Nematoda

## summary

A highly diverse phylum of unsegmented, cylindrical invertebrates known as roundworms, **Nematoda** are pseudocoelomate protostomes. They are characterized by a tough outer cuticle that they must shed to grow. They play crucial roles as decomposers in soil ecosystems and are widespread as plant and animal parasites.

## you gotta know

- *Are pseudocoelomate*, possessing a body cavity that is only partially lined with tissue derived from the mesoderm.
- *Protected by a thick, non-cellular cuticle* made of collagen, which they must periodically shed (a process called *ecdysis* or molting) to grow.
- Includes the model organism *Caenorhabditis elegans* (C. elegans), a soil-dwelling roundworm whose cellular lineage, genetics, and neural connections have been completely mapped by developmental biologists.
- Includes major human parasites such as *pinworms*, *hookworms*, and the filarial worms that cause elephantiasis and trichinosis.
- Move with a characteristic whip-like, thrashing motion due to having only longitudinal muscles that pull against their pressurized, fluid-filled body cavity.

## connections

- [[platyhelminthes]] — flatworms, which lack a body cavity (acoelomate) and do not shed their outer skins.
- [[annelida]] — segmented worms, which are coelomates with true segments and closed circulatory systems.
- [[arthropoda]] — fellow members of the Ecdysozoa clade that also undergo ecdysis (molting) to grow.
