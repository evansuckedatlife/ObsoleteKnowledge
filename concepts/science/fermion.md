## summary

**Fermions** are particles with half-integer spin (1/2, 3/2, …) that obey *Fermi-Dirac statistics*, including the Pauli exclusion principle. The Standard Model's matter consists of *leptons* and *quarks*, both fundamental fermions, while *baryons* are composite fermionic hadrons. Fermions are the building blocks of matter and distinctly contrast with integer-spin *bosons*.

## you gotta know

- Half-integer spin: 1/2, 3/2, 5/2, … (cannot have zero spin)
- Obey *Fermi-Dirac statistics*—antisymmetric under particle exchange
- *Pauli exclusion principle*: no two identical fermions occupy the same quantum state
- Fundamental fermions: *leptons* (electron, neutrinos) and *quarks* (six flavors)
- Composite fermions: *baryons* (protons, neutrons, hyperons)
- Antiparticles: every fermion has a corresponding *antimatter* partner
- Form matter through degeneracy pressure—electrons in white dwarfs, neutrons in neutron stars

## context

The division between fermions and bosons is among the deepest symmetries in physics, with profound consequences for the structure of matter. Electrons' fermionic nature explains chemical periodicity and atomic stability. The accumulation of non-relativistic fermions resists compression through quantum pressure—a phenomenon that prevents stellar collapse and shapes the universe's end states.

## connections

- [[boson]] — complementary to fermions; carry integer spin
- [[lepton]] — fundamental spin-1/2 fermions without color charge
- [[quark]] — fundamental spin-1/2 fermions with color charge
- [[baryon]] — composite spin-1/2 or spin-3/2 fermions
- [[pauli-exclusion]] — the defining statistical property of fermions
- [[standard-model]] — fermions are matter particles in the SM
- [[antimatter]] — fermionic antiparticles exist in equal measure
