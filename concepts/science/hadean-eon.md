# Hadean Eon

## summary

The **Hadean Eon** spans Earth's first ~500 million years (4.6–4.1 billion years ago), named for the underworld Hades because of its hellish, molten conditions. The planet was still coalescing from cosmic dust, with a magma ocean covering its surface and no solid crust. Life had not begun; the atmosphere was unbreathable hydrogen and helium.

## you gotta know

- Named after *Hades* (the underworld) because the Earth was a hellish inferno of molten rock and cosmic bombardment.
- The *Late Heavy Bombardment* (the final ~200 million years) brought asteroids and comets that may have delivered water and organic molecules.
- Earth's *Moon* formed during this eon, likely from a giant impact with a Mars-sized body (the Giant Impact Hypothesis).
- The *iron core* separated and sank, releasing enough heat to keep the surface molten; this *planetary differentiation* is still ongoing.
- No evidence of life survives from this eon—the temperatures and radiation made it inhospitable.
- The Earth's *magnetic field* may have begun forming by the end of the eon, as the liquid outer core started generating a dynamo.
- The Hadean left almost no rock record; any early crust was recycled or destroyed.
