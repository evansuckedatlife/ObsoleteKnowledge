# Water

## summary

*Water* (chemical formula *H₂O*) is a small, polar molecule composed of two hydrogen atoms bonded covalently to one oxygen atom. Ubiquitous on Earth and essential to all known life, water exists in three states (solid, liquid, gas) at biologically relevant temperatures and serves as the universal solvent for biochemical reactions, thermal buffer, and the medium in which life evolved.

## you gotta know

- Water is *polar*: oxygen is more electronegative than hydrogen, creating a *dipole moment* with oxygen slightly negative and hydrogen slightly positive, enabling water to dissolve ionic compounds and form hydrogen bonds.
- *Hydrogen bonding* between water molecules is responsible for water's unusual properties: high boiling point, high specific heat capacity (thermal inertia), high surface tension, and solid phase (ice) being less dense than liquid.
- Water's role as a *universal solvent* makes it the medium for metabolic pathways, transport of nutrients and waste, and the environment in which proteins fold and enzymes function.
- In *photosynthesis*, water is photolysed (split) in the light-dependent reactions; electrons are extracted and used to form ATP and NADPH, while molecular oxygen is released as the final electron acceptor product.
- *Osmosis*—the movement of water across semipermeable membranes down a water-potential gradient—is critical to cell homeostasis and plant turgor.
- Water's high *specific heat capacity* (4.18 J/g·°C) stabilises temperatures in organisms and on a planetary scale (ocean heat storage).
- *Hydrolysis* reactions use water to break apart larger molecules (proteins, polysaccharides, lipids); *dehydration synthesis* releases water when joining monomers into polymers.

## connections

- [[hydrogen]] — one of water's constituent elements, forming covalent bonds with oxygen.
- [[oxygen]] — water's electron-rich component, providing the polarity essential to water's chemical personality.
- [[photosynthesis]] — water is the electron and hydrogen source in light-dependent photosynthesis.
- [[polarity]] — water's polar nature underpins its solvent and biological properties.
- [[hydrogen-bond]] — interactions between water molecules and between water and other polar molecules.
- [[solvent]] — water's role as the universal dissolving medium for biochemistry.
