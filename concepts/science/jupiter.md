# Jupiter

## summary

*Jupiter* is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the solar system, a gas giant with a mass greater than all other planets combined. Named after the king of Roman gods, it orbits the Sun every 12 Earth years and has long been one of the most prominent objects in the night sky. Its dramatic appearance—dark bands and bright zones, whirling storms, and a distinctive Great Red Spot—makes it a cornerstone of planetary science and a primary target for space exploration.

## you gotta know

- Jupiter is a *gas giant*, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium with no solid surface; its atmosphere displays violent jet streams and dynamic cloud systems.
- The *Great Red Spot* is a storm larger than Earth that has raged for at least 350 years, visible through modest telescopes.
- Jupiter has at least 95 known moons, including the four *Galilean moons*—*Io*, *Europa*, *Ganymede*, and *Callisto*—discovered by [[galileo-galilei]] in 1610.
- *Io* is volcanically active and geologically turbulent; *Europa* harbors a subsurface ocean under its icy crust, making it a prime candidate in the search for extraterrestrial life.
- The planet's *magnetosphere* is the strongest in the solar system, creating hazardous radiation belts.
- Jupiter's rapid rotation (one day lasts ~10 hours) flattens it visibly at the poles, a shape called *oblate spheroid*.
- The planet lies just outside the *asteroid belt*; gravitational interactions with Jupiter are thought to have shaped the asteroid belt's architecture.

## connections

- [[galilean-moons]] — the four major satellites discovered by Galileo, each geologically distinct and scientifically significant.
- [[galileo-galilei]] — observed Jupiter's moons in 1610, providing evidence for heliocentrism.
- [[nasa]] — has conducted major Jupiter missions: Pioneer 10/11, Voyager 1/2, Galileo, Juno, and planned Europa Clipper.
- [[asteroid-belt]] — lies between Mars and Jupiter; the planet's gravity helped shape its structure.
- [[great-red-spot]] — the iconic atmospheric feature, studied for centuries.
- [[io]] — the innermost Galilean moon, famed for its active volcanism.
- [[europa]] — the second Galilean moon, with a potentially habitable subsurface ocean.
