# Apollo 11

## summary

The *first* crewed lunar landing, on July 20, 1969, when *Apollo 11* carried *Neil Armstrong*, *Buzz Aldrin*, and *Michael Collins* to the Moon. Armstrong and Aldrin descended to the lunar surface while Collins orbited above; Armstrong's words—"That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind"—became the century's most iconic utterance.

## you gotta know

- *Neil Armstrong* was the first human to set foot on the Moon, famously saying "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind" in *1969*.
- *NASA's Apollo 11* mission landed on the Moon with *Apollo Lunar Module Eagle*, while command module *Columbia* remained in orbit.
- *Buzz Aldrin* was the second person to walk on the Moon, following Armstrong minutes later.
- *Michael Collins* piloted *Columbia* in lunar orbit and did not land; he spent the mission in the command module alone.
- The mission validated the *lunar orbit rendezvous* technique—undocking the landing module from the main spacecraft—proving the approach for future landings.
- *Armstrong* and *Aldrin* spent about *2 hours 31 minutes* on the surface, collecting 47 pounds of moon rocks.
- The mission launched from *Kennedy Space Center* atop a *Saturn V* rocket.
- It was completed just *before the decade's end*, fulfilling President *John F. Kennedy's* 1961 pledge to land on the Moon before 1970.
