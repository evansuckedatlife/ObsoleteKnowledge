## summary

The **Cretaceous Period** (145–66 million years ago) saw the final flourishing of dinosaurs alongside the rise of *flowering plants*, which came to dominate the landscape. Ceratopsians and ankylosaurs diversified; *Tyrannosaurus rex* stalked the Late Cretaceous. The period ended abruptly 66 million years ago with an asteroid impact that erased dinosaurs and reshaped Earth's biosphere. The name comes from the chalk (*creta* in Latin) deposits that characterise the rocks.

## you gotta know

- *Flowering plants* (angiosperms) radiated dramatically during the Cretaceous, becoming the dominant flora and providing new food sources for dinosaurs.
- *Ceratopsian dinosaurs* like *Triceratops* evolved elaborate horns and frills, possibly for display and combat.
- *Ankylosaurs*—armored herbivores with bony clubs on their tails—diversified during the Late Cretaceous.
- *Tyrannosaurus rex*, the apex predator of the Late Cretaceous, evolved sophisticated hunting behaviours and sensory abilities.
- The *K-Pg extinction* (66 million years ago)—triggered by a massive *asteroid impact* in what is now Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula—killed ~75% of all species, including all non-avian dinosaurs.
- *Birds* (avian dinosaurs) survived the extinction, as did mammals, which remained small and opportunistic in the shadow of dinosaurs.
