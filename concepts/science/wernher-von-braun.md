# Wernher von Braun

## summary

German-American rocket engineer (1912–1977) who designed the *V-2 ballistic missile* for Nazi Germany and later became the chief architect of the American *Saturn V rocket* that sent humans to the moon. Von Braun was the public face of the Space Age, a visionary who inspired generations to see spaceflight as mankind's destiny.

## you gotta know

- Designed and built the *V-2 rocket* (Vergeltungswaffe 2, "Vengeance Weapon 2") for Nazi Germany during World War II — the first human-made object to reach the edge of space (1942).
- Surrendered to the Americans in 1945 and was spirited away to the United States under *Operation Paperclip*, where he became the director of NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center.
- Led the design of the *Saturn V*, the most powerful rocket ever built, which launched the Apollo missions; the Saturn V remains unmatched in payload capacity.
- Published popular books and appeared on American television (Walt Disney documentaries, 1950s) to promote spaceflight as technically feasible and culturally essential — a master of public persuasion.
- Advocated for a manned *Mars mission* as the next great goal after the moon; his vision of human space colonization influenced NASA planning for decades.
- The V-2's *liquid-fueled engines* and *guidance systems* became the template for all large launch vehicles; von Braun's engineering was meticulous and forward-thinking.
