# Io

## summary

**Io** is the innermost of Jupiter's four large *Galilean moons*, famous for being the most volcanically active body in the solar system. Discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610, Io's violent tidal heating from Jupiter's gravity produces hundreds of active volcanoes and makes it a unique laboratory for understanding volcanism. Its surface is continuously reshaped by lava flows, sulfur geysers, and crustal recycling.

## you gotta know

- Most *volcanically active* body known; eruptions detected at altitudes exceeding 500 km.
- Tidal heating: Jupiter's gravity alternately compresses and releases Io's interior, generating internal friction and heat (more than radioactive decay alone).
- Diameter 3,643 km; fourth-largest moon overall; similar in size and density to Earth's Moon.
- Surface covered in *sulfur compounds*, creating a colorful landscape of yellows, oranges, reds, and blacks.
- Discovered by *Galileo* in 1610; one of the four *Galilean moons*.
- Lacks a permanent atmosphere; sulfur dioxide snow condenses and sublimates seasonally in polar regions.
- *Io-Jupiter plasma torus*: atoms stripped from Io's volcanoes form a doughnut of charged particles around Jupiter.
