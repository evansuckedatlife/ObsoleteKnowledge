# Titan

## summary

**Titan** is Saturn's largest moon and the second-largest moon in the solar system, remarkable for being the only moon with a substantial atmosphere. Its thick nitrogen atmosphere is denser than Earth's, and its surface is shrouded by organic-chemistry clouds and methane rain. Titan has liquid hydrocarbon seas at its poles and is considered one of the most Earth-like worlds, making it a prime candidate for astrobiology research.

## you gotta know

- Diameter 5,150 km; second-largest moon; only moon with a dense atmosphere.
- Atmosphere is mostly *nitrogen* with methane clouds; surface pressure is 1.5× Earth's atmospheric pressure.
- *Methane cycle*: methane rain, seas, and lakes exist at the poles; underground *cryovolcanism* may replenish atmospheric methane.
- Surface is obscured by haze; radar reveals mountains, dunes, and impact craters beneath the clouds.
- Liquid hydrocarbon seas (*Kraken Mare*, *Ligeia Mare*) are reservoirs of liquid methane and ethane.
- Discovered by *Christiaan Huygens* in 1655; first moon discovered around Saturn.
- *Organic chemistry*: the atmosphere may produce tholins (complex organic molecules) analogous to early Earth.
