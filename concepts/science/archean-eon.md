# Archean Eon

## summary

The **Archean Eon** (4.1–2.5 billion years ago) witnessed the formation of Earth's first stable continents and the emergence of life. Oceans covered most of the planet, and the atmosphere was still anoxic (oxygen-free), but *prokaryotes*—simple single-celled organisms—thrived in the water. The name comes from Greek *archaios* (ancient).

## you gotta know

- The first *solid continental crust* formed and began to accumulate into small landmasses called *cratons* (the cores of modern continents).
- *Stromatolites*—layered structures built by cyanobacteria—are the oldest known fossils and appeared in the late Archean.
- The earliest evidence of *life* (chemical signatures in rocks) dates to ~3.8–3.5 billion years ago, making the Archean the *origin of life* on Earth.
- *Photosynthesis* was already happening by the mid-Archean, though it took another billion years for oxygen to accumulate in the atmosphere (*Great Oxidation Event*).
- The planet was still hot; impacts from space objects continued, but much less frequently than in the Hadean.
- Archean rocks are rare at the surface because they have been metamorphosed (altered by heat and pressure) over billions of years.
