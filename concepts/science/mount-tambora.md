# Mount Tambora

## summary

An active stratovolcano on the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia, **Mount Tambora** is the site of the largest volcanic eruption in recorded human history. The April 1815 explosion had a Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) of 7 and injected vast amounts of volcanic ash and sulfur into the stratosphere. This caused severe global climate disruptions, leading to the infamous "Year Without a Summer" in 1816.

## you gotta know

- *Erupted in April 1815*, which remains the largest and most destructive volcanic eruption in recorded history, ejecting an estimated 160 cubic kilometers of material and killing over 70,000 people directly and through subsequent famine.
- *Caused the "Year Without a Summer" in 1816*, as Stratospheric aerosol clouds blocked sunlight, causing widespread crop failures, food riots, and unseasonable summer snowstorms in New England and Western Europe.
- *Inspired the writing of Frankenstein*, which Mary Shelley drafted during the cold, wet, and gloomy summer of 1816 while vacationing with Lord Byron at Lake Geneva.
- *Completely decimated the indigenous Tambora culture*, wiping out a small agricultural kingdom on Sumbawa and causing the extinction of its unique, non-Austronesian language.
- *Left a massive summit caldera* measuring over four miles wide and 3,600 feet deep, which reduced the mountain's height from approximately 14,000 feet to 9,350 feet.

## connections

- [[krakatoa]] — another Indonesian stratovolcano whose 1883 eruption was also famous for its explosive force and global effects.
- [[mount-pinatubo]] — a modern Philippine volcano that caused a similar, though less severe, global temperature drop in 1991.
