## summary

A symbolic crewed space mission flown in July 1975 by the *United States* and *Soviet Union*, in which an *Apollo* command module and a *Soyuz* spacecraft docked in orbit and exchanged crew members. The *Apollo-Soyuz Test Project* (ASTP) was the first joint spaceflight between the two superpowers and signaled a thaw in Cold War tensions. The crews spent two days in orbit together, conducting joint experiments and establishing a protocol for future international space cooperation.

## you gotta know

- The *Apollo-Soyuz Test Project* launched on *July 15, 1975*, with *Apollo* carrying astronauts *Tom Stafford*, *Vance Brand*, and *Deke Slayton*, and *Soyuz* carrying cosmonauts *Alexei Leonov* and *Oleg Makarov*.
- The two spacecraft *docked* in orbit above Earth on *July 17, 1975*, marking the first time American and Soviet crews met in space.
- The American crew spent time aboard the Soviet *Soyuz*, and the Soviet cosmonauts visited the American *Apollo*—the first direct exchange of personnel between the two programs.
- *Deke Slayton*, a *Mercury* astronaut whose career was interrupted by a heart condition, finally flew to space as part of this mission.
- The mission used a special *docking module* to enable the two spacecraft to dock, because *Apollo* used pure *oxygen* at lower pressure while *Soyuz* used an *air mixture* at higher pressure.
- The joint mission demonstrated that the *United States* and *Soviet Union* could cooperate in space despite Cold War tensions, paving the way for future international partnerships like the *International Space Station*.
- The flight lasted *nine days* total, with *two days* spent docked together.
