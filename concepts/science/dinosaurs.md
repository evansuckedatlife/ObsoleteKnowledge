# Dinosaurs

## summary

*Dinosaurs* were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated terrestrial ecosystems for approximately 165 million years, from the Middle Triassic through the end of the Cretaceous Period (~66 million years ago). Ranging from pigeon-sized theropods to the largest land animals ever known (sauropods), dinosaurs exhibited extraordinary morphological and behavioural variety and left a legacy as one of Earth's most successful vertebrate radiations.

## you gotta know

- Dinosaurs are divided into two major orders by hip structure: *Saurischia* (lizard-hipped; includes theropods and sauropods) and *Ornithischia* (bird-hipped; herbivorous, often ornate).
- *Theropods* were bipedal carnivores; *Tyrannosaurus rex* is the archetypical apex predator, but small feathered theropods like *Deinonychus* and *Velociraptor* were sophisticated hunters.
- *Sauropods* were the largest land animals: *Brachiosaurus*, *Diplodocus*, and *Apatosaurus* were herbivorous quadrupeds with long necks and tails, reaching lengths of 100+ feet.
- *Ornithischians* were exclusively herbivorous; *Triceratops* had a bony frill and horns for defence and display, while *Stegosaurus* had dorsal plates and a spiked tail.
- Dinosaurs were not ectothermic "cold-blooded" reptiles; evidence from growth rates, bone structure, and ecological dominance suggests many were mesothermic or endothermic.
- Modern birds are considered avian dinosaurs—the only dinosaur lineage to survive the *Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event* 66 million years ago, which killed non-avian dinosaurs.
- Fossil trackways, nesting sites, and growth rings indicate complex social behaviour, parental care, and possible gregariousness in many groups.
