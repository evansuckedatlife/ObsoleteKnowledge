## summary

The first crewed spaceflight, launched by the *Soviet Union* on April 12, 1961, with *Yuri Gagarin* aboard. Vostok 1 completed one orbit of Earth in about 108 minutes, proving that humans could survive in space and ushering in the age of human spaceflight. Gagarin became an instant international hero.

## you gotta know

- *Yuri Gagarin* became the first human in space aboard *Vostok 1* on *April 12, 1961*.
- The mission lasted *108 minutes* and completed *one complete orbit* of Earth.
- *Gagarin* ejected from the capsule and parachuted back to Earth separately—a fact kept secret by the *Soviet Union* for years because the rules of competition required the cosmonaut to land *in* the spacecraft.
- The *Soviet Union* designed and launched *Vostok*, giving them a decisive early victory in the *Space Race*.
- *Vostok 1* was fully automated; *Gagarin* monitored systems but the flight was controlled from the ground.
- The spacecraft had a *re-entry capsule* that Gagarin sat atop, exposing him to extreme heat and G-forces during descent.
- *Vostok 6* (mentioned as an alias) followed as the last and final piloted Vostok mission in *1963*, carrying *Valentina Tereshkova*, the first woman in space.
