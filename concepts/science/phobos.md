# Phobos

## summary

**Phobos** is the larger of Mars's two moons, orbiting much closer to the planet than any other known moon in the solar system. It was discovered in 1877 by American astronomer Asaph Hall and is named after the Greek god of fear. Phobos is slowly spiraling toward Mars and will eventually be torn apart by *tidal forces*.

## you gotta know

- Only ~22 km across; extremely small—smaller than the asteroid Apophis.
- Orbits Mars every 7.66 hours, faster than Mars rotates; from the Martian surface, Phobos rises in the west and sets in the east.
- *Tidal disruption*: in roughly 30–50 million years, Mars's gravity will shred Phobos into a ring system.
- Discovered by *Asaph Hall* in 1877 using the U.S. Naval Observatory telescope; named after the Greek god of panic.
- Heavily cratered, irregular potato-shaped body; largest crater *Stickney* is 10 km across.
- *Synchronously tidally locked*: always shows the same face to Mars.
- Density suggests origin as a captured asteroid rather than co-accretion with Mars.
