# Cambrian Period

## summary

The **Cambrian Period** (541–485 million years ago) marks the beginning of the Paleozoic Era and the *Cambrian Explosion*—a burst of animal diversification in which most major phyla appeared suddenly in the fossil record. The period is named after Cambria, the Latin name for Wales, where the rocks were first studied. Oceans teemed with life while land remained barren.

## you gotta know

- The *Cambrian Explosion* saw the rapid emergence of arthropods, mollusks, echinoderms, chordates, and other phyla in a geological blink—a pattern that puzzled Darwin.
- *Trilobites* dominated the oceans, armored arthropods with three lobes that would persist for nearly 300 million years.
- *Anomalocaris* was a fearsome apex predator of Cambrian seas, up to a metre long, with a circular mouth and grasping appendages.
- Most animals lacked hard shells at first; the *Burgess Shale* fauna (in modern Canada) preserves soft-bodied creatures in extraordinary detail.
- *Oxygen levels* rose during the Cambrian, enabling larger bodies and more active metabolisms than earlier life could support.
- The continents were still scattered; most life was marine, and the land was bare rock and early soil with no plants yet.
