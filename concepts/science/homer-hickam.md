# Homer Hickam

## summary

American author, engineer, and former NASA employee (1949–present) who is best known for his memoir *October Sky* (1999), which tells the true story of his high school rocket club in 1950s West Virginia and his path to becoming an aerospace engineer. Hickam represents the democratization of spaceflight aspiration — the engineers who were inspired by Sputnik and the Space Race and built their own rockets from scrap materials.

## you gotta know

- Founded the *Coalwood High School Rocket Club* (called the *Big Creek Missile Agency* or BCMA) in Coalwood, West Virginia, in the late 1950s, where he and his friends designed and tested solid-fueled rockets from improvised materials.
- His memoir *October Sky* (1999) was adapted into a film (1999) starring Jake Gyllenhaal, which became a cultural touchstone for the Space Age and inspired a generation of young engineers.
- Worked as an engineer for NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center and later as a payload officer for the Space Shuttle, contributing to the practical continuity of the space program.
- Represents the *grassroots rocket movement* of the Space Age — the amateur and student builders who proved that spaceflight was not the exclusive domain of governments and corporate giants.
- His story captures the cultural moment when the Soviet Sputnik shocked America and when spaceflight became an accessible dream for working-class teenagers in coal-mining towns.
- Later became a prolific author, writing dozens of books on space history, rockets, and the personal experience of participating in the Space Age.
