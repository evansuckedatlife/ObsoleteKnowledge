# Gauge boson

## summary

**Gauge bosons** (also *vector bosons*) are spin-1 *bosons* that mediate fundamental forces through gauge symmetry principles. The *photon* carries electromagnetism, the *W* and *Z* bosons mediate the *weak force*, and *gluons* mediate the *strong force*. Their masslessness or mass is linked to their respective gauge symmetries—broken symmetry can generate mass, as in the electroweak theory.

## you gotta know

- Spin-1 particles; appear as force mediators in the Standard Model
- *Photon*: massless, couples to electric charge, infinite range
- *Gluons*: eight massless carriers of color charge, strong force
- *W bosons*: massive (80 GeV), mediate charged-current weak decays
- *Z boson*: massive (91 GeV), mediates neutral-current weak interactions
- Gauge symmetry determines interaction properties: unbroken symmetry → massless; broken → massive
- Couple with strength determined by coupling constants (fine-structure constant for photon, strong coupling for gluons)

## context

Gauge bosons are the foundation of the Standard Model's force framework. The unification of electromagnetism and weak force through *electroweak symmetry* predicted the massive *W* and *Z* bosons decades before their discovery. The theory's predictive power—masses, decay widths, coupling strengths—was spectacularly confirmed, validating the gauge principle as a guiding law of nature.
