# Triton

## summary

**Triton** is Neptune's largest moon, famous for orbiting *retrograde*—backward relative to Neptune's rotation. This unusual orbit, combined with evidence of past geological activity and an icy, nitrogen-dominated surface, suggests that Triton was captured from the Kuiper Belt early in the solar system's history. It is one of the coldest places in the solar system, with a surface temperature around −235 °C.

## you gotta know

- Diameter 2,707 km; largest moon of Neptune; only major moon with retrograde orbit.
- *Retrograde orbit*: circles Neptune backward, indicating it was captured rather than formed with Neptune.
- *Cryovolcanism*: nitrogen ice geysers erupt from the surface, driven by solar heating of subsurface nitrogen ice.
- Surface temperature ~−235 °C; among the coldest known worlds.
- Likely *captured from the Kuiper Belt* based on its retrograde orbit and density similar to Pluto.
- Thin atmosphere of nitrogen detected by *Voyager 2* in 1989.
- Massive cratering and rifting suggests a geologically complex past; currently may be spiraling toward Neptune.
