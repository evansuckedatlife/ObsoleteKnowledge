## summary

**Partons** are the generic name for *quarks* and *gluons* that constitute *hadrons*, used when their exact identity or behavior within high-momentum-transfer interactions is being considered. The parton model, developed to explain *deep inelastic scattering*, treats hadrons as collections of point-like constituents that carry fractions of the hadron's momentum. Parton distribution functions quantify the probability of finding each parton type at a given momentum fraction.

## you gotta know

- Shorthand term for hadron constituents: *quarks* (valence and sea) and *gluons*
- Discovered operationally through deep inelastic scattering experiments
- Parton distribution function (PDF): f(x, Q²) describes momentum fraction x carried by a parton at scale Q²
- Valence partons: quarks that define hadron quantum numbers (proton: two up, one down)
- Sea partons: transient quark-antiquark pairs and gluons generated by quantum fluctuations
- Momentum sum rule: all partons collectively carry 100% of hadron momentum
- Gluon splitting dominates at small x (< 0.01); valence dominates at large x (> 0.1)

## context

The parton model emerged from *Bjorken scaling*—the surprising observation that deep inelastic scattering rates depend only on a dimensionless ratio. This revealed hadrons' internal compositeness and vindicated the quark model quantitatively. Modern PDFs, refined from thousands of experiments, are essential for predicting cross-sections at the Large Hadron Collider and remain central to precision tests of the Standard Model.
