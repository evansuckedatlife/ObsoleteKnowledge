# Arthropoda

## summary

Accounting for over 80% of all described animal species, **Arthropoda** is the largest and most ecologically diverse phylum on Earth. They are coelomate protostomes characterized by a rigid exoskeleton composed of chitin, segmented bodies, and jointed appendages. Occupying virtually every environment on Earth, the phylum includes insects, spiders and other arachnids, crustaceans, centipedes, and millipedes.

## you gotta know

### anatomy & physiology

- *Possess a rigid exoskeleton made of chitin* (often reinforced with calcium carbonate in crustaceans), which protects the body and provides muscle attachment sites but must be periodically shed via *ecdysis* (molting) to allow growth.
- *Characterized by jointed appendages*, which are highly adapted for locomotion, feeding, sensory reception, and defense.
- *Possess segmented bodies* that are grouped into functional regions called *tagmata* (such as the head, thorax, and abdomen in insects, or the cephalothorax and abdomen in arachnids).
- *Excrete nitrogenous wastes using Malpighian tubules*, branching tubules extending from the gut that absorb solutes and waste from the hemolymph, allowing extreme water conservation.
- *Utilize an open circulatory system* in which a dorsal vessel pumps hemolymph into a primary body cavity (the hemocoel), bathing internal organs directly.

### classification & fossil record

- Divided into four major living subphyla: *Hexapoda* (insects), *Chelicerata* (spiders, scorpions, horseshoe crabs), *Crustacea* (crabs, lobsters, krill), and *Myriapoda* (centipedes and millipedes).
- Includes the extinct class *Trilobita* (trilobites), which were dominant marine organisms during the Paleozoic era and serve as key index fossils for geologists.

## connections

- [[mollusca]] — the second-largest invertebrate phylum, which also utilizes an open circulatory system.
- [[chordata]] — the dominant deuterostome phylum with which arthropods compete for ecological dominance.
- [[nematoda]] — fellow ecdysozoans that share the trait of molting a tough outer cuticle.

## context

Arthropods are the undisputed champions of animal diversity and colonization of land. Their development of flight (in insects) and complex social behaviors (in ants, bees, and termites) represent major evolutionary milestones. In the quiz bowl canon, Arthropoda is heavily tested on its physiological features (chitin, ecdysis, Malpighian tubules, book lungs, spiracles) and its taxonomic subphyla (especially chelicerae versus mandibles).
