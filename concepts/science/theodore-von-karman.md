# Theodore von Kármán

## summary

Hungarian-American aerodynamicist and physicist (1881–1963) who pioneered the theory of *transonic and supersonic flow*, *turbulence*, and *rocket propulsion*. Though primarily a theorist, Kármán was the intellectual godfather of the *Jet Propulsion Laboratory* and shaped American military and civilian rocket programs through mentorship and scientific authority.

## you gotta know

- Founder and director of the *Jet Propulsion Laboratory* (JPL) at Caltech — the premier American rocket research facility, which became NASA's center for planetary exploration.
- Developed rigorous mathematical theories of *aerodynamic drag*, *shock waves*, and *hypersonic flow*, providing the theoretical underpinning for rocket and missile design.
- Mentored and collaborated with *Wernher von Braun*, *Frank Malina*, and a generation of American rocket engineers, lending scientific credibility to spaceflight when it was derided as fantasy.
- Advised the U.S. military on *ballistic missile* and *rocket propulsion* programs; his authority gave rocketry legitimacy in American defense circles.
- Published seminal papers on *turbulence theory* and *nonlinear fluid dynamics* that remain foundational to aerospace engineering.
- Lived through two World Wars and emigrated from Europe to America, becoming a symbol of European scientific genius enriching American innovation.
