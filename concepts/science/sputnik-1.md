# Sputnik 1

## summary

The first artificial satellite, launched by the *Soviet Union* on October 4, 1957, marking the beginning of the Space Age and the Space Race. *Sputnik 1* was a polished metal sphere about 58 centimetres in diameter, orbiting Earth every 96 minutes and transmitting simple radio signals. Its launch shocked the West and galvanized American efforts in space and science education.

## you gotta know

- *Sputnik 1* was launched by the *Soviet Union* on *October 4, 1957*, becoming the *first artificial object to orbit Earth*.
- The satellite was a *polished metal sphere* approximately *58 centimetres* (23 inches) in diameter with four external radio antennas.
- *Sputnik 1* orbited Earth every *96 minutes* and transmitted simple *radio beeps* on two frequencies, which radio operators worldwide could detect.
- The launch shocked *American* leadership and the public, sparking the Space Race and intensifying Cold War anxieties about Soviet technological superiority.
- The name *Sputnik* means "*traveling companion*" in Russian; the full name was *Sputnik Zemlya* ("Earth Satellite").
- The satellite lasted *three weeks* before its batteries were depleted, though it remained in orbit for *three months* before burning up on re-entry.
- *Sputnik 1's* success prompted the creation of *NASA* in *1958* and shifted American education and science funding dramatically.
