## summary

A *comet* is an icy body orbiting the Sun, composed of frozen volatiles (water ice, methane, ammonia, carbon dioxide) and dust. When a comet's elliptical orbit brings it close to the Sun, solar heating causes its ices to sublimate, forming a luminous *coma* (halo of gas and dust) and a spectacular *tail* that can stretch millions of kilometers. Comets have been observed and interpreted as omens for millennia; modern astronomy reveals them as preserved relics of the early solar system and crucial sources of information about its formation.

## you gotta know

- Comets originate from two reservoirs: the *Kuiper Belt* (source of short-period comets with orbital periods under 200 years) and the *Oort Cloud* (source of long-period and hyperbolic comets from the outer solar system).
- A comet consists of a solid *nucleus* (typically a few kilometers across), a *coma* of dust and gas surrounding it, and a *tail* always pointing away from the Sun due to solar wind and radiation pressure.
- *Halley's Comet*, the most famous, returns every ~76 years; it was observed and recorded by ancient civilizations and its next appearance is predicted for 2061.
- Comets deposit dusty trails in their orbits; when Earth passes through these trails, the particles burn up in the atmosphere, creating *meteor showers*.
- *Comet Shoemaker-Levy 9* collided with Jupiter in 1994, leaving visible impact scars and providing direct evidence of catastrophic bombardment in the solar system.
- The European Space Agency's *Rosetta* mission orbited *Comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko* and landed the *Philae* probe on its nucleus, revealing surprising geological complexity.
- Historical comets were often regarded as harbingers of disaster or divine signs; their predictability (once orbital mechanics advanced) eliminated superstition.
