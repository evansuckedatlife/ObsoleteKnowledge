# Permian Period

## summary

The **Permian Period** (299–252 million years ago) saw *synapsids* rule the land while Pangaea reached its full assembly. It ended catastrophically with the *Great Dying*—the worst mass extinction in Earth's history, erasing ~96% of marine species and fundamentally reshaping life. The period is named after the Perm region in Russia.

## you gotta know

- *Synapsids* (including *Dimetrodon* and *Therapsids*) dominated terrestrial ecosystems, competing with early sauropsid reptiles for ecological niches.
- The *supercontinent Pangaea* was fully assembled, creating vast interior deserts and extreme climates; monsoons raged along the coasts.
- *Reptiles* diversified, including the first truly large predators and the ancestors of dinosaurs (*sauropsids*).
- The *Permian extinction* (~252 million years ago)—the *Great Dying*—was catastrophic, possibly triggered by massive *volcanic eruptions* in Siberia, ocean anoxia, and climate collapse.
- ~96% of marine species and ~70% of terrestrial vertebrate families vanished, the only extinction that threatened all complex life.
- The *Therapsids*, which had dominated the Permian, were nearly wiped out and would cede dominance to reptiles in the Triassic.
