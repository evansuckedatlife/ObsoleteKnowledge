# Platyhelminthes

## summary

A phylum of bilaterally symmetrical, unsegmented invertebrates known as flatworms, **Platyhelminthes** are acoelomate triploblasts. Lacking specialized respiratory and circulatory systems, they rely on diffusion across their flattened bodies for gas exchange and nutrient transport. The phylum includes both free-living planarians and major human parasites.

## you gotta know

- *Are acoelomate*, meaning they completely lack a fluid-filled body cavity (coelom) between their digestive tract and outer body wall.
- *Possess flame cells* (protonephridia), specialized excretory cells lined with cilia that function in osmoregulation and waste removal.
- Includes *planarians*, free-living freshwater flatworms famous for their extraordinary capacity to regenerate complete bodies from tiny tissue fragments.
- Includes major internal parasites such as *tapeworms* (cestodes), which use a hooked *scolex* on their head to attach to host intestines, and *flukes* (trematodes).
- Must maintain a highly flattened body shape to allow oxygen and carbon dioxide to diffuse directly through their tissues, as they have no lungs or blood vessels.

## connections

- [[nematoda]] — roundworms, which are pseudocoelomates and represent a more advanced worm body plan.
- [[annelida]] — segmented worms, which are coelomates and possess a closed circulatory system.
