## summary

An English theoretical physicist, **Paul Dirac** made fundamental contributions to the early development of both quantum mechanics and quantum electrodynamics. He successfully unified quantum mechanics with special relativity through his famous wave equation, which predicted the existence of antimatter. He was awarded the 1933 Nobel Prize in Physics alongside Erwin Schrödinger.

## you gotta know

- *Formulated the Dirac equation* in 1928, a relativistic wave equation for the electron that successfully predicted the existence of the positron (the electron's antimatter counterpart) and naturally explained electron spin.
- *Co-developed Fermi-Dirac statistics* with Enrico Fermi, which mathematically describe the behavior of assemblies of identical particles with half-integer spin (fermions).
- *Introduced bra-ket notation* (such as $\langle \phi | \psi \rangle$), a standard mathematical notation for representing quantum states and inner products in Hilbert space.
- *Proposed the Dirac delta function* ($\delta(x)$), a generalized function (or distribution) that is zero everywhere except at zero, and integrates to one, used to model point-like densities.
- *Hypothesized the existence of magnetic monopoles* in 1931, showing that the existence of a single magnetic monopole would explain the quantization of electric charge.
- *Wrote the classic 1930 textbook The Principles of Quantum Mechanics*, which served as the definitive mathematical guide for generations of quantum physicists.

## connections

- [[werner-heisenberg]] — his close contemporary, whose matrix mechanics Dirac reformulate using his own operator algebra.
- [[erwin-schrodinger]] — with whom he shared the 1933 Nobel Prize and whose non-relativistic wave equation Dirac modified to be Lorentz invariant.
- [[enrico-fermi]] — with whom Dirac co-developed the statistical mechanics governing fermions.
