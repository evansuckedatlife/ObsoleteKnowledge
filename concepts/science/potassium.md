# Potassium

## summary

*Potassium* (atomic number 19, symbol *K*) is a soft, reactive alkali metal and an essential macronutrient for all living organisms. Organisms maintain high intracellular potassium and low extracellular potassium via active transport; this electrochemical gradient drives nerve impulses, muscle contraction, and osmotic regulation, making potassium fundamental to animal and plant physiology.

## you gotta know

- Potassium is the *eighth most abundant element* in the Earth's crust and is highly soluble in water as a *monovalent cation* (*K⁺*).
- The *sodium-potassium pump* (*Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase*) actively transports three Na⁺ ions out and two K⁺ ions in, consuming ATP; this gradient is the electrochemical basis of all excitable cells.
- *Resting membrane potential* in neurons (~−70 mV) arises from the K⁺ gradient and the relative permeability of K⁺ and Na⁺ channels; action potentials depend critically on K⁺ efflux repolarising the membrane.
- In *skeletal muscle*, potassium release during contraction can raise extracellular K⁺ transiently; severe hyperkalaemia (excess K⁺) can cause cardiac arrhythmias or cardiac arrest.
- Potassium is an essential macronutrient for plants; it controls stomatal opening (via guard cell turgor), maintains cell osmotic potential, and activates numerous enzymes in photosynthesis and carbohydrate metabolism.
- Dietary potassium from fruit, vegetables, and nuts is critical in humans; deficiency (hypokalaemia) causes muscle weakness, arrhythmias, and impaired glucose metabolism.
- Potassium has a single stable isotope (⁴⁰K is radioactive and contributes slightly to natural background radiation in the human body).
