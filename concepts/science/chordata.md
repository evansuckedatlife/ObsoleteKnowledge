## summary

Comprising all vertebrates (mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish) alongside two invertebrate subphyla, **Chordata** is a major phylum of deuterostome animals. Chordates are defined by the presence of a notochord, a dorsal hollow nerve cord, pharyngeal slits, and a post-anal tail at some stage of their development. This phylum contains the most complex and familiar animals, including humans.

## you gotta know

### diagnostic characteristics

- *Defined by the presence of a notochord*, a flexible, rod-like structure extending along the back that provides skeletal support and is replaced by the vertebral column in most adult vertebrates.
- *Possess a dorsal hollow nerve cord*, a tube of nervous tissue located above the notochord that develops into the brain and spinal cord in vertebrates.
- *Have pharyngeal slits* (or pouches) during embryonic development, which evolve into gill arches in fish and elements of the jaw, inner ear, and neck in terrestrial vertebrates.
- *Possess a muscular, post-anal tail* extending past the anus, which contains skeletal elements and muscles and is used for balance or propulsion, though it is reduced or lost in some adult forms.
- *Feature an endostyle* (or its homologous descendant, the thyroid gland) on the floor of the pharynx, which secretes mucus to trap food particles in filter-feeding invertebrate chordates.

### subphyla

- Divided into three subphyla: *Vertebrata* (vertebrates with backbones), *Urochordata* (tunicates or sea squirts, which lose their notochord in adulthood), and *Cephalochordata* (lancelets, which retain all chordate features throughout life).

## connections

- [[echinodermata]] — the closest major invertebrate relative of chordates, sharing a deuterostome pattern of embryonic development.
- [[arthropoda]] — the dominant protostome phylum with which chordates compete across terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems.
- [[peter]] — as a human, Peter is a member of the subphylum Vertebrata within Chordata.

## context

Phylum Chordata represents the lineage that produced the most complex nervous systems and skeletons in the animal kingdom, culminating in vertebrates. In the quiz bowl canon, Chordata is a frequent answer tested on the four classic anatomical structures that define the phylum (notochord, dorsal hollow nerve cord, pharyngeal slits, post-anal tail) as well as its invertebrate subphyla (such as the tunic of tunicates, which is uniquely made of cellulose-like tunicin).
