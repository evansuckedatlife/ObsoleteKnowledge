# Ivan Pavlov

## summary

Russian physiologist celebrated for his experiments on conditional reflexes, particularly the *conditioned response* demonstrated through his famous dogs. His work on how animals learn through stimulus-response pairing became foundational to behavioural psychology and earned him the Nobel Prize in 1904. He spent most of his career at the Military Medical Academy in St. Petersburg.

## you gotta know

- Discovered the *conditioned reflex* through dogs that learned to salivate at the sound of a bell (paired with food presentation), establishing that behavioural responses can be trained.
- Originally studied circulation and digestion; his Nobel Prize recognized his work on the physiology of digestion, not his famous conditioning experiments.
- Developed objective, measurable methods for studying nervous system function through animal behaviour — a scientific revolution in psychology.
- Applied his theories to psychiatric disorders, proposing that neuroses resulted from imbalanced nervous processes.
- Later work explored higher-order conditioning and the distinction between excitation and inhibition in the nervous system.
- Remained a Soviet icon after the revolution; Stalin's regime endorsed his theories as compatible with materialist philosophy.
