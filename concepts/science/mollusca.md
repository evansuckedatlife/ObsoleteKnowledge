# Mollusca

## summary

Comprising soft-bodied animals such as snails, clams, squids, and octopuses, **Mollusca** is the second-largest phylum of invertebrates. They are coelomates characterized by a specialized fold of tissue called the mantle, which often secretes a protective shell, a muscular foot for locomotion, and a unique rasping feeding organ called a radula.

## you gotta know

- *Possess a mantle*, a fold of tissue that covers the animal's visceral mass and secretes a calcium carbonate shell in shelled species.
- *Feed using a radula*, a specialized rasping ribbon of tissue lined with microscopic chitinous teeth used to scrape food.
- *Feature a muscular foot*, which is modified for digging in clams, crawling in snails, and forms tentacles and arms in cephalopods.
- Divided into major classes including *Gastropoda* (snails and slugs), *Bivalvia* (clams, oysters, and scallops), and *Cephalopoda* (squids, octopuses, and cuttlefish).
- *Maintain an open circulatory system* (except for cephalopods), in which a heart pumps blood-like hemolymph into a body cavity (hemocoel) to bathe organs directly.

## connections

- [[annelida]] — with which mollusks share a common ciliated marine larval stage called a trochophore.
- [[arthropoda]] — the only invertebrate phylum larger and more diverse than Mollusca.
