# Gluon

## summary

**Gluons** are massless *gauge bosons* that mediate the *strong nuclear force* by coupling to *color charge*. Eight gluons exist—each carrying a color-anticolor pair (e.g., red-antiblue)—and bind *quarks* within *hadrons*. Unlike *photons*, gluons interact with themselves, creating self-coupling phenomena that generate most of the mass in ordinary matter.

## you gotta know

- Spin-1 *gauge bosons*, massless and confined like quarks
- Eight types: three colors × three anticolors minus one singlet (technically SU(3) octet)
- Each gluon carries both *color charge* and *anticolor charge*
- Couple with strength determined by the *strong coupling constant* (αs ≈ 0.12 at typical scales)
- Gluon self-interaction: a gluon can emit or absorb other gluons—unique among force carriers
- Responsible for asymptotic freedom: coupling weakens at high energy, strengthens at low energy
- Running coupling: αs depends on energy scale; this beta function governs hadron phenomenology

## context

Gluons are responsible for roughly 99% of the proton's mass through binding energy, despite quarks contributing only ~1%. Self-coupling of gluons creates non-abelian dynamics unique to *quantum chromodynamics*. This nonlinearity explains *color confinement*—why quarks cannot escape hadrons—and generates the rich spectrum of resonances observed in hadron collisions.
