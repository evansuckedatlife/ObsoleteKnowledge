# Quark

## summary

**Quarks** are *fundamental fermions* that carry *color charge* and are confined within *hadrons* via the *strong nuclear force*. Six flavors exist—*up*, *down*, *charm*, *strange*, *bottom*, *top*—spanning three generations. Quarks possess spin-1/2 and fractional electric charge, and their interaction via *gluons* forms the basis of *quantum chromodynamics*.

## you gotta know

- Six flavors: up, down, charm, strange, bottom, top (grouped in three generations)
- Spin-1/2 *fermions*; obey Pauli exclusion principle
- Fractional charge: up-type quarks carry +2/3 e, down-type carry −1/3 e
- Carry *color charge* (red, green, blue and anticolors) required for strong force interaction
- Cannot exist free; confined within *hadrons* as *partons*
- Antiquarks exist with opposite charge and color
- Lightest (up, down) are the building blocks of ordinary matter

## context

Quarks represent the deepest level of matter structure accessible to experiment. Unlike leptons, quarks are permanently trapped inside composite particles through *color confinement*—a phenomenon unique to the strong force. The discovery of quarks unified seemingly diverse particles (baryons, mesons) under a single framework and explained strange particle decays through quark flavor mixing.

## connections

- [[fermion]] — quarks are spin-1/2 fundamental fermions
- [[hadron]] — all hadrons are bound states of quarks and gluons
- [[baryon]] — hadrons composed of three quarks
- [[meson]] — hadrons composed of a quark-antiquark pair
- [[gluon]] — the mediator of strong force between quarks
- [[quantum-chromodynamics]] — the gauge theory governing quark interactions
- [[parton]] — quarks as constituents within hadrons
