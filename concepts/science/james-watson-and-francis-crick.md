# James Watson and Francis Crick

## summary

American biologist James Watson and British physicist Francis Crick solved the three-dimensional structure of DNA in 1953, revealing the double helix and immediately suggesting the mechanism of hereditary replication. Their model unified genetics and chemistry, founding molecular biology. They shared the 1962 Nobel Prize with Maurice Wilkins, whose X-ray crystallography provided crucial evidence (though Rosalind Franklin, who generated the critical images, had died and could not be nominated).

## you gotta know

- Deduced the *double helix structure* of DNA from X-ray crystallography data (particularly *Photo 51* from Rosalind Franklin) and model-building, published in `Nature` 1953.
- Immediately recognized that the complementary base pairing (A-T and G-C) implied a replication mechanism: each strand serves as a template for a new partner strand.
- Watson brought *Phage Group* molecular biology traditions; Crick brought theoretical physics and deep structural reasoning — their collaboration combined empiricism and abstraction.
- Launched the *central dogma*: DNA → RNA → Protein, the chain of information flow that became the foundation of molecular biology.
- Their model was elegant and correct on the first major attempt — unusual in science and a mark of their insight and luck.
- Later work: Watson established modern molecular genetics; Crick pursued neuroscience and consciousness studies.

## connections

- [[thomas-hunt-morgan]] — predecessors who mapped genes; Watson and Crick revealed the physical molecule genes were made from.
- [[barbara-mcclintock]] — contemporary geneticist; her transposable elements were later explained at the molecular level through DNA structure.
- [[rosalind-franklin]] — X-ray crystallographer whose *Photo 51* was essential to their model; she died before the prize was awarded.
- [[alexander-fleming]] — predecessor; DNA structure later explained antibiotic resistance mechanisms and drug targets at the molecular level.
