# Karl Landsteiner

## summary

Austrian-American pathologist and immunologist who discovered the human ABO blood groups and later the Rh factor, transforming transfusion medicine from a deadly gamble into a safe procedure. He also identified the poliovirus and studied the immunological basis of disease. His discoveries in serology and immunology won him the 1930 Nobel Prize.

## you gotta know

- Discovered the *ABO blood group system* (1901): the landmark finding that human blood types could be classified by antigen-antibody reactions, eliminating transfusion incompatibility deaths.
- Identified *poliovirus* (1909), proving that a filterable infectious agent (too small for light microscopy) could cause disease — validating the viral theory of infection.
- Developed *Landsteiner's rule*: the principle that blood sera contain antibodies to antigens absent on the individual's own red cells.
- Won the Nobel Prize in 1930, one of the last recognitions for classical serology before molecular methods eclipsed it.
- Worked primarily in Vienna and later at the Rockefeller Institute in New York, conducting rigorous immunological experiments.
- Pioneered *precipitation reactions* and *complement fixation*: laboratory methods for detecting and measuring immune responses.

## connections

- [[paul-ehrlich]] — peer in immunology; Ehrlich theorized receptors; Landsteiner experimentally verified immune specificity through antigen-antibody reactions.
