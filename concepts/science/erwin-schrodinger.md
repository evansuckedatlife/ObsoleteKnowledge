# Erwin Schrödinger

## summary

An Austrian-Irish theoretical physicist, **Erwin Schrödinger** developed wave mechanics, a formulation of quantum mechanics based on wave motion. He formulated the fundamental equation of quantum wave mechanics and proposed the famous *Schrödinger's cat* thought experiment. He was awarded the 1933 Nobel Prize in Physics alongside Paul Dirac.

## you gotta know

- *Formulated the Schrödinger equation* in 1925, a partial differential equation that describes how the wave function ($\psi$) of a quantum system changes over time.
- *Proposed the Schrödinger's cat thought experiment* in 1935, illustrating the problem of quantum superposition by imagining a cat in a sealed box that is simultaneously alive and dead until observed.
- *Wrote the influential 1944 book What Is Life?*, which explored genetics from a physicist's perspective and inspired James Watson and Francis Crick to study the structure of DNA.
- *Proved the mathematical equivalence* of his wave mechanics and Werner Heisenberg's matrix mechanics, despite the two theories using completely different mathematical formalisms.
- *Coined the term quantum entanglement* (*Verschränkung*) to describe the correlated states of separated particles, following his analysis of the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen (EPR) paradox.

## connections

- [[albert-einstein]] — who praised Schrödinger's wave mechanics and collaborated with him on criticisms of the Copenhagen interpretation (such as the cat thought experiment).
- [[niels-bohr]] — who hosted Schrödinger in Copenhagen to debate wave mechanics and eventually incorporated his wave equation into the Copenhagen interpretation.
- [[werner-heisenberg]] — whose matrix mechanics Schrödinger criticized as being abstract and difficult to visualize, prompting him to develop his own wave-based theory.
