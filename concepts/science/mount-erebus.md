# Mount Erebus

## summary

Located on Ross Island in Antarctica, **Mount Erebus** is the southernmost active volcano on Earth. It is the second-highest volcano in Antarctica and is famous for its persistent active lava lake and its unique ice towers formed by geothermal steam. Its summit was the tragic site of the 1979 Air New Zealand Flight 901 crash.

## you gotta know

- *Southernmost active volcano on Earth*, situated on Ross Island in Antarctica's Ross Dependency.
- *Contains a persistent, convective lava lake* of rare phonolitic lava, one of only a handful of long-lived lava lakes active on Earth today.
- *Discovered in 1841 by British explorer Sir James Clark Ross*, who named the volcano and its nearby extinct neighbor, Mount Terror, after his expedition's two ships, HMS *Erebus* and HMS *Terror*.
- *Site of the Air New Zealand Flight 901 disaster* on November 28, 1979, in which a sightseeing flight crashed into the mountain's lower slope due to a coordinates error and sector whiteout, killing all 257 passengers and crew.
- *Features unique ice towers (fumaroles)* on its upper slopes, hollow ice chimneys created when warm, sulfurous geothermal steam rises through vents and freezes in the sub-zero Antarctic air.
