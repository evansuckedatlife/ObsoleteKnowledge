## summary

*Bell's inequality* is a mathematical result proving that no *local hidden-variable* theory can reproduce all the predictions of quantum mechanics. Derived by physicist John Bell in 1964, it showed that if reality is determined by hidden variables that travel no faster than light (locality), then statistical correlations in measurements on entangled particles must obey a certain bound. Experiments since the 1980s have consistently violated Bell's inequality, vindicating quantum mechanics and demonstrating that either locality or realism (or both) must be abandoned—a profound shift in our understanding of nature.

## you gotta know

- Bell's original inequality sets a bound on correlation functions for three measurement settings on each of two entangled particles. Any local hidden-variable theory obeys this bound; quantum mechanics can violate it.
- The *CHSH inequality* (Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt, 1969) is a more practical version designed for realistic experiments. Modern Bell tests measure violations of the CHSH inequality to very high statistical significance.
- *Quantum entanglement*: two particles can be prepared in a state where measuring one instantaneously affects the outcomes of measurements on the other, regardless of separation. Bell's theorem shows this non-local correlation cannot be explained by local hidden variables set at the time of entanglement.
- Bell's theorem does not imply instantaneous signaling (no information faster than light can be transmitted); it rules out only a specific class of hidden-variable theories that assume local realism.
- The *loopholes*: early Bell tests suffered potential loopholes (detection loophole, locality loophole, freedom-of-choice loophole). Since 2015, experiments have closed all major loopholes simultaneously, ruling out local hidden-variable theories decisively.
- Key experiments: Aspect et al. (1982) first observed a significant violation; Weihs et al. (1998) closed the locality loophole; Hensen et al. (2015) and Liu et al. (2021) closed multiple loopholes, confirming quantum mechanics's weirdness beyond reasonable doubt.
