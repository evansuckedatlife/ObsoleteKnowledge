# DNA

## summary

*DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid)* is the polymer that stores genetic information in all living organisms and many viruses. It consists of two complementary strands wound in a double helix, each strand a sugar-phosphate backbone with nucleotide bases (adenine, thymine, guanine, cytosine) extending inward. The sequence of these four bases encodes instructions for building and regulating proteins; life as we know it depends entirely on DNA's ability to be accurately replicated, transcribed, and expressed.

## you gotta know

### Structure and Composition
- A polymer of *nucleotides*: each consists of a deoxyribose sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base.
- The two strands are *antiparallel* — one runs 5' to 3', the other 3' to 5' — and held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary bases.
- Base pairing rules: *adenine* pairs with *thymine* (2 hydrogen bonds); *guanine* pairs with *cytosine* (3 hydrogen bonds). This complementarity is the basis of replication and gene regulation.
- The double helix winds so that 10 base pairs complete one full turn; this structure was elucidated by *Watson, Crick, and Franklin* in the early 1950s.

### Function and Replication
- DNA is the *template* for its own replication during cell division. Each strand serves as the template for a new complementary strand, producing two identical daughter molecules via semi-conservative replication.
- DNA is also the template for *transcription*: RNA polymerase reads DNA and synthesizes messenger RNA (mRNA), which carries genetic instructions to the ribosome for protein synthesis.
- The genetic *code* uses sequences of three nucleotides (codons) to specify amino acids; 64 possible codons encode just 20 amino acids, creating redundancy.

### Organization and Regulation
- In prokaryotes, DNA is a single circular chromosome in the nucleoid region; in eukaryotes, DNA is organized into linear chromosomes housed in the nucleus.
- Eukaryotic DNA is packaged with *histone* proteins into *nucleosomes*, and further coiled into *chromatin*, allowing ~2 meters of DNA to fit into a nucleus ~10 micrometers across.
- *Gene expression* is regulated at multiple levels: chromatin remodeling, transcription control, RNA processing, and protein modification. Not all genes are active in every cell.

### Replication and Mutations
- DNA replication is semiconservative: the two original strands separate, and each serves as a template for a new strand, giving two DNA molecules that each contain one old and one new strand.
- *DNA polymerase* catalyzes bond formation with extraordinary fidelity (~1 error per 10^10 bases), though mutations still accumulate over generations and drive evolution.
- *Mutations* — insertions, deletions, substitutions — can alter protein function or regulation; some are neutral, harmful, or beneficial.

## context

DNA is the molecule at the heart of molecular biology. Its discovery and structure revolutionized our understanding of heredity, disease, and evolution. The Human Genome Project's sequencing of all ~3 billion base pairs revealed that humans share ~99.9% of their DNA with each other and significant portions with other animals, grounding evolutionary biology in molecular fact. DNA's central role makes it the focus of modern medicine (gene therapy, personalized genomics), agriculture (crop breeding, GMOs), and forensics (DNA fingerprinting). Understanding DNA is foundational to understanding life itself.
