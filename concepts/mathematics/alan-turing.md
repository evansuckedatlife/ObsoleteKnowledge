# Alan Turing

## summary

**Alan Turing** (1912–1954) was a British mathematician, logician, and computer scientist who formalised the concepts of algorithm and computation. During World War II, he worked at Bletchley Park, playing a pivotal role in breaking the German Enigma code. He formulated the Turing machine to resolve Hilbert's decision problem, proving the existence of undecidable problems via the halting problem, and laid the foundations of artificial intelligence with the Turing test.

## you gotta know

- Formulated the *Turing machine*, a mathematical model of computation that defines the limits of what can be computed by an algorithm.
- Proved that the *halting problem* is undecidable—showing that no general algorithm can determine if an arbitrary program will run forever or halt.
- Played a key role at *Bletchley Park* in breaking the German naval *Enigma* cipher, designing the electromechanical *Bombe* search engine.
- Resolved the *Entscheidungsproblem* (decision problem) independently of Alonzo Church, demonstrating that first-order logic has no general decision algorithm.
- Proposed the *Turing test* (originally the imitation game) in 1950 as a criterion for machine intelligence.
- Detailed the chemical basis of morphogenesis in his 1952 paper, *The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis*, using reaction-diffusion equations to explain biological patterns.
