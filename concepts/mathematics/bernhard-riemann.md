# Bernhard Riemann

## summary

**Bernhard Riemann** (1826–1866) was a German mathematician who made groundbreaking contributions to analysis, number theory, and differential geometry. He defined the first rigorous integration theory using Riemann sums and introduced Riemann surfaces to analyze multi-valued complex functions. His habilitation lecture on the foundations of geometry established Riemannian geometry, which later provided the mathematical language for Einstein's general relativity. He also formulated the Riemann hypothesis, a famous conjecture about the zeroes of the zeta function that remains one of the most important unsolved problems in mathematics.

## you gotta know

- The *Riemann hypothesis* states that all non-trivial zeroes of the zeta function have a real part equal to 1/2, a conjecture central to understanding the distribution of prime numbers.
- *Riemannian geometry* generalises geometry to arbitrary dimensions and curved spaces, introducing the Riemann curvature tensor and defining the metric properties of manifolds.
- The *Riemann integral* provides a rigorous definition of integration on a closed interval by approximating the area under a curve using *Riemann sums* of rectangular partitions.
- *Riemann surfaces* are one-dimensional complex manifolds that allow multi-valued functions, such as the complex logarithm, to be treated as single-valued holomorphic functions.
- His habilitation lecture, *On the Hypotheses which lie at the Bases of Geometry*, was delivered in 1854 under the supervision of Carl Friedrich Gauss.
- He introduced the *Riemann-Roch theorem*, which relates the topological genus of a Riemann surface to its algebraic properties.
