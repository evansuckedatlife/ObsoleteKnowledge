## summary

A *lemniscate* is a figure-eight or infinity-shaped curve defined as the locus of points whose product of distances to two fixed foci equals a constant. It is named after the Latin word for "ribbon" and stands as one of the most elegant algebraic curves, combining visual symmetry with remarkable mathematical properties.

## you gotta know

- The *lemniscate of Bernoulli* is the most famous type; Cartesian equation: `(x² + y²)² = a²(x² - y²)`, where `2a` is the distance between the two foci.
- Polar equation: `r² = a² cos 2θ`, which reveals the figure-eight shape compactly.
- The lemniscate has two loops (one on each side) that meet at a central point (the origin), forming the characteristic infinity shape.
- The curve has a special property: the product of distances from any point on the lemniscate to the two foci is constant and equals `a²/2`.
- The area of one loop equals `a²`, and the total area is `2a²`.
- *Jacob Bernoulli* (of the famous Bernoulli family) first studied the lemniscate in 1694 as a variation on the ellipse.
- The lemniscate inspired the mathematical symbol for infinity (∞), which visually resembles the curve's two-looped form.
