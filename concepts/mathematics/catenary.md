# Catenary

## summary

A *catenary* is the curve formed by a hanging chain or cable under its own weight, held fixed at both ends. Despite its appearance of being parabolic, the catenary is exponential in nature and represents the natural equilibrium shape of any flexible material subject to uniform gravity.

## you gotta know

- The equation is `y = a·cosh(x/a) = a(e^(x/a) + e^(-x/a))/2`, where `cosh` is the hyperbolic cosine function and `a` is a parameter determined by tension and weight.
- *Galileo* incorrectly believed a hanging chain was parabolic; the truth remained unknown until the late 17th century.
- The catenary is the solution to the variational problem of minimizing the gravitational potential energy of a hanging flexible cable.
- *Hyperbolic functions* (cosh, sinh, tanh) emerge naturally in the catenary's equation; this curve is the prototype for applications of hyperbolic trigonometry.
- The catenary appears as the inverse curve (dual) of the *parabola* in certain geometric transformations.
- *Gateway Arch* in St. Louis and many other architectural structures follow catenary profiles for structural efficiency.
- The problem of the catenary was posed to the mathematical community in 1690; *Leibniz*, *Huygens*, and the Bernoulli brothers solved it independently.
