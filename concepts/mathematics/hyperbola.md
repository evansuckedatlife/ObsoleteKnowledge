# Hyperbola

## summary

A *hyperbola* is the set of all points in a plane such that the absolute difference of distances to two fixed points (the *foci*) is constant. It consists of two separate branches and is the most "open" conic section, appearing in navigation, optics, and the graphs of reciprocal functions.

## you gotta know

- Standard form: `(x²/a²) - (y²/b²) = 1` (horizontal) or `(y²/a²) - (x²/b²) = 1` (vertical).
- The two branches open away from each other; the closest points are the two *vertices* on the transverse axis.
- The difference of distances from any point on the hyperbola to the two foci is always `2a`.
- The *eccentricity* `e = c/a` is always greater than 1, making the hyperbola "wider open" than a parabola.
- The hyperbola has two *asymptotes*—diagonal lines that the curve approaches infinitely close to but never reaches.
- The hyperbola appears in *LORAN navigation*, where differences in signal arrival times from two transmitters place an observer on a hyperbolic curve.
- The function `f(x) = 1/x` (and its generalizations) graphs as a rectangular hyperbola.

## connections

- [[ellipse]] — both use two foci, but ellipse sums distances while hyperbola takes their difference.
- [[parabola]] — the eccentricity transition: ellipse < parabola < hyperbola.
- [[conic-sections]] — one of the four fundamental conic types.
- [[reciprocal-function]] — the graph of `1/x` is a hyperbola.
