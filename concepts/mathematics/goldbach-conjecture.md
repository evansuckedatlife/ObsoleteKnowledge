# Goldbach's Conjecture

## summary

Goldbach's conjecture, posed by the Prussian mathematician *Christian Goldbach* in 1742, states that every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two primes. Despite over two centuries of effort and computational verification for numbers up to astronomical magnitudes, no proof or counterexample has been found, making it one of the oldest unsolved problems in mathematics.

## you gotta know

- The conjecture asserts: for every even n > 2, there exist primes p and q such that p + q = n.
- Examples: 4 = 2+2, 6 = 3+3, 8 = 3+5, 10 = 5+5, 12 = 5+7, 14 = 7+7.
- Computationally verified for all even numbers up to extremely large values (over 10^18), yet a general proof remains out of reach.
- A related "weak" version (every odd number > 5 is the sum of three odd primes) was proven in 2013 by *Harald Helfgott*.
- The conjecture illustrates how simple to state, yet deep and hard to prove, some of the most important problems in mathematics can be.
