# Polynomial Function

## summary

A *polynomial function* is an algebraic function composed of a sum of terms, each a constant coefficient times a variable raised to a non-negative integer power. They form the simplest and most fundamental class of functions, arising everywhere from physics to economics, and their behavior—roots, degree, end behaviour—anchors much of calculus and algebra.

## you gotta know

- Expressed in the form `f(x) = aₙxⁿ + aₙ₋₁xⁿ⁻¹ + ... + a₁x + a₀`, where exponents are whole numbers and *aₙ* ≠ 0.
- The *degree* of the polynomial is the highest exponent; a degree-*n* polynomial has at most *n* roots and *n*−1 turning points.
- Degree 1 is linear; degree 2 quadratic; degree 3 cubic; degree 4 quartic and higher quintic, sextic, etc.
- Polynomials are continuous and differentiable everywhere on the real line; their derivatives are also polynomials of one degree lower.
- The *Fundamental Theorem of Algebra* guarantees that a degree-*n* polynomial has exactly *n* roots in the complex numbers (counting multiplicity).
- End behaviour is determined by the leading term: even-degree polynomials with positive leading coefficient approach `+∞` on both ends; odd-degree approach `−∞` on the left and `+∞` on the right.
