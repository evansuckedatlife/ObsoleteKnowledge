# Monomial

## summary

A *monomial* is a single term consisting of a constant coefficient multiplied by a variable raised to a non-negative integer power. Monomials are the building blocks of polynomials and the simplest algebraic functions, including constants, linear terms, and pure powers of *x*.

## you gotta know

- General form: `f(x) = axⁿ`, where *a* is a nonzero constant and *n* ≥ 0 is an integer.
- The *degree* of a monomial is the exponent *n*; the constant *a* is the *coefficient*.
- Degree 0 (just *a*, a constant); degree 1 (`ax`, linear); degree 2 (`ax²`, quadratic); higher degrees are possible.
- Monomials are the individual terms in a polynomial; a polynomial with exactly one term is monomials; two terms is a binomial; three is a trinomial.
- All monomials are continuous, differentiable, and defined for all real *x*; their graphs are smooth curves.
- The derivative of `axⁿ` is `n·a·xⁿ⁻¹`, which is itself a monomial (or zero).
