# Henri Poincaré

## summary

**Henri Poincaré** (1854–1912) was a French mathematician, theoretical physicist, and philosopher of science, often described as the last universalist in mathematics. He founded algebraic topology, made seminal contributions to the three-body problem that laid the groundwork for modern chaos theory, and independently developed concepts of special relativity. His most famous conjecture concerning the topology of 3-spheres remained unsolved for nearly a century until Grigori Perelman proved it in 2003.

## you gotta know

- Formulated the *Poincaré conjecture*, which asserts that every simply connected, closed 3-manifold is homeomorphic to the 3-sphere; it was the only Millennium Prize Problem solved (by Grigori Perelman).
- His work on the *three-body problem* for a king's prize contest revealed that even simple deterministic systems can exhibit chaotic behaviour, effectively founding *chaos theory*.
- Founded *algebraic topology* by introducing the concept of the *fundamental group* and homology groups in his paper *Analysis Situs*.
- Formulated the *Poincaré recurrence theorem*, which states that certain systems will, after a sufficiently long time, return to a state arbitrary close to their initial state.
- Independently developed parts of *special relativity*, introducing the Lorentz transformations in their modern form and the Poincaré group.
- Proposed the *Poincaré half-plane model* and disk model, providing concrete realizations of hyperbolic geometry.

## connections

- [[topology]] — he is considered the father of algebraic topology.
- [[chaos-theory]] — his study of the three-body problem discovered deterministic chaos.
- [[special-relativity]] — he contributed key mathematical formulations alongside Einstein and Lorentz.
- [[non-euclidean-geometry]] — his half-plane and disk models provided maps for hyperbolic geometry.
