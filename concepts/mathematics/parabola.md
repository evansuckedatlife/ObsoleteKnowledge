# Parabola

## summary

A *parabola* is the set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point, known as the focus, and a fixed line, known as the directrix. As a conic section with an eccentricity of exactly one, it represents the critical boundary between the closed ellipse and the open hyperbola. Parabolas are of immense practical importance in science and engineering, defining the trajectories of projectiles under uniform gravity and the reflective properties of antennas, telescopes, and headlights. The mathematical study of the parabola dates back to classical antiquity, where its quadrature by Archimedes served as a major precursor to the development of calculus.

## you gotta know

### Algebraic Forms and Equations

- *Vertex Form*: Expressed as $y = a(x - h)^2 + k$, where $(h, k)$ is the vertex of the parabola.
- *Focus-Directrix Form*: Expressed as $(x - h)^2 = 4p(y - k)$ for a vertically opening parabola, where $p$ is the distance from the vertex to the focus (and from the vertex to the directrix).
- *Latus Rectum*: The chord passing through the focus, parallel to the directrix; its length is $|4p|$, and its endpoints lie on the parabola.

### Geometric and Reflective Properties

- *Eccentricity*: Crucially equals exactly 1, placing it as the mathematical limit between the ellipse ($e < 1$) and the hyperbola ($e > 1$).
- *Optical/Acoustical Reflection*: Any light or sound ray traveling parallel to the axis of symmetry reflects off the parabolic surface directly into the focus.
- *Constructive Definition*: Can be generated as the envelope of a family of circles whose centers lie on a line and which pass through a fixed point.

### Physical and Historical Applications

- *Projectile Trajectory*: In the absence of air resistance, the path of a projectile launched under uniform gravity is a parabola, as demonstrated by *Galileo*.
- *Archimedes' Quadrature*: Archimedes proved in *The Quadrature of the Parabola* that the area enclosed by a parabola and a line is $4/3$ times the area of an inscribed triangle, utilizing a geometric infinite series.
- *Suspension Bridges*: The cables of a suspension bridge with a uniformly distributed load along its horizontal deck hang in a parabolic curve, distinct from the catenary curve of a free-hanging chain.

## context

The parabola is a fundamental bridge between algebra and geometry, representing the graph of every quadratic equation and the physical path of falling objects. In optics and acoustics, the parabola's reflective properties are utilized to focus energy—whether gathering faint light from distant stars in reflecting telescopes or projecting a beam of light from car headlights. In quiz bowl, the parabola is tested on its geometric parameters (focus, directrix, latus rectum), its unique eccentricity of 1, Galileo's projectile calculations, and Archimedes' early integration methods.
