# Parabola

## summary

A *parabola* is the set of all points in a plane equidistant from a fixed point (the *focus*) and a fixed line (the *directrix*). It is the most practically important conic section, appearing in projectile motion, satellite dishes, and the graph of any quadratic function.

## you gotta know

- Standard form: `y = ax² + bx + c` (vertex form), or `(x - h)² = 4p(y - k)` (focus-directrix form).
- The *vertex* is the point on the parabola closest to the directrix; it lies midway between the focus and directrix.
- The distance from vertex to focus equals the distance from vertex to directrix, denoted `p`.
- The parabola is the only conic section with *eccentricity* exactly 1, making it the boundary between ellipses and hyperbolas.
- Light or sound rays parallel to the axis reflect off the parabola toward the focus; this *focus-reflection property* is used in telescopes, microphones, and headlights.
- The path of a projectile under gravity (ignoring air resistance) is a parabola.
- The area under a parabolic arc played a central role in the development of calculus via *Archimedes*' method of exhaustion.

## connections

- [[ellipse]] — parabola arises as ellipse with one focus at infinity.
- [[hyperbola]] — parabola separates the ellipse (eccentricity < 1) from the hyperbola (eccentricity > 1).
- [[conic-sections]] — one of the four fundamental conic types.
- [[quadratic-function]] — polynomials of degree 2 graph as parabolas.
- [[archimedes]] — computed areas under parabolic curves, foundational to calculus.
