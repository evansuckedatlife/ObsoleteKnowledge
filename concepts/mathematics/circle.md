# Circle

## summary

A *circle* is the set of all points in a plane equidistant from a fixed point called the center. It is the most fundamental and symmetric of all geometric curves, appearing throughout mathematics, nature, and design from the earliest civilizations onward.

## you gotta know

- Defined by the equation `(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²`, where `(h, k)` is the center and `r` is the radius.
- The *circumference* (perimeter) equals `2πr`; the *area* equals `πr²`.
- A circle is the limiting case of an *ellipse* when the two foci coincide at the center.
- The circle appears as a *conic section* when a plane cuts a cone parallel to its base.
- Perfect rotational symmetry: invariant under all rotations about its center.
- Ancient Euclid defined the circle in purely geometric terms as a curve traced by a compass; modern mathematics expresses it algebraically.
- The *pi* (π) ratio emerges inevitably from circles: the ratio of circumference to diameter is always `π ≈ 3.14159...`

## connections

- [[ellipse]] — a generalized circle where the two foci separate.
- [[conic-sections]] — the circle as one of the four fundamental conic sections.
- [[euclid]] — gave the earliest geometric definition in the *Elements*.
- [[cardioid]] — a curve traced by a point on a rolling circle.
- [[cycloid]] — another rolling-circle curve, more famous in calculus.
