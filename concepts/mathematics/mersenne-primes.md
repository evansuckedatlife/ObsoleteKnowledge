## summary

A Mersenne prime is a prime number of the form 2^p - 1, where p is itself prime. Named after the French monk *Marin Mersenne*, these numbers have fascinated mathematicians for centuries and include some of the largest known primes, discovered through intensive computational searches.

## you gotta know

- Mersenne primes have the form M_p = 2^p - 1 where p is prime; if M_p is prime, it is a Mersenne prime (not all such numbers are prime).
- Examples include M₂ = 3, M₃ = 7, M₅ = 31, M₇ = 127; the first non-prime Mersenne number is M₁₁ = 2047 = 23 × 89.
- As of recent counts, only around 50 Mersenne primes are known, despite intensive searching; the gaps between consecutive discoveries can span years or decades.
- The *Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search* (GIMPS) is a distributed computing project dedicated to finding new Mersenne primes.
- Every known *perfect number* (a number equal to the sum of its proper divisors) is even and related to Mersenne primes via the *Euclid–Euler theorem*.
