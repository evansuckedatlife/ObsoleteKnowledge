# Ellipse

## summary

An *ellipse* is the set of all points in a plane such that the sum of distances to two fixed points (the *foci*) is constant. It is the most familiar conic section after the circle, appearing in planetary orbits, architectural domes, and countless mechanical and optical systems.

## you gotta know

- Standard form: `(x²/a²) + (y²/b²) = 1`, where `a` and `b` are the semi-major and semi-minor axes.
- The two foci lie on the major axis at distance `c` from the center, where `c² = a² - b²`.
- The *eccentricity* `e = c/a` measures how "stretched" the ellipse is; `e = 0` gives a circle, `e → 1` approaches a parabola.
- The sum of distances from any point on the ellipse to the two foci is always `2a`.
- *Kepler's First Law* states that planets orbit the sun in ellipses, not circles; the sun occupies one focus.
- The perimeter of an ellipse has no closed-form algebraic formula; it requires elliptic integrals.
- *Whispering galleries* exploit the reflection property: sound or light rays from one focus reflect to the other.

## connections

- [[circle]] — the special case when the two foci coincide (`e = 0`).
- [[parabola]] — the limiting case as one focus moves to infinity (`e → 1`).
- [[hyperbola]] — similar two-focus definition but with difference instead of sum.
- [[conic-sections]] — one of the four fundamental conic sections.
- [[kepler-laws]] — elliptical orbits form the foundation of celestial mechanics.
