# Quadratic Function

## summary

A *quadratic function* is a polynomial of degree 2, written as `f(x) = ax² + bx + c` with *a* ≠ 0. Its graph is a parabola, and it appears ubiquitously in physics (projectile motion), engineering, and optimization, making it one of the most recognizable and applied function types.

## you gotta know

- Standard form: `f(x) = ax² + bx + c`; vertex form: `f(x) = a(x − h)² + k`, where (*h*, *k*) is the vertex.
- The sign and magnitude of *a* determine shape: *a* > 0 opens upward (minimum); *a* < 0 opens downward (maximum).
- Roots (zeros) are found via the *quadratic formula*: `x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) / 2a`; the *discriminant* `Δ = b² − 4ac` tells whether roots are real (Δ ≥ 0) or complex (Δ < 0).
- The vertex (axis of symmetry) lies at `x = −b/2a`; the parabola is symmetric about this vertical line.
- The *axis of symmetry* and vertex form are central to graphing and optimization problems.
- Quadratics are continuous, differentiable; their derivative is linear: `f'(x) = 2ax + b`.
