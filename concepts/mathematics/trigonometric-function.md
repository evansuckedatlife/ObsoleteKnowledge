# Trigonometric Function

## summary

*Trigonometric functions*—sine, cosine, tangent, and their reciprocals—relate angles in a right triangle to ratios of its sides. They are periodic, oscillating functions fundamental to modeling wave phenomena, circular motion, and oscillatory behaviour in physics, engineering, and signal processing.

## you gotta know

- The six main trig functions: sine (*sin*), cosine (*cos*), tangent (*tan*), cosecant (*csc*), secant (*sec*), cotangent (*cot*).
- *Sine* and *cosine* have period 2π and range [−1, 1]; *tangent* has period π and range all real numbers.
- Key identities: `sin²θ + cos²θ = 1` (Pythagorean); `sin(α ± β) = sin α cos β ± cos α sin β` (angle sum); `cos(2θ) = cos²θ − sin²θ` (double angle).
- Defined on the unit circle: for angle *θ*, `cos θ` is the *x*-coordinate and `sin θ` is the *y*-coordinate of the point at radius 1 and angle *θ*.
- All six trig functions are continuous and differentiable wherever defined; `d/dx sin x = cos x`, `d/dx cos x = −sin x`.
- Applications span from simple harmonic motion to Fourier series to AC electrical circuits.
