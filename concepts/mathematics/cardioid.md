# Cardioid

## summary

A *cardioid* is a heart-shaped curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle rolling around another circle of the same radius. It is the simplest and most elegant of the epicycloid family, combining aesthetic beauty with rich mathematical properties.

## you gotta know

- The name "cardioid" means "heart-shaped," from the Greek *cardia* (heart).
- Parametric equations (for a circle of radius `a` rolling on a fixed circle of radius `a`): `x = a(2cos t - cos 2t)`, `y = a(2sin t - sin 2t)`.
- Polar equation: `r = a(1 + cos θ)`, one of the simplest and most elegant polar curves.
- The cardioid has a single *cusp* (sharp point) at the origin where the rolling circle begins and ends its path.
- The curve is bounded, fitting entirely within a region determined by the rolling circle's radius and initial position.
- The area enclosed by a cardioid equals `6πa²/4 = 3πa²/2`, or 1.5 times the area of the generating circle.
- The cardioid appears in *acoustics* and *antenna theory* as the polar pattern of certain microphones and directional radiators.

## connections

- [[cycloid]] — another rolling-circle curve, but with a line instead of a circle.
- [[limacon]] — the general curve of which the cardioid is a special case (when the rolling circle equals the fixed circle).
- [[epicycloid]] — the cardioid is the simplest member of the epicycloid family.
- [[spiral]] — both are rolling-motion curves studied in parametric geometry.
