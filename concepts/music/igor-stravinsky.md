# Igor Stravinsky

## summary

Igor Stravinsky (1882–1971) was a Russian-born composer, pianist, and conductor widely considered one of the most influential and versatile musical figures of the 20th century. His career is characterized by dramatic stylistic shifts, beginning with his early Russian nationalist period, moving through a cool, structured Neoclassical phase, and ending in a rigorous Serialist style. He achieved international fame through his collaborations with Sergei Diaghilev's Ballets Russes in Paris, composing the landmark ballets *The Firebird*, *Petrushka*, and *The Rite of Spring*. The premiere of *The Rite of Spring* in 1913 became legendary for triggering a full-scale riot in the audience due to its aggressive rhythms and avant-garde choreography.

## you gotta know

### stylistic periods & landmark works

- **Russian Period** is defined by his collaborations with the **Ballets Russes**, producing *The Firebird* (1910), *Petrushka* (1911), and *The Rite of Spring* (1913). These works utilized polytonality, complex meters, and driving, primitive rhythms.
- **Neoclassical Period** (c. 1920–1950) was a reaction against Romantic excess, seeking inspiration from 18th-century Classical structures. Major works include the ballet **Pulcinella** (based on themes attributed to Pergolesi), the opera-oratorio **Oedipus Rex**, and the choral-orchestral **Symphony of Psalms**.
- **Serialist Period** (c. 1950–1971) saw him adopt the twelve-tone method developed by his rival Arnold Schoenberg, resulting in late works like the *Requiem Canticles* and *Agon*.

### individual compositions

- **The Rite of Spring** (*Le Sacre du printemps*) depicts a pagan sacrifice in prehistoric Russia, famous for its opening bassoon solo in its highest register and its polyrhythmic "Augurs of Spring" chord.
- **Petrushka** tells the story of a tragic puppet brought to life, characterized by the **Petrushka chord** (a combination of C-major and F-sharp-major triads) representing the puppet's dual human-puppet nature.
- **The Soldier's Tale** (*L'Histoire du soldat*) is a theatrical work for narrator and a small chamber ensemble of seven instruments, depicting a soldier who trades his violin to the devil for wealth.
- **The Rake's Progress** (1951) is his major English opera, with a libretto by W.H. Auden and Chester Kallman, inspired by William Hogarth's series of paintings.

## context

Stravinsky was a cosmopolitan figure whose life spanned major historical shifts; he fled Russia during World War I, lived in France, and eventually emigrated to the United States during World War II, settling in Los Angeles. His innovative use of rhythm, particularly metric modulation and sudden accents, influenced nearly every subsequent 20th-century composer. He was a close friend of intellectual figures such as writer Aldous Huxley and artist Pablo Picasso, who designed costumes for *Pulcinella*.
