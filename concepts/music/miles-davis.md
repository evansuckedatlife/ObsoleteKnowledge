# Miles Davis

## summary

*Miles Davis* was an American jazz trumpeter, bandleader, and composer whose restless innovation transformed jazz repeatedly across five decades, from cool jazz to modal jazz to rock fusion. His distinctive muted trumpet tone and ability to recognize and nurture genius in sidemen made him a central figure in modern jazz's aesthetic evolution.

## you gotta know

- Pioneered *cool jazz* in the late 1940s, a reaction against bebop's virtuosic intensity, featuring intricate orchestrations and a more reserved aesthetic.
- Released *Kind of Blue* (1959), the best-selling jazz album of all time, which introduced modal improvisation and redefined how jazz musicians approached harmonic structure.
- Led the *First Great Quintet* (1964–1968) featuring *John Coltrane*, and later the *Second Great Quintet* (1965–1968) whose rhythmically complex interplay reshaped ensemble jazz.
- Ventured into rock fusion with *Bitches Brew* (1970), incorporating electric instruments and funk grooves to reach broader audiences and influence both jazz and rock musicians.
- Distinctive muted trumpet tone and ability to play minimally—letting silence speak—redefined trumpet aesthetics for generations.
- Worked across styles—bebop, hard bop, modal jazz, fusion—perpetually reinventing himself rather than repeating past successes.
