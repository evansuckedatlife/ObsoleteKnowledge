# Franz Schubert

## summary

Franz Schubert (1797–1828) was an Austrian composer who bridged the late Classical and early Romantic eras. Despite dying at the age of 31, he was exceptionally prolific, composing over 600 vocal art songs (*Lieder*), nine symphonies, and a vast body of chamber and piano music. Schubert's music is marked by its gift for melody and expressive harmony, establishing German song as a major classical genre. Living in Vienna in the shadow of Beethoven, he struggled for financial success and was chiefly celebrated in private gatherings called *Schubertiades* before achieving posthumous fame.

## you gotta know

- **Lieder** is the German art song genre Schubert revolutionized, composing over 600 pieces, including famous settings of Goethe's poems like **Erlkönig** (depicting a father and child chased by the supernatural Erlking) and **Gretchen am Spinnrade** (imitating a spinning wheel in the piano accompaniment).
- **Symphony No. 8 in B minor, D. 759** is universally known as the **Unfinished** Symphony because it consists of only two completed movements rather than the traditional four.
- **Trout Quintet** (Piano Quintet in A major, D. 667) is a popular chamber work named because its fourth movement is a set of variations on Schubert's own Lied *Die Forelle* (The Trout), scored for piano, violin, viola, cello, and double bass.
- **Death and the Maiden** (String Quartet No. 14 in D minor) is a dramatic chamber masterpiece named for its second movement, which is a set of variations on his Lied *Der Tod und das Mädchen*.
- **Winterreise** (*Winter Journey*) is his celebrated late song cycle of 24 poems by Wilhelm Müller, charting a rejected lover's lonely, winter wanderings.
- **Schubertiades** were the intimate salon gatherings hosted by Schubert's friends in private Viennese homes to perform and socialize around his music.
