# John Coolidge Adams

## summary

John Coolidge Adams (b. 1947) is an acclaimed American composer, conductor, and writer whose music is rooted in minimalism, though he is widely regarded as a pioneer of **post-minimalism**. His compositions combine the motoric drive and repeating loops of minimalism with the dramatic sweep, rich orchestration, and emotional depth of late-Romanticism. Adams is particularly famous for his "docu-operas," which dramatize controversial, real-life events of the late 20th century.

## you gotta know

- **Nixon in China** (1987) is his landmark first opera, with a libretto by Alice Goodman, depicting Richard Nixon's historic 1972 diplomatic visit; it features the iconic minimalist-backed aria "News has a kind of mystery" and Chiang Ch'ing's (Madame Mao) coloratura aria "I am the wife of Mao Tse-tung."
- **Harmonielehre** (1985) is a massive three-movement symphonic work named after Arnold Schoenberg's textbook on harmony; its creation was inspired by a vivid dream Adams had of a giant tanker truck exploding out of San Francisco Bay and flying like a rocket.
- **On the Transmigration of Souls** (2002) is a choral and orchestral piece commissioned to commemorate the victims of the September 11 attacks, blending live performers with tape loops of street noises and whispered names of the victims, which won the Pulitzer Prize for Music.
- **Doctor Atomic** (2005) is an opera centering on J. Robert Oppenheimer and the scientists of the Manhattan Project in the final hours before the **Trinity test**; its emotional climax is Oppenheimer's aria setting John Donne's holy sonnet **Batter my heart, three-person'd God**.
- **The Death of Klinghoffer** (1991) is a highly controversial opera dramatizing the 1985 hijacking of the cruise ship *Achille Lauro* by the Palestine Liberation Front and the murder of wheelchair-bound passenger Leon Klinghoffer.
- **Shaker Loops** (1978) is a kinetic early piece for string septet (later arranged for string orchestra) that utilizes rapidly oscillating bow strokes ("shimmers" and "loops") to mimic the spiritual shaking dances of the Shaker religious sect.
