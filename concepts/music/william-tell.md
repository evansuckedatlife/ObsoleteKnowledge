# William Tell

## summary

Gioachino Rossini's *William Tell* (1829) is a four-act French grand opera based on Friedrich Schiller's play *Wilhelm Tell*. Set in the Swiss Alps in the 14th century, it depicts the Swiss struggle for independence against the tyrannical occupying Austrian Habsburgs, led by the cruel governor Gessler. The legendary marksman William Tell emerges as the leader of the rebellion. After refusing to bow to Gessler's hat placed on a pole, Tell is arrested and forced to shoot an apple off his young son Jemmy's head with a crossbow. Tell succeeds, but reveals he had a second arrow intended for Gessler had he failed. Tell escapes, kills Gessler, and leads the Swiss to victory. This was Rossini's final opera, written when he was only 37, after which he retired from opera composition.

## you gotta know

- **William Tell** is the Swiss patriot and archer whose defiance of Austrian authority sparks the cantons to rise in rebellion.
- **Gessler** is the tyrannical Austrian governor of Schwyz and Uri who orders Tell's ordeal and represents imperial Habsburg oppression.
- **Arnold Melchthal** is a Swiss conspirator torn between his love for the Austrian princess **Mathilde** and his duty to his country, particularly after Gessler executes his elderly father.
- **Jemmy** is Tell's brave son who holds the apple on his head during the famous test of marksmanship.
- **William Tell Overture** is one of the most famous pieces of classical music in the world, consisting of four movements: Dawn, Storm, Call to the Cows, and the iconic "March of the Swiss Soldiers" featuring galloping brass.
- **Sois immobile** (*Be still*) is Tell's Act III aria, sung to Jemmy before the apple shot, advising his son to pray, remain completely still, and keep his eyes on the ground.
- **Asile héréditaire** (*Ancestral refuge*) is Arnold's challenging Act IV tenor aria, lamenting his father's death and expressing his grief before leading the rebels.
- **Mathilde** is a Habsburg princess who opposes Gessler's cruelty, rescues Jemmy from execution, and ultimately sides with the Swiss patriots.
