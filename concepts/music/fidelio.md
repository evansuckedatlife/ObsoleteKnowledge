# Fidelio

## summary

*Fidelio*, Op. 72 (1805, revised 1814) is Ludwig van Beethoven's only completed opera. A German *rescue opera* in two acts, it tells the story of Leonore, a courageous Spanish noblewoman. To save her husband, Florestan, who is being held as a political prisoner in a secret dungeon, Leonore disguises herself as a young man named "Fidelio" to secure a job working for the prison jailer Rocco. The opera is celebrated for its themes of political liberty, marital fidelity, and triumph over tyranny, culminating in the dramatic rescue of Florestan.

## you gotta know

- **Leonore** (disguised as **Fidelio**) is the heroic wife who infiltrates the prison and stands between her husband and his executioner.
- **Florestan** is the political prisoner chained in the deepest dungeon, who begins Act II with the dramatic cry "Gott! Welch Dunkel hier!" (*God! What darkness here!*).
- **Don Pizarro** is the villainous governor of the prison who has secretly locked Florestan away and resolves to murder him to cover up his crimes.
- **Rocco** is the kindly, money-conscious jailer whose daughter **Marzelline** falls in love with Fidelio, ignoring her former suitor, **Jaquino**.
- **Prisoners' Chorus** (*O welche Lust*) is a famous Act I choral number in which the prisoners express their joy at briefly walking in the sunlight.
- **Trumpet Call** is played offstage during the climactic Act II murder attempt, signaling the arrival of the state minister, **Don Fernando**, which halts the execution.
- **Overtures** were a source of great struggle for Beethoven, who composed four distinct versions: three known as *Leonore* (Nos. 1, 2, and 3) and the final, lighter *Fidelio* overture used in the 1814 revision.
