# La bohème

## summary

Giacomo Puccini's *La bohème* (1896) is a four-act Italian opera that stands as one of the most beloved and frequently performed works in the operatic repertoire. Based on Henri Murger's novel *Scènes de la vie de bohème*, it is set in Paris's Latin Quarter around 1830 and depicts the lives of a group of impoverished young artists—a poet, a painter, a musician, and a philosopher. The heart of the opera is the tragic love story between the poet Rodolfo and the frail seamstress Mimì, whose romance is threatened by poverty and Mimì's terminal tuberculosis. Celebrated for its soaring melodies and emotional realism (*verismo*), the opera ends with Mimì's quiet death in the bohemians' cold attic.

## you gotta know

- **Mimì** is a fragile young seamstress who meets Rodolfo when she knocks on his door looking for a match to relight her candle, only to drop her key in his dark room.
- **Rodolfo** is a struggling poet who falls in love with Mimì but later tries to separate from her, fearing that his drafty, unheated room will worsen her declining health.
- **Marcello** is a passionate painter and Rodolfo's roommate who shares a stormy, on-again-off-again relationship with the flirtatious Musetta.
- **Musetta** is Marcello's former lover who initially appears with a wealthy government minister, Alcindoro, whom she tricks into paying for the bohemians' dinner at Café Momus.
- **Che gelida manina** (*What a cold little hand*) is Rodolfo's Act I aria, sung when his hand touches Mimì's in the dark as they search for her lost key.
- **Sì, mi chiamano Mimì** (*Yes, they call me Mimì*) is Mimì's Act I response aria, in which she describes her quiet life embroidering flowers.
- **Quando men vo** (Musetta's Waltz) is Musetta's seductive Act II waltz, sung to draw Marcello's attention and make him jealous while dining at the Café Momus.
- **Vecchia zimarra** (*Old coat*) is a solemn Act IV aria sung by the philosopher **Colline**, in which he bids farewell to his beloved overcoat before pawning it to buy medicine for the dying Mimì.
