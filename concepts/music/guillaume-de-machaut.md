# Guillaume de Machaut

## summary

*Guillaume de Machaut* (c. 1300–1377) was the supreme figure of the *Ars Nova* (new art) movement, a poet-composer whose sophisticated rhythmic innovation, intricate polyphony, and integration of lyrical poetry and music fundamentally transformed European medieval composition. His *Messe de Notre Dame* stands as the first complete setting of the mass by a single composer, while his secular works epitomized courtly refinement.

## you gotta know

- Composed the *Messe de Notre Dame*, a four-voice polyphonic mass that unified the ordinary into a single cohesive work—a structural innovation that influenced mass composition for centuries.
- Pioneer of *isorhythm*: repeating rhythmic patterns (taleae) combined with repeating melodic patterns (color) in the tenor voice, creating architectural complexity beneath flowing upper melodies.
- Wrote over 140 secular songs—rondeaux, virelais, and lais—that married sophisticated rhythmic and harmonic language to refined love poetry, often his own verse.
- Composed 19 motets in which the *isorhythmic* technique reaches supreme sophistication, balancing mathematical construction with emotional expressivity.
- Served as a canon at Reims Cathedral and held positions in aristocratic courts, making him one of the first music composers to achieve secular prestige and documented fame.
- His secular and sacred works diverge sharply in style: motets are abstract and structurally complex, while secular songs prioritize melodic grace and lyrical intimacy.
