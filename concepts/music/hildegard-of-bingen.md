# Hildegard of Bingen

## summary

*Hildegard of Bingen* (1098–1179) was a Benedictine abbess, mystic, theologian, and composer whose liturgical chants represent the only extensive musical corpus by a woman from the medieval period. Her *Ordo Virtutum* (Play of Virtues) stands as the earliest known drama-play with musical accompaniment, and her visionary theology infused her compositional work with singular spiritual intensity.

## you gotta know

- Composed approximately 70 sacred lyrics and chants, preserved in her *Symphonia armonie celestium revelacionum* (Symphony of the Harmony of Heavenly Revelations), set to flowing, emotionally direct melodies.
- Created the *Ordo Virtutum*, a liturgical drama pitting virtues against vice, featuring the human soul as protagonist—an early ancestor of the mystery play and music drama tradition.
- Founded and directed her own convent at Disibodenberg (later Rupertsberg), overseeing scriptoria, music production, and a distinguished intellectual community.
- Her chants employed modal melodies with unusually large intervallic leaps and expressive contours, departing from the more formulaic Gregorian tradition.
- Wrote extensively on theology, medicine, natural science, and moral philosophy; her *Liber Scivias* (Book of Divine Knowledge) detailed her mystical visions and earned papal approval.
- Claimed direct divine inspiration for her visions and compositions—a claim that, remarkably, was validated by the Church during her lifetime.

## connections

- [[gregorian-chant]] — she worked within the plainsong tradition but expanded its expressive range.

- [[medieval-drama]] — the *Ordo Virtutum* pioneered musical drama.
