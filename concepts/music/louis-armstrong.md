# Louis Armstrong

## summary

*Louis Armstrong* was an American jazz trumpeter, vocalist, and composer whose innovative improvisational style and warm, gravelly singing voice made him one of the most influential figures in jazz history. Rising from poverty in New Orleans to international stardom, he pioneered the art of the jazz solo and became jazz's first great ambassador to the world.

## you gotta know

- A master of the trumpet who transformed the instrument from an ensemble voice into a vehicle for virtuosic solo improvisation.
- Sang with a distinctive, raspy vocal timbre and pioneering scat technique that influenced countless vocalists; "What a Wonderful World" remains his most beloved recording.
- Recorded *Hot Five* and *Hot Seven* sessions (1925–1928) that defined New Orleans jazz and established him as the preeminent soloist of the era.
- Bandleader and entertainer who brought jazz to mainstream audiences through film appearances, radio broadcasts, and international tours throughout Europe and Africa.
- Signature compositions include "West End Blues," "La Vie en Rose" (French version), and his trademark closing phrase "and this is a wonderful world."
- Pioneered the jazz virtuosity and showmanship that defined the instrument's role in popular music for generations.
