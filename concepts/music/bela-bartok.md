# Béla Bartók

## summary

Béla Bartók (1881–1945) was a Hungarian composer, pianist, and ethnomusicologist who was one of the most influential figures of 20th-century music. Alongside Zoltán Kodály, Bartók traveled throughout Eastern Europe, North Africa, and Asia Minor to record and transcribe thousands of peasant folk tunes, co-founding the field of ethnomusicology. His compositions brilliantly synthesize these traditional folk melodies and irregular rhythms with modern Western techniques like atonality, creating a highly personal style marked by driving rhythms, mathematical symmetries, and visceral energy.

## you gotta know

- **Concerto for Orchestra** (1943) is his most popular and celebrated orchestral work, commissioned by Serge Koussevitzky while Bartók was dying of leukemia in American exile; its fourth movement (*Intermezzo interrotto*) famously parodies a repetitive theme from Shostakovich's *Leningrad Symphony*.
- **Music for Strings, Percussion and Celesta** (1936) is a masterpiece of complex symmetry that utilizes the Fibonacci sequence and the golden ratio to determine its structural climaxes; the slow third movement is a prime example of his atmospheric **night music** style.
- **Duke Bluebeard's Castle** (1911) is his sole opera, a dark, symbolist one-act work setting a Hungarian libretto, in which Judith insists on opening seven locked doors inside the castle of her new husband Bluebeard, discovering blood behind each door.
- **Mikrokosmos** (1926–1939) is a pedagogical monument consisting of 153 piano pieces arranged in six volumes of progressive difficulty, designed to introduce students to modern piano technique and musical concepts.
- **String Quartets** (six in total) are considered by musicologists to be the most important string quartet cycle written since Beethoven's, utilizing innovative string techniques like the **Bartók snap** (a snap pizzicato where the string rebounds violently off the fingerboard).
- **The Miraculous Mandarin** (1926) is a scandalous pantomime ballet about a gang of thieves who use a young woman to lure men to be robbed and killed; a wealthy Mandarin survives hanging and stabbing out of sheer lust for the girl until his desire is finally satiated.
