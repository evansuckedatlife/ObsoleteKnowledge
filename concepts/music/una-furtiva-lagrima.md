# Una furtiva lagrima

## summary

*Una furtiva lagrima* (*A furtive tear*) is the rapturous tenor aria sung by Nemorino in Gaetano Donizetti's comic opera *L'elisir d'amore* (*The Elixir of Love*). Believing that a magic love potion has finally won him Adina's affection—when in fact she has genuinely fallen for him—Nemorino sings of his joy with one of opera's most purely lyrical and beautiful melodies. The aria captures the triumph of love and hope over adversity, sung with infectious optimism and touching sincerity.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Gaetano Donizetti* for tenor voice; one of the most beloved tenor arias and a standard encore in concert and recital programs.
- The aria occurs in Act II of *L'elisir d'amore*, after Nemorino has mistakenly believed that a fraudulent "magic elixir" has granted him Adina's love.
- A *furtive tear* on Adina's cheek is the outward sign that convinces Nemorino of the potion's efficacy, when in reality she is simply overcome with genuine emotion for him.
- The melody is in a soaring, arching style characteristic of Donizetti's gift for tunefulness; it ascends and descends with apparent effortlessness, showcasing the tenor's vocal beauty.
- The orchestration is delicate and intimate, with a sparse accompaniment that allows the tenor's voice to shine and soar without competition.
- *L'elisir d'amore* (1832) is a comic opera (*opera buffa*), yet this aria, like many of Donizetti's finest moments, balances humor with genuine pathos and sentiment.
- The aria's popularity extends beyond opera circles; it has been recorded by popular singers and featured in films, commercials, and concert performances worldwide.
