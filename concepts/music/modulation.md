# Modulation

## summary

*Modulation* is the act of changing from one key to another within a piece of music, creating harmonic variety and narrative momentum. It is a central technique in tonal music, allowing composers to explore related and distant harmonic regions before returning to the home key, deepening emotional and structural interest.

## you gotta know

- *Pivot-chord* (or *common-chord*) modulation uses a single chord that belongs to both the old and new key, easing the transition; the most common and smooth method.
- *Direct* or *abrupt* modulation simply shifts to a new key without preparatory chords; used for dramatic effect or surprise.
- A *tonicization* is a temporary emphasis on a non-tonic chord (making it sound like a temporary key) without fully committing to a new key; useful for local color.
- The *development section* of a sonata form modulates extensively through a series of keys, generating restlessness that propels toward recapitulation.
- Modulation from a minor key to its relative major (or vice versa) requires no accidental adjustments, as they share a key signature.
