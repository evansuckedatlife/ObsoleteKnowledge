# Josquin des Prez

## summary

*Josquin des Prez* (c. 1450–1521) was a Franco-Flemish master whose motets and masses defined the High Renaissance ideal of balanced, emotionally engaging polyphony. His reputation—already towering during his lifetime—cemented him as perhaps the greatest composer of the 15th and 16th centuries, revered even by contemporaries as the "father of musicians."

## you gotta know

- Composed the motet *Ave Maria* (*Gratia plena*), which exemplifies his gift for singable, soaring counterpoint and textual clarity within complex voicing.
- Revolutionary use of *imitative counterpoint*: each melodic idea enters in succession across all voices, creating unity and momentum while maintaining independence.
- Served at the courts of Milan, Rome, the Medici, and the House of Burgundy, traveling across Europe and absorbing local styles—making him a bridge between national musical traditions.
- Masses include the *Missa Pange Lingua* and *Missa Ave Maris Stella*, both using cantus firmus technique with extraordinary structural coherence.
- Known for text-painting: musical gestures reflecting the meaning of the lyrics, such as ascending lines for "ascended" or dissonant clusters for "suffering."
- The term *musica reservata* (reserved music) is sometimes attributed to him—the idea that music should be performed by skilled singers for discerning audiences, not every crowd.
