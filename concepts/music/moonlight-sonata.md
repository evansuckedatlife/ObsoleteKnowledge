# Moonlight Sonata

## summary

Ludwig van Beethoven's *Piano Sonata No. 14 in C-sharp minor*, Op. 27, No. 2 (1801), universally known as the **Moonlight Sonata**, is one of the most famous keyboard works in history. Beethoven subtitled the piece *Sonata quasi una fantasia* ("Sonata in the manner of a fantasy") to signal its departure from traditional Classical sonata form, which typically opened with a fast sonata-allegro movement. The nickname "Moonlight" was coined posthumously in 1832 by the German music critic Ludwig Rellstab, who compared the ethereal first movement to the effect of moonlight reflecting on Switzerland's Lake Lucerne.

## you gotta know

- **First Movement** (*Adagio sostenuto*) is characterized by its hypnotic, continuous right-hand triplets over slow, dark bass octaves. Beethoven instructed that the dampers should be raised throughout the movement (keeping the sustain pedal down), creating a blurred, sustained acoustic wash.
- **Third Movement** (*Presto agitato*) is a stormy, technically demanding finale in sonata-allegro form, characterized by rapid, ascending arpeggios and sharp, aggressive accents.
- **Second Movement** (*Allegretto*) is a brief, gentle minuet and trio in D-flat major, famously described by Franz Liszt as "a flower between two abysses."
- **Countess Giulietta Guicciardi** is the young Austrian noblewoman and Beethoven's piano pupil to whom the sonata is dedicated, with whom Beethoven was deeply in love.
- **C-sharp minor** is an unusual key for Classical keyboard works; the sonata begins in C-sharp minor and concludes in the same key.
