# John Coltrane

## summary

*John Coltrane* was an American jazz saxophonist, bandleader, and composer whose harmonic innovations, spiritual searching, and technical mastery made him one of the most revered and influential jazz musicians of all time. His fearless exploration of advanced chord progressions and sheets-of-sound technique transformed what was possible on the tenor saxophone.

## you gotta know

- Saxophonist whose revolutionary "sheets of sound" technique—playing multiple notes simultaneously through rapid fingering—created a cascade of harmonic color previously unheard in jazz.
- Pioneered *modal jazz* improvisation, abandoning traditional chord changes in favor of improvising over sustained scales; featured on *Kind of Blue* and developed further in his own albums.
- Released *A Love Supreme* (1964), a masterpiece featuring four movements that declared jazz's spiritual and artistic legitimacy; one of jazz's best-selling and most revered albums.
- Composed "Giant Steps," whose rapid, complex harmonic progressions became a proving ground for saxophonists for generations—still considered one of jazz's most challenging standards.
- Played with *Miles Davis* (1955–1960) and later led his own quartet (1960–1967) that explored increasingly experimental territory before his death at age 40.
- Spiritual seeker whose later work reflected his interest in Eastern philosophy, African music, and metaphysical themes.

## connections
