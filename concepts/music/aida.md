## summary

Giuseppe Verdi's *Aida* (1871) is a grand opera in four acts set in ancient Egypt during a conflict with Ethiopia. Commissioned by Cairo's Khedivial Opera House to celebrate its opening, the opera depicts the tragic love triangle between Radamès, a heroic Egyptian military commander, Amneris, the Pharaoh's jealous daughter, and Aida, an enslaved Ethiopian princess. When Radamès is chosen to lead the Egyptian army, he is caught between his love for Aida and his loyalty to the Pharaoh, while Aida must choose between her lover and her homeland. After Radamès is tricked into betraying military secrets, he is sentenced to be buried alive; Aida secretly slips into the tomb to join him, and they die together in each other's arms.

## you gotta know

- **Aida** is an Ethiopian princess captured and enslaved as a handmaiden to Amneris, who struggles to balance her love for Radamès with her loyalty to her father and country.
- **Radamès** is the Egyptian captain of the guard who is chosen by the goddess Isis to lead the war effort against Ethiopia, hoping a victory will allow him to marry Aida.
- **Amneris** is the Pharaoh's daughter who is passionately in love with Radamès and orchestrates schemes to confirm Aida's feelings for him, leading to the ultimate tragedy.
- **Amonasro** is Aida's father and the King of Ethiopia who disguises his royal status when captured, later pressure-guilting Aida into extracting Egyptian troop movements from Radamès.
- **Triumphal March** (*Gloria all'Egitto*) is the grand, brass-heavy Act II instrumental and choral piece celebrating Radamès's victorious return with prisoners.
- **Ritorna vincitor** (*Return victorious*) is Aida's conflicted Act I aria in which she realizes she is praying for her lover's victory, which would mean the defeat of her father.
- **O patria mia** (*O my homeland*) is Aida's Act III aria reflecting on the beauty of her native country, which she fears she will never see again.
- **O terra, addio** (*O earth, farewell*) is the serene final duet sung by Aida and Radamès as they suffocate inside the dark vault beneath the Temple of Vulcan.
