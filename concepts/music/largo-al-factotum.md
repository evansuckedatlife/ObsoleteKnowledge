# Largo al factotum

## summary

*Largo al factotum* (*Make way for the factotum*) is the swaggering baritone aria sung by Figaro in Act I of Gioachino Rossini's opera *The Barber of Seville*. In this patter aria, Figaro introduces himself with boastful charm, cataloging his services as barber, surgeon, and man-of-all-trades while prancing about the town square. The aria showcases a baritone's ability to deliver rapid-fire lyrics with comic precision, and it stands as one of opera's most delightful and instantly memorable show-stoppers.

## you gotta know

- Composed by *Gioachino Rossini* for baritone voice; one of the most written-about arias in opera and a signature vehicle for baritone virtuosity.
- The title character *Figaro* is not merely a barber but the "factotum" (man-of-all-work)—he shaves, bleeds, and sets bones, and is intimate with every household and secret in Seville.
- The aria is a *patter aria*, filled with rapid syllable-driven singing that requires comedic timing and clear diction; the orchestra accompanies with a bouncy, almost dance-like rhythm.
- Figaro's self-description (*Figaro qua, Figaro là, Figaro su, Figaro giù*) is the aria's most famous refrain, establishing his ubiquitous, indispensable presence.
- The aria's opening phrase *Largo al factotum* is an instruction to the crowd to "make way for the factotum," reinforcing Figaro's importance and cheeky self-regard.
- Rossini's witty orchestration, particularly the use of pizzicato strings and lighthearted woodwinds, underscores the aria's comedic spirit and operatic farce.
- This aria is considered essential repertoire for any baritone and appears frequently at concert galas and auditions, often bringing down the house with its infectious energy.
