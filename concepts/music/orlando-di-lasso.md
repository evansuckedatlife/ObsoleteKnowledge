# Orlando di Lasso

## summary

*Orlando di Lasso* (1532–1594) was a Franco-Flemish composer of extraordinary prolific genius and stylistic range, equally celebrated for madrigals, motets, and chansons across all major European courts. His mastery of both text-painting and structural perfection made him arguably the most admired composer of the late Renaissance alongside Palestrina.

## you gotta know

- Composed over 2,000 works across all sacred and secular genres, including over 1,000 motets alone—a staggering output maintained across six decades.
- Master of the madrigal, crafting vivid dramatic settings with sophisticated text-painting, from intimate love lyrics to violent narratives (*La dolce vista*).
- Served as *maestro di cappella* at the court of Duke Albrecht of Bavaria in Munich for 38 years, the most stable and prestigious post of his era.
- His motets span both the strict Counter-Reformation piety of Palestrina and more emotionally expressive Baroque gestures, showing his stylistic adaptability.
- Excelled in secular chanson tradition, composing witty French texts with driving rhythms and sophisticated rhyme-schemes.
- Known for masterful counterpoint, harmonic sophistication, and an uncanny ability to match musical language to poetic content across languages (Latin, Italian, French, German).
