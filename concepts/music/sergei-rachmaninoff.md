# Sergei Rachmaninoff

## summary

Sergei Rachmaninoff (1873–1943) was a Russian composer, virtuoso pianist, and conductor who was one of the last great champions of late-Romanticism in classical music. Possessing legendary technical ability and unusually large hands, he wrote some of the most challenging and expressive piano music in the repertoire, characterized by sweeping melodies, rich harmonies, and bell-like tolling chords. Following the Russian Revolution of 1917, he fled Russia and spent the remainder of his life in exile in the West, working primarily as a touring concert pianist.

## you gotta know

- **Piano Concerto No. 2 in C minor, Op. 18** (1901) is his most celebrated work, written to mark his recovery from a severe three-year depression and writer's block using hypnotherapy by Dr. Nikolai Dahl, to whom the piece is dedicated.
- **Rhapsody on a Theme of Paganini, Op. 43** (1934) is a concertante work for piano and orchestra consisting of 24 variations on Paganini's *Caprice No. 24*, featuring a famous, soaring **18th variation** that is a melodic inversion of Paganini's original theme.
- **Piano Concerto No. 3 in D minor, Op. 30** (1909) is famously dubbed the "Rach 3" and is widely feared by pianists as one of the most technically demanding and physically exhausting concertos in the classical canon.
- **Prelude in C-sharp minor, Op. 3, No. 2** (1892) is a dramatic solo piano work written when he was just 19; it became so popular worldwide that audiences demanded he play it as an encore at nearly every recital, much to his eventual frustration.
- **Symphony No. 2 in E minor, Op. 27** (1907) is a grand, lushly romantic symphony that restored his confidence in symphonic writing after the disastrous premiere of his Symphony No. 1 in 1897, which had been poorly conducted by a reputedly intoxicated Alexander Glazunov.
- **The Bells, Op. 35** (1913) is a choral symphony setting a Russian translation of Edgar Allan Poe's poem of the same name, representing four stages of life (silver sleigh bells, golden wedding bells, brass alarm bells, and iron mourning bells).
