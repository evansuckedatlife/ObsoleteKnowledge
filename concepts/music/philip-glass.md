# Philip Glass

## summary

Philip Glass (b. 1937) is a prolific American composer and pianist who is one of the most influential and widely celebrated figures in late-20th-century classical music. He is a primary pioneer of **minimalism** (a term he dislikes, preferring to describe his style as "music with repetitive structures"), building large-scale compositions out of small, repeating melodic cells and additive rhythms. Glass has achieved immense mainstream success across multiple genres, composing symphonies, concertos, film scores, and landmark avant-garde operas.

## you gotta know

- **Einstein on the Beach** (1976) is his first and most famous opera, a five-hour plotless collaboration with director Robert Wilson; its libretto consists entirely of numbers, solfège syllables, and poetry read over a repeating solo violin played by a performer dressed as Albert Einstein.
- **Portrait Trilogy** is the collective name for his three early biographical operas about figures who transformed the world through ideas: *Einstein on the Beach* (science), **Satyagraha** (1980, depicting Mahatma Gandhi in South Africa with a Sanskrit text), and **Akhnaten** (1983, centering on the monotheistic Egyptian pharaoh).
- **Koyaanisqatsi** (1982) is his iconic score for Godfrey Reggio's non-narrative cult film; the music features a deep bass vocal repeating the Hopi title word (meaning "life out of balance") alongside rapidly undulating organ and brass arpeggios.
- **Glassworks** (1982) is a six-movement studio album designed to introduce Glass's music to a wider pop audience, featuring accessible, melodic movements like the opening "Opening" for solo piano.
- **Ravi Shankar** is the legendary Indian sitar player whom Glass assisted in Paris in the 1960s; transcribing Shankar's music introduced Glass to the non-Western additive rhythmic cycles that replaced traditional Western bar lines in his music.
- **Philip Glass Ensemble** is the performing group he founded in 1968, comprising woodwinds, keyboards, and amplified vocals, which toured extensively to perform his early experimental compositions.
