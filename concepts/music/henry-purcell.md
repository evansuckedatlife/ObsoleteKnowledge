# Henry Purcell

## summary

*Henry Purcell* (1659–1695) was the greatest English Baroque composer, a master of dramatic expression and instrumental brilliance who elevated the English court and theater to international musical standing. Though his life was brief, his operas, anthems, and chamber works fused English tradition with Continental sophistication in an idiom of unmatched grace and invention.

## you gotta know

- Composed the semi-opera *Dido and Aeneas* (c. 1689), which remains one of the Baroque's most moving works, with the celebrated lament aria *When I Am Laid in Earth* representing the height of tragic expression.
- Wrote incidental music for over 40 plays, including *The Tempest* and *The Fairy Queen*, employing dances, recitatives, and instrumental interludes to elevate spoken drama.
- Served as organist at Westminster Abbey and composer to the Chapel Royal, creating an enormous body of sacred music: anthems, services, and devotional songs balancing Purcellian drama with liturgical propriety.
- Master of the ground bass (a repeating bass line over which variations unfold), using it to create obsessive, emotionally heightened effects in vocal music and keyboard works alike.
- Composed virtuoso instrumental music: fantasias for strings, dance suites for keyboard, and trio sonatas following the Italian trio-sonata model yet stamped with English character.
- Pioneer of English opera and musical theater; his idiom influenced the Handel era that immediately followed.
