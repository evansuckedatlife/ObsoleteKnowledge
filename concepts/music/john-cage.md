# John Cage

## summary

John Cage (1912–1992) was an American composer, music theorist, philosopher, and artist who was a leading figure of the post-war avant-garde. A pioneer of indeterminacy in music, electroacoustic music, and non-standard instrument usage, Cage questioned the fundamental boundaries of art and music. He is best known for his silent piece *4'33"* and for inventing the prepared piano, a technique that transformed the traditional keyboard into a microtonal percussion ensemble.

## you gotta know

- **4'33"** (1952) is his most famous and controversial work, a three-movement piece in which the performer(s) remain completely silent; the "music" consists of the ambient sounds of the environment and the audience during the performance.
- **Prepared piano** is a technique Cage invented in 1940, inserting screws, bolts, rubber bands, and felt between the strings of a grand piano to alter its pitch, timbre, and resonance, creating a gamelan-like percussive sound.
- **Sonatas and Interludes** (1946–1948) is a landmark cycle of sixteen sonatas and four interludes for prepared piano, designed to express the nine *rasas* (permanent emotions) of Indian aesthetic theory.
- **Music of Changes** (1951) is a piece for solo piano that pioneered aleatoric (chance-based) composition, in which Cage used the **I Ching** (the ancient Chinese *Book of Changes*) to determine all parameters of pitch, duration, and dynamics.
- **As Slow as Possible** (*Organ²/ASLSP*, 1987) is a piece whose ongoing performance at a church in Halberstadt, Germany, began in 2001 and is scheduled to last **639 years**, making it the longest musical performance in history.
- **Black Mountain College** was the experimental North Carolina arts school where Cage staged *Theater Piece No. 1* (1952), a multi-media event widely regarded as the first artistic "happening."
