# Raga

## summary

A raga is a melodic framework in Indian classical music (both Hindustani and Carnatic traditions) that defines a scale, typical melodic patterns, ornamentation techniques, and emotional character. Like a mode crossed with a set of improvisational rules, each raga is associated with a specific time of day, season, or emotion. Ragas are the vehicle for Indian music's primary practice: real-time improvisation within a deeply structured framework, remarkably parallel to Western jazz.

## you gotta know

- A **raga** consists of a selection of notes (usually 5–7 from the 12-note chromatic scale), with specific rules about which notes are emphasized, which are approached with slides or bends, and which are avoided in certain contexts.
- Ragas are **scale-plus-rules**: unlike Western major and minor scales that treat all notes equally, ragas assign hierarchical importance to notes; the *vadi* (most important) and *samvadi* (second most important) provide melodic landmarks.
- Each raga is associated with a **rasa** (emotion or aesthetic mood) and often a specific **time of day** or **season**; for example, *Yaman* is evening raga evoking longing, while *Bhairav* is a morning raga of spiritual awakening.
- **Improvisation is paramount**: a raga performance (called a *raaag* or *alap-jor-jhala*) begins slowly, exploring the raga's characteristics, then accelerates into faster, more rhythmically complex passages, allowing the musician to demonstrate mastery.
- The **alap** is the opening section, free of rhythmic cycle, where the soloist slowly introduces the raga's scale and character; the **jor** adds rhythmic pulse without the [[tala]] (drum cycle); finally, the **jhala** and rhythmic portions feature rapid exchange with the accompanist.
- The most famous ragas include *Yaman*, *Bhairav*, *Kharaharapriya*, and *Marwa*; each is instantly recognizable to Indian music listeners and carries centuries of tradition.

## connections

- [[hindustani-classical-music]] — the North Indian raga tradition.
- [[carnatic-music]] — the South Indian raga tradition (*melakarta* system).
- [[improvisation]] — the core practice of raga performance, parallel to jazz.
- [[dastgah]] — the analogous melodic framework in Persian/Middle Eastern music.
- [[jazz]] — shares with raga the principle of improvisation within a melodic/harmonic structure.
- [[modal-music]] — Western modal improvisation owes conceptual debt to ragas.
- [[sitar]] — the leading raga instrument in Hindustani music.
- [[tabla]] — the drum that accompanies ragas, establishing the [[tala]] (rhythmic cycle).
- [[tala]] — the rhythmic cycle that underlies raga performance.
