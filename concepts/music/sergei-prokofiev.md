# Sergei Prokofiev

## summary

Sergei Prokofiev (1891–1953) was a Soviet and Russian composer, pianist, and conductor who was one of the major creators of 20th-century music. After leaving Russia following the 1917 Revolution to live in the West, he returned to the Soviet Union in 1936, where he produced some of his most famous works under the pressure of Soviet arts censorship. He is celebrated for his dramatic ballets, including *Romeo and Juliet*, his early *Classical Symphony*, the film score *Alexander Nevsky*, and the children's guide *Peter and the Wolf*. In an ironic twist of fate, Prokofiev died on the exact same day as Joseph Stalin.

## you gotta know

- **Peter and the Wolf, Op. 67** is his beloved children's symphonic tale with a narrator, in which characters are represented by specific instruments: **Peter** (strings), **the bird** (flute), **the duck** (oboe), **the cat** (clarinet), **the grandfather** (bassoon), **the wolf** (three French horns), and **the hunters' shots** (timpani and bass drum).
- **Romeo and Juliet, Op. 64** is his celebrated ballet score, famous for the heavy, menacing **Dance of the Knights** (also known as *Montagues and Capulets*), which features a driving bassline and soaring saxophone.
- **Classical Symphony (Symphony No. 1 in D major, Op. 25)** was composed in 1917 as a deliberate imitation of the style of Joseph Haydn, becoming a pioneering work of Neoclassicism.
- **Alexander Nevsky, Op. 78** is his score for Sergei Eisenstein's landmark 1938 historical film, which Prokofiev later adapted into a concert cantata, featuring the dramatic "Battle on the Ice" scene.
- **Lieutenant Kijé, Op. 60** is a suite adapted from his film score for a satirical Soviet movie about a fictional soldier created by a clerical error, famous for its *Troika* movement utilizing sleigh bells.
- **Co-incident death** occurred on March 5, 1953. Because Joseph Stalin died on the same day, all flowers and hearses in Moscow were confiscated for the dictator's state funeral, leaving Prokofiev's small funeral with only a few attendees and paper flowers.
