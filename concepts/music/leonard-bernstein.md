# Leonard Bernstein

## summary

Leonard Bernstein (1918–1990) was a towering American composer, conductor, author, and pianist who was one of the most charismatic and influential figures in 20th-century classical music. He served as the long-time music director of the New York Philharmonic and conducted many of the world's finest orchestras. As a composer, he effortlessly bridged the gap between serious classical genres and popular musical theater, writing symphonies, ballets, and acclaimed Broadway shows, most notably *West Side Story*.

## you gotta know

- **West Side Story** (1957) is his legendary musical that updates Shakespeare's *Romeo and Juliet* to 1950s gang rivalry in Manhattan between the Jets and the Sharks, featuring lyrics by Stephen Sondheim.
- **Candide** (1956) is an operetta based on Voltaire's satirical novella; it is celebrated for its virtuosic overture and the notoriously difficult coloratura soprano aria **Glitter and Be Gay**.
- **Symphony No. 2** ("The Age of Anxiety", 1949) is a symphonic work for orchestra and solo piano based on the Pulitzer Prize-winning poem of the same name by **W. H. Auden**.
- **Chichester Psalms** (1965) is a three-movement choral work setting Hebrew Psalm texts, scored for mixed choir, boy soprano (or countertenor), organ, harp, and percussion.
- **On the Town** (1944) is his first hit Broadway musical, expanding on his ballet *Fancy Free*, which follows three sailors on a 24-hour shore leave in New York City and includes the song "New York, New York."
- **Young People's Concerts** was a series of 53 televised concert lectures with the New York Philharmonic that introduced millions of children to classical music and music theory between 1958 and 1972.
