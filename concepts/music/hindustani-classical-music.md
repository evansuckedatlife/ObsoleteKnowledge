# Hindustani Classical Music

## summary

*Hindustani classical music* is the principal art-music tradition of North India, emphasizing melodic improvisation within a framework of *ragas* (melodic modes) and *talas* (rhythmic cycles). Performed on instruments like the sitar, sarod, and bansuri, it prioritizes the musician's spontaneous exploration of a raga's emotional and tonal possibilities, making each performance unique.

## you gotta know

- Built on *ragas*, systematic melodic frameworks that specify ascending and descending note sequences, characteristic phrases, and the emotional mood (*rasa*) each evokes.
- *Tala* is the rhythmic framework—a repeating cycle of beats (typically 4, 8, 10, or 16) that structures the improvisation and is played by the tabla drummer.
- The *sitar*, with its sympathetic strings and curved frets, is the most iconic Hindustani instrument; the *sarod* (fretless) and *bansuri* (bamboo flute) are also central.
- Performances often begin with a slow, rubato-like *alap* section (no rhythm), progress to *jor* (accelerating), then enter the *jhala* (rapid, rhythmic) and *gat* (fixed composition) with tabla accompaniment.
- Each raga is traditionally associated with a specific time of day or season; performance at the "wrong" time was historically considered spiritually inappropriate.
- Master-apprentice transmission (*guru-shishya*) is the traditional learning method; formalized music schools are a recent Western influence.
- Notable instruments include the sarangi (bowed), tabla (paired drums), and harmonium (keyboard); the sarod and sitar are the virtuoso soloists' choices.

## connections

- [[carnatic-music]] — the South Indian classical tradition, using similar concepts of raga and tala but with distinct instruments and stylistic features.
- [[raga]] — the melodic foundation of Hindustani music, a structured improvisational framework.
- [[tabla]] — the paired drums that provide rhythm and are often featured as solo instruments.
- [[sitar]] — the most recognizable Hindustani instrument, popularized globally by Ravi Shankar.
- [[bansuri]] — the bamboo flute, traditionally associated with Krishna in Hindu mythology.
