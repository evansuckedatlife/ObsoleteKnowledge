# Benjamin Britten

## summary

Benjamin Britten (1913–1976) was the preeminent English composer of the 20th century, revitalizing British opera and choral music. A pacifist who left England for the United States during the early years of World War II, his works often center on themes of the social outsider, innocence corrupted, and the tragedy of war. His breakthrough opera *Peter Grimes* (1945) put English opera back on the international map. He is widely celebrated for his educational work *The Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra* and his monumental *War Requiem*.

## you gotta know

- **Peter Grimes** is his tragic opera about a misanthropic fisherman in a small Suffolk village who faces social ostracism after his apprentices die at sea, famous for the atmospheric orchestral **Four Sea Interludes**.
- **The Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra, Op. 34** is a popular educational piece based on a theme by Baroque composer Henry Purcell, which systematically introduces each section of the orchestra before concluding with a grand fugue.
- **War Requiem, Op. 66** was composed for the consecration of the rebuilt Coventry Cathedral (destroyed by German bombs), combining the Latin Mass for the Dead with the anti-war poetry of **Wilfred Owen**.
- **Peter Pears** was the tenor singer, muse, and lifelong partner of Britten, for whom he wrote the title role of Peter Grimes and many other leading vocal parts.
- **Billy Budd** (1951) is his all-male opera based on Herman Melville's sea novella, set aboard the HMS *Indomitable* during the French Revolutionary Wars.
- **Aldeburgh Festival** is the music festival Britten co-founded in 1948 in the Suffolk coastal town where he lived and worked.
