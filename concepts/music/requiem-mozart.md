# Requiem in D minor

## summary

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's *Requiem Mass in D minor* (K. 626) is a choral masterpiece left unfinished at the composer's death in December 1791. Commissioned anonymously by Count Franz von Walsegg to commemorate his late wife, the mystery surrounding the patron fueled rumors that Mozart was composing a death mass for himself. Following Mozart's death, his widow Constanze sought to have the work finished secretly to claim the commission fee. The completion was undertaken primarily by Mozart's pupil Franz Xaver Süssmayr, who worked from Mozart's brief sketches, vocal parts, and figured bass. The Requiem is celebrated for its dramatic power, dark orchestral colors, and profound expression of grief, making it one of the most performed choral works in the world.

## you gotta know

### movements & musical features

- **Introitus** (*Requiem aeternam*) is the only movement of the Requiem completely written, voiced, and orchestrated by Mozart himself, opening with a somber bassoon and basset horn melody.
- **Kyrie** is a complex, dramatic double fugue based on two contrasting themes, developing a high-tension counterpoint between the choir and orchestra.
- **Tuba mirum** (*The trumpet shall sound*) is introduced by a famous, lyrical trombone solo, which is answered in turn by the bass, tenor, alto, and soprano soloists.
- **Rex tremendae** (*King of majesty*) features a stark contrast between fortissimo shouts of "Rex!" from the choir and soft, pleading cries of "Salva me" (*Save me*).
- **Confutatis** (*When the damned are confounded*) features high-contrast textures, pitting aggressive, driving lines for the bass and tenor voices (representing the flames of hell) against gentle, ethereal lines for the sopranos and altos.
- **Lacrimosa** (*Weeping day*) is a famous, mournful movement in 12/8 time with a sighing string motif; Mozart completed only the first eight measures before his death, breaking off after the words "homo reus" (*guilty man*).

### completion & commissioning

- **Count Franz von Walsegg** was the eccentric nobleman who commissioned the work anonymously through a messenger, intending to plagiarize it and claim authorship.
- **Franz Xaver Süssmayr** was Mozart's student who completed the orchestration for the middle movements and composed the entirety of the *Sanctus*, *Benedictus*, and *Agnus Dei* using Mozart's verbal instructions.
- **Instrumentation** calls for basset horns (a type of low clarinet) and bassoons to create a dark woodwind color, while completely omitting flutes and oboes.

## context

The Requiem is enveloped in romantic myth, notably popularized by Alexander Pushkin's play *Mozart and Salieri* and Peter Shaffer's movie *Amadeus*, which falsely depict Antonio Salieri as the masked stranger who commissions and steals the piece to poison Mozart. The work remains a cornerstone of the choral repertoire and was performed at the memorial services of monumental cultural figures such as Joseph Haydn, Ludwig van Beethoven, Napoleon Bonaparte, and Frédéric Chopin.
