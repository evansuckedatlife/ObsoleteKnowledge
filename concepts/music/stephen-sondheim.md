# Stephen Sondheim

## summary

Stephen Sondheim (1930–2021) was a legendary American composer and lyricist who is widely regarded as the most important and influential figure in late-20th-century musical theater. Celebrated for his intricate, witty lyrics, sophisticated harmonic language, and willingness to tackle dark, unconventional subject matter, he fundamentally revolutionized the American musical. Over a career spanning six decades, he collaborated on landmarks like *West Side Story* and composed masterpieces including *Sweeney Todd* and *Into the Woods*.

## you gotta know

- **Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street** (1979) is his operatic musical thriller about a vengeful barber who murders his customers in Victorian London, whose corpses are then baked into meat pies by his partner Mrs. Lovett.
- **Sunday in the Park with George** (1984) is a Pulitzer Prize-winning musical inspired by **Georges Seurat**'s pointillist masterpiece *A Sunday on La Grande Jatte*, exploring the obsessive nature of artistic creation.
- **Into the Woods** (1987) is a celebrated musical that intertwines and deconstructs Brothers Grimm fairy tales (including *Cinderella*, *Rapunzel*, and *Jack and the Beanstalk*), exploring the complex moral consequences of "happily ever after."
- **Company** (1970) is a pioneering concept musical with no linear plot, centering on Bobby, a single 35-year-old bachelor in New York contemplating commitment through interactions with his married friends.
- **West Side Story** (1957) and **Gypsy** (1959) are the two landmark early Broadway musicals for which Sondheim wrote the lyrics, collaborating with composers Leonard Bernstein and Jule Styne, respectively.
- **Send in the Clowns** is a melancholic ballad from his 1973 musical *A Little Night Music* that became his most famous individual song, covered by Frank Sinatra, Judy Collins, and many others.
