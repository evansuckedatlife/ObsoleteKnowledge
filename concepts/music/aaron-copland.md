# Aaron Copland

## summary

Aaron Copland (1900–1990) was an American composer often referred to as the "Dean of American Composers." He is celebrated for crafting a distinctively American populist musical style, characterized by open harmonies (reflecting the vast American landscape) and the integration of traditional folk tunes. After early experiments in jazz and modernism, Copland shifted during the Great Depression and World War II to write accessible, patriotic music. His most famous works include the ballets *Appalachian Spring*, *Rodeo*, and *Billy the Kid*, as well as his iconic *Fanfare for the Common Man*.

## you gotta know

- **Appalachian Spring** (1944) is his most famous ballet, commissioned and choreographed by **Martha Graham**, which depicts a wedding celebration of a young pioneer couple in Pennsylvania and famously incorporates the Shaker hymn **Simple Gifts**.
- **Fanfare for the Common Man** (1942) is a brief, stirring work for brass and percussion composed during World War II, inspired by Vice President Henry A. Wallace's speech declaring the start of the "Century of the Common Man."
- **Rodeo** (1942) is a cowboy ballet choreographed by **Agnes de Mille** about a tomboy cowgirl seeking love on a ranch, containing the famous, energetic **Hoedown** movement.
- **Lincoln Portrait** (1942) is a patriotic symphonic work that features a speaker reciting passages from Abraham Lincoln's speeches and letters, over an orchestral score incorporating folk tunes like *Camptown Races*.
- **Billy the Kid** (1938) is his first cowboy ballet, which depicts the life of the outlaw William Bonney and quotes traditional Western songs like *Goodbye Old Paint*.
- **Nadia Boulanger** was the legendary French pedagogue in Paris under whom Copland studied, who went on to teach many of the leading American composers of the century.
