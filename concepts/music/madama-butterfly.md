# Madama Butterfly

## summary

Giacomo Puccini's *Madama Butterfly* (1904) is a tragic three-act Italian opera set in Nagasaki, Japan. Based on a short story by John Luther Long, it tells the story of Cio-Cio-San (Japanese for "Madam Butterfly"), a young Japanese geisha who falls deeply in love with and marries Benjamin Franklin Pinkerton, a callous lieutenant in the United States Navy. Pinkerton views the marriage as a temporary, exotic convenience and soon returns to America. Cio-Cio-San raises their child in isolation, steadfastly believing Pinkerton will return for her. When Pinkerton finally reappears years later with his new American wife to claim the boy, Cio-Cio-San, devastated by the betrayal, agrees to give up her son and commits ritual suicide (*jigai*) with her father's dagger.

## you gotta know

- **Cio-Cio-San** (Madam Butterfly) is a 15-year-old geisha who renounces her Shinto faith to marry Pinkerton, causing her uncle, the Bonze, to publicly curse and disown her.
- **Benjamin Franklin Pinkerton** is the American naval lieutenant who leases a house and marries Butterfly, showing complete disregard for her culture and emotional devotion.
- **Sharpless** is the compassionate American consul in Nagasaki who tries to warn Pinkerton against playing with Butterfly's feelings and later attempts to gently break the news of Pinkerton's remarriage to her.
- **Suzuki** is Cio-Cio-San's devoted servant who comforts her, prays to her ancestors, and helps her watch for Pinkerton's ship.
- **Un bel dì, vedremo** (*One fine day we shall see*) is Cio-Cio-San's famous Act II aria, in which she passionately describes the day she will see Pinkerton's ship appear in Nagasaki harbor.
- **Humming Chorus** is a famous wordless, melancholic choral number sung at the end of Act II as Butterfly, Suzuki, and the child keep an all-night vigil waiting for Pinkerton's arrival.
- **Dolore** (*Sorrow*) is the name of Cio-Cio-San's blue-eyed son; she promises Pinkerton that his name will be changed to **Gioia** (*Joy*) when his father returns.
- **Con onor muore** (*To die with honor*) is the final aria sung by Butterfly before she stabs herself, reading the inscription on her father's dagger: "He dies with honor who cannot preserve life with honor."
