# Giuseppe Verdi

## summary

Giuseppe Verdi (1813–1901) was the dominant composer of 19th-century Italian opera and a national icon of the Italian unification movement (*Risorgimento*). Over his long career, he composed 28 operas, refining the *bel canto* tradition into highly dramatic, character-driven works. Among his most famous masterpieces are *Rigoletto*, *La traviata*, *Il trovatore*, and the grand opera *Aida*. Later in life, he came out of retirement to write two Shakespearean masterpieces, *Otello* and *Falstaff*. Beyond opera, Verdi is celebrated for his dramatic and operatic *Messa da Requiem*, composed to honor the writer Alessandro Manzoni.

## you gotta know

- **Risorgimento Patriotism** was closely linked to Verdi's name; Italian patriots shouted the slogan **"Viva V.E.R.D.I."** as a coded acronym for *Vittorio Emanuele Re D'Italia* (supporting the future King of Italy).
- **Va, pensiero** (the Chorus of the Hebrew Slaves) from his early opera **Nabucco** (1842) became an unofficial anthem of Italian unification, expressing longing for a lost homeland.
- **Rigoletto** (1851) is a tragic opera about a hunchbacked court jester, famous for the lighthearted tenor aria **La donna è mobile** (*Woman is fickle*).
- **La traviata** (1853) tells the story of the courtesan Violetta Valéry who dies of consumption, containing famous numbers like the drinking song *Libiamo ne' lieti calici*.
- **Messa da Requiem** (1874) is his monumental setting of the funeral mass, celebrated for its terrifying, brass-heavy **Dies irae** depicting the day of judgment.
- **Arrigo Boito** was the poet and composer who persuaded Verdi out of retirement to write the librettos for his final, continuous-music masterpieces, *Otello* and *Falstaff*.
