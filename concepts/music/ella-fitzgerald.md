# Ella Fitzgerald

## summary

*Ella Fitzgerald* was an American jazz vocalist renowned for her exceptional vocal range, perfect intonation, and improvisational brilliance. Her ability to swing with infectious joy and navigate complex bebop harmonies with flawless technique established her as one of jazz's greatest and most beloved singers.

## you gotta know

- Jazz vocalist with a warm, inviting soprano voice capable of spanning nearly three octaves, combining technical perfection with emotional authenticity.
- Master of scat singing—wordless vocal improvisation using nonsense syllables—creating horn-like lines that demonstrated her harmonic sophistication and improvisational genius.
- Recorded definitive interpretations of the Great American Songbook, including collaborations with pianist *Oscar Peterson* and other jazz luminaries.
- Composed and recorded "A-Tisket, A-Tasket," a novelty hit that launched her career in 1938; later expanded her repertoire to include complex bebop standards.
- Known for her collaborative spirit and willingness to work with virtually every major jazz musician, from *Duke Ellington* to *Louis Armstrong* to bebop innovators.
- Career spanning over six decades demonstrated her extraordinary longevity, consistency, and refusal to exploit her fame through novelty or commercialization.

## connections

- [[duke-ellington]] — bandleader and composer with whom she recorded memorable collaborations.
- [[jazz]] — vocal virtuoso who elevated jazz singing through technical mastery and improvisational brilliance.
