# Arnold Schoenberg

## summary

Arnold Schoenberg (1874–1951) was an Austrian-born composer, music theorist, and painter who was one of the most radical and influential figures of 20th-century music. He led the Second Viennese School and pioneered the abandonment of traditional key signatures (atonality). To organize this new harmonic space, Schoenberg invented the **twelve-tone technique** (dodecaphony), a system of composition using serial rows of all twelve chromatic pitches. His works moved from lush late-Romanticism to dramatic Expressionism and full serialism, generating intense public controversy and permanently altering the course of music history.

## you gotta know

### twelve-tone technique & theories

- **Twelve-tone technique** (dodecaphony) is his compositional system in which a piece is built upon a specific permutation of the twelve notes of the chromatic scale, manipulated through inversion (upside down), retrograde (backwards), and retrograde-inversion.
- **Second Viennese School** refers to Schoenberg and his two most famous pupils, **Alban Berg** (composer of the opera *Wozzeck*) and **Anton Webern** (known for his sparse, brief pointillist works).
- **Emancipation of the dissonance** is the term Schoenberg coined to describe his harmonic philosophy, arguing that dissonance no longer needed to resolve to consonance, treating all intervals as equally stable.

### major compositions

- **Pierrot Lunaire, Op. 21** (1912) is an Expressionist setting of 21 poems for female reciter and a chamber ensemble, famous for utilizing **Sprechstimme** (a vocal style where the performer strictly follows the rhythm but slides between pitches, halfway between speaking and singing).
- **Verklärte Nacht** (*Transfigured Night*, Op. 4) is an early, lush late-Romantic string sextet based on a poem by Richard Dehmel, depicting a couple walking in a forest.
- **Gurrelieder** is a massive, late-Romantic cantata requiring colossal orchestral and choral forces (over 400 performers), representing his transition to his mature style.
- **Moses und Aron** is his major, unfinished twelve-tone opera, depicting the biblical conflict between Moses (who only speaks) and Aaron (who sings lyrically).
- **A Survivor from Warsaw, Op. 46** is a dramatic 1947 cantata for narrator, male chorus, and orchestra, depicting the Holocaust in the Warsaw Ghetto, concluding with the singing of the *Shema Yisrael*.

## context

Born into a Jewish family in Vienna, Schoenberg converted to Lutheranism in his youth but formally returned to Judaism in Paris in 1933 after fleeing Nazi Germany. He emigrated to the United States, settling in Los Angeles and teaching at USC and UCLA. He suffered from severe triskaidekaphobia (the fear of the number 13), ironically dying on Friday, July 13, 1951, at 13 minutes before midnight.
