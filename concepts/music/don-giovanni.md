# Don Giovanni

## summary

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's *Don Giovanni* (1787) is a two-act opera formally classified as a *dramma giocoso* (a blend of comedy and tragedy). With a libretto by Lorenzo Da Ponte based on the legendary Spanish libertine Don Juan, it follows the final, chaotic days of the unscrupulous nobleman Don Giovanni. Giovanni's life of relentless seduction and deceit begins to unravel after he murders the Commendatore, the father of Donna Anna, one of his victims. Despite being pursued by those he has wronged, Giovanni remains defiant. His fate is sealed when he arrogantly invites a statue of the dead Commendatore to dinner; the stone guest arrives, demands Giovanni's repentance, and upon his refusal, drags him screaming down to Hell. 

## you gotta know

### plot & characters

- **Don Giovanni** is the antihero, a wealthy, remorseless Spanish nobleman driven solely by his desire to seduce every woman he encounters.
- **Leporello** is Giovanni's disgruntled servant who provides comic relief, assists in Giovanni's escapes, and begrudgingly keeps a list of his master's conquests.
- **Donna Anna** is a noblewoman who attempts to arrest Giovanni after he breaks into her bedroom, leading to her father's death; she vows vengeance.
- **The Commendatore** is Anna's father, who is killed by Giovanni in a sword fight in the opening scene, only to return as a supernatural stone statue.
- **Donna Elvira** is a lady from Burgos whom Giovanni abandoned; she pursues him alternately seeking revenge and trying to save his soul.
- **Zerlina** is a rustic peasant girl whom Giovanni attempts to seduce on her wedding day, much to the dismay of her fiancé, **Masetto**.
- **Don Ottavio** is Donna Anna's devoted fiancé, whose principal role is supporting her quest for justice and singing lyrical declarations of love.

### musical numbers

- **Catalogue Aria** (*Madamina, il catalogo è questo*) is Leporello's Act I aria to Donna Elvira, detailing Giovanni's 2,065 conquests, famously noting that 1,003 of them were in Spain.
- **Là ci darem la mano** (*There we will entwine our hands*) is a seductive Act I duet between Giovanni and Zerlina, where he convinces her to accompany him to his villa.
- **Champagne Aria** (*Finch'han dal vino*) is Giovanni's brief, high-energy Act I aria instructing Leporello to organize a lavish party with wine and dancing.
- **Deh, vieni alla finestra** (*Ah, come to the window*) is a delicate Act II serenade sung by Giovanni to Donna Elvira's maid, accompanied by a solo mandolin.
- **Il mio tesoro** (*My treasure*) is Don Ottavio's Act II tenor aria, a notoriously difficult coloratura piece where he vows to avenge Donna Anna's grief.
- **Don Giovanni, a cenar teco** (*Don Giovanni, you invited me to dine*) is the climactic Act II confrontation where the Commendatore's statue crashes Giovanni's banquet.

## context

*Don Giovanni* is celebrated for its psychological complexity, showing a protagonist who is evil yet charismatic, and a dramatic tone that prefigured the Romantic era's obsession with the supernatural and individual defiance. The opera premiered at the National Theatre in Prague, where Mozart personally conducted it to ecstatic reviews. Its overture, famously written by Mozart in a single night just before the premiere, begins with the dark, heavy D-minor chords that herald the arrival of the stone statue at the end of the opera.
