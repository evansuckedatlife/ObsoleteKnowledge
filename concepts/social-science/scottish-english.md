# Scottish English

## summary

*Scottish English* is the accent and dialect of Scotland, influenced by Scots Gaelic and the native Scots language. It features distinctive vowel pronunciations, grammatical differences including the deletion of auxiliary verbs, and strong regional pride tied to Scottish national identity.

## you gotta know

- Rhotic pronunciation: the "r" is always pronounced, often as an alveolar tap or trill, even at the end of words like *car*.
- The *kit/fleece* distinction is often merged, so *fill* and *feel* may sound nearly identical.
- Use of *eh* or *aye* as general agreement particles, and *naw* for negation.
- Vowel inversions make *boot* sound like *bate* and *goat* like *geet*, differing markedly from RP.
- Phonological contrasts preserved between *whine* and *wine* that are lost in most other English accents.
- Past participles often drop the auxiliary, so "I've gone" becomes "I'm gone," and periphrastic *dae* replaces standard English *do*.
