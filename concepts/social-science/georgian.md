# Georgian

## summary

*Georgian* is a Caucasian language spoken in Georgia, remarkable for its unique writing system—the *Mkhedruli* script—which is neither alphabet, abjad, nor abugida but a distinct writing system. It has an ancient literary tradition stretching to the 5th century and is known for its complex consonant clusters and ergative-absolutive alignment.

## you gotta know

- A Kartvelian language with roughly 4 million native speakers primarily in Georgia; classified as ergative-absolutive in alignment.
- Features three native writing systems (*Asomtavruli*, *Nuskhuri*, *Mkhedruli*), with Mkhedruli used in modern Georgian; each developed independently and was adapted to fit the language's phonology.
- Famous for tolerating consonant clusters unusual in world languages; clusters like "gvprtskvni" (you peel us) are pronounced as single syllabic units.
- Has a rich inflectional morphology with numerous cases and a complex system of verb agreement; verbs mark person, number, tense, and mood.
- An ancient literary tradition with the oldest texts dating to the 5th century (hagiographies); Georgian became a literary language and vehicle for Christian orthodoxy in the Caucasus.
- Linguistically isolated; its relationship to other Caucasian language families (Northwest and Northeast Caucasian) is disputed, though some scholars group it with the Kartvelian family.
