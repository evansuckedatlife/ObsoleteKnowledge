# George Lakoff

## summary

*George Lakoff* is an American linguist and cognitive scientist who pioneered cognitive linguistics by arguing that language structure reflects the embodied human mind and conceptual system. His theory of conceptual metaphor — that abstract thought is grounded in metaphorical mappings from physical experience — transformed how linguists understand meaning and reasoning.

## you gotta know

- Proposed that human thought is fundamentally metaphorical: we understand abstract domains (time, emotion, ethics) by mapping them onto concrete, embodied experiences.
- Demonstrated that metaphor is not merely a literary device but a core cognitive mechanism shaping how we think and reason in everyday language.
- Challenged the Chomskyan view that syntax is autonomous from meaning, arguing instead that grammar reflects underlying conceptual organisation.
- Applied cognitive linguistic insights to political language and rhetoric, showing how metaphorical framing shapes political perception and ideology.
- His work bridges cognitive science, psychology, linguistics, and philosophy, positioning language as emerging from embodied interaction with the world.
