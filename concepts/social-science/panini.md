# Panini

## summary

*Panini* was an ancient Indian grammarian and linguistic theorist, likely active in the 4th century BCE, who compiled an extraordinarily precise formal grammar of Sanskrit. His work *Ashtadhyayi* (Eight Chapters) established a system so comprehensive and elegant that it became the foundation for all Sanskrit study and remains one of the most sophisticated grammars ever written.

## you gotta know

- Authored the *Ashtadhyayi*, a condensed Sanskrit grammar of roughly 4,000 aphoristic rules that describe the entire language with remarkable precision.
- Developed a highly economical notation system using metarules and transformations — a proto-computational approach that anticipated modern linguistic formalism by over 2,000 years.
- His rules apply in a specific order (the "order of operations" for grammar), making *Ashtadhyayi* one of the first algorithmic descriptions of language.
- Standardised Sanskrit as a formal system independent of living speech, cementing it as the language of Vedic ritual and Hindu scholarship.
- His work demonstrates a distinction between the synchronic system (the language as it is) and the diachronic processes (how it changes) — concepts not formally isolated in Western linguistics until the 20th century.
