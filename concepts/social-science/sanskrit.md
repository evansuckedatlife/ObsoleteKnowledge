## summary

*Sanskrit* is an ancient Indo-European language of India, the liturgical language of Hinduism and Buddhism, and the vehicle of vast classical literature. Systematically described by the grammarian *Panini* around the 4th century BCE, Sanskrit exemplifies linguistic structure and became central to 19th-century historical linguistics and the discovery of the Indo-European language family.

## you gotta know

- Classical Sanskrit was the language of the Vedas (Hinduism's oldest scriptures), the epics (Mahabharata, Ramayana), and scientific and philosophical treatises.
- *Panini* authored the *Ashtadhyayi*, a remarkably concise and systematic grammar of Sanskrit—a model of linguistic description that influenced modern formal linguistics.
- *William Jones*, an English judge in India (1786), observed striking similarities between Sanskrit, Latin, and Greek, proposing they shared a common ancestor (*Proto-Indo-European*); this insight launched historical comparative linguistics.
- Sanskrit's regular morphology and grammatical structure made it ideal for historical-comparative studies; the language family (Indo-European) contains most European and many Asian languages.
- Distinguished from *Prakrit* (vernacular Middle Indo-Aryan languages) and modern Hindi, Bengali, etc., which evolved from earlier Prakrit dialects.
- Remains a liturgical language in Hinduism and Buddhism; studied in universities worldwide for its grammar and as a window into ancient Indian civilization.
