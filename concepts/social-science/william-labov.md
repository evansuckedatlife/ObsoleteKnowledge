# William Labov

## summary

*William Labov* is an American sociolinguist who founded the systematic study of linguistic variation and change, demonstrating that dialect differences follow hidden social and stylistic patterns rather than being random or degraded. His meticulous methods for documenting how speakers vary their speech across social contexts transformed linguistics into an empirical social science.

## you gotta know

- Pioneered variationist sociolinguistics, using quantitative methods to show that variation (different pronunciations, grammatical forms) correlates with social class, ethnicity, age, and gender in systematic ways.
- Conducted landmark studies on vowel shifts in American English, demonstrating that language change is ongoing and follows predictable patterns, contradicting the view that modern language is static.
- Developed the "observer's paradox" — the challenge of collecting natural speech when the act of observation changes behaviour — and methods to overcome it.
- Studied speech in urban communities (New York City, Philadelphia) and rural areas, establishing that "non-standard" dialects are fully rule-governed, not defective.
- His work showed that language is not a fixed code but a constantly shifting social practice, with different speakers and communities advancing changes at different rates.
