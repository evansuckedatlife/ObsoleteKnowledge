# Stonehenge

## summary

*Stonehenge* is a monumental circle of standing stones on the Salisbury Plain in England, built in stages over more than 1,500 years (c. 3100–1500 BCE). Its massive sarsen stones arranged in precise astronomical alignments, combined with evidence of ritual gatherings and burials, suggest it served as a temple, ceremonial calendar, or sacred meeting place, though its exact purpose remains debated and mysterious.

## you gotta know

- Located on Salisbury Plain in Wiltshire, England, built in multiple phases over the Neolithic and Bronze Age periods.
- The outer circle consists of 30 sarsen stones (sandstone) weighing up to 25 tons each, quarried from Marlborough Downs 20 miles away.
- An inner circle of 60 smaller bluestones (dolerite) was transported from the Preseli Mountains in Wales, a distance of 150 miles, an extraordinary logistical feat.
- The monument is oriented to align with the winter and summer solstices—the sun rises over the Heel Stone at the summer solstice sunrise.
- Archaeological excavations reveal cremated bones of thousands of individuals, indicating it served as a burial site and place of ritual gathering.
- The name "Stonehenge" derives from Old English; its original purpose remains unknown, with theories ranging from astronomical observatory to healing temple to ceremonial calendar.
- Built by Neolithic and Bronze Age societies with no written language, suggesting the knowledge required was preserved through oral tradition and specialized craftspeople.
