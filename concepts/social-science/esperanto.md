# Esperanto

## summary

*Esperanto* is a planned auxiliary language created in 1887 by L. L. Zamenhof, a Polish-Jewish ophthalmologist, with the utopian goal of fostering international peace and understanding through a shared neutral language. Despite never becoming an official international language, it has sustained a global community of hundreds of thousands of speakers and speakers, and remains the most successful constructed language in history.

## you gotta know

- Designed with simplified phonetics (no irregular sounds), agglutinative morphology (words built from transparent roots and affixes), and a regular grammar system with no exceptions.
- Approximately 100,000–2 million speakers worldwide, depending on fluency definition; annual international conventions bring together enthusiasts.
- Built from a vocabulary base drawn from major European languages (primarily Romance, Germanic, and some Slavic roots) to maximize intelligibility.
- The *Esperanto movement* developed alongside the language as a peace-advocacy community; literature, music, radio, and internet forums sustain the speaker base.
- Never achieved the political adoption Zamenhof hoped for; instead consolidated into a subculture with its own identity and cultural institutions.
- Grammar is entirely regular; verb conjugation and noun declension follow a single paradigm with no irregular forms.
