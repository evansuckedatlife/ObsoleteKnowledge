# Navajo

## summary

*Navajo* is an Athabaskan language spoken by the Navajo people across the southwestern United States, most notably in the Navajo Nation spanning Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah. It is the most widely spoken Native American language north of Mexico and features an unusually complex verb system with extensive aspectual distinctions and directional prefixes.

## you gotta know

- Approximately 150,000–170,000 native speakers, primarily on the Navajo Nation; endangered due to declining youth fluency despite official status and school programs.
- Athabaskan family; linguistically related to Apache, Dene (Chipewyan), and various Pacific Northwest languages; part of the larger Dene-Yeniseian hypothesis linking Siberian and North American languages.
- A highly synthetic language with complex verb morphology; verbs encode extensive information about aspect, mode, and directionality through prefixes and stem changes.
- Famously used as a code in World War II: Navajo Code Talkers transmitted encrypted military messages using the language's complexity and low number of non-native speakers.
- Has a unique writing system and considerable written literature; recent revival efforts focus on digital resources, schools, and community initiatives to reverse language shift.
- Nominative-accusative alignment (like most Indo-European languages), which makes it structurally distinct from neighboring Pueblo languages.
