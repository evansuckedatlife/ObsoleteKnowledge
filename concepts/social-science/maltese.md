## summary

*Maltese* is the national language of Malta, a Mediterranean island republic. Unlike other Romance-language-speaking regions, Maltese is a Semitic language descended from Arabic, making it linguistically unique in Europe; it has been heavily influenced by Italian and English over centuries of colonial and trade contact.

## you gotta know

- A Semitic language of the Arabic family, descended from medieval Andalusian Arabic brought by Arab rule in the 9th–11th centuries.
- The only Semitic language that is an official EU language and is written in the Latin alphabet (rather than Arabic script).
- Extensively borrowing from Sicilian Italian, and more recently English; roughly 40% of its vocabulary is Romance-derived.
- Spoken by about 400,000 native speakers, concentrated on the islands of Malta and Gozo.
- Has a written standard established after independence in 1964; dialect variation between the islands and mainland communities.
- Minimal speaker base outside the archipelago, making transmission to diaspora communities challenging.

## connections
