# Leonard Bloomfield

## summary

*Leonard Bloomfield* was an American linguist whose rigorous methodological approach to language description and whose influential textbook *Language* (1933) made structural and descriptive linguistics the dominant paradigm in mid-20th-century American linguistics. He elevated linguistics from a philological appendage of classical studies into an autonomous empirical science.

## you gotta know

- Authored *Language* (1933), a comprehensive textbook that defined how linguists should approach the systematic, observable study of language — emphasising data over speculation.
- Pioneered structural linguistics in America, treating language as a closed system of elements and their arrangements rather than as a window onto history or psychology.
- Developed rigorous methods for phonetic transcription, morphological analysis, and dialect mapping — establishing field linguistics as a technical discipline.
- Studied Native American languages (particularly Menomini and other Algonquian languages) using meticulous descriptive techniques that set standards for language documentation.
- His behaviourist approach (meaning arises from use and context, not inner mental states) dominated American linguistics from the 1930s through the 1950s.
