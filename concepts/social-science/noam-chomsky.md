# Noam Chomsky

## summary

*Noam Chomsky* is an American linguist and cognitive scientist whose theory of generative grammar revolutionised linguistics in the 1950s and 60s by proposing that humans possess an innate universal capacity for language. His work shifted the discipline from descriptive cataloguing toward the investigation of the mental structures underlying language, making him arguably the most influential linguist of the past century.

## you gotta know

- Proposed that all human languages share a deep "universal grammar" — an innate mental system that constrains what languages can be.
- Introduced the concept of "competence" (the mental system underlying language) versus "performance" (what speakers actually do), refocusing linguistics on mental structures.
- Developed formal theories of syntax (phrase structure grammars, transformational grammar) that model how humans generate and understand sentences.
- Demonstrated that simple descriptive methods (cataloguing sentence structures) cannot explain how finite rules generate infinite utterances — a central puzzle he solved with recursive generation.
- His work instigated a cognitive turn in linguistics and adjacent fields; he is equally famous as a political commentator and social critic.
