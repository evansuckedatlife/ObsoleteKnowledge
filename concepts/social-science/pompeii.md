# Pompeii

## summary

*Pompeii* is a Roman city preserved in volcanic ash after the catastrophic eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 CE. The ash froze the city in time, capturing everyday life—homes, shops, artwork, graffiti, and casts of the inhabitants' final moments—offering an unparalleled window into Roman daily life that no other site provides.

## you gotta know

- Buried by *Vesuvius* in a single day in 79 CE under a pyroclastic flow of superheated gas, ash, and pumice that killed thousands of inhabitants.
- The city lay forgotten for nearly 1,700 years until systematic excavations began in the 18th century, revealing buildings, mosaics, and household objects frozen in place.
- Plaster casts of the dead reveal human forms trapped in their final poses—fleeing, sheltering, praying—providing haunting evidence of the eruption's violence.
- Preserved frescoes and mosaics demonstrate sophisticated Roman art, including the famous *Alexander the Great Mosaic* from the House of the Faun.
- Contains evidence of Roman social hierarchy: grand villas for the wealthy stand near cramped tenements and slave quarters for the poor.
- Graffiti on walls—political slogans, advertisements, love declarations, insults—reveals the literacy and personality of ordinary Romans.
- Named after Clodiola Pulchra, one theory, though its etymology is disputed; the site is Italy's most visited archaeological monument.
