# Xhosa

## summary

*Xhosa* is a Bantu language spoken in South Africa, most notably by the Xhosa people of the Eastern and Western Cape. It is famous among linguists for its inventory of *click consonants*—a rare feature produced by making air suction noises in the mouth—which distinguish it from most other world languages.

## you gotta know

- One of the 11 official languages of South Africa, spoken by roughly 8 million native speakers and 11 million second-language speakers.
- Features three distinct click consonants (*dental*, *lateral*, *palatal*) used in ordinary words, making it phonetically distinctive and challenging for non-native learners.
- A Bantu (Nguni) language with an extensive noun class system; gender is marked on nouns and triggers agreement on adjectives and verbs.
- Has a large inventory of consonants (including the clicks) and a relatively simple vowel system (5 vowels).
- Historically suppressed during the Apartheid era; revitalized as part of post-apartheid language rights and constitutional protections.
- Click consonants are thought to derive from a substrate influence from neighboring Khoisan languages, which are primarily click languages.
