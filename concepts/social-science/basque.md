# Basque

## summary

*Basque* is a language isolate spoken in the Basque Country of northern Spain and southwestern France, with no known linguistic relatives. Its mysterious origins—predating the arrival of Indo-European languages to Western Europe—make it a living relic of Europe's prehistoric linguistic landscape.

## you gotta know

- A language isolate with no documented connection to any other world language family, despite centuries of scholarly investigation.
- Spoken by roughly 700,000 people in the Basque Country (Euskal Herria), a region spanning parts of Spain and France.
- Has an agglutinative morphology and a completely different phoneme inventory from surrounding Romance languages.
- Uses an ergative-absolutive alignment system rather than the nominative-accusative system of Romance and other Indo-European languages.
- Predates the Indo-European language expansion into Western Europe; its origin is debated by linguists (theories range from substrate remnants to trans-Caucasian affinities).
- Underwent revival in the late 20th century following suppression during the Franco regime; now co-official in the Spanish Basque Country.
