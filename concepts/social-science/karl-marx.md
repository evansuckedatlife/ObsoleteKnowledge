# Karl Marx

## summary

*Karl Marx* was a 19th-century German philosopher and economist whose theories of historical materialism and class struggle provided the intellectual foundation for communism and socialism. He argued that capitalism inevitably produces surplus value extracted from workers, driving toward concentration of wealth and its own eventual collapse.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *The Communist Manifesto* (1848, with Friedrich Engels) and *Das Kapital* (1867), the latter a mammoth critique of political economy and capitalist production.
- Developed the theory of *surplus value*: the difference between the value workers produce and the wages they receive, appropriated by capitalists as profit.
- Advocated *historical materialism*: the idea that economic systems and class relations drive the course of history, not ideas or great men.
- Predicted the eventual collapse of capitalism under the weight of its internal contradictions—overproduction, falling profit rates, and worker impoverishment.
- Distinguished between labor-power (what workers sell) and labor (what they perform), a conceptual move that reveals the exploitative nature of wage work.
- Argued that the working class, or *proletariat*, must inevitably revolt and establish a classless, communist society.

## connections

- [[friedrich-engels]] — collaborator and co-author of *The Communist Manifesto*.
- [[adam-smith]] — Marx built his critique partly in dialogue with Smith's economics, refuting the "invisible hand" narrative.
- [[david-ricardo]] — adopted Ricardian rent and value theories as a starting point for his own analysis.
- [[john-kenneth-galbraith]] — 20th-century critic of capitalism who shared Marx's skepticism about unregulated markets, though not his revolutionary conclusions.
