# Claude Lévi-Strauss

## summary

*Claude Lévi-Strauss* (1908–2009) was a French anthropologist who founded *structuralism*, arguing that beneath the surface diversity of cultures lie universal mental structures and logical operations. His analysis of myths, kinship systems, and classification schemes revealed how human minds organize meaning through binary oppositions and transformation rules, influencing not only anthropology but philosophy, literary theory, and linguistics.

## you gotta know

- Developed *structuralism*, the theory that cultural phenomena (myths, kinship, art) encode underlying logical structures reflecting how the human mind categorizes reality.
- Published *The Elementary Structures of Kinship* (1949), a monumental analysis of marriage rules and kinship systems across societies, revealing mathematical patterns in how families organize themselves.
- Conducted fieldwork among the *Bororo* and *Nambikwara* peoples of Brazil; his journals became *Tristes Tropiques* (A World on the Wane), a canonical ethnographic essay.
- Analyzed myths in *The Raw and the Cooked* and subsequent volumes, showing how myths transform universal oppositions (nature/culture, raw/cooked, life/death) into locally meaningful narratives.
- Argued that non-Western peoples are not "primitive" in thought but employ the same logical operations as Western science, displacing evolutionism in anthropology.
- Synthesized insights from linguistics (Saussure, Jakobson) and mathematics to argue that human culture is a language-like system.
