# Estuary English

## summary

*Estuary English* is a modern accent emerging in Southeast England and the Thames Estuary region, representing a blend between Received Pronunciation and London working-class Cockney speech. It has become increasingly dominant among young educated speakers in London and Southeast England since the 1980s, marking a significant shift in prestige norms away from traditional RP.

## you gotta know

- Emerges from mixing RP and Cockney features, adopted by middle-class speakers moving between these traditional poles.
- Glottal stops appear sometimes but inconsistently, less marked than in traditional Cockney (*water* may be *wa'er* or *water*).
- Preserves some RP distinctions lost in Cockney while adopting some Cockney vowel patterns, creating an intermediate system.
- *L*-vocalization: the *l* becomes a vowel sound or disappears in words like *milk* (prounced *miw* or *mi*).
- Rising intonation on statements, borrowed from Australian English and spreading through youth speech patterns.
- Represents a more casual, friendly register compared to formal RP, appealing to younger and middle-class speakers seeking to escape perceived stuffiness.
