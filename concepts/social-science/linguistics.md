# Linguistics

## summary

*Linguistics* is the systematic scientific study of human language—its sounds, structures, meanings, and social context. Emerging as a discipline in the 19th century, it treats language as a natural phenomenon worthy of rigorous analysis rather than mere prescription, revealing universal patterns beneath the surface diversity of the world's languages.

## you gotta know

- Treats language as a system governed by underlying rules, not as a collection of correct/incorrect forms prescribed by grammarians.
- Divides into subfields: *phonetics* (speech sounds), *morphology* (word structure), *syntax* (sentence structure), *semantics* (meaning), *pragmatics* (language use), and *sociolinguistics* (language and society).
- *Ferdinand de Saussure's* early 20th-century distinction between *langue* (the abstract system) and *parole* (actual speech) launched modern linguistics as a scientific discipline.
- Assumes human language is fundamentally discrete, recursive, and compositional—properties not found in animal communication systems.
- *Generative grammar*, developed by *Noam Chomsky*, proposes that all humans are born with an innate language faculty encoding universal principles.
- Seeks both to describe language variation and to uncover universals—patterns true of all human languages.

## connections

- [[noam-chomsky]] — revolutionized the field with generative grammar and the universal language faculty hypothesis.
- [[structural-linguistics]] — early framework for analyzing language as a system of relations rather than isolated items.
- [[generative-grammar]] — modern linguistic theory emphasizing innate principles governing all human language.
- [[ferdinand-de-saussure]] — pioneered scientific treatment of language as a sign system (signifier + signified).
- [[benjamin-lee-whorf]] — controversial figure proposing that language shapes thought (linguistic relativity).
- [[cognitive-science]] — interdisciplinary field integrating linguistics with psychology and neuroscience.
