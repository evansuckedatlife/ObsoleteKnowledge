# Steven Pinker

## summary

*Steven Pinker* is a Canadian-American cognitive psychologist and linguist who has brought generative grammar and cognitive science to popular audiences while contributing to debates about language acquisition, language evolution, and human nature. His accessible writing and synthesising approach have made him one of the most publicly visible linguists of the late 20th and early 21st centuries.

## you gotta know

- Developed the theory that children acquire language through an innate "language instinct" — drawing on Chomskyan universal grammar but grounding it in evolutionary psychology and cognitive development.
- Demonstrated that grammatical errors children make (e.g., "goed," "two mouses") reflect the operation of learned rules, not mere imitation, supporting nativist theories of language acquisition.
- Argued that language is a biological adaptation shaped by natural selection, and that its evolutionary origins remain mysterious but deserve serious investigation.
- Authored bestselling books (*The Language Instinct*, *How the Mind Works*) that popularised cognitive science and linguistics for general readers, becoming a public intellectual.
- His work engages not only linguistics but evolutionary biology, psychology, philosophy of mind, and cultural criticism, attempting to synthesise insights across disciplines.
