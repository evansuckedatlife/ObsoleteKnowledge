# Welsh

## summary

*Welsh* is a Celtic language spoken primarily in Wales and the only surviving Celtic language with a continuous literary tradition stretching back to the 6th century. Despite centuries of English political dominance, Welsh has persisted and undergone a 20th–21st century revival, with significant revival policies and education infrastructure protecting its survival.

## you gotta know

- A Brythonic Celtic language, more distantly related to Irish and Scottish Gaelic than to other European languages.
- The language of Welsh mythology and early medieval Welsh poetry (the *Mabinogion*, *Aneirin*, *Taliesin*); the oldest substantial literature of any Celtic language.
- Spoken by about 870,000 people, with roughly 750,000 claiming some competence; native speakers concentrated in North and West Wales.
- Features a complex system of *initial consonant mutation* (sound changes at the start of words based on grammatical context) unique among modern European languages.
- Welsh place names, legal status, and media presence (S4C, the Welsh-language television channel) reflect modern language policy initiatives.
- Written in the Latin alphabet; the modern standard was established in the early 20th century.
