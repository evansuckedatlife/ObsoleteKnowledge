## summary

*Benjamin Lee Whorf* was an American linguist best known for developing the theory of linguistic relativity, which proposes that the structure of a speaker's language influences or shapes their perception of reality and worldview. His work on Hopi language became the cornerstone of debates over language and thought that continue in cognitive science today.

## you gotta know

- Proposed the "Sapir-Whorf hypothesis" (named for him and his mentor Edward Sapir), claiming that linguistic categories determine or heavily influence thought patterns and cultural behaviour.
- Analysed the Hopi language, arguing that its radically different tense system (absence of past/present/future distinction) reflects and shapes a non-linear perception of time.
- Worked as a fire inspector for an insurance company by day while conducting linguistic research and theory-building — an unusual career path for a prominent linguist.
- Made strong claims about how language structure constrains reality perception: different languages entail different "world views" or cosmologies.
- His work has been both highly influential and controversial; critics argue he overstated the extent to which language determines thought.
