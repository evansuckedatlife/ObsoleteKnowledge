# A.R. Radcliffe-Brown

## summary

*A.R. Radcliffe-Brown* (1881–1955) was a British anthropologist who developed *structural functionalism*, a theoretical framework for analyzing how institutions, rituals, and kinship systems maintain social cohesion and structure. His fieldwork among the *Andaman Islanders* and his influence on British anthropology established kinship analysis and the concept of social structure as central to the discipline.

## you gotta know

- Pioneered the study of *kinship systems* as structural wholes, demonstrating how marriage rules, descent, and obligation patterns organize entire societies.
- Developed *structural functionalism*, arguing that social institutions exist to maintain social integration and must be analyzed in relation to the whole social structure.
- Conducted fieldwork among the *Andaman Islanders*, analyzing their rituals, kinship, and emotion-management practices in *The Andaman Islanders* (1922).
- Distinguished his "structural functionalism" from *Malinowski's* psychological functionalism, emphasizing society's logical structure over individual need-satisfaction.
- Founded the Department of Social Anthropology at Oxford University (1937), training British anthropologists (*Max Gluckman*, *Meyer Fortes*) who dominated mid-20th-century anthropology.
- Argued for a "natural science of society," rejecting history and psychology as explanatory frameworks in favor of comparative structural analysis.
