# Clifford Geertz

## summary

*Clifford Geertz* (1926–2006) was an American anthropologist who pioneered *interpretive anthropology*, arguing that cultural analysis is fundamentally a hermeneutic practice — reading culture as a text to uncover meaning rather than identifying universal laws. His concept of *thick description* and his studies of religion, art, and politics in Indonesia and Morocco demonstrated anthropology as a humanistic interpretive endeavor.

## you gotta know

- Introduced *thick description*, the idea that ethnographic writing must capture not just behavior but the layers of meaning, context, and intention surrounding it.
- Developed *interpretive anthropology* (also called symbolic or hermeneutic anthropology), rejecting positivist models of science in favor of meaning-centered analysis.
- Conducted extended fieldwork in *Indonesia* and *Morocco*, analyzing cockfighting in Bali as a window into Balinese concepts of self and status.
- Analyzed religion not as belief-systems but as cultural meaning-making, influencing how anthropologists approach ritual and cosmology.
- Wrote for wide academic and public audiences; his essays on nationalism, art, and modernity became central to cultural studies and literary theory.
- Insisted on the *observer effect* in ethnography — that the anthropologist's presence, subjective positioning, and interpretive choices shape ethnographic knowledge.
