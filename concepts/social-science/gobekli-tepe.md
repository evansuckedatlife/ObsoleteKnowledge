# Göbekli Tepe

## summary

*Göbekli Tepe* is a Neolithic sanctuary in southeastern Turkey, dating to around 9600 BCE, making it one of the world's oldest known monumental structures. Its massive stone pillars carved with abstract and animal motifs predate pottery, agriculture, and permanent settlements, challenging assumptions that monumental architecture required complex civilization and suggesting that spiritual or social needs motivated the construction.

## you gotta know

- Located in southern Turkey near the border with Syria, discovered in 1994 and systematically excavated beginning in 1995.
- Built around 9600 BCE during the Pre-Pottery Neolithic period, roughly 6,000 years before the pyramids of Egypt.
- Contains multiple circular or oval stone structures with T-shaped limestone pillars standing up to 7 meters tall, arranged in concentric rings.
- The pillars are carved with images of animals (lions, boars, snakes, scorpions, insects) and abstract symbols, the earliest monumental art known.
- Covers roughly 15 acres with multiple construction phases, suggesting repeated rebuilding and expansion over centuries.
- Shows no evidence of domestic habitation—no homes, storage facilities, or permanent settlement—implying it was a ritual or ceremonial center.
- Excavations led by German archaeologist Klaus Schmidt revealed evidence of communal feasts and suggest it functioned as a gathering place for multiple hunter-gatherer bands.
