# Olduvai Gorge

## summary

*Olduvai Gorge* is a ravine in Tanzania containing some of the world's most important fossil evidence of human evolution and early stone tool manufacture, spanning roughly 2 million years of human prehistory. Systematic excavations beginning in 1931 uncovered remains of human ancestors, Homo habilis, and the earliest known stone tools (Oldowan industry), fundamentally reshaping understanding of human origins and the African genesis of our species.

## you gotta know

- Located in northern Tanzania near the Serengeti, a 30-mile-long gorge carved by the Olduvai River through multiple geological layers dating back 2 million years.
- Excavated primarily by the Leakey family—Louis, Mary, and Richard Leakey—whose discoveries revolutionized paleoanthropology over seven decades of fieldwork.
- Contains fossils of *Homo habilis* ("handy man"), the earliest known member of the genus Homo, dated to approximately 2.3 million years ago.
- The Oldowan stone tool industry—simple flakes and choppers made by striking one stone against another—appears in the earliest layers, showing that tool-making preceded modern human anatomy.
- Provided evidence that human evolution occurred in Africa, contrary to earlier European-centric theories, establishing Africa as the birthplace of humankind.
- Multiple hominin species are represented in the fossil record at Olduvai, including *Australopithecus*, *Homo habilis*, and *Homo erectus*, spanning the transition from ape-like ancestors to modern humans.
- The exceptional preservation of fossils and tools occurs because volcanic ash repeatedly blanketed the site, preventing decay and creating clear temporal markers.
