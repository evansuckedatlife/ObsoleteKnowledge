# Machu Picchu

## summary

*Machu Picchu* is a 15th-century Inca citadel set high in the Andes mountains of Peru, built at the height of Inca imperial power. Hidden from Spanish conquistadors during the colonial conquest, it remained unknown to the outside world until 1911, and its remote mountain location and sophisticated stone construction make it one of the most iconic symbols of Inca engineering and civilization.

## you gotta know

- Located at 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level on a mountain ridge above the Urubamba River valley in southeastern Peru.
- Built around 1450 CE during the reign of *Pachacuti*, the Inca emperor who expanded the empire to its greatest extent.
- The Spanish conquistadors never found it during their campaigns in the 16th century, allowing it to remain hidden and undisturbed for nearly 400 years.
- Constructed without mortar—fitted stones so precisely that a knife blade cannot be inserted between them, demonstrating mastery of Inca architecture.
- Contains temples, residences, storage structures, and agricultural terraces built into the mountainside, suggesting it served as a royal estate or sacred site.
- Rediscovered in 1911 by American historian Hiram Bingham, who brought it to international attention and began systematic excavation.
- The Inca Trail, a four-day hiking route connecting nearby Inca sites to Machu Picchu, remains one of the world's most famous treks.
