# Friedrich Hayek

## summary

*Friedrich Hayek* (1899–1992) was an Austrian-born economist and political philosopher who championed free markets, individual liberty, and decentralized knowledge as arguments against central planning and socialism. Winner of the Nobel Prize in Economics (1974), he profoundly influenced modern conservatism and libertarianism, though his ideas were often caricatured and contested.

## you gotta know

- Argued that *centrally planned economies cannot succeed* because no authority can gather and process the dispersed knowledge held by millions of individuals; markets solve this problem through price signals.
- *"The Road to Serfdom"* (1944): even well-intentioned central planning, by concentrating power, tends toward totalitarianism; individual liberty and the rule of law are prerequisites for prosperity.
- Defended laissez-faire capitalism not as perfect but as the system most compatible with human liberty and knowledge generation.
- Critiqued *John Maynard Keynes* and interventionist macroeconomics; argued that artificial stimulus distorts prices and leads to unsustainable booms and busts.
- Influenced *Milton Friedman* and the Chicago School; both defended market economics against Keynesian consensus of mid-20th century.
- Believed that *spontaneous order*—complex social structures arising without central design—is the source of innovation and efficiency (not just in markets but in law and culture).
