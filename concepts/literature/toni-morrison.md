# Toni Morrison

## summary

*Toni Morrison* was an American novelist whose work transformed American literature by centering Black interiority, history, and trauma with lyrical depth and formal innovation. Her novels—especially *Beloved* (1988), which won the Pulitzer Prize—grappled with slavery's psychological legacy and redefined the literary canon.

## you gotta know

- *Beloved* (1988) tells the story of an enslaved mother who kills her child to prevent enslavement, blending realism, magical elements, and stream-of-consciousness to explore trauma and love under slavery.
- Won the Nobel Prize in Literature (1993), becoming the first Black woman to receive this honor and cementing her status as one of the major writers of the 20th century.
- Wrote *Song of Solomon* (1977), exploring a Black man's search for identity and family history across generations, weaving African-American folk mythology and flight imagery.
- Her novels *The Bluest Eye* (1970), *Sula* (1973), and *Tar Baby* (1981) examined internalized racism, female friendship, class, and the moral complexities of Black life.
- Worked as an editor and mentor, championing African-American writers and shaping the literary landscape from within the publishing industry.
- Synthesized modernist techniques (non-linear narrative, mythic resonance, symbolic density) with Black oral traditions and blues aesthetics.

- [[langston-hughes]] — literary predecessor whose jazz-poetry influence echoes in Morrison's prose rhythms.
