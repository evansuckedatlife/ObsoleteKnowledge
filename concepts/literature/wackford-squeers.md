# Wackford Squeers

## summary

**Wackford Squeers** is the brutish headmaster of Dotheboys Hall in *Nicholas Nickleby*, a Yorkshire boarding school where he systematically starves, beats, and exploits neglected children sent to him by parents eager to be rid of them. He embodies Dickens' rage at institutional cruelty and the abuse of the powerless.

## you gotta know

- Operates Dotheboys Hall ostensibly to educate boys but actually to work them to exhaustion while providing minimal food and maximum punishment.
- Admits only unwanted children—"illegitimate" or burdensome sons—whose parents pay to be rid of them, ensuring no one will inquire into their welfare.
- Employs a philosophy of instruction through starvation and the cane; his few "lessons" are designed to extract labor value from pupils rather than impart knowledge.
- His own son Wackford Jr. is spoiled and cruel, suggesting Squeers' evil permeates his family circle.
- Becomes the personal antagonist of Nicholas Nickleby when Nicholas takes employment at the school and attempts to protect the boys.
- Eventually exposed, thrashed by Nicholas, prosecuted, and imprisoned—a rare instance of explicit justice in Dickens' world.
