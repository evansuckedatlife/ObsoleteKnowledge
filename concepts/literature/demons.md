# Demons

## summary

*Demons* (alternately *The Possessed*, 1872) is Dostoevsky's caustic political novel depicting a revolutionary cell infiltrating a provincial Russian town, exposing the spiritual emptiness and destructive amorality of radical ideologies. The brilliant manipulator *Pyotr Verkhovensky* orchestrates murder and conspiracy to unmask the pretensions of socialist revolution, while the passive intellectual *Nikolai Stavrogin* becomes an unwitting idol for their nihilist movement.

## you gotta know

- The title alludes to the biblical story of demons entering swine; Dostoevsky frames radical politics as spiritual possession, a form of demonic madness rather than rational reform.
- *Pyotr Verkhovensky* is a Machiavellian schemer who fabricates a secret society and orchestrates murders to advance his vision of destruction and remake Russian society through terror.
- *Nikolai Stavrogin*, brilliant and amoral, becomes the "great man" around whom the radicals gather; his confession and suicide expose the void at the heart of the conspiracy.
- The novel features a famous *wreckers' manifesto* and depicts how cynical manipulation can exploit genuine social discontent for destructive ends.
- Dostoevsky drew inspiration from the *Nechayev conspiracy* (1869), a real revolutionary cell and murder that scandalized Russia.
- Published serially in *The Russian Messenger* and was banned in Russia until 1906; its political pessimism made it controversial across the ideological spectrum.
