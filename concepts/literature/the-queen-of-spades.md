# The Queen of Spades

## summary

Pushkin's *The Queen of Spades* is a supernatural novella of gambling obsession and buried secrets. A young officer becomes convinced that an elderly countess possesses knowledge of a hidden card sequence that guarantees fortune, leading him to pursue and ultimately terrorize her for the secret—only to discover that fate and guilt have their own cards to play.

## you gotta know

- *German*, a relatively poor officer, overhears rumors that the Countess, an elderly woman of high rank, once possessed a formula for winning at cards using three secret numbers.
- Obsessed with achieving fortune through this secret, he engineers an introduction to the Countess's young ward *Lizaveta*, intending to use her to gain access to the Countess's chambers.
- He enters the Countess's room at night and, when she refuses to reveal the secret, confronts her with a pistol; the shock of the encounter kills her.
- German attends the Countess's funeral where he seems to witness her spirit communicating the three winning cards to him through a mysterious glance.
- The next night, he uses the numbers to gamble and wins; on the final hand, the card he believes to be the Ace reveals itself as the Queen of Spades—the Countess's card.
- German loses everything and is driven mad, haunted by the image of the dead Countess and the Queen of Spades, his obsession consuming him entirely.
- The tale is a morality play on greed and the dangers of bargaining with dark forces, rendered through Pushkin's precise, atmospheric prose.
