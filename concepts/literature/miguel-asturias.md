# Miguel Ángel Asturias

## summary

*Miguel Ángel Asturias* (1899–1974) was a Guatemalan novelist, poet, and diplomat whose work draws on Mayan mythology and the political violence of Central America to create hallucinatory, dreamlike fictions that blur the boundary between realism and the mythic. A pioneer of magical realism before the term was coined, Asturias used indigenous Mesoamerican thought and surrealist technique to expose dictatorship, colonialism, and the dispossession of indigenous peoples, making his novels weapons of cultural and political resistance.

## you gotta know

- *El Señor Presidente* (1946) — a baroque, nightmarish novel set in an unnamed Central American dictatorship, depicting surveillance, torture, and paranoia through fragmented, hallucinatory prose that mirrors the protagonist's psychological dissolution.
- *Men of Maize* (1949) — an experimental novel drawing on Mayan creation mythology and the relationship between indigenous peoples and the land, employing non-linear narrative and indigenous Quiché perspectives.
- Nobel Prize in Literature (1967) — awarded for his vivid literary achievement, rooted in strong national traits and individual authorial inventiveness.
- Influenced by surrealism and his study of Mayan texts and culture; his work is steeped in indigenous mythology and the history of indigenous dispossession.
- Served as ambassador and diplomat, representing Guatemala internationally while remaining engaged with its political turmoil and the struggle for indigenous rights.
- Pioneer of *magical realism* in Latin American letters, anticipating the movement's global influence; his blend of the mythic and political anticipates García Márquez.
