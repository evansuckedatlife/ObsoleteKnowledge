# How Much Land Does a Man Need?

## summary

Tolstoy's *How Much Land Does a Man Need?* is a folk parable about insatiable greed told with devastating simplicity. A peasant, offered the chance to acquire as much land as he can walk around in a single day, embarks on an increasingly desperate journey, only to discover that the only land a man truly needs is enough to be buried in.

## you gotta know

- *Pahom*, a poor peasant, dreams of owning land and believes that if only he had sufficient acreage, he would never want for anything and could live in comfort.
- He acquires some land through hard work but, dissatisfied, learns of the Bashkirs, a distant people who supposedly offer vast tracts of land for a pittance.
- The Bashkir elder offers him a bargain: any land he can circumscribe on foot in a single day is his, for a small fee.
- Pahom begins walking at dawn, driven by the magnitude of possibility, striding farther and farther across the steppe as the sun descends.
- As evening approaches and he realizes he has overextended himself, he runs desperately back to the starting point to complete the circuit before sundown.
- He collapses upon reaching his destination—dead from exertion, having claimed vast acreage that he will never possess or enjoy.
- His servant buries him in a six-foot plot of earth, answering the story's title question with grim irony.
- The tale is structured as moral fable, tracing the arc of human desire and its inevitable terminus.
