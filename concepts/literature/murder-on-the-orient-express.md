## summary

Agatha Christie's 1934 novel features detective Hercule Poirot aboard a snowbound luxury train where a wealthy American is found murdered in his locked cabin. With no escape possible and a train full of suspects, Poirot must unravel a murder with multiple motives and solve a puzzle of extraordinary complexity.

## you gotta know

- An American businessman, Samuel Ratchett (actually a criminal in hiding), is murdered in a locked compartment aboard the Orient Express.
- The train becomes snowbound in the Balkans, trapping all suspects on board with no possibility of escape or outside interference.
- Poirot examines each of the twelve suspects, discovering that all have plausible motives and opportunities for the murder.
- The investigation reveals that Ratchett was involved in a kidnapping and murder years prior, for which he was never punished.
- Poirot deduces that all twelve suspects conspired together to murder Ratchett, each striking a blow in revenge for the victim they knew.
- The solution is a democratic murder: a conspiracy of justice rather than an individual crime.
- Poirot presents multiple possible solutions to the Turkish police and train officials, allowing them to accept the most convenient lie.
