## summary

*A Tale of Two Cities*, serialized from 1859 to 1860, is Dickens's historical novel of the French Revolution, set in London and Paris. It weaves personal love and redemption into a cataclysmic historical backdrop, with the famous opening "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times" and the closing sacrifice that "is a far, far better thing."

## you gotta know

- Opens with the famous line "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times," setting the paradox of revolution and hope.
- *Sydney Carton*, the dissolute lawyer who resembles the French aristocrat *Charles Darnay*, redeems himself by dying on the guillotine in Darnay's place with the words "It is a far, far better thing that I do, than I have ever done."
- *Charles Darnay* renounces his French title and oppressive heritage to live quietly in England; he represents the conscience of the aristocracy.
- *Lucie Manette*, the heroine, is the daughter of a political prisoner long confined to the Bastille; her love motivates both Carton and Darnay.
- *Madame Defarge*, a female revolutionary who knits coded messages encoding the names of those to be executed, represents the implacable violence of the Revolution.
- Set against the Terror and the storming of the Bastille, the novel balances individual redemption against historical violence.
