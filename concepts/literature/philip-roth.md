# Philip Roth

## summary

*Philip Roth* (1933–2018) was an American novelist whose fiction explores Jewish identity, sexuality, and the mythologies of America with provocative irony and metafictional sophistication. His work ranges from realist family sagas to elaborate satirical fantasies, but all turn on the collision between individual desire and collective expectation. He is one of the most decorated American writers of the late 20th century.

## you gotta know

- *Portnoy's Complaint* (1969) — scandalous comic monologue of a Jewish man's sexual neurosis and oedipal rebellion, became a cultural watershed.
- *The Plot Against America* (2004) — alternate history where Charles Lindbergh becomes US President, exploring antisemitism and American authoritarianism.
- *American Pastoral* (1997) — tragic bildungsroman of a Jewish businessman undone by his daughter's radicalism, won the Pulitzer Prize.
- *Zuckerman* tetralogy (1976–1988) — novels featuring an alter-ego writer tracking American obsessions across decades.
- Won the National Book Award twice and was shortlisted for the Pulitzer four times; one of literature's most nominated figures.
- Pioneered metafictional play in American realism—characters debate the author's motives, stories within stories destabilize truth claims.
- Wrote about sexuality and bodily existence with unprecedented explicitness in literary fiction, reshaping the bounds of American letters.
