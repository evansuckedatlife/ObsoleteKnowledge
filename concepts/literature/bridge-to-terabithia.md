## summary

*Bridge to Terabithia* is Katherine Paterson's novel about Jess Aarons, a shy, artistic boy who forms a deep friendship with a free-spirited new neighbor, Leslie Burke, and together they create an imaginary kingdom in the woods called Terabithia. When Leslie drowns in a tragic accident, Jess must find the strength to continue their shared world and understand that imagination is a form of immortality.

## you gotta know

- Jess (Jess) is a lonely, introspective fifth-grader who feels invisible and misunderstood at home and school, harboring a secret love of drawing and music that his family deems impractical.
- Leslie moves into the house next door and immediately establishes herself as unconventional, brilliant, and kind; she opens Jess's world to imagination and friendship.
- The two children create Terabithia, a fantasy realm in the woods that they enter by swinging across a creek on a rope, filled with imaginary kingdoms, quests, and moral lessons.
- Leslie's sudden death by drowning while Jess is away is the novel's emotional and moral center—it forces Jess to grieve, to honor Leslie's memory, and to recognize that he has been transformed by her.
- Won the Newbery Medal in 1978, celebrated for its honest treatment of grief and the redemptive power of imagination and friendship.
- The novel explores how imagination is not escapism but a way of understanding and surviving reality; it is also a form of spiritual legacy.
- Jess's final decision to build a bridge to Terabithia for his younger sister May Belle suggests that he will carry Leslie's gift of imagination forward into his life.
