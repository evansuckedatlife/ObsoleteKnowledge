## summary

*A Christmas Carol*, published in 1843, is Dickens's novella of spiritual redemption: the miserly *Ebenezer Scrooge* is visited by three ghosts on Christmas Eve and transformed from a heartless miser into a generous, warm-hearted man. Its message of charity, social responsibility, and personal transformation has become the template for Christmas literature.

## you gotta know

- *Ebenezer Scrooge*, a wealthy businessman who famously declares "Bah, humbug!" to Christmas and charity, is the protagonist.
- Visited by *Jacob Marley's ghost* (his dead business partner), then the *Ghost of Christmas Past*, *Ghost of Christmas Present*, and *Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come*.
- Each spirit shows Scrooge moments of his life and their consequences—his lost love, the happiness he denies others, and the hollow death awaiting him.
- *Tiny Tim*, the sickly but cheerful son of Bob Cratchit (Scrooge's clerk), becomes the moral touchstone for human kindness and mortality.
- Concludes with Scrooge's complete transformation: he becomes generous, adopts Tiny Tim, and is redeemed through awareness of his own mortality.
- Established the modern *Christmas ghost story* as a literary genre and became the most adapted literary work of the 19th century.
