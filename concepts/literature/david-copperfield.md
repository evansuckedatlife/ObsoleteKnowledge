## summary

*David Copperfield*, serialized from 1849 to 1850, is Dickens's semi-autobiographical novel following a boy from birth through adulthood, narrated by David himself. Tracing his journey from childhood suffering through betrayal and self-discovery, it is both a bildungsroman and a deeply personal statement about resilience, memory, and the shaping of character.

## you gotta know

- Narrated by the adult *David Copperfield* reflecting on his childhood, beginning with his birth and early mistreatment after his father's death.
- *Peggotty*, his faithful nurse, and her family provide warmth; her brother *Dan Peggotty* becomes a father figure.
- *Uriah Heep*, Copperfield's rival and the novel's villain, is a hypocritical, obsequious clerk whose false humility conceals ruthless ambition.
- *Agnes Wickfield*, the gentle, moral heroine, becomes David's ultimate love and wife, representing his emotional grounding.
- Features *Steerforth*, David's charismatic friend whose seduction of Little Em'ly (Dan Peggotty's daughter) brings tragedy and redemption.
- Dickens drew heavily on his own childhood trauma, making it his most introspective and autobiographical major novel.
