## summary

**Bill Sikes** is the brutal, violent burglar in *Oliver Twist* who serves as Fagin's enforcer and Nancy's abuser, embodying brute force and animal cruelty within the criminal underworld. His murder of Nancy and subsequent flight and self-destruction form the moral climax of the novel.

## you gotta know

- A powerful, muscular criminal who specializes in housebreaking and uses violence to maintain control over the gang and his paramour Nancy.
- Keeps a vicious mastiff as a constant companion, a reflection of his own brutish nature.
- Beats Nancy to death in a rage when he discovers she has informed the authorities of their hideout, committing the novel's most horrific act of cruelty.
- Pursued by guilt and a vengeful mob after Nancy's murder; haunted by visions of her bloodied face, he effectively engineers his own death by accident while attempting to escape.
- Represents Dickens' conviction that violence and cruelty inevitably generate their own destruction and retribution.
- In later theatrical adaptations, Sikes' dog famously dies with him in the famous scene of his flight across rooftops.
