# Margaret Atwood

## summary

*Margaret Atwood* is a Canadian author whose speculative works—particularly *The Handmaid's Tale*—articulate feminist anxieties about power, reproduction, and the fragility of rights in patriarchal societies. Her narratives ground futuristic scenarios in plausible extrapolation from contemporary politics, using speculative extrapolation to illuminate the violence already embedded in social systems. She has been central to revitalizing the novel as a vehicle for political and philosophical critique.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *The Handmaid's Tale* (1985), imagining a theocratic military dictatorship, *Gilead*, where fertile women are enslaved to bear children for the ruling class—a novel of bodily autonomy and state violence.
- Created the *MaddAddam* trilogy (starting 2003), depicting bioengineered futures marked by ecological collapse and genetic experimentation, exploring human survivalism and ingenuity.
- Authored *The Blind Assassin* (2000), a metafictional family history woven with a speculative narrative-within-the-narrative, interrogating female voice and historical erasure.
- Her speculative premises are rigidly grounded in extrapolation from existing institutions and power structures—no fantastical elements, only the logical extension of present-day trends.
- Merges literary ambition with genre facility, proving that speculative fiction could achieve canonical literary status and critical recognition.
- Her work is centrally concerned with the female body as a contested site of power—reproduction, sexuality, agency, and autonomy under regimes of control.
