# Norman Mailer

## summary

*Norman Mailer* (1923–2007) was an American novelist, essayist, and provocateur whose fiction and nonfiction tackle American power, sexuality, violence, and the masculine ego with unflinching intensity. His work spans novels of war and politics to innovative narrative nonfiction; he was equally celebrated and reviled for his uncompromising style and contentious public persona. He remains one of the most influential American writers of the postwar period.

## you gotta know

- *The Naked and the Dead* (1948) — debut war novel set in the Pacific, massive bestseller establishing him as a major voice.
- *The Armies of the Night* (1968) — pioneering work of new journalism about the 1967 March on the Pentagon, won the Pulitzer Prize.
- *Ancient Evenings* (1983) — sprawling metaphysical novel set in ancient Egypt, demonstrates his ambition and formal risk-taking.
- Won the Pulitzer Prize twice (1969, 1980); National Book Award and numerous other recognitions.
- His essays shaped American cultural criticism; *Advertisements for Myself* (1959) collects provocative pieces on literature, power, and art.
- Wrote about sexuality and violence with unprecedented candour; his testosterone-soaked masculinity was both his subject and his style.
- Lived publicly and controversially; his life and work were inseparable—the author as protagonist and sage.
