## summary

Mary Shelley's 1818 novel is the foundational text of Gothic horror and science fiction, telling the story of Victor Frankenstein, a young scientist consumed by ambition who assembles and animates a creature from dead flesh. Narrated through nested letters and confessions, the novel follows Victor's descent into obsession, guilt, and madness as his creation—intelligent, eloquent, and desperately lonely—seeks revenge against his creator by destroying everything Victor holds dear.

## you gotta know

- Victor Frankenstein becomes obsessed with the secrets of life and death, and succeeds in constructing and animating a living being from assembled human tissue.
- The Creature, though physically hideous, possesses intelligence and sensitivity; he learns language and develops a conscience while being rejected and hunted by every human he encounters.
- Victor abandons his creation immediately after animation, unable to bear the sight of what he has made, traumatizing the Creature and setting in motion the tragedy to follow.
- The Creature, seeking companionship and a mate, demands that Victor create a female companion; when Victor destroys his work, the Creature vows revenge.
- The Creature systematically destroys Victor's life: killing his closest friend, his bride on her wedding night, and his father, leaving Victor alone and obsessed with hunting the Creature.
- Shelley frames the narrative as a rescue—a ship captain pulls Victor from the Arctic ice—creating a story-within-a-story that emphasizes the power of confession and testimony.
- The novel questions who is truly the monster: the Creature, who learns empathy despite his appearance, or Victor, whose selfishness and moral cowardice doom everyone around him.
