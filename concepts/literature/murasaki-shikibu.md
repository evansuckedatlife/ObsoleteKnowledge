# Murasaki Shikibu

## summary

**Murasaki Shikibu** (973–1025 CE) was a noblewoman of the Heian court who authored *The Tale of Genji*, widely regarded as the world's first psychological novel. Serving as a lady-in-waiting and tutor to the imperial family, she crafted a portrait of court life, romantic relationships, and the inner consciousness of her characters with unprecedented depth and subtlety.

## you gotta know

- Authored *The Tale of Genji* (~1010), an epic narrative poem in 54 chapters depicting the life and romantic adventures of Genji, a son of an ancient Japanese emperor.
- *The Tale of Genji* is often credited as the world's first novel due to its psychological realism, development of character interiority, and sophisticated narrative structure predating European novels by centuries.
- Employed *kana* (Japanese phonetic script) rather than *kanji* (Chinese characters) for literary expression, breaking with aristocratic convention and establishing a literary tradition for women writers.
- Her *Diary* (*Murasaki Shikibu Nikki*) is an invaluable firsthand account of Heian court politics, aesthetic practices, and the social constraints on women of her rank and era.
- Served as tutor (*governess*) to the young empress, granting her intimate access to imperial family dynamics and courtly ceremony—material woven into her narratives.
- Lived during the apex of *Heian culture*, an era of Japanese aesthetic refinement, literary flowering, and the rise of women's literary voices in the vernacular.
