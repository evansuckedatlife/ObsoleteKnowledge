# Maya Angelou

## summary

*Maya Angelou* (1928–2014) was an American autobiographer, poet, and performer known for *I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings* (1969), a bestselling memoir about overcoming poverty, racism, trauma, and mutism. Her lyrical, resilient voice and commitment to Black dignity and healing made her one of the most beloved and influential writers of her generation.

## you gotta know

- *I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings* (1969) recounts her childhood in the segregated South, her temporary mutism after witnessing trauma, and her journey toward voice and self-acceptance.
- The title draws from Paul Laurence Dunbar's poem and uses the metaphor of a caged bird struggling toward freedom—applicable to her own and her race's experience under oppression.
- Published six volumes of autobiography continuing her life story across decades, making autobiography itself a form of resistance and witness.
- Worked as a dancer, actress, streetcar conductor, and journalist before becoming a writer, embodying the multifaceted resilience her work celebrates.
- Her poetry collections, including *Just Give Me a Cool Drink of Water 'fore I Diiie* (1971), brought lyrical depth and vernacular language to meditations on love, racism, and redemption.
- Became a cultural icon and public intellectual, using her platform to advocate for civil rights, women's dignity, and the power of literature.
