# A Raisin in the Sun

## summary

Lorraine Hansberry's 1959 play follows the African American *Younger family* in Chicago as they await a life insurance check following their patriarch's death. The windfall becomes a source of hope and conflict as family members pursue competing dreams—education, business, dignity—against the backdrop of segregation and limited opportunity.

## you gotta know

- The first major Broadway play written by a Black woman and featuring an all-Black cast; it won the New York Drama Critics' Circle Award and made Hansberry the youngest winner at the time.
- The title comes from Langston Hughes' poem "Harlem" ("What happens to a dream deferred? / Does it dry up like a raisin in the sun?").
- The father's dream was to own a business; his widow Lena wants to buy a house in a white neighborhood; his son Walter wants to invest in a liquor store.
- The family's neighbor *George Murchison*, a wealthy, educated Black man, rejects his African heritage and assimilationist values—a counterpoint to Walter's hunger for wealth.
- The family's move to an all-white neighborhood becomes both a personal victory and an act of resistance against housing discrimination.
- Each family member must reconcile their individual aspirations with collective survival and dignity.
