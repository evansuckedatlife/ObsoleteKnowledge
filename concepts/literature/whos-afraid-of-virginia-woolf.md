# Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?

## summary

Edward Albee's 1962 play depicts a brutal evening between two married couples—the intellectual *George* and his acerbic, alcohol-fueled wife *Martha*, and their younger guests *Nick* and *Honey*. Under the guise of late-night games and intellectual sparring, the play exposes the lies, cruelty, and desperate longing that bind marriages and destroy friendships.

## you gotta know

- Won the Pulitzer Prize for Drama in 1963; the 1966 film adaptation with Elizabeth Taylor is equally iconic and earned 13 Oscar nominations.
- The play contains shocking profanity for its era and raw sexual language; it was controversial and helped break censorship barriers in American theater.
- Martha's famous line, "What a dump!", opens the play and sets the tone for her savage, witty contempt for her husband's passivity.
- The evening is structured around games: "Get the Guests," "Humiliate the Host," and "Bringing Up Baby"—each one a weapon in the marital warfare.
- George and Martha have invented a fantasy child who never existed; its fictional status becomes a symbol of their emotional void and mutual delusion.
- Albee uses the title (a reference to Virginia Woolf, not a person present) as a game itself—the characters are afraid to confront the emptiness they've created.
