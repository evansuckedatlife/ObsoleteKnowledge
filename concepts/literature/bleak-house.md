# Bleak House

## summary

*Bleak House*, serialized from 1852 to 1853, is Dickens's darkest novel, weaving together multiple narratives in a sprawling indictment of the law, class, and social paralysis. Alternating between third-person narration and the voice of *Esther Summerson*, it presents a world entangled by the endless lawsuit *Jarndyce v. Jarndyce*, where the law destroys lives as thoroughly as any physical malady.

## you gotta know

- Centered on *Jarndyce v. Jarndyce*, a legal case in Chancery that has consumed so much time and money that the disputed estate is nearly worthless—a metaphor for English bureaucracy.
- *Esther Summerson* is the narrator and heroine, a patient, dutiful woman raised as a foundling, secretly the illegitimate daughter of *Lady Dedlock* and *Captain Hawdon*.
- *John Jarndyce*, the protagonist, is a kind man ensnared by the suit; his home, "Bleak House," becomes a refuge for the wounded.
- *Lady Dedlock* hides her past and dread of discovery, while her husband (Sir Leicester Dedlock) represents the ossified aristocracy.
- *Harold Skimpole*, a charming parasitic aesthete, represents the moral bankruptcy of those indifferent to others' suffering.
- The novel's famous feature is its dual narration: Esther's intimate voice alternates with an omniscient narrator's sardonic condemnation of institutions.
