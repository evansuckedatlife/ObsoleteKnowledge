# Hard Times

## summary

*Hard Times*, serialized in 1854, is Dickens's shortest novel and his only explicitly political one, set in the industrial town of Coketown. It attacks utilitarian philosophy and industrial capitalism through the story of *Thomas Gradgrind*, a man who raises his children on facts alone, and the working-class *Stephens*, whose suffering the system ignores.

## you gotta know

- *Thomas Gradgrind*, a schoolmaster and entrepreneur, believes education consists of facts alone: "Now, what I want is Facts"; his system crushes imagination and feeling.
- *Sissy Jupe*, the circus performer's daughter, represents the imagination and human warmth that Gradgrind's philosophy destroys; she becomes the novel's moral center.
- *Louisa Gradgrind*, Gradgrind's daughter, is married off to the utilitarian *Bounderby* and becomes an emblem of Victorian female sacrifice to abstract principle.
- *Stephen Blackpool*, a mill worker, suffers exploitation, injustice, and social indifference; his tragedy shows the moral bankruptcy of systems that see humans as productive units.
- *Josiah Bounderby*, a pompous industrialist, embodies capitalist self-deception and the abuse of workers.
- The shortest of Dickens's major novels, written with pointed anger at Benthamite utilitarianism and industrial-age dehumanization.
