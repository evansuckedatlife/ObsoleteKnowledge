# Fagin

## summary

**Fagin** is the calculating criminal mastermind in *Oliver Twist* who directs a gang of child pickpockets from a squalid London hideout. A Jewish criminal of legendary cunning, he exploits orphans' vulnerability and hunger to steal, then shields himself behind them when the law closes in.

## you gotta know

- Operates a school of pickpockets—training orphaned children to steal handkerchiefs, watches, and purses from unsuspecting Londoners.
- Lodges in a dilapidated house where his pupils return their stolen goods; he pays them in scraps of food and false camaraderie.
- Calls himself "the Artful Dodger's master" and rules through a mixture of paternal affection and calculated fear.
- When cornered by magistrates and the law, attempts to save himself by betraying his own gang, but is ultimately captured and hanged.
- His characterization is marked by Dickens' use of stereotypical Jewish traits—though the novel ultimately condemns the system that produced him as much as his crimes.
- Iconic phrase: "You are made a member of the gang, old boy," spoken to new recruits who become his victims rather than his partners.
