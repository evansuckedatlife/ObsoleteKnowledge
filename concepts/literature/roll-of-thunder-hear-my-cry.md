# Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry

## summary

*Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry* is Mildred D. Taylor's novel about the Logan family, a resilient Black household in 1930s Mississippi, as they navigate racial segregation, economic hardship, and the struggle to maintain dignity and family integrity. Told through the eyes of Cassie Logan, the story reveals both the quiet courage and the devastating realities of Black life in the Jim Crow South.

## you gotta know

- Cassie is a spirited nine-year-old girl who becomes increasingly aware of the injustices and humiliations of segregation as the novel progresses, especially after a harrowing encounter with white racism in a store.
- Her parents, Mary and David Logan, own their land—rare and precious for a Black family—and struggle to keep it while resisting both economic exploitation and social pressure to submit to racial hierarchy.
- A subplot involves the family's moral response when a white man dies in a confrontation with one of their neighbors; the Logans refuse to participate in or condone the violence, but also refuse to betray their community.
- Won the Newbery Medal in 1977, becoming a cornerstone work in the canon of African American children's literature.
- Taylor uses the interior perspective of a Black child to center Black agency, humanity, and familial love rather than positioning Black characters as victims narrated by outsiders.
- The novel is part of a series featuring the Logan family, expanding the emotional and historical scope across multiple books.
- Themes include the price of self-respect, the bonds of family and community, and the ways love sustains resistance to dehumanization.
