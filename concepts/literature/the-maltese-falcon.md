# The Maltese Falcon

## summary

Dashiell Hammett's 1930 novel introduces Sam Spade, a hard-boiled San Francisco private detective caught in a tangle of lies and greed when a mysterious woman hires him to recover a statue. The novel pioneered the American hard-boiled detective archetype: cynical, morally ambiguous, and untrusting in a world of deception.

## you gotta know

- Sam Spade's partner Miles Archer is murdered at the story's outset, setting Spade on a collision course with multiple factions seeking the Maltese Falcon.
- The Maltese Falcon is a priceless statuette, studded with jewels, whose ownership has been disputed across centuries and continents.
- Multiple shadowy figures—Brigid O'Shaughnessy, Casper Gutman, the Levantine—pursue Spade, each claiming the falcon or hiring him to find it.
- Spade navigates a web of betrayals and double-crosses where no one can be trusted, including his client Brigid.
- The investigation reveals that Brigid herself murdered Spade's partner Archer, framing Spade's involvement.
- Spade turns Brigid over to the police despite their romantic entanglement, choosing professional ethics over personal desire.
- The falcon itself proves to be a copy; the real treasure remains lost, leaving Spade with only the knowledge of his own integrity.
