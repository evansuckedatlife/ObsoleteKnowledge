# An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge

## summary

Ambrose Bierce's *An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge* is a short story set during the American Civil War, depicting a condemned prisoner about to be executed by hanging from a bridge over a creek. In the story's second and third sections, the prisoner escapes the noose, flees through a surreal landscape, and returns to his home and family, seemingly having cheated death. The stunning twist reveals that the entire escape sequence occurred in the prisoner's mind during the moment of execution—he did not survive. The twist is a meditation on consciousness, mortality, and the mind's capacity to construct elaborate fantasies in moments of trauma.

## you gotta know

- Peyton Farquhar, a civilian and Confederate sympathiser, is captured and condemned to execution by Union forces during the Civil War.
- As he stands on the bridge with the noose around his neck, the rope is cut; he falls into the creek and swims to safety.
- The second half of the story narrates his escape in vivid, dreamlike detail—he travels through a dangerous landscape, evading Union soldiers pursuing him.
- After hours of struggle, exhausted and desperate, Farquhar reaches his home. He embraces his wife at the threshold.
- The final twist: the entire escape was a hallucination lasting only seconds in the moment of his execution. He did not survive.
- The final line is brutally ironic: Farquhar's thoughts return to the moment of hanging, his neck broken, his vision darkening.
- Published in 1890; considered a masterpiece of the short-story form and a pioneering use of stream-of-consciousness technique.
