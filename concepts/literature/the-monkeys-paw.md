# The Monkey's Paw

## summary

William Wymark Jacobs's 1902 short story presents a supernatural artifact that grants wishes with devastating consequences. When a working-class family acquires a monkey's paw with the power to fulfill desires, their wish for financial security comes true—but at the cost of their son's life in a machinery accident. The climax pivots on the mother's desperate wish to resurrect her son and the father's final act of mercy to undo it, exploring the horror of consequences that no one can escape.

## you gotta know

- The White family receives the monkey's paw as a curiosity from a friend returned from India, who warns them of its power and the fate it has brought to every previous owner.
- The father wishes for £200 to pay off the mortgage and learn their son Herbert is dead, the compensation paid by his employer being precisely £200.
- The mother becomes obsessed with resurrecting Herbert through a second wish on the paw, while the father recognizes that ten days of burial have rendered his son unrecognizable.
- The father's final wish undoes the resurrection, and Herbert is allowed to return to the grave, sparing both his parents and himself further suffering.
- The paw grants exactly three wishes: the White family's use of all three creates a tragic arc from greed through grief to mercy.
- Jacobs masterfully invokes supernatural horror while keeping the paw's actual mechanism deliberately vague—the magic is secondary to the human psychology it triggers.
- The story's ending, in which mercy and acceptance are the only escape from the curse, marks it as a meditation on grief rather than a simple tale of wishing.
