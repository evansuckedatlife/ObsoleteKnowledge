# Ebenezer Scrooge

## summary

**Ebenezer Scrooge** is the miser and misanthrope in *A Christmas Carol* who is transformed by supernatural visitation on Christmas Eve, moving from callous indifference to genuine charity and human connection. His redemption arc—from denial to acceptance to transformation—defines the novella and has become the archetypal conversion narrative in English literature.

## you gotta know

- A London moneylender obsessed with profit and contemptuous of the poor, uttering the phrase "If they would rather die, they had better do it, and decrease the surplus population."
- Haunted by the ghosts of Christmas Past, Present, and Yet to Come, forced to witness the consequences of his greed and the fate awaiting him if unchanged.
- Particularly moved by the vision of Tiny Tim's death and the revelation that his actions (or indifference) contribute to the suffering of innocents.
- Awakens on Christmas morning transformed, becoming a second father to Tiny Tim and raising the wages of his clerk Bob Cratchit.
- His phrase "Bah, humbug" becomes iconic shorthand for commercial cynicism and the rejection of human sentiment.
- Represents Dickens' belief in the possibility of moral redemption through confrontation with consequence and the demands of compassion.
