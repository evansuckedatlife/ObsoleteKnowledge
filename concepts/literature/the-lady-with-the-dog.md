## summary

*The Lady with the Dog* is Chekhov's meditation on unexpected love, following a bored married man who encounters a woman at a seaside resort and begins a passionate affair. Through their clandestine meetings and evolving emotional connection, the story explores how authentic feeling can emerge from the most conventional lives, leaving both lovers trapped between desire and social obligation.

## you gotta know

- *Dmitri Gurov*, a jaded Moscow banker in his forties, is accustomed to affairs and views women as distractions; he is deeply unhappy in his marriage.
- He meets *Anna Sergeyevna*, a young woman traveling with her Pomeranian dog, at the resort in Yalta and they quickly become intimate.
- The relationship begins as a diversion but deepens into genuine emotion as they share confidences and discover unexpected tenderness.
- When Anna departs for her provincial home, Gurov realizes for the first time that he has experienced real love, not mere desire.
- He travels to her town and they resume their affair in secret, meeting in hotel rooms and separated by the unbridgeable gap of their marriages.
- The ending is suspended between hope and resignation—they speak of a new life together but remain bound to their existing commitments, their love both absolute and impossible.
- Chekhov's sparse, understated prose captures the interior emotional landscape of quietly desperate people.
