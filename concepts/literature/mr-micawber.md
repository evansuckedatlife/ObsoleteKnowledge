## summary

**Mr. Micawber** is the perpetually optimistic but hopelessly imprudent father figure in *David Copperfield*, a man of grand rhetoric and elaborate speech who is forever on the brink of financial ruin yet remains buoyantly confident that "something will turn up." He embodies the comic tragedy of self-delusion and wasted potential.

## you gotta know

- Moves in and out of debtors' prison throughout the novel, yet maintains unflagging faith that fortune will reverse.
- Speaks in elaborate, circumlocutious sentences filled with classical references and self-important language that obscures his actual helplessness.
- Frequently borrows money he cannot repay, convinced each new scheme will be his salvation—until it inevitably collapses.
- Works as David's lodger in London and later becomes entangled with the villainous Uriah Heep before finding redemption in Australia.
- His famous phrase: "Something will turn up," repeated despite mounting evidence to the contrary, captures his delusional optimism.
- Eventually emigrates to Australia where he supposedly finds success, suggesting that Dickens reserves ultimate redemption even for the feckless.
