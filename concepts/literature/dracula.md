# Dracula

## summary

Bram Stoker's 1897 novel is a masterwork of epistolary Gothic horror depicting the invasion of Victorian England by Count Dracula, an ancient Transylvanian vampire. Told through letters, journal entries, and newspaper clippings, the narrative follows a band of ordinary people—a lawyer, a doctor, a schoolmaster, and a woman who must choose between her love and her salvation—as they battle an ancient evil that threatens both their individual sanity and the moral order of civilization.

## you gotta know

- Count Dracula, a centuries-old vampire from the Carpathian Mountains, leases a house in England with the goal of establishing new hunting grounds.
- Jonathan Harker, a young solicitor, encounters Dracula in Transylvania and becomes a prisoner in the vampire's castle, learning the monster's true nature.
- Dracula travels to England via ship, bringing boxes of earth from his homeland, and begins preying on Lucy Westenra, the fiancée of Harker's friend.
- Lucy's transformation into the "Un-Dead" forces her friends and her fiancé to recognize the reality of the supernatural and transcend Victorian skepticism.
- Professor Van Helsing, a Dutch vampire-hunting expert, leads the chase against Dracula, teaching the band of heroes how to oppose supernatural evil with faith, science, and conviction.
- The novel pioneered the modern vampire fiction tradition and established many genre conventions: the vampire's aversion to crosses and sunlight, the need for native soil, and the power of collective human resistance.
- Published during the height of the British Empire, Dracula reflects Victorian anxieties about invasion, sexual transgression, and the hidden threats lurking beneath civilization's surface.
