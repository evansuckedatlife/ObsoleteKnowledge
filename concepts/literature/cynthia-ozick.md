# Cynthia Ozick

## summary

*Cynthia Ozick* (b. 1928) is an American writer of extraordinary intellectual density whose fiction and essays are grounded in Jewish philosophical tradition, aesthetics, and ethics. Her work—novels, stories, and polemical essays—refuses sentimentality while exploring how art engages with morality, idolatry, and memory. She is one of the finest American prose stylists and a commanding voice in contemporary letters.

## you gotta know

- *The Puttermesser Papers* (1997) — novel of a lawyer-magician recreating herself through narrative and desire; her most playful and formally inventive work.
- *The Shawl* (1989) — novella and related stories, devastating compression of Holocaust and moral aftermath, won the prestigious PEN/Faulkner Award.
- *Bloodshed and Three Novellas* (1976) — collection demonstrating her range: metaphysics, eroticism, religious questioning, philosophical comedy.
- Essays on Jewish writers, literature, and ethics collected in *Art and Ardor* (1983) and *Quarrel & Quandary* (2000); intellectually rigorous and uncompromising.
- Known for dense, allusive prose and the fusion of high literary modernism with Jewish theological thinking.
- Addresses repeatedly: the relationship between art and idolatry; the inescapability of Jewish history; women's intellectual and erotic autonomy.
