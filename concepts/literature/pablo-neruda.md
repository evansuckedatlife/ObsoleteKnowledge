## summary

*Pablo Neruda* (1904–1973) was a Chilean poet whose voice ranged from intimate romantic reverie to epic political witness, making him one of the twentieth century's most vital and influential poets. Beginning with lush, erotic love lyrics and evolving into increasingly ambitious explorations of time, power, and history, Neruda demonstrated that poetry could be both sensually immediate and politically urgent, both personal and civilizational in scope. He won the Nobel Prize in Literature for his achievement and remained a fierce voice for the dispossessed until his death.

## you gotta know

- *Twenty Love Poems and a Song of Despair* (1924) — a book of erotic, luminous, melancholic love poems that established him as a major voice while still in his early twenties.
- *Residencia en la Tierra* (Residence on Earth, 1933–1935) — a dense, surrealist sequence on decay, eros, and the dissolution of identity; a modernist masterpiece.
- *Canto General* (1950) — an ambitious epic poem spanning the history and geography of the Americas, from pre-Columbian times to the present, blending myth, history, and politics.
- Nobel Prize in Literature (1971) — awarded for his powerful, biting verse which has become a symbol of the aspirations of an entire continent.
- A diplomat and exile; spent time in Asia, Spain, Mexico, and Moscow, and his political engagement deepened over his career toward communism and anti-imperialism.
- Wrote odes celebrating the everyday — an onion, socks, a tomato — giving lyrical grandeur to the humble and forgotten.
