# Oliver Twist

## summary

*Oliver Twist* is Dickens's second novel, serialized from 1837 to 1839, depicting the orphaned Oliver's suffering in a workhouse, his flight to London, and his entanglement with a criminal underworld. A dark social commentary on poverty, child abuse, and the criminal economy, it combines melodrama with urgent advocacy for the vulnerable.

## you gotta know

- Features *Oliver Twist*, an orphan born in a workhouse who asks for "more gruel" and is sold into apprenticeship.
- *Fagin* runs a school for child pickpockets in London's criminal underworld, training Oliver after he escapes his master.
- *Bill Sikes*, the brutal thief and murderer, represents the violent depths of urban crime; his death by his own dog is iconic.
- *Nancy*, a prostitute and Sikes's lover, helps Oliver but is bludgeoned to death by Sikes, one of Dickens's most shocking scenes.
- The plot climaxes with Oliver discovering his true parentage and legitimacy—a melodramatic resolution that contrasts the grim realism of much of the novel.
- Notable for its *social realism*: vivid depiction of London's poor quarters, workhouses, and the criminal economy.
