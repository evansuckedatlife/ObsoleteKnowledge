## summary

Arthur Conan Doyle's 1901–1902 serial novel in which Sherlock Holmes investigates a family curse on the English moors—the legendary hound that has plagued the Baskerville line for generations. The narrative weaves Gothic atmosphere with Holmes's rationalism as he works to protect the heir from a supernatural threat that proves to have a rational explanation.

## you gotta know

- The Baskerville family is cursed by a spectral hound, according to local legend; the creature has allegedly killed several family members over centuries.
- Sir Henry Baskerville, the last heir, seeks Holmes's protection after inheriting the title and the curse along with the estate.
- Holmes sends Watson to investigate and guard Sir Henry on the moors while Holmes pursues his own inquiries in London.
- The atmosphere of the Devon moors—fog, isolation, and unexplained sounds—creates genuine suspense around the supernatural threat.
- Holmes methodically exposes the hound as a real animal: a trained hunting dog covered in phosphorescent paint to create the illusion of a ghostly creature.
- The true villain is a relative, Stapleton, who has raised the hound to murder his way to the Baskerville fortune.
- The climax involves a dramatic confrontation on the moors, with Holmes capturing the criminal and saving Sir Henry's life.
