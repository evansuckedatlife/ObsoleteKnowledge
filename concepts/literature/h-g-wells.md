# H.G. Wells

## summary

*Herbert George Wells* was an English author whose scientific romances defined the trajectory of science fiction in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. His works explored the social and philosophical consequences of scientific advancement, imagining alien invasion, time travel, and biological manipulation with prescient concern for human nature and power. He bridged Victorian society and the modern anxious future.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *The Time Machine* (1895), imagining a future divided into the Eloi and Morlocks—a meditation on class stratification and evolutionary devolution.
- Created *The War of the Worlds* (1898), depicting a Martian invasion of England and pioneering the alien-invasion narrative as a vehicle for social critique.
- Authored *The Invisible Man* (1897), exploring the moral corrosion of power and invisibility as metaphor for the powerless individual against society.
- Wrote *The Island of Doctor Moreau* (1896), featuring human-animal hybrids and interrogating the ethics of scientific experimentation and dominion over nature.
- Produced extensively across multiple genres—sociological essays, futuristic romances, and moral fables—grounding speculation in contemporary concerns.
- His work was foundational to science fiction as a vehicle for social and philosophical exploration, not mere technological novelty.
