# Jane Eyre

## summary

Charlotte Brontë's *Jane Eyre* follows an orphaned governess who falls in love with her employer, the brooding Mr. Rochester. Their impending marriage promises Jane a path to love and security after years of hardship. The wedding ceremony is interrupted by the revelation of a dark secret: Rochester already has a wife—a woman named Bertha Mason, confined to the attic of Thornfield Hall, mad and dangerous. The twist reframes Jane's entire narrative of romantic fulfillment, transforming the love story into a reckoning with hidden oppression and psychological darkness.

## you gotta know

- Jane Eyre is an orphaned, impoverished young woman trained as a governess; she takes a position at Thornfield Hall in Yorkshire.
- She becomes emotionally intimate with her employer, Edward Rochester, a wealthy but mysterious man haunted by an unnamed past.
- Rochester proposes marriage; Jane accepts, believing she has finally found love and security. Plans proceed for the wedding.
- The wedding ceremony is stopped by a man (Mason) who reveals that Rochester is already married—to his ward, Bertha Mason, who lives in a locked room in the attic.
- Bertha is portrayed as violent and deranged, prone to fire-setting and dangerous rages. Her confinement is presented as necessary for everyone's safety.
- The twist destabilises the romantic narrative: Rochester deceived Jane about his past; the "secret" wife is a hidden person, not merely a past relationship.
- Jane eventually learns that Bertha is not merely mad but a victim of Rochester's control and isolation; modern critical readings suggest Bertha's rage is justified resistance.
