# Isaac Asimov

## summary

*Isaac Asimov* was an American biochemist and prolific science fiction author whose works defined the genre's technical and philosophical possibilities. His *Foundation* series imagined galactic empires and psychohistory; his robot stories explored consciousness and ethics through the famous *Three Laws of Robotics*. He synthesized scientific training with narrative imagination, establishing science fiction as a medium for intellectual speculation as much as adventure.

## you gotta know

- Wrote the *Foundation* series (beginning 1951), imagining a galactic empire applying mathematical psychohistory to predict and shape civilizational collapse and renewal.
- Created the *Three Laws of Robotics*—a logical framework governing machine obedience and the ethics of artificial minds—which became canonical to robot fiction.
- Authored the *Robot* stories and novels including *The Naked Sun* and *I, Robot*, exploring the boundary between human and artificial consciousness.
- Produced over 500 works across science fiction, mysteries, and scientific essays, establishing himself as the field's most prolific voice.
- Grounded his speculative premises in rigorous scientific reasoning, treating speculative scenarios as logical extrapolations rather than fantastic wish-fulfillment.
- His accessible style and playful engagement with ideas made complex scientific speculation available to mass audiences.
