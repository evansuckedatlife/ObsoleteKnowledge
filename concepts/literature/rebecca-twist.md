# Rebecca

## summary

Daphne du Maurier's *Rebecca* is a gothic romance narrated by an unnamed woman who marries the wealthy, aloof Maxim de Winter and moves to his grand estate, Manderley. She becomes consumed with anxiety about Maxim's deceased first wife, the glamorous Rebecca, whose presence seems to haunt the house through the servant Mrs. Danvers. The narrative builds toward a climactic revelation: Maxim murdered Rebecca in a moment of rage, and their marriage was far darker and more violent than his public grief suggested. The twist reframes the entire relationship and transforms the heroine from romantic victim to complicit participant in a crime.

## you gotta know

- The unnamed narrator, a shy young woman, meets and marries Maxim de Winter, a wealthy, mysterious widower.
- She moves to Manderley, Maxim's grand estate, where she is constantly compared to Rebecca, his glamorous first wife who died in a boating accident.
- The housekeeper Mrs. Danvers venerates Rebecca's memory and subtly torments the new Mrs. de Winter, suggesting Rebecca was perfect and irreplaceable.
- A salvaged boat surfaces in the sea with a skeleton; investigation reveals Rebecca did not die in a simple accident but was murdered.
- The final revelation: Maxim murdered Rebecca during a confrontation where she revealed she was pregnant with another man's child.
- Maxim confesses that he shot Rebecca and left her body in the boat, then fabricated the accident narrative.
- The narrator, despite this revelation, reconciles with Maxim; their marriage is reframed as a partnership built on complicity and shared secrets.
