# Kurt Vonnegut

## summary

*Kurt Vonnegut* was an American novelist and social critic whose darkly comic, science-inflected narratives interrogated war, free will, and meaninglessness. Drawing on his experience as a prisoner of war who survived the firebombing of Dresden, he deployed time-travel, alien perspectives, and formal experimentation to render catastrophe absorbable and human dignity legible amidst systemic horror. His work blended science fiction, satire, and philosophical rumination into a singular voice.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *Slaughterhouse-Five* (1969), following *Billy Pilgrim*, who becomes "unstuck in time" and witness to the Dresden bombing—fusing autobiography, science fiction, and anti-war polemic.
- Created *Cat's Cradle* (1963), introducing the fictional religion *Bokononism* and the apocalyptic substance "ice-nine," satirizing faith and the hubris of technoscience.
- Authored *Breakfast of Champions* (1973), an metafictional collage combining prose, drawings, and postmodern pastiche to explore American violence and consumer culture.
- Employed the phrase "So it goes" as refrain throughout his work—a mantra of acceptance and darkly comic resignation to death and chaos.
- Developed an immediately recognizable voice: colloquial, aphoristic, interrupted by metanarrative asides, oscillating between nihilism and tenderness.
- His speculative premises (time-travel, alien contact, doomsday science) served the psychological and spiritual themes rather than technical novelty.
