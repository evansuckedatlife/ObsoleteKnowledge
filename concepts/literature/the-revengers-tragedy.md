# The Revenger's Tragedy

## summary

*The Revenger's Tragedy*, attributed variously to Thomas Middleton or Cyril Tourneur (the question unresolved), is a visceral Jacobean revenge tragedy of sexual corruption and misogyny in a decadent ducal court. The protagonist *Vindice* uses the skull of his murdered fiancée as a macabre tool of revenge, and the play descends into a riot of poisoning, assassination, and cannibalistic revenge where nearly every character is both predator and prey in a game of mutual annihilation.

## you gotta know

- *Vindice* holds the skull of his slain beloved *Gloriana*; driven by this relic and by rage at the Duke's libertine cruelty, he orchestrates an elaborate revenge spanning two acts.
- The Duke, a senile voluptuary, attempts to seduce *Castiza*, Vindice's virtuous sister; Vindice's mother *Gratiana* initially encourages the seduction for money, then repents, creating a plot of familial betrayal and redemption.
- Vindice poisons the skull with a toxin and places it in the Duke's path; when the Duke kisses it (mistaking it for a living woman), he absorbs the venom and dies in agony.
- The play's revenge cascades: Vindice kills the Duke's son *Lussurioso* in a brothel; the Duchess and her illegitimate children are poisoned by a banquet; murder begets murder in exponential chaos.
- *Spurio*, the Duchess's bastard, and the Duke's legitimate heirs vie for dominance; all are morally compromised, and the revenge becomes indiscriminate destruction of the entire court.
- Vindice's final revelation of his own hand in the murders—his confession driven by pride—leads to his own execution; revenge consumes the avenger.
- The play's language is densely metaphorical and grotesque: disease imagery, sexual transgression, skull rhetoric—a verbal and visual feast of corruption and decay.

## connections

- [[thomas-middleton]] — likely playwright; his most baroque and violent work.
- [[cyril-tourneur]] — possibly the author; the attribution remains disputed and enriches literary scholarship.
- [[revenge-tragedy]] — the genre's darkest, most nihilistic expression; revenge as disease consuming all.
- [[vindice]] — the protagonist; intelligence and moral purpose corrupted into sadistic vengeance.
- [[gloriana]] — Vindice's dead fiancée; her skull a symbolic and literal tool of revenge.
- [[jacobean-misogyny]] — female sexuality and ambition portrayed as inherent corruption; Castiza and Gratiana are morally tested by lust.
