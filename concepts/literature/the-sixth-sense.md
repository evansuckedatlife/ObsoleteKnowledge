# The Sixth Sense

## summary

*The Sixth Sense* is a 1999 psychological thriller about a child psychologist treating a troubled young boy who claims to "see dead people." Throughout the narrative, the doctor believes he is helping the boy come to terms with trauma and supernatural visions. The film's climactic twist reveals that the psychologist himself is dead—killed by a former patient years earlier—and has been unable to accept his own death, constructing an elaborate false reality.

## you gotta know

- Haley Joel Osment plays Cole Sear, a withdrawn nine-year-old boy who whispers the film's famous line: "I see dead people."
- Bruce Willis plays Dr. Malcolm Crowe, a renowned child psychologist who becomes Cole's mentor and confidant, helping him accept and understand his visions.
- The film portrays Crowe as a successful, living professional treating a traumatised child; their relationship is portrayed as genuine therapeutic progress.
- The boy's supernatural ability is gradually accepted by both Crowe and the audience, and the narrative shifts toward spiritual acceptance and healing.
- In the final minutes, a twist reveals that Crowe is a ghost—shot by a resentful former patient at the film's opening (a scene recontextualised as his death, not a random attack).
- Crowe's entire narrative role is revealed as a form of unconscious purgatory—he has been unable to leave behind his work or accept his death.
- Directed by M. Night Shyamalan in his directorial breakthrough.
