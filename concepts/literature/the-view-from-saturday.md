# The View from Saturday

## summary

*The View from Saturday* is Lois Lowry's novel about a sixth-grade Academic Quiz Bowl team competing at the state championship level, revealing through interwoven narratives how four very different children—a visually impaired girl, a withdrawn immigrant boy, a physically disabled girl, and an intellectually brilliant social outcast—become a team bound by intellectual curiosity, loyalty, and unexpected friendship. The novel explores how knowledge and kindness together create community.

## you gotta know

- The team is coached by Mrs. Olinski, a recently disabled (wheelchair-using) teacher who selects four gifted but socially isolated sixth-graders to compete in an Academic Quiz Bowl.
- The four team members are Nadia (a girl who is visually impaired and struggles socially), Julian (a withdrawn boy processing his parents' divorce), Ethan (a socially awkward genius), and Ada Sophie (an immigrant girl who speaks multiple languages but feels culturally displaced).
- The novel interweaves the team's quiz bowl competition with flashback narratives revealing how each child was chosen and how they learned to trust one another despite their profound differences.
- Mrs. Olinski is revealed to have chosen these children partly because she observes their hidden kindness and intellectual gifts, and partly because she recognizes her own experience of disability and isolation in them.
- Won the Newbery Medal in 1997, Lowry's second Newbery, celebrated for its structure, its celebration of intellectual life, and its portrayal of disability with nuance.
- Quiz bowl itself becomes a metaphor for community—the competition requires perfect synchronization, trust, and the willingness to let your teammates' knowledge fill the gaps in your own.
- Themes include the power of intellectual shared joy, the way difference is a strength, the redemptive capacity of being truly seen and chosen by another person.
