# 1984 (Ending Twist)

## summary

George Orwell's *1984* chronicles the life of Winston Smith in a totalitarian superstate ruled by the Party, where truth, reality, and individuality are controlled and manipulated. The narrative suggests Winston's rebellion and love affair will lead to freedom or meaningful resistance. The ending's "twist" is not a dramatic reveal but a psychological inversion: the Party tortures Winston into betraying the woman he loves, and he is finally broken—not executed but genuinely converted to Party doctrine, finding peace in absolute surrender. The twist is that resistance was futile from the beginning.

## you gotta know

- Winston Smith works at the Ministry of Truth, rewriting history according to Party doctrine, but secretly harbours doubts and keeps a forbidden diary.
- He begins a love affair with Julia, a fellow Party member; their relationship seems to represent a pocket of authentic feeling in a dehumanised world.
- Both are arrested by the Thought Police; Winston is imprisoned and tortured, facing his greatest fear—rats—in Room 101.
- Rather than endure torture, Winston betrays Julia, offering her to the rats instead of himself.
- The final twist: after betrayal, Winston is released and finds Julia again, but both have been fundamentally broken—they no longer love each other and accept Party ideology.
- The ending suggests Winston's "redemption" and "acceptance" are actually signs of total psychological defeat—his individuality has been annihilated.
- Written by George Orwell and published in 1949; the title refers to the year in which Orwell wrote it.
