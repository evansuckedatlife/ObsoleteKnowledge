# Fight Club

## summary

*Fight Club* is a 1999 novel (and film adaptation) in which an unnamed office worker befriends the charismatic Tyler Durden and together they found an underground fight club. The narrative builds toward social chaos and anarchic rebellion as Tyler's ideology spreads. The climactic twist reveals that Tyler Durden is not a separate person but the narrator's alternate personality—a dissociative projection born from rage, trauma, and existential despair. The narrator has been unaware of his own violent double life.

## you gotta know

- The narrator, an insomniac corporate drone, meets Tyler Durden, a soap manufacturer and charismatic radical, aboard a plane.
- Tyler and the narrator form "Fight Club"—an underground fighting ring for men to reclaim primal masculinity against consumer culture and emasculation.
- Fight Club metastasises into a massive anarchist organisation called "Project Mayhem," orchestrating terrorism and infrastructure destruction.
- The narrator believes Tyler is a separate person with independent agency, making decisions and controlling Project Mayhem's operations.
- The twist: Tyler is the narrator's dissociative identity—created during a period of psychological breakdown, manifesting Tyler's ideology while the narrator remains partially aware.
- The climax reveals the narrator and Tyler as the same person; he must confront his unconscious desires and violent impulses, which have been acted out in his blackout periods.
- Written by Chuck Palahniuk; directed by David Fincher in the film adaptation.
