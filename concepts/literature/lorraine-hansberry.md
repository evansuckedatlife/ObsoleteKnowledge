# Lorraine Hansberry

## summary

*Lorraine Hansberry* was an American playwright whose debut *A Raisin in the Sun* (1959) became the first play by an African-American woman to reach Broadway, marking a watershed moment for Black representation in American theater. Her work brought Black family life, economic aspiration, and dignity to the mainstream stage.

## you gotta know

- *A Raisin in the Sun* won the New York Drama Critics Circle Award and tells the story of the Younger family in Chicago, each member with competing dreams, confronting racism and economic hardship.
- The title derives from Langston Hughes's poem "Harlem," asking what happens to a dream deferred—a question that animates the entire drama.
- Her work was groundbreaking for centering Black family complexity and inner conflict rather than portraying Black life as primarily a victim narrative.
- Wrote the television play *The Drinking Gourd* (1960) about slavery, and began work on other pieces that explored historical and political dimensions of Black experience.
- Published essays and speeches engaging with American politics, Civil Rights, and the role of the artist in social movements.
- Died of cancer at age 34, cutting short a luminous theatrical career but leaving an indelible mark on American drama.
