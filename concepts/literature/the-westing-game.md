# The Westing Game

## summary

*The Westing Game* is Ellen Raskin's intricate mystery novel about sixteen characters brought together by a will to solve the puzzle of who murdered the eccentric millionaire Samuel Westing, with the prize being his enormous estate. The novel is a narrative puzzle designed as much for the reader as for the characters, inviting active participation in uncovering the truth.

## you gotta know

- The characters are tenants in a new apartment building, drawn together by the mysterious death of Westing and his stipulation that one of them is his murderer.
- They are divided into pairs and given cryptic clues and a $10,000 bond each; their task is to identify Westing's killer within one year and win his fortune.
- Each pair is connected by a single word or phrase on their clue envelope, which they must interpret to guide their investigation—the puzzle is linguistic as much as narrative.
- Samuel Westing has designed the game as a kind of will-reading game in which the characters must become detectives, unraveling secrets and connections among themselves.
- Won the Newbery Medal in 1979, widely praised for its structural ingenuity, complex characterization of a diverse cast, and the way it rewards careful reading.
- The solution is deliberately constructed so that multiple interpretations are possible—Raskin crafted it so readers can "win" with more than one answer if they reason carefully.
- Themes include human interconnection, the way secrets bind communities, and the power of games and storytelling to transform lives.
