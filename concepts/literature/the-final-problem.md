# The Final Problem

## summary

Arthur Conan Doyle's 1893 short story in which Sherlock Holmes pursues his nemesis, Professor Moriarty, to the edge of a Swiss waterfall. The tale is narrated by Watson as a flashback, recounting Holmes's confession that Moriarty is a criminal genius who has orchestrated much of London's underworld. The story dramatizes Holmes and Moriarty's fatal struggle at Reichenbach Falls, apparently killing both.

## you gotta know

- Holmes reveals to Watson that Professor Moriarty—an unseen but vastly powerful criminal mind—is his true antagonist and the brains behind much of London's crime.
- Moriarty is described as a former mathematics professor with a towering intellect who commands a vast network of thieves and murderers.
- Holmes explains that he has been methodically bringing down Moriarty's criminal empire, but the professor now pursues him relentlessly for revenge.
- Watson records Holmes's account of their final chase across Europe, ending at Reichenbach Falls in Switzerland.
- Holmes and Moriarty apparently plunge to their deaths together at the waterfall, the ultimate battle between detective and criminal.
- Holmes leaves a farewell letter for Watson, bidding him goodbye and thanking him for their friendship.
- The story is deliberately ambiguous about whether Holmes truly died, leaving an opening for later resurrection.
