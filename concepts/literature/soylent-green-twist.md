# Soylent Green

## summary

*Soylent Green* is a 1973 science-fiction film set in an overpopulated, resource-depleted future New York where the masses subsist on synthetic food. Detective Thorn investigates the murder of a wealthy man and discovers a horrifying truth: the government's "Soylent Green" food product is made from human corpses. The film uses the twist to condemn indifference to mass suffering and the commodification of human life.

## you gotta know

- Set in 2022 in an apocalyptic New York where overpopulation has exhausted resources and the poor survive on government-issued wafers.
- Charlton Heston plays detective Frank Thorn, investigating the death of a corporate executive named Simonson.
- The twist is discovered through forensic investigation—plant matter in Soylent Green's supposedly vegetable-based formula that matches human tissue composition.
- The final scene shows Thorn, mortally wounded, learning the truth and crying out to the masses: "Soylent Green is people!"
- Directed by Richard Fleischer; loosely adapted from Harry Harrison's novel *Make Room! Make Room!*
- The film critiques overpopulation, corporate greed, environmental collapse, and the devaluation of human dignity.
- Soylent Green is colour-coded; the most abundant variety is the secret product distributed to the poorest districts.
