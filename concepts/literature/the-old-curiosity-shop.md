## summary

*The Old Curiosity Shop*, serialized from 1840 to 1841, follows a young orphan girl, *Little Nell*, and her grandfather, whose obsession with gambling and debt drives them from their home and into a harrowing journey across England. A sentimental and melodramatic tale, it combines picaresque adventure with Dickens's growing critique of social exploitation and economic desperation.

## you gotta know

- *Little Nell*, a virtuous, innocent girl, is forced to wander England with her grandfather after his debts force them to flee London.
- *Grandfather Trent*, Nell's grandfather, is a gambling addict whose obsession with games of chance destroys the family and drives the plot.
- *Daniel Quilp*, a grotesque dwarf and scheming moneylender, hounds Nell and her grandfather, representing predatory capitalism and sexual menace.
- Nell's tragic death in the countryside became famous—contemporaries wept over her passing, making it one of Dickens's most emotionally manipulative scenes.
- The novel combines sentimentality with darker social observation: Nell's journey exposes poverty, exploitation, and the vulnerability of the homeless and orphaned.
- Famous for the *Old Curiosity Shop* itself, an antique dealer's ramshackle store filled with a jumble of artifacts.

## connections

- [[charles-dickens]] — serialized to enormous popular response and demonstrates his sentimental mode.
- [[little-nell]] — one of Dickens's most famous and tragic characters; her death was wept over by Victorian readers.
- [[daniel-quilp]] — the grotesque villain, sexually predatory and economically exploitative.
- [[victorian-sentimentality]] — exemplifies the maudlin emotionalism Dickens could deploy.
