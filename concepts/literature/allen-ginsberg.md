# Allen Ginsberg

## summary

*Allen Ginsberg* (1926–1997) was an American poet and activist who became the voice of the Beat Generation and the counterculture through explosive, confessional verse that broke with literary convention. His epic poem *Howl* is one of the most significant American poems of the 20th century—a howl of rage, grief, and prophetic vision against conformity and capitalism. Beyond poetry, he was a tireless political activist, pacifist, and cultural provocateur.

## you gotta know

- *Howl* (1956) — epic confessional poem of prophecy and condemnation, sparked obscenity trials and redefined American poetry's possibilities.
- *Kaddish* (1961) — long elegy for his mother that blends Jewish prayer-form with modernist autobiography, considered his masterpiece by many critics.
- Pioneer of open-form poetry breaking from formal constraints; his long lines and breath-based measure influenced generations.
- Publicly declared his homosexuality in an era of profound repression; his erotic and queer imagery expanded poetry's subject matter.
- Traveled extensively, meeting and corresponding with world figures including the Dalai Lama; global activist for peace and literary freedom.
- Taught at many universities; his readings became public performances, blending poetry with oral tradition and moral witness.
