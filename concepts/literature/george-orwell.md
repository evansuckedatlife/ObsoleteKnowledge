# George Orwell

## summary

*George Orwell*, pen name of *Eric Arthur Blair*, was an English author and political essayist whose dystopian novels warned against totalitarian power and the manipulation of language and thought. His *1984* presents the bleakest vision of state surveillance and control, while *Animal Farm* satirizes revolutionary betrayal. His work merged speculative extrapolation with urgent moral witness, making him a touchstone for dissent and intellectual freedom.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *1984* (1949), depicting a totalitarian state of perpetual war, omniscient surveillance, and linguistic reengineering—*doublethink*, *newspeak*, and the *Ministry of Truth*.
- Created the concept of *Thought Police* and the telescreens—surveillance apparatus that merged menace and the mundane in a way that seemed prophetic decades later.
- Authored *Animal Farm* (1945), an allegory of the Russian Revolution in which revolutionary ideals are betrayed by their own inheritors, particularly the pig *Napoleon* (Stalin).
- Coined or popularized the terms *doublethink*, *thoughtcrime*, and *Big Brother*, which entered public discourse and persist as touchstones for critiquing power.
- Worked as a journalist, essayist, and political commentator, grounding his speculative fiction in lived experience of fascism, colonialism, and communist bureaucracy.
- His oeuvre emphasizes that totalitarianism is not a mere political system but a colonization of thought itself.
