# The Alchemist

## summary

Ben Jonson's *The Alchemist* is a masterpiece of satirical comedy set in plague-stricken London, where a con-artist alchemist, his female accomplice, and a smooth-talking servant dupe a parade of gulls—greedy merchants, lustful knights, and desperate social climbers—with the promise of transmuting lead into gold or granting impossible desires. The play's relentless momentum and verbal virtuosity lay bare the appetites and delusions of Stuart London with surgical precision.

## you gotta know

- *Face*, a servant, poses as a chemist's assistant; *Subtle*, the alchemist, poses as a master of the hermetic arts; *Doll Common* plays the "Queen of Faery" and various other roles to entrap clients.
- Their targets arrive in succession: *Dapper*, a lawyer seeking gambling luck via a fairy's touch; *Abel Drugger*, a tobacco-seller wanting alchemical prosperity; *Sir Epicure Mammon*, a merchant obsessed with sexual pleasure and wealth.
- Mammon's monologues on the riches and libertine delights alchemy will procure are sublime in their excess—the play's most eloquent expression of unbridled appetite.
- The scam escalates: they promise transmutation, spirit communion, and personal transformation; the con-artists extract money, jewelry, and goods under various pretexts.
- *Surly*, a cynical knight, penetrates the fraud and attempts to expose it; his unmasking precipitates the climax, but Subtle and Doll escape before being fully apprehended.
- *Face*, the servant, ingratiates himself with his master *Lovewit* upon the master's unexpected return, and through quick thinking, he survives by admitting to the scheme and throwing his partners under the wheel.
- The play's final twist: Face keeps his place in the household, having profited and deflected blame; the con succeeds despite exposure because greed and gullibility are eternal.

## connections

- [[ben-jonson]] — the dramatist; *The Alchemist* is his second masterpiece of satirical city comedy.
- [[satire]] — the play skewers avarice, credulity, and sexual appetite with Jonsonian precision.
- [[volpone]] — Jonson's other masterwork; similar anatomy of vice but with darker resolution.
- [[london-city-comedy]] — the genre Jonson perfected: urban intrigue, merchant and knight folly, servant wit.
- [[alchemy-and-hermetic-knowledge]] — the historical context; pseudo-scientific hucksterism was rampant in early Stuart England.
- [[face-the-servant]] — a predecessor to the clever servant archetype; his survival through amoral adaptation is the play's moral conclusion.
