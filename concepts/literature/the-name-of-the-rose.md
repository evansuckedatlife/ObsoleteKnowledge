# The Name of the Rose

## summary

Umberto Eco's 1980 novel is a sophisticated mystery set in a medieval monastery where a series of seemingly unrelated deaths plague the community. The protagonist, a Franciscan friar and detective named William of Baskerville, must uncover the connection between the deaths and a secret hidden in the monastery's forbidden library, all while navigating theological disputes and political intrigue.

## you gotta know

- The setting is a 14th-century Benedictine monastery in northern Italy, isolated and bound by strict rules of silence and obedience.
- Deaths occur among the monks: poisonings, murders, and accidents that on the surface appear unconnected and possibly supernatural.
- William of Baskerville, a monk known for his powers of observation and deduction, arrives to investigate whether the deaths constitute a pattern.
- The investigation reveals that someone in the monastery is systematically murdering people to protect a secret text in the monastery's most forbidden wing.
- The mystery traces back to a book of Aristotle's lost writings on comedy, which the church has suppressed because comedy is considered spiritually dangerous.
- William deduces that the killer, an elderly librarian, murders to keep the text hidden and preserve the church's control over knowledge and interpretation.
- The novel weaves detective mystery with philosophy, theology, semiotics, and political history, creating a layered narrative about truth and power.
