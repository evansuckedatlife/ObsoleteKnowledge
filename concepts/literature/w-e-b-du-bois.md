# W.E.B. Du Bois

## summary

*W.E.B. Du Bois* (1868–1963) was an American sociologist, historian, novelist, and essayist who became the intellectual leader of Black America in the early 20th century. His concept of "double consciousness"—the experience of seeing oneself through the eyes of a society that views one as other—profoundly influenced African-American thought and cultural criticism.

## you gotta know

- Introduced the concept of "double consciousness" in *The Souls of Black Folk* (1903), describing the internal conflict of Black Americans forced to view themselves through white America's contemptuous gaze.
- Founded and led the NAACP (National Association for the Advancement of Colored People), making him a central figure in organized civil rights activism.
- His essays in *The Souls of Black Folk* blend social analysis, spiritual meditation, and poetic language, establishing a hybrid form of intellectual expression.
- Wrote *The Philadelphia Negro* (1899), an early sociological study of an urban Black community, pioneering empirical social science examining race and class.
- Advocated for "the talented tenth"—the idea that educated Black elites had a responsibility to uplift the broader race through intellectual and moral leadership.
- Later in life embraced Pan-Africanism and socialism, eventually joining the Communist Party and emigrating to Ghana, showing his intellectual evolution across a century-long career.
