# Chikamatsu Monzaemon

## summary

**Chikamatsu Monzaemon** (1653–1724) was the preeminent playwright of the Edo period who wrote for both *bunraku* (puppet theatre) and *kabuki*, crafting emotionally devastating plays that explored the collision between duty and desire, especially in lovers driven to double suicide. His works established theatre as a serious art form and elevated the status of playwrights in Japanese culture.

## you gotta know

- Authored over 120 plays for bunraku puppet theatre, which was the dominant form during his lifetime; his scripts remain the core of the bunraku repertory.
- Pioneered the *sewamono* (domestic play) genre, moving beyond historical and legendary subjects to stage contemporary stories of merchants, courtesans, and ordinary people entangled in ethical crises.
- Wrote *The Love Suicides at Sonezaki* (1703), the most famous of his love-suicide plays, which depicted the double suicide of two lovers forbidden to marry by class and family obligations; the play's premiere sparked actual imitative suicides, drawing government censure.
- His protagonist characters are driven by intense emotion and moral conviction, creating empathetic, psychologically complex figures rather than stock types—a major innovation in theatre characterisation.
- Collaborated with the master puppet manipulator *Takemoto Gidayū*, whose chanting and musical accompaniment (*shamisen*) complemented Chikamatsu's dialogue and spectacle.
- Wrote plays that were equally performed in kabuki by live actors, demonstrating the theatrical power of his scripts across different performance modes.

- [[bunraku]] — puppet theatre for which he wrote his masterworks.
- [[kabuki]] — live theatre tradition that adopted and performed many of his plays.
