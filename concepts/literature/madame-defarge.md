## summary

**Madame Defarge** is the implacable revolutionary avenger in *A Tale of Two Cities* who knits coded death lists and presides over the Reign of Terror with cold, methodical vengeance. A woman of granite will and historical grievance, she pursues the destruction of entire families to settle old wounds, embodying the destructive power of revolution unchecked by mercy.

## you gotta know

- Knits coded names into patterns at her post in the wine shop, recording the identities of those marked for execution during the French Revolution.
- Carries a loaded revolver concealed on her person and actively participates in mob violence, including the storming of the Bastille and the massacre of aristocrats.
- Driven by a decades-old vendetta: her sister was seduced and destroyed by an aristocrat, and she seeks to eliminate his entire lineage in retaliation.
- Confronts Lucie Manette with threats and coercion, attempting to destroy the family she sees as complicit in her sister's ruin.
- Dies when her own revolver misfires during a struggle with Miss Pross, killed by the very weapon she intended to use as an instrument of vengeance.
- Her relentless pursuit of justice becomes monstrous justice; Dickens shows how personal trauma can corrupt revolutionary ideals into mere cruelty.

## connections

- [[a-tale-of-two-cities]] — the novel structured partly around her vendetta and its consequences.
- [[charles-dickens]] — a portrait of how history consumes individuals driven by private grievance.
- [[sydney-carton]] — his sacrifice gains meaning partly in opposition to her senseless destruction.
- [[lucie-manette]] — the woman whose family Madame Defarge targets and who ultimately escapes through Carton's act.
