# The Moonstone

## summary

Wilkie Collins's 1868 novel, considered the first detective novel in English literature, centers on the theft of a valuable Indian diamond from an English country house during a celebration. The narrative unfolds through multiple perspectives as detective Sergeant Cuff investigates the case, uncovering a web of family secrets and complex motivations.

## you gotta know

- A large diamond called the Moonstone, stolen from an Indian temple, is given as a gift to Rachel Verinder on her eighteenth birthday.
- The diamond vanishes on the same night, and suspicion falls on Indian jugglers who have been loitering around the house.
- Sergeant Cuff, the famous detective from Scotland Yard, is called in to investigate; he quickly identifies an unlikely suspect.
- The novel unfolds through different narrators—servants, family members, and the detective—each revealing new pieces of the puzzle.
- Rachel Verinder herself is revealed to be the thief, taken in her sleep under the influence of opium administered for medical reasons.
- The real plot involves a complicated love story and family loyalty—Rachel took the diamond to protect her family honor from suspicion.
- Indian Brahmins are ultimately connected to the stone's fate; the resolution involves themes of cultural appropriation and restitution.
