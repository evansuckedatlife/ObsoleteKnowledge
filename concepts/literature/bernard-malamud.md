# Bernard Malamud

## summary

*Bernard Malamud* (1914–1986) was an American novelist and short-story writer whose fiction centres on working-class Jewish immigrants and outsiders struggling toward spiritual redemption. His prose combines realism with fable-like moral clarity; his characters endure hardship not for success but for the possibility of ethical renewal. He is a master of the American short story and the tragic novel of ordinary lives.

## you gotta know

- *The Assistant* (1957) — novel of a Jewish grocer and his Italian assistant, redemptive drama of sacrifice and moral growth set in postwar Brooklyn.
- *The Fixer* (1966) — historical novel of a Jewish handyman imprisoned in Tsarist Russia, won the Pulitzer Prize, explores dignity under persecution.
- *The Natural* (1952) — mythic baseball novel transmuting American sport into Arthurian tragedy and moral corruption.
- Master of short stories; collections *The Magic Barrel* (1958) and *Idiots First* (1963) contain devastating miniatures of spiritual crisis.
- Won the National Book Award twice; his spare, symbolic prose influenced generations of American writers.
- Stories often feature ordinary men—shopkeepers, failed athletes, immigrants—facing sudden moral tests without supernatural resolution.
- Taught creative writing; his pedagogical influence shaped postwar American fiction pedagogy.
