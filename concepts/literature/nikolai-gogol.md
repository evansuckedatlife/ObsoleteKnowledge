# Nikolai Gogol

## summary

*Nikolai Gogol* (1809–1852) was a Russian writer whose grotesque comedies and satirical tales transformed Russian literature through a fusion of realism, absurdism, and grotesque humor. Despite his Ukrainian origins, he became the chronicler of Russian bureaucratic corruption and provincial mediocrity. His works—particularly *Dead Souls*, *The Inspector General*, and *The Overcoat*—blend social satire with an unsettling supernatural atmosphere.

## you gotta know

- Born in Ukraine to a Cossack gentry family; studied in St. Petersburg and became a writer and minor official in the imperial bureaucracy.
- *The Inspector General* (1836): a comedy of errors where a poor official is mistaken for a government inspector by a corrupt provincial town; brilliant satire of bureaucratic fear and imposture; caused scandal at premiere.
- *Dead Souls* (1842): unfinished epic novel about *Chichikov*, a rogue who travels Russia buying "dead souls"—serfs no longer alive but still on tax rolls—to pose as a wealthy landowner. Intended as a Dantean moral allegory but remains a masterpiece of grotesque realism.
- *The Overcoat* (1842): novella of *Akaky Akakievich*, a meek copying clerk who saves for a new overcoat; it is stolen, and his humiliation and death spiral into the supernatural. Often called the birth of the modern short story; Dostoevsky famously said Russian writers came "out of Gogol's overcoat."
- Master of grotesque detail, absurdist logic, and the fantastic; his stories blend social realism with nightmarish atmosphere and dark comedy.
