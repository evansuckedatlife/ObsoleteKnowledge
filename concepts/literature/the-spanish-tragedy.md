# The Spanish Tragedy

## summary

Thomas Kyd's *The Spanish Tragedy* is the foundational revenge tragedy of English drama, depicting an aging father's descent into madness and murder after his son is killed in court intrigue. Written in the 1580s, it established the template for the genre: the ghost demanding vengeance, the delay and feigned madness, the play-within-a-play, and the final stage littered with corpses—a formula Shakespeare and his contemporaries would inherit wholesale.

## you gotta know

- *Hieronimo*, a knight-marshal of Spain, seeks vengeance for his son *Horatio*, murdered by the Portuguese prince *Balthazar* and Spain's military commander *Lorenzo*.
- The play opens with a ghost and supernatural machinery, establishing the supernatural obligation to revenge that haunts the protagonist and the stage.
- Hieronimo feigns madness—cutting down his son's tomb, speaking in fractured verse, distributing poison as gifts—using his apparent lunacy as cover for methodical murder.
- The climax employs a play-within-a-play (a court entertainment depicting *Babylon's Fall*) in which Hieronimo engineers the real deaths of the murderers; he kills Lorenzo and Balthazar on stage.
- Hieronimo's final act is his own suicide, biting off his tongue to frustrate further interrogation—a gesture of defiant silence and the ultimate refusal of the legal system.
- The subplot of the Portuguese *Bel-imperia*, Balthazar's captive who falls in love with Horatio, deepens the play's tangle of honour, desire, and feudal obligation.
- The play is a tour de force of theatrical spectacle and rhetoric: madness speeches, embedded plays, supernatural apparitions, and bodies accumulating on stage.

## connections

- [[thomas-kyd]] — the playwright; his only major surviving work, yet it defined a genre.
- [[revenge-tragedy]] — the foundational text; every subsequent revenge play owes structural debt to Kyd.
- [[hamlet]] — Shakespeare's masterpiece owes its entire framework to *The Spanish Tragedy*: the ghost, the delay, the feigned madness, the dénouement of mutual slaughter.
- [[elizabethan-theatre]] — the play epitomizes the 1580s taste for blood, spectacle, and rhetorical excess.
- [[hieronimo]] — the protagonist; madness as both cover and genuine psychological fracture.
- [[bel-imperia]] — the female lover whose agency and tragic complicity complicate the revenge plot.
