# Jules Verne

## summary

*Jules Verne* was a French author who pioneered the scientific romance and is widely regarded as a father of science fiction. His prolific output spanned submarine adventures, lunar voyages, and extraordinary journeys across and beneath the Earth, marrying scientific extrapolation with page-turning adventure. He inspired generations of readers and inventors to imagine the technological future.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea* (1870), featuring the mysterious submarine *Nautilus* and Captain *Nemo*—a work that anticipated submarines and oceanic exploration.
- Authored *Journey to the Center of the Earth* (1864), imagining a subterranean world with prehistoric flora and fauna accessible through volcanic tunnels.
- Created *Around the World in Eighty Days* (1873), following the globe-trotting journey of *Phileas Fogg*—a feat of near-futuristic travel technology and speed.
- Wrote *From the Earth to the Moon* (1865) and *Around the Moon* (1870), imagining lunar voyages in remarkable technical detail, decades before actual spaceflight.
- Produced over 60 novels in his "Extraordinary Voyages" series, spanning science fiction, adventure, and speculative geography.
- Combined meticulous attention to scientific plausibility with breathless narrative pacing, creating a template for scientific romance.
