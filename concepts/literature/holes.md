# Holes

## summary

*Holes* is Louis Sachar's novel about Stanley Yelnats, a boy wrongly imprisoned at Camp Green Lake, a brutal detention center in the Texas desert where inmates dig holes ostensibly searching for a buried treasure. The novel weaves together Stanley's present-day struggle for survival with two interconnected historical narratives—one of his great-grandfather's curse and one of a legendary outlaw—revealing that all three stories converge in a lesson about friendship, redemption, and destiny.

## you gotta know

- Stanley is sent to Camp Green Lake as punishment for stealing sneakers he did not take, a crime of circumstance rather than malice, and must dig holes in the blazing desert as a form of punishment and character-building.
- The holes are supposed to help the warden search for buried treasure, but Stanley slowly discovers that the treasure never existed; the camp and the digging are part of a pattern of exploitation and control.
- Through flashbacks, the novel reveals that Stanley's family carries a curse placed by a woman named Elya Yelnats over a century ago when Elya's ancestor wronged her, and the curse has shadowed the family ever since.
- A second historical narrative tells of Kissin' Kate Barlow, a school teacher turned outlaw in the 1890s, whose hidden treasure and love story connect the camp's sinister past to Stanley's present.
- Won the Newbery Medal in 2000, celebrated for its intricate plotting, complex characterization, and the way it suggests that fate and friendship can heal inherited wounds.
- Stanley and his friend Zero (a brilliant but neglected boy at the camp) forge a bond based on mutual respect and shared defiance, and together they escape the camp and climb the mountain.
- The ending suggests that Stanley's loyalty and kindness have broken the family curse, turning a legacy of bad luck into a legacy of love and connection.
