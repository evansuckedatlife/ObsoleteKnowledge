# The Overcoat

## summary

*The Overcoat* is Gogol's masterpiece of bureaucratic absurdity and human tragedy, following a minor copying clerk whose sole ambition is to acquire a new overcoat. When a thief steals his prized possession, the humble man's desperate plea to authority crumbles into despair, transforming a tale of petty longing into a meditation on dignity, poverty, and the invisible suffering of the overlooked.

## you gotta know

- The protagonist *Akaky Akakievich* is a lowly copying clerk whose existence is entirely consumed by his work, barely earning enough to sustain himself.
- He spends years saving to purchase a new overcoat, his singular dream representing the hunger for respectability and a moment of personal pride.
- After finally acquiring the coat and experiencing a brief social awakening, a group of thieves steals it from him on his way home.
- Akaky visits an Important Person of rank seeking help, only to be humiliated and dismissed—the system offers no protection for the powerless.
- The story ends ambiguously with Akaky's death, though rumors circulate of his ghost now stealing overcoats from officials in supernatural revenge.
- Gogol's narrative voice oscillates between mockery and sympathy, creating an unsettling tension that elevates the tale beyond simple comedy.
