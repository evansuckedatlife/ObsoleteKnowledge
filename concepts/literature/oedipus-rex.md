# Oedipus Rex

## summary

*Oedipus Rex* is Sophocles' masterpiece tragedy, performed around 429 BCE, examining fate, knowledge, and the limits of human agency. A king discovers through relentless investigation that he has unknowingly killed his father and married his mother, fulfilling a prophecy he attempted to escape. The play stands as perhaps the most perfect tragic structure ever written, crystallising the idea that knowledge itself can be a form of punishment.

## you gotta know

- Oedipus becomes king of Thebes by solving the Sphinx's riddle and marrying the widowed Queen Jocasta as reward.
- A plague ravages Thebes; Oedipus vows to find and punish the murderer of the previous king, Laius.
- Through interrogation of a blind prophet *Tiresias* and pursuit of witnesses, Oedipus learns he is the murderer—he killed his father at a crossroads years ago.
- The play's tragic irony: Oedipus fled a prophecy that he would kill his father and marry his mother, only to fulfil it precisely by fleeing.
- Jocasta hangs herself upon learning the truth; Oedipus blinds himself and goes into exile.
- The play emphasises *anagnorisis* (recognition/revelation) and *peripeteia* (reversal of fortune) as the mechanism of tragedy.
- Sophocles uses dramatic irony masterfully—the audience and characters speak truths about Oedipus's parentage long before he understands them.

## connections
