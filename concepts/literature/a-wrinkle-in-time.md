## summary

*A Wrinkle in Time* is a science-fiction adventure by Madeleine L'Engle about a girl named Meg Murry who travels across dimensions to rescue her father from the forces of darkness, learning to embrace her perceived flaws as strengths. The novel blends theoretical physics, theological themes, and coming-of-age growth into a story where love and individual difference triumph over conformity and evil.

## you gotta know

- Meg Murry is searching for her missing physicist father, who has been trapped by a malevolent force called the *Black Thing* while working on a secret government project.
- Meg is joined by her younger brother Charles Wallace and a neighborhood girl named Calvin, guided by three mysterious beings: Mrs. Whatsit, Mrs. Which, and Mrs. Who.
- The trio teaches the children about "tessering"—folding space to travel between dimensions—and takes them to the planet Camazotz, which is entirely controlled by a pulsating brain called *It*.
- Meg's greatest weakness—her anger and willfulness—becomes her greatest strength when she uses these emotions to break free from *It*'s mind control and confront the evil directly.
- Published in 1962, the novel won the Newbery Medal in 1963 and challenged conventional children's literature by embracing philosophical complexity and ambiguous characters.
- The book draws on both classical mythology (the three Fates, references to biblical and Shakespearean figures) and modern physics (relativity, dimensions, tesseracts).
- Meg's journey is as much spiritual and emotional as it is physical—she must accept herself and her family to save them.
