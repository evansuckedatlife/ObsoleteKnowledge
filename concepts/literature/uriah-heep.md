# Uriah Heep

## summary

**Uriah Heep** is the obsequious and hypocritical clerk in *David Copperfield* whose false humility and greasy manner conceal ruthless ambition and malevolence. He manipulates those around him through a calculated performance of servile meekness, all while secretly plotting to seize power and wealth.

## you gotta know

- Works as a clerk to Mr. Wickfield and gradually insinuates himself into a position of authority through a combination of slavish deference and blackmail.
- Constantly refers to himself as "humble" and "umble," using exaggerated courtesy and hand-wringing as a weapon to disarm suspicion.
- Harbors a passionate, possessive desire for Agnes Wickfield and uses his growing control over her father's affairs to pressure her.
- His true nature is revealed when David discovers that Heep has forged documents, embezzled funds, and coerced both Micawber and Wickfield into complicity.
- Eventually imprisoned for his crimes, yet maintains his contemptible pose of injured humility even after exposure.
- Represents Dickens' warning against judging by appearances and his contempt for false piety masking greed and cruelty.
