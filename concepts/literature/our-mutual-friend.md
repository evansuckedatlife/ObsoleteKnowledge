## summary

*Our Mutual Friend*, serialized from 1864 to 1865, is Dickens's final completed novel, set in Victorian London's world of money, dust, and moral corruption. Through interlocking plots, it traces the transformation of *John Harmon*, who returns from the dead, and the lives entangled around a dustman's inheritance—a fortune built from garbage, symbolizing the spiritual emptiness of wealth.

## you gotta know

- *John Harmon*, presumed drowned, returns in disguise to observe the world and the girl intended for him, *Bella Wilfer*.
- *Bella Wilfer*, a materialistic young woman, is transformed from valuing fortune and appearances to genuine love when she discovers true wealth lies in the heart, not the purse.
- *Lizzie Hexam*, a riverside girl, loves the noble *Eugene Wrayburn*, a lawyer of good birth but dissolute character; their arc is one of redemption through love.
- *Bradley Headstone*, a schoolmaster and rival for Lizzie, represents the dangerous passions hidden beneath respectability; his jealous violence darkens the narrative.
- *Noddy Boffin*, the honest dustman who inherits the fortune, becomes corrupted by wealth before a redemptive reversal.
- The *Thames River* and the world of "dust"—garbage pickers and refuse—provide metaphors for London's moral and economic underbelly.

## connections

- [[charles-dickens]] — his final completed novel, returning to themes of wealth, corruption, and redemption.

- [[lizzie-hexam]] — the working-class heroine whose love and loyalty anchor the novel's moral center.
