# Agatha Christie

## summary

*Agatha Christie* (1890–1976) was a British mystery writer and the bestselling novelist of all time, credited with writing 66 detective novels and numerous short stories. Her ingenious plots, fair-play mysteries, and iconic detectives *Hercule Poirot* and *Miss Marple* defined the golden age of detective fiction and made her the most-performed playwright in the world (through *The Mousetrap*, which had the longest theatrical run in history).

## you gotta know

- Born in Devon to an upper-middle-class family; married and widowed; turned to writing mystery novels during her first marriage to support herself.
- *Hercule Poirot*: a fastidious Belgian detective with a "little grey cells"; appeared in 39 novels and numerous short stories over six decades; her most famous creation.
- *Miss Marple*: an elderly spinster living in a quiet English village who solves crimes through her knowledge of human nature; 12 novels spanning her career.
- *The Murder of Roger Ackroyd* (1926): a landmark mystery where the detective *Poirot* investigates a wealthy man's death; famous for its narrative twist.
- *Murder on the Orient Express* (1934): *Poirot* investigates a murder aboard a luxury train snowbound in the Alps; elegant and intricate plotting.
- *And Then There Were None* (1939): ten strangers invited to an island are killed one by one according to a nursery rhyme; considered her masterpiece and one of the greatest mystery novels ever written.
- *The Mousetrap* (1952): a stage play about strangers snowed in at a guest house where a murderer strikes; premiered in 1952 and ran for over 70 years, the longest-running play in theatrical history.
