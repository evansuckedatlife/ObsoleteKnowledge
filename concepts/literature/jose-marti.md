# José Martí

## summary

*José Martí* (1853–1895) was a Cuban poet, essayist, and revolutionary who wielded the pen as a weapon against Spanish colonialism and American imperialism, making literature and politics inseparable. His luminous, innovative verse helped define Spanish-American Modernism while his essays and journalism articulated a vision of Cuban independence and Pan-American solidarity. He died fighting for Cuban freedom, embodying the Romantic ideal of the artist-activist and becoming a foundational symbol of Latin American identity and resistance.

## you gotta know

- *Ismaelillo* (1882) — a collection of tender, playful poems dedicated to his son, introducing a more intimate and vulnerable Modernist voice into Spanish-language poetry.
- *Simple Verses* (*Versos Sencillos*, 1891) — a collection of deceptively simple, aphoristic poems that became iconic in Cuban culture; includes "My Verse" ("Cultivo una rosa blanca"), a statement of artistic independence.
- Pioneered Spanish-American *Modernism*: a literary movement that rejected 19th-century realism in favour of musicality, innovation, and aesthetic experimentation, drawing on Symbolism and Parnassianism.
- Lived in exile in New York, where he wrote prolifically as a journalist and essayist, earning a living while articulating his vision of Cuban independence and the responsibilities of the artist.
- Died in 1895 fighting for Cuban independence against Spain; his death elevated his status from poet to martyr and revolutionary hero.
- Influencer of Rubén Darío and the broader Modernist movement; his formal innovation and fusion of personal emotion with political conviction became a model for generations.
