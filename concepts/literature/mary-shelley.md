# Mary Shelley

## summary

*Mary Shelley* was an English author and the author of *Frankenstein*, often considered the first science fiction novel. Born into a family of radical intellectuals, she synthesized gothic sensibilities with scientific speculation, creating a work that explored the ethics of creation and the consequences of unchecked ambition. Her imaginative depth extended to other speculative works that shaped the contours of fantastic literature.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *Frankenstein* (1818) at age eighteen, which presented a rationalized monster-making narrative that grounded the fantastic in plausible science—a turning point for speculative fiction.
- Conceived *Frankenstein* during the famous 1816 ghost-story contest at Villa Diodati with her husband *Percy Bysshe Shelley*, Lord Byron, and others.
- Author of *The Last Man* (1826), an apocalyptic novel set in the 21st century that pioneered plague-narrative and end-times speculation.
- Was the daughter of philosopher *William Godwin* and feminist writer *Mary Wollstonecraft*, inheriting a tradition of challenging conventional thought.
- Wrote numerous works of short fiction, essays, and the autobiographical novel *Lodore*, exploring themes of isolation and social constraint.
- Her works emphasize the isolation of the outsider—whether creator or created—and the cost of transgressing natural and social boundaries.
