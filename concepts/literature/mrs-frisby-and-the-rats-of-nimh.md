# Mrs. Frisby and the Rats of NIMH

## summary

*Mrs. Frisby and the Rats of NIMH* is Robert C. O'Brien's novel about a widowed mouse who must save her ill child and her family from the plow by seeking help from a colony of highly intelligent rats who were subjects of a sinister government laboratory experiment. The story explores themes of intellectual capacity, ethics, and the moral cost of progress.

## you gotta know

- Mrs. Frisby is a timid field mouse facing the seasonal destruction of her home by a farmer's plow and searching for a way to move her chronically ill son Timothy to a safe place before spring plowing.
- The rats she encounters were genetically enhanced at the fictional National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) through experimental procedures, granting them human-level intelligence and the ability to read and build civilization.
- The rats have escaped their laboratory captivity and built an underground society with strict codes of honor, planning to make themselves self-sufficient by stealing grain rather than relying on NIMH's experiments forever.
- Mrs. Frisby discovers that Justin, the noble rat leader, knew her late husband, a mouse named Jonathan Frisby, who died in an experiment after helping the rats escape.
- The rats' leader, Nicodemus, is wise and tormented by the ethics of their enhanced intelligence—they are aware that their powers were granted artificially and debate whether they have the right to use stolen resources.
- The climax involves a device called the "Boniface Estate" poisoning system and Mrs. Frisby's bravery in retrieving a crucial item to help the rats relocate their entire colony.
- Won the Newbery Medal in 1972, celebrated for its sophisticated portrayal of animal intelligence and philosophical depth.
