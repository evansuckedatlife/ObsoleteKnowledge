# Nicholas Nickleby

## summary

*Nicholas Nickleby*, serialized from 1838 to 1839, follows a young gentleman's struggles after his father's death, his abuse at a brutal Yorkshire boarding school, and his eventual triumph. Spanning London and the provinces, it combines picaresque adventure with Dickens's scathing critique of exploitative institutions and villains.

## you gotta know

- Follows *Nicholas*, a young man thrust into poverty after his father's death, sent to teach at *Dotheboys Hall*, a notorious Yorkshire boarding school.
- *Wackford Squeers*, the headmaster of Dotheboys Hall, is a grotesque villain whose beatings and neglect of students embody institutional cruelty.
- Nicholas flees the school and joins a traveling theater company, experiencing poverty and adventures across England.
- Features *Smike*, a poor mistreated student at Dotheboys Hall who becomes Nicholas's loyal friend and suffers a tragic early death.
- *Newman Noggs*, the comic but kind-hearted clerk, and *Mrs. Nickleby*, Nicholas's scatterbrained mother, provide secondary humor and pathos.
- The novel pivots between broad comic episodes (the theatrical scenes) and darker social satire (institutional cruelty).
