# Douglas Adams

## summary

*Douglas Adams* was a British author and humorist whose *The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy* defined comedic science fiction for generations. Blending absurdist humor with genuine philosophical speculation, his narratives interrogate meaning, existence, and bureaucracy across galaxies with the same anxious wit he brought to terrestrial social critique. He elevated the comic premise to philosophical vehicle, making profound questions feel inevitable consequences of the ridiculous.

## you gotta know

- Wrote *The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy* (1979), following the accidental space traveler *Arthur Dent* through an absurdist cosmos ruled by improbable bureaucracy and alien incompetence.
- Created the *Deep Thought* supercomputer, which calculates the "Answer to the Ultimate Question of Life, the Universe, and Everything" as "42"—a joke about the impossibility of existential certainty.
- Authored the *Dirk Gently* detective series, applying the same comedic speculation to contemporary England, tracking how the "Holistic Detective" navigates impossible causalities.
- Deployed rapid-fire absurdist humor, portmanteau wordplay, and deflationary punchlines—descending from radio comedy (*Monty Python*) rather than literary tradition.
- His speculative premises exist primarily to create incongruity and philosophical vertigo—the mismatch between human expectation and cosmic reality.
- Merged profound pessimism about meaning with exuberant formal play, suggesting that laughter is the only sane response to existence.
