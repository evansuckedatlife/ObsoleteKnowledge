# Sydney Carton

## summary

**Sydney Carton** is the dissolute, brilliant barrister in *A Tale of Two Cities* whose life of dissipation is redeemed through his final act of self-sacrifice, dying in place of the man his beloved Lucie truly loves. His resurrection through sacrifice embodies Dickens' vision of redemption through agape and the transcendent power of willing death for another's sake.

## you gotta know

- A lawyer of great talent but no ambition, squandering his gifts through drink and cynicism, haunted by his wasted potential.
- Bears a striking physical resemblance to Charles Darnay, the French aristocrat who becomes his friend and the rival for Lucie Manette's affection.
- Confesses his love to Lucie but accepts her rejection with grace, telling her that he would do anything for her, including dying.
- Switches places with Darnay at the guillotine during the Terror, taking his condemned friend's identity and mounting the scaffold in his stead.
- Dies with the famous final words: "It is a far, far better thing that I do, than I have ever done; it is a far, far better rest that I go to than I have ever known."
- His sacrifice transforms his wasted life into meaning and saves Lucie from losing her husband; Dickens suggests that redemption is possible for even the most seemingly lost.
