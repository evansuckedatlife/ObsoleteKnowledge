# Chaim Potok

## summary

*Chaim Potok* (1929–2002) was an American novelist and rabbi whose fiction explores the tensions between Orthodox Jewish tradition and secular modernity, particularly across generations. His work centres on gifted young men torn between the world of yeshiva study and the allure of secular culture. He brings the interior life of Orthodox Judaism into English-language literature with unprecedented depth and authority.

## you gotta know

- *The Chosen* (1967) — debut novel of friendship between two Orthodox Jewish boys with different philosophies, winner of multiple awards, his most celebrated work.
- *My Name Is Asher Lev* (1972) — novel of a Hasidic prodigy drawn to visual art against his community's will; explores the price of artistic calling within strict tradition.
- *The Promise* (1969) — sequel to *The Chosen*, following the boys into young adulthood and philosophical maturity.
- Was an ordained rabbi and scholar of Hebrew; his novels draw on deep knowledge of Jewish texts and theology.
- His prose is lucid and psychologically penetrating; he makes Orthodox thought accessible to secular readers without condescension.
- Repeatedly addresses the question: can one be both authentically Jewish and authentically modern?
