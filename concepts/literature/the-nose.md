# The Nose

## summary

*The Nose* is a surreal comedy in which a St. Petersburg barber discovers his customer's nose in his morning bread, only to find that the man has lost it entirely. The nose, detached yet fully sentient, embarks on its own adventures while its owner descends into social mortification, culminating in a mysteriously restored status quo that defies logical explanation.

## you gotta know

- Major Kovalyov, a midlevel official, awakens to discover his nose is missing, leaving only smooth skin where it once protruded.
- The barber Ivan discovers the nose wrapped in dough in his bread and attempts to dispose of it, setting up the surreal plot.
- Kovalyov encounters his nose functioning as an independent gentleman of higher rank, who refuses to acknowledge their former connection.
- The nose visits a cathedral, appears in the newspaper, and eludes capture with absurd dignity, outranking its former owner socially.
- Police and authorities intervene, treating the missing nose as both a medical and a civil matter, generating bureaucratic confusion.
- The story ends without resolution—the nose suddenly reattaches itself, and Kovalyov's life resumes as though nothing happened.
- Gogol's tale is an allegory of alienation and social pretension, where the absurd becomes grotesquely plausible.
