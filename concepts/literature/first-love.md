# First Love

## summary

Turgenev's *First Love* captures the emotional turbulence of adolescence through a young man's tender infatuation with a beautiful older woman who has just moved into his neighborhood. The protagonist's naïve, all-consuming passion encounters the complexities of adult desire and social hierarchy, ultimately teaching him that the idealized love of youth cannot survive contact with human reality and moral compromise.

## you gotta know

- The unnamed narrator, a sheltered sixteen-year-old, becomes obsessed with *Zinaida*, a sophisticated young woman three years his senior who hosts fashionable gatherings.
- He constructs an idealized vision of her, believing her superior to all others, and experiences the exquisite pain of unrequited desire mixed with fleeting moments of encouragement.
- Zinaida is surrounded by suitors of various ages and ranks; the narrator's jealousy and confusion grow as he struggles to understand her behavior and true feelings.
- The narrator discovers that his own father, married and mature, has become romantically entangled with Zinaida, creating a shocking betrayal that shatters the boy's illusions.
- His father's affair with Zinaida reveals her moral plasticity—she is capable of genuine feeling but also calculation—dismantling the narrator's romantic mythology.
- The novella ends with the narrator older, reflecting on how his first love, though painful, taught him both the intensity of youthful passion and the necessity of disillusionment.
- Turgenev's prose captures the poignancy of remembering one's own past with both tenderness and ironic distance.
