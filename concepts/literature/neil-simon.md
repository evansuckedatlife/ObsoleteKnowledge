# Neil Simon

## summary

*Neil Simon* (1927–2018) was an American playwright and screenwriter whose comedies are among the most-performed and commercially successful in American theatre. His work mines domestic relationships, family dysfunction, and urban Jewish life for both laugh and pathos. He was a master of comic timing and the emotional turn—making laughter the vehicle for genuine feeling.

## you gotta know

- *The Odd Couple* (1965) — comedy of two divorced men sharing an apartment, became a television and film classic, exemplary of his ear for dialogue and character.
- *Barefoot in the Park* (1963) — debut play about newlyweds navigating marriage in a cramped New York apartment; immediate success establishing his style.
- *The Sunshine Boys* (1972) — comedy of retired vaudeville comedians, demonstrates his ability to blend humour with melancholy and nostalgia.
- Won multiple Tony Awards; his plays have been translated into numerous languages and remain staples of community and university theatres.
- Screenwriter of equal note: adapted his own plays and wrote original film scripts (*The Goodbye Girl*, *The Poseidon Adventure*).
- His comedy is grounded in specificity: Jewish New York, the rhythms of married life, the pathos underlying suburban conformity.
