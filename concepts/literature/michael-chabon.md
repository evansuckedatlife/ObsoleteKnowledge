# Michael Chabon

## summary

*Michael Chabon* (b. 1964) is an American novelist and comic-book writer who blends pulp genres, Jewish heritage, and postmodern playfulness into inventive literary fantasies. His work treats comic books, pulp detective fiction, and genre adventure as serious artistic material while exploring Jewish identity and American myth. He represents a younger generation of Jewish-American writers integrating popular culture into high literature.

## you gotta know

- *The Amazing Adventures of Kavalier & Clay* (2000) — epic novel of comic-book artists in 1940s New York, won the Pulitzer Prize, became a major cultural landmark.
- *Wonder Boys* (1995) — debut novel of a dissolute professor and his precocious student, established his comic sensibility and depth.
- *The Yiddish Policemen's Union* (2007) — alternate-history noir where a Jewish settlement exists in Alaska, genre pastiche with philosophical weight.
- *Gentlemen of the Road* (2010) — adventure novel set in medieval Azerbaijan, demonstrates his commitment to popular storytelling as serious art.
- Won the Pulitzer Prize for fiction; has written acclaimed comic books (*Kabuki* graphic novel, *Runaways*).
- His prose combines literary sophistication with the plot momentum of pulp, making high art of genre excitement.
- Openly explores his own Jewish identity through characters and thematic preoccupations while refusing ethnic pigeonholing.
