# The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde

## summary

Robert Louis Stevenson's 1886 novella explores the Gothic terror of the divided self through the story of Dr. Henry Jekyll, a respectable physician who develops a potion enabling him to physically transform into Mr. Edward Hyde, a being embodying all his repressed desires and moral corruption. As the transformations become involuntary and Hyde gains control, Jekyll loses his humanity, leaving only a monster in his place.

## you gotta know

- Dr. Jekyll is a respected chemist and physician whose private life harbors shameful impulses he struggles to conceal beneath a veneer of respectability.
- He creates a chemical compound that allows him to separate his good and evil natures into two distinct physical forms, initially believing he can control the transformation.
- Mr. Hyde emerges as a creature of pure appetite and violence, committing acts of sadistic cruelty and murder without moral restraint.
- Jekyll initially enjoys his new freedom, using the potion to indulge in vices while maintaining his social reputation, but the transformations become increasingly frequent and involuntary.
- A mysterious attack on a prominent politician—revealed to be committed by Hyde—brings the creature under intense scrutiny, and Jekyll realizes he cannot stop the transformation.
- The novella is structured as a detective mystery: friends of Jekyll piece together the truth through observation and testimony before Jekyll's final confession.
- Stevenson's exploration of the duality within every person—the capacity for both good and evil—made the work a meditation on Victorian hypocrisy and the repression inherent in bourgeois morality.
