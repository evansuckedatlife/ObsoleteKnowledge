# Great Expectations

## summary

*Great Expectations*, serialized from 1860 to 1861 and narrated by the protagonist *Pip* in adulthood, follows a young orphan's transformation from country boy to gentleman to disillusioned man. Set partly in the Kent marshes and partly in London society, it explores aspiration, shame, class mobility, and the discovery that one's benefactor is not who was imagined.

## you gotta know

- *Pip* begins as a young boy in the marshes, helps an escaped convict *Magwitch*, and is summoned to Satis House to keep company with the wealthy *Miss Havisham* and her adopted daughter *Estella*.
- *Miss Havisham*, a woman jilted on her wedding day, has frozen Satis House in time at the moment of her abandonment; she manipulates Pip through Estella.
- *Estella*, beautiful, cold, and emotionally unavailable, embodies Pip's romantic ideal and becomes the object of his thwarted love.
- Pip's "great expectations" are shattered when he discovers his mysterious benefactor is not Miss Havisham but the convict *Magwitch*, whom he aided in childhood.
- *Herbert Pocket*, Pip's loyal friend and the moral anchor, contrasts with Pip's snobbish self-advancement.
- The novel concludes ambiguously: Pip encounters Estella again, suggesting possible reunion, yet the ending remains deliberately uncertain.
