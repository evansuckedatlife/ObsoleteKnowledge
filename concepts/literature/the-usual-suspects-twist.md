# The Usual Suspects

## summary

*The Usual Suspects* is a 1995 crime thriller in which five career criminals are gathered for an apparent heist, only to discover they have been orchestrated by a legendary criminal kingpin named Keyser Söze. The final twist reveals that Verbal Kint, the meek, seemingly inconsequential con artist being interrogated by police, is actually Söze himself. The film recontextualises every preceding scene, transforming the mild-mannered criminal into a terrifying mastermind.

## you gotta know

- Five career criminals are rounded up by police; Detective Kujan interrogates the sole survivor, Verbal Kint, a disabled con artist.
- As Kint narrates the story of their recent job, it becomes clear that an invisible force—a job organised by the notorious Keyser Söze—has eliminated four of the five.
- Throughout the interrogation, Kujan and the audience assume Kint is a low-level participant; his physical infirmities and nervous demeanor make him seem harmless.
- Söze is mythologised through the testimonies as a legendary figure—a criminal so ruthless that his reputation alone inspires terror and obedience.
- The final reveal: Kint stands up, his limp disappears, his stutter evaporates, and he walks away as a free man—he is Söze.
- The film is narrated almost entirely by Kint's interrogation, making the audience complicit in Kujan's (and their own) misreading of his character.
- Written by Christopher McQuarrie; directed by Bryan Singer.
