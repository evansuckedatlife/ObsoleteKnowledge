# Filippo Brunelleschi

## summary

*Filippo Brunelleschi* (1377–1446) was a Florentine architect and engineer who pioneered Renaissance architecture and linear perspective. His most famous achievement—the vast *dome* of Florence Cathedral—transformed architectural possibility through ingenious structural engineering and mathematical precision.

## you gotta know

- Designed and constructed the massive *dome of Florence Cathedral* (begun 1420), a feat of engineering without interior scaffolding that became one of history's greatest architectural triumphs.
- Invented (or perfected) *linear perspective*, the mathematical system for representing three-dimensional space on a flat surface, revolutionizing both architecture and painting.
- Designed the *Basilica of San Lorenzo* in Florence, establishing the principles of Renaissance proportion and harmonic design based on geometric ratios.
- Created the *Pazzi Chapel*, a small chapel embodying symmetry, classical orders, and harmonious proportions that defined early Renaissance aesthetics.
- Studied in Rome, measuring classical ruins and integrating Roman architectural principles (*columns*, *entablatures*, *proportional systems*) into Renaissance design.
- His dome employed a double-shell structure and herringbone brick pattern, combining structural necessity with geometric beauty—no precedent existed for such a span.
- Recognized as the first major architect of the Renaissance, bridging medieval Gothic with classical revival and establishing Florence as the artistic center of Europe.
