# Museo del Prado

## summary

The *Prado*, Spain's national art museum in Madrid, opened in 1819 and showcases the finest Spanish paintings alongside works collected by Spanish royalty across centuries. Its unparalleled holdings of *Velázquez*, *Goya*, and *Bosch* make it indispensable for understanding European painting.

## you gotta know

- Founded by Ferdinand VII to house the Spanish royal collection; the Neoclassical building by Juan de Villanueva remains an architectural icon.
- Holds Velázquez's *Las Meninas*, arguably the most influential painting in Western art, alongside his royal portraits and court scenes.
- Contains Goya's *Black Paintings*, searing murals transferred from his house, which revolutionized Romantic and modern aesthetics.
- Bosch's *Garden of Earthly Delights* is the world's most significant holding of the 15th-century master's visionary and deeply enigmatic works.
- Strong Spanish Gothic, Mannerist, and Baroque presence; also significant holdings of Flemish and Italian masters purchased by Spanish kings.
- The building's triple-tiered architecture was innovative; recent 2007 expansions by Rafael Moneo added modern gallery space without overshadowing the original.

## connections
