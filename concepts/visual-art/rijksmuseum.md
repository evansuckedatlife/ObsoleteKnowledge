# Rijksmuseum

## summary

The *Rijksmuseum* in Amsterdam, the Netherlands' national art museum, was founded in 1798 and housed in its iconic 1880s Gothic Revival building by Pierre Cuypers. It preserves the world's finest collection of Dutch Golden Age painting, notably *Rembrandt* and *Vermeer*, and is the most visited museum in the Netherlands.

## you gotta know

- Built in 1885 as a symbol of Dutch national pride; the building itself is a major monument with distinctive architectural features including a grand staircase and soaring halls.
- Rembrandt's *Night Watch* is the centerpiece—a vast Baroque masterpiece that dominates the main gallery and encapsulates the painter's dramatic chiaroscuro and psychological depth.
- Vermeer's *The Milkmaid* and *Woman Reading a Letter* exemplify Dutch domestic interiors and luminous, understated technique.
- Comprehensive holdings of Golden Age painters including Frans Hals, Jacob van Ruisdael, and Jan Steen document the 17th-century commercial and cultural flourishing of the Dutch Republic.
- Also holds Delftware ceramics, silverware, and decorative arts from the period, contextualizing the museum within Dutch material culture.
- Underwent a decade-long renovation (2003–2013) with glass bridge additions by Cruz and Ortiz, modernizing infrastructure while preserving its 19th-century character.
