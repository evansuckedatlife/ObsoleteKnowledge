# Burj Khalifa

## summary

The *Burj Khalifa* is a 828-meter (2,717-foot) supertall skyscraper completed in 2010 in Dubai, holding the record as the world's tallest building by architectural height since its completion. Its mixed-use design combines residential, commercial, and hotel functions within a tapered Y-shaped footprint inspired by desert plants.

## you gotta know

- Designed by American architect *Adrian Smith* with the Chicago firm Skidmore, Owings and Merrill, representing a synthesis of Western engineering and Middle Eastern urban ambition.
- Contains 163 floors above ground plus additional floors below; the facade uses reflective glass and anodized aluminum to minimize heat absorption in the desert climate.
- The tower's height of 828 meters (compared to the Willis Tower's 442 meters) represents a technological leap enabled by advances in wind resistance and structural engineering.
- Houses the *At the Top* observation deck at 124 stories, affording views across the Persian Gulf and the sprawling development of Dubai.
- Consumes approximately 330,000 tons of materials and required five years of construction; serves as both a luxury residence and commercial flagship for Dubai.
- Symbolizes the 21st-century shift of architectural ambition from Western cities to the Arabian Gulf and emerging global economies.

## connections
