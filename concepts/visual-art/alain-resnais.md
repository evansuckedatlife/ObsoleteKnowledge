# Alain Resnais

## summary

*Alain Resnais* was a French filmmaker whose obsession with memory, narrative structure, and the gap between word and image created some of cinema's most formally sophisticated and philosophically dense works. Beginning with documentaries, he moved into features that fragmented causality and chronology, exploring how the mind reconstructs the past and constructs identity.

## you gotta know

- Directed *Hiroshima Mon Amour* (1959), a rhapsodic meditation on memory and love between a French woman and Japanese man, interweaving atomic-bomb testimony with romantic narrative.
- Made *Last Year at Marienbad* (1961), a puzzle-box narrative where past and present collapse; a man insists a woman promised to run away with him at a resort, though she denies memory of him—reality becomes ambiguous.
- Began his career with documentary shorts, including *Night and Fog* (1955), a devastating essay on the Holocaust using archival footage and contemporary images.
- Pioneered the use of voice-over as a second narrative layer; characters' thoughts and memories inhabit the soundtrack while images occupy the screen.
- Collaborated with writers Alain Robbe-Grillet and Marguerite Duras, adapting the nouveau roman's foregrounding of style and the instability of narrative itself.
- Composed films using visual rhyming and symphonic structure rather than plot; he saw film as music rather than literature.
- Later work integrated musical theatre and modernist techniques; he remained formally adventurous until his death at 91.

## connections

- [[marguerite-duras]] — writer and collaborator on *Hiroshima Mon Amour*; her poetic approach to language and time shaped his narrative philosophy.
- [[jean-luc-godard]] — contemporary formalist; both questioned narrative convention, though Resnais retained more lyricism.
- [[nouveau-roman]] — the literary movement that influenced his approach to ambiguity and the instability of memory.
- [[agnes-varda]] — contemporary explorer of subjective time and fragmented narrative.
