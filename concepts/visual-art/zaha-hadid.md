# Zaha Hadid

## summary

*Zaha Hadid* (1950–2016) was an Iraqi-British architect pioneering parametric and computational design to create sweeping, flowing forms. Her futuristic buildings defied Euclidean geometry, establishing her as a leading visionary of 21st-century architecture before her unexpected death.

## you gotta know

- Designed the *MAXXI* (National Museum of 21st Century Arts) in Rome (2010), a fluid concrete structure with soaring interior galleries that seems to defy traditional museum constraints.
- Created the *Heydar Aliyev Center* in Baku, Azerbaijan (2012), a smooth, billowing white building with no sharp corners, epitomizing her flowing, organic computational aesthetic.
- Pioneered *parametric design*—using mathematical algorithms and computational modeling to generate complex, efficient forms impossible to design by hand.
- Designed the *London Aquatics Centre* (2011) for the Olympics, a steel-and-membrane structure evoking the fluidity of water while meeting Olympic competition standards.
- Studied under *Rem Koolhaas* at the Architectural Association, absorbing deconstructivist principles before innovating her own flowing language.
- Her work rejected the box-like International Style, instead creating buildings that appeared to move and shift, celebrating curves and motion.
- Won the *Pritzker Prize* (2004), the first woman to win architecture's highest honor, though many of her landmark works were completed after.
