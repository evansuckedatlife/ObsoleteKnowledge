# Empire State Building

## summary

The *Empire State Building* is a 102-story Art Deco skyscraper completed in 1931 in Manhattan, rising 1,250 feet to the roof and 1,454 feet with its antenna. It dominated as the world's tallest building for over 40 years and remains an iconic symbol of American industrial ambition and 1930s modernism.

## you gotta know

- Designed by the architectural firm Shreve, Lamb & Harmon and completed in just 410 days, an engineering feat for the Depression era.
- Rises 102 stories with 6,514 windows; its Art Deco aesthetic features limestone and granite cladding with aluminum and stainless steel accents.
- Held the title of world's tallest building from 1931 until 1972, when the Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower) was completed in Chicago.
- Contains approximately 2.7 million square feet of office and commercial space across its 37 million cubic feet of volume.
- Anchors the Manhattan skyline from multiple vantage points and serves as a major tourist destination with an observation deck on the 86th floor.
- Constructed during the Great Depression, symbolizing American economic resilience and capitalist optimism during economic crisis.
