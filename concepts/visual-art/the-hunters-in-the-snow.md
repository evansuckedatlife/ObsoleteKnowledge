# The Hunters in the Snow

## summary

Pieter Bruegel the Elder's 1565 *Hunters in the Snow* depicts a winter landscape with hunters and dogs returning from the field, overlooking a frozen valley dotted with villagers skating and playing on ice. The painting is celebrated for its vast panoramic vista, its keen observation of peasant life, and its atmospheric mastery of cold, distance, and seasonal transition.

## you gotta know

- Measures approximately 117 by 162 centimetres and is housed in the Kunsthistorisches Museum in Vienna.
- Part of Bruegel's series of months and seasons; this one represents December or January in the peasant calendar.
- The composition emphasises extreme depth: hunters in the foreground descend into a plunging valley where ice-skaters, villages, and mountains recede toward a luminous horizon.
- The painting showcases Bruegel's fascination with peasant life and labour—hunting, farming, festival, play—rendered with both humour and dignity.
- His intricate observation of textures, from snow and ice to the texture of woollen clothing, creates an almost ethnographic precision.
- Bruegel's landscape tradition influenced generations of Flemish and Dutch painters and helped establish the winter landscape as a major European genre.
