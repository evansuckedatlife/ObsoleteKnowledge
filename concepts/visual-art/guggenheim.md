# Guggenheim

## summary

The *Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum* on Fifth Avenue in New York, completed in 1959, is Frank Lloyd Wright's only major museum commission and an architectural landmark that redefines how art is displayed and experienced. Its spiral rotunda structure and modernist holdings make it as much a destination for architecture as for art.

## you gotta know

- Designed by Frank Lloyd Wright and completed posthumously; the building's continuous spiral gallery is Wright's masterpiece—a unified, organic form meant to be experienced as a totality.
- The cantilevered white exterior, circular footprint, and interior spiral ramp are internationally iconic; the museum building is as celebrated as its artworks.
- Founded by Solomon Guggenheim and Baroness Hilla Rebay; the collection emphasizes abstract art, modernism, and contemporary practice, particularly non-objective and geometric abstraction.
- Holdings include major works by Kandinsky, Mondrian, and Calder; also strong in 20th-century European and American modernism and contemporary art.
- The spiral rotunda allows visitors to ascend (or descend, depending on use) through art historically; the central void creates unique sightlines and spatial relationships unmatched in traditional rectangular museums.
- Expanded by Gwathmey Siegel & Associates (1992) with a ten-story tower addition preserving Wright's exterior and vision while doubling internal gallery space.
