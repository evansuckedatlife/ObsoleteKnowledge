# The Louvre

## summary

The *Louvre* is the world's largest art museum by floor area, located in Paris and housed in a former royal palace. It spans from ancient Egyptian antiquities to 19th-century painting and sculpture, serving as the enduring benchmark for encyclopedic Western museums.

## you gotta know

- Built as a royal residence starting in the 12th century; became the principal French royal palace under Louis XIV and later the national art repository after the French Revolution.
- Houses over 38,000 artworks, including the *Mona Lisa* and *Venus de Milo*, making it the most visited museum in the world.
- The iconic glass pyramid entrance, designed by I.M. Pei and completed in 1989, modernized access while sparking initial controversy over its aesthetic.
- The *Nike of Samothrace* (Winged Victory), a Hellenistic marble sculpture, and the *Denon Wing* are organisational anchors for Greek, Roman, and Renaissance holdings.
- Medieval fortress origins visible in the basement; the Louvre's evolution mirrors French royal patronage and the museum movement itself.
- Occupies over 782,000 square meters, requiring visitors to choose galleries rather than attempt exhaustive viewing in a single day.

## connections

- [[mona-lisa]] — its most famous artwork and a symbol of Renaissance technique and mystery.
- [[venus-de-milo]] — Hellenistic marble that exemplifies the encyclopedic reach of Western museums.
- [[french-neoclassicism]] — benefited from and shaped the aesthetic canon of 18th–19th century France.
- [[museum-movement]] — the Louvre's transformation from palace to public institution set the template for modern museums.
