# National Gallery, London

## summary

The *National Gallery* in London, founded in 1824, preserves one of the world's most comprehensive collections of European painting from the 13th to 19th centuries. Located in Trafalgar Square, it was Britain's first major public art collection and remains a model for democratic museum access.

## you gotta know

- Founded to purchase John Julius Angerstein's private collection; originally housed in his house before moving to purpose-built premises designed by William Wilkins in 1838.
- The collection spans Italian Renaissance masters (Botticelli, Leonardo, Raphael), Northern European Old Masters (Van Eyck, Bosch), and Spanish, French, and British schools comprehensively.
- Vermeer's *Woman Standing at a Virginals* is one of the world's most celebrated paintings; also houses Constable's *The Hay Wain* and Turner's seascapes.
- Acquisition policy emphasizes diversity and comparative display: paintings grouped by artistic relationships rather than strict chronology, encouraging active looking and interpretation.
- The building's main gallery is a famous Victorian neoclassical space; recent extensions (Sainsbury Wing, 1991, by Venturi Scott Brown) added Renaissance galleries without overwhelming the original structure.
- Offered free admission to permanent collections from its founding, a principle maintained and influential on museums globally.
