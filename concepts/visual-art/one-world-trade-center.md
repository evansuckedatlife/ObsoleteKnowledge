# One World Trade Center

## summary

*One World Trade Center* (also known as the Freedom Tower) is a 94-story supertall skyscraper completed in 2014 in lower Manhattan, rising 1,776 feet including its spire. Symbolizing American resilience and renewal, it occupies the primary site of the former World Trade Center destroyed on September 11, 2001.

## you gotta know

- Designed by architect *David Childs* of Skidmore, Owings and Merrill, the tower's height of 1,776 feet references the year of American independence.
- The spire rises 408 feet above the roof, making it one of the tallest structures in the Western Hemisphere and a dominant feature of the Manhattan skyline.
- Houses office space, the *9/11 Memorial & Museum* at the base, and the *One World Observatory* on the 100th and 101st floors.
- The facade uses prismatic glass to reflect light and the sky, symbolizing transparency and openness; the reflective quality creates a dynamic appearance throughout the day.
- Incorporates extensive security features and sustainability measures, including LED lighting and wind turbines, reflecting 21st-century building standards.
- Its completion marked the closure of a decade-long memorial period and the economic revitalization of lower Manhattan.
