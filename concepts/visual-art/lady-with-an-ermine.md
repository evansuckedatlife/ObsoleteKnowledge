# Lady with an Ermine

## summary

*Lady with an Ermine* is Leonardo's portrait of Cecilia Gallerani, the young mistress of Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan, painted circa 1489–1491. The work exemplifies Leonardo's mastery of portraiture through its dynamic composition, psychological penetration, and the symbiotic presence of the ermine—a symbol of purity and power—held by the sitter.

## you gotta know

- Oil on walnut panel, approximately 54 × 39 cm (21 × 15 inches), now housed in the Czartoryski Museum in Kraków, Poland.
- The young woman turns gracefully in three-quarter view, her hand gently restraining the white ermine—a playful visual pun on the sitter's name (Gallerani) and the duke's emblem.
- Leonardo captures a moment of psychological engagement: Cecilia glances over her shoulder with an expression combining confidence, intelligence, and subtle self-awareness.
- The ermine's coat, rendered with meticulous detail, contrasts with the soft sfumato of the background, emphasizing the interplay between precise observation and atmospheric mystery.
- The work was likely commissioned by Ludovico Sforza to commemorate his liaison, yet Leonardo elevates it beyond a mere love-gift into a profound study of feminine grace and presence.
- The painting demonstrates Leonardo's characteristic spiral composition, with the body and gaze creating dynamic tension within a geometrically balanced frame.
- The background is simple—dark and undefined—allowing the figure to project presence, a compositional choice that became influential in Renaissance portraiture.
