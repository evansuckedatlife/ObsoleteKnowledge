# Tate Modern

## summary

*Tate Modern*, located in London in a converted power station on the Thames, opened in 2000 and is the world's leading museum of modern and contemporary art outside the U.S. It houses over 70,000 works and has transformed both the institutional museum and London's South Bank into a cultural destination.

## you gotta know

- Housed in the Bankside Power Station, a 1950s industrial building redesigned by Herzog & de Meuron; the conversion preserves the industrial aesthetic while creating gallery spaces.
- The iconic *Turbine Hall*, a soaring entrance space originally housing generators, serves as a dynamic venue for large-scale contemporary installations and performance.
- Strong holdings of European Modernism, Russian Constructivism, Surrealism, and Abstract Expressionism; also pioneering in global and non-Western contemporary art.
- Free admission to permanent collections established it as a public institution for broad audiences, a model followed worldwide after its success.
- Opened the *Millennium Bridge* (pedestrian suspension bridge designed by Norman Foster and Anthony Caro) connecting it to St. Paul's Cathedral, remaking London's riverfront.
- The *Switch House* extension by Herzog & de Meuron (2016) added 60% more gallery space and established Tate Modern as the world's most visited modern art museum.
