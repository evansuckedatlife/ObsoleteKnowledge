# The Anatomy Lesson of Dr. Tulp

## summary

Rembrandt's 1632 *The Anatomy Lesson of Dr. Tulp* depicts the celebrated Amsterdam anatomist Nicolaes Tulp dissecting a cadaver before a gathered audience of physicians and students. The painting transforms a documentary record into a drama of enlightenment, with Tulp's gesture and the revealed anatomy commanding the eye through masterful dramatic lighting.

## you gotta know

- Commissioned by the Amsterdam Guild of Surgeons, the painting was hung in their guildhall and measures approximately 1.7 by 2.3 metres.
- Shows Dr. Tulp demonstrating the muscles and tendons of a cadaver's arm, with onlookers leaning in with expressions ranging from fascination to squeamishness.
- Rembrandt's use of chiaroscuro—bright light on the corpse and Tulp's face, shadow on the intent observers—creates a theatre-like intensity.
- The anatomical accuracy reflects the era's scientific revolution and the prestige of anatomy as a cutting-edge intellectual pursuit in Amsterdam.
- Each physician was charged a fee to appear in the portrait, funding both the guild and Rembrandt's commission; their placement and prominence reflect rank and payment.
- Hangs in the Mauritshuis museum in The Hague.
