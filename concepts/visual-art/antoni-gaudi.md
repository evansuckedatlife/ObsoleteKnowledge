# Antoni Gaudí

## summary

*Antoni Gaudí* (1852–1926) was a Catalan architect whose organic, sculptural approach created fantastical buildings that seemed to grow rather than stand. His curves, natural forms, and integration of ceramic and stone made him the defining figure of Catalan Modernisme, a movement parallel to Art Nouveau.

## you gotta know

- Designed the *Sagrada Família* basilica in Barcelona (1883–1926), an ongoing construction blending Gothic and organic forms, with towering spires and intricate stone carving that has become Barcelona's icon.
- Created *Park Güell* (1900–1914), a terraced garden suburb with undulating benches, colorful ceramic mosaics, and organic architectural forms nestled into a Catalan hillside.
- Pioneered the use of *trencadís*, a mosaic technique using broken ceramic tiles and salvaged materials, creating richly patterned, colorful surfaces throughout his work.
- Designed *Casa Batlló* and *Casa Milà* in Barcelona, apartment buildings with rippling, bone-like stone facades and undulating balconies that blur the line between architecture and sculpture.
- Rejected Cartesian geometry and straight lines, instead using parabolic arches, hyperbolic surfaces, and forms derived from nature (bones, shells, plants).
- Studied the structural properties of catenary curves (the natural form of hanging cables), applying them inverted as load-bearing elements.
- His late work became increasingly religious, and he lived in virtual solitude at the Sagrada Família, perfecting its construction.
