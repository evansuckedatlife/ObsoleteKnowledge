## summary

*Lorenzo Ghiberti* (c. 1378–1455) was a Florentine sculptor and goldsmith whose bronze doors for the Florence Baptistry became a defining monument of the early Renaissance. His narrative reliefs—depicting biblical scenes with architectural depth and naturalistic figures—influenced an entire generation and proved sculpture's capacity for complex storytelling.

## you gotta know

- Designed and cast the *North Doors* (1403–24) of the Florence Baptistry, depicting New Testament scenes with elegant Gothic-Renaissance hybrid style.
- Created the celebrated *East Doors* (1425–52), later called the *Gates of Paradise*, with ten Old Testament scenes framed in mathematical perspective.
- Pioneered *linear perspective* in relief sculpture, creating architectural landscapes and vistas within shallow bronze fields.
- Mastered intricate *narrative relief*, layering multiple figures and temporal moments within unified compositions.
- Worked as both sculptor and goldsmith, bringing jeweler's precision and decorative sophistication to large-scale bronzes.
- Won the famous 1401 competition for the Baptistry doors, establishing his reputation and launching the Florentine Renaissance.

## connections

- [[baptistry-doors]] — his two monumental commissions that defined Renaissance architectural sculpture.
- [[florence-cathedral]] — the Baptistry is part of Florence's cathedral complex, center of his patronage.
- [[donatello]] — contemporary and fellow pioneer of Renaissance realism, though Ghiberti favored more ornate narrative.
- [[renaissance-art]] — foundational sculptor of the Florentine Renaissance style.
