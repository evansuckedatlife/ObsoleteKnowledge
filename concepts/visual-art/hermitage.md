# Hermitage

## summary

The *Hermitage* in Saint Petersburg, housed in the Winter Palace and adjacent buildings, is the largest art museum in Russia and one of the world's oldest, founded by Catherine the Great in 1764. Its vast collection spans ancient civilizations through the 19th century, with particular strength in European Old Masters.

## you gotta know

- Built as Catherine the Great's private collection and adjacent galleries; the Winter Palace complex itself is a baroque architectural masterpiece.
- Contains over 3 million artworks, including masterpieces by Rembrandt, Caravaggio, Titian, Rubens, and Matisse—testament to royal acquisition over two centuries.
- Major holdings of Dutch and Flemish paintings, Impressionist and Post-Impressionist works, and Russian imperial acquisitions from across Europe and the Mediterranean.
- The building's layout evolved organically as wings were added; visitors navigate through opulent rooms designed as galleries themselves.
- Became a public museum after the October Revolution in 1917; the Bolsheviks transformed it into a monument to revolutionary people's culture.
- The Hermitage Theatre, an 18th-century structure, is a distinct building within the complex; the basement archaeology museum preserves finds from Russian imperial excavations.
