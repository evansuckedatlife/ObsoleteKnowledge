# Auguste Rodin

## summary

*Auguste Rodin* (1840–1917) was a French sculptor who bridged the 19th and 20th centuries, founding modern sculpture through psychological realism and expressive surface finish. His rough bronze castings and emotionally raw figures—*The Thinker*, *The Kiss*, *The Gates of Hell*—broke with neoclassical idealization and opened sculpture to modern feeling.

## you gotta know

- Created *The Thinker* (1880), a hunched nude pondering existence, later adopted as the symbol of philosophy and contemplation.
- Conceived *The Gates of Hell* (1880–90), a monumental bronze door inspired by Dante, from which dozens of individual figures evolved into independent works.
- Sculpted *The Kiss* (1888–98), a marble pair whose intertwined intimacy scandalized conservative critics but epitomized modern sensuality.
- Pioneered the *non-finito* — deliberately rough, fragmentary bronze surfaces that suggest emerging form and raw emotion.
- Established the modern sculptor's studio as a laboratory, casting multiples and allowing assistants and molds to generate variations.
- Engaged a famous sculptural collaboration with *Camille Claudel*, a gifted sculptor and his lover whose own works were overshadowed by his fame.

## connections

- [[the-thinker]] — his most recognizable work, now an icon of philosophy.
- [[camille-claudel]] — artist and collaborator who was both influenced and eclipsed by Rodin.
- [[michelangelo]] — Renaissance predecessor Rodin revered and consciously departed from.
- [[the-gates-of-hell]] — his largest project, a textbook of modern sculptural innovation.
