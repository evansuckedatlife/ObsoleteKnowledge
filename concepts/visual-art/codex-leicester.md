# Codex Leicester

## summary

The *Codex Leicester* is a 36-page scientific and technical notebook compiled by Leonardo from 1506–1510, now held in the private collection of Bill Gates, making it the most expensive historical document ever owned. Filled with Leonardo's dense mirror-script annotations, observational sketches, and speculative theories on geology, hydrology, astronomy, and cosmology, it stands as a monument to his restless scientific inquiry and visionary imagination.

## you gotta know

- Handwritten notebook in Leonardo's characteristic reversed Italian script (mirror writing), with detailed drawings throughout illustrating observations and theoretical concepts.
- Addresses vast topics: the nature of fossils and the antiquity of the earth, the circulation of water through mountains and valleys, the properties of light and shadow, the moon's surface.
- Explores geological time and the evidence of seas where mountains now stand, an insight anticipating modern uniformitarianism by centuries.
- Contains wild speculative ideas alongside careful observations—Leonardo theorizes about the moon's atmosphere, the possibility of life beyond earth, and the sun's role in powering the cosmos.
- Demonstrates Leonardo's habit of gathering knowledge unsystematically—pages leap from hydraulic engineering sketches to astronomical observation to natural philosophy without linear progression.
- The notebook reveals Leonardo's belief that understanding nature requires observation, reason, and imagination in equal measure, not authority or tradition.
- Purchased by Bill Gates in 1994 for $30.8 million at auction, making it a rare example of a Leonardo original in private hands.
