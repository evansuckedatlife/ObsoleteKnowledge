# Museum of Modern Art

## summary

*MoMA*, the *Museum of Modern Art* in New York, opened in 1929 and established the canonical taste for 20th-century modernism and contemporary practice. Its collection defines what "modern art" means: painting, sculpture, photography, film, and design from Impressionism onward.

## you gotta know

- Founded by Abby Aldrich Rockefeller and others; located in Midtown Manhattan since 1939, with multiple expansions through the 1980s–2000s.
- Holds the world's most influential collection of 20th-century painting and sculpture, including Picasso's *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon* and works by Matisse, Pollock, and Rothko.
- Pioneered the museum exhibition of photography, film, and graphic design as fine art; its photography collection is canonical and world-class.
- The building by Philip Goodwin and Edward Durrell Stone (1939) became a Modernist landmark; recent expansions by Diller Scofidio + Renfro (2019).
- Emphasis on living artists and contemporary practice sets it apart from purely historical collections; hosts provocative and sometimes controversial exhibitions.
- Owns works by nearly every major modernist and contemporary artist; its *Starry Night* (Van Gogh) and *The Persistence of Memory* (Dalí) are globally recognizable.
