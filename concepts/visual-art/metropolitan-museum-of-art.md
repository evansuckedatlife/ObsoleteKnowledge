# Metropolitan Museum of Art

## summary

The *Metropolitan Museum of Art*, located on Fifth Avenue in New York City, is one of the world's largest and most comprehensive art museums, spanning from ancient Egypt to contemporary work. Founded in 1870, it holds over 1.7 million objects and epitomizes the American civic museum tradition.

## you gotta know

- Opened to the public in 1871 in a Beaux-Arts building designed by Richard Morris Hunt and expanded multiple times; now spans two city blocks.
- Houses the world's largest collection of American art, as well as major holdings of Egyptian, Greek, Roman, and Asian antiquities.
- The *Temple of Dendur*, an ancient Egyptian sandstone structure relocated to the museum in 1978, is its architectural and curatorial centerpiece.
- Strong medieval arms and armor collection, including royal and knightly pieces; also significant costume and textile departments.
- Nearly free admission with suggested "pay what you wish" model for New York residents (since 1870s, a founding principle).
- The American Wing preserves furniture, decorative arts, and paintings that document U.S. cultural development from colonial times onward.
