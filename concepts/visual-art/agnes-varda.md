# Agnès Varda

## summary

*Agnès Varda* was a Belgian-French director and pioneer of the cinéma-vérité documentary style who brought radical subjectivity and poetic observation to both fiction and non-fiction. Her films explore female consciousness, memory, and the texture of everyday life with a visual sensibility influenced by painting and photography, making her a singular voice within and adjacent to the French New Wave.

## you gotta know

- Directed *Cléo from 5 to 7* (1962), following a woman through 90 minutes of Paris as she awaits a cancer diagnosis—a real-time exploration of fear, beauty, and female interiority.
- Made *Vagabond* (1985), a Palme d'Or winner that backtracks through the life of a young woman found frozen in a ditch, interviewing those she encountered.
- Pioneered *cinéma-vérité*—direct cinema using lightweight cameras and minimal crews—giving her documentary work an immediacy and authenticity.
- Blended documentary and fiction; *The Gleaners and I* (2000) intermixes philosophy, portraiture, and personal reflection, challenging genre boundaries.
- Known for her visual sophistication—colours, compositions, the formal beauty of the image—even in realist contexts.
- Remained a politically engaged filmmaker; her work often features working-class characters, women, and those society marginalises.
- Continued making films into her nineties, maintaining formal invention and intellectual vitality.

## connections

- [[jean-luc-godard]] — fellow New Wave figure; both questioned narrative and authorship, though Varda emphasised collective and personal memory.
- [[robert-bresson]] — shared ascetic sensibility and interest in non-professional acting.
- [[alain-resnais]] — contemporary explorer of memory and subjective time.
- [[feminism-in-film]] — her work centring female perspective and experience.
