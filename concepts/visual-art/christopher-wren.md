# Christopher Wren

## summary

*Sir Christopher Wren* (1632–1723) was an English polymath—mathematician, astronomer, and architect—whose work defined English Baroque architecture. Following the Great Fire of London (1666), he redesigned the city and created masterpieces that shaped British architecture for centuries.

## you gotta know

- Designed and oversaw construction of *St. Paul's Cathedral* (1668–1710), a Baroque masterpiece featuring a soaring dome and classical portico that became London's most iconic building.
- Rebuilt 50+ churches in London following the 1666 Great Fire, each a small jewel of Baroque design, establishing a coherent architectural style for post-fire London.
- Designed the *Royal Observatory* in Greenwich (1675), combining scientific function with architectural elegance, establishing a new typology for scientific buildings.
- Pioneered the use of classical orders and Baroque ornament in English architecture, adapting continental European style to English tradition.
- Studied mathematics and astronomy, bringing scientific rigor and geometric proportion to his designs.
- Designed secular buildings including *Kensington Palace* (additions) and *Royal Naval College, Greenwich*, expanding beyond religious commissions.
- His *City churches* (small urban parish churches) became models for economical, elegant sacred architecture, many still standing and used today.
