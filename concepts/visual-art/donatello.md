## summary

*Donatello* (c. 1386–1466) was an early Renaissance Florentine sculptor who revolutionized the art through anatomical realism, emotional intensity, and technical innovation in marble and bronze. His figures abandon Gothic linearity for a new naturalism and psychological depth, establishing sculpture as a primary Renaissance medium.

## you gotta know

- Created the first freestanding bronze *David* (c. 1440–43), a youthful, unarmored figure that shocked audiences with its nudity and introspective pose.
- Sculpted *Saint George* (c. 1416) for Florence's Orsanmichele, a vigilant warrior-saint whose tense musculature and alert gaze defined Renaissance masculinity.
- Pioneered *bronze casting* as a viable sculptural medium in the Renaissance, moving beyond the dominance of marble.
- Innovated *linear perspective* in relief sculpture, creating illusions of depth within shallow space (e.g., his *Saint George and the Dragon* relief).
- Worked for the *Medici family*, becoming the foundational sculptor of Florence's artistic identity.
- Created expressive ecclesiastical works like the marble *Mary Magdalene* that combined ascetic suffering with raw human pathos.
