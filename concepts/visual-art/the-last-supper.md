## summary

*The Last Supper* is Leonardo's monumental mural depicting Jesus' final meal with his apostles before the Crucifixion, painted circa 1495–1498 in the refectory of the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan. The work revolutionized religious art through its psychological depth, capturing the apostles' varied reactions to Christ's announcement of his betrayal, and its pioneering use of linear perspective.

## you gotta know

- A tempera and oil mural (though not true fresco) approximately 4.6 × 8.8 meters (15 × 29 feet) on the refectory's north wall.
- Captures the moment of maximum psychological tension: each of the twelve apostles reacts distinctly to Jesus' declaration that one will betray him.
- Employs mathematical linear perspective with the vanishing point centered precisely on Jesus' head, making him the focal point of both geometric and dramatic space.
- The composition divides the apostles into four groups of three, creating rhythmic balance while individualizing each figure's emotional response—shock, denial, confusion, guilt.
- Deteriorated significantly over the centuries due to Leonardo's experimental tempera technique on a dry wall rather than wet plaster, but remains a pilgrimage site for art lovers.
- Leonardo may have spent over three years on the work, leaving it incomplete in some areas and continuously revising during execution.
- The work's influence on Western art is immeasurable—it set the standard for narrative religious art and the representation of emotional complexity.
