# Home Insurance Building

## summary

The *Home Insurance Building* is a ten-story office building completed in 1885 in Chicago, widely recognized as the world's first steel-frame skyscraper. Though modest in height by modern standards, its revolutionary iron and steel skeleton construction transformed architecture and enabled the vertical city.

## you gotta know

- Designed by architects *William Le Baron Jenney*, it pioneered the iron skeleton framework that transferred loads to the frame rather than relying on thick load-bearing walls.
- The use of internal iron supports and external masonry curtain wall created a new structural paradigm, allowing buildings to rise much higher without massive walls at the base.
- Standing only 138 feet tall (ten stories), it is dwarfed by subsequent towers but represents the crucial invention that made supertall buildings possible.
- The building's success inspired a generation of Chicago architects (including Louis Sullivan and others) who developed the distinctive "Chicago School" style.
- Demolished in 1931 to make way for the Field Building, erasing the birthplace of modern skyscraper technology.
- The invention of the steel frame is considered as significant as the development of reinforced concrete and is foundational to modern urban architecture worldwide.

- [[chicago]] — the birthplace of the skyscraper and the epicenter of early steel-frame innovation.
