# Shanghai Tower

## summary

The *Shanghai Tower* is a 128-story supertall skyscraper completed in 2015 in Shanghai, China, rising 2,073 feet and currently the world's second-tallest building. Its spiraling form and advanced environmental systems represent the cutting edge of 21st-century sustainable high-rise design.

## you gotta know

- Designed by the international firm *Gensler*, the tower's distinctive twisted silhouette reduces wind loading and creates a dynamic appearance as the building spirals toward the sky.
- At 632 meters tall, it is the tallest building in China and surpassed only by the Burj Khalifa worldwide, reflecting China's ascendant economic and architectural ambitions.
- The facade incorporates a double-skin envelope with photovoltaic panels and a rainwater collection system that supplies energy and water to building operations.
- Houses office space, luxury residences, a hotel, and a retail complex; the observation deck spans multiple floors with 360-degree views of Shanghai and the Yangtze River.
- Contains China's first hotel-occupancy observation deck, advanced fire suppression systems, and earthquake engineering tailored to the building's height and location.
- Completed during China's rapid urbanization and represents the country's emergence as a global economic and technological power in the 21st century.
