# Flatiron Building

## summary

The *Flatiron Building* is a 22-story triangular office building completed in 1902 in Manhattan, located at the intersection of Broadway, Fifth Avenue, and 23rd Street. Its distinctive wedge shape and Beaux-Arts Renaissance Revival styling make it one of the most iconic and photographed buildings in the world.

## you gotta know

- Designed by architect *Daniel Burnham* of the Chicago School, the building's triangular footprint fills an entire lot where two major streets converge at an acute angle.
- Standing 307 feet tall, it was among the tallest buildings in the world at its completion and remains one of the most recognizable due to its unique silhouette.
- The facade of limestone, granite, and terra-cotta features Beaux-Arts detailing with arched windows and ornamental cornices, creating a highly sculptural appearance.
- Named for its resemblance to a clothes iron, particularly when viewed from afar; the building's pointed north edge created wind vortexes that caused skirts to blow up, earning it early notoriety.
- The triangular plan was an engineering feat for its time, requiring careful structural calculation to distribute loads across the narrow footprint.
- Originally headquarters for the Fuller Company but became best known as an architectural monument and tourist destination, featured in countless photographs and artworks.
