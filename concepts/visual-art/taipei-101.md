# Taipei 101

## summary

*Taipei 101* is a 101-story supertall skyscraper completed in 2004 in Taipei, Taiwan, rising 1,667 feet and holding the world's tallest-building record for six years. Its segmented pagoda-inspired design incorporates traditional Chinese architectural language with cutting-edge wind-resistance engineering.

## you gotta know

- Designed by Taiwanese architect *C.Y. Lee*, the tower's form divides into eight segments representing the number eight, which is auspicious in Chinese culture and reinforces the connection between tradition and modernity.
- At 508 meters tall, it surpassed the Petronas Twin Towers and held the record until the Burj Khalifa opened in 2010.
- Features a massive tuned mass damper (660 tons) that moves in response to wind and earthquakes, making it one of the world's most seismically engineered buildings.
- The facade uses a golden-green tinted glass that references Taiwan's landscape and reduces heat absorption in the subtropical climate.
- Houses office space, luxury residences, and a shopping mall; the observation deck on the 89th floor is a major tourist attraction.
- Completed at the peak of Taiwan's economic expansion and serves as a symbol of the island's technological prowess and cultural confidence.
