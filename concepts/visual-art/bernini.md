# Gian Lorenzo Bernini

## summary

*Gian Lorenzo Bernini* (1598–1680) was an Italian Baroque sculptor and architect who dominated 17th-century Rome, defining the Baroque aesthetic of movement, drama, and sensory richness. His marble figures seem to writhe with psychological intensity and theatrical energy, while his architectural commissions reshaped the papal capital.

## you gotta know

- Sculpted *Apollo and Daphne* (1622–25), a marble group capturing the moment of metamorphosis in exquisite detail and movement.
- Created his *David* (1623–24), a muscular, tense figure about to hurl a stone—a dynamic reinterpretation of Michelangelo's iconic statue.
- Designed the baldachin (canopy) over the altar of *St. Peter's Basilica*, a bronze structure combining architecture and sculpture in theatrical grandeur.
- Master of *marble drapery*—capable of rendering cloth so delicate it seems almost weightless and ethereal.
- Architect as well as sculptor: designed the *Piazza San Pietro* colonnades, urban spaces, and church façades that define Rome's Baroque silhouette.
- Worked as the principal artist for multiple popes, wielding enormous influence over papal commissions.
