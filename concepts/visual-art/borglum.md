# Gutzon Borglum

## summary

*Gutzon Borglum* (1867–1941) was an American sculptor famous for monumental public works carved directly into stone and granite. His masterpiece, *Mount Rushmore*, remains one of the world's largest sculpture projects—a feat of engineering, artistic vision, and nationalist ambition that defined American public sculpture in the 20th century.

## you gotta know

- Designed and directed the carving of *Mount Rushmore* (1927–41), embedding the faces of Washington, Jefferson, Lincoln, and Theodore Roosevelt into a South Dakota mountain.
- Pioneered *large-scale stone carving* using pneumatic tools and explosives, enabling sculpture at monumental scales previously impossible.
- Earlier in his career, sculpted the *Mares of Diomedes* (1904), a dramatic marble group of rearing horses showing classical influence.
- Conceived *Mount Rushmore* as a nationalist symbol—American presidents carved in permanent stone as an assertion of permanence and power.
- Died before *Mount Rushmore* was fully finished; his son *Lincoln Borglum* completed the work.
- Also designed the unfinished *Crazy Horse Memorial* in South Dakota, an even larger horse-and-rider carving still under construction.
