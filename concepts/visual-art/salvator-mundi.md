# Salvator Mundi

## summary

*Salvator Mundi* is a portrait of Christ holding a crystal orb, painted by Leonardo circa 1490s, in which Jesus gestures in blessing while gazing directly at the viewer with serene authority. The work exemplifies Leonardo's investigation of divine presence through portraiture, combining Renaissance ideals of physical beauty with spiritual majesty, though its attribution and provenance remain subjects of scholarly debate.

## you gotta know

- Oil on walnut panel, approximately 66 × 45 cm (26 × 18 inches), depicting Christ as a young man of idealized features and Renaissance dignity.
- Christ holds a transparent crystal orb (the world, *mundus*) in his left hand while raising his right in a gesture of benediction and blessing, a pose echoing medieval iconography but infused with Renaissance grace.
- The painting's attribution to Leonardo has been contested: while stylistically consistent with his work, some experts question whether it is entirely autograph or partly executed by assistants.
- The crystal sphere is rendered with extraordinary technical skill, demonstrating Leonardo's mastery of light refraction and transparency—a subject he investigated in his notebooks.
- Christ's face exhibits the soft sfumato modeling characteristic of Leonardo's portraiture, with an expression balancing humility and cosmic authority.
- The work exemplifies the Renaissance humanization of religious imagery: Christ appears neither stern judge nor remote deity, but a compassionate presence engaging the viewer directly.
- A 2017 sale at Christie's priced a restored version at $450.3 million, making it the most expensive painting ever sold at auction, though its condition and attribution remain controversial.
