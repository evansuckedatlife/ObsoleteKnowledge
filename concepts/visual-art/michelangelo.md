# Michelangelo

## summary

*Michelangelo Buonarroti* (1475–1564) was an Italian Renaissance master of sculpture, painting, and architecture whose works define the High Renaissance ideal. His monumental bronze-casted and marble figures—from the *David* to the *Pietà*—combine anatomical precision with spiritual intensity, embodying humanism and divine aspiration.

## you gotta know

- Carved the *David* (1504), a 17-foot marble nude that became the symbol of Renaissance Florence and human potential.
- Sculpted the *Pietà* (1498–99), a marble group of Mary cradling the dead Christ that perfected Renaissance pathos and delicacy.
- Painted the *Sistine Chapel ceiling* (1508–12), a vast fresco of biblical scenes including the iconic *Creation of Adam*.
- Created the *Moses* (1515) for the tomb of Pope Julius II, a bearded figure whose intensity suggests barely-contained divine knowledge.
- Designed the dome of *St. Peter's Basilica* in Rome, the Renaissance's architectural apex.
- Known for his *terribilità* — a style of overwhelming, almost violent grandeur and realism.
- Carved into his 80s, producing works like the unfinished *Rondanini Pietà*, proving his mastery lasted a lifetime.
