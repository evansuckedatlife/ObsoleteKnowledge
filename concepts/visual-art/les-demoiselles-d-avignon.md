# Les Demoiselles d'Avignon

## summary

*Les Demoiselles d'Avignon* is Pablo Picasso's groundbreaking 1907 painting that shattered European artistic tradition by depicting five distorted female nudes in a brothel scene with aggressive, flattened forms and clashing perspectives. This work is widely credited as the first Cubist painting and the birth of modern art, abandoning Renaissance perspective for radical formal innovation.

## you gotta know

- Painted in 1907 and now housed at the Museum of Modern Art in New York, it initiated Cubism and upended the aesthetic principles of Western painting.
- Depicts five nude female figures and a still life of fruit; the composition rejects spatial consistency and volumes appear fragmented, reconceived as flat geometric shards.
- The faces are distorted and mask-like—some painted green, some with sharp angular features—evoking influences from African and Iberian sculpture.
- Shocked contemporary viewers for both its crude eroticism (the title alludes to an Avignon brothel) and its apparent "ugliness" in form, violating ideals of classical beauty.
- Picasso worked on it obsessively, making multiple studies and revisions; it marked a turning point away from his Blue and Rose Periods.
- Decisively influenced the trajectory of 20th-century art, prompting a cascade of isms (Fauvism, Expressionism, Futurism) and establishing modernism as the dominant artistic language.
