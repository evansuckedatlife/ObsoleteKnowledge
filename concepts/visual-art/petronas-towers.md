# Petronas Twin Towers

## summary

The *Petronas Twin Towers* are a pair of 88-story skyscrapers completed in 1998 in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, each rising 1,482 feet and linked by a skybridge on the 41st and 42nd floors. The iconic twin design with Islamic geometric influences represented a new paradigm for supertall buildings in Southeast Asia.

## you gotta know

- Designed by American architect *Cesar Pelli*, the towers incorporate Islamic geometric patterns and a stainless-steel facade that reflects Kuala Lumpur's tropical environment.
- Each tower is 452 meters tall, making them the world's tallest buildings from 1998 to 2004, surpassed only by the Taipei 101 and later the Burj Khalifa.
- The connecting skybridge, at 170 meters above ground, is an engineering marvel that allows occupants to move between towers and serves as a dramatic visual anchor for the complex.
- Serves as the headquarters of Petronas, the Malaysian national oil company, and symbolizes Malaysia's entry into the global economy and technological modernity.
- The towers' pinnacles reflect the country's Muslim heritage through their pointed spires, blending commercial ambition with cultural identity.
- Completed during the height of the Asian financial boom, though the subsequent crisis slowed initial occupancy.
