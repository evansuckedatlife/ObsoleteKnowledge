# Chrysler Building

## summary

The *Chrysler Building* is a 77-story Art Deco masterpiece completed in 1930 in Manhattan, rising to 1,046 feet and briefly serving as the world's tallest building for 11 months. Its gleaming stainless-steel crown and terraced eagles remain one of architecture's most recognizable silhouettes.

## you gotta know

- Designed by architect *William Van Alen* for the Chrysler Corporation as its corporate headquarters, blending commercial ambition with aesthetic innovation.
- Features a distinctive tiered crown of stainless-steel triangular windows that create a radiating, sunburst effect visible across the city.
- Briefly claimed the title of world's tallest building in 1930, surpassed by the Empire State Building in 1931—a competition that exemplified the era's architectural rivalry.
- Contains ornamental eagles and radiator caps sculpted at the crown, referencing Chrysler automobiles and synthesizing commercial identity with fine art.
- The lobby, clad in African marble, steel, and chrome, features a towering mural ceiling and represents the opulent interior standards of 1930s skyscraper design.
- Constructed during the late 1920s economic boom and completed just before the Great Depression, marking the end of unfettered skyscraper ambition.
