# I. M. Pei

## summary

*I. M. Pei* (1917–2019) was a Chinese-American architect celebrated for geometric modernism and the elegant integration of buildings into urban landscapes. His crystalline forms and meticulous craftsmanship earned him the Pritzker Prize and made him a defining figure in late modernist architecture.

## you gotta know

- Designed the *Louvre Pyramid* (1989) in Paris, a glass and steel pyramid that modernized the entrance to the historic museum, initially controversial but now iconic.
- Created the *Bank of China Tower* (1989) in Hong Kong, a striking geometric glass structure with a lattice exterior that became the city's skyline symbol.
- Designed the *Rock and Roll Hall of Fame* (1995) in Cleveland, a glass and steel building evoking both musical energy and architectural monumentality.
- Pioneer of the *geometric crystalline form*—using triangles, hexagons, and tessellating shapes as both structural and aesthetic elements.
- Studied under *Walter Gropius* at Harvard, absorbing Bauhaus principles while developing his own minimalist, geometric language.
- Emphasized the relationship between building and urban context, designing structures that create public space and enhance their surroundings.
- His *National Center for Atmospheric Research* (1967) in Boulder, Colorado exemplifies his integration of structure into natural landscape through careful site planning.

## connections

- [[mies-van-der-rohe]] — shared geometric minimalism and commitment to structural clarity through glass and steel.
- [[le-corbusier]] — influenced by Corbusier's rationalism, though Pei softened it with sculptural geometry.
- [[walter-gropius]] — direct mentor; absorbed Bauhaus philosophy and applied it to contemporary urbanism.
