# Girl with a Pearl Earring

## summary

Johannes Vermeer's circa 1665 *Girl with a Pearl Earring* depicts an unknown young woman in exotic turban and luminous pearl earring, turning over her shoulder in a moment of intimate eye contact. The painting's enigmatic subject, masterful handling of light, and melancholic beauty have made it one of the most recognised works of Dutch Golden Age portraiture.

## you gotta know

- Painted around 1665, its subject remains unidentified—possibly Vermeer's daughter, a servant, or an imaginative fancy dress composition ("tronie").
- The girl wears an exotic yellow turban and a dark dress against a featureless dark background that throws her face into luminous isolation.
- The pearl earring—almost certainly not a real pearl but rendered to catch light magnificently—catches and reflects light in Vermeer's signature style.
- Housed in the Mauritshuis museum in The Hague, where it is one of the most visited artworks in the Netherlands.
- Famously sparked Tracy Chevalier's 1999 novel *Girl with a Pearl Earring* and a 2003 film adaptation starring Scarlett Johansson.
- Vermeer's extraordinarily subtle palette and optical realism suggest he may have used a camera obscura to achieve his precise tonality.
