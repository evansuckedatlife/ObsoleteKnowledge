# Constantin Brancusi

## summary

*Constantin Brancusi* (1876–1957) was a Romanian-French modernist sculptor who pioneered abstraction and formal reduction in sculpture. Rejecting representational tradition, he carved and cast simplified forms—eggs, birds, columns—in marble, stone, and bronze, creating a vocabulary of pure shape that became foundational to 20th-century abstract art.

## you gotta know

- Created *Bird in Space* (c. 1923–40), a series of increasingly abstract bronze birds that dissolve form into pure ascending motion and elegance.
- Pioneered *direct carving*—working stone and marble without preliminary models, allowing the material's nature to guide the final form.
- Developed the *Endless Column* (1918 onwards), a towering modular form that suggests infinity and rhythm, influencing minimal and conceptual sculpture.
- Known for works of profound *simplification*—reducing figures to essential geometric volumes, stripping away detail.
- Rejected Rodin's influence early, declaring "When you see a fish, you do not think of its scales, but of its speed"—a mantra for abstraction.
- Lived and worked in a legendary Paris studio, which became a pilgrimage site for modern sculptors and is now preserved as a museum.
