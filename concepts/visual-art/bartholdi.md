# Frédéric-Auguste Bartholdi

## summary

*Frédéric-Auguste Bartholdi* (1834–1904) was a French sculptor best known for conceiving and overseeing the creation of the *Statue of Liberty*, a gift from France to the United States. His monumental copper colossus—151 feet of robed woman holding a torch—became the defining symbol of American liberty and immigration, standing in New York Harbor since 1886.

## you gotta know

- Designed the *Statue of Liberty* (*Liberty Enlightening the World*, 1875–1886), a copper-sheeted monument whose torch and crown have guided immigrants to American shores for over a century.
- Collaborated with engineer *Gustave Eiffel* (who designed the iron framework) to solve the technical challenges of a hollow copper statue of unprecedented scale.
- The statue's face is said to have been modeled after Bartholdi's mother, and her broken chains symbolize freedom from tyranny.
- Earlier sculptural career included public monuments and commemorative statues throughout France and Europe.
- Campaigned tirelessly to secure funding and support for the Liberty project, traveling to the United States and advocating for its completion.
- Created the conceptual vision of liberty as a maternal, welcoming figure—a powerful symbolic choice for a nation of immigrants.
