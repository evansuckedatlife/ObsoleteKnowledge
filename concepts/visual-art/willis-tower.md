# Willis Tower

## summary

The *Willis Tower* (formerly the Sears Tower) is a 110-story skyscraper completed in 1973 in Chicago, rising 1,450 feet and holding the record as the world's tallest building for 25 years. Its bundled-tube structural system and striking black facade made it an architectural and engineering milestone of the 1970s.

## you gotta know

- Designed by the firm Skidmore, Owings and Merrill (SOM) and completed in just over three years, despite Chicago's harsh winter conditions during construction.
- Employs a novel bundled-tube structural system of nine square tubes clustered together, allowing the tower to sway in wind while maintaining stability—a paradigm shift in tall-building engineering.
- Clad in black anodized aluminum and black granite, giving it a distinctive visual presence distinct from the lighter-colored towers of Manhattan.
- At 1,450 feet to the roof, it surpassed the Empire State Building (1,250 feet) and held the world record until the Burj Khalifa was completed in 2010.
- Originally built as the global headquarters of Sears, Roebuck and Company, but the company's decline left much of the office space vacant by the 2000s.
- The Skydeck observation deck on the 103rd floor remains a major Chicago tourist attraction, offering 360-degree views across Lake Michigan and the city.
