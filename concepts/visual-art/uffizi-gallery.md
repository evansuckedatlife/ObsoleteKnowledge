# Uffizi Gallery

## summary

The *Uffizi Gallery* in Florence is one of the world's oldest and most revered art museums, housing the most comprehensive collection of Italian Renaissance painting and sculpture. Originally built as administrative offices for the Medici family, it became a public museum in 1765 and remains the crown jewel of Renaissance art.

## you gotta know

- Built between 1560–1580 as offices (*uffizi* = offices) for the Medici administration under Cosimo I; the ruling family accumulated art in its halls over centuries.
- Contains Botticelli's *Birth of Venus* and *Primavera*, Leonardo's *Adoration of the Magi*, and Raphael's *Madonna of the Goldfinch*—touchstones of Renaissance aesthetics.
- The long east-west gallery corridor with its famous second-floor sightlines has been replicated in museum design worldwide.
- Strong Florentine and Tuscan holdings document the Renaissance from 14th-century Giotto onward; also includes significant works by Brunelleschi, Donatello, and Michelangelo.
- Became a public collection in 1737 under Anna Maria Luisa de Medici's bequest, a rare instance of aristocratic patronage yielding public cultural property.
- Damaged by a 1993 bombing; meticulously restored, reaffirming its status as both a political symbol and cultural anchor for Florence.
