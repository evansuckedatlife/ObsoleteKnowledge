# The Virgin and Child with St. Anne

## summary

*The Virgin and Child with St. Anne* is Leonardo's painting (circa 1503–1519) of three generations—the grandmother Saint Anne, her daughter the Virgin Mary, and the infant Christ—unified in a pyramidal composition of extraordinary psychological complexity. The work represents Leonardo's mature synthesis of geometric harmony, sfumato technique, and tender human interaction within a sacred framework.

## you gotta know

- Oil on poplar panel, approximately 168 × 130 cm (66 × 51 inches), now in the Louvre Museum, Paris.
- Features a complex three-figure composition arranged pyramidally, with Saint Anne at the apex, Mary in the middle, and Christ and the lamb at the base.
- The landscape background is rendered in atmospheric perspective, receding into misty mountains and winding paths, creating a dreamlike spatial depth.
- Christ playfully grasps the lamb (symbolizing his future sacrifice), while Mary gently restrains him, and Anne benignly observes—capturing both innocence and foreknowledge.
- Leonardo employed sfumato to create seamless transitions between figures and landscape, with soft modeling of flesh tones and a pervasive golden light.
- The work may have remained unfinished in Leonardo's eyes; he continually revised and perfected it, carrying it with him in his travels across Italy and France.
- The composition influenced countless later renderings of the subject and established Leonardo's mature approach to multi-figure sacred narrative.
