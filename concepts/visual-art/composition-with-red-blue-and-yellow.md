# Composition with Red, Blue and Yellow

## summary

*Composition with Red, Blue and Yellow* is Piet Mondrian's austere 1921 geometric abstraction comprising primary colors, black lines, and white fields arranged in a grid—a quintessential work of De Stijl that distills painting to its mathematical essence. It represents the radical elimination of representation in favor of pure formal harmony and universal visual order.

## you gotta know

- Painted in 1921, part of Mondrian's mature period after founding the De Stijl movement with fellow Dutch artists in 1917.
- Composed entirely of rectangular fields bounded by thick black lines, filled with primary colors (red, blue, yellow), black, and white in careful asymmetrical balance.
- Eliminates all reference to nature, objects, or emotion—instead pursues a utopian vision of universal harmony through geometric reduction and primary colors.
- Mondrian believed this formal vocabulary could express underlying spiritual truths and transcend individual subjectivity, influencing architecture, design, and ideology.
- Works in this series vary in proportion and color distribution, yet each maintains the same vocabulary of grid, line, primary color, and void.
- The painting exemplifies the De Stijl philosophy: abstraction, geometric rigor, and the integration of art into all aspects of modern life and design.
