# Frank Gehry

## summary

*Frank Gehry* (born 1929) is a Canadian-American architect known for deconstructionist and expressionist buildings that challenge conventional form. His signature use of titanium-clad, billowing surfaces and fragmented geometries has made him one of the most visually striking contemporary architects.

## you gotta know

- Designed the *Guggenheim Museum Bilbao* (1997), a curved titanium-clad structure that became an international landmark and revitalized the Basque industrial city through architectural tourism.
- Created the *Walt Disney Concert Hall* (2003) in Los Angeles, with steel and aluminum sails evoking billowing fabric, redefining the civic concert venue.
- Pioneered the use of computer modeling (CATIA software) to design complex curved forms that would be impossible to build using traditional methods.
- Designed *Dancing House* in Prague (1992), a playfully tilted structure with undulating glass and stone that mimics a pair of dancers.
- Known for deconstructed, fragmented exteriors that appear to deconstruct and reassemble form, rejecting the clean lines of modernism.
- His *Santa Monica residence* (1978) wrapped his own modest house in corrugated metal and plywood, a radical statement about material and beauty.
- His work merges fine art sensibilities with architecture, often collaborating with artists and creating buildings as sculptural objects.
