# Minecraft

## summary

**Minecraft** is a sandbox survival game released by Mojang in 2011 where players explore infinitely procedurally generated cube-based worlds, gathering resources and crafting tools to build structures and survive against monsters. Its creative freedom and accessibility made it a cultural phenomenon, becoming the best-selling video game of all time with over 300 million copies sold across all platforms.

## you gotta know

- Procedurally generates infinite blocky landscapes using a voxel-based engine; players can place and destroy any block to reshape the world.
- Offers three core modes: Survival (gather resources, manage hunger and health), Creative (unlimited blocks and flight), and Adventure (explore hand-crafted maps).
- Crafting system requires combination of raw materials in a 3×3 grid to create tools, weapons, and building materials—the foundation of progression.
- Nighttime spawns hostile mobs like *Creepers*, *Skeletons*, and *Zombies*; players must fortify shelters or fight to survive.
- *The Nether* (an underworld dimension accessed via obsidian portals) and *The End* (a dimension containing the final boss, the *Ender Dragon*) extend exploration.
- Originally created solo by *Markus "Notch" Persson* and later expanded into a global community with yearly *Minecraft Live* events and massive multiplayer servers.
- Has spawned an educational version (*Minecraft: Education Edition*) used in schools to teach STEM, history, and creative problem-solving.
