# Sonic the Hedgehog

## summary

*Sonic the Hedgehog* is Sega's flagship platformer franchise debuting in 1991, centered on a super-fast blue hedgehog racing through loop-de-loop filled levels to stop the evil scientist Dr. Robotnik. Known for emphasis on speed, momentum-based level design, and a pop-culture aesthetic, the franchise became Sega's mascot and a cultural icon of the 1990s.

## you gotta know

- Debuted in 1991 on the Sega Genesis as *Sonic the Hedgehog*, designed to compete with Nintendo's Mario; Sonic's speed and attitude appealed to older, hipper audiences than Mario's mustachioed plumber.
- Core design: levels are designed for high-speed traversal with loops, half-pipes, and enemy lines that reward momentum awareness; Sonic collects rings as a health buffer—taking damage drops rings, which can be recollected if quick.
- *Sonic the Hedgehog 2* (1992) introduced Tails (Miles Prower), Sonic's flying fox sidekick, and the ability to emerge as Super Sonic (invulnerable, golden form) by collecting Chaos Emeralds.
- *Sonic 3 & Knuckles* (1994) introduced Knuckles the Echidna, a rival-turned-ally, and expanded Chaos Emerald collection into half-pipe bonus stages; the game's level design sophistication peaked here.
- Shifted to 3D with *Sonic Adventure* (2001, Dreamcast), introducing multiple playable characters (Tails, Knuckles, Big the Cat) with different play styles; later 3D entries received mixed reception for experimental mechanics.
- Modern entries (*Sonic Unleashed*, *Sonic Colors*, *Sonic Mania*) oscillate between speed-focused design and gimmick-heavy stages; *Sonic Mania* (2017) returned to 2D with original-style level design.
- Recently, *Sonic Frontiers* (2022) introduced open-zone exploration, departing from linear level design; the franchise's tone and mechanical consistency has been historically inconsistent.

## connections
