# Nosferatu

## summary

*Nosferatu*, an unauthorized 1922 adaptation of Bram Stoker's *Dracula*, is F.W. Murnau's masterwork of atmospheric horror. Using German Expressionist shadows and grotesque design, it follows the parasitic Count Orlok's curse spreading from Transylvania to a Baltic port town, establishing the visual language and psychological terror of vampire cinema while its production history itself became legend.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *F.W. Murnau*, released in 1922; unauthorized adaptation of *Dracula* (the Stoker estate nearly destroyed all prints, so copies are patchy).
- Count *Orlok* is cinema's first iconic vampire: skeletal, rat-like, moving with unnatural jerks; actor *Max Schreck*'s performance remains unsettling.
- Uses extreme chiaroscuro (sharp contrasts between light and shadow) and distorted set design to create psychological dread without gore—shadows of Orlok climbing stairs terrify more than explicit violence.
- Employs innovative techniques: negative film for supernatural effects, iris shots to isolate dread, and a slow, deliberate pacing that builds mounting inevitability.
- The film's plot centers on *Hutter*, a real-estate agent who travels to Transylvania, unknowingly facilitates Orlok's journey to the West, and watches the vampire consume his town and wife.
- *Nosferatu* influenced virtually every vampire film after it; Orlok's visual design remains the template for horror iconography.
- The film was nearly erased (Stoker's widow sued and won the right to destroy copies) but restored in the 1960s and has become a cornerstone of cinema history and horror canon.
