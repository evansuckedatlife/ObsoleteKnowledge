# Dominion

## summary

*Dominion* is a deck-building board game designed by Donald X. Vaccarino and released in 2008, where players construct custom decks of cards during the game by purchasing new cards from a shared market, using their evolving deck to generate resources and victory points. By making deck-building the core mechanic rather than a preset starting resource, Dominion invented the deck-building genre wholesale and launched a wave of games built on the same principle.

## you gotta know

- Each player begins with an identical starter deck (seven coppers and three estates) and improves it by buying cards during the game, creating divergent strategies.
- Cards serve dual purposes: treasure cards generate coins for purchasing, and action cards trigger special effects; victory cards provide end-game points but clog the deck during play.
- The kingdom (set of 10 distinct action card stacks available for purchase) varies by expansion or random selection, ensuring different games reward different strategies.
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* Kennerspiel award in 2009, validating deck-building as a core mechanic worthy of top prizes.
- Numerous expansions introduce new card types, mechanics (duration cards, attacks, curses), and themes; some standalone sets (*Intrigue*, *Seaside*, *Prosperity*) play independently or combine.
- The game's streamlined turn structure (action phase, buy phase, cleanup) became a design template adopted by countless deck-building games that followed.
- Players must balance short-term card quality (buying strong cards) with long-term deck consistency (buying cards that synergize).
