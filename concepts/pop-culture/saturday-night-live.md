# Saturday Night Live

## summary

*Saturday Night Live* is a groundbreaking sketch-comedy and music television show that debuted on *NBC* in 1975 and has become the longest-running variety series in American history. Created by *Lorne Michaels*, the show revolutionized comedy television by building a repertory company of performers, rotating guest hosts and musical artists, and maintaining a deliberately unpredictable, occasionally anarchic energy through live broadcast.

## you gotta know

- Premiered on *NBC* on October 11, 1975, at 11:30 p.m. on Saturday nights, launching the careers of an extraordinary roster of comedians and actors including *Dan Aykroyd*, *Gilda Radner*, *Eddie Murphy*, *Tina Fey*, and *Kristen Wiig*.
- Format: a five-minute monologue from a rotating guest host, followed by three to five sketch comedy segments, interspersed with music from a different musical guest each week—all performed live to a national audience.
- Created the "Not Ready for Prime Time Players," a stable of cast members who became household names and launched film and television careers; the show's alumni are a who's-who of contemporary comedy and entertainment.
- Early seasons featured the "cold open," a topical sketch that often spoofed current events or public figures, a format now imitated by nearly every sketch-comedy show.
- Famous recurring sketches include "The Church Lady," "Wayne's World," "Stefon," and countless characters and catchphrases that entered the cultural lexicon.
- The live format creates genuine unpredictability—moments of flubbed lines, breaking character, or creative spontaneity—which became part of the show's appeal and mystique.
- Served as a launching pad for musical artists: *The Beatles* (via Beatles recordings), *Simon & Garfunkel*, *Sinéad O'Connor*, and countless others have performed on the show.
