# Settlers of Catan

## summary

*Settlers of Catan* is a foundational modern board game designed by Klaus Teuber and released in Germany in 1995 (as *Die Siedler von Catan*), where players build settlements, cities, and roads on a procedurally generated island hexagon while trading resources. Its elegant mechanics, replayability through randomized board layouts, and emphasis on negotiation and trading made it the gateway game that introduced millions to modern hobby board games and sparked the global board game renaissance.

## you gotta know

- Players collect five resource types—wheat, sheep, ore, clay, and lumber—rolled via two dice each turn, determining which hexes produce.
- Victory is achieved by earning 10 points through settlements (1 point), cities (2 points), and longest road or largest army cards.
- The robber, placed initially on the desert hex, blocks resource production and can be moved by rolling a seven or playing a knight card, adding player interaction and blocking strategy.
- Trading is central: players negotiate with each other to swap resources (e.g., "I'll trade you two sheep for a wheat"), making diplomacy and deal-making as important as board position.
- Won the prestigious *Spiel des Jahres* (Game of the Year) award in 1995, cementing its status as a landmark title.
- The game's randomized hex-tile system ensures no two games play identically, encouraging repeated play and reducing advantage from memorization.
- Numerous expansions exist, including *Seafarers* (navigation and ship placement) and *Cities and Knights* (adds knights, city improvements, and a separate progress track).

## connections

- [[ticket-to-ride]] — another modern gateway game released in the same era, emphasizing route-building and accessible mechanics.
- [[agricola]] — a more complex resource-management game released in 2007 that built on Catan's foundation with worker placement.
- [[dominion]] — pioneered deck-building as a central mechanic, defining a major genre of modern games.
- [[carcassonne]] — tile-placement game offering a different strategic depth from Catan's trading focus.
- [[eurogames-hobby-movement]] — Catan is the game most credited with launching the modern European board game movement into mainstream awareness.
