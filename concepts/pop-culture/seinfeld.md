# Seinfeld

## summary

*Seinfeld* aired on NBC from 1989 to 1998 and starred stand-up comic *Jerry Seinfeld* as a fictionalized version of himself, living in New York with his best friend *George Costanza* (*Jason Alexander*) and across the street from his neighbor *Cosmo Kramer* (*Michael Richards*). The show's famous tagline was "a show about nothing"—each episode mined comedy from the mundane minutiae of daily life: social awkwardness, romantic anxieties, and the bizarre rituals of urban existence. *Seinfeld* became a generational touchstone, proving that mainstream television audiences would embrace observational comedy untethered to plot, sentiment, or character growth.

## you gotta know

- Created by *Jerry Seinfeld* and *Larry David*; *David* was the head writer for the first five seasons and drove the show's comic sensibility.
- The "show about nothing" format rejected the traditional sitcom arc; episodes resolved without change or lesson-learning, reflecting the cyclical nature of real social anxiety.
- *Michael Richards* as Kramer, the eccentric neighbor who burst through the apartment door, became a breakout character whose physical comedy was central to the show's success.
- Introduced minutiae-mining as a comedy tactic: episodes centered on button placement, Thanksgiving traditions, or the discomfort of wearing another person's cologne.
- The show's power was in recognizing social behaviors that no one else dared to examine: "close talkers," "low talkers," "high talkers," and the awkwardness of public eating.
- Became a global phenomenon; the finale drew 76 million viewers and sparked debate about the characters' moral bankruptcy.
- Spawned cultural catchphrases ("yada yada yada," "master of the domain," "not that there's anything wrong with that").
