# Codenames

## summary

*Codenames* is a word-association party game designed by Vladimír Chvátal and released in 2015, where two competing teams race to identify 25 secret agent code names on a grid by interpreting one-word clues from their team's spymaster. Its deceptively simple mechanic—guessing which words match a cryptic clue—creates hilarious misunderstandings, clever wordplay, and tense decision-making, making it one of the most commercially successful and socially beloved modern board games.

## you gotta know

- The game uses a 5×5 grid of 25 random words; one card reveals which are red agents, which are blue, which are innocent bystanders, and which is the assassin (losing the game if guessed).
- Each team's spymaster provides a one-word clue and a number (e.g., "AGENT: 3") indicating how many words on the grid match the clue; teammates must guess, but are blocked once they misidentify or reach the clue number.
- Guessing the assassin ends the game immediately in a loss; tension escalates as teams balance aggressive guessing (more words revealed, higher risk) with caution.
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* Kennerspiel award in 2016, remarkable for a party game's recognition at gaming's highest level.
- The game's minimal components (a deck of 400 word cards, a key card) and sub-hour playtime made it extremely accessible, driving massive adoption in board game cafés and casual circles.
- Numerous spin-off editions (*Codenames: Duels*, *Codenames: Marvel*, *Codenames: Pictures*) and international variants adapted the mechanic to different word lists and themes.
- Online versions (Netflix, Board Game Arena) democratized play without physical cards, introducing millions to the game.
