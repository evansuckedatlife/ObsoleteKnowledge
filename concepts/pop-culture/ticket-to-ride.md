# Ticket to Ride

## summary

*Ticket to Ride* is a railroad route-building board game designed by Alan R. Moon and released in 2004, where players claim railway routes across a map (initially North America) to connect distant cities and earn points. Praised for its simple yet engaging mechanic of collecting train cards to claim colored routes, the game became one of the most commercially successful modern board games and spawned numerous map expansions set across the globe.

## you gotta know

- Players draw colored train cards and use matching colors to claim routes between cities on the board, placing plastic train tokens as evidence of ownership.
- Victory points come from claimed routes (varying lengths award different points) and from fulfilling destination cards—secret point-to-point connections worth bonus points if completed or penalties if failed.
- The longest route card awards 10 bonus points, incentivizing players to build continuously connected networks rather than scattered routes.
- Received the *Spiel des Jahres* in 2005, a year after release, affirming its standing as a modern classic.
- The base game is North America; sequels and expansions map Europe, the United Kingdom, Nordic countries, Asia, South America, and many others.
- Mechanics are immediately learnable—drawing cards, claiming routes, scoring—making it accessible to families and new players while remaining strategically rich.
- Multiple digital adaptations (mobile apps, Steam) have introduced the game to millions and made asynchronous play convenient.
