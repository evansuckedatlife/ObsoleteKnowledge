# The Sims

## summary

*The Sims* is a life simulation game franchise debuted by Maxis in 2000, where players build and manage households of artificial characters navigating daily routines, relationships, careers, and goals. A cultural landmark in game design, it popularized the "sandbox" genre and proved simulation games could achieve blockbuster appeal without combat.

## you gotta know

- Designed by Will Wright; debuted in 2000 on PC; *The Sims* outsold competitors by offering creative freedom—players design homes, manage finances, craft narratives—rather than prescribed winning conditions.
- Sims have needs (hunger, bladder, energy, hygiene, social) visualized as bars; players balance desires against time and resources; neglect leads to deterioration (dirty home, failed relationships, job loss).
- Gameplay centers on relationship-building: Sims form friendships, romantic partnerships, and families; careers provide income and experience; hobbies and skills unlock activities (cooking, painting, logic).
- *The Sims 2* (2004) introduced aging (Sims progress from infant to elder), generational play, and the neighborhood concept; *The Sims 3* (2009) added open-world neighborhoods and a robust create-a-sim tool.
- *The Sims 4* (2014) refined emotion systems (Sims experience moods affecting their behavior), expanded DLC categories (Seasons, Get Famous, Eco Lifestyle), and introduced a live-service model with regular updates.
- Expanded universe: *SimCity* (city-building spin-off), *The Sims Medieval* (medieval life simulation), and countless expansion packs totaling billions in revenue.
- Cultural impact: the franchise normalized simulation as a legitimate game genre and demonstrated that player agency and emergent storytelling could drive engagement more effectively than narrative scripting.

## connections

- [[maxis]] — developer; Will Wright's studio, later acquired by Electronic Arts (EA).
- [[will-wright]] — designer and visionary who conceptualized player-driven sandbox gameplay.
- [[simulation-games]] — the prototype for modern life/management simulations.
- [[emergent-narrative]] — players generate stories through system interaction rather than scripted plots.
