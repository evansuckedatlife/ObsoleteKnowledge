# Puerto Rico

## summary

*Puerto Rico* is a worker-placement and trading game designed by Andreas Seyfarth and released in 2002, where players develop colonial plantations on Puerto Rico, managing sugar, corn, tobacco, coffee, and indigo production while shipping goods to gain victory points. Praised for its innovative role-selection mechanism (players take turns choosing roles that unlock actions for all players in a novel way) and economic depth, Puerto Rico is often credited as the blueprint for worker-placement games and remains a benchmark for elegant design.

## you gotta know

- The role-selection system allows the active player to choose a role (e.g., Planter, Trader, Builder, Shipper, Mayor), which unlocks actions for all players but gives the selector a bonus—a elegant asymmetry that drives strategy.
- Players plant crops on their plantation tiles, using workers (limited supply) to improve production; more workers become available as players build larger plantations.
- The shipping action converts planted goods into victory points, creating a market-based economy where timing and player prediction matter enormously.
- Building plantations and structures generates points; goods shipped scale exponentially in value for players who specialize, rewarding focused strategies.
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* Kennerspiel award in 2003, validating complex economic simulation in hobby games.
- No expansions were released; the base game remained complete and balanced, a rarity for modern board games.
- Often cited as the first true worker-placement game, predating Agricola's more systematic treatment of the mechanic by five years.

## connections

- [[agricola]] — released five years later, Agricola formalized and systematized the worker-placement mechanic that Puerto Rico pioneered.
- [[settlers-of-catan]] — both emphasize resource management and trading; Puerto Rico is more economically sophisticated.
- [[ticket-to-ride]] — contemporary gateway game representing a lighter approach to modern mechanics.
- [[dominion]] — released 2008, another landmark game emphasizing player-driven resource acquisition.
- [[colonial-themes-in-board-games]] — Puerto Rico's colonial setting is notable (and increasingly questioned) in the board game community.
