# Pandemic

## summary

*Pandemic* is a cooperative board game designed by Matt Leacock and released in 2008 where players assume roles of disease-fighting specialists working together to contain and cure four outbreaking diseases spreading across a global map before they overwhelm civilization. Unlike competitive games where one player wins, Pandemic succeeds only if all players cooperate, making it one of the first modern board games to mainstream cooperative play and spawning numerous expansions and a thriving franchise.

## you gotta know

- Players collectively control specialists (scientist, dispatcher, medic, researcher, quarantine expert, etc.), each with unique abilities that synergize when coordinated.
- Four diseases spread each turn through infection cards, with outbreaks cascading into neighboring cities; if three outbreaks occur globally, the team loses.
- Victory requires discovering cures for all four diseases by collecting city cards matching a disease's color, requiring players to strategically share information and resources.
- The difficulty scales with preset epidemic levels; higher difficulties increase infection spread, rewarding skilled play and creating replayability.
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* recommendation (Kennerspiel award) in 2009, validating cooperative gameplay as a viable mainstream mechanic.
- Expansions introduce legacy mechanics (*Pandemic Legacy*), new roles, harder diseases, and alternate settings, fundamentally reshaping the franchise across multiple sub-games.
- The game's 2020 release during the real COVID-19 pandemic made it accidentally culturally resonant, though the game itself is unrelated to actual epidemiology.
