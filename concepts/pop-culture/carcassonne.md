# Carcassonne

## summary

*Carcassonne* is a tile-placement board game designed by Klaus Teuber and released in 2000, named after a walled medieval town in southern France, where players draw and place square terrain tiles (roads, cities, monasteries, grasslands) to build a shared landscape, claiming features with their meeple tokens to earn points. Its elegant mechanic of progressive board-building, minimal luck, and intuitive rules made it a modern classic and established tile-placement as a core game design mechanic.

## you gotta know

- Each turn a player draws one tile and places it adjacent to the existing board, then optionally places a meeple on a road, city, monastery, or grassland to claim it.
- Meeples commit players' pieces to incomplete features; reclaiming them requires finishing the feature, creating a risk-reward tension that drives tactical play.
- Completed roads (paths between intersections) award 1 point per tile; completed cities award 2 points per tile plus 2 points for every pennant; monasteries award 9 points if surrounded by all eight adjacent tiles.
- The game's random tile draw and modular board ensure high replayability despite the deterministic mechanics (no dice rolling).
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* in 2001, cementing tile-placement as a major modern mechanic.
- Numerous expansions introduce inns, cathedrals, abbeys, markets, and variants like *Carcassonne: Hunters and Gatherers* or *Carcassonne: The City*.
- Named after Carcassonne, a real medieval fortified town in Occitanie, France, famous for its distinctive concentric walls and historical importance.
