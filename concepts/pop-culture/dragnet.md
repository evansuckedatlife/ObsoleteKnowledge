# Dragnet

## summary

*Dragnet* is a pioneering police procedural television series that debuted on *NBC* in 1951 and ran for eight seasons, establishing the template for the countless cop shows that followed. The series, created by and starring *Jack Webb*, eschewed violence and melodrama in favor of meticulous, mundane police work, transforming the procedural into a form that could dramatize the unglamorous reality of law enforcement while maintaining narrative tension through authenticity.

## you gotta know

- Premiered on *NBC* in December 1951, adapted from *Jack Webb's* earlier and more famous radio series; the television version ran for 276 episodes through 1959.
- Created by and starred *Jack Webb* as *Sergeant Joe Friday*, a deadpan, unflappable detective whose flat delivery and bureaucratic attention to detail became the show's signature tone.
- Introduced the famous opening: "Ladies and gentlemen, the story you are about to see is true. The names have been changed to protect the innocent."
- Episodes focused on the actual procedures of detective work—following leads, interviewing witnesses, gathering evidence, navigating bureaucratic channels—rather than elaborate chases or violent confrontations.
- *Jack Webb* cultivated relationships with the Los Angeles Police Department, gaining access and information that lent the show authenticity; officers served as consultants and many episodes were inspired by actual cases.
- The show's emphasis on procedure, institutional hierarchy, and the moral clarity of law enforcement influenced subsequent procedurals including *The Defenders*, *Hill Street Blues*, and modern shows like *Law & Order*.
- Webb's minimalist directing style, stark cinematography, and refusal to glamorize police work or sensationalize crime established a different aesthetic than contemporary crime dramas.
