# Gone with the Wind

## summary

*Gone with the Wind* is an epic historical drama set during the American Civil War and Reconstruction, following the headstrong *Scarlett O'Hara* as she navigates love, war, and survival. The 1939 film, based on *Margaret Mitchell's* novel, became a defining portrait of the South and remains one of cinema's most culturally significant works.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *Victor Fleming*, starring *Vivien Leigh* as Scarlett O'Hara and *Clark Gable* as *Rhett Butler*.
- The film opens in Atlanta before the siege and unfolds across the Civil War's devastation and the subsequent Reconstruction era.
- Scarlett's most famous line: "Frankly, my dear, I don't give a damn"—shocking language for 1939 cinema.
- Won a record eight Academy Awards at the 1940 ceremony, including Best Picture, Best Director, Best Actress, and Best Supporting Actress (Hattie McDaniel).
- The film's length (nearly four hours) and scale set new standards for epic Hollywood productions.
- Notorious for its romanticized portrayal of the pre-war South and its complex legacy regarding race and slavery.
