# Singin' in the Rain

## summary

*Singin' in the Rain* is a 1952 musical comedy set in 1920s Hollywood during the transition from silent to talking pictures. Starring *Gene Kelly* and featuring the legendary title-song dance sequence, the film celebrates the creative spirit and exuberance of the era while satirizing showbiz vanity and ambition.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *Gene Kelly* and *Stanley Donen*, starring Gene Kelly, *Donald O'Brien*, and *Debbie Reynolds*.
- The iconic "Singin' in the Rain" sequence features Gene Kelly performing a joyful tap-dancing routine on a rain-soaked street—one of cinema's most celebrated moments.
- Set against Hollywood's transition to sound film ("talkies"), a transitional moment that threatened many silent-film stars.
- The film includes the showstopper "Make 'Em Laugh" number, choreographed with physical comedy reminiscent of *Charlie Chaplin*.
- Originally conceived as a revue of *Arthur Freed* and *Nacio Herb Brown* songs; the narrative was built around existing numbers.
- Reynolds' character, Lina Lamont, is a vain, selfish star who takes credit for O'Brien's choreography—a sharp commentary on ego in cinema.
