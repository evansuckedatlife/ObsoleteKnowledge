# Super Mario

## summary

*Super Mario* is Nintendo's flagship platformer franchise debuting in 1985, following the adventures of Mario, a plumber who jumps through Mushroom Kingdom levels to rescue Princess Peach from the villainous Bowser. The series revolutionized side-scrolling game design with precise physics, inventive level layouts, and power-ups that remain iconic across gaming.

## you gotta know

- Created by Nintendo in 1985 as an arcade game, then adapted for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) in *Super Mario Bros.*, which revitalized the video game industry after the 1983 crash.
- Introduced core mechanics: jumping on enemies, collecting coins, power-ups like the Super Mushroom (growth) and Fire Flower (projectiles), and flagpoles marking level completion.
- *Super Mario Bros. 3* (1988) expanded level design vocabulary with creative themes, suits (Raccoon, Frog, Tanuki), and world-spanning structure.
- *Super Mario 64* (1996) pioneered 3D platforming on the Nintendo 64, with analog stick control and dynamic camera, influencing a generation of 3D games.
- *Super Mario Galaxy* (1 & 2, 2007-2010) brought gravity-shifting mechanics and spherical level design to the Wii.
- More recent entries like *Super Mario Odyssey* (2017) introduce possession mechanics via the hat companion Cappy, and *Super Mario Bros. Wonder* (2023) returns to 2D with dreamlike visual themes.
- The franchise spans multiple genres: racing (*Mario Kart*), sports, RPGs, and party games; Mario is Nintendo's de facto mascot.

## connections

- [[luigi]] — Mario's brother and frequent co-star in multi-player adventures.
- [[princess-peach]] — the kidnapped objective across most games; sometimes a playable character herself.
- [[bowser]] — the Koopa King, Mario's arch-rival and Princess Peach's abductor.
- [[nintendo]] — the publisher and creator; Mario defines Nintendo's brand.
- [[video-game-design]] — revolutionized platformer physics and level design.
