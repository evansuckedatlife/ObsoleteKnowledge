# Street Fighter

## summary

*Street Fighter* is a fighting game franchise launched by Capcom in 1987, featuring hand-to-hand martial artists competing in tournaments across the globe. Pioneering competitive fighting mechanics and esports culture, the series defined the genre and spawned a multi-decade competitive community centered on frame-data precision and split-second decision-making.

## you gotta know

- Debuted in 1987 as an arcade game; *Street Fighter II* (1991) revolutionized fighting games by introducing eight distinct fighters (Ryu, Ken, Guile, Blanka, E. Honda, Chun-Li, Zangief, Dhalsim), each with unique move sets and fighting styles.
- Command inputs (quarter-circle motions, half-circles) unlock special moves—Ryu's Hadoken fireball, Zangief's Spinning Piledriver—requiring dexterity and memory; combos (linked sequences) reward execution skill.
- The game became an arcade sensation; tournament players pushed the meta-game forward, discovering infinite combos and frame-trap sequences; arcades became social hubs and proving grounds.
- *Street Fighter II Turbo* and *Super Street Fighter II* added new fighters, balanced matchups, and variation speeds; *Street Fighter: Alpha* (1995) introduced the Grooves system (three fighting styles per character).
- *Street Fighter III* (2000) introduced parries (blocking into forwards automatically negates chip damage) and a 15-fighter roster; *Street Fighter IV* (2008) revived the franchise with 3D graphics and Focus Attacks (armor-breaking counterattack mechanic).
- Modern competitive scene centered on *Street Fighter 6* (2023), which emphasizes accessibility (modern control scheme) alongside traditional arcade-stick play; esports titles like CEO, Combo Breaker, and Evo draw thousands.
- Expanded to film (*Street Fighter*, 1994; *Street Fighter 6*, 2023), manga, and the broader "fighting game" genre (spawned by this franchise's success).
