# Metropolis

## summary

*Metropolis* is Fritz Lang's 1927 German science-fiction epic depicting a futuristic city divided between a wealthy elite living in gleaming towers and enslaved workers toiling underground. A visionary masterpiece of production design and thematic ambition, it pioneered cinematic sci-fi and German Expressionist technique while exploring capitalism, class struggle, and technological dehumanization.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *Fritz Lang* and released in 1927; Germany's most expensive film ever made at that time, with elaborate sets designed by *Erich Kettelhut*.
- The robot *Maria*—a mechanical woman created to control the working class—is an icon of cinema; her dance scene remains unforgettable.
- Employs German Expressionist visual language: stark shadows, distorted angles, and monumental architecture to convey the city's oppressive structure.
- The plot centers on the love between *Freder* (son of the city's master) and *Maria* (a worker), which ignites a revolution and challenge to the ruling class.
- Features groundbreaking special effects: the robot transformation, miniatures, matte paintings, and the metropolis set itself, which took over a year to construct.
- Lang's original cut (about 2.5 hours) was heavily edited and lost in various markets; reconstructions in 2008 and 2010 restored much footage, though the full original remains partially lost.
- Influenced virtually every subsequent science-fiction film and visual tradition, from *Blade Runner* to anime aesthetics.
