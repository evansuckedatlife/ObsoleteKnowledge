# Sunset Boulevard

## summary

*Sunset Boulevard* is a 1950 noir masterpiece directed by *Billy Wilder* that exposes the dark underbelly of Hollywood through the tragic entanglement of a struggling screenwriter and a delusional silent-film star. Narrated by a dead man, the film is a caustic satire of fame, delusion, and the American Dream's corruption.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *Billy Wilder*, starring *William Holden* as Joe Gillis (the narrator), *Gloria Swanson* as *Norma Desmond*, and *Erich von Stroheim* as her devoted servant Max.
- Norma Desmond, a silent-film queen living in faded grandeur, represents the obsolescence of the silent era and the pathology of celebrity.
- The film is narrated by the dead Joe Gillis, establishing a fatalistic tone and dark humor from the opening.
- Won three Academy Awards for Best Art Direction, Best Original Score, and Best Original Screenplay.
- Features the chilling final line: "I'm ready for my close-up, Mr. DeMille"—Norma's descent into delusion as she descends the staircase.
- A scathing indictment of Hollywood's cruelty, transience, and the objectification of women in the entertainment industry.

## connections

- [[billy-wilder]] — master director whose cynical worldview and formal innovation define mid-century cinema.
- [[gloria-swanson]] — silent-film icon cast in her comeback role, playing a version of herself.
- [[william-holden]] — everyman actor whose moral compromise mirrors the audience's complicity.
- [[erich-von-stroheim]] — legendary director cast as the butler, adding layers of Hollywood history.
