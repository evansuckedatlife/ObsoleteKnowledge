# Scythe

## summary

*Scythe* is a worker-placement and asymmetric game designed by Jamey Stegmaier and released in 2016, set in an alternate-history 1920s Europe dominated by mecha (enormous mechanical walkers), where players control different factions competing for resources, territory, and military supremacy. Celebrated for stunning artwork by Jake Parker, highly asymmetric faction powers, and dynamic gameplay that rewards different strategic approaches, Scythe became a bestseller and benchmark for asymmetric design in modern board gaming.

## you gotta know

- Five asymmetric factions (Saxony, Crimea, Polania, Rusviet, Nordic) begin in different board positions with unique special abilities, mechanical abilities, and economic bonuses, ensuring divergent viable strategies.
- Worker-placement actions (Move, Gain Resources, Build, Produce, Trade, Bolster) are taken in turn order, but turn order is dynamic and can be purchased, adding resource-conversion strategy.
- Victory requires a mix of territorial control, factory presence, power tokens, and resource conversion into power—a balanced scoring system that prevents dominant strategies.
- The game incorporates a threat system: military aggression is penalized by opponent power increases, incentivizing diplomacy over conflict despite military presence.
- Acclaimed for world-building and art direction; the steampunk alternate-history setting is integrated thematically without overwhelming mechanics.
- Numerous expansions introduce new factions, modules, and mechanics (*Windcrossed*, *All Creatures*, *Modular Board*).
- A successor title, *Scythe: New Division*, was released in 2021, exploring different factions in the same universe.
