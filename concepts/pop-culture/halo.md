# Halo

## summary

*Halo* is a science-fiction first-person shooter franchise launched by Bungie for Xbox in 2001, featuring the super-soldier Master Chief aboard massive ring-world Halo installations. Known for expansive sci-fi worldbuilding, cooperative campaign design, and competitive multiplayer innovation, it became Xbox's defining franchise and influenced a generation of console shooters.

## you gotta know

- Debuted in 2001 as an Xbox exclusive; *Halo: Combat Evolved* established the franchise's identity: Master Chief, a genetically modified SPARTAN soldier, aboard the orbital megastructure Halo—a Forerunner superweapon designed to exterminate all sentient life in the galaxy.
- Halo rings are ancient alien installations created by a precursor species, the Forerunners, meant to contain and annihilate the parasitic Flood; humanity's discovery of their purpose becomes the narrative thrust.
- Introduced the rechargeable shield system (overcharging shields depletes health) and weapon-swapping between alien Covenant and human UNSC arms, fundamentally shaping console FPS balance and feel.
- *Halo 2* (2004) and *Halo 3* (2007) dominated Xbox Live online multiplayer; *Halo 3* introduced Forge Mode (map editor) and Theater (record/rewatch gameplay), influencing user-generated content in games.
- Shifted from Bungie's vision with *Halo 4* (2012) onward under 343 Industries, emphasizing lore (Didact, the Created), Spartan cosmetics, and battle pass systems (*Halo Infinite*).
- Expanded universe: *Halo: Reach* (2010) chronicles the doomed planet Reach and the fall of the Spartans; TV adaptation (2022–) recontextualizes canon; novels and comics elaborate lore.
- *Halo Infinite* (2021) returned to a live-service free-to-play multiplayer model with mixed reception; a Legendary-difficulty campaign revisited core themes.
