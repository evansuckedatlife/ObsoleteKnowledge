# Call of Duty

## summary

*Call of Duty* is an annual first-person shooter franchise launched by Activision in 2003, centered on modern military campaigns and competitive multiplayer. Dominating console gaming and esports for two decades, the series exemplifies the blockbuster shooter formula: cinematic campaign, large-scale multiplayer maps, and unlockable progression systems.

## you gotta know

- Debuted in 2003 on PC; *Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare* (2007) revolutionized the franchise by abandoning World War II settings for contemporary geopolitics, introducing the iconic Captain "Roach" and the SAS operator Captain Price.
- Established the annual release cycle (nearly) and the premium multiplayer model: fast-paced deathmatch, team modes (Capture the Flag, Search & Destroy), and progression-based unlocks (weapons, attachments, cosmetics).
- Split into two alternating sub-franchises: *Modern Warfare* (contemporary/near-future) and *Black Ops* (covert Cold War and fictional missions), each with distinct tone and roster of characters.
- Multiplayer map design emphasizes three-lane symmetry for balanced team play; killstreaks (reward chains for consecutive kills) let players call in airstrikes or helicopters, turning individual performance into team advantage.
- *Call of Duty: Black Ops* (2010) introduced Zombie mode—cooperative waves of undead enemies—which spawned its own competitive community and lore spanning multiple entries.
- Franchise peaks occurred with *Modern Warfare 2* (2009), which was a cultural phenomenon, and *Black Ops* (2010); recent entries compete with *Valorant*, *CS:GO*, and *Overwatch* for esports and casual audiences.
- Warzone, the free-to-play battle royale launched in 2020, brought the franchise into the live-service model, though later entries have struggled with integration and player retention.
