# The Simpsons

## summary

*The Simpsons* premiered on Fox in 1989 as an animated sitcom following the dysfunctional Simpson family—Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, and baby Maggie—in the fictional town of Springfield. Using animation to bypass the constraints of live-action sitcoms, the show perfected satirical comedy rooted in family dysfunction, consumer culture, and American mediocrity, while maintaining genuine emotional stakes between family members. Over three-plus decades, *The Simpsons* has become the longest-running American sitcom and the most-referenced television show in contemporary culture, establishing animation as a serious vehicle for sophisticated comedy and social critique.

## you gotta know

- Created by *Matt Groening*, who developed the characters from his comic strip *Life in Hell*; originally debuted as a segment on *The Tracey Ullman Show* (1987).
- Starred *Dan Castellaneta* as Homer Simpson, the bumbling father whose stupidity and love for his family drove the show's emotional center.
- Pioneered the animated sitcom format; animation allowed visual gags and satirical exaggeration impossible in live-action comedies.
- The show's humor was multilayered: jokes for children, cultural references for adults, and sophisticated satirical commentary on American consumer culture.
- Each episode is set on the same day of the week (Sunday) in a fictional perpetual present, allowing the show to age without the characters aging.
- Won 39 Emmy Awards and countless accolades; its cultural references and quotes permeate American discourse.
- Achieved global cultural dominance; the show airs in nearly 200 countries and is considered the most-watched television program in history.
