# Sunrise

## summary

*Sunrise* is F.W. Murnau's 1927 American masterpiece depicting the moral and emotional journey of a married couple tempted toward destruction by urban vice and infidelity, then restored to love and grace through near-tragedy. A visual symphony of German Expressionist technique applied to intimate human drama, it won the first Academy Award for Best Picture in 1929 and remains a profound meditation on redemption.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *F.W. Murnau*, released in 1927; his first American film after *Nosferatu*'s success, made for Fox Studios.
- The *Man* is tempted by a seductive woman (*Woman from the City*) to drown his wife; when they go to the city, the city's lights and attractions momentarily seduce the Man, but he abandons the plan.
- The film shifts from dark German Expressionist shadow (rural guilt and temptation) to bright, stylized city scenes (artifice and corruption), then back to redemptive nature.
- Centers on a symbolic boat ride: after nearly drowning his wife, the Man rescues her, and in the *Sunrise* they experience rebirth and renewed love.
- Uses innovative visual effects: miniatures for city shots, soft focus for romantic moments, and chiaroscuro to embody moral psychology.
- The title references Psalm 113 ("There is a land of pure delight") and the promise of spiritual dawn after moral night.
- Won the Academy Award for Outstanding Picture at the inaugural ceremony in 1929 (later renamed Best Picture); recognized immediately as an artistic achievement.
- Murnau died in a car accident in 1931, making it his final major work and casting it as a tragic culmination.
