# Pokémon

## summary

*Pokémon* is a multimedia franchise launched by Nintendo and Game Freak in 1996, centered on capturing, training, and battling creatures called Pokémon. Beginning as a pair of Game Boy titles, it exploded into a global phenomenon spanning games, trading cards, film, and merchandise—one of the highest-grossing media franchises of all time.

## you gotta know

- Debuted with *Pokémon Red* and *Pokémon Blue* (1996) on Game Boy; players travel as trainers catching and training Pokémon to battle each other and gym leaders, culminating in the Elite Four and Champion.
- Core mechanic: Pokémon have types (Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, etc.), moves with different power/accuracy, and evolution lines—a Charmander evolves into Charmeleon, then Charizard, gaining stats and new moves.
- The original games featured 151 Pokémon (counting Mew as secret); each generation adds new species—over 1,000 Pokémon exist across ten generations of main-series games.
- Trading is central: *Red* and *Blue* could only catch different Pokémon subsets, forcing players to trade to complete their Pokédex—a codex of all encountered species.
- Pikachu, the franchise mascot, is Ash Ketchum's companion in the long-running anime (1997–present); the trading card game (1996–present) creates a parallel collecting economy.
- Main-series games span Nintendo handhelds (Game Boy through Switch) and consoles; spin-offs include *Pokémon GO* (mobile AR, 2016), mystery dungeon games, and *Pokémon Legends: Arceus*.
- Recent entries (*Sword & Shield*, *Scarlet & Violet*) offer open-world exploration and dynamic difficulty, moving away from strictly linear gym progression.
