# Citizen Kane

## summary

*Citizen Kane* is *Orson Welles's* 1941 directorial debut, a revolutionary biographical drama about the rise and moral decay of newspaper mogul *Charles Foster Kane*. Told through fragmentary flashbacks and investigative interviews, the film redefined cinematic language through innovative cinematography, narrative structure, and sound design.

## you gotta know

- Directed by and starring *Orson Welles*, alongside *Joseph Cotten* and *Dorothy Comingore*.
- Loosely based on the life of *William Randolph Hearst*, a powerful American newspaper tycoon.
- The film's central mystery: the meaning of "Rosebud," Kane's dying word—revealed only at the end to be his childhood sled.
- Won the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay; nominated for Best Picture and Best Director but lost to *How Green Was My Valley*.
- Pioneered deep focus cinematography (courtesy of *Gregg Toland*), low-angle shots, and non-linear narrative structure.
- Examines the corrupting power of wealth, media manipulation, and the American Dream's hollow promise.

## connections

- [[orson-welles]] — child prodigy director and actor whose career was forever shaped by this film.
- [[william-randolph-hearst]] — the historical figure Kane parodies; Hearst's influence suppressed the film's initial release.

- [[joseph-cotten]] — plays Jedediah Leland, Kane's idealistic friend and eventual critic.
- [[rosebud]] — the symbolic object that anchors the entire narrative.
