# Casablanca

## summary

*Casablanca* is a 1942 wartime romance and espionage thriller set in French Morocco, where an embittered American barkeep named *Rick* must choose between love and conscience when his former lover arrives with her freedom-fighter husband. The film blends cynicism, moral ambiguity, and one of cinema's most iconic final scenes.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *Michael Curtiz*, starring *Humphrey Bogart* as Rick Blaine and *Ingrid Bergman* as *Ilsa Lund*.
- The most famous line: "Here's looking at you, kid"—Bogart's improvised farewell to Ilsa.
- Won the Academy Award for Best Picture in 1944, alongside Best Director and Best Adapted Screenplay.
- Set in the Blue Parrot nightclub in Casablanca during the Nazi occupation of France, a hub for refugees and black-market deals.
- Features the iconic emotional scene where *La Marseillaise* is sung to drown out Nazi patrons' songs.
- The ending defies Hollywood convention: Rick sacrifices his love to help Ilsa and her husband Victor Laszlo escape, joining the Resistance himself.
