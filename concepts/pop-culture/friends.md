# Friends

## summary

*Friends* aired on NBC from 1994 to 2004 and followed six twentysomethings—*Rachel*, *Monica*, *Phoebe*, *Ross*, *Chandler*, and *Joey*—navigating careers, relationships, and friendship in New York City. The show centered emotional weight on the ensemble bond itself; the "will-they-won't-they" romantic plots were secondary to the core appeal of watching friends support, betray, and reunite with each other. *Friends* became a global phenomenon that defined 1990s television, influenced fashion and hairstyles, and established the friend group as the primary social unit in popular culture, challenging the family-centered sitcom of earlier decades.

## you gotta know

- Starred *Jennifer Aniston* (Rachel Green), *Courteney Cox* (Monica Geller), *Lisa Kudrow* (Phoebe Buffay), *David Schwimmer* (Ross Geller), *Matthew Perry* (Chandler Bing), and *Matt LeBlanc* (Joey Tribbiani).
- Set primarily in the characters' apartments and at Central Perk, a coffeehouse where the group gathered; the setting became almost a character itself.
- The central relationship was Ross and Rachel's on-and-off romance, a "will-they-won't-they" plotline stretched across all 10 seasons.
- *Jennifer Aniston*'s "Rachel" haircut became a cultural phenomenon; women requested the hairstyle so frequently that it became iconic of the 1990s.
- Won 59 Emmy nominations and received praise for its inclusive casting and portrayal of diverse sexualities (Chandler's father, and later Monica's lover Carol).
- Episodes followed a classic sitcom structure: problem in first act, complications in second, resolution in third; each episode a self-contained story.
- The show proved the friend group—not the nuclear family—could anchor a major television series and resonate with a generation.
