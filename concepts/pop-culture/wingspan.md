# Wingspan

## summary

*Wingspan* is a card-driven engine-building game designed by Elizabeth Hargrave and released in 2019, where players attract birds to their personal habitats by playing bird cards and purchasing eggs, with each bird offering unique actions that synergize to create productive economic engines. Lauded for its stunning bird artwork, educational ecological information, and elegant mechanics that simulate habitat diversity, Wingspan became a commercial and critical success, appealing to naturalists and hobbyists alike.

## you gotta know

- Each player builds three habitats (forest, grassland, wetland), playing bird cards that generate eggs, seeds, food tokens, or card draws—cascading into powerful engine combos.
- Bird cards carry accurate ornithological information (wingspan, diet, habitat, interesting facts), transforming the game into an educational experience about real species.
- Turn actions (play a bird, gain resources, lay eggs) are taken from left to right across habitats, with birds from earlier habitats often generating bonuses for later actions—sequential engine-building.
- The game features goal cards (e.g., "most species in grassland," "most eggs in total") that award bonus points, encouraging different strategic focuses.
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* in 2020, affirming accessible design and educational integration as valued in modern games.
- Expansions and regional editions (*Oceania*, *Europe*, *Asia*) introduce hundreds of new bird species and habitats, appealing to serious bird enthusiasts.
- Gender milestone: Hargrave was the first female designer to win the Spiel des Jahres, and the game's success opened doors for more diverse designer representation in hobby games.
