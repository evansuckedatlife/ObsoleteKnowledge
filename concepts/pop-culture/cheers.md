# Cheers

## summary

*Cheers* aired on NBC from 1982 to 1993 and centered on the staff and patrons of a Boston dive bar called Cheers, where regulars and the bartender *Sam Malone* engaged in a mix of romantic complications, witty banter, and ensemble chemistry. The show refined the workplace sitcom formula into a well-oiled machine of sharp writing, complex character relationships, and the illusion of continuity between episodes; it made the local bar a universal backdrop for American loneliness and connection. *Cheers* became a cultural phenomenon that established the template for prestige network sitcoms throughout the 1980s and 1990s.

## you gotta know

- *Ted Danson* starred as Sam Malone, a former baseball player turned bartender, the show's romantic anchor who pursued various love interests across the series.
- The ensemble included *Shelley Long* as Diane Chambers (seasons 1–5), an intellectual cocktail waitress who clashed with Sam; *Kirstie Alley* replaced her as Rebecca Howe, the bar's manager.
- Introduced the "will-they-won't-they" romance as a central sitcom device; the 11-year Sam-and-Diane tension kept viewers invested.
- Refined the multi-character ensemble to perfection: each regular had distinct personality and comedic timing.
- Won 28 Emmy Awards during its run; the finale drew 80 million viewers.
- Set in a single location (the bar) but managed to deepen character arcs and romantic plots over a full season, blending sitcom immediacy with soap-opera continuity.
- The theme song, "Where Everybody Knows Your Name," became iconic shorthand for the show's central metaphor: community.
