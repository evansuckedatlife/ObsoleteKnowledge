# Agricola

## summary

*Agricola* is a worker-placement board game designed by Uwe Rosenberg and released in 2007, where players manage family farms across multiple seasons, placing workers to gather resources, build fields, and raise livestock while balancing subsistence needs with agricultural expansion. Its deeply strategic design, rich theming, and emphasis on long-term planning made it one of the most influential modern board games and established worker-placement as the defining Euro-game mechanic.

## you gotta know

- Players place wooden meeples (family members) on action spaces to gather resources (wood, stone, clay, crops, livestock) or construct improvements to their farm.
- The game progresses through 14 rounds (seasons), increasing the number of available actions each round as players' families grow—a elegant pacing mechanism.
- Victory requires balancing immediate subsistence (feeding family members prevents penalties) with long-term development (pastures, fields, stables, and stone/clay structures score end-game points).
- Numerous professions and minor improvements (52 cards) shuffle into decks, adding strategic variability and rewarding player adaptation.
- Won the *Spiel des Jahres* Kennerspiel award in 2008, cementing worker-placement as a major mechanic for complex games.
- The base game provides substantial content; expansions (*Agricola: Farmers of the Moor*, *Animeeples*, *Revised Edition*) explore new regions, themes, and mechanics.
- Heavy-hitter game requiring 90+ minutes and strategic planning; often cited as the gateway to complex "heavy" Euro-games.

## connections

- [[settlers-of-catan]] — foundational modern game that established German design principles; Agricola builds on its success with greater complexity.
- [[puerto-rico]] — released a year earlier, another landmark worker-placement game emphasizing economic simulation.
- [[dominion]] — released the same year, sharing emphasis on expanding player resources through acquired additions.
- [[worker-placement-mechanics]] — Agricola's design became the template for the worker-placement genre.
- [[ticket-to-ride]] — lighter contemporary classic showing the spectrum of modern game complexity.
