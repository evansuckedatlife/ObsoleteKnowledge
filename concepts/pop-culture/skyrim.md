# The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

## summary

**The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim** is a fantasy role-playing game developed by Bethesda Game Studios, released in 2011 for PlayStation 3, Xbox 360, and PC. Set in the Nordic province of *Skyrim*, players explore dragon-filled landscapes, join factions, craft gear, and pursue a main quest to defeat the world-ending dragon *Alduin*. Its emphasis on player agency, modding community, and exploration made it one of gaming's most enduring titles.

## you gotta know

- Features an open world divided into nine holds, each with towns, dungeons, and wilderness; players traverse freely from the start, though underleveled areas punish early exploration.
- The protagonist is "Dragonborn"—able to absorb dragon souls and use *Thu'um* (dragon shouts), which unlock powerful abilities tied to the narrative of ancient dragon worship.
- Four joinable factions shape playstyle: the *Thieves Guild* (stealth and larceny), the *Dark Brotherhood* (assassination), the *College of Winterhold* (magic), and the *Stormcloaks* vs. *Imperial Legion* (civil war).
- Crafting (alchemy, smithing, enchanting) is central; players loot raw materials and craft or improve weapons, armor, and potions to customize builds.
- Dragon encounters are unpredictable and dramatic: skyrim is populated by resurrected dragons as part of the main plot, making them both main-quest markers and random open-world threats.
- The modding community has sustained the game's popularity for over a decade; mods range from visual overhauls to gameplay mechanics to total conversion projects.
- Released on Nintendo Switch (2017), PlayStation VR (2020), and re-released on PC in 2021 as *Anniversary Edition*, testament to its staying power.
