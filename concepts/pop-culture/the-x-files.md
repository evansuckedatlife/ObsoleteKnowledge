# The X-Files

## summary

*The X-Files* is a science-fiction television series that aired on *Fox* from 1993 to 2018 (with intermittent breaks), becoming one of the most culturally resonant and commercially successful television programs of the 1990s and 2000s. Created by *Chris Carter*, the show featured two FBI agents investigating paranormal cases, blending conspiracy thriller elements, procedural investigation, romance, and an overarching mythology that kept audiences engaged across nine seasons and two theatrical films.

## you gotta know

- Premiered on *Fox* in September 1993 and ran for nine seasons (202 episodes through 2002), later revived for limited runs, becoming one of *Fox's* flagship programs during its development as a major network.
- Starred *David Duchovny* as Agent *Fox Mulder*, a believer in the paranormal hunting the truth, and *Gillian Anderson* as Agent *Dana Scully*, a skeptical scientist whose partnership with Mulder defined the show's dynamic.
- Format combined "mythology" episodes advancing an overarching government-conspiracy narrative about extraterrestrial contact and alien-government collusion, with "monster-of-the-week" standalone episodes exploring paranormal phenomena.
- The show's chemistry between *Mulder* and *Scully*—their unresolved romantic tension, contrasting worldviews, and genuine affection—became the emotional core that sustained the series through inconsistent mythology writing.
- Created a cultural obsession with conspiracy theories, government secrecy, and extraterrestrial life; introduced the phrase "The truth is out there" and the image of the *I Want to Believe* poster became iconic.
- Famous episodes include "Home" (a family living in a depraved, isolated compound), "Bad Blood" (a humorous episode told from both agents' contrasting perspectives), and mythology entries that advanced the alien-conspiracy narrative.
- The show influenced subsequent television series in embracing serialized storytelling, mythology-driven narratives, and the appeal of romantic partnership between protagonists, influencing shows like *Fringe* and *Supernatural*.
