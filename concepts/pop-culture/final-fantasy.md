# Final Fantasy

## summary

*Final Fantasy* is a multimedia role-playing game franchise debuted by Squaresoft in 1987, known for elaborate fantasy worlds, emotionally driven narratives, and turn-based (later real-time) combat systems. The series pioneered JRPG storytelling and has evolved across 16 main entries, spin-offs, films, and remakes, exerting enormous influence on the genre.

## you gotta know

- Launched on the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) in 1987; *Final Fantasy III* (1990) and *Final Fantasy VI* (1994) on SNES introduced cinematic storytelling, ensemble casts, and villain-driven narratives.
- *Final Fantasy VII* (1997) for PlayStation became a cultural touchstone: Cloud Strife, the ex-soldier protagonist, leads a ragtag resistance against the megacorp Shinra and the god-like Sephiroth, across a globe-spanning quest with catastrophic stakes.
- Job system (prominent in entries 1, 3, 5, 10): players assign class-based roles—Knight, Mage, Thief—shaping character abilities and team composition.
- The "Final Fantasy" title is unrelated to numbering: Squaresoft named the first game *Final Fantasy* believing it would be their last; its success meant each new entry got a new number but no direct plot connection.
- *Final Fantasy X* (2001) introduced fully voice-acted characters, real-time combat segments (Active Time Battle), and the Sphere Grid character progression system.
- *Final Fantasy XIV* (2010, then *A Realm Reborn* 2013) is a massively multiplayer online RPG (MMORPG) that became a cultural phenomenon, with an acclaimed narrative campaign spanning expansions (*Shadowbringers*, *Endwalker*).
- *Final Fantasy VII Remake* (2020) is a full reimagining of the original, splitting the story across multiple installments and recontextualizing plot points.
