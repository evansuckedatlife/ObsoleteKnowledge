# Tetris

## summary

*Tetris* is a Soviet-designed puzzle game released in 1984, built on the mechanic of rotating and dropping geometric tetrominoes (four-square blocks) to clear horizontal lines. Simple yet infinitely replayable, it became one of the best-selling and most-played video games of all time, transcending gaming to become a cultural phenomenon.

## you gotta know

- Designed by Alexey Pajitnov in 1984 at the Soviet Academy of Sciences; released commercially on the Nintendo Game Boy in 1989, bundled with most units, driving handheld gaming adoption worldwide.
- Core mechanic: seven distinct tetromino shapes (I, O, T, S, Z, J, L) fall from the top of a grid; players rotate and position them to complete horizontal lines, which vanish and award points.
- Speed increases as the game progresses—blocks fall faster—creating escalating difficulty and the tension between strategic placement and reaction time; no failure state, only increasing challenge.
- Became a cultural icon: the *Tetris* theme (based on the Russian folk song "Korobeiniki") is one of the most recognizable video game pieces; the game's simple premise masks deep strategic depth.
- Countless adaptations across platforms: Game Boy, NES, Sega Genesis, arcade cabinets, mobile phones, Alexa devices, and even Glow Soccer pieces; each generation rediscovered the game.
- Modern entries include *Tetris Effect* (2018) with psychedelic visuals and music integration, and *Tetris 99* (2019), a battle-royale variant on Nintendo Switch where 99 players compete simultaneously.
- Holds world records for both single-game engagement and lifetime revenue; the 2020 film dramatized the Cold War licensing battles over *Tetris* rights.
