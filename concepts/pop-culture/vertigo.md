# Vertigo

## summary

*Vertigo* is *Alfred Hitchcock's* 1958 psychological thriller starring *James Stewart* as a detective struggling with acrophobia and obsession. The film blends crime, mystery, and dark romance into a visually inventive exploration of desire, deception, and psychological unraveling that has grown in critical esteem since its release.

## you gotta know

- Directed by *Alfred Hitchcock*, starring *James Stewart* as John Ferguson and *Kim Novak* in dual roles.
- Stewart's character, a retired detective with acrophobia (fear of heights), becomes obsessed with a woman who may not be who she claims.
- The film's visual innovation includes the "dolly zoom" (push-forward, pull-back) effect, creating a distinctive sense of vertigo and disorientation for the viewer.
- A partial remake of the 1954 French film *Diaboliques*, though Hitchcock transforms the source material entirely.
- Initially received mixed reviews and modest box office; now regarded as one of cinema's greatest films and Hitchcock's masterpiece.
- The film explores themes of manipulation, identity, and the male protagonist's destructive projection onto the female character.

## connections

- [[alfred-hitchcock]] — master of suspense whose psychological sophistication reached a peak with this film.
- [[james-stewart]] — everyman actor cast against type in a darker, morally ambiguous role.
- [[kim-novak]] — actress whose dual performance as two sides of desire anchors the film's central mystery.
- [[ernest-lehman]] — screenwriter who adapted the story with psychological depth.
- [[bernard-herrmann]] — composer whose unsettling orchestral score intensifies the film's emotional vertigo.
