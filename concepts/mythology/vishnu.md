# Vishnu

## summary

**Vishnu** is the preserver god of the Hindu trinity, responsible for maintaining cosmic order and protecting the universe from chaos. He is revered across Hindu traditions as the sustainer of existence and is known for descending to Earth in multiple *avatars* to restore dharma when it is threatened.

## you gotta know

- The second member of the *Trimurti* (trinity) alongside Brahma and Shiva, responsible for *preservation* and maintenance.
- Depicted as blue-skinned, holding a *chakra* (discus) and *conch*, often reclining on the serpent *Shesha* in the cosmic ocean.
- Has innumerable *avatars* (incarnations), the most celebrated being *Krishna* and *Rama*, who embody his will on Earth.
- *Lakshmi*, goddess of wealth and fortune, is his eternal consort and appears at his side in most depictions.
- Central to the *Bhagavad Gita*, where Krishna (his avatar) instructs Arjuna on dharma and duty.
- Worshipped in Vaishnavism, one of the major branches of Hinduism that prioritizes devotion to Vishnu.
- In cosmology, he maintains the balance between creation and destruction across infinite cycles of time.
- Rides *Garuda*, the divine eagle, as his mount and celestial vehicle.

## connections

- [[krishna]] — his most celebrated avatar, incarnated to defeat demonic forces.
- [[rama]] — another major avatar, hero of the *Ramayana*, embodiment of righteous dharma.
- [[lakshmi]] — his eternal consort and counterpart, goddess of prosperity and grace.
- [[brahma]] — fellow member of the Trimurti, responsible for creation.
- [[shiva]] — the third member of the Trimurti, responsible for dissolution and transformation.
- [[trimurti]] — the divine trinity in which Vishnu holds the role of preserver.
- [[bhagavad-gita]] — text in which Krishna (Vishnu's avatar) teaches dharma to Arjuna.
