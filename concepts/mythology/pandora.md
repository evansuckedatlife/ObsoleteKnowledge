## summary

The first woman in Greek mythology, created by the gods as punishment for humanity after *Prometheus* stole fire. She is most famous for opening a forbidden jar that released all the evils and sufferings into the world, leaving only hope behind.

## you gotta know

- Created by *Zeus* himself as a punishment for *Prometheus*' theft of fire for mortals.
- Crafted with irresistible beauty by *Hephaestus*, the god of smithcraft, making her a tool of divine vengeance.
- Given a sealed jar (often called Pandora's Box) containing all the world's miseries, diseases, and hardships by *Hermes*.
- Married *Epimetheus*, the Titan brother of *Prometheus*, who accepted her despite warnings from his brother.
- Opened the jar out of curiosity or temptation, releasing all evils into the world and bringing suffering to humanity.
- Only *Hope* remained trapped in the jar at the bottom, a bittersweet legacy for mankind.
- Represents the first of womankind and stands as a cautionary figure about the consequences of curiosity and disobedience.

## connections

- [[prometheus]] — the Titan whose gift to humanity prompted her creation as divine punishment.
- [[zeus]] — her maker, who orchestrated her creation to bring suffering to mankind.
- [[epimetheus]] — her husband, whose name means "afterthought," and whose poor judgment led him to accept her.
- [[hermes]] — the god who delivered the sealed jar that became the instrument of humanity's suffering.
