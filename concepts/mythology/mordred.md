## summary

Mordred emerges as the dark shadow of Arthur's reign—a prince of ambiguous parentage whose ambition and betrayal precipitate the final, catastrophic conflict that destroys Camelot. Born from forbidden union or incest depending on the version, he embodies the price of Arthur's hidden shames and the inevitability of fate.

## you gotta know

- Variously described as Arthur's son by his half-sister *Morgan le Fay*, or his nephew born of Morgan's schemes—the exact relationship varies in tradition
- Often portrayed as a warrior of considerable strength and skill, raised in ignorance of his true parentage or in deliberate rebellion
- Led or participated in the rebellion at *Camlann*, the final battle where Arthur's kingdom was utterly destroyed
- Mortally wounded Arthur at Camlann even as Arthur struck him down, fulfilling the prophecy that Arthur's greatest threat would come from within his own blood
- In some versions, he ruled or attempted to rule Britain after Arthur's fall before being hunted down and slain
- Represents the inexorable working of fate and *Merlin's* prophecies: Arthur's attempts to prevent his destruction only ensured it
- Symbolizes the corruption of kingship through sin, the danger of hidden legitimacies, and the tragic consequence of unacknowledged wrongs
