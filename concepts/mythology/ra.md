# Ra

## summary

The sun god and supreme deity of ancient Egypt, Ra travels across the sky in his solar barque each day and through the underworld each night. He represents the creative force, light, and the ordered cosmos itself, worshipped across every era of Egyptian civilization from the earliest dynasties onward.

## you gotta know

- *Falcon-headed* god associated with the sun disk, often depicted wearing the uraeus (sacred serpent)
- Journeys across the sky by day in his solar boat and battles the serpent of chaos *Apophis* each night
- Merged with *Amon* during the New Kingdom to form Amon-Ra, the chief god of the imperial pantheon
- Father of *Shu* (air) and *Tefnut* (moisture), and grandfather of *Geb* (earth) and *Nut* (sky)
- The pharaoh was considered the son of Ra, legitimizing royal authority through divine descent
- Called different names at different times: Khepri at dawn (as a scarab), Ra at noon, and Atum at dusk
- His evening journey through the *Duat* (underworld) was believed to renew the world each night
- Major cult center at *Heliopolis*, where he was worshipped as the primary creator deity

## connections

- [[amon]] — merged with Ra to become Amon-Ra, the supreme god of Thebes
- [[thoth]] — his associate in maintaining cosmic order and recording divine knowledge
- [[aten]] — the solar disk god elevated to supreme status by Akhenaten, challenging Ra's dominance
- [[pharaoh]] — divine son of Ra, granting legitimacy to rule
