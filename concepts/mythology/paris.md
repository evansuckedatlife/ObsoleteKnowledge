# Paris

## summary

Prince of *Troy*, famous for his beauty and weakness, Paris abducted *Helen* of Sparta and triggered the ten-year Trojan War. Though less formidable in battle than his brother *Hector*, he played the central role in the conflict that destroyed his entire civilization.

## you gotta know

- Originally named *Alexandros*, raised as a shepherd in exile due to prophecy of his destructive nature
- Won Helen's hand through *Aphrodite's* blessing after judging the goddess most beautiful in the infamous judgment
- His abduction of Helen—the world's most desired woman—caused the thousand-ship invasion of Troy
- Lacked the martial prowess of *Hector* and other great warriors despite royal birth
- Killed *Achilles* with an arrow to the heel, the Greek champion's only vulnerable point
- Preyed upon by *Philoctetes'* bow and poisoned arrows, which ultimately brought about his death
- Embodied the tension between desire and duty, personal passion and civilizational consequence
