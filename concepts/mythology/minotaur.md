## summary

A hybrid creature with the head of a bull and the body of a man, the *Minotaur* was imprisoned in an elaborate labyrinth on the island of Crete. He fed on human tributes sent from Athens until the hero *Theseus* ventured into the maze and slew the beast, escaping with the help of the princess *Ariadne*.

## you gotta know

- His true name was *Asterion*, though he became known only as the "bull-man" (*Minotaur*) after his transformation
- He was the offspring of *Pasiphae*, wife of King *Minos*, and a sacred bull—the result of divine curse or punishment
- The *Labyrinth* holding him was designed by the master craftsman *Daedalus* with such complexity that escape was impossible without magical aid
- *Theseus* used a thread given to him by *Ariadne* to navigate back through the maze after killing the beast
- The Athenian tribute consisted of fourteen young men and women, sent every seven or nine years as food for the monster
- The *Minotaur* was neither fully man nor fully beast—he could not speak coherently and was driven by pure hunger
- His death marked the end of Athens' shameful obligation to Crete and freed the city from a terrible burden
- Some versions describe him as being part of an unfinished prophecy, suggesting he might have been something greater had circumstances been different

## connections

- [[theseus]] — the Athenian hero who navigated the Labyrinth and slew him
- [[ariadne]] — the Cretan princess who gave Theseus the thread to escape
- [[daedalus]] — the craftsman who built the Labyrinth that imprisoned him
- [[minos]] — the king of Crete who kept the tribute system in place
