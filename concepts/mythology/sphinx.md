# Sphinx

## summary

A winged creature with a human head, typically depicted as female, and the body of a lion, the *Sphinx* posed a deadly riddle to travelers at the gates of Thebes. Those who answered incorrectly were devoured, while the correct answer—given by the young *Oedipus*—forced the monster to hurl herself from a cliff in shame and fury.

## you gotta know

- She had the face and upper body of a beautiful woman, the wings of an eagle or bat, and the powerful haunches and tail of a lion
- Her famous riddle was: "What walks on four legs in the morning, two legs at noon, and three legs in the evening?" (*humanity*, in its stages of life)
- She had been sent by the gods as a plague upon Thebes, devouring anyone who failed to answer her riddle correctly
- *Oedipus*, despite his youth and lowly status, approached her fearlessly and solved the riddle with intelligence rather than strength
- In rage and humiliation at being bested, the *Sphinx* threw herself from her perch, dying on the rocks below
- Some accounts suggest she had been immortal and that her defeat defied prophecy, breaking a divine curse over the city
- Her parentage varied in different versions—some claimed she was the offspring of *Typhon* and *Echidna*, others that she had different divine parents
- Her intelligence and use of cunning over brute force distinguished her from many other classical monsters

## connections

- [[oedipus]] — the young hero whose wit allowed him to solve her riddle and destroy her
- [[thebes]] — the city she plagued and which was freed by her death
- [[echidna]] — sometimes named as her mother in accounts tying her to other primordial monsters
