# Hecuba

## summary

Queen of *Troy* and wife of *Priam*, Hecuba endured unimaginable tragedy as she witnessed her sons' deaths and her city's destruction. Tormented by nightmares foretelling doom and haunted by prophetic knowledge she could not prevent, she embodied the suffering of women caught in men's wars.

## you gotta know

- Queen of Troy, wife to *Priam* and mother of *Hector*, *Paris*, *Cassandra*, and many others
- Plagued by nightmares predicting her sons' destruction, including the fall of Troy itself
- Attempted to prevent *Paris'* birth by ordering his death as an infant due to her visions
- Witnessed *Hector's* death at *Achilles'* hands and the subsequent mutilation of his corpse
- Forced to watch her surviving children enslaved and her city consumed by fire
- After Troy's fall, enslaved to *Odysseus* or other Greek masters, carrying her grief into captivity
- Her tragedy symbolized the collateral human cost of the war, beyond the valor of the heroes
