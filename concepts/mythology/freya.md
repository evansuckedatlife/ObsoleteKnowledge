# Freya

## summary

*Freya* is the goddess of love, beauty, fertility, magic, and war—embodying the full range of female power in Norse mythology. She leads the *Valkyries* in her own right, claiming half the honored dead for her hall *Folkvangr*, and practices the magical arts with a mastery that rivals *Odin* himself.

## you gotta know

- Sister of *Frey* and a member of the *Vanir*, the fertility gods who merged with the *Aesir*.
- Wears the necklace *Brisingamen*, crafted by dwarves and representing her dominion over beauty and desire.
- Owns the golden boar *Hildisvíni* and travels in a chariot drawn by cats, symbols of independence and magic.
- Skilled in *seidr*, a form of prophecy and magic that allowed her to see and shape fate itself.
- Claims half of all warriors who die honorably in battle for her hall *Folkvangr*; she and *Odin* divide the slain.
- Represents the intersection of love and warfare—neither purely gentle nor purely martial, but complex and whole.

## connections

- [[frey]] — her brother, also a Vanir god of fertility and prosperity.
- [[odin]] — rivals and partners in claiming the honored dead.
- [[frigg]] — another powerful goddess of the Aesir.
- [[loki]] — the trickster whose schemes sometimes steal her treasured necklace.
