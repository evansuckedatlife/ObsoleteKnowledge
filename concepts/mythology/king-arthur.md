# King Arthur

## summary

The legendary king of Britain who united the realm and established the *Round Table* as a symbol of chivalric equality and justice. Arthur is the central figure of Arthurian legend, whose reign represents both the heights of medieval chivalry and its tragic downfall, torn between his noble ideals and the flaws of those closest to him.

## you gotta know

- The *once and future king*, destined to sleep in *Avalon* and return in Britain's hour of greatest need
- Rose to kingship by drawing the *Excalibur* from the stone, proving his right to rule
- Founded *Camelot* and the *Round Table*, where knights gathered in brotherhood without hierarchy
- Married *Guinevere*, though her love affair with *Lancelot* fatally weakened the kingdom
- Fathered *Mordred* (variously his son or nephew), who became his nemesis and betrayed him at *Camlann*
- Depended heavily on *Merlin's* counsel and magic in his early years
- Fell in the final battle against Mordred at *Camlann*, mortally wounded but spirited away by the *Lady of the Lake*

## connections

- [[merlin]] — his wise advisor and magical protector from birth
- [[guinevere]] — his queen, whose infidelity with Lancelot brought ruin to Camelot
- [[lancelot]] — his finest knight, whose love for Guinevere destroyed the fellowship
- [[mordred]] — his treacherous son/nephew who led the rebellion that ended his reign
- [[gawain]] — his loyal nephew and one of his greatest champions
- [[excalibur]] — the magical sword that grants him invulnerability and legitimacy
- [[round-table]] — the symbol of his egalitarian vision of chivalry
- [[camelot]] — his legendary seat of power
- [[lady-of-the-lake]] — the mystical figure who gave him Excalibur and received him at his end
