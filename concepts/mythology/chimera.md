# Chimera

## summary

A fire-breathing composite monster combining the front parts of a lion, the middle of a goat, and the rear of a serpent, the *Chimera* ravaged the kingdom of Lycia until the hero *Bellerophon* mounted the winged horse *Pegasus* and slew her from the air. Her hybrid form represented the chaotic mingling of bestial natures.

## you gotta know

- Her body was a chimeric assemblage: the head and foreparts of a lion, a goat's head and torso emerging from her back, and a serpent for a tail
- Fire and smoke erupted continuously from her lion mouth and goat head, allowing her to incinerate anything in her path
- She was the offspring of *Typhon* and *Echidna*, making her sister to the *Lernaean Hydra* and *Cerberus*
- *Bellerophon* could not defeat her through conventional combat, so he mounted the winged horse *Pegasus* to attack from above
- According to the most famous account, he used a lead-tipped lance that melted in her throat as she breathed fire, causing her to choke to death
- She lived in the remote regions of Lycia in Asia Minor, terrorizing the countryside and devouring livestock and travelers alike
- Despite her monstrous nature, she was not described as mindlessly brutal; some accounts suggest she had a degree of cunning
- Her name became synonymous with any impossible or fantastical hybrid creature, a meaning that persists to modern language

## connections

- [[bellerophon]] — the hero who rode Pegasus and slew her with cleverness and courage
- [[pegasus]] — the winged horse without which Bellerophon could never have approached her in flight
- [[echidna]] — her mother, the progenitrix of many classical monsters
- [[typhon]] — her father, the primordial fire-giant
