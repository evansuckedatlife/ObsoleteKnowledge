# Laocoon

## summary

Trojan priest of *Apollo* or *Poseidon*, Laocoon stands as the figure who warned Troy against accepting the wooden horse but was punished for his defiance. His death—strangled by divine serpents along with his sons—became a symbol of fate and divine wrath that humans could not escape.

## you gotta know

- Prominent Trojan priest, either serving *Apollo* or *Poseidon* depending on the tradition
- Famously warned the Trojans not to bring the wooden horse into their city: "I fear the Greeks, even bearing gifts"
- Pleaded with *Priam* to reject the horse, recognizing it as a Greek deception
- Punished by the gods—attacked and strangled by giant sea serpents sent to silence his warning
- His sons were killed alongside him in the serpent attack, a divine demonstration of fate
- The *Aeneid* presents his death as divine retribution for his presumption or his role in the war
- The statue of Laocoon and his sons became one of antiquity's most famous works of art
