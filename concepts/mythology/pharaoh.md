# Pharaoh

## summary

The Pharaoh was the political and religious leader of ancient Egypt, serving as the supreme ruler, high priest, and commander-in-chief. Crucially, the pharaoh was viewed as a divine intermediary between the gods and humanity, possessing a dual nature as both a mortal man and a living god. This institution was central to Egyptian religion, statecraft, and monumental architecture for over three millennia.

## you gotta know

- In life, the pharaoh was considered the earthly embodiment of the sky god *Horus*, the rightful heir and king.
- Upon death, the pharaoh was believed to become *Osiris*, the god of the underworld, transfigured to rule eternally in the afterlife.
- The pharaoh was styled as the "Son of Ra" starting in the Fourth Dynasty, linking royal authority directly to the supreme solar deity.
- A primary duty of the pharaoh was to maintain *Ma'at* (universal truth, balance, and order) by performing rituals, defending the borders, and administering justice to prevent the universe from descending into chaos.
- The pharaoh's royal regalia included the *Uraeus* (a rearing cobra on the headdress representing sovereignty) and the *crook and flail* (representing leadership and coercive power).
- During the New Kingdom, the heretic pharaoh *Akhenaten* temporarily dismantled this traditional theology, declaring himself the sole intermediary for the single god Aten.
