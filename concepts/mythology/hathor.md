# Hathor

## summary

The goddess of love, beauty, fertility, and joy, Hathor was also a protective cosmic force depicted as a cow or as a woman with cow horns and the solar disk. She served as mother and consort to various gods, embodying both the nurturing aspects of motherhood and the ferocious protection of a divine mother.

## you gotta know

- *Cow-headed* goddess or depicted as a woman with a *solar disk between cow horns*, representing fertility and celestial abundance
- Associated with love, music, dance, and intoxication—all forms of ecstasy and celebration that brought joy to mortals and gods
- Sometimes portrayed as Ra's *eye*, a fierce form that defended him against threats (the *Eye of Ra* could embody her wrathful aspect)
- Protective mother figure; depicted nursing or protecting the pharaoh, ensuring his divine legitimacy and nurture
- Goddess of fertility and sexuality; women invoked her for pregnancy and childbirth, and she was celebrated in erotic contexts
- Associated with foreign lands and trade, making her a protector of Egyptian merchants and travelers abroad
- Depicted as gentle and generous in her loving aspects, but fierce and destructive when defending Ra or the cosmic order
- Major cult center at *Dendera*, where her temple was a center for healing, fertility rites, and celebration

## connections

- [[ra]] — sometimes identified as his eye or daughter; served as his fierce defender against chaos
- [[horus]] — sometimes depicted as her son, or she served as a protective mother figure to him
- [[amon]] — occasionally associated through fertility aspects, as both represented generative and nurturing cosmic forces
- [[isis]] — both goddesses were protective mothers, though Hathor had broader associations with love and beauty
- [[pharaoh]] — depicted nursing and protecting the pharaoh, legitimizing and securing his divine right to rule
