# Ajax the Great

## summary

**Ajax the Great**, also called *Telamonian Ajax*, was the mightiest Greek warrior after *Achilles*, renowned for his enormous size, strength, and unwavering courage. Though less clever than *Odysseus*, he was more reliable and faced *Hector* in single combat—one of the *Trojan War*'s most celebrated duels. His tragic end reveals the cost of honor in a world where cunning sometimes trumps merit.

## you gotta know

- Fought *Hector* in single combat for an entire day without either gaining clear victory; their duel ended in mutual respect and an exchange of gifts.
- Defended the Greek ships relentlessly, holding back Trojan forces even as *Achilles* withdrew from battle, often called "the bulwark of the Greeks."
- Second only to *Achilles* in strength and martial prowess; some accounts suggest they were evenly matched in combat.
- Competed with *Odysseus* for the armor of the dead *Achilles*; when *Odysseus* won the prize through rhetoric rather than merit, *Ajax* was devastated.
- Went mad with grief and shame after losing the armor competition, famously slaughtering a herd of sheep while believing them to be his enemies.
- Ended his life by suicide, unable to bear the dishonor, though he was ultimately honored as a hero and worshipped in some Greek cities.

## connections

- [[achilles]] — the only warrior greater than Ajax; he competed for Achilles' armor after the hero's death.
- [[hector]] — his greatest rival, with whom he fought the war's most famous duel.
- [[odysseus]] — the craftier hero whose rhetorical victory over Ajax led to the tragic aftermath.
- [[agamemnon]] — the Greek commander under whom Ajax fought.
- [[diomedes]] — fellow great Greek warrior; they often fought side by side.
- [[the-trojan-war]] — the conflict that defined his glory and his doom.
