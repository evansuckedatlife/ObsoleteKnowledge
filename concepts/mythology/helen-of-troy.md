# Helen of Troy

## summary

The legendary beauty whose abduction by *Paris* triggered the Trojan War, one of the greatest conflicts in Greek mythology. Born from *Zeus* and a mortal woman, Helen became both a coveted prize and the catalyst for the destruction of Troy.

## you gotta know

- Daughter of *Zeus* and *Leda*, sister to *Clytemnestra*, making her half-divine and extraordinarily beautiful.
- Called "the face that launched a thousand ships" for her beauty, which inspired the Greek armada to sail for Troy.
- Originally married to *Menelaus*, king of Sparta, before being spirited away by *Paris*, prince of Troy.
- Taken to Troy by *Paris* under the influence of *Aphrodite*, who promised him the most beautiful woman in the world.
- Her absence from Sparta and the dishonor of her abduction became the justification for the entire ten-year Trojan War.
- Stayed in Troy for a decade, though accounts vary on whether she loved *Paris* or resented her captivity.
- Eventually returned to *Menelaus* after Troy's fall, either honored as a prize of war or reconciled with her husband.

## connections

- [[menelaus]] — her husband, king of Sparta, whose honor was insulted by her abduction.
- [[paris]] — the Trojan prince who took her to Troy, setting the war in motion.
- [[clytemnestra]] — her sister, wife of *Agamemnon*, who shared in the royal bloodline of Sparta.
- [[the-trojan-war]] — the entire conflict sparked by her abduction and the competing claims over her.
- [[achilles]] — the greatest Greek warrior who died fighting in the war for her sake.
