# The Aeneid

## summary

The Aeneid is a Latin epic poem written by Virgil between 29 and 19 BC. It tells the legendary story of Aeneas, a Trojan who fled the fall of Troy and travelled to Italy, where he became the ancestor of the Romans. Written to provide Rome with a national origin myth and glorify the reign of Emperor Augustus, the poem is widely considered Virgil's masterpiece and a cornerstone of Western literature.

## you gotta know

- The epic is divided into twelve books: the first six model the wanderings of the *Odyssey*, while the second six model the battles of the *Iliad*.
- The poem begins *in media res* with the goddess Juno causing a massive storm that wrecks Aeneas's fleet near Carthage, where he is welcomed by Queen *Dido*.
- Book II contains the most famous surviving description of the sack of Troy, recounted by Aeneas to Dido, including the tragic death of the priest *Laocoon* and his sons.
- Aeneas abandons Carthage to fulfill his destiny, prompting a devastated Queen *Dido* to commit suicide on a funeral pyre, cursing Aeneas's descendants and initiating the legendary enmity between Rome and Carthage.
- In Book VI, Aeneas visits the underworld with the Sibyl of Cumae, where his deceased father Anchises shows him a parade of future Roman heroes, including Julius Caesar and Augustus.
- The second half of the epic focuses on the war in Latium between the Trojans and the native Rutuli, culminating in a final duel where Aeneas kills the Italian champion *Turnus*.
