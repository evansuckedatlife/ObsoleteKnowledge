# Frey

## summary

*Frey* is the god of fertility, summer, sunshine, and prosperity. Originally a giant sent to live among the Aesir, he represents growth, abundance, and the peaceful flourishing of the world—though his greatest sacrifice for love leaves him vulnerable at the end of all things.

## you gotta know

- Member of the *Vanir* (fertility gods) who joined the *Aesir* after a truce between the two divine families.
- Sacrificed his magical sword—which fought of its own accord—for love of the giantess *Gerdr*, leaving him weaponless at Ragnarok.
- Governs summer, sunshine, and fair weather; his blessing brings bountiful harvests and prosperous years.
- Rides the golden boar *Gullinbursti*, whose bristles glow bright enough to light the darkest nights.
- Associated with peace, joy, and sensual pleasure—a gentler face of divinity than the warrior gods *Odin* and *Thor*.
- His tragic love story and the loss of his sword foreshadow his death at Ragnarok, when he will fight a giant without his legendary weapon.

## connections

- [[freya]] — his twin sister, also a Vanir deity of fertility and magic.
- [[odin]] — the king of the gods, himself a Vanir-aligned figure.
- [[loki]] — the trickster involved in many transformations and curses.
