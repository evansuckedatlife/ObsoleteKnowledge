## summary

The goddess and abstract principle of truth, justice, cosmic order, and balance, Ma'at represented the fundamental laws governing the universe. Every morning Ra restored Ma'at by triumphing over chaos; every soul was judged against her feather in the afterlife, making her the foundation of Egyptian moral and cosmic philosophy.

## you gotta know

- Depicted as a *woman wearing a single ostrich feather* on her head, symbolizing truth that cannot bend or hide
- The feather of Ma'at was weighed against the hearts of the dead to judge their worthiness for the afterlife
- Embodied cosmic order itself—the balance and harmony that prevented the universe from returning to primordial chaos
- Not merely a goddess but a *principle*: truth, justice, harmony, and the natural law of existence
- Every action, decree, and divine judgment was made *in accordance with Ma'at*; her violation caused cosmic disruption
- Her opposite was *Isfet* (chaos), which Ra fought against nightly alongside *Thoth*, who was often her divine partner
- Fundamental to Egyptian ethics: living by Ma'at meant living truthfully, justly, and in harmony with the cosmic order
- The concept extended beyond religion into law, governance, and daily conduct—to "live by Ma'at" was to live correctly

## connections

- [[thoth]] — her divine associate and cosmic partner, who records and enforces truth and justice
- [[osiris]] — judges the dead by weighing their hearts against Ma'at's feather in the Hall of Judgment
- [[ra]] — restores Ma'at each day by defeating the chaos serpent *Apophis* in his nightly underworld journey
- [[pharaoh]] — ruled by claiming to uphold and enforce Ma'at on behalf of the gods
- [[book-of-the-dead]] — guides souls through trials that test their adherence to Ma'at's principles
