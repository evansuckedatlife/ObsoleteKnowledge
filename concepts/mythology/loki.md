## summary

*Loki* is the trickster god, a shape-shifter whose schemes both aid and threaten the Aesir. Born of giants but living among the gods, he embodies chaos, cunning, and contradiction—until his betrayals culminate in triggering *Ragnarok*, the destruction of all things.

## you gotta know

- A giant by birth but accepted into the gods' company through oath-brotherhood with *Odin*.
- Master of shape-shifting and illusion; he can change his form and sex, allowing him to infiltrate and manipulate.
- Directly responsible for the death of *Balder* by tricking the blind god *Hod* into throwing the mistletoe dart that kills the beloved.
- Father of *Fenrir* (the wolf), *Jormungandr* (the World Serpent), and *Hel* (the death goddess)—all destined to fight against the gods at Ragnarok.
- His schemes often result in the gods acquiring their greatest treasures—*Gungnir*, *Mjolnir*, *Draupnir*—despite being motivated by trickery.
- Bound under the earth with a serpent dripping venom on his face as punishment; he will break free to lead the forces of chaos at the end of all things.
- Represents the necessary chaos within order, the boundary-crosser who reveals uncomfortable truths and tests the gods' limits.

## connections

- [[odin]] — his oath-brother, who ultimately cannot control him.
- [[thor]] — his frequent antagonist, though bound by complex ties.
- [[balder]] — his indirect victim, whose death sets Ragnarok in motion.
- [[heimdall]] — his final opponent at Ragnarok, the watchman and the trickster in cosmic opposition.
- [[frigg]] — whose attempts to save Balder he directly undermines.
