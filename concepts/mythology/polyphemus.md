# Polyphemus

## summary

A Cyclops giant with a single massive eye in the center of his forehead, Polyphemus is famous for his encounter with *Odysseus* in Homer's epic. Trapped in the monster's cave with several of his men, *Odysseus* outsmarted the brutish giant, blinding his eye with a heated stake and escaping by clinging to the undersides of sheep.

## you gotta know

- He was a *Cyclops*, one of the one-eyed giants born from *Poseidon* and the sea nymph *Thoosa*
- Despite his strength and size, *Polyphemus* was remarkably simple-minded, a shepherd who kept flocks of enormous sheep
- His eye, the size of a wagon wheel, was positioned in the center of his forehead and could see considerable distances
- *Odysseus* deceived him by calling himself "Nobody," so when *Polyphemus* cried out that "Nobody is killing me," the other Cyclopes thought he was mad
- After his blinding, *Polyphemus* called upon his father *Poseidon* to curse *Odysseus*, resulting in years of divine punishment and wandering
- He lived in a cave on the island of Sicily (or in Sicily-adjacent lands in various versions) where he made cheese and kept his flocks
- The giant ate several of *Odysseus*' men raw, tearing them apart and devouring them while the hero watched helplessly
- His parentage made him partially divine, giving him immortality despite his lost eye
