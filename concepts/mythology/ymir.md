# Ymir

## summary

*Ymir* is the primordial giant whose body became the physical world itself. In Norse creation mythology, Ymir's sacrifice by Odin and his brothers marks the boundary between chaos and order, making him the foundational sacrifice upon which all existence rests.

## you gotta know

- His body was dismembered to create the cosmos: flesh became earth, blood became seas and rivers, bones became mountains, and skull became sky.
- Born from the chaos of *Muspelheim* (fire realm) and *Niflheim* (ice realm) meeting in the void of *Ginnungagap*.
- Originally depicted as neither fully good nor evil—a force of primordial nature rather than a character with agency or intention.
- His eyelashes became the boundary wall *Midgard*, protecting the realm of humans from giants.
- The eternal conflict between chaos and order begins with his death; Ragnarok (the end of all things) echoes this original destruction.

## connections

- [[odin]] — led the killing of Ymir and used his corpse to shape reality.
- [[norns]] — dwell at the base of Yggdrasil and weave fate over the world made from Ymir's remains.
