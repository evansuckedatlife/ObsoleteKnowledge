# Indra

## summary

**Indra** is the king of the gods and the lord of storms, thunder, and rain in Hindu and Vedic tradition. A warrior deity of immense power, he commands the celestial army and is celebrated in the *Vedas* for his victory over demons and his role in maintaining cosmic order through martial might and divine authority.

## you gotta know

- King of the *Devas* (gods) and ruler of the heavens, commanding all divine beings and celestial forces.
- The god of *storms*, *thunder*, *lightning*, and *rain*, wielding the *Indra danda* (thunderbolt or club) as his primary weapon.
- Rides on the celestial elephant *Airavata*, a white, four-tusked creature that serves as his divine mount.
- Depicted with multiple eyes, symbolizing his omniscience and ability to perceive all events across the universe.
- Celebrated in the *Vedas* for his victory over the demon *Vritra*, a serpent who hoarded water and prevented rain from falling.
- Holds the *vajra* (thunderbolt), the most powerful weapon in Hindu mythology, forged from the celestial sage *Dadhichi's* bones.
- Though supreme among gods, he is subject to the cycles of cosmic time and can be challenged or dethroned in later Hindu mythologies.
- Associated with courage, heroism, and the martial virtues, making him a patron of warriors and rulers.

## connections

- [[vedas]] — ancient texts that celebrate Indra's power and role as king of the gods.
- [[agni]] — the fire god, often depicted alongside Indra in Vedic hymns and cosmic narratives.
