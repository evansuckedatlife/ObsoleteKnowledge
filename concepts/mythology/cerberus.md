# Cerberus

## summary

The three-headed monstrous hound of the underworld, *Cerberus* stood guard at the gates of *Hades*, ensuring that no living soul could pass and no dead soul could escape. *Heracles* captured this fearsome beast as his twelfth and final labor, temporarily bringing him to the surface world before returning him to his eternal post.

## you gotta know

- He possessed three massive dog heads, each capable of independent action and fury, allowing him to watch in all directions simultaneously
- His tail was said to be a serpent itself, coiling and striking at intruders, making him part hound and part serpent composite
- *Cerberus* was not evil by nature but served his master *Hades* faithfully, showing mercy only to those who came willingly to the underworld
- The musician *Orpheus* famously charmed all three heads with his lyre, the only living mortal known to have pacified the beast through art
- *Heracles* subdued him through strength and determination, wrestling the hound without weapons and bringing it to the surface as proof of his twelfth labor
- His parentage was often given as *Typhon* and *Echidna*, making him kin to the *Chimera* and *Lernaean Hydra*
- After *Heracles* displayed him to King *Eurystheus*, the beast was safely returned to the underworld, where he resumed his post
- The very presence of *Cerberus* was said to instill dread in mortals; even seeing a representation of him could inspire terror

## connections
