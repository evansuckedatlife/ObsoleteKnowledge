# Calydonian Boar

## summary

A massive, supernatural wild boar sent by the goddess *Artemis* to ravage the land of Calydon as punishment for a king's impiety, the *Calydonian Boar* became the target of a legendary hunt that drew heroes from across Greece. The beast was finally brought down by the swift huntress *Atalanta*, whose first strike gave her honor alongside the greatest male heroes of the age.

## you gotta know

- *Artemis* sent the boar to devastate the kingdom of Calydon after King *Oeneus* failed to honor her with proper sacrifices
- The beast's hide was impenetrable to normal weapons, making it nearly impossible to kill through conventional means
- Its size was enormous, described as being as large as a bull or larger, with tusks capable of goring warriors and their horses alike
- The hunt required assembly of the greatest heroes of the era, including *Jason*, *Theseus*, *Heracles*, and many others—a precursor to the later expedition for the *Golden Fleece*
- *Atalanta*, a swift huntress and warrior, was the first to draw blood from the beast, striking it with her spear before the male heroes could land meaningful blows
- *Meleager*, the king's son, ultimately delivered the killing blow to the weakened beast, though the honor belonged to *Atalanta*
- The boar's death sparked conflict over the trophy pelt, with *Atalanta*'s role becoming a point of contention that revealed the prejudices of the age
- Despite its supernatural origin and destructive power, the boar was described more as an instrument of divine will than as a conscious monster
