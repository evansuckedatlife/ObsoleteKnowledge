## summary

Sir Gawain stands as one of Arthur's most formidable and loyal knights, celebrated for strength that waxes and wanes with the sun. His confrontation with the mysterious *Green Knight* remains one of medieval literature's greatest tests of honor, courage, and the moral ambiguities underlying chivalric virtue.

## you gotta know

- Arthur's nephew, often portrayed as his heir or champion in matters of the Crown's honor
- Possessed supernatural strength that increased as the sun climbed toward noon and diminished toward evening—a power that defined many of his victories
- Star of the tale *Sir Gawain and the Green Knight*, where he accepts a beheading challenge from an otherworldly opponent
- Accepted the Green Knight's challenge to prove his courage, traveling to a mystical castle and ultimately learning hard lessons about temptation and honor
- Known for courtesy and grace as well as martial prowess, embodying the ideal of the complete knight
- Remained fiercely loyal to Arthur even when other knights turned against the king, particularly in the final conflicts
- Represents the tension between supernatural gifts, human weakness, and the genuine heroism required to maintain honor despite temptation
