# Round Table

## summary

The Round Table is the iconic table in Arthurian legend around which King Arthur and his knights congregated. Representing the egalitarian spirit of the chivalric order, its circular design ensured that all who sat there were of equal status, with no single seat designated as the head. It served as both a literal meeting place in Camelot and a symbol of the code of honor, unity, and questing that defined Arthur's court.

## you gotta know

- The table was designed with no head or foot so that all knights sitting around it would be considered equal, preventing disputes over precedence.
- According to Robert de Boron's poem *Merlin*, the table was created by the wizard *Merlin* for Arthur's father, Uther Pendragon, and was modeled after the table of the Last Supper.
- It was later given to Arthur as a wedding gift by Leodegrance, the father of Queen *Guinevere*, along with a contingent of a hundred knights.
- The table featured a single vacant seat called the *Siege Perilous*, which was reserved exclusively for the knight destined to find the Holy Grail; anyone else who sat in it was instantly swallowed by the earth or struck dead.
- The Siege Perilous was successfully occupied by *Galahad*, the pure son of Lancelot, signifying the beginning of the Quest for the Holy Grail.
- The fellowship of the Round Table was ultimately fractured and destroyed by the discovery of Lancelot and Guinevere's affair, leading to a civil war and the final battle at Camlann.
