# Ganesha

## summary

**Ganesha** is the elephant-headed god of beginnings, wisdom, and the remover of obstacles in Hindu tradition. Beloved across all branches of Hinduism and worshipped before undertaking any significant endeavor, he represents intelligence, prudence, and the clearing of hindrances on the path to success.

## you gotta know

- Son of *Shiva* and *Parvati*, the divine couple of the Hindu pantheon; one of the most widely worshipped deities across India.
- Depicted with an elephant's head and human body, embodying both earthly strength and divine wisdom in a single form.
- Carries a *modak* (sweet dumpling), a *goad*, and a noose, symbolizing prosperity, discipline, and the binding of desires.
- Known as the *remover of obstacles* (*vighnaharta*), invoked at the beginning of rituals, ceremonies, and new ventures for blessings and success.
- Associated with intelligence, cunning, and the written word; worshipped by scholars, artists, and those seeking knowledge.
- His *vahana* (mount) is a mouse or rat, representing humility and the ability to navigate even the smallest spaces.
- One of the most recognizable deities in the world due to his distinctive elephant head, making him a symbol of Hinduism globally.
- Celebrates obstacles as gateways to growth, teaching that wisdom lies in transcending rather than avoiding challenges.
