# Typhon

## summary

The most fearsome of all primordial monsters, Typhon was a fire-breathing giant born from *Gaia* herself as a weapon against the Olympian gods. His body was said to contain a hundred dragon heads, and his sheer power nearly overthrew *Zeus* and the entire divine order before he was defeated and imprisoned beneath Mount Aetna.

## you gotta know

- *Typhon* had a human upper body but from the waist down consisted of massive coiled serpents with venomous fangs and ever-moving heads
- He fathered (or was father-figure to) some of the most dangerous monsters in Greek mythology, including the *Lernaean Hydra*, the *Chimera*, and *Cerberus*
- His attack on *Olympus* forced the gods to flee in animal form, temporarily scattering the divine council
- Fire and molten lava spurted from his many mouths and eyes, making him a walking natural disaster
- *Zeus* ultimately defeated him using his master bolt (*adamantine lightning*), then imprisoned the monster under the volcanic mountain *Aetna* in Sicily
- The trembling of *Aetna* was believed by ancients to be *Typhon* shifting and stirring beneath the earth
- His mother *Gaia* bore him specifically to challenge the gods after they had defeated her previous champion, the *Titans*
- Some sources describe his hundred heads regenerating or being so numerous that counting them was impossible

## connections

- [[zeus]] — his arch-enemy who ultimately defeated him with the master bolt
- [[echidna]] — often cited as his mate, mother of his monstrous offspring
- [[lernaean-hydra]] — his offspring, the many-headed serpent of the marshes
- [[chimera]] — another of his progeny
- [[cerberus]] — the three-headed guard dog of the underworld, sometimes claimed as his child
