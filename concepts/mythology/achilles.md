# Achilles

## summary

**Achilles** was the mightiest warrior of the *Trojan War*, nearly invulnerable and unmatched in combat prowess. The son of *Thetis*, a sea-nymph, and *Peleus*, a mortal king, he represents the ideal of martial excellence yet also the tragedy of a short, glorious life. His rage, honor, and eventual death define much of the *Iliad*.

## you gotta know

- Made invulnerable by his mother *Thetis*, who dipped him in the river *Styx* as an infant—except for his heel, where she held him.
- The greatest warrior at *Troy*, slaying countless Trojan soldiers and their champions throughout the ten-year siege.
- Withdrew from battle after his captive *Briseis* was taken by *Agamemnon*, nearly causing Greek defeat in his absence.
- Killed *Hector*, the Trojan prince and greatest warrior on their side, in single combat, dragging his body around Troy's walls in revenge for *Patroclus*.
- His closest companion, *Patroclus*, was killed wearing Achilles' armor; grief over this death drove him back to war.
- Died when *Paris* shot a poisoned arrow through his heel, the only vulnerable part of his body, fulfilling the prophecy that he could not be killed in battle.
