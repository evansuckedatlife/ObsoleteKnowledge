# Atalanta

## summary

**Atalanta** was the legendary huntress and warrior, unique among Greek heroes for her determination to remain independent and unmarried. A skilled archer and runner of supernatural speed, she demanded that her suitors race her—and defeated them until a clever challenger used golden apples to distract her. Her story explores themes of freedom, identity, and the cost of losing one's self.

## you gotta know

- Raised by *Artemis* and her nymphs after being exposed as an infant; she became devoted to the goddess and sworn to chastity.
- The only woman to sail with *Jason* aboard the *Argo* in search of the *Golden Fleece*, earning respect as an equal to the male heroes.
- Famously raced any suitor willing to compete, vowing to marry only the man who could beat her in a footrace; she killed all who lost.
- Outran *Hippomenes* until he dropped three golden apples given to him by *Aphrodite*, which distracted her long enough for him to win.
- Married *Hippomenes*, but the couple's disrespect toward the gods led *Aphrodite* and *Zeus* to transform them both into lions.
- Participated in the hunt for the *Calydonian Boar*, the only woman among the hunters to strike the beast.

## connections

- [[jason]] — fellow Argonaut; she was the only woman on the quest.
