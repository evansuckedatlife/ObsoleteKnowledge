## summary

The falcon-headed god of the sky and rightful heir to Egypt's throne, Horus was born to Isis after his father Osiris's death. He defeated his uncle Set in a legendary combat to claim his inheritance and became the model for every pharaoh, who claimed descent from him as the legitimate kings of Egypt.

## you gotta know

- *Falcon-headed* god with piercing eyes representing his dominion over the sky and his watchful protection
- Son of *Osiris* and *Isis*, conceived after his father's death through Isis's magical resurrection of Osiris
- Defeated *Set* in a violent conflict that lasted years, ultimately claiming the throne and uniting Egypt under legitimate rule
- Embodied kingship itself; every pharaoh was called the "Horus" of their time, legitimizing their divine right to rule
- One of his eyes (the *Wedjat Eye*) was torn out in battle with Set but restored, becoming a powerful protective amulet
- Associated with the sky and the sun, sometimes depicted as a child being nursed by Isis or as a grown warrior
- Worshipped as a solar deity in some contexts, merging aspects with Ra's identity as the sun's journey
- Major cult center at *Edfu*, where a vast temple preserved the myth of his triumph over Set

## connections

- [[osiris]] — his father, whom he avenged through his defeat of Set
- [[isis]] — his mother, who protected him in the marshes and raised him in secret
- [[set]] — his uncle and opponent, whom he defeated in combat for rightful kingship
- [[ra]] — sometimes identified with Ra or depicted as his son in solar mythology
- [[thoth]] — served as judge and mediator in disputes between Horus and Set
- [[pharaoh]] — every pharaoh was the living Horus, embodying his kingship and divine legitimacy
