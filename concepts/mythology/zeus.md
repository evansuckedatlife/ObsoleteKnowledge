# Zeus

## summary

Zeus is the king of the Olympian gods and the supreme deity in Greek mythology, ruling over the sky, weather, thunder, lightning, law, order, and justice. He overthrew his father Cronus to lead the gods in the Titanomachy, establishing his seat of power on Mount Olympus. Known for his supreme power and numerous divine and mortal trysts, his complex family tree forms the backbone of Greek mythological genealogy.

## you gotta know

- He overthrew his father, the Titan *Cronus*, by serving him an emetic to regurgitate his swallowed siblings, then led the Olympians to victory in the ten-year *Titanomachy*.
- His signature weapon is the *thunderbolt*, which was crafted for him by the Cyclopes during the Titanomachy.
- He defeated the gargantuan serpent-monster *Typhon*, Gaia's final challenge to his rule, and imprisoned him beneath Mount Etna.
- He frequently transformed himself to seduce mortals and goddesses, taking the form of a *swan* to seduce Leda, a *bull* for Europa, and a *shower of gold* for Danaë.
- His sacred animal is the *eagle*, and his sacred tree is the *oak*, with his primary oracle located at Dodona, where priests interpreted the rustling of oak leaves.
- He is the father of numerous prominent heroes and gods, including Heracles, Perseus, Athena, Apollo, Artemis, and Hermes.

## connections

- [[danae]] — he visited her as a shower of gold, fathering the hero Perseus.
- [[heracles]] — his son, the strongest mortal, whom he fathered with Alcmene.
- [[perseus]] — his hero son whom he fathered with Danaë.
- [[typhon]] — the monstrous giant whom Zeus defeated and imprisoned under Mount Etna.
- [[bellerophon]] — he sent a gadfly to sting Pegasus, throwing Bellerophon to the earth when he tried to fly to Olympus.
- [[helen-of-troy]] — his daughter, whom he fathered with Leda after transforming into a swan.
