## summary

Princess of *Troy* cursed by *Apollo* to speak truth that no one would believe, Cassandra witnessed the Trojan War unfold exactly as she warned. Her tragic fate—ignored, prophesying doom, then enslaved after the war—made her the archetype of the unheeded prophet condemned to watch disaster unfold.

## you gotta know

- Daughter of *Priam* and *Hecuba*, blessed with prophecy but cursed that no one would believe her
- Cursed by *Apollo* after she rejected his romantic advances, turning her gift into a burden
- Warned the Trojans against bringing the wooden horse into the city, but was dismissed
- Watched Troy burn exactly as she had predicted, her words proven accurate only in destruction
- After the war, enslaved to *Agamemnon* and taken to Mycenae as his concubine
- Murdered upon arrival in Greece, either by *Agamemnon's* wife *Clytemnestra* or alongside him
- Symbolized the tragedy of being right at the wrong time: a prophet whose wisdom saved no one

## connections

- [[priam]] — her father, the king who refused to heed her warnings
