## summary

The goddess of magic, motherhood, and devotion, Isis used her supernatural powers to resurrect her murdered husband Osiris and conceived their son Horus in secret. She became one of the most widely worshipped goddesses in Egypt and eventually spread throughout the Greco-Roman world as a symbol of feminine power and protection.

## you gotta know

- *Winged* goddess crowned with a throne symbol on her head, often depicted with Horus as an infant at her breast
- Performed the first act of magic in Egyptian mythology by using a spell to resurrect her husband *Osiris* after Set's murder
- Concealed herself and her infant son *Horus* in the papyrus marshes to protect him from Set's vengeance
- Queen of all magic (*heka*), wielding spells to heal, protect, and restore life itself
- Sister and wife of *Osiris*, sister of *Set* and *Nephthys*, mother of *Horus*—central to the greatest myth cycle
- Her devotion to Osiris and maternal protection of Horus made her the ideal model of *fidelity* and *motherhood*
- Worshipped throughout Egypt and later adopted into Greco-Roman mystery religions as a universal mother goddess
- Major cult centers at *Philae* and *Dendera*, where her healing and protective powers were especially invoked

## connections

- [[osiris]] — her husband and brother, whom she restored to life through her magical power
- [[horus]] — her son, whom she fiercely protected and raised to avenge his father
- [[nephthys]] — her sister, who assisted her in Osiris's resurrection and embalming
- [[set]] — her brother, whom she opposed and whose crimes she reversed through magic
- [[anubis]] — the god of embalming, associated with her efforts to restore Osiris
- [[ra]] — she traveled with Ra on his solar barque, aiding in his battles against chaos
