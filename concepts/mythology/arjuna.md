# Arjuna

## summary

**Arjuna** is one of the five Pandava princes and a central hero of the *Mahabharata*, the world's longest epic poem. A warrior of extraordinary skill and virtue, he is best remembered as the recipient of Krishna's philosophical teachings in the *Bhagavad Gita*, a sacred dialogue that forms the ethical and spiritual cornerstone of Hindu philosophy.

## you gotta know

- One of the five Pandava brothers, legitimate heirs to the throne, whose right to rule is contested in the great war of *Kurukshetra*.
- An unparalleled archer and master of martial arts, blessed with divine weapons and the favor of the gods themselves.
- Receives the *Bhagavad Gita*, a transformative philosophical discourse from *Krishna*, who appears as his charioteer on the battlefield.
- Struggles with moral doubt before the war, questioning the righteousness of fighting his own cousins and kinsmen, a crisis Krishna resolves.
- Married to *Draupadi*, whose public humiliation at the gambling table becomes a pivotal cause of the great conflict.
- Journeys through exile and hardship alongside his brothers, embodying resilience and devotion to dharma despite profound suffering.
- Carries *Gandiva*, a divine bow gifted by the fire god *Agni*, making him the most feared archer of his age.
- His dialogue with Krishna establishes the path of *karma yoga* (disciplined action without attachment to results), central to Hindu ethics.

## connections

- [[krishna]] — his divine guide and charioteer, who teaches him wisdom and righteousness through the *Bhagavad Gita*.
- [[bhagavad-gita]] — the sacred teaching Krishna delivers to Arjuna on the eve of the great war, addressing his existential crisis.
- [[mahabharata]] — the epic that chronicles Arjuna's life, struggles, moral dilemmas, and ultimate triumph.
