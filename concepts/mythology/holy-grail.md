# Holy Grail

## summary

The Holy Grail is a sacred vessel in Arthurian legend that served as the focus of the ultimate spiritual quest for King Arthur's knights. Associated with both Christian relics (most commonly the cup used by Jesus at the Last Supper) and Celtic mythology (cauldrons of abundance), the Grail possessed miraculous powers of healing, nourishment, and divine grace. Achieving the Grail required absolute spiritual purity, rendering it unattainable to all but a select few.

## you gotta know

- In Christian tradition, the Grail is identified as the cup used by *Jesus* at the Last Supper and later used by Joseph of Arimathea to collect his blood during the Crucifixion.
- The wizard Merlin prophesied that the quest for the Grail would only be achieved by the knight who successfully sat in the *Siege Perilous* at the Round Table without being destroyed.
- *Galahad*, the exceptionally pure son of Lancelot, is the primary knight who fully achieves the Grail, accompanied by *Percival* and *Bors*.
- Upon achieving the Grail at the spiritual city of Sarras, Galahad requests to die, and the Grail is taken up into heaven, never to be seen on Earth again.
- In earlier versions of the myth, such as Chrétien de Troyes' *Perceval, the Story of the Grail*, the hero fails to ask the crucial question that would heal the wounded *Fisher King* and restore his wasted lands.
- Lancelot seeks the Grail but is denied the full vision due to his adulterous affair with Queen Guinevere, though he is allowed a brief, partial glimpse before being struck unconscious.
