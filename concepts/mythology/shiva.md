# Shiva

## summary

**Shiva** is the destroyer and transformer of the Hindu trinity, embodying the cycles of death, renewal, and cosmic destruction necessary for creation to begin anew. Ascetic and meditative, he represents the ultimate yogi and the force that dissolves the universe at the end of each cosmic age.

## you gotta know

- The third member of the *Trimurti*, responsible for *dissolution* and transformation of the cosmos.
- Depicted with a third eye on his forehead, ash covering his body, and a serpent coiled around his neck (*Vasuki*).
- Holds a *trident* (*trishul*) and a *damaru* (drum), symbolizing cosmic creation and destruction.
- *Parvati* is his consort, and together they are parents to *Ganesha* and *Kartikeya* (also called Murugan).
- Considered the supreme yogi, dwelling in meditation on Mount Kailash, the cosmic axis of the universe.
- His *tandava* (cosmic dance) represents the cycles of creation and destruction that govern all existence.
- Worshipped in Shaivism, one of the major branches of Hinduism centered on devotion to Shiva.
- The *lingam* (stone pillar) is his primary symbol and the focal point of Shiva worship in temples.

## connections

- [[parvati]] — his consort, the goddess of love, fertility, and power (*Shakti*).
- [[ganesha]] — their son, the elephant-headed remover of obstacles, beloved across Hindu traditions.
- [[brahma]] — fellow member of the Trimurti, responsible for creation.
- [[vishnu]] — the second member of the Trimurti, responsible for preservation.
- [[trimurti]] — the divine trinity in which Shiva holds the role of destroyer and transformer.
- [[vedas]] — ancient texts that reference Shiva among the principal deities of Hindu cosmology.
