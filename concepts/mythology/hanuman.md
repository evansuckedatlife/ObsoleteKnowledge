## summary

**Hanuman** is the monkey god and one of the most beloved figures in Hindu mythology, revered for his unwavering devotion, courage, and loyalty. A central character in the *Ramayana*, he embodies pure dedication to duty and represents the ideal of selfless service and spiritual surrender to a higher purpose.

## you gotta know

- The divine monkey god and devoted follower of *Rama*, becoming the embodiment of loyalty and bhakti (devotional love) in Hindu tradition.
- Possesses extraordinary strength, agility, and magical powers, capable of transforming his size and leaping across vast distances.
- Central hero of the *Ramayana*, performing crucial feats including the search for *Sita* and the destruction of Ravana's kingdom.
- Depicted with a monkey's body and a noble human face, carrying a mace and wearing sacred marks, symbolizing his divine nature.
- His devotion to Rama transcends the story itself, making him a symbol of unwavering faith and surrender to the divine.
- Renounces all claims to personal power or reward, seeking only to serve Rama's cause and will without expectation of recognition.
- Worshipped as the ideal of celibacy, strength, courage, and discipline, with temples and festivals dedicated to his honor.
- Represents the principle that spiritual greatness lies not in divinity itself, but in perfect devotion and obedience to higher truth.

## connections

- [[rama]] — his divine master and the object of his eternal devotion and service in the *Ramayana*.
- [[sita]] — whose rescue from Ravana's captivity becomes Hanuman's defining heroic quest and greatest service.
- [[ramayana]] — the epic in which Hanuman's courage, wisdom, and loyalty transform the course of events.
