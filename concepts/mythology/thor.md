# Thor

## summary

*Thor* is the god of thunder, strength, and protection of the common people. Wielding the mighty hammer *Mjolnir*, he is the second most powerful Aesir and the defender of *Midgard* against the forces of chaos—his greatest enemy is the World Serpent *Jormungandr*.

## you gotta know

- Son of *Odin*, born of the giantess *Jord* (Earth), connecting him to both divine and earthly realms.
- Possesses *Mjolnir*, the magic hammer that returns after being thrown and cannot be lifted by those unworthy.
- Wears the belt *Megingjord*, which doubles his already immense strength, and iron gloves *Jarngreidr* for wielding Mjolnir.
- Travels in a chariot pulled by goats, *Tanngrisnir* and *Tanngnjostr*, whose bones he can resurrect each evening if cooked properly.
- His greatest rival is *Jormungandr*, the serpent so vast it encircles Midgard; at Ragnarok they will kill each other in final combat.
- Represents protection, order, and strength—the defender of gods and humans alike against the chaos of giants and monsters.
- Less interested in wisdom or magic than his father; his authority comes from power, straightforwardness, and reliability.

## connections

- [[odin]] — his father, king of the Aesir.
- [[loki]] — the trickster who sometimes helps, often harms; they are both rivals and reluctant companions.
- [[balder]] — his brother, whose death affects all the gods.
- [[heimdall]] — the watchman who will stand against him at Ragnarok.
