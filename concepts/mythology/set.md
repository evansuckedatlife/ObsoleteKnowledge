## summary

The god of chaos, desert, and violence, Set murdered his brother Osiris in a power struggle but was ultimately defeated by Osiris's son Horus. Despite his chaotic nature, Set was not purely evil in Egyptian belief but rather represented the necessary wild forces that needed containment within the ordered cosmos.

## you gotta know

- *Red-haired* and depicted with a strange composite animal head (possibly an aardvark or donkey), symbolizing his otherness and chaos
- Murdered his brother *Osiris* by drowning him and tearing him to pieces, sparking the central conflict of Egyptian mythology
- Defeated in combat by *Horus*, who claimed his father's throne and the kingdom of Egypt
- God of the desert, storms, and foreign lands—the untamed forces outside the *Nile* Valley's ordered civilization
- Also invoked as a protector against chaos and disease, wielding destructive power for beneficial purposes when needed
- Associated with the *Apophis* serpent's battles against Ra, sometimes depicted fighting the chaos serpent himself
- Despite his villainy in the Osiris myth, maintained a place in the divine pantheon and was worshipped, especially in *Avaris* in the Delta
- Embodied the principle that chaos and order must coexist; Egyptian cosmos required both

## connections

- [[osiris]] — his brother, whom he murdered for power, setting the great mythological conflict in motion
- [[horus]] — his nephew, who defeated him in combat to avenge Osiris and claim the throne
- [[isis]] — his sister-in-law, whose magic undid his crimes and enabled Osiris's resurrection
- [[nephthys]] — his wife, who nonetheless assisted in Osiris's resurrection alongside Isis
- [[ra]] — Set battles the chaos serpent *Apophis* to protect Ra's nightly journey through the underworld
- [[thoth]] — sometimes depicted as a neutral judge in the conflict between Set and Horus
