## summary

The goddess of the boundaries, mourning, and hidden things, Nephthys was the sister of Osiris and Isis and wife of Set. Though tied to the chaotic Set, she remained devoted to justice and assisted Isis in the resurrection of Osiris, representing the principle that even those connected to chaos can choose compassion and order.

## you gotta know

- Depicted as a *woman wearing a house symbol on her head* (or sometimes a vulture crown), representing boundaries and protection
- Sister of *Osiris*, *Isis*, and *Set*; though married to Set, she opposed his crimes and sided with Isis
- Assisted *Isis* in gathering the pieces of *Osiris* after his dismemberment and preservation through mummification
- Goddess of mourning and lamentation; her cries of grief at Osiris's death became the model for funerary rituals
- Associated with the boundaries between life and death, light and darkness, fertility and barrenness
- Protector of the dead; her wings were said to shelter and protect souls in the afterlife
- Represented fidelity and loyalty despite difficult circumstances; her choice to help Osiris despite her marriage to Set showed moral strength
- Often depicted in funerary texts as one of the protective guardians of the dead alongside Isis and other protective deities

## connections

- [[isis]] — her sister, with whom she mourned Osiris and worked to restore him to life
- [[osiris]] — her brother, whose resurrection she enabled through her devotion and magical assistance
- [[set]] — her husband, though she actively opposed his crimes and the consequences of his actions
- [[anubis]] — both deities associated with mummification, the dead, and funerary protection
- [[book-of-the-dead]] — appears throughout as a protective figure guiding and shielding the dead
