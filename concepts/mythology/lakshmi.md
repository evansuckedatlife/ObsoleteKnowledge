## summary

**Lakshmi** is the goddess of wealth, fortune, abundance, and prosperity in Hindu tradition. The eternal consort of *Vishnu*, she embodies grace, beauty, and the blessings that flow from living according to dharma. Worshipped especially during *Diwali*, the festival of lights, she represents both material and spiritual wealth.

## you gotta know

- The consort and *shakti* (divine power) of *Vishnu*, appearing at his side in all his incarnations and cosmic forms.
- Goddess of *wealth*, *fortune*, *abundance*, and *prosperity*, representing both material and spiritual riches.
- Depicted adorned in gold and jewels, often seated or standing on a lotus flower, symbolizing purity rising from worldly existence.
- Holds lotus flowers and pours coins from her hands, representing the continuous flow of blessings and generosity.
- Worshipped during *Diwali*, the festival of lights, when homes are cleaned and decorated to invite her blessings.
- Associated with *lakshya* (goal) and the achievement of auspicious aims through devotion and righteous living.
- Believed to withdraw from those who act unethically or with greed, rewarding virtue and punishing vice.
- Often depicted with four arms, each holding a lotus, a pot of gold, and a gesture of blessing and protection.

## connections

- [[vishnu]] — her eternal consort and divine counterpart, with whom she appears in all cosmic cycles.
- [[brahma]] — alongside whom she is recognized as part of the broader divine feminine principle in creation.
- [[shiva]] — her divine equal in the cosmic trinity, respected as the supreme power of existence.
