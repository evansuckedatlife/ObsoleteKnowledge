# Patroclus

## summary

Companion and closest friend to *Achilles*, Patroclus fought alongside the greatest Greek warrior at Troy. When *Hector* killed him in battle while wearing Achilles' armor, his death shattered the hero's grief and drove Achilles to unleash his full rage, transforming the course of the entire war.

## you gotta know

- Childhood companion and intimate friend of *Achilles*, raised alongside him
- Served as mediator between Achilles and *Agamemnon* during their bitter feud
- Donned Achilles' armor and armor-bearer's role in *The Iliad* to inspire the flagging Greek forces
- Slain by *Hector* in single combat when he ventured too far from the Greeks' safety
- His death became the turning point: Achilles' grief-fueled vengeance drove him back to war
- The *Iliad* portrays their bond as the deepest emotional connection for Achilles
- Later Greek writers increasingly emphasized the romantic nature of their relationship
