## summary

**Rama** is a legendary prince and an avatar of Vishnu, revered as the exemplar of righteousness (*dharma*) and virtue in Hindu tradition. The protagonist of the *Ramayana*, one of Hinduism's two great epics, he represents the ideal king and the perfect devotion to duty, even in the face of personal sacrifice.

## you gotta know

- An *avatar* of *Vishnu*, incarnated to defeat the demon king *Ravana* and restore cosmic order to the world.
- Born as a prince of Ayodhya, he embodies the virtues of a perfect son, husband, warrior, and king throughout his life's journey.
- The central hero of the *Ramayana*, the epic that details his exile, his wife *Sita's* abduction, and their triumphant return.
- Accompanied by his devoted brother *Lakshman* and the monkey god *Hanuman*, forming one of literature's most celebrated relationships.
- Depicted with blue or golden skin, holding a bow and arrows, symbolizing his martial prowess and his readiness to defend dharma.
- His reign, the *Rama Rajya*, becomes the Hindu ideal of perfect governance: just, peaceful, and prosperous.
- Though an avatar of the divine, he lives and struggles as a mortal, accepting exile to honor his father's oath and maintain dharma.
- Worshipped during *Diwali*, the festival of lights, which celebrates his victory over evil and return to his kingdom.

## connections

- [[vishnu]] — his divine parent form, of which Rama is a celebrated avatar incarnated to defeat evil.
- [[sita]] — his beloved wife, whose virtue and devotion are central to the *Ramayana*.
- [[hanuman]] — the devoted monkey god who aids Rama's quest and becomes his eternal follower.
- [[ravana]] — the demon king and antagonist whose defeat by Rama restores dharma to the world.
- [[ramayana]] — the epic narrative that chronicles Rama's life, struggles, and triumphs.
