# Ariadne

## summary

Princess of Crete who fell in love with the hero *Theseus* and saved him from the *Minotaur* by giving him a thread to navigate the labyrinth. Abandoned on an island, she became the bride of *Dionysus* and ascended to divine status as a goddess of wine and festivity.

## you gotta know

- Daughter of *Minos*, king of Crete, and sister to the monstrous *Minotaur* trapped in the infamous *labyrinth*.
- Fell in love with *Theseus* when he arrived in Crete as a tribute victim destined to be devoured by the *Minotaur*.
- Gave *Theseus* a ball of thread so he could navigate the labyrinth's passages and find his way back after slaying the *Minotaur*.
- Fled Crete with *Theseus*, only to be abandoned by him on the island of *Naxos* while she slept, a heartbreaking betrayal.
- Discovered by *Dionysus* on *Naxos*, who fell in love with her and made her his divine bride, elevating her to immortality.
- Became a goddess associated with wine, festivity, and eternal union with *Dionysus*, crowned with a constellation in her honor.
- Represents transformation from mortal love and loss to divine transcendence and eternal happiness.

## connections

- [[theseus]] — the hero she loved and saved, but who abandoned her cruelly on *Naxos*.
- [[minotaur]] — her monstrous half-brother, whom *Theseus* killed with her help.
- [[hades]] — sometimes invoked in her underworld journeys with *Theseus* through the labyrinth.
- [[dionysus]] — the god who rescued her and made her his immortal wife.
