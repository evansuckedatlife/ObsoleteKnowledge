# Odin

## summary

*Odin* is the king of the Aesir, the chief Norse god of wisdom, war, death, and magic. He sacrifices his eye and hangs himself on Yggdrasil to gain forbidden knowledge, embodying the price of wisdom and the relentless pursuit of understanding even at great personal cost.

## you gotta know

- Traded his right eye to drink from *Mimir's Well*, gaining wisdom and foresight of the future.
- Hung himself on *Yggdrasil* for nine nights, pierced by his own spear *Gungnir*, to learn the runes—the magic that underpins reality.
- Commands the *Valkyries*, who choose the honored dead to bring to *Valhalla*; he gathers these warriors for the final battle at *Ragnarok*.
- Possesses two ravens, *Huginn* and *Muninn* (Thought and Memory), that fly across the nine worlds each day bringing him knowledge.
- His spear *Gungnir* never misses its target; his steed *Sleipnir* is an eight-legged horse faster than the wind.
- Father of *Thor*, *Balder*, and *Vidar*; his quest for knowledge sometimes conflicts with his role as father and god-king.
- Often depicted as a wanderer in disguise, testing mortals and gods alike to judge their worthiness.

## connections

- [[ymir]] — he and his brothers killed and dismembered Ymir to create the world.
- [[frigg]] — his wife, goddess of marriage and motherhood.
- [[thor]] — his son, god of thunder and strength.
- [[loki]] — trickster whose schemes both aid and threaten the gods.
- [[balder]] — his beloved son whose death leads toward Ragnarok.
- [[norns]] — the fate-weavers he consults but cannot fully control.
