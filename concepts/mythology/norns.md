## summary

The *Norns* are three ancient beings—*Urd*, *Verdandi*, and *Skuld*—who weave and guard the fates of all things. Dwelling at the base of *Yggdrasil*, they are older and more powerful than the gods themselves, and even *Odin* cannot escape the destinies they spin.

## you gotta know

- Represent the three aspects of time: *Urd* (the past/what has become), *Verdandi* (the present/what is becoming), and *Skuld* (the future/what shall become).
- Dwell at the Well of Urd, the sacred spring at the base of Yggdrasil where they daily water and tend the World Tree.
- Older and more fundamental than the gods; they are beings of creation rather than created beings themselves.
- Spin and weave the threads of fate for gods, humans, giants, and all creatures—their decisions are absolute and cannot be changed even by divine power.
- *Odin* consults them but cannot control them; even he must accept the fates they have woven, including his own death at Ragnarok.
- Carve runes and symbols into the bark of Yggdrasil, encoding the patterns of destiny into the very structure of existence.
- Represent the paradox that all things—even gods—are subject to forces greater than themselves.

## connections

- [[odin]] — the All-Father who seeks their counsel but cannot command them.
- [[balder]] — whose death they foresee and cannot prevent, even to save the brightest of the gods.
- [[heimdall]] — born of nine mothers and connected to the mystical depths they inhabit.
- [[ymir]] — they exist in the world created from Ymir's body.
