# Krishna

## summary

**Krishna** is a central figure in Hindu mythology, an avatar of Vishnu who played a pivotal role in the *Mahabharata*. Revered as both a divine deity and a yogi-prince, he is best known as the charioteer and philosophical guide who delivered the *Bhagavad Gita* to the warrior prince Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.

## you gotta know

- An *avatar* (incarnation) of *Vishnu*, appearing on Earth to defeat evil and restore dharma during the age of Dwapara Yuga.
- Born into royalty but raised among cowherd communities, blending divine and earthly experiences in his legendary childhood.
- Central protagonist of the *Mahabharata*, particularly the *Bhagavad Gita* section, where he teaches Arjuna the philosophy of karma yoga and duty.
- Depicted with blue skin, holding a flute (*bansuri*) and wearing a peacock feather in his hair, symbolizing divine beauty and enchantment.
- Known for his romantic escapades with the *gopis* (cowherd girls) and his eternal love for *Radha*, which symbolize the soul's devotion to the divine.
- Employs both wisdom and cunning in the *Mahabharata*, orchestrating events to eliminate evil and guide dharma's victory.
- Worshipped in multiple forms: as the cowherd lover, the philosophical teacher, the divine diplomat, and the ultimate sage.
- His teachings on non-attachment (*nishkama karma*), discipline, and righteous action remain foundational to Hindu ethics.

## connections

- [[vishnu]] — his divine parent form, of which Krishna is a celebrated avatar.
- [[arjuna]] — the Pandava prince to whom Krishna delivers the *Bhagavad Gita*, establishing their eternal bond.
- [[bhagavad-gita]] — the sacred dialogue in which Krishna teaches Arjuna philosophy and the path to liberation.
- [[mahabharata]] — the epic that chronicles Krishna's life and role in the great war of dharma.
- [[radha]] — his beloved consort, symbolizing the devotional love between soul and divine.
