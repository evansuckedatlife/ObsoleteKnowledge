# Galahad

## summary

Sir Galahad stands as the purest and most virtuous knight of the *Round Table*, destined from birth to achieve what no other mortal could: the *Holy Grail*. As the son of Lancelot and Elaine, he represents redemption for his father's sin and the spiritual climax of Arthurian legend.

## you gotta know

- Born of a magical deception orchestrated by enchantresses who desired Lancelot's strength to produce a perfect heir
- The *Siege Perilous*, the seat at the Round Table reserved for the worthiest knight, was meant for him alone
- Undertook the *Grail Quest*, where his absolute purity allowed him to succeed where older, more worldly knights could not
- Achieved the Holy Grail through virtue rather than strength or cleverness, transcending the martial tradition of Camelotry
- Said to be transported to *Sarras* (a mystical land) when he achieved the Grail, his mortal body transformed or taken to heaven
- Represents the spiritual ideal toward which Arthurian chivalry aspired, even as the flawed reality (his father's affair) undermined it
- Often depicted with a white surcoat and brilliant countenance, visibly marked as holy and set apart
