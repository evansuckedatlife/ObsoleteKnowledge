# Andromache

## summary

Wife of *Hector* and mother of *Astyanax*, Andromache stands as the tragic image of the devoted woman watching helplessly as her family is destroyed by war. Her farewell scene with Hector in the *Iliad* remains one of literature's most poignant moments of domestic love contrasted against inevitable doom.

## you gotta know

- Devoted wife to the greatest Trojan warrior *Hector*, deeply in love with her husband
- Mother of infant *Astyanax*, the prince she feared would be killed when the war came
- Watched *Hector* depart for battle in the *Iliad*, knowing his doom was written in fate
- Witnessed *Hector's* death at *Achilles'* hands from Troy's walls
- After Troy's fall, enslaved to *Achilles'* son *Neoptolemus* and forced to bear his children
- Her final role as a slave and concubine exemplified the fate of Trojan women after the city's destruction
- Became the symbol of feminine resilience and maternal devotion amid the horrors of war
