## summary

Once a beautiful woman transformed into a horrifying monster with serpents for hair, *Medusa* possessed the terrifying power to turn anyone who looked upon her into stone. She was slain by the hero *Perseus*, who used her own petrifying gaze against her and claimed her severed head as a legendary weapon.

## you gotta know

- Her hair consisted of living venomous snakes that constantly writhed and hissed, replacing the golden locks she once had
- A single glance into her eyes could instantly petrify any living creature, freezing them in stone for eternity
- *Medusa* was cursed (or transformed) by *Athena* after being violated by *Poseidon* in one of the goddess's own temples
- *Perseus* defeated her by using a polished bronze shield as a mirror, avoiding her deadly gaze while he struck
- Her severed head retained its petrifying power even in death, and *Perseus* mounted it on his shield as protection
- Before her transformation, she was the only mortal among the three *Gorgon* sisters—*Stheno* and *Euryale* were immortal
- She lived in a cavern far to the west, beyond the land of the *Hyperboreans*, in a place of desolation and stone
- Some accounts describe her as a victim of circumstance rather than a true monster, transformed against her will

## connections

- [[perseus]] — the young hero who slew her to complete one of his early quests
- [[athena]] — the goddess who cursed her, transforming her from woman to monster
- [[poseidon]] — the god who violated her, setting off the chain of events leading to her transformation
