# Agamemnon

## summary

The king of *Mycenae* and supreme commander of the Greek forces at *Troy*, Agamemnon led the thousand-ship expedition against the Trojans to reclaim his brother's wife *Helen*. Though powerful and ambitious, his pride and poor judgment brought disaster upon the Greeks, prolonging the war and costing countless lives.

## you gotta know

- Command of the Greek forces at Troy; supreme authority over all allied kingdoms
- Sacrificed his daughter *Iphigenia* to the goddess *Artemis* to secure favorable winds for the voyage to Troy
- Clashed repeatedly with *Achilles* over war prizes and honor, nearly destroying Greek unity
- Claimed *Briseis* as his prize, which triggered Achilles' withdrawal from battle
- Ruled *Mycenae* after inheriting the throne from his father *Atreus*
- Slain by his wife *Clytemnestra* and her lover *Aegisthus* upon returning home from Troy
- The central dramatic focus of the *Iliad*, where his leadership and quarrels shape the entire war
