# Aphrodite

## summary

Aphrodite is the ancient Greek goddess of love, beauty, pleasure, and procreation. In Roman mythology, she is identified with Venus. She was born from the sea foam generated by the severed genitals of Uranus and was wed to the smith god Hephaestus, though she famously carried on a passionate affair with the war god Ares. She was a key instigator of the Trojan War, having promised Helen of Troy to Paris in exchange for winning the Judgment of Paris.

## you gotta know

- According to Hesiod's *Theogony*, she arose from the sea foam (*aphros*) near Cyprus after the Titan Cronus castrated Uranus and threw his genitals into the ocean.
- She instigated the Trojan War by winning the *Judgment of Paris* over Hera and Athena, promising Paris the hand of the world's most beautiful woman, Helen.
- She was forced by Zeus to marry the lame blacksmith god *Hephaestus*, but she frequently betrayed him with her primary lover, *Ares*, the god of war.
- She is the mother of the Trojan hero *Aeneas* (with the mortal Anchises) and she actively protected him during the sack of Troy, being wounded by Diomedes in the process.
- She aided the suitor Hippomenes in winning the hand of *Atalanta* by gifting him three golden apples from the Hesperides to distract the swift huntress in a race.
- She is often accompanied by her son *Eros* (Cupid) and her sacred symbols include the *dove*, *myrtle*, *rose*, and the *girdle* (which possessed the power to make anyone fall in love with its wearer).
