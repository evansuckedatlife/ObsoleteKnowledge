# Odysseus

## summary

**Odysseus** was the legendary king of *Ithaca*, renowned equally for his cunning intellect as for his warrior's prowess. After ten years fighting at *Troy*, he spent another ten years struggling to return home in one of mythology's greatest journeys. The *Odyssey* chronicles his impossible voyage, filled with monsters, gods, and tests of will that define heroism beyond mere combat strength.

## you gotta know

- Devised the *Trojan Horse*, the clever deception that broke the ten-year siege of *Troy* by hiding Greek soldiers inside a wooden vessel.
- Encountered the *Cyclops Polyphemus*, blinded him, then boasted of his deed—angering *Poseidon*, who punished him with endless wanderings.
- Sailed past the *Sirens*, creatures whose songs lured sailors to death, by stopping his crew's ears with wax and binding himself to the mast.
- Lost all his men to various perils—the *Cyclops*, the lotus-eaters, *Circe*'s magic, the sun god's cattle—before finally reaching home alone.
- Remained faithful to his wife *Penelope* throughout his ordeals, though she faced suitors pressuring her to remarry during his decade-long absence.
- Reclaimed his throne and household by proving his identity and slaying the suitors who had invaded his home.
