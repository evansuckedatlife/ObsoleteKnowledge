# Clytemnestra

## summary

Queen of Argos and wife of *Agamemnon*, she is remembered as one of Greek mythology's most complex figures—a woman driven to murder by her husband's hubris and her own passion. She ruled in his absence and killed him upon his return from the Trojan War.

## you gotta know

- Queen of *Argos* and sister to *Helen*, sharing royal blood and watching her sister become the cause of a ten-year war.
- First married to *Tantalus*, a man *Agamemnon* slew before marrying her himself—a marriage begun in violence and grief.
- Ruled *Argos* alone for a decade while *Agamemnon* commanded the Greek forces at Troy, bearing the burden of kingship.
- Took a lover, *Aegisthus*, during her husband's long absence, finding solace and alliance in his arms.
- Murdered *Agamemnon* upon his triumphant return from Troy, either to avenge his willingness to sacrifice their daughter *Iphigenia* or from passion and politics.
- Also killed *Cassandra*, the Trojan princess *Agamemnon* brought home as a war prize, in a fury of jealousy and betrayal.
- Ruled with *Aegisthus* for years until her children—*Orestes* and *Electra*—grew old enough to avenge their father's death.
