# Amon

## summary

The hidden king of the gods and chief deity of the New Kingdom, Amon was originally a local Theban god who rose to supreme prominence. Merged with Ra to form Amon-Ra, he became the most powerful god in Egypt's pantheon, associated with fertility, the wind, and the creative force behind all existence.

## you gotta know

- *Ram-headed* god, sometimes depicted with curled horns, though also shown in human form as a king crowned with feathers
- Originally a *Thebes* (modern Luxor) local deity, elevated to supreme status during the New Kingdom as imperial power consolidated there
- Merged with *Ra* to create *Amon-Ra*, combining the hidden creative force with the visible sun god's power
- Called "King of the Gods," ruling the divine pantheon from his great temple at *Karnak* in Thebes
- Associated with fertility, sexuality, and creative breath; his name means "the hidden one," referring to invisible creative forces
- The *Nile* flood was attributed to Amon's generosity, making him essential to Egypt's agricultural prosperity
- Pharaohs styled themselves as "son of Amon," further elevating his status as a legitimizing divine parent
- His priests at Karnak became so wealthy and influential they rivaled pharaonic power by the late New Kingdom

## connections

- [[ra]] — merged with Ra to form Amon-Ra, combining hidden creative force with visible solar power
- [[thoth]] — associated with divine wisdom and cosmic order, complementing Amon's creative authority
- [[hathor]] — sometimes depicted as his consort or linked through fertility associations
- [[pharaoh]] — pharaohs claimed direct descent from Amon, making him the source of their divine authority
- [[thebes]] — his primary cult center and the seat of his priesthood's immense power
