## summary

Trojan hero and son of the goddess *Aphrodite*, Aeneas escaped Troy's destruction to become the ancestor of Rome. While less central to the *Iliad* than *Hector*, he carried the war's narrative forward through the *Aeneid*, driven by destiny to found a new civilization in Italy.

## you gotta know

- Son of *Aphrodite* and the mortal *Anchises*, granting him divine protection during the war
- Fought bravely for Troy throughout the ten-year war alongside *Hector* and other Trojan princes
- Escaped the burning city of Troy carrying his elderly father on his shoulders and leading his son *Ascanius*
- The *Aeneid* follows his seven-year journey from Troy to Italy, struggling against divine and human obstacles
- Destined by *Zeus* and *Fate* to establish a settlement that would eventually become Rome
- His relationship with *Dido* of Carthage and his subsequent abandonment of her formed the *Aeneid's* central emotional tragedy
- Represents the theme of duty and fate superseding personal desire, unlike *Paris'* opposite choice
