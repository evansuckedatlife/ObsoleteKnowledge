## summary

*Frigg* is the queen of the Aesir and goddess of marriage, motherhood, home, and domestic life. Though she wields considerable power and foresight, her defining story centers on her desperate attempt to save her son *Balder* from a prophesied death.

## you gotta know

- Wife of *Odin* and one of the most powerful Aesir, with knowledge of the fates though she speaks them only rarely.
- Binds all things with a solemn oath to spare *Balder* from harm, creating a vulnerable loophole when *Loki* exploits the mistletoe plant.
- Her maids and handmaidens carry out important tasks across the nine worlds, extending her influence beyond Asgard.
- Presides over *Fensalir*, her sacred hall, where she receives honored dead alongside *Odin's* warriors.
- Represents the civilizing forces of home, family, and loyalty—counterbalance to *Odin's* relentless quest for wisdom and war.
- Her knowledge of future events parallels *Odin's*, but she embodies acceptance rather than intervention.

## connections
