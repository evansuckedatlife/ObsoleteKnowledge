# Arachne

## summary

A mortal weaver whose extraordinary skill rivaled that of *Athena*, the goddess of crafts. When she challenged the goddess to a contest and dared to weave scenes that insulted the gods, she was transformed into a spider, forever weaving her threads.

## you gotta know

- Born a simple mortal, she became the most talented weaver in her land, known throughout the region for her unprecedented skill.
- Boasted that she could weave better than *Athena* herself, refusing to acknowledge the goddess as her superior or teacher.
- Accepted *Athena's* challenge to a weaving contest, the two creating elaborate tapestries to display their artistry and power.
- Wove scenes depicting the gods' shameful deeds and transformations, deliberately insulting and mocking the divine pantheon.
- *Athena's* tapestry showed scenes of divine triumph and the punishment of mortals who challenged the gods—a stark warning.
- Confronted with her own audacity and *Athena's* superior work, Arachne attempted to hang herself in despair.
- Transformed by *Athena* into a spider, condemned to weave forever as a creature rather than as a celebrated artisan.
