# Parvati

## summary

**Parvati** is the goddess of love, fertility, beauty, and cosmic power in Hindu tradition. The consort of *Shiva* and mother of *Ganesha*, she embodies the divine feminine principle (*Shakti*) and represents the nurturing, creative force that balances Shiva's ascetic, destructive energy within the cosmic order.

## you gotta know

- The consort of *Shiva* and embodiment of his *shakti* (divine power and feminine principle), together representing the complete cosmic force.
- Mother of *Ganesha*, the elephant-headed remover of obstacles, and *Kartikeya* (also called Murugan), the god of war.
- Goddess of *love*, *fertility*, *beauty*, and *compassion*, worshipped as the divine mother across Hindu traditions.
- Depicted with a golden or fair complexion, wearing a red sari and adorned with jewels, often seated beside Shiva in meditative bliss.
- Also called *Devi* (the Goddess), signifying her status as the supreme feminine principle in Hindu cosmology.
- Her devotion to Shiva, including arduous tapas (spiritual practices), exemplifies the power of love and dedication.
- Associated with the mountain *Kailash*, the cosmic axis where she dwells with Shiva in eternal meditation.
- Worshipped as the source of all creative and regenerative energy, without which the universe cannot manifest or persist.

## connections

- [[shiva]] — her divine consort, the destroyer and transformer whose power she embodies and complements.
- [[ganesha]] — her beloved son, the remover of obstacles, born of her divine energy and will.
