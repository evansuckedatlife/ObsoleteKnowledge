## summary

The *jackal-headed* god of mummification, embalming, and the dead, Anubis was the divine mortician who first mummified Osiris. He served as guardian of cemeteries and guide for souls in the underworld, making him one of the most essential deities in Egyptian beliefs about death and the afterlife.

## you gotta know

- *Jackal-headed* god depicted in black (the color of mummified flesh and fertile Nile mud), representing death and regeneration
- First practiced mummification on *Osiris*, establishing the funerary arts that allowed eternal life in the afterlife
- God of cemeteries, tombs, and all necropoles; his watchful jackal form protected the dead from grave robbers and desecration
- Associated with the embalming process; priests performing mummification wore Anubis masks and were considered his earthly representatives
- Originally the most important god of the dead before *Osiris* rose to prominence and took much of his role
- Guide and protector of souls in the *Duat* (underworld), helping the dead navigate dangers and trials
- The *Wedjat Eye* and protective amulets were often associated with Anubis as he guarded the dead
- Black jackals naturally inhabited Egyptian cemeteries, leading to his identification with these scavengers of the dead

## connections

- [[osiris]] — Anubis mummified Osiris first, establishing the funerary process that ensured his resurrection
- [[isis]] — assisted her in the preservation and restoration of Osiris, enabling his magical resurrection
- [[thoth]] — both deities were guardians of sacred knowledge and guides in the afterlife
- [[horus]] — Anubis was sometimes depicted as Horus's brother or associate in protecting the dead
- [[book-of-the-dead]] — Anubis appears throughout these funerary texts as a guide and protector of souls
- [[pharaoh]] — mummified pharaohs were placed under Anubis's protection for eternity
