# Athena

## summary

Athena is the Greek goddess of wisdom, handicraft, and warfare. Unlike Ares, who represents the physical violence of war, Athena represents strategy, reason, and just combat. She was born fully armed from the forehead of her father Zeus, and she famously served as the patron and protector of the city of Athens and many of mythology's greatest heroes.

## you gotta know

- She was born fully formed and clothed in armor from the forehead of *Zeus* after he swallowed her pregnant mother, Metis.
- She won the patronage of Athens in a contest with *Poseidon* by offering the citizens the first cultivated *olive tree*, while Poseidon offered a salt spring.
- She transformed the mortal weaver *Arachne* into a spider after Arachne boasted of her superior skills and challenged the goddess to a contest.
- She cursed *Medusa*, turning her hair into snakes and her gaze to stone, after Medusa was violated by Poseidon in the goddess's temple.
- She is represented by the *owl* (symbolizing wisdom) and carries the *aegis*, a protective shield or breastplate adorned with Medusa's head.
- She acted as a key advisor and champion for several Greek heroes, notably *Odysseus* on his journey home and *Perseus* in his quest to slay Medusa.

## connections

- [[zeus]] — her father, from whose head she was born.
- [[arachne]] — the weaver she challenged and transformed into a spider.
- [[medusa]] — the monster she cursed and whose head eventually adorned her shield.
- [[perseus]] — the hero she aided by providing a polished shield to avoid Medusa's gaze.
- [[diomedes]] — the hero she favored in the Trojan War, guiding his spear and lifting the veil from his eyes.
- [[odysseus]] — her favorite hero, whom she consistently championed during the Trojan War and on his long return.
