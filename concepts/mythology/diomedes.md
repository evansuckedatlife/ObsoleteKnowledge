## summary

**Diomedes** was a formidable Greek warrior at the *Trojan War*, second only to *Achilles* in martial prowess and remarkable for his cool judgment and tactical brilliance. Son of *Ares*, the god of war, he embodied both strength and wisdom, earning the respect of enemies and allies alike. His deeds at *Troy* rank among the war's most celebrated moments.

## you gotta know

- Received aid from *Athena* during the war, who granted him the ability to distinguish gods from mortals and fight with supernatural courage.
- Wounded *Aphrodite* when she tried to protect her son *Aeneas*, and later wounded *Ares* himself—the only mortal to successfully strike the war god in combat.
- Fought *Hector* and other Trojan champions repeatedly, proving himself the Greeks' most reliable warrior after *Achilles*.
- Partnered with *Odysseus* on night raids and reconnaissance missions, including the capture of the *Palladium*, a sacred Trojan relic.
- Ruled *Argos*, a powerful Greek city-state, before departing for Troy; he was one of the few Greek leaders who returned home successfully.
- Known for his council and wisdom as much as his spear; he spoke prudently and earned the trust of commanders like *Agamemnon*.

## connections
