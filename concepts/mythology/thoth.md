# Thoth

## summary

**Thoth** was the ibis-headed god of wisdom, writing, and cosmic order in Egyptian mythology, serving as the divine scribe and record-keeper of the gods themselves. He invented writing and all forms of communication, and Egyptians credited him with the sacred knowledge that sustained both the universe and the possibility of an afterlife. As keeper of the *Book of the Dead* and judge at the weighing of the heart in the underworld, Thoth represented the triumph of knowledge and truth over chaos and ignorance. His association with the moon, magic, mathematics, and medicine made him one of Egypt's most complex and revered deities. Thoth embodied the idea that the word—spoken and written—possesses creative and binding power, making him essential not only to the gods' maintenance of the cosmos but to human civilization itself.

## you gotta know

### Form and Identity
- Ibis-headed deity (ibis sacred to him for its curved beak resembling the crescent moon), though also depicted as a baboon in some contexts.
- Often shown wearing the lunar crescent and disk, associating him with the moon's cycles and nocturnal wisdom.
- Represented the principle of divine intelligence and the power of spoken and written language.

### Divine Roles and Attributes
- Inventor of writing (*hieroglyphics*) and all forms of communication; Egyptians called their script "the language of Thoth" (*medu netjer*).
- Divine scribe who recorded the deeds of gods and humans, maintaining cosmic record and preserving divine knowledge.
- God of wisdom, magic (*heka*), mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and all learned arts—the patron of scribes, scholars, and priests.

### Judgment and the Afterlife
- Divine judge who recorded the judgment of souls in the afterlife, weighing their hearts against *Ma'at's* feather of truth at the scales.
- Keeper of the *Book of the Dead*, the funerary texts that guided souls through the perils of the *Duat* (underworld) toward eternal life.
- His true name and the power of his words were believed to grant access to divine knowledge and the secrets of resurrection.

### Cosmic Functions
- Associated with the moon and its cycles, serving as the regulator of time, seasons, and cosmic rhythms.
- His baboon form was believed to greet the rising sun each dawn, celebrating *Ra's* nightly victory over the chaos serpent *Apophis*.
- Served as a judge and mediator between conflicting gods, including the prolonged dispute between *Horus* and *Set* over the Egyptian throne.

## context

Thoth occupies a unique position in Egyptian theology as the embodiment of intellectual power and the transformative force of language. Unlike gods who ruled through force (Amun through creation, Ra through judgment, Horus through rightful succession), Thoth's authority derives from knowledge and the word. This made him especially important in Egyptian civilization, where writing and scribal practice were central to maintaining social order. In later Hellenistic times, when Greeks encountered Egyptian culture, Thoth was syncretized with *Hermes*, and the fusion became *Hermes Trismegistus* ("Thrice-Great Hermes"), the legendary founder of Hermeticism and Western occult tradition. Medieval and Renaissance alchemists attributed profound mystical texts to Hermes Trismegistus, treating Thoth's association with secret knowledge as authoritative. Psychologically, Thoth represents the rational mind, memory, and the power of symbolic thought—functions that modern neuroscience continues to explore. His role as the scribe of the gods influenced countless mythologies' depictions of wisdom figures who record and judge human deeds.
