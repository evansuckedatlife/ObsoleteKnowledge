# Thoth

## summary

The *ibis-headed* god of wisdom, writing, and cosmic order, Thoth served as the divine scribe and record-keeper of the gods. He invented writing itself and maintained the sacred knowledge that sustained the universe, making him essential to the preservation of Egyptian civilization and the afterlife.

## you gotta know

- *Ibis-headed* deity, often shown wearing the lunar crescent and disk or depicted as a baboon, both animals sacred to him
- Inventor of writing and all forms of communication; Egyptians called their script "the language of Thoth"
- Divine scribe who recorded the judgment of souls in the afterlife, weighing their hearts against *Ma'at's* feather of truth
- Associated with the *moon* and its cycles, serving as a regulator of time and cosmic rhythms
- God of wisdom, magic, and esoteric knowledge; his words held absolute creative and binding power
- Keeper of the *Book of the Dead*, the funerary texts that guided souls through the underworld's perils
- Served as a judge and mediator between conflicting gods, including the dispute between *Horus* and *Set*
- His baboon form was believed to greet the rising sun each dawn, celebrating Ra's nightly victory over chaos
