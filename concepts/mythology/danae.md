## summary

Princess of Argos who became the mother of the hero *Perseus* after *Zeus* visited her in her tower as a shower of gold. Locked away by her father to prevent the fulfillment of a prophecy, she endured captivity and divine seduction, her fate forever tied to the birth of mythology's greatest heroes.

## you gotta know

- Daughter of *Acrisius*, king of Argos, locked in a bronze tower by her father to prevent a prophecy that her son would kill him.
- Desired by *Zeus*, who transformed himself into a shower of gold to penetrate her tower and seduce her, circumventing her imprisonment.
- Gave birth to *Perseus*, the hero destined to slay *Medusa*, becoming the mother of one of Greece's greatest legends.
- Either willingly or unwittingly conceived of divine seduction while imprisoned, a woman caught between mortal constraints and divine will.
- Set adrift in a wooden chest on the sea with her newborn *Perseus* by her terrified father, cast out to face an uncertain fate.
- Rescued by a fisherman and raised in obscurity while her son *Perseus* grew and eventually proved his heroic worth.
- Connected to the golden god and the generational burden of divine prophecy that shaped her son's extraordinary destiny.

## connections

- [[zeus]] — the god who seduced her in his shower of gold form, fathering *Perseus*.
- [[perseus]] — her son, the legendary hero who slew *Medusa* and avenged the suffering his grandfather inflicted on her.
- [[medusa]] — the monster *Perseus* killed, whose head became his most powerful weapon.
