## summary

**Agni** is the god of fire and one of the most ancient and revered deities in Hindu and Vedic tradition. Both a celestial and earthly force, he serves as the divine intermediary between humans and the gods, carrying sacrificial offerings through flame and transforming matter through his purifying power.

## you gotta know

- God of *fire* in all its manifestations: the sacrificial flame, the digestive fire within the body, lightning, and the warmth of the sun.
- One of the most prominent deities in the *Vedas*, worshipped and invoked in nearly every ritual and sacred ceremony since ancient times.
- Serves as the *purifier* and *intermediary*, accepting ritual offerings from humans and carrying them to the gods via smoke and flame.
- Depicted with reddish or golden skin, riding a ram as his *vahana* (mount), often holding flames and sacred implements.
- Has four arms and two faces in some depictions, symbolizing his dual nature as both celestial and earthly fire.
- Associated with *digestion*, *metabolism*, and the internal fire that sustains life (*jataragni*), making him essential to health and vitality.
- Worshipped in *Homa* (fire ritual) ceremonies, where offerings are made directly into sacred flames as an act of devotion and gratitude.
- In cosmology, Agni represents the transformative power that breaks down the old to create the new, essential to both destruction and creation.

## connections

- [[vedas]] — ancient sacred texts in which Agni is celebrated as one of the principal and most frequently invoked deities.
- [[indra]] — often depicted alongside Agni in Vedic hymns as a companion divine force in maintaining cosmic order.
