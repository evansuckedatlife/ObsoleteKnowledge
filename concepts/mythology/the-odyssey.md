# The Odyssey

## summary

The Odyssey is an ancient Greek epic poem in dactylic hexameter, traditionally attributed to the blind poet Homer. It chronicles the ten-year journey of the Greek hero Odysseus, king of Ithaca, as he struggles to return home after the fall of Troy. Along with the *Iliad*, it is one of the foundational texts of Western literature, establishing the archetype of the heroic quest.

## you gotta know

- The epic focuses on the *nostos* (homecoming) of *Odysseus*, whose journey is prolonged for ten years due to the wrath of Poseidon after Odysseus blinds his son, the Cyclops Polyphemus.
- While Odysseus is away, his loyal wife *Penelope* must fend off a court of aggressive suitors in Ithaca, keeping them at bay by claiming she will choose one only after she finishes weaving a shroud for Laertes, which she secretly unravels every night.
- The narrative begins *in media res* with Odysseus held captive on the island of Ogygia by the nymph *Calypso*, who desires to make him her immortal husband.
- Odysseus's travels feature famous encounters with fantastical perils, including the lotus-eaters, the giant Cyclops *Polyphemus*, the witch *Circe* who turns his men to swine, the *Sirens*, and the sea monsters Scylla and Charybdis.
- Odysseus visits the underworld (*nekyia*) on Circe's advice to consult the blind prophet Tiresias, where he also encounters the shades of his mother Anticleia and his war comrade Achilles.
- Upon returning to Ithaca disguised as a beggar, he reveals himself to his son *Telemachus* and defeats the suitors in an archery contest using his great bow, which only he can string.
