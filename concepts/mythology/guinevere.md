# Guinevere

## summary

Queen of Britain and wife to King Arthur, Guinevere embodies the tragic contradiction between duty and desire. Her forbidden love for *Lancelot*, the greatest of Arthur's knights, becomes the catalyst that unravels the fellowship of the *Round Table* and precipitates Camelot's fall.

## you gotta know

- Beautiful and accomplished, the epitome of courtly grace and medieval queenship at *Camelot*
- Her passionate affair with *Lancelot* is discovered and publicly exposed, causing irreconcilable shame and political crisis
- The revelation fractures the *Round Table*, forcing many of Arthur's greatest knights to choose sides
- Lancelot rescues her from execution, an act that turns loyal knights against him and against Arthur
- Some versions depict her as entering a convent in her final years, choosing religious penance and withdrawal from the world
- Represents the central tragedy of Arthurian legend: that even the noblest ideals cannot survive human passion and flawed judgment
- Often portrayed sympathetically in later literature as a woman caught between conflicting loves and impossible duty
