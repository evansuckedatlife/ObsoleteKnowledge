## summary

The faithful wife of *Odysseus*, king of Ithaca, who waited twenty years for her husband's return from the Trojan War. She is celebrated for her constancy, her cleverness in resisting the advances of suitors, and her steadfast hope against despair.

## you gotta know

- Wife of *Odysseus* and mother of *Telemachus*, a princess of Sparta by birth and queen of Ithaca.
- Left alone to rule Ithaca when *Odysseus* sailed for Troy, she endured a decade of war waiting for him in vain.
- Besieged by over a hundred noble suitors demanding she marry one of them, all consumed her household's resources and her patience.
- Famously wove a burial shroud for her father-in-law by day and unraveled it by night, buying years of time without choosing a suitor.
- Remained utterly faithful to *Odysseus* despite his ten-year absence after the war, sustained only by rumors and hope.
- Used her wit and constancy to preserve her son's inheritance and her own honor against impossible pressure.
- Eventually reunited with *Odysseus* after he slew the suitors, their marriage and kingdom restored after two decades of separation.

## connections

- [[odysseus]] — her husband, whose long journey home she faithfully awaited.
- [[the-odyssey]] — the epic that tells of her patient vigil and *Odysseus*' struggle to return.
- [[telemachus]] — their son, who came of age during his father's absence and sought news of him.
- [[athens]] — where the goddess *Athena* championed both *Odysseus* and his son's cause.
