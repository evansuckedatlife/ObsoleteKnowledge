# Osiris

## summary

The god of the afterlife, resurrection, and fertility, Osiris was murdered by his jealous brother Set but restored to life by his devoted wife and sister Isis. He rules as judge of the dead in the underworld and became the model for pharaonic kingship and the promise of eternal life.

## you gotta know

- *Green-skinned* god, often depicted mummified and holding the crook and flail (symbols of kingship)
- Murdered and dismembered by his brother *Set*, then reassembled and magically resurrected by *Isis*
- Father of *Horus*, who avenged him by defeating Set in combat for control of Egypt
- Judge of the dead in the underworld; souls' hearts were weighed against the feather of *Ma'at* in his presence
- Original king of Egypt who brought civilization and agriculture, especially grain cultivation
- His annual flooding of the *Nile* and rebirth symbolized renewal, making him central to Egyptian continuity
- Every deceased pharaoh was believed to become Osiris, ensuring their eternal kingship in the afterlife
- Major cult center at *Abydos*, where his resurrection myth was reenacted in elaborate festivals

## connections

- [[isis]] — his wife and sister, who pieced him together and brought him back to life through magic
- [[set]] — his brother and murderer, whose defeat by Horus restored Osiris's legacy
- [[horus]] — his son, conceived after his death, who avenged him and became the rightful king
- [[anubis]] — associated with mummification and embalming, key to Osiris's resurrection
- [[nephthys]] — his sister, who also assisted in his resurrection alongside Isis
- [[ma'at]] — the principle of truth and justice by which Osiris judges the dead
- [[pharaoh]] — every pharaoh was identified with Osiris upon death
