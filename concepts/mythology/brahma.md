# Brahma

## summary

**Brahma** is the creator god of the Hindu trinity, responsible for bringing the universe into being at the start of each cosmic cycle. Despite his supreme role in creation, he is worshipped less frequently than Vishnu or Shiva, with relatively few temples dedicated solely to him.

## you gotta know

- The first member of the *Trimurti*, responsible for *creation* and the birth of all beings.
- Depicted with four heads and four arms, each head facing a cardinal direction to symbolize his omniscience.
- Holds sacred texts (the *Vedas*) in one hand and a *mala* (prayer beads) in another, representing knowledge and spiritual practice.
- His consort is *Saraswati*, goddess of knowledge, arts, and learning, who emerges from his mind.
- Associated with the cosmic egg (*Hiranyagarbha*) from which all creation emerged in Hindu cosmology.
- Significantly less worshipped than Vishnu or Shiva, with few temples dedicated to him as the sole deity.
- In Vedic times, rituals and sacrifices were thought to sustain his creative powers and maintain the universe.
- The *Brahma Sutras*, philosophical texts, attempt to explain the relationship between Brahman (ultimate reality) and the created universe.

## connections

- [[vishnu]] — fellow member of the Trimurite, responsible for preservation and sustenance.
- [[shiva]] — the third member of the Trimurti, responsible for dissolution and transformation.
- [[trimurti]] — the divine trinity in which Brahma holds the role of creator.
- [[saraswati]] — his consort, the goddess of knowledge, wisdom, and the arts.
- [[vedas]] — the ancient sacred texts associated with Brahma and Hindu knowledge.
