# Balder

## summary

*Balder* is the god of light, beauty, and goodness—the most beloved of all the Aesir. His death at the hands of the blind god *Hod*, manipulated by *Loki*, becomes the pivotal tragedy that sets the inevitable destruction of *Ragnarok* in motion.

## you gotta know

- The brightest of the gods, associated with light, purity, and beauty; his mere presence dispels darkness.
- Son of *Odin* and *Frigg*, cherished by all gods and giants alike—the only being in all the nine worlds without cause to hate him.
- *Frigg* binds all things with an oath to spare him from harm, but the mistletoe plant is overlooked as too young and harmless.
- Killed by the blind god *Hod*, wielding a mistletoe dart planted in his hand by the trickster *Loki*.
- His death is irreversible even by the gods' power; *Frigg* sends a messenger to the realm of death seeking his return, but he cannot leave until Ragnarok.
- The gods mourn so deeply that all things weep for his loss—the origin of weeping in the world.
- Represents the tragic lesson that beauty and goodness offer no protection against fate and treachery.

## connections

- [[odin]] — his father, king of the gods who grieves his passing deeply.
- [[frigg]] — his mother, whose desperate attempts to save him fail against Loki's cunning.
- [[loki]] — the trickster whose manipulation of Hod kills Balder and breaks the peace of the gods.
- [[thor]] — his brother, who like all the Aesir mourns his death.
- [[norns]] — the weavers of fate whose decisions cannot be unmade, even by gods.
