## summary

King of *Troy* throughout the ten-year war, Priam witnessed the destruction of his kingdom while grieving the loss of his sons. Though elderly and unable to prevent the catastrophe, he retained dignity and paternal devotion, famously begging *Achilles* for his dead son *Hector's* body.

## you gotta know

- Aged king of Troy who ruled during the entire ten-year war
- Fathered *Hector*, *Paris*, *Cassandra*, and many other children whose fates shaped the war
- Married to *Hecuba*, enduring her prophecies of doom throughout the conflict
- Watched *Hector* die at *Achilles'* hands and subsequently was forced to ransom his son's corpse
- Made the fateful decision to allow Paris to abduct Helen, which triggered the Greek invasion
- Maintained his role as king and protector despite the city's inevitable downfall
- Killed by *Achilles'* son *Neoptolemus* at the altar during Troy's final sack
