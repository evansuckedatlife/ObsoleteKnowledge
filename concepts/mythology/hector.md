# Hector

## summary

Prince of *Troy* and mightiest of the Trojan warriors, Hector was the defender of his city and a figure of tragic nobility. Though ultimately defeated by *Achilles*, he embodied the Trojan cause and faced his doom with dignity, fully aware that his death meant Troy's fall.

## you gotta know

- Son of *Priam* and *Hecuba*, eldest prince of Troy and supreme Trojan commander
- Married to *Andromache* and father of infant *Astyanax*, whose welfare drove his heroic stand
- Killed *Patroclus* in single combat while the latter wore *Achilles'* armor
- Faced *Achilles* in the climactic duel of the *Iliad*, fully knowing it would be his last fight
- Dragged around Troy's walls three times by Achilles as final indignity after his death
- Described by both Greeks and Trojans as noble, honorable, and devoted to family and city
- The Trojan War's narrative pivot: his death symbolized the beginning of Troy's inevitable doom
