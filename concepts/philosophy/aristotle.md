# Aristotle

## summary

**Aristotle** (384–322 BCE) was a Greek philosopher born in Macedonia who studied under Plato, founded his own school (the Lyceum), and systematized logic, metaphysics, ethics, and natural science. His belief in empirical observation, final causes, and the primacy of the individual (not abstract forms) made him the counterweight to Platonism and the architect of Western science and philosophy.

## you gotta know

### Life and Career
- Born in Stagira, northern Greece; joined Plato's Academy at age 17 and remained for 20 years.
- Tutor to the young *Alexander the Great*; had access to Macedonia's resources and specimens.
- Founded the *Lyceum* in Athens (335 BCE); taught while walking (`peripatetic` school).
- Wrote over 200 treatises; most surviving works are lecture notes, fragmentary and dense.

### Logic and Method
- Invented *formal logic*, the theory of the syllogism: if A=B and B=C, then A=C.
- Logic was his organon (tool) for all other inquiry; this framework lasted 2000 years.
- Advocated empirical observation and classification; rejected Plato's forms as unnecessary.
- Believed knowledge comes from examining particulars and deriving universal principles.

### Metaphysics and Nature
- Argued that being is not transcendent but immanent: the form (essence, eidos) exists *in* the individual thing.
- Every thing has a *telos* (final cause, purpose); understanding nature means grasping what something is for.
- Matter and form are inseparable; change happens when potential becomes actual.
- Natural motion (falling, rising) and celestial motion (eternal circular) reveal divine hierarchy.

### Ethics and Politics
- Virtue is a *habit* (not knowledge): the mean between extremes (courage between cowardice and recklessness).
- *Eudaimonia* (flourishing, often mistranslated as "happiness") is the highest good, achieved by exercising virtue.
- Humans are *political animals*; the state exists to enable human flourishing.
- Natural slavery and gender hierarchy are defended by appeal to nature (a claim modern philosophy rejects).

## context

Aristotle is the most systematizing mind in ancient philosophy. His logic, metaphysics, and natural philosophy dominated Western thought through the Middle Ages and Renaissance; even his errors (geocentrism, impetus theory) were so well-articulated that they persisted until Galileo and Newton. His virtue ethics experienced a 20th-century revival as an alternative to consequentialism and deontology. The breadth of his work—spanning logic, biology, politics, rhetoric, poetics—makes him the model of the universal scholar.
