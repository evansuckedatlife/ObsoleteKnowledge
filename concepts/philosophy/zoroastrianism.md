# Zoroastrianism

## summary

*Zoroastrianism* is an ancient Persian religion founded by the prophet *Zoroaster* (exact date disputed; c. 1500–500 BCE, scholarly debate centers on the earlier date). It is one of the world's oldest ethical monotheistic religions, teaching that the universe is a cosmic struggle between *Ahura Mazda* (the Wise Lord, the god of good) and *Angra Mainyu* (the Destructive Spirit, evil). The religion introduces key theological concepts later adopted by Judaism, Christianity, and Islam: eschatology (end times), resurrection, judgment after death, heaven and hell, and the ultimate triumph of good over evil. Zoroastrianism shaped Persian imperial ideology and remains a living tradition practiced by Parsis and other communities.

## you gotta know

- The prophet *Zoroaster* taught that the cosmos is fundamentally ethical: a struggle between good (*Ahura Mazda* and his angels, the *Amesha Spentas*) and evil (*Angra Mainyu* and his demons, the *daevas*), with humans as moral agents choosing sides.
- *Ahura Mazda* is the supreme god, creator of all good; evil is co-eternal with good but will ultimately be defeated—unlike later monotheisms where God is absolutely transcendent and all-powerful.
- Zoroastrianism introduced the concept of *apocalypse* (*afrīnagān*): at the end of history, *Saoshyant* (a savior figure) will be born of Zoroaster's seed, the dead will be resurrected, and the world will be purified by fire and molten metal.
- Individual *judgment after death* precedes the cosmic judgment; the soul is tested and crosses the *Chinvat Bridge* (the bridge of separation); the righteous cross safely to paradise, the wicked fall to hell.
- Good conduct follows the principle of *asha* (truth, righteousness, order) against *druj* (lie, chaos, disorder); virtue consists of good thoughts, good words, and good deeds (*humata, hukhta, hvarshta*).
- *Fire* is sacred in Zoroastrianism, symbolizing divine presence and purity; the faith included fire temples and the practice of exposing the dead (sky burial) to avoid polluting earth and water.
- Zoroastrianism heavily influenced later Western and Middle Eastern religions: the concepts of Satan, angels, resurrection, paradise, and apocalyptic end-times likely derive from Zoroastrian sources.
