# Five Great Lakes

## summary

The *Five Great Lakes* are the largest system of fresh water by surface area on Earth, straddling the border between the United States and Canada. These interconnected lakes—Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, and Ontario—were shaped by glaciation and have defined North American geography, commerce, and ecology for millennia. Together they hold roughly 20% of the world's surface fresh water and remain vital to shipping, industry, and settlement in the Great Lakes region.

## you gotta know

- *Superior* is the largest by surface area (31,700 square miles), the deepest, and the coldest; it borders Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, and Ontario.
- *Michigan* is the only Great Lake wholly within the United States, bordered by Michigan, Indiana, Illinois, and Wisconsin.
- *Huron* is the second-largest by surface area; it lies between Michigan and Ontario and is connected to Superior via the Saint Marys River.
- *Erie* is the shallowest and warms fastest; it connects the upper lakes (through Niagara Falls and the Welland Canal) to *Ontario* and the Saint Lawrence River.
- *Ontario* is the smallest by surface area but second-deepest; it lies between New York and Ontario and drains eastward into the Saint Lawrence toward the Atlantic.
- The lakes are connected by natural straits and engineering (especially the Welland Canal, which bypasses Niagara Falls) forming a single navigable system.
- They were carved by glacial retreat at the end of the last ice age and fed by the drainage from melting glaciers across the continent.
