# Centripetal and Centrifugal Force

## summary

A widespread misconception treats centripetal and centrifugal force as equal-and-opposite real forces; in fact, only centripetal force is a genuine force. Centrifugal force is a pseudo-force or *fictitious* force that appears to act in a rotating reference frame but has no physical cause—it is an artifact of non-inertial observation, not a force that actually pulls outward.

## you gotta know

- *Centripetal force* is the real force directed toward the center of a circular path, required to keep an object in circular motion (tension in a string, friction on a curve, gravity for orbits).
- *Centrifugal force* is not a real force but a perceived effect in rotating frames of reference—it describes the apparent outward acceleration experienced by an observer in that rotating frame, due to the inertia of the object.
- Newton's first law of motion (inertia) explains the confusion: an object in circular motion tends to continue in a straight line; the centripetal force continuously changes its direction, and from a rotating observer's perspective, an outward force appears to act.
- Centripetal acceleration depends on mass, velocity, and radius: `a = v²/r`; there is no corresponding "centrifugal acceleration" in inertial frames.
- In problems on a merry-go-round, the centripetal force (from friction or tension) pulls you toward the center; the sensation of being pushed outward is your inertia resisting the direction change, not a centrifugal force.
- Engineers and physicists use the fictional centrifugal force as a practical tool in rotating reference frames (e.g., centrifuges) because it simplifies calculations in non-inertial frames, but it remains fictitious.
