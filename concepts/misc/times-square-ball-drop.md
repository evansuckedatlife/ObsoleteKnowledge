# Times Square Ball Drop

## summary

The **Times Square Ball Drop** is an American New Year's Eve tradition centered in Manhattan, where a large illuminated crystal sphere descends from a pole at midnight on December 31 to mark the transition to a new year. The event has become a global symbol of Western New Year celebration, broadcast to hundreds of millions of viewers worldwide. Nearly a million people gather in Times Square annually to witness the sixty-second descent.

## you gotta know

- The ball has been dropped annually since 1907, making it one of the oldest continuous civic celebrations in America.
- The current ball, installed in 2008, is made of 2,688 Waterford crystal panels and weighs nearly 12,000 pounds; it is illuminated by 32,256 LED lights.
- The ball descends 141 feet from its starting position atop a pole on One Times Square building.
- Midnight on the Eastern Time Zone is the official "drop moment" in the United States, establishing New Year for the country's televised celebration.
- The event is free to attend in person; crowds gather hours in advance in barriers that divide Times Square into viewing pens.
- Confetti cannons fire 3,000 pounds of confetti as the ball touches the bottom, creating the iconic visual closure.
- The tradition began as a simple iron-and-wood ball in 1907; periodic redesigns have made it larger, brighter, and more elaborate.
