# Cinque Ports

## summary

The *Cinque Ports* (French for "five ports") are a historic maritime confederation of five fortified English coastal towns—Hastings, New Romney, Hythe, Dover, and Sandwich—established in the medieval period to organize naval defense and commerce along the southeastern English coast. Granted royal charters and privileges in exchange for maintaining ships and military readiness, the Cinque Ports formed an early form of coordinated maritime administration and became some of medieval England's wealthiest and most influential towns.

## you gotta know

- *Hastings*, *New Romney*, *Hythe*, *Dover*, and *Sandwich* are the core five ports; two additional "Cinque Ports" (Winchelsea and Rye) were later added as confederate members.
- They controlled vital shipping lanes across the English Channel and served as England's primary naval bulwark against French and Continental threats.
- Their merchants enjoyed monopolies on wine imports and other goods, accumulating vast wealth and political power in medieval England.
- The confederation had its own court (the Court of Brotherhood and Guestling) to adjudicate disputes between members.
- They were first formally recognized by royal charter in the 12th century, with privileges expanded through the 13th and 14th centuries.
- Their importance declined after the Tudor period as naval power became centered on permanent royal warships rather than merchant vessels.
