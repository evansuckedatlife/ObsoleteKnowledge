# 1962 Seattle World's Fair (Century 21 Exposition)

## summary

The 1962 Seattle World's Fair, officially titled *Century 21 Exposition*, celebrated humanity's optimism about space exploration and the future. Anchored by the iconic *Space Needle*, a 605-foot observation tower that remains Seattle's most recognisable landmark, the fair attracted nearly 10 million visitors and established themes of futurism and technological progress that dominated mid-century expositions.

## you gotta know

- Themed "Century 21" to emphasise the promise of space-age technology and the Space Race, coinciding with American efforts to reach the moon.
- Featured the *Space Needle*, designed by architect *John Graham Jr.*, which has become an enduring symbol of Seattle and the era's optimistic futurism.
- Included a working *Monorail* system connecting fair attractions, representing advanced urban transportation and automation ideals.
- Displayed exhibits focused on science, technology, and space exploration, reflecting the period's fascination with the cosmos and scientific advancement.
- Held on 74 acres in *Seattle Center*, which became a permanent cultural district hosting museums, theatres, and public spaces.
- Established the template for post-war world's fairs that emphasised science, technology, and forward-looking cultural narratives.
