# 1893 Chicago World's Columbian Exposition

## summary

The 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago celebrated the 400th anniversary of Columbus's voyage while showcasing American ambitions to rival European capitals. Designed by *Daniel Burnham*, the fair featured the iconic *Ferris wheel* invented by *George Ferris* and attracted over 27 million visitors with its "White City" neoclassical architecture and innovative attractions.

## you gotta know

- Officially commemorated the quatercentennial of Christopher Columbus's arrival in the Americas.
- Featured the *Ferris wheel*, a revolutionary 80-meter rotating observation wheel that became a world's fair staple.
- The "White City" nickname came from the white-painted neoclassical buildings that dominated the fairgrounds, a architectural statement of American grandeur.
- Held in *Jackson Park* on the shores of Lake Michigan, covering 633 acres and displaying exhibits from 46 nations.
- Introduced novel attractions and technological demonstrations that captivated American audiences, from electricity to transportation innovations.
- The fair's success made Chicago a cultural and industrial destination, cementing American prominence on the world stage.
