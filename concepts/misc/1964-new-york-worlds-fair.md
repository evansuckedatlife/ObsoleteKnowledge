# 1964 New York World's Fair

## summary

The 1964 New York World's Fair returned to *Flushing Meadows, Queens*, a quarter-century after the 1939 exposition, celebrating technological progress and global culture during the height of the Space Race. Its iconic symbol, the *Unisphere*—a 140-foot stainless-steel globe—represented global unity and interconnectedness, while over 51 million visitors experienced exhibits spanning entertainment, technology, and the promise of modernity.

## you gotta know

- Officially opened by President *Lyndon B. Johnson* and themed to celebrate human progress, technological innovation, and world peace.
- Featured the *Unisphere*, a massive hollow globe designed by *Buckminster Fuller*, which became an enduring symbol of global unity and remains in *Flushing Meadows Park*.
- Hosted *Futurama*, an evolution of the 1939 exhibit, showcasing advanced automation, robotics, and urban planning ideals.
- Included performances and cultural exhibits from participating nations, emphasising international cooperation and cultural exchange during Cold War tensions.
- Displayed cutting-edge technology including computers, advanced consumer goods, and space-age design reflecting 1960s optimism.
- Marked one of the last grand American world's fairs before the format declined in popularity and frequency.
