# Primates Classification

## summary

A persistent misconception distinguishes primates from humans, treating humans as fundamentally separate from the primate order, when in fact *Homo sapiens* is a species within the order *Primates*. Primates include lemurs, tarsiers, monkeys, apes, and humans; the confusion arises from the cultural and psychological separation humans maintain from other animals, not from any biological distinction.

## you gotta know

- *Primates* is a mammalian order characterized by grasping hands, forward-facing eyes, complex brains, and generally social behavior—humans fit all of these criteria and are unambiguously classified as primates.
- The order *Primates* is divided into *Haplorhini* (dry-nosed primates: monkeys, apes, and humans) and *Strepsirrhini* (wet-nosed primates: lemurs, lorises); humans belong to Haplorhini.
- Within Haplorhini, humans are members of the family *Hominidae* (great apes), alongside orangutans, gorillas, and chimpanzees; this makes humans literally apes in the biological sense.
- The misconception often stems from education that treats "apes" and "primates" as categories distinct from humans, reinforcing a false separation for cultural and religious reasons.
- Genetic evidence shows humans and chimpanzees share approximately 98–99% of their DNA; humans and other primates share progressively fewer genetic similarities by evolutionary distance.
- Modern biological taxonomy, established through evolution and genetics, places humans squarely within Primates; denying this is akin to denying that humans are mammals.
