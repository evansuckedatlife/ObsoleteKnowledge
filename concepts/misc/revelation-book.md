## summary

*Revelation* is the final book of the New Testament, a visionary apocalyptic text attributed to John the Apostle, describing end-times prophecies, cosmic battles, and divine judgment. It is frequently confused with other apocalyptic or mystical Christian texts, and its symbols are often misinterpreted in popular culture.

## you gotta know

- Written in the late 1st century CE, likely during persecution of early Christians under Roman rule; the visions address the suffering church's hope for vindication.
- Not the only apocalyptic text in the Bible—Daniel, parts of Matthew, and 1 Thessalonians also contain eschatological material, but Revelation is the *only* canonical Christian apocalypse.
- The "Four Horsemen" (Conquest, War, Famine, Death) appear in Revelation 6, not in Ezekiel or other prophetic books (though Ezekiel has visions of wheels and thrones).
- The number 666 in Revelation 13:18 is *not* a universal symbol of evil outside that specific chapter; it refers to a beast and was likely a coded reference to Nero Caesar in gematria.
- The "Rapture"—the sudden taking away of believers before tribulation—is *not explicitly named* in Revelation; the concept is primarily drawn from 1 Thessalonians 4:17.
- Millennialism (the thousand-year reign in Revelation 20) has produced three major Christian interpretive traditions—premillennial, amillennial, and postmillennial—each reading the same text differently.
