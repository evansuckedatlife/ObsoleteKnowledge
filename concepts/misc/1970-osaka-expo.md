## summary

Expo 70 in Osaka was the first world's fair held in Asia, marking Japan's emergence as a postwar economic and technological powerhouse. With over 64 million visitors—the highest attendance of any world's fair at that time—it showcased Japanese innovation, futuristic design, and cultural identity while serving as a global stage for demonstrating the nation's recovery and modern achievements.

## you gotta know

- Officially celebrated Japan's economic miracle and cultural renaissance following World War II and the 1964 Tokyo Olympics.
- Themed "Progress and Harmony for Mankind," emphasising technological optimism and human-centred development.
- Featured the *Tower of the Sun*, a monumental sculptural symbol designed by artist *Taro Okamoto*, representing the fair's visionary spirit and becoming an iconic landmark.
- Held in *Senri Hills*, a newly developed area north of Osaka, showcasing Japanese urban planning and modernisation expertise.
- Displayed cutting-edge Japanese technology, from consumer electronics to robotics, establishing Japan's global reputation for innovation and manufacturing excellence.
- Attracted record attendance, demonstrating Asia's growing cultural and economic significance during a period of geopolitical rebalancing.
