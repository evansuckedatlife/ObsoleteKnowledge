# Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley

## summary

Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley is often confused with or conflated with her mother, the philosopher and feminist *Mary Wollstonecraft*, despite living in different centuries and having distinct legacies. The daughter and author of *Frankenstein*, Mary Shelley (1797–1851) was a novelist and editor in her own right, while her mother (1759–1797) was a polemicist and writer on women's rights who died shortly after Mary's birth.

## you gotta know

- *Mary Wollstonecraft* (1759–1797) was the philosopher and writer, famed for *A Vindication of the Rights of Woman* (1792), a foundational feminist text advocating women's education and autonomy.
- *Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley* (1797–1851), the daughter, was a novelist best known for *Frankenstein* (1818), which she conceived during a creative summer in 1816 with her husband Percy Bysshe Shelley.
- Mary Shelley was born after her mother's death from complications of childbirth; she never knew Mary Wollstonecraft personally, though her mother's legacy shaped her intellectual upbringing.
- Mary Shelley married Percy Bysshe Shelley, a Romantic poet, in 1816; after his drowning in 1822, she spent her later life editing his works and writing novels.
- The confusion arises because Mary Shelley's full married name honors her mother's name, and both were intellectually accomplished women, but their eras and disciplines differ significantly.
- Mary Shelley also edited a major anthology of her husband's poetry and wrote several other novels beyond *Frankenstein*, including *The Last Man* and *Mathilda*.
