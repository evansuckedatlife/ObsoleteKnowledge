# Five Civilized Tribes

## summary

The *Five Civilized Tribes* are the five major Native American nations—the *Cherokee*, *Chickasaw*, *Choctaw*, *Creek*, and *Seminole*—who lived in the southeastern United States and adopted elements of European American governmental, economic, and cultural practices during the 18th and early 19th centuries. Named by American settlers who regarded their political structures, literacy, and agricultural systems as "civilized" by European standards, these tribes maintained internal sovereignty despite increasing pressure from U.S. expansion. However, they were forcibly removed from their homelands to Oklahoma during the 1830s, an event memorialized as the *Trail of Tears*.

## you gotta know

- The *Cherokee* nation: the largest of the five, occupied the Appalachian region (present-day Carolinas and Tennessee); developed a syllabary script created by *Sequoyah*.
- The *Choctaw* nation: occupied present-day Mississippi and Louisiana; one of the first to be forcibly relocated westward, in 1831.
- The *Creek* nation: occupied the southeastern coastal plain; signed treaties with the U.S. but were eventually displaced.
- The *Chickasaw* nation: occupied present-day Tennessee, Kentucky, and Mississippi; fiercely resisted removal but were displaced by the 1830s.
- The *Seminole* nation: occupied Florida; resisted removal longest, leading to the prolonged Seminole Wars (1817–1858) before final displacement.
- The term "Civilized" reflected only American prejudices—these were complex, sovereign nations with sophisticated legal systems, written laws, and trade networks.
