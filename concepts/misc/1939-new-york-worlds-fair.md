# 1939 New York World's Fair

## summary

The 1939 New York World's Fair, held in *Flushing Meadows, Queens*, was a celebration of progress and technological optimism amid the shadows of economic depression and impending global conflict. Its iconic symbols—the *Trylon* and *Perisphere*—represented humanity's aspirations toward a brighter future, while over 44 million visitors explored exhibits showcasing innovation, design, and the marvels of tomorrow.

## you gotta know

- Themed "Building the World of Tomorrow," it offered escapism from the Great Depression and geopolitical tensions of the era.
- Featured the monumental *Trylon* (triangular obelisk) and *Perisphere* (colossal sphere), architectural symbols that became emblematic of 1930s futurism and optimism.
- Included the *Futurama* exhibit designed by *Norman Bel Geddes*, showing an imagined automated city of the future with elevated highways and integrated urban planning.
- Displayed cutting-edge technology including early television demonstrations, advanced automobiles, and household innovations.
- Held on 1,216 acres in Flushing Meadows, a former wasteland transformed by *Robert Moses* into fair infrastructure and, later, permanent parkland.
- Despite opening just months before World War II, the fair symbolised pre-war American confidence and aspirations to shape modernity.
