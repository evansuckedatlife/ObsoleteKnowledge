# 1967 Montreal Expo 67

## summary

Expo 67 in Montreal celebrated Canada's centennial year as a young nation and established the fair as a spectacular showcase of architecture, design, and human culture. Held on man-made islands in the *Saint Lawrence River*, the exposition attracted over 50 million visitors and featured *Buckminster Fuller's* iconic *Geodesic Dome*, representing a pinnacle of innovative structural design and forward-thinking environmental consciousness.

## you gotta know

- Officially opened on the centennial of Canadian Confederation (1867–1967), positioning the fair as a celebration of Canada's identity and maturity as a nation.
- Featured *Buckminster Fuller's* *Geodesic Dome* (the American Pavilion), a 60-meter hemisphere that symbolised technological innovation and became an enduring icon of the era.
- Built on man-made islands in the Saint Lawrence River (Île Sainte-Hélène and Île Notre-Dame), showcasing ambitious engineering and environmental transformation.
- Emphasised cultural exchange and "Man and His World" as the central theme, with pavilions from 62 nations celebrating human creativity and diversity.
- Introduced innovative transport systems, including a suspended *Minirail* and *Minicab*, representing futuristic mobility concepts.
- Marked a pinnacle of world's fair design and cultural prestige, influencing exposition aesthetics and themes globally.
