# United Kingdom

## summary

The *United Kingdom* is a political union comprising England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. It is frequently confused with *Great Britain* (the geographical island containing England, Scotland, and Wales) and the *British Isles* (a geographical term including Ireland, the UK, and smaller islands), leading to persistent errors in geography and international relations.

## you gotta know

- The United Kingdom as a political entity was formally established in 1707 when the Kingdom of Scotland merged with the Kingdom of England and Wales; Ireland joined in 1801 (the Union became the "United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland").
- *Great Britain* is a geographical island containing England, Scotland, and Wales—but *not* Northern Ireland (which is on the island of Ireland) and *not* the Republic of Ireland.
- The *British Isles* is a geographical term encompassing the islands of Great Britain, Ireland, and thousands of smaller islands—including the Republic of Ireland, which is *not* part of the United Kingdom politically.
- The Republic of Ireland gained independence in 1922 (formally declared a republic in 1949); it is a sovereign nation not part of the UK, though it shares the island of Ireland with Northern Ireland.
- Northern Ireland is part of the United Kingdom but occupies the north of the island of Ireland; the island itself is divided between the UK (Northern Ireland) and the Republic of Ireland.
- Common mistake: calling all of the British Isles "Britain" or "Great Britain"; or calling the United Kingdom the "British Isles" (the latter is purely geographical, not political).

## connections

- [[england]] — the dominant constituent country of the UK, historically the seat of power.
- [[scotland]] — united with England in 1707; held referendums on independence (2014) and Brexit.
- [[wales]] — united with England in 1536; Welsh language and culture persist.
- [[northern-ireland]] — established as a separate political entity in 1922 when Ireland partitioned; disputed status affects UK-EU relations.
- [[republic-of-ireland]] — independent nation sharing the island of Ireland but not part of the UK.
