# 1900 Paris Exposition Universelle

## summary

The 1900 Paris Exposition Universelle, held from April to November, was the second major world's fair in Paris and one of the largest expositions ever staged. It welcomed over 50 million visitors and celebrated the dawn of the new century with Art Nouveau design, technological marvels, and the grandeur of the Belle Époque at its peak.

## you gotta know

- Officially celebrated the transition to the twentieth century and showcased French cultural and technological achievements.
- Introduced the *Métropolitain* (Paris Metro), a marvel of modern engineering that allowed efficient transport of fair visitors and became permanent infrastructure.
- Featured extensive Art Nouveau design and architecture, establishing the aesthetic movement's visibility and influence across Europe.
- The fairgrounds extended along both banks of the Seine and utilised iconic structures including the *Grand Palais* and *Petit Palais*.
- Displayed innovations in electricity, aviation, automotive design, and entertainment that exemplified fin-de-siècle optimism.
- Marked the zenith of Paris's cultural dominance before World War I reshuffled European power dynamics.
