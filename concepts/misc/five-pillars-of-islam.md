# Five Pillars of Islam

## summary

The *Five Pillars of Islam* are the foundational religious obligations for all Muslims, codified in Islamic theology and law. They form the structural and ethical backbone of Islamic practice and belief, binding the faith community through shared ritual and commitment. Performing the five pillars is essential to a Muslim's spiritual standing and forms the minimum required observance of the faith.

## you gotta know

- *Shahada* (Testimony of Faith): the declaration "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is his prophet"—the central doctrinal statement that defines Islamic monotheism.
- *Salat* (Prayer): mandatory ritual prayer five times daily (*dawn, midday, afternoon, sunset, night*) facing Mecca, performed individually or communally.
- *Zakat* (Almsgiving): an obligatory tax on wealth and income (typically 2.5% of accumulated savings) given to the poor and needy to purify one's earnings.
- *Sawm* (Fasting): abstaining from food, drink, and other bodily needs during daylight hours throughout the month of Ramadan, Islam's holiest month.
- *Hajj* (Pilgrimage): the sacred journey to Mecca, required once in a lifetime for all able-bodied and financially able Muslims, performed during the annual pilgrimage season.
