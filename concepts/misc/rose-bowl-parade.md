# Rose Bowl Parade

## summary

The **Tournament of Roses Parade**, commonly known as the **Rose Bowl Parade**, is an elaborate New Year's Day celebration held annually in Pasadena, California, featuring floats decorated entirely with fresh flowers and greenery. The parade has been held every January 1 since 1890 and is one of the most prestigious parades in the United States. The event originated from the Pasadena Valley Hunt Club's winter festival and has evolved into a globally televised spectacle drawing millions of viewers.

## you gotta know

- Held every January 1 in Pasadena, California (except when January 1 falls on a Sunday, when it is moved to January 2).
- The parade features elaborate floats created by civic organizations, community groups, and corporations, entirely covered with fresh flowers—no artificial flowers allowed.
- The parade tradition began in 1890 as a way to celebrate Southern California's winter bloom and demonstrate the mild climate to attract settlers and tourists.
- The Rose Bowl football game, traditionally played on New Year's Day featuring the top-ranked teams from the *Pac-12* and *Big Ten* conferences, follows the parade.
- Grand Marshal—a notable celebrity or public figure—rides in the leading float; past marshals include presidents, athletes, and entertainment icons.
- The parade route spans 5.5 miles through Pasadena streets, attracting crowds along the entire path; millions more watch via television broadcast.
- Floats typically feature themes celebrating achievements, dreams, innovation, or regional culture; marching bands, equestrian units, and walking groups accompany the floats.
