## summary

The 1904 Louisiana Purchase Exposition in St. Louis commemorated the centennial of the *Louisiana Purchase* and marked the first major world's fair held west of the Mississippi River. With over 20 million visitors, it showcased American progress, hosted the *Summer Olympic Games*, and introduced iconic fair foods and attractions that became cultural fixtures.

## you gotta know

- Celebrated the 100th anniversary of the United States' purchase of the Louisiana Territory from France in 1803.
- Hosted the *Summer Olympic Games* of 1904, integrating athletic competition with the exposition's broader celebration.
- Featured the innovative "ice-cream cone," reportedly invented and popularised at the fair as a convenient way to serve ice cream.
- Held in *Forest Park*, covering 635 acres with neoclassical and Beaux-Arts architecture echoing the Chicago fair's success.
- Exhibited innovations in transportation, including early automobiles and aeronautic designs reflecting technological optimism.
- Introduced attractions and concessions that became staples of American carnival culture and outdoor entertainment.
