# 1851 Great Exhibition, London

## summary

The Great Exhibition of 1851, held in London's *Hyde Park*, was the first major international exposition showcasing industrial and cultural innovations from around the world. Housed in the revolutionary *Crystal Palace*, a prefabricated iron-and-glass structure designed by *Joseph Paxton*, it attracted over six million visitors and established the template for modern world's fairs.

## you gotta know

- Opened by Queen *Victoria* on 1 May 1851 as a celebration of British industrial supremacy and progress.
- Featured the *Crystal Palace*, an innovative structure of cast iron and prefabricated glass panes that revolutionised exhibition architecture.
- Displayed over 100,000 exhibits from 25 nations, ranging from industrial machinery to raw materials and fine arts.
- Turned a substantial profit, which funded the *Victoria and Albert Museum* and other South Kensington cultural institutions.
- The *Crystal Palace* was later relocated to *Sydenham* and became a permanent entertainment venue until its destruction by fire in 1936.
- Inspired the creation of subsequent world's fairs and exposed design, engineering, and manufacturing trends globally.
