# Five Good Emperors

## summary

The *Five Good Emperors* were five successive Roman emperors of the second century A.D.—*Nerva*, *Trajan*, *Hadrian*, *Antoninus Pius*, and *Marcus Aurelius*—whose reigns are considered the zenith of the Roman Empire. Their 84-year period (96–180 A.D.) saw territorial expansion, cultural flourishing, legal development, and relative peace and stability, earning them the title "Antonine" from the dynasty name. Historians regard their era as the high-water mark of Roman civilization before the empire's gradual decline.

## you gotta know

- *Nerva* (r. 96–98 A.D.): initiated the practice of adopting the most qualified successor rather than hereditary succession, stabilizing the realm after Domitian's tyranny.
- *Trajan* (r. 98–117 A.D.): expanded the empire to its widest territorial extent, conquering Dacia and improving infrastructure; celebrated for military prowess and public works.
- *Hadrian* (r. 117–138 A.D.): consolidated rather than expanded, commissioned monumental building projects (the Pantheon, Hadrian's Wall), and codified Roman law.
- *Antoninus Pius* (r. 138–161 A.D.): maintained peace and prosperity with minimal military campaigns; known as a restrained, judicious ruler focused on governance.
- *Marcus Aurelius* (r. 161–180 A.D.): the Stoic philosopher-emperor, whose *Meditations* survives; combated plague and barbarian incursions while developing moral philosophy.
- This era saw the apex of Roman economic prosperity, architectural achievement, and intellectual culture; the empire's borders reached their maximum stability.
