# Nowruz

## summary

**Nowruz** (meaning "new day" in Persian) is the Persian New Year celebrated on the spring equinox, typically March 20 or 21, and marks the beginning of spring across Persian, Central Asian, and parts of Middle Eastern and South Asian cultures. The celebration derives from ancient Zoroastrian traditions and emphasizes renewal, cleaning, new clothes, and family gatherings. It is observed by nearly 300 million people worldwide and carries immense cultural weight in Iran, Afghanistan, and the Caucasus.

## you gotta know

- Occurs precisely at the spring equinox (vernal equinox) in the Northern Hemisphere, when day and night are balanced.
- Rooted in *Zoroastrianism*, the ancient Persian religion founded by the prophet *Zoroaster*, which emphasizes cosmic renewal.
- The celebration lasts thirteen days, with the thirteenth day called *Sizdah Be-dar* ("thirteen outdoors"), spent in nature to cast off misfortune.
- Homes are cleaned thoroughly (*khane takan-ye bazi*, "house shaking") to purify spaces and prepare for renewal.
- The *Haft Sin* table displays seven items beginning with the Persian letter "S": *sabze* (sprouts), *samanu* (pudding), *senjed* (fruit), *sir* (garlic), *sib* (apple), *somaq* (sumac), *serkeh* (vinegar).
- New clothes are traditionally worn, debts are settled, and relationships are reconciled before the holiday.
- Families dye eggs red (symbolizing fertility) and share decorated hard-boiled eggs during meals.
