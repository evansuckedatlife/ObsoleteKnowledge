# Gamal Abdel Nasser

## summary

*Gamal Abdel Nasser* was Egypt's second president (1954–1970) and the architect of Arab nationalism and non-alignment in the Cold War. A military officer who led the 1952 revolution toppling the monarchy, he became the Arab world's most influential leader through anti-imperialism, socialist modernization, and the defiant Suez Crisis.

## you gotta know

- Led the Free Officers Movement that overthrew King Farouk in 1952, emerging as Egypt's strongman by 1954 and reshaping the nation as an Arab socialist state.
- Nationalized the Suez Canal in 1956, triggering the Suez Crisis when Britain, France, and Israel invaded; he survived the attack and emerged a continental hero, cementing pan-Arab leadership.
- Promoted Arab nationalism and unity, including the short-lived union with Syria (United Arab Republic, 1958–1961), positioning Egypt as the leader of the Arab world against Israel and Western imperialism.
- Expanded Egypt's military, built the Aswan High Dam to harness the Nile, and pursued rapid industrialization and land reform to modernize the nation.
- Aligned with the Soviet Union for military and economic support, becoming a key figure in non-aligned politics during the Cold War.
- Died in office in 1970, widely mourned across the Arab world as a symbol of dignity, resistance, and Arab power.
