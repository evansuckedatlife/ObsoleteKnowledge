# Hubert Humphrey

## summary

**Hubert Humphrey** was the thirty-eighth Vice President under Lyndon B. Johnson (1965–1969) and a central figure in American liberalism, civil rights, and Cold War politics. Though he ran for president in 1968 and again in 1972, he never reached the presidency, his career marked by unwavering commitment to New Deal liberalism and by internal party conflict over the Vietnam War.

## you gotta know

- Vice President during the escalation of the Vietnam War; his initial support for Johnson's policies created tension with the party's emerging antiwar faction, damaging his 1968 presidential campaign.
- Known as "the Happy Warrior" for his relentless optimism and passionate speeches on civil rights, government activism, and American idealism.
- Served as a U.S. Senator from Minnesota and was a founding member of the Americans for Democratic Action, helping shape post-war liberal politics.
- Championed the Civil Rights Act of 1964 as a senator and the Fair Housing Act of 1968 as Vice President.
- Lost the 1968 presidential election to Richard Nixon in one of the closest races in American history, hampered by his association with Vietnam and inner-party divisions.
- Attempted a political comeback with a second presidential run in 1972, finishing third in the Democratic primary behind Edmund Muskie and George McGovern.
- Returned to the Senate in 1971 and remained an influential voice for progressive causes until his death in 1978.
