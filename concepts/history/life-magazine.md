# Life Magazine

## summary

*Life* magazine, launched in 1936 by *Henry Luce*, pioneered the picture-magazine format and became the dominant visual chronicle of American life and world events. With lavish color photography and cinematic layouts, it defined the aesthetics of mid-century journalism and influenced how Americans saw their world.

## you gotta know

- Founded by Henry Luce in 1936; first issue featured striking color photography of everyday American life.
- Pioneered the *photo-essay* format, using sequences of carefully curated images to tell stories with minimal text.
- Peak circulation reached nearly *13 million* in the mid-1970s, making it the most widely read magazine ever published.
- Documented major historical events through photography: World War II, the space program, the Vietnam War, and the civil rights movement.
- Employed famous photographers including *Margaret Bourke-White*, *Alfred Eisenstaedt*, and *John Dominis*.
- Famous for its bold, luxurious color photography and dramatic layouts that set the standard for visual magazines.
- Published for 36 years continuously before suspending in 1972; revived in 1978 as a monthly.
