## summary

*Thomas Jefferson's* inauguration on March 4, 1801, occurred during the intensely partisan aftermath of the disputed 1800 election between Federalists and Democratic-Republicans. Delivered in a whisper rather than a shout—a departure from Washington's public vigor—his address articulated the phrase "we are all Republicans, we are all Federalists," seeking national healing after contentious political divisions.

## you gotta know

- Took place March 4, 1801, establishing the March 4 inauguration date for subsequent presidents.
- Followed the contentious election of 1800, decided by the House of Representatives in the 12th-round ballot.
- Jefferson deliberately spoke in a very quiet voice, requiring listeners to lean forward—a symbolic humility.
- His address advocated for unity across party lines despite recent partisan bitterness.
- First inauguration held in Washington, D.C., the newly designated capital (moved from New York).
- Wore a modest suit rather than formal court dress, signaling republican simplicity over aristocratic pomp.
- Marked the peaceful transfer of power from Federalist to Democratic-Republican, a significant democratic precedent.
