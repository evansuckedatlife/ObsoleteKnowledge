## summary

*One Inc. v. Olesen* (1958) is the Supreme Court decision affirming that a magazine published by a homophile organization could not be banned from the mail based solely on its LGBTQ content. The unanimous ruling, issued per curiam without opinion, marked the first U.S. Supreme Court victory for LGBTQ rights and paved the way for the modern gay rights movement.

## you gotta know

- Decided January 13, 1958 by a unanimous 9–0 margin, decided per curiam with no signed opinion.
- Overturned the Post Office's attempt to exclude *ONE Magazine*, published by the One, Incorporated homophile organization, from the mail as "obscene."
- The magazine contained a short story, poetry, and essays on homosexuality, but no explicit sexual content; the Post Office objected to its existence and content alone.
- By affirming without commentary, the Supreme Court effectively barred the government from censoring materials simply because they discussed homosexuality openly.
- The Ninth Circuit's lower court decision had been momentous in itself, but the Supreme Court's affirmation gave it the weight of final authority.
- Enabled the homophile movement to distribute literature freely, contributing to the growth of LGBTQ activism in the late 1950s and 1960s.
- Established an important First Amendment principle: the government cannot suppress speech based on viewpoint or subject matter alone.
