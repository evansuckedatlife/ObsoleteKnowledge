# Augustus

## summary

Augustus was the first emperor of the Roman Empire, ruling from 27 BC until his death in AD 14. Born Gaius Octavius, he became the adopted heir of his great-uncle Julius Caesar and emerged victorious from the resulting civil wars. His reign initiated the *Pax Romana*, an era of relative peace, stability, and cultural flourishing that transformed Rome from a republic into an imperial power.

## you gotta know

- He defeated the combined forces of Mark Antony and Cleopatra at the naval *Battle of Actium* in 31 BC.
- Together with Mark Antony and Marcus Aemilius Lepidus, he formed the *Second Triumvirate* to defeat the assassins of Julius Caesar.
- He boasted that he "found Rome a city of bricks and left it a city of marble" after an extensive building program.
- His forces suffered a devastating defeat in the Teutoburg Forest, where three Roman legions under Varus were annihilated by Germanic tribes.
- He exiled the poet Ovid to the Black Sea for an unspecified "poem and a mistake."
- In 27 BC, the Roman Senate granted him the title *Augustus* (meaning "the revered one"), marking the traditional start of the Roman Empire.

## connections

- [[tiberius]] — his stepson and reluctantly chosen successor to the imperial throne.
- [[julius-caesar]] — his great-uncle and adoptive father, whose assassination propelled Augustus into politics.
- [[mark-antony]] — his primary political rival and former ally in the Second Triumvirate.
- [[cleopatra]] — the Egyptian queen whom he defeated at Actium to secure sole control of the Roman world.
