## summary

*Woodrow Wilson* was the 28th president of the United States, serving from 1913 to 1921. A Democrat and former president of Princeton University, Wilson led America through World War I and its aftermath, championing the principle of national self-determination and the creation of the League of Nations. His domestic agenda, the *New Freedom*, sought to restore competition and reduce corporate monopolies. Though idealistic in his foreign policy vision, his presidency ended amid the League's rejection and a personal stroke.

## you gotta know

- Won the 1912 election as a Progressive, splitting the Republican vote between himself and Theodore Roosevelt; governed with a coalition of Southern Democrats and reformers.
- Passed landmark domestic legislation including the Federal Reserve Act (1913), the Clayton Antitrust Act, and the 16th Amendment (income tax).
- Campaigned for reelection in 1916 on the slogan "He kept us out of war," but declared war on Germany in April 1917 after unrestricted submarine warfare threatened American vessels and lives.
- Articulated the *Fourteen Points* in 1918, outlining his vision for a just postwar order based on open covenants, national self-determination, and a League of Nations.
- Negotiated the *Treaty of Versailles* (1919) and embedded the League of Nations covenant within it; however, the Republican-controlled Senate rejected the treaty, dealing Wilson a devastating political defeat.
- Suffered a severe stroke in October 1919 while touring America to promote the League; spent his final eighteen months largely incapacitated, with his wife and cabinet managing his duties.

## connections

- [[theodore-roosevelt]] — Republican predecessor whose expansionist foreign policy influenced Wilson's later interventionism despite their partisan divide.
- [[world-war-i]] — the conflict Wilson tried to keep America out of until German aggression made intervention inevitable.
- [[league-of-nations]] — Wilson's signature creation, the prototype for the modern United Nations, though the U.S. Senate's refusal to ratify it was a major humiliation.
- [[treaty-of-versailles]] — the peace settlement whose harsh terms and unresolved conflicts foreshadowed World War II.
- [[franklin-d-roosevelt]] — Democratic successor who would realize aspects of Wilson's internationalist vision through the United Nations.
