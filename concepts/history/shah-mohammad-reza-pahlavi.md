# Mohammad Reza Pahlavi

## summary

*Mohammad Reza Pahlavi* (1919–1980), the last Shah of Iran, ruled for 37 years and attempted to modernize Iran into a secular, Western-aligned nation through rapid industrialization and military buildup. His authoritarian methods, lavish spending, and perceived disrespect toward Islamic tradition provoked the Iranian Islamic Revolution, which deposed him and created the Islamic Republic.

## you gotta know

- Ascended to the throne in 1941 after his father's forced abdication; inherited a weakened state and gradually consolidated power.
- Pursued the *White Revolution* (1960s–1970s), a top-down modernization campaign including land reform, women's suffrage, and industrial expansion.
- Aligned Iran with the United States and Israel; accepted Western military and economic presence; angered nationalist and religious constituencies.
- Built up one of the largest militaries in the region; spent vast oil revenues on military hardware and development projects.
- Maintained a secret police force (*SAVAK*) that suppressed dissent through torture and surveillance, radicalizing opposition movements.
- Attempted to minimize Islam's role in public life, earning opposition from *Ayatollah Khomeini* and other clerics.
- Fled Iran in January 1979 as the Islamic Revolution swept the country; died in exile in Egypt in July 1980, dethroned and reviled.
