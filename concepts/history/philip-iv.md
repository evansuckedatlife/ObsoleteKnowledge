# Philip IV

## summary

*Philip IV* (1268–1314), called the Fair, was a centralizing king who challenged papal authority and expanded royal power in France. His reign witnessed the downfall of the Knights Templar, a decisive conflict between state and Church, and the consolidation of Capetian dominance.

## you gotta know

- Reigned 1285–1314; he aggressively centralized royal authority, replacing feudal nobles with royal administrators and lawyers loyal to the crown.
- Clashed with Pope Boniface VIII over taxation of the clergy and ecclesiastical privileges; the conflict marked a turning point in the balance of power between state and Church.
- Arrested and dismantled the Knights Templar (1307–1312), seizing their vast wealth and lands under charges of heresy; the order was dissolved, and most Templars were executed.
- Reorganized and strengthened French law and administration, creating a more bureaucratic and centralized state that anticipated the modern nation-state.
- Married his three surviving sons, two of whom became kings after him, each reigning briefly; the rapid succession of his sons without clear male heirs created succession crises.
- His policies toward nobles, the Church, and the Templars presaged the declining power of feudalism and the rising authority of the national monarch.
