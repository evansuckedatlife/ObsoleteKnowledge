# Plague of Justinian

## summary

The *Plague of Justinian* was a major bubonic plague pandemic that ravaged the Byzantine Empire and Mediterranean region from 541 to 750 CE, killing an estimated 25 to 50 million people. It struck during Emperor Justinian I's reign while he was attempting to reconquer the Western Roman Empire, weakening the Byzantine state and reshaping the political map of the Mediterranean.

## you gotta know

- Caused by *Yersinia pestis*, the same bacterium responsible for the Black Death; spread via trade routes from East Africa and Asia.
- First documented outbreak in Egypt in 541 CE, then spread rapidly to the Byzantine capital Constantinople, where thousands died daily at peak.
- Killed an estimated 25–50 million people across the Mediterranean, Near East, and parts of Europe over two centuries of recurring waves.
- Contemporary historian Procopius recorded that during the worst months in Constantinople, mortality was so high that mass burial became impossible and corpses were dumped into trenches.
- Devastated Justinian's military campaigns to reclaim the Western Empire, draining the treasury and depleting his armies at critical moments.
- Caused severe labor shortages and economic contraction across the Byzantine state, leading to agricultural decline and weakened trade networks.
- Gradually faded by the 8th century, though sporadic outbreaks continued; the exact reasons for its decline remain debated by historians.
