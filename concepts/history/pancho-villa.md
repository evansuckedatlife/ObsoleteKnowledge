## summary

*Pancho Villa* (born *Doroteo Arango*) was a northern Mexican revolutionary general whose Division del Norte became the largest military force during the Mexican Revolution. Known for dramatic cavalry charges and agrarian zeal, he commanded peasant armies while resisting foreign intervention.

## you gotta know

- Rose from banditry to command the *Division del Norte*, the revolution's most formidable military force, with over 50,000 soldiers.
- Allied with *Emiliano Zapata* in the south to challenge both the Porfiriato and later centrist revolutionary governments.
- Known for lightning cavalry raids and bold military tactics; became a legendary figure in northern Mexico.
- Briefly held Mexico City with *Zapata* (1914–1915) before being defeated by the constitutionalist *Venustiano Carranza*.
- In 1916, crossed the US border and attacked Columbus, New Mexico—the only foreign military force to invade the continental United States in a century.
- Assassinated in 1923 in Parral, Chihuahua; his legend grew after death as a champion of the common man.
