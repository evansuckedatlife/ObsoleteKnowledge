# Hadrian

## summary

Hadrian was a Roman emperor who ruled from AD 117 to 138, known as the third of the "Five Good Emperors." Unlike his expansionist predecessor Trajan, Hadrian focused on consolidating the empire and securing its borders. He was a passionate admirer of Greek culture and spent more than half of his reign travelling extensively throughout the empire's provinces.

## you gotta know

- He ordered the construction of *Hadrian's Wall* in northern Britain to mark the empire's frontier and defend against the Picts.
- He rebuilt the *Pantheon* in Rome, constructing the famous domed structure with a central oculus that stands to this day.
- He crushed the *Bar Kokhba revolt* in Judaea, devastating the Jewish population and renaming the province Syria Palaestina.
- He deeply grieved the death of his Greek lover *Antinous*, who drowned in the Nile, and subsequently deified him and founded the city of Antinoöpolis in his honour.
- He abandoned Trajan's recent conquests in Mesopotamia and Armenia, judging them too difficult to defend.

## connections

- [[trajan]] — his predecessor, adoptive father, and fellow Spaniard, whose aggressive expansionist policies Hadrian largely abandoned.
- [[antoninus-pius]] — his adopted heir and successor, who was required by Hadrian to adopt Marcus Aurelius and Lucius Verus in turn.
