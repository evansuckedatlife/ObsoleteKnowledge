# Persia → Iran

## summary

*Persia* was the ancient and classical name for the vast empire and region centered on what is now modern-day *Iran*. The nation officially adopted "Iran" (meaning "land of the Aryans" in Persian) as its official English name in 1935 under Reza Shah Pahlavi, though the change gained wider recognition and enforcement after the 1979 Islamic Revolution. The shift reflected a desire to emphasize national identity rooted in Aryan heritage rather than European colonial nomenclature.

## you gotta know

- The region was called *Persia* throughout antiquity and into the modern era, deriving from the Achaemenid heartland in southwestern Iran.
- Reza Shah Pahlavi formally requested all nations use "Iran" in international discourse beginning in 1935, though the change was gradual outside the region.
- The Persian Empire under the *Achaemenids* (550–330 BCE) was one of the ancient world's largest empires, encompassing three continents.
- Persian culture, language, and philosophy profoundly influenced Islamic civilization and extended into medieval European thought via Spain and the Crusades.
- The 1979 *Islamic Revolution* consolidated the identity shift, establishing the *Islamic Republic of Iran* and severing ties with the Pahlavi monarchy.
- The name "Iran" derives from Old Persian *Aryanam*, meaning "realm of the Aryans," emphasizing indigenous heritage over colonial-era European terminology.
- Contemporary Iran straddles ancient Zoroastrian, Islamic, and secular identities, maintaining Persian as its lingua franca across ethnic minorities.

## connections

- [[achaemenid-empire]] — the classical Persian superpower that defined the region's ancient might.
- [[sassanid-empire]] — the last pre-Islamic Persian empire, lasting until the 7th-century Arab conquest.
- [[safavid-empire]] — established Shia Islam as the state religion, fundamentally shaping modern Iranian identity.
- [[islamic-revolution]] — the 1979 upheaval that consolidated the nation-state and reaffirmed its Persian-Islamic identity.
- [[zoroastrianism]] — ancient Persian religion with lasting cultural influence despite Islam's dominance.
