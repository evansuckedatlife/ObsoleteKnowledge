# House of Tudor

## summary

The **House of Tudor** ruled England from 1485 to 1603, emerging from the chaos of the Wars of the Roses to establish one of history's most celebrated dynasties. Tudor monarchs, especially *Henry VIII* and *Elizabeth I*, transformed England into a major European power, severed its ties with Rome, and made it a center of Renaissance learning and culture.

## you gotta know

- *Henry VII* founded the dynasty in 1485 by defeating *Richard III* at the *Battle of Bosworth Field*, ending the Wars of the Roses.
- *Henry VIII* (r. 1509–1547) broke with Rome over his marriage to *Catherine of Aragon*, establishing the *Church of England* and making himself Supreme Head; married six times and executed two queens.
- The *English Reformation* under Tudor rule severed England's Catholic ties and reshaped religion and politics for centuries to come.
- *Elizabeth I* (r. 1558–1603), the last Tudor monarch, restored Protestantism, defeated the *Spanish Armada*, and presided over England's golden age—a period of literature, exploration, and emerging power.
- Patronized the English Renaissance; *William Shakespeare* wrote his greatest works under Elizabethan rule.
- Consolidated royal power over the nobility after the Wars of the Roses, establishing strong centralized governance.
- The dynasty ended with Elizabeth's death; the Scottish *House of Stuart* inherited the English throne.
