# Navajo (Diné)

## summary

The *Navajo* (Diné, meaning "the People") are the largest federally recognized Native American nation, inhabiting the Southwest (Arizona, New Mexico, Utah) and known for their distinctive weaving, jewelry, sand painting, and shepherding economy. Descended from Athabaskan migrants, they developed a unique cultural synthesis and today maintain one of the strongest tribal governments and language preservation programs.

## you gotta know

- Call themselves the *Diné*; the Navajo name derives from the Spanish. Language is an Athabaskan language distantly related to Apache.
- Developed a pastoral economy based on sheep herding (introduced by Spanish colonizers), becoming master weavers whose blankets and rugs are world-renowned.
- Created intricate sand paintings as part of healing ceremonies; mastered silverwork and turquoise jewelry-making as trade commodities.
- Forcibly relocated in the *Long Walk* (1864–1868), imprisoned at *Bosque Redondo*, an internment camp where disease and starvation killed thousands; freed under the Treaty of 1868.
- The *Navajo Code Talkers* served in World War II, using the Diné language as an unbreakable military cipher; about 400 served in the Marine Corps.
- Today the Navajo Nation is the second-largest reservation by population; the Navajo language is actively spoken and taught, with strong sovereignty and cultural revitalization.
