# Seljuk Empire

## summary

The *Seljuk Empire* (1037–1194 CE) was a Turkish dynasty descended from the *Oghuz* nomads, which conquered Central Asia, Persia, and Anatolia, becoming the dominant military power in the Islamic world. Under sultans like *Alp Arslan* and *Nizam al-Mulk*, the Seljuks revitalized the weakened Abbasid caliphate, established Persian cultural norms across their vast realm, and clashed with Crusader armies in the Holy Land.

## you gotta know

- Founded by *Seljuk ibn Duqaq* in 1037 CE; expanded rapidly under *Alp Arslan* and his vizier *Nizam al-Mulk*, who reorganized the state with a sophisticated bureaucracy and military hierarchy.
- Defeated a *Byzantine* army at the *Battle of Manzikert* (1071 CE), a pivotal victory that opened *Anatolia* to Turkish settlement and led to the Byzantine emperor's desperate appeal to the Pope—a trigger for the *Crusades*.
- Fragmented into competing *atabegs* (military governors) in Syria and Mesopotamia by the 12th century, losing central cohesion even as individual Turkish emirates remained powerful.
- Patronized Islamic scholarship and architecture; *Nizam al-Mulk* founded the *Nizamiyyah madrasas*, institutions of learning that became models for Islamic universities across the medieval world.
- Reconstituted Sunni orthodoxy by supporting the Abbasid caliph (though holding supreme power) and championed Sunni jurisprudence against Shiite rivals.
- Declined after Nizam al-Mulk's assassination in 1092 CE; further weakened by Crusader incursions and internal fragmentation, finally succeeded by regional successors like the *Zengids*.
