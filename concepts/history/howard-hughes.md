# Howard Hughes

## summary

*Howard Hughes* was an aviation pioneer, industrialist, and filmmaker who set multiple world speed records in the 1930s, designed and built experimental aircraft, and owned Trans World Airlines (TWA) for decades. His reckless flying style, engineering ambition, and later eccentricity made him one of aviation's most polarizing figures.

## you gotta know

- Set world air-speed record of 352 mph in 1935 flying his custom *Hughes H-1 Racer*.
- Flew around the world in 1938 in the twin-engine *Lockheed 14*, completing it in 3 days, 19 hours.
- Founded Hughes Aircraft Company, which became a major defense contractor and manufacturer.
- Owned and ran TWA, transforming it into a major transatlantic airline before selling his stake in 1966.
- Designed the *Hughes H-4 Hercules*, an enormous flying boat with an 8-foot wingspan—the largest ever built.
- Suffered injuries in several crashes but continued to fly experimental aircraft personally.
- Later withdrew from public life, becoming reclusive and eccentric; his wealth and isolation fueled legend.
