## summary

*Hollingsworth v. Perry* (2013) is the Supreme Court decision that invalidated California's Proposition 8 on standing grounds, effectively voiding the state's same-sex marriage ban without establishing a nationwide right to marriage. The 5–4 ruling, written by Chief Justice Roberts, sidestepped the broader constitutional question by finding that the initiative's official proponents lacked legal standing to defend it.

## you gotta know

- Decided June 26, 2013 by a 5–4 margin, with Chief Justice John Roberts writing the majority opinion.
- Invalidated Proposition 8, California's 2008 constitutional amendment banning same-sex marriage after the state supreme court had briefly legalized it.
- The majority held that the official proponents of Proposition 8 lacked constitutional standing to defend the measure in federal court when California's governor and attorney general declined to appeal the lower court ruling striking it down.
- Roberts' opinion emphasized that "we have never before upheld the standing of a private party to defend the constitutionality of a state statute when state officials have chosen not to."
- Justice Kennedy's concurrence outlined the constitutional case for marriage equality, presaging *Obergefell* two years later.
- Because the decision turned on standing rather than merits, it did not establish a constitutional right to same-sex marriage nationwide; *Obergefell* completed that task.
- Dissenters argued the proponents had cognizable standing as the architects and defenders of Proposition 8.
