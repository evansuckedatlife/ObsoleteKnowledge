# Kim Philby

## summary

*Kim Philby* was a high-ranking officer in British intelligence who was secretly recruited by Soviet intelligence while at Cambridge University. He became the most influential mole in MI6's history, exposing operations and agents throughout the Cold War before defecting to Moscow in 1963.

## you gotta know

- Born in India; recruited by Soviet intelligence at Cambridge in the 1930s as part of the Cambridge spy ring while reading for a degree in modernist literature.
- Rose through the ranks of MI6 to become head of the anti-Soviet counterintelligence section—the perfect position from which to betray agents and operations to Moscow.
- Provided the KGB with intelligence about British intelligence networks, agent identities, and diplomatic cables for over twenty years, compromising operations across Europe and the Middle East.
- Co-founded the intelligence liaison with the CIA after World War II, giving the Soviets access to American intelligence secrets as well; his reports shaped Moscow's entire understanding of Western operations.
- Defected to Moscow in January 1963 when British and American intelligence finally closed in; lived comfortably in the Soviet Union until his death in 1988, writing his memoir in exile.
- Embodied the "mole" archetype—the trusted insider who exploits intimacy and access to deliver maximum damage.
