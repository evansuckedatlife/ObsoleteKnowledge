# Free Soil Party

## summary

The Free Soil Party was a short-lived antislavery political movement of the 1848–1854 era, formed by Northern Democrats opposed to slavery expansion into western territories. Running on the slogan "Free Soil, Free Speech, Free Labor," the party successfully nominated former President Martin Van Buren as its 1848 presidential candidate and later merged into the nascent Republican Party, making it a crucial bridge between the Whigs and the GOP.

## you gotta know

- The party emerged from a coalition of "Barnburner" Democrats (radical New York Democrats opposed to slavery) and some antislavery Whigs and Liberty Party members.
- Rather than demanding slavery's abolition in existing states, the Free Soilers opposed slavery's *expansion* into new territories, framing it as a matter of free labor competition and worker rights rather than moral abolition.
- Van Buren's 1848 candidacy split the Democratic vote, contributing to Whig Zachary Taylor's election and establishing the party as a significant force: he won nearly 10% of the popular vote.
- The party's platform held that new Western lands should be reserved for free Northern settlers and laborers, not slaveholders and enslaved workers.
- After the 1848 election, Free Soilers elected several representatives and maintained a presence in Congress, notably John C. Frémont, who would become the Republican Party's first presidential nominee in 1856.
- The party dissolved by the mid-1850s as most members joined the new Republican Party, which absorbed the Free Soil program of opposing slavery's territorial expansion.
