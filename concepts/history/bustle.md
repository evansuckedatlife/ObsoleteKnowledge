# Bustle

## summary

The *bustle* is a framework of steel hoops, petticoats, or padding worn beneath skirts at the rear to create projection and silhouette at the back of a woman's dress, dominant in the 1870s–1880s and again briefly in the 1880s–1890s. Replacing the all-around volume of the crinoline, the bustle concentrated decorative fabric and shape at the rear, creating one of history's most dramatically angular and impractical silhouettes.

## you gotta know

- The first bustle era (1869–1876) featured a sharp projection from the lower back, creating an almost shelf-like protrusion that shifted the body's center of gravity and required altered posture and movement.
- Constructed from flexible steel hoops sewn into a petticoat frame worn under the skirt; the rear projection could extend up to 24 inches from the body.
- The second bustle era (1881–1889) featured an even more dramatic, nearly vertical projection, sometimes with elaborate flounces and decorative layers creating additional height and volume.
- Required altered furniture design: narrow chairs, carriage seats, and even theater seats had to be modified to accommodate women wearing bustles, as sitting was functionally difficult.
- Associated with a top-heavy, exaggerated feminine silhouette that contemporary critics considered ugly and ungainly—yet fashion devotees embraced it as the height of modernity.
- Simplified skirt designs that worked with bustles featured dramatic asymmetry, layered drapery, and elaborate trains that compensated for the rear projection with visual complexity.
- Declined rapidly by the 1890s as the "Gibson Girl" aesthetic and modern fashion movements rejected extreme silhouettes in favor of more natural proportions.

## connections

- [[crinoline]] — earlier garment that distributed volume evenly; the bustle concentrated it posteriorly.
- [[corset]] — essential companion that exaggerated the S-curve silhouette of the bustle era.
- [[victoria]] — British queen during both bustle eras, representing the enduring Victorian commitment to extreme fashion.
