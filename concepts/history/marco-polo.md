# Marco Polo

## summary

*Marco Polo* was a 13th-century Venetian merchant and explorer whose account of his journey to the court of *Kublai Khan* opened European awareness of Asia. His travels across the Silk Road and service under the Mongol ruler made him the most famous early European visitor to the Far East, though later scholars debate how much of his travels he actually witnessed.

## you gotta know

- Left Venice in 1271 as a young teenager and traveled overland to China with his father and uncle, taking over three years to reach *Kublai Khan's* court.
- Served *Kublai Khan* directly, possibly as a diplomat and administrator, and was granted the title of *Messere* (Mister) by the Mongol ruler.
- Traveled extensively throughout the *Mongol Empire*, visiting regions of modern-day China, Burma, and Southeast Asia, gathering geographical and political intelligence.
- Returned to Venice in 1295 after 24 years in Asia, bringing jewels and letters of recommendation from *Kublai Khan*.
- Dictated his memoirs to a fellow prisoner, *Rustichello of Pisa*, while imprisoned after a naval battle; these writings became *The Travels of Marco Polo* (also called *Il Milione*).
- His account described the wealth, culture, and organization of the *Yuan Dynasty*, shocking Europeans with tales of vast cities, paper money, and advanced governance.
- Though his book spread knowledge of Asia, some scholars note inconsistencies and question whether he traveled the full route or relied partly on hearsay.

## connections

- [[kublai-khan]] — ruled the Mongol Empire at the height of Marco Polo's service.
- [[zheng-he]] — another great Asian explorer, but sailed by ship centuries later.
- [[christopher-columbus]] — inspired partly by reading accounts like Marco Polo's, seeking a western route to Asia.
- [[the-silk-road]] — the trade networks he traversed to reach the Khan's court.
- [[mongol-empire]] — the political order that enabled his safe passage across Asia.
