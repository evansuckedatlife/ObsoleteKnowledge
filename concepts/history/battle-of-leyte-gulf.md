# Battle of Leyte Gulf

## summary

Fought October 23–26, 1944 in the waters around the Philippine island of Leyte, this battle ranks as the largest naval engagement in history by tonnage and surface area. American forces returning to liberate the Philippines crushed the Japanese fleet in a three-pronged battle that shattered Japan's remaining naval power and confirmed American dominance of the Pacific.

## you gotta know

- Largest naval battle in history: three distinct engagements (*Kurita's Center Force*, *Nishimura's Southern Force*, *Ozawa's Northern Force*).
- Admiral William Halsey commanded the American assault force; Japanese forces under Admirals Kurita, Nishimura, and Ozawa.
- Sank or crippled all four Japanese battleships involved, including the massive *Yamato*; Japanese lost 26 ships total.
- General Douglas MacArthur fulfilled his promise "I shall return" by landing on Leyte the day the battle began.
- U.S. naval superiority now absolute; Japan possessed no fleet capable of organized operations; marked functional end of Japanese naval combat capability.
- Japanese deployed *kamikaze* suicide aircraft tactics for the first time in large numbers as desperation weapon.
- American losses: one light carrier sunk, two escort carriers sunk by air attack; overall tactical and strategic victory.
