# Bessie Coleman

## summary

*Bessie Coleman* became the first Black aviator and the first Black woman to hold a pilot's license, obtaining her license in 1921. She broke through racial and gender barriers to become a pioneering aviator in the nascent aviation age, though her career was cut short by her death in 1926.

## you gotta know

- First Black American and first Black woman to earn a pilot's license, from the Fédération Aéronautique Internationale in 1921.
- Learned French to attend flight school in France, where racial discrimination was less pervasive than in the United States.
- Worked as a stunt pilot and barnstormer, performing aerial shows and demonstrations across the country.
- Died in 1926 in an accident during a test flight for an aeronautical show in Jacksonville, Florida.
- Never realized her dream of opening a flight school for African Americans; her legacy was reclaimed decades later.
- Faced discrimination from both racial and gender perspectives in an industry dominated by white men.
- Became a symbol of breaking barriers decades after her death; now recognized as a pioneer.
