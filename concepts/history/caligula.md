# Caligula

## summary

Caligula was the third Roman emperor, ruling from AD 37 until his assassination in AD 41. The son of the popular general Germanicus, his brief reign began with promise but quickly descended into tyranny, extravagance, and madness following a severe illness. He is remembered as a cruel and unpredictable despot who showed open contempt for the Roman Senate before being murdered by his own guards.

## you gotta know

- His childhood nickname "Caligula" translates to "little boots," given to him by soldiers when he accompanied his father on campaigns in miniature military uniform.
- According to the historian Suetonius, he planned to make his favourite horse, *Incitatus*, a consul and a priest.
- He ordered his soldiers to collect seashells on the coast of Gaul as "spoils of the sea" after abandoning a planned invasion of Britain.
- He engaged in scandalous and incestuous relationships with his three sisters, particularly Drusilla, whom he deified upon her death.
- He was assassinated by members of the Praetorian Guard led by Cassius Chaerea in a corridor beneath the palatine palaces.

## connections

- [[tiberius]] — his great-uncle and predecessor, whom Caligula was rumoured to have smothered to hasten his own succession.
- [[claudius]] — his uncle, whom he often publicly mocked, but who unexpectedly succeeded him as emperor.
- [[germanicus]] — his highly beloved father, whose legacy secured the early loyalty of the Roman legions for Caligula.
