# Lyndon B. Johnson

## summary

*Lyndon Baines Johnson* was the 36th president of the United States, serving from 1963 to 1969. Ascending to the presidency following John F. Kennedy's assassination, LBJ was a Texas Democrat and master legislator who championed the *Great Society*—an ambitious agenda of civil rights reform, anti-poverty programs, and federal aid to education. His presidency achieved landmark domestic legislation but was consumed and ultimately defined by the escalating Vietnam War, which divided the nation and forced him to announce he would not seek reelection in 1968.

## you gotta know

- Became president unexpectedly at age 55 following Kennedy's assassination on November 22, 1963; quickly moved to consolidate power and promote Kennedy's stalled legislation.
- Orchestrated passage of the *Civil Rights Act of 1964*, which banned racial discrimination in public accommodations; won the 1964 election in a landslide over Republican Barry Goldwater.
- Introduced the *Great Society* program, which included Medicare, Medicaid, federal aid to education (the Elementary and Secondary Education Act), the Head Start program, and the War on Poverty.
- Escalated American military involvement in Vietnam dramatically—from about 900 advisors when he took office to over 536,000 troops by 1968—following the *Gulf of Tonkin incident* (1964).
- Pushed through the *Voting Rights Act of 1965*, landmark legislation that empowered federal oversight of elections in states with histories of racial discrimination.
- Announced on March 31, 1968, during a televised address that he would not seek reelection, citing the divisiveness of the war; left office with his approval rating deeply damaged.
