# Lincoln's Second Inauguration

## summary

*Abraham Lincoln's* second inaugural address on March 4, 1865, stands as one of the greatest political speeches in American history, delivered with Union victory nearly assured. In just 703 words, Lincoln sought to explain the war's purpose and frame Reconstruction around reconciliation and charity toward the South: "malice toward none; with charity for all."

## you gotta know

- Took place March 4, 1865, with the Civil War's outcome no longer in doubt.
- The address is the shortest of any second-term inauguration in American history.
- Presented the war as divine judgment upon the nation for slavery, transcending partisan blame.
- Emphasized forgiveness and reconstruction without punishment: "malice toward none; with charity for all."
- Introduced the phrase "with firmness in the right, as God gives us to see the right," balancing moral clarity with humility.
- Refused to celebrate Northern triumph, instead urging mutual responsibility for healing.
- Lincoln was assassinated barely six weeks later on April 14, 1865, making this his final major public address.
