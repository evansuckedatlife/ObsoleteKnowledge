# Roald Amundsen

## summary

*Roald Amundsen* was a Norwegian explorer who achieved both the *Northwest Passage* and the *South Pole*, becoming the first person to reach the South Pole in December 1911. His systematic, methodical approach to polar exploration—including careful planning, use of dog sleds, and superior supply management—contrasted sharply with the traditions of European explorers and proved more effective in the harsh polar environment. His success made him an international hero, though it came at the cost of his rival Robert Falcon Scott's tragic failure.

## you gotta know

- First successfully navigated the *Northwest Passage* (1903–1906), confirming the existence of the route polar explorers had sought for centuries.
- Changed his expedition's destination from the Arctic to the South Pole after hearing that *Frederick Cook* and *Robert Peary* had already reached the North Pole.
- Led a five-man team to the *South Pole* in December 1911, beating *Robert Falcon Scott's* British expedition by about a month.
- Used dog teams extensively, following indigenous Inuit practices rather than the European tradition of man-hauling sleds, which proved far more efficient.
- Proved meticulous in pre-positioning supply depots along his route, allowing him to travel with minimal loads and preserve energy.
- Returned safely to civilization and spent years lecturing, writing, and conducting Arctic expeditions; his later expeditions sought the *North Pole* by airship.
- Disappeared in 1928 while attempting a rescue mission for an Arctic airship, dying near the *North Pole* he had spent years pursuing—a tragic end to an explorer's life.
