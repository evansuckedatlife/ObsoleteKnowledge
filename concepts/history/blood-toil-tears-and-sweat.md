## summary

Winston Churchill's maiden address as Prime Minister to Parliament on *May 13, 1940*, just days after taking office amid Nazi Germany's invasion of France, sets the tone for British wartime sacrifice. The speech offers no false hope — only a promise of *"blood, toil, tears and sweat"* — and became the keynote of Churchill's steely determination that made him the embodiment of British resistance.

## you gotta know

- Delivered on *May 13, 1940*, Churchill's first speech as Prime Minister, three days after assuming office.
- Opens with characteristic modesty: *"I have nothing to offer but blood, toil, tears and sweat,"* rejecting easy reassurance.
- Churchill declares the war's singular purpose: *"Victory at all costs, victory in spite of all terror, victory however long and hard the road may be."*
- Acknowledges the *"abyss of a new Dark Age"* if Britain fails, grounding the struggle in existential terms.
- Addresses the recent fall of France and Low Countries: *"Behind us gather a group of shattered states and bludgeoned races"* — appealing to lingering hope in European resistance.
- The speech explicitly rejects the previous government's *appeasement* policy without naming Chamberlain, signaling a complete strategic reversal.
- Established Churchill as the voice of grim, unflinching resolve — a tone he maintained throughout the war.
