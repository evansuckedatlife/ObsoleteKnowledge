# Māori

## summary

The *Māori* are the indigenous Polynesian people of New Zealand, with origins tracing to migrations from central Polynesia around the 13th century. They developed a complex society with distinct iwi (tribes), a rich oral tradition, and sophisticated knowledge systems including navigation, agriculture, and warfare before European contact in the 17th century.

## you gotta know

- Arrived in *New Zealand* in great migration voyages around the 1200s–1300s from *Hawaiki*, a legendary Polynesian homeland, using sophisticated wayfinding techniques.
- Speak *Te Reo Māori*, an Austronesian language; it was suppressed in schools for decades but has undergone significant revival since the 1980s.
- Traditionally organized into *iwi* (tribes) and *hapū* (sub-tribes) with distinct genealogies (*whakapapa*) and territorial rights; kinship and ancestry are central to identity.
- Developed *moa* hunting culture after arrival; hunted large flightless birds to extinction and adapted to New Zealand's unique ecology.
- Practiced *tā moko*, intricate facial tattoos unique to individuals, encoding genealogy and social status; still a living art form.
- Created fortified settlements called *pā* (hill forts) featuring sophisticated architecture and defensive design.
- *Haka* is a ceremonial war dance and cultural practice; the *Ka Mate* variant became globally known through the New Zealand rugby team.
