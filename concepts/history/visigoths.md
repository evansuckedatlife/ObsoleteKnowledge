# Visigoths

## summary

The *Visigoths* were a Germanic people who became the dominant power in the Iberian Peninsula after the Western Roman Empire's collapse, ruling Spain and Portugal from the 5th to 8th centuries. Under leaders like *Alaric II*, they established a sophisticated kingdom that blended Roman administrative structures with Germanic tradition, until Muslim conquest abruptly ended their reign in 711.

## you gotta know

- *Alaric I* sacked Rome in 410, one of the most shocking violations of the empire's sanctity and a symbolic marker of Rome's decline.
- Settled primarily in southern Gaul and the Iberian Peninsula after being displaced from Italy by the *Ostrogoths* and *Huns*.
- Under *Leovigild*, expanded their territory and consolidated power, though they struggled with religious conflict between Catholic and Arian factions.
- *Visigothic Code* (Liber Iudiciorum) was an early legal synthesis blending Roman law with Germanic custom, serving as a model for medieval European law.
- Fell to rapid Muslim invasion in 711; their kingdom fragmented and Christian remnants retreated to the north, beginning the centuries-long Reconquista.
- Produced remarkable scholarship and preserved much classical learning during their reign, evident in the work of *Isidore of Seville*.
- Internal civil war and succession disputes weakened the kingdom in the early 8th century, making it vulnerable to outside conquest.

## connections

- [[alaric]] — his sack of Rome made the Visigoths infamous in Roman memory.
- [[ostrogoths]] — rival Germanic power; both vied for dominance in the Mediterranean world.
- [[roman-empire]] — inherited and adapted its institutions; Visigothic law codes show direct Roman influence.
- [[iberian-peninsula]] — their primary settlement and power base from the 5th century onward.
- [[reconquista]] — their kingdom's conquest by Muslim armies launched centuries of Christian reconquest.
