# Gloria Steinem

## summary

*Gloria Steinem* (born 1934) is an American feminist activist, writer, and editor whose career spanned grassroots organizing, journalism, and media entrepreneurship. As co-founder of *Ms.* magazine, she brought feminist ideas into mainstream culture and remains one of the most recognizable faces of second-wave feminism.

## you gotta know

- Co-founded *Ms.* magazine in 1971, the first mainstream publication edited by and for women, which became a platform for feminist theory, culture, and activism.
- Before *Ms.*, worked as a writer and organizer for the women's liberation movement, contributing articles to major publications and speaking at hundreds of campus events.
- Became a national symbol of feminism through her wit, eloquence, and media savvy; famous for the line "A woman without a man is like a fish without a bicycle."
- Co-organized the Women's Strike for Equality (1970) and remained active in fights for reproductive rights, equal pay, and ending violence against women.
- Unlike some feminists, emphasized that feminism was about choice — that women should be free to work, raise children, or do both, as they decided.
- Continues writing and speaking into her 90s, reflecting on feminism's evolution and unfinished work.
