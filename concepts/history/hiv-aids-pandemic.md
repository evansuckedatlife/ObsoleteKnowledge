# HIV/AIDS Pandemic

## summary

The *HIV/AIDS pandemic* emerged in the early 1980s as a global health crisis, killing an estimated 40 to 45 million people since its onset through 2024. Caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), which attacks the immune system, AIDS initially devastated gay communities, intravenous drug users, and later spread to heterosexual populations, particularly in sub-Saharan Africa, forcing fundamental reckoning with stigma, sexuality, and health equity.

## you gotta know

- First identified in 1981 when clusters of *Pneumocystis jirovecii* pneumonia and Kaposi's sarcoma appeared in young gay men in Los Angeles.
- The virus (HIV) is believed to have jumped from non-human primates to humans in Central Africa, likely through bushmeat hunting in the early-to-mid 20th century.
- Transmitted through sexual contact, blood exposure, and mother-to-child transmission; initially spread rapidly through sexual networks and unscreened blood transfusions.
- By the early 1990s, AIDS was the leading cause of death for Americans aged 25–44; millions died without effective treatment during the 1980s and 1990s.
- The development of antiretroviral therapy (ART) beginning in 1996—particularly combination ART—transformed HIV from a death sentence into a chronic manageable condition.
- Sub-Saharan Africa was and remains disproportionately affected; at the pandemic's peak, over 7% of the population in countries like South Africa and Botswana were HIV-positive.
- Activists and affected communities (ACT UP, TAG) forced rapid drug development, transparency in clinical trials, and equity in treatment access—reshaping patient-researcher relationships.

## connections

- [[virology]] — HIV is a retrovirus that integrates into human genome, making it uniquely challenging to cure.
- [[antiretroviral-therapy]] — drug combinations developed to suppress viral replication; a major success in medical chemistry and pharmacology.
- [[public-health-policy]] — the pandemic exposed gaps in disease surveillance, equity, and international health coordination.
- [[lgbtq-history]] — the early pandemic's association with gay communities prompted both devastating stigma and unprecedented activism and community care.
- [[pandemic-response]] — modern pandemic preparedness frameworks partly emerged from HIV response lessons.
