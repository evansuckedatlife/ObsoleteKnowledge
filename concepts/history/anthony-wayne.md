## summary

*Anthony Wayne*, nicknamed "Mad Anthony" for his fearlessness and aggressive tactics, was one of the Continental Army's most daring combat leaders. He earned renown for his daring raids, his devotion to his troops, and his ruthless pursuit of British forces, becoming a symbol of American military audacity during the Revolution and its aftermath.

## you gotta know

- Rose from militia officer to major general through sheer combat talent and ferocity, serving under *George Washington* with distinction throughout the war.
- Commanded the Pennsylvania Line and earned the nickname "Mad Anthony" for his willingness to undertake dangerous missions and fight against overwhelming odds with unshakeable determination.
- Led the famous midnight bayonet assault on *Stony Point* (July 1779), a virtually impregnable British redoubt on the *Hudson River*, which he captured with minimal casualties through audacious tactics.
- Endured the horrors of *Valley Forge* alongside Washington and his troops, sharing their suffering and emerging as a beloved figure to the enlisted men.
- Fought in numerous engagements from the *Brandywine* campaign through the southern theater, accumulating a reputation for never refusing a dangerous order and always fighting to the last.
- After the war, led expeditions against Native Americans on the American frontier, most notably defeating a confederacy of tribes at *Fallen Timbers* (1794) to secure Ohio.
