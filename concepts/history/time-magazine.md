# Time Magazine

## summary

*Time* magazine, founded in 1923 by *Henry Luce* and *Briton Hadden*, revolutionized American journalism by condensing the week's news into a digestible, lively format. It pioneered the news-summary magazine concept and became the most influential publication of the twentieth century, shaping how millions understood current events.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1923 with the mission to bring news to the busy modern reader; first issue published on March 3, 1923.
- Created the distinctive *"Time style"* — short, snappy paragraphs, vivid reporting, and irreverent commentary that made news engaging rather than dry.
- Introduced the *"Man of the Year"* (later *"Person of the Year"*) tradition in 1927, naming outstanding figures of the previous year.
- Circulation climbed to over *3 million* by the 1950s, making it essential reading for educated Americans.
- Famous for innovative layouts, color photography, and the *"Timestyle"* red border on the cover that became instantly recognizable.
- Henry Luce built Time Inc., a media empire that later included *Life*, *Fortune*, and *Sports Illustrated*.
- Maintained a conservative editorial stance during the Cold War while claiming objectivity as a news digest.
