# Battle of Pharsalus

## summary

The *Battle of Pharsalus* (48 BC) was the decisive engagement of the Roman Civil War, where *Julius Caesar* crushed the forces of *Pompey the Great* in Thessaly. The victory established Caesar as Rome's undisputed leader and initiated the political upheaval that ended the Republic and paved the way for the Empire.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 48 BC near the city of Pharsalus in Thessaly, Greece.
- *Caesar* commanded approximately 23,000 legionaries; *Pompey* mustered roughly 45,000, including substantial cavalry and allied forces loyal to the optimates faction.
- Pompey's superior numbers and cavalry led him to expect an easy victory, but Caesar's tight discipline and unconventional tactics proved decisive.
- Caesar positioned his right flank obliquely and held a cavalry reserve to counter Pompey's superior mounted forces.
- When Pompey's cavalry charged, Caesar's hidden cavalry and cohorts destroyed them, then wheeled to attack Pompey's infantry from the flank and rear.
- Pompey's army collapsed; he himself fled Egypt and was eventually assassinated by the Egyptian king's order.
- The victory made Caesar dictator and effectively ended the optimates' power; his subsequent assassination sparked another civil war.
