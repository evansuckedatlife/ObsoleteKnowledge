# Margaret Thatcher

## summary

*Margaret Thatcher* (1925–2013) was Britain's first female Prime Minister and the architect of a radical rightward shift in British politics, combining free-market economics with strong nationalism. Her tenure (1979–1990) as the "Iron Lady" reshaped the British state, confronted Soviet power, and left a deeply polarizing legacy that still defines British politics.

## you gotta know

- Became PM at 53 and served for 11 years, the longest tenure of any British PM in the 20th century, second only to Walpole and Asquith historically.
- Dismantled state industries, curbed trade union power through confrontational labor legislation, and cut welfare programs—a revolution in economic philosophy that reversed the postwar consensus.
- Won the Falkland Islands War in 1982, reasserting British military power and recapturing a remote colony invaded by Argentina, consolidating her authority at a politically vulnerable moment.
- Cultivated a close alliance with US President Ronald Reagan, coordinating anti-Soviet strategy and defending Cold War deterrence even as détente was being negotiated elsewhere.
- Pioneered privatization as a tool of economic policy, selling state industries to private investors to reduce government spending and align Britain with market capitalism.
- Resigned in 1990 over the unpopular "poll tax" and internal party rebellion, ending an era but ensuring her dominance persisted in Conservative ideology for decades.
