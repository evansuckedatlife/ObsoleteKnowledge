# Dahomey → Benin

## summary

*Dahomey* was the historical kingdom and French colonial territory in West Africa, lasting from the 17th century until decolonization in 1960. The nation renamed itself *Benin* in 1975 under the revolutionary government of Mathieu Kérékou, honoring the *Benin Empire*, a powerful pre-colonial West African state centered in what is now Nigeria. The renaming reflected anti-colonial pride and a broader pan-African reorientation toward celebrating indigenous African heritage over colonial nomenclature.

## you gotta know

- The *Dahomey Kingdom* (c. 1625–1900) was a powerful West African state known for its military prowess, female warrior regiments, and involvement in the Atlantic slave trade.
- France colonized Dahomey in stages, making it a French protectorate by 1894 and a full colony by 1900, integrating it into *French West Africa*.
- Dahomey gained independence in 1960 as a French-speaking republic, but the name *Dahomey* perpetuated colonial associations with the old kingdom.
- In 1975, President *Mathieu Kérékou* instituted a socialist revolution and renamed the country *Benin*, after the historical *Benin Empire* of Nigeria.
- The *Benin Empire* was a powerful medieval and early-modern state (13th–19th centuries) centered in what is now southern Nigeria, not geographically in present-day Benin.
- The name change was part of a broader post-colonial gesture to reclaim African heritage; Kérékou's government also changed the capital from Porto-Novo to Cotonou.
