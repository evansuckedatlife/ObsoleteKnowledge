# Clement Attlee

## summary

*Clement Attlee* (1883–1967) was the unassuming Labour Prime Minister who fundamentally reshaped post-war Britain by building the modern welfare state and the NHS. His tenure (1945–1951) remains the most transformative for social policy in British history, establishing the principle that the state is responsible for the health and security of all citizens.

## you gotta know

- Won a landslide victory in 1945 over Churchill, the wartime hero, signaling a national desire for domestic reform over imperial grandeur.
- Created the National Health Service in 1948, establishing universal healthcare free at the point of use—a radical act that made medicine a public good rather than a commodity.
- Implemented sweeping nationalization of railways, coal, steel, and other key industries, making government ownership the explicit engine of postwar reconstruction.
- Expanded education through the Butler Act (1944) and created the welfare state's architecture: unemployment insurance, family allowances, old-age pensions coordinated into a comprehensive net.
- Oversaw Indian independence (1947), recognizing that the British Empire could not survive the war and that colonial holdings were indefensible—a pragmatic admission that accelerated decolonization.
- Served a second term (1950–1951) but lost momentum as the economy strained and rearmament costs rose, ceding power back to Churchill and the Conservatives.
