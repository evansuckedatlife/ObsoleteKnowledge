# Harper's Magazine

## summary

*Harper's Magazine*, founded in 1850, is one of the oldest continuously published American magazines. Known for its long-form journalism, political commentary, and literary fiction, it has served as a forum for serious discourse on American culture, politics, and ideas throughout the nation's transformations.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1850 as *Harper's New Monthly Magazine*; one of the first American magazines to achieve mass circulation.
- Early circulation exceeded *200,000*, unprecedented for a literary magazine; known for serializing Charles Dickens' works.
- Features *"The Easy Chair,"* a column of satirical essays dating back to 1851, making it one of journalism's longest-running features.
- Published major investigative journalism and social commentary; famous for exposing political corruption and social injustice.
- Editorial voice emphasized literary quality, progressive social reform, and intellectual engagement with American issues.
- Circulation declined in the twentieth century but maintained prestige as a journal of serious non-fiction and criticism.
- Known for its Harper's Index — monthly statistics and facts illustrating trends in American culture and politics.
