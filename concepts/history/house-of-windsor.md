# House of Windsor

## summary

The **House of Windsor** has ruled the United Kingdom and the Commonwealth since 1917, when the British royal family renamed itself from the *House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha* to distance itself from Germany during World War I. Modern and adaptable, the Windsors have navigated the decline of empire, social upheaval, and constitutional monarchy while preserving their institution and cultural prestige into the 21st century.

## you gotta know

- Renamed from the *House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha* by *King George V* in 1917 as a patriotic gesture during World War I, adopting the name of Windsor Castle.
- *George V* (r. 1910–1935) established a more restrained, conscientious style of monarchy after the flamboyance of his predecessor.
- *Edward VIII* (r. Jan–Dec 1936) abdicated in a constitutional crisis over his intention to marry *Wallis Simpson*, an American divorcée deemed unsuitable.
- *George VI* (r. 1936–1952), Edward's brother, restored dignity to the monarchy through World War II leadership and helped transition the *British Empire* to a *Commonwealth*.
- *Elizabeth II* (r. 1952–2022), the longest-reigning British monarch, presided over decolonization, social change, and the modernization of the monarchy while maintaining its ceremonial role.
- Adapted to democratic expectations and media scrutiny in ways earlier monarchies could not; the family became more visible and approachable.
- Reigns over the Commonwealth of Nations, with the sovereign as head of state in 14 countries beyond the UK as of the 21st century.
