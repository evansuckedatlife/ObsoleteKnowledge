## summary

*Manfred von Richthofen*, known as the *Red Baron*, was a German fighter pilot in World War I who became aviation's most famous combat flyer with 80 confirmed aerial victories. His scarlet aircraft made him instantly recognizable, and his tactical genius and lethal efficiency defined the age of aerial combat.

## you gotta know

- Recorded 80 confirmed kills, the highest of any World War I pilot—far exceeding most rivals.
- Painted his fighter bright red, making him easily visible but giving him psychological advantage over opponents.
- Flew the *Fokker Dr.I* triplane, a nimble aircraft suited to his aggressive dogfighting tactics.
- Shot down by Canadian or Australian ground fire near Amiens on April 21, 1918, near the war's end.
- Posthumously became a legend; his autobiography *The Red Fighter Pilot* became a bestseller.
- Represented the romanticism and honor of early aerial combat before tactics became more mechanical.
- Commanded a fighter squadron, demonstrating leadership and mentoring of younger pilots.
