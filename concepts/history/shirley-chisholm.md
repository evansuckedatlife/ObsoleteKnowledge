# Shirley Chisholm

## summary

*Shirley Chisholm* (1924–2005) was the first Black woman elected to Congress, representing Brooklyn from 1969 to 1983. A teacher, activist, and fiery orator, she challenged both racism and sexism, embodying the intersectional voice of the late-20th-century civil rights and women's movements.

## you gotta know

- First Black woman in the *House of Representatives*, elected in 1968 from New York's 12th district (Brooklyn).
- Ran for the Democratic presidential nomination in 1972 under the slogan "Unbought and Unbossed," making her the first Black candidate in a major-party primary.
- A former schoolteacher and early childhood education advocate who brought populist energy and moral clarity to legislative debates.
- Co-founded the *Congressional Black Caucus* in 1971 and fought vocally against the Vietnam War and poverty.
- Served 14 years in the House on education and agriculture committees, championing underserved communities and women's rights.
- Authored *Unbought and Unbossed* (1970), her influential memoir connecting her personal journey to the broader struggle for Black and women's liberation.
