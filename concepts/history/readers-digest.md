# Reader's Digest

## summary

*Reader's Digest*, founded in 1922 by *DeWitt Wallace* and *Lila Acheson Wallace*, revolutionized magazine publishing by condensing articles from other publications and making them accessible to a mass audience. Its editorial formula of brevity, optimism, and practical wisdom made it the best-selling magazine in American history.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1922; pioneered the *digest* concept — reprinting, condensing, and repackaging articles from hundreds of other sources.
- Circulation climbed to over *10 million* in the United States alone, and it was distributed in dozens of languages worldwide.
- Editorial philosophy emphasized uplifting, practical, and inspirational content: "how to" articles, human-interest stories, humor, and condensed books.
- Famous sections included *"Humor in Uniform,"* *"Toward More Picturesque Speech,"* and *"Most Unforgettable Character I've Met."*
- Aggressively marketed to rural and small-town America; became synonymous with middle-American values.
- Published condensed books — abridged versions of bestsellers — which became a major revenue stream and influenced American reading habits.
- Maintained a conservative, patriotic stance; avoided controversial social and political topics.
