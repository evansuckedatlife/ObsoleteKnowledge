# Assassination of John F. Kennedy

## summary

On November 22, 1963, President *John F. Kennedy* was shot and killed in *Dallas, Texas* during a presidential motorcade, allegedly by *Lee Harvey Oswald*. The assassination shocked the world and is among the most scrutinized events in American history, spawning decades of investigation, conspiracy theories, and debate. Kennedy's death marked the end of the brief, idealistic *Camelot* presidency and a cultural inflection point for the nation.

## you gotta know

### The Assassination
- Occurred November 22, 1963, in Dallas, Texas, during a motorcade through *Dealey Plaza*.
- Gunman: *Lee Harvey Oswald*, a former Marine who fired from the *Texas School Book Depository*.
- Kennedy was struck multiple times and pronounced dead at *Parkland Memorial Hospital* at 1 p.m. local time.
- Jackie Kennedy was in the car; her composed behavior and attempts to aid her dying husband were captured on film and etched into public memory.

### Investigation & Controversy
- The *Warren Commission* (1964) concluded Oswald acted alone, firing three shots from the Book Depository.
- Oswald was arrested but killed two days later by nightclub owner *Jack Ruby* while in police custody.
- The single-bullet theory (Oswald's second shot passed through both Kennedy and Governor Connally) remains disputed.
- The House Select Committee on Assassinations (1979) concluded a conspiracy was *likely*, reviving doubt about the Warren Commission's findings.

### Historical Impact
- Occurred during the *Cold War* and heightened U.S.-Soviet tensions; conspiracy theories swirl around Soviet, Cuban, or mob involvement.
- Funeral attended by world leaders; broadcasting of the assassination and the *Zapruder film* made it a shared national trauma.

## context

The Kennedy assassination is perhaps the defining example of how a single violent act can fracture a nation's narrative and spawn parallel historical interpretations. The existence of the *Zapruder film*—amateur footage of the shooting itself—made it the first major U.S. event whose details could be rewatched and analyzed infinitely, fueling both investigation and speculation. The event inaugurated the modern conspiracy-theory ecosystem and raised permanent questions about institutional transparency, the *Warren Commission's* credibility, and the limits of forensic certainty. For quiz-bowl purposes, it is inseparable from Cold War anxieties, the 1960s cultural shift, and the mythology of the Kennedy family.
