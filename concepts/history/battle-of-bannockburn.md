# Battle of Bannockburn

## summary

The *Battle of Bannockburn* was fought on 23–24 June 1314 near Stirling in Scotland, where *Robert the Bruce*'s Scottish forces defeated a much larger English army led by *Edward II*. The victory secured Scottish independence after decades of conflict and established Scotland as a sovereign kingdom, with profound consequences for the British Isles.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the River Bannock, south of Stirling Castle; English forces numbered roughly 20,000 while Scottish forces were estimated at 8,000–12,000.
- *Robert the Bruce*, Scotland's king, positioned his troops on a bog and steep terrain that negated the English numerical advantage and cavalry superiority.
- The Scottish schiltron formation—tightly packed pikemen bristling with long spears—broke English cavalry charges and inflicted devastating casualties.
- The English defeat forced *Edward II* to abandon the invasion and accept Scottish independence through the *Treaty of Edinburgh* (1328).
- The victory became a symbol of Scottish national identity and remains celebrated as one of the great upsets in medieval military history.
- *Robert the Bruce* became a legendary figure in Scotland, securing his place as the architect of national independence.
