# Charlemagne

## summary

*Charlemagne* (c. 748–814), king of the Franks and crowned emperor of the Romans, unified much of Western Europe and laid the foundation for the later kingdoms of France and Germany. His reign marked a cultural renaissance and the re-establishment of centralized authority after Rome's collapse.

## you gotta know

- Crowned *Emperor of the Romans* by Pope Leo III on Christmas Day 800, the first such coronation since the fall of the Western Roman Empire, claiming a restored empire spanning from the Pyrenees to the Danube.
- Conquered and subdued Saxony after brutal campaigns, expanding Frankish territory and Christianizing the pagan peoples of Northern Europe.
- Established the *Carolingian Renaissance*, sponsoring scholars, monasteries, and the standardization of Latin script (*Carolingian minuscule*) that preserved classical texts.
- Convened councils at his palace in *Aachen*, creating centers of learning and reforming the clergy and ecclesiastical structure across his realm.
- Inherited a fragmented kingdom from his father Pepin III and transformed it into the largest Western European empire since antiquity.
- His empire fractured within a generation; the Treaty of Verdun (843) partitioned it into what would become France, Germany, and Italy.
