# Clovis

## summary

*Clovis* (c. 466–511), the first king of the Franks, conquered most of Roman Gaul and established the Frankish kingdom that would become France. His conversion to Catholic Christianity and his military victories transformed him into the founder of both a realm and a Christendom.

## you gotta know

- Reigned c. 481–511; expanded Frankish territory from the lower Rhine to the Pyrenees, consolidating his power over most of what is now France.
- Converted to Catholic Christianity, allegedly after his wife Clotilda convinced him to invoke Christ during the Battle of Tolbiac (c. 496); this Christianization made him an ally of the Roman ecclesiastical establishment.
- Defeated the Visigoths at the Battle of Vouillé (507), eliminating a competing Germanic kingdom and extending Frankish dominion to Spain's borders.
- Established his capital at Paris, transforming it from a minor Roman city into the seat of Frankish power and a symbol of Christian kingship.
- Murdered several of his own sons and kinsmen to consolidate power, a pattern of ruthlessness that became common among the Merovingians.
- His reign marked the end of Roman Gaul and the beginning of a Germanic-Christian kingdom that would eventually become medieval France.
