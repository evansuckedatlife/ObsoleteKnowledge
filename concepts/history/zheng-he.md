# Zheng He

## summary

*Zheng He* was a 15th-century Chinese admiral and explorer who commanded massive maritime expeditions across the Indian Ocean, establishing Chinese dominance in Asian waters and discovering distant lands on behalf of the *Ming Dynasty*. His fleets dwarfed contemporary European explorers' vessels, and his voyages demonstrated Chinese maritime superiority—yet China abandoned ocean exploration shortly after his death, ceding the seas to European powers and reshaping the global balance of power.

## you gotta know

- Rose from slavery to become a trusted eunuch-admiral at the court of the *Yongle Emperor* of the *Ming Dynasty*.
- Commanded seven massive expeditions across the *Indian Ocean* between 1405 and 1433, sailing with fleets of over 300 ships carrying thousands of men, tribute collectors, and scientists.
- His largest vessels, the "treasure ships," were reputedly over 400 feet long—dwarfing Columbus's *Santa María* by a factor of five or more.
- Sailed to *East Africa*, the *Arab Peninsula*, and the *Indian Ocean* coast, establishing Chinese prestige and gathering tribute from dozens of foreign kingdoms.
- Brought back exotic animals (giraffes, ostriches, zebras), plants, and spices; his expeditions expanded Chinese geographical knowledge and demonstrated maritime power.
- Mapped and charted routes and lands; his detailed maps influenced cartography throughout Asia and reached the Middle East.
- After his death, the *Ming Dynasty* dismantled the treasure fleet and turned inward, abandoning maritime exploration; within decades, the Indian Ocean became dominated by European powers instead.
