# John Hay

## summary

*John Hay* was Secretary of State under President Theodore Roosevelt from 1901 to 1905, a pivotal figure in establishing American dominance in the Western Hemisphere and the Pacific. His diplomacy brought the United States into the First Hague Peace Conference, negotiated the construction of the Panama Canal, and formulated the Open Door Policy toward China—foundational principles of American imperialism at the turn of the 20th century.

## you gotta know

- Formulated the *Open Door Policy* (1899–1900), asserting American commercial interests in China against European partition and Japanese expansion.
- Negotiated the *Hay-Pauncefote Treaty* (1901) with Britain, clearing the way for American construction of the *Panama Canal*.
- Secured the *Panama Canal Zone* (1903) through support for Panama's separation from Colombia, enabling U.S. control of the strategic waterway.
- Supported American intervention in the *Boxer Rebellion* (1900), protecting American missionaries and commercial interests in China.
- Mediated an end to the *Russo-Japanese War* (1904–1905), winning the Nobel Peace Prize and establishing American diplomatic weight in Asian affairs.
- Believed the United States should expand commerce and influence globally while remaining largely aloof from European entanglements.
