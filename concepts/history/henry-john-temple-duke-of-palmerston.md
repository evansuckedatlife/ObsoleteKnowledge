# Henry John Temple, 3rd Viscount Palmerston

## summary

*Henry John Temple, 3rd Viscount Palmerston* (1784–1865) embodied mid-Victorian assertiveness as Foreign Secretary and Prime Minister, pursuing aggressive gunboat diplomacy to expand British interests and reshape international law. His tenure at the Foreign Office (1830–1841, 1846–1851) and as PM (1855–1858, 1859–1865) made him one of the most recognizable statesmen of his era.

## you gotta know

- Dominated British foreign policy for three decades, intervening in Portugal, Greece, and the Ottoman Empire to extend British influence and weaken rivals.
- Became a national hero after the Opium War (1840–1842), which he championed as Foreign Secretary to open Chinese markets to British trade, even though it was internationally condemned as imperialism.
- Led Britain through the Crimean War (1853–1856), fighting Russia to prop up the Ottoman Empire and prevent Russian expansion toward India.
- His phrase "might is right" captured the philosophy that British naval supremacy and willingness to use force were the supreme tools of diplomacy.
- Backed independence movements against autocratic powers in Italy and the Balkans, positioning Britain as the defender of Protestant nationalism and liberal institutions.
- Died in office in 1865, still PM at 80, and was mourned as the last embodiment of British dominance unchallenged by rival powers.
