# Battle of Bosworth Field

## summary

The *Battle of Bosworth Field* was fought on 22 August 1485 in Leicestershire, England, where *Henry Tudor*'s forces defeated *Richard III* and the Yorkist army. The victory ended the *Wars of the Roses* and established the *Tudor dynasty*, fundamentally reshaping English politics and initiating a new era of national stability and cultural flourishing.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the village of Bosworth in central England; *Richard III* commanded the incumbent Yorkist forces while *Henry Tudor* led the opposing Lancastrian and allied forces.
- *Richard III*, the last Plantagenet king, was killed in the battle—accounts suggest he died charging *Henry Tudor* and was stripped and discarded on the field, symbolising the end of the Plantagenet line.
- *Henry Tudor*'s victory allowed him to crown himself king as *Henry VII*, marry *Elizabeth of York* (uniting the warring factions), and establish the Tudor dynasty.
- The battle ended decades of civil strife known as the *Wars of the Roses*, restoring central royal authority and initiating a period of relative stability and cultural growth.
- Key noble defections during the battle, particularly the surprise withdrawal of the Stanley family from Richard's side, contributed decisively to the Yorkist defeat.
- The victory became a symbolic threshold: the ending of medieval feudalism and the rise of the Tudor dynasty, which would produce *Elizabeth I* and transform England into a global power.
