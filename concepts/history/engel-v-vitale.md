# Engel v. Vitale

## summary

*Engel v. Vitale* (1962) held that school-sponsored prayer violates the Establishment Clause of the First Amendment, even when the prayer is nondenominational and participation is voluntary. The landmark decision made clear that government cannot endorse or promote prayer.

## you gotta know

- New York's Board of Regents composed a "nondenominational" prayer recommending it to public schools for recitation by students each morning.
- Parents of students sued, arguing the prayer violated their First Amendment rights by promoting religion.
- Justice *Hugo Black* wrote that the *Establishment Clause* forbids government composition and sponsorship of prayers, regardless of their content or nondenominational nature.
- The Court held that even a brief prayer at the start of the school day, with voluntary participation, is unconstitutional government religious speech.
- The decision provoked fierce backlash from religious conservatives who saw it as an attack on faith in schools.
- Became the foundation for cases striking down school prayer, student-led prayer at graduations, and other school-sponsored religious exercises.
