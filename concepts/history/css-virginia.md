# CSS Virginia

## summary

**CSS Virginia**, the Confederate ironclad built from the salvaged hull of the Union frigate *USS Merrimack*, devastated Union wooden warships at the Battle of Hampton Roads until checked by the Union ironclad *USS Monitor*. Though not ultimately a strategic success for the Confederacy, her engagement with Monitor fundamentally transformed naval warfare.

## you gotta know

- Constructed by the Confederacy from the burned hull of *USS Merrimack*, a Union steam frigate scuttled in Norfolk harbor, with armor plating built from railroad iron over wooden backing.
- On March 8, 1862, attacked the Union blockade fleet at Hampton Roads, destroying the wooden frigate *USS Cumberland* and the sloop *USS Congress* in a shocking display of ironclad superiority.
- The next day (March 9), engaged *USS Monitor* in a historic duel that lasted four hours with neither ship able to decisively damage the other, ending her dominance of the roadstead.
- Withdrawn by Confederate forces and eventually scuttled in May 1862 when Union forces captured Norfolk, denying her to the enemy.
- Her maiden action proved that ironclad warships could overwhelm traditional wooden navies, accelerating armament races worldwide.
- The combined Hampton Roads engagement marks the definitive end of the wooden warship era in naval history.
