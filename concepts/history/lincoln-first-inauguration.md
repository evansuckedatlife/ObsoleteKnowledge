# Lincoln's First Inauguration

## summary

*Abraham Lincoln's* inaugural address on March 4, 1861, came as the nation teetered on the brink of dissolution, with seven Southern states already seceded. Lincoln appealed to "the better angels of our nature," repudiated secession as unconstitutional, yet promised not to interfere with slavery where it already existed—a delicate balance that within weeks gave way to the Civil War.

## you gotta know

- Took place March 4, 1861, as the secession crisis reached its climax.
- Lincoln warned that no state had the right to unilaterally dissolve the Union.
- Pledged to maintain federal authority and defend federal forts, a commitment that would lead to Fort Sumter.
- Invoked "the better angels of our nature," calling for reconciliation despite sectional rage.
- Promised not to disturb slavery in the states where it existed (repeating his campaign position).
- Was interrupted by rumors and reports of Confederate attack plans, reflecting national instability.
- Set the tone for his presidency: constitutional authority, moral clarity on union, pragmatism on slavery.
