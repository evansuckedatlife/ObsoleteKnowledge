# Miguel Hidalgo

## summary

*Miguel Hidalgo* was a Mexican Catholic priest whose 1810 *Grito de Dolores* (Cry of Dolores) launched the Mexican War of Independence against Spanish colonial rule. Though executed before independence was won, he became the symbolic father of Mexican nationhood.

## you gotta know

- On September 16, 1810, rang the church bell in Dolores and issued the *Grito de Dolores*, calling for rebellion against Spanish rule.
- Mobilised tens of thousands of indigenous and mestizo peasants in the Bajío region, creating a mass independence movement.
- Abolished slavery and forced labour in territories under his control, positioning independence as a social revolution.
- Was captured, tried for heresy, and executed by firing squad in Chihuahua in July 1811, becoming a martyr.
- His declaration that day is celebrated annually on *Día de la Independencia* (September 16), Mexico's national holiday.
- Though he did not live to see independence (won in 1821), his rebellion fundamentally transformed Mexican society and consciousness.
