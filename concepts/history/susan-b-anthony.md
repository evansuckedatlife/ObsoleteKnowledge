# Susan B. Anthony

## summary

*Susan B. Anthony* (1820–1906) was an American women's rights activist and temperance advocate who spent more than 50 years fighting for women's suffrage. Her partnership with *Elizabeth Cady Stanton* shaped the first wave of American feminism, and she became the public face of the suffrage movement until her death, fourteen years before the Nineteenth Amendment passed.

## you gotta know

- Co-founded the National Woman Suffrage Association with *Elizabeth Cady Stanton* in 1869, after tensions within the broader women's movement over tactics and alliances.
- Traveled extensively giving speeches across America and internationally, becoming the primary public orator and organizer of the suffrage cause.
- In 1872, illegally voted in the presidential election in Rochester, New York; was arrested, convicted, and fined $100, which she refused to pay.
- Co-authored *The History of Woman Suffrage* (a three-volume work with Stanton and Matilda Joslyn Gage), the authoritative record of the movement.
- Called for a federal amendment to grant women the vote (what became the Nineteenth Amendment, ratified in 1920, four years after her death).
- Never married, dedicating her entire adult life to the cause; late in life said she had "not a regret except that I have not lived long enough to vote."
