# Emiliano Zapata

## summary

*Emiliano Zapata* was a Mexican agrarian revolutionary leader whose 1911 *Plan de Ayala* became the moral centre of the Mexican Revolution. His cry of *"Land and Liberty"* mobilised southern peasants and campesinos against the Porfiriato's hacienda system.

## you gotta know

- Led peasant armies in the state of Morelos, fighting for the return of communal lands (*ejidos*) stolen during the Porfiriato.
- Issued the *Plan de Ayala* (1911), demanding immediate land redistribution and the dissolution of haciendas—more radical than Madero's reforms.
- His famous slogan *"Tierra y Libertad"* (Land and Liberty) became the rallying cry for agrarian reform across Mexico.
- Rejected both *Madero* and later leaders as insufficiently radical, remaining in armed rebellion for a decade.
- Assassinated in 1919 by a political rival under ambiguous circumstances; became a legendary martyr of the revolution.
- His agrarian program directly inspired Article 27 of the 1917 Mexican Constitution, which mandates land reform.

## connections

- [[porfirio-diaz]] — the authoritarian whose land policies Zapata fought against.
- [[francisco-madero]] — the revolutionary whom Zapata initially supported but later opposed as too conservative.
- [[pancho-villa]] — northern revolutionary leader; the two represented different wings of the revolution.
- [[mexican-revolution]] — the upheaval Zapata shaped through his radical agrarian agenda.
- [[lázaro-cardenas]] — later president who finally implemented large-scale land redistribution following Zapata's vision.
