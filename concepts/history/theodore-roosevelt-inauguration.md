# Theodore Roosevelt's Inauguration

## summary

*Theodore Roosevelt's* second inauguration on March 4, 1905, confirmed his ascent to the presidency in his own right after assuming office following McKinley's 1901 assassination. At 46, he delivered an address emphasizing America's role as a world power, individual duty, and the Square Deal—principles that would guide his presidency and reshape American progressivism.

## you gotta know

- Took place March 4, 1905, his election in 1904 making him the first president elected solely in his own right after succession.
- Emphasized the Square Deal ideology: fair labor, conservation, and corporate regulation balancing capital and labor.
- Declared America's duty as a great power to maintain order and civilization in its sphere of influence.
- Highlighted conservation of natural resources, a signature Roosevelt initiative.
- Called for honest dealing in government and business, contrasting with Gilded Age corruption.
- Emphasized personal vigor and national strength, reflecting his famous philosophy: "speak softly and carry a big stick."
- The address positioned the United States as a global imperial power maintaining order, especially in Latin America and the Pacific.
