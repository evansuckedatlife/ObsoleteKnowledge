# Robert Hanssen

## summary

*Robert Hanssen* was an FBI counterintelligence officer who spied for the Soviet Union and Russia for over twenty years, making him the most damaging mole in FBI history. Arrested in 2001, he exposed American intelligence operations and agent identities, rivaling Aldrich Ames in the scope and sensitivity of his betrayals.

## you gotta know

- Joined the FBI in 1976 and rose through the ranks to become a senior counterintelligence specialist with access to the agency's most sensitive secrets about Soviet and Russian operations.
- Began spying for the Soviet Union in 1979; motivated initially by financial hardship and later by ideology and a desire for status; received approximately $1.4 million in payments over his espionage career.
- Worked in the FBI's Soviet/Russian counterintelligence section, giving Moscow advance warning of FBI operations, the names of Russian double agents, and technical surveillance capabilities.
- His betrayals included compromising the identity of *Dmitri Polyakov*, a senior KGB officer working as an American spy, who was executed by Moscow; the loss of such assets crippled American intelligence gathering.
- Remained undetected for over twenty years, even after the FBI began investigating a mole in the agency in the 1980s; finally caught in 2001 when a Russian defector provided evidence of his treason.
- Sentenced to life imprisonment without parole in 2002; remains incarcerated, having never explained his motivations with clarity or shown remorse.
