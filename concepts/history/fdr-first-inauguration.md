## summary

*Franklin Delano Roosevelt's* inaugural address on March 4, 1933, occurred at the nadir of the Great Depression, with unemployment near 25% and the banking system collapsing. His famous declaration that "the only thing we have to fear is fear itself" became the symbolic rallying cry of American resilience, setting the tone for his activist New Deal presidency.

## you gotta know

- Took place March 4, 1933, exactly four years after Herbert Hoover's last inauguration.
- Introduced the phrase "the only thing we have to fear is fear itself," defining the Depression era's psychology.
- Promised aggressive executive action and called for "broad executive power to wage a war against the emergency."
- Warned that if Congress refused to act, he would seek "the only instrument of my choice: the broad executive power which would be given to me if we were invaded by a foreign foe."
- Blamed the Depression's severity on the selfishness of financial and banking leadership.
- Signaled an unprecedented expansion of federal power and executive action, beginning the New Deal.
- Over 100,000 people gathered on the National Mall, one of the largest inauguration crowds to that date.
