# Lombards

## summary

The *Lombards* were a Germanic people who invaded and settled northern Italy in 568, establishing a kingdom that lasted over two centuries and became one of the more durable and culturally sophisticated of the post-Roman Germanic states. They developed the first medieval Italian political structure, leaving a lasting mark on the peninsula's history and fragmenting it into competing regional powers.

## you gotta know

- Invaded Italy in 568 under *Alboin*, conquering the Po Valley and much of the peninsula after the chaos of the Gothic Wars had weakened Byzantine hold.
- Established a decentralized kingdom ruled by dukes in different regions, creating the first medieval Italian political fragmentation that would persist for centuries.
- Gradually adopted Roman law and Catholic Christianity, blending Germanic and Roman institutions into a hybrid kingdom that served as a bridge to medieval Europe.
- Produced remarkable legal documents, including the *Edict of Rothari*, a written law code showing sophisticated administration and legal thought.
- Their kingdom was eventually conquered by *Charlemagne* (774), ending Lombard independence and bringing Italy under Frankish (later Holy Roman) authority.
- Unlike the *Ostrogoths* or *Visigoths*, the Lombards did not vanish entirely; they were absorbed into the Frankish sphere and their cultural influence persisted in northern Italian society.
- Controlled key Alpine passes and trade routes, making them economically important to early medieval Europe.

## connections

- [[theodoric]] — earlier Germanic ruler of Italy whose model influenced Lombard administration.
