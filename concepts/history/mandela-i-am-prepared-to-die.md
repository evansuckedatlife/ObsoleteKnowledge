# I Am Prepared to Die

## summary

Nelson Mandela's statement from the dock at the Rivonia Trial on *April 20, 1964*, articulated his rationale for armed resistance to apartheid and his vision of a non-racial democracy, while accepting the likelihood of a death sentence. The speech transformed a criminal trial into a moral reckoning with apartheid and established Mandela as the conscience of the anti-apartheid movement, even as he entered decades of imprisonment.

## you gotta know

- Delivered at the *Rivonia Trial* in *April 1964*, where Mandela and others were charged with sabotage and conspiracy.
- Mandela's concluding assertion: *"I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve; but if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die."*
- Mandela explains the ANC's turn to violence: previous peaceful petitions and protests had been ignored or suppressed by the apartheid state.
- He distinguishes between sabotage (property) and terrorism (killing civilians), locating the ANC's actions within a moral framework.
- Mandela's vision is explicitly non-racial: *"the African majority will be governed"* — not that Africans will dominate.
- The trial was broadcast and widely reported; Mandela's statement reached a global audience and became a rallying point for anti-apartheid movements.
- Despite the speech's eloquence, Mandela received a life sentence and remained imprisoned for 27 years.
