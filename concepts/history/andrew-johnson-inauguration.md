## summary

*Andrew Johnson's* assumption of the presidency on April 15, 1865, occurred amid the wreckage of Lincoln's assassination and the Civil War's immediate aftermath. A Tennessee tailor with little formal education and deep regional prejudices, Johnson's approach to Reconstruction would prove far harsher on freedmen and more lenient toward the South than Lincoln's vision implied, shaping decades of subsequent conflict.

## you gotta know

- Took place April 15, 1865, immediately following Lincoln's assassination the previous evening.
- Johnson had not attended Lincoln's second inauguration; he was notified of the presidency by telegram while traveling.
- Gave no formal inaugural address, instead issuing brief, hastily drafted remarks.
- His Reconstruction policies favored rapid Southern readmission with minimal protections for freedmen.
- Opposed the 14th Amendment and vetoed civil rights legislation, setting him at odds with Republican Congress.
- Openly held racist views and opposed voting rights for Black Americans.
- Impeached in 1868 on charges stemming from his conflict with Congressional Republicans over Reconstruction policy.

## connections

- [[andrew-johnson]] — the principal figure, assuming presidency under traumatic circumstances.

- [[freedmen-bureau]] — established under Lincoln but reformed under Johnson's opposition.
