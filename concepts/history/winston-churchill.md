# Winston Churchill

## summary

*Winston Churchill* (1874–1965) was the paramount wartime leader of the 20th century, rallying Britain to resist Nazi Germany when defeat seemed imminent. His oratory, combative resolve, and romantic vision of British power became synonymous with defiance and leadership, and he returned to the premiership in peacetime (1951–1955) as the elder statesman of the Cold War.

## you gotta know

- Became PM in May 1940 when Nazi Germany was advancing across Europe and France was collapsing; his refusal to negotiate with Hitler and his rhetoric of defiance stiffened British resolve in the nation's darkest hour.
- Forged the "Special Relationship" with the United States, convincing President Roosevelt to support Britain before America entered the war and securing the Atlantic alliance that defeated Nazi Germany.
- Coined the term "Iron Curtain" in a 1946 speech to describe Soviet domination of Eastern Europe, helping to define Cold War rhetoric and justify Western rearmament.
- Served as PM again from 1951 to 1955, presiding over Britain's adjustment to its diminished postwar status and the loss of empire, particularly the independence of India.
- Won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1953 for his massive multi-volume history of the Second World War, a work of strategic analysis wrapped in grand narrative.
- Remains the most recognizable British statesman of the modern era, iconic as both a warrior and a man of letters.
