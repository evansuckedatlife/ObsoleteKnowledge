# Emmeline Pankhurst

## summary

*Emmeline Pankhurst* (1858–1928) was a British suffragist and leader of the Women's Social and Political Union (WSPU), which pioneered militant tactics including arson, bombing, and property destruction to demand women's voting rights. Her movement made suffrage impossible to ignore in Edwardian Britain and shaped the global second wave of feminism.

## you gotta know

- Founded the Women's Social and Political Union (WSPU) in 1903, transforming the British suffrage movement from petitions and speeches into direct action.
- Adopted the slogan "Deeds not words," organizing dramatic public demonstrations, including heckling politicians, disrupting Parliament, and chaining herself to railings.
- Between 1909 and 1914, orchestrated campaigns of property destruction—arson against pillar boxes, breaking windows in government buildings, bombs—to draw international attention to the cause.
- Was arrested multiple times; endured force-feeding in prison after hunger strikes, an experience that horrified the public and became a moral rallying point.
- Suspended the WSPU's militant campaign during World War I, pivoting to patriotic support; controversy over this decision fractured the movement.
- Saw partial victory in 1918 (suffrage for women over 30) and complete victory in 1928, just before her death; she lived to vote.

## connections

- [[suffrage]] — made voting rights for women a moral and political impossibility to ignore.
- [[womens-rights]] — her militant tactics influenced feminist movements worldwide.
- [[civil-disobedience]] — pioneered the strategic use of illegality and prison to amplify a political message.
- [[british-politics]] — her campaigns transformed electoral politics and forced the government to respond.
