# Battle of Saipan

## summary

Fought June–July 1944 in the Mariana Islands, the Battle of Saipan saw American forces capture a strategic island within striking distance of Japan itself. The battle broke Japanese will to defend further west and enabled B-29 Superfortress bombers to reach Japanese cities; it also prompted a major Japanese carrier battle that resulted in further devastation to Japanese aviation.

## you gotta know

- Island campaign lasted 25 days; Japanese garrison of approximately 29,000 soldiers led by General Yoshitsugu Saito.
- American assault force: 67,000 troops of the Army and Marines under Vice Admiral Richmond Kelly Turner.
- U.S. casualties: 3,426 killed, 10,364 wounded; Japanese casualties: approximately 27,000 killed (very few surrendered).
- Saipan civilian population estimated at 30,000; over 20,000 civilians jumped to their deaths from cliffs to avoid capture, a mass tragedy remembered as the "Banzai Cliff."
- Coincided with the *Battle of the Philippine Sea* (June 19–20), where American carrier forces annihilated remaining Japanese naval aviation.
- Saipan's airfields granted B-29 bombers a base within range of Japanese home islands, especially Tokyo.
- Marked the first major battle on an island with large native population; demonstrated difficulties of amphibious assault against determined defense.
