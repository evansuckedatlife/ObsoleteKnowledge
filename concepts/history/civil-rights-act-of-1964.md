## summary

The *Civil Rights Act of 1964* was a landmark federal law passed by the United States Congress that prohibited discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin. Signed by President *Lyndon B. Johnson*, it was the most comprehensive civil rights legislation in American history, outlawing segregation in public accommodations (hotels, restaurants, theaters), mandating school desegregation, protecting voting rights, and establishing the *Equal Employment Opportunity Commission* to combat workplace discrimination. The Act represented the culmination of the Civil Rights Movement's nonviolent direct action campaign and fundamentally transformed American law and society.

## you gotta know

- The Act was introduced by President *Lyndon B. Johnson* following the assassination of President *John F. Kennedy* and the historic *March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom* (1963), where *Martin Luther King Jr.* delivered his "I Have a Dream" speech.
- *Title II* of the Act prohibited discrimination in public accommodations—hotels, restaurants, theaters, and other businesses offering services to the public—a direct response to *sit-in* campaigns that had challenged segregation in the South.
- *Title III* authorized the federal government and the *Attorney General* to initiate litigation to desegregate public schools, providing federal enforcement power for school integration previously required by the *Brown v. Board of Education* decision (1954).
- *Title VI* cut off federal funding to any institution or program practicing discrimination, giving federal agencies powerful leverage to enforce desegregation in schools, hospitals, and other federally-funded institutions.
- *Title VII* established the *Equal Employment Opportunity Commission* (EEOC) to investigate complaints of employment discrimination and enforce prohibitions against discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.
- The Act mandated the collection of voting statistics to document racial discrimination in voter registration, supporting the subsequent *Voting Rights Act of 1965*, which directly addressed Southern voter suppression of Black Americans.
- The Act's passage required overcoming a Southern Democratic *filibuster* in the Senate, with *Senate Majority Leader Hubert Humphrey* and Republican Senator *Everett Dirksen* building a cross-party coalition to secure the necessary votes.
- *Title IX* (added 1972) extended the Act's prohibition of discrimination to education, mandating equality in school athletics and academic programs, becoming the foundation for gender equality in sports.
- The Act's passage represented a pivotal moment in the *Civil Rights Movement*, validating the strategy of nonviolent direct action and legislative advocacy pursued by *Martin Luther King Jr.* and others.
- Enforcement of the Act was uneven and contested: Southern states and private individuals resisted integration through *massive resistance* campaigns, and subsequent court decisions narrowed some of the Act's protections, but it remained foundational to American civil rights law.
