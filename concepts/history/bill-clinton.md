# Bill Clinton

## summary

Bill Clinton (born 1946) was the 42nd President of the United States, serving from 1993 to 2001. The first president born after World War II and the second to face impeachment, he presided over the post-Cold War era, economic prosperity, and a series of foreign interventions in the Balkans and elsewhere. His presidency is remembered both for economic achievement and for moral and legal controversy stemming from his affair with Monica Lewinsky.

## you gotta know

- Governor of Arkansas (1979–1981, 1983–1992); portrayed himself as a "New Democrat" bridging old party divisions; won the 1992 election with 43% of the vote against an incumbent George H.W. Bush weakened by recession.
- Signed the *North American Free Trade Agreement* (NAFTA) in 1993, expanding continental trade and becoming a pillar of his economic agenda, though it angered labor unions.
- Passed the *Family and Medical Leave Act*, expanded the *Earned Income Tax Credit*, and signed welfare reform (1996) that replaced guaranteed assistance with time limits and work requirements, reflecting his "New Democrat" pragmatism.
- Presided over a period of economic expansion, budget surpluses, and declining unemployment; the "dot-com boom" created a sense of prosperity, though underlying financial risks accumulated.
- Intervened militarily in Somalia (1992–1993), Bosnia (1995), and Kosovo (1999), establishing a pattern of humanitarian and strategic interventionism but with mixed results and criticism of American imperialism.
- Failed to respond effectively to the Rwandan genocide (1994), a humanitarian catastrophe in which 800,000 Tutsi were killed; his inaction became a defining regret.
- Conducted an affair with White House intern Monica Lewinsky (1995–1997); denied it under oath ("I did not have sexual relations..."); impeached by the House (1998) for perjury and obstruction of justice, but acquitted by the Senate.
- Re-elected in 1996 with 49% of the vote; left office with high approval ratings despite the scandal, the economy still expanding.
