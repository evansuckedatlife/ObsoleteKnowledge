# Ferdinand Magellan

## summary

*Ferdinand Magellan* was a Portuguese explorer who led the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe, though he died before its completion. Born around 1480, Magellan sailed for the Spanish crown, seeking a westward route to the Spice Islands. His voyage (1519–1522) proved the true scope of Earth's oceans, dramatically enlarged the known world, and established Spain's colonial claim to the Philippines. Though Magellan himself perished in the Philippines, his expedition's return to Spain was the first to complete the circuit of the world.

## you gotta know

- Born in Portugal, served the Portuguese crown in India and Malaysia, but quarreled with the Portuguese king and offered his services to Spain, where he persuaded King Charles V to fund a westward expedition to the Spice Islands.
- Departed Spain in September 1519 with five ships and roughly 270 men; discovered the *Strait of Magellan* at the southern tip of South America, a crucial waterway allowing passage to the Pacific Ocean.
- Named the Pacific Ocean *Mar Pacifico* (the Peaceful Sea) for its apparent calm after months at sea; crossed the vast ocean largely without resupply, enduring starvation and scurvy.
- Reached the Philippines in March 1521; was killed on April 27, 1521, during a skirmish on the island of Mactan against indigenous chief Lapu-Lapu in a battle over Spanish authority.
- Though Magellan died, his expedition continued under the command of *Juan Sebastián Elcano*, who returned one ship to Spain in 1522 with 18 survivors—the first to complete a circumnavigation.
- The expedition's achievement proved Earth was far larger than previously believed, established the vastness of the Pacific, and demonstrated the feasibility of global sea routes, reshaping navigation and geography forever.
