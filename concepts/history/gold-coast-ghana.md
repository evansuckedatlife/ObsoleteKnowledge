# Gold Coast → Ghana

## summary

*Gold Coast* was the British colonial territory in West Africa, named for the region's historical abundance of gold and its status as a prime trading post for European merchants since the 15th century. Upon independence in 1957, *Kwame Nkrumah* led the nation to adopt the name *Ghana*, honoring the ancient *Ghana Empire* whose medieval power had spread across West Africa. Ghana became the first sub-Saharan African nation to achieve independence from colonial rule, making its renaming a symbol of pan-African liberation and the dawn of African self-determination.

## you gotta know

- The *Gold Coast* earned its name from European traders seeking gold; the region was a major Atlantic trade hub, especially for the slave trade.
- Britain consolidated control over the Gold Coast through a series of colonial charters and military campaigns (1874 full colonization).
- *Kwame Nkrumah*, the nationalist leader and first president, spearheaded the independence movement and chose "Ghana" to honor the medieval West African empire.
- The ancient *Ghana Empire* (roughly 700–1000 CE) had dominated the Sahel region, though it was geographically distinct from modern Ghana.
- Ghana's 1957 independence inspired the wave of African decolonization that transformed the continent over the next decade; Nkrumah championed pan-African unity.
- The Asante Kingdom, a powerful Akan confederacy in the Gold Coast interior, resisted British rule and became a symbol of indigenous African resistance.

## connections

- [[kwame-nkrumah]] — the pan-Africanist leader who negotiated independence and selected the name "Ghana."
- [[british-west-africa]] — the colonial framework that administered the Gold Coast and other West African territories.
- [[asante-empire]] — the powerful inland kingdom that resisted British colonization and embodied West African sovereignty.
- [[ghana-empire]] — the medieval Sahel power whose name was reclaimed for the modern nation.
- [[pan-africanism]] — the ideological movement that Nkrumah championed, inspired by Ghana's independence.
