## summary

*Francisco Madero* was a Mexican politician and revolutionary who challenged *Porfirio Díaz's* dictatorship in 1910 and briefly served as president during the early Mexican Revolution. His moderate reformism and inability to satisfy radical factions made his presidency turbulent and brief.

## you gotta know

- Published *The Presidential Succession of 1910*, advocating free elections and challenging Díaz's reelection—triggering the Mexican Revolution.
- His slogan *"No reelection"* resonated across Mexico and mobilised peasants, workers, and intellectuals against the Porfiriato.
- Forced *Porfirio Díaz* to resign in May 1911 and was elected president, but his moderate Land reforms disappointed radical agrarians like *Zapata*.
- Rejected the radical *Plan de Ayala*, alienating southern revolutionaries and creating a schism in the revolution.
- Overthrown in a military coup by *Victoriano Huerta* in 1913; was killed alongside his vice president while under army guard.
- His murder reignited the revolution and shifted it toward greater social radicalism.
