# Cherokee

## summary

The *Cherokee* are one of the largest Native American nations, originally occupying the southeastern Appalachian region (present-day Tennessee, North Carolina, Georgia, and South Carolina). Known for their sophisticated governmental systems, agricultural practices, and Sequoyah's creation of a syllabary, they were forcibly removed to Oklahoma in 1838–1839 on the Trail of Tears, one of the most devastating forced relocations in U.S. history.

## you gotta know

- Developed a complex matrilineal society with the Women's Council holding significant political power; governed by the Great Council and a Principal Chief.
- *Sequoyah* invented the Cherokee syllabary (1821), a writing system enabling mass literacy in the Cherokee language within a decade and making the Cherokee Nation among the first indigenous peoples to have a written language.
- Adopted many elements of European American culture, including cotton farming, slavery, and a written constitution (1827), in hopes of gaining land security.
- Forcibly removed from their homeland by the U.S. government along the *Trail of Tears* (1838–1839); approximately 4,000 of 15,000 died during the forced march to Indian Territory (Oklahoma).
- The Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians refused removal and remain in North Carolina; the Cherokee Nation and United Keetoowah Band established themselves in Oklahoma.
- Maintained their language, traditions, and governance structures; the Cherokee Nation is today one of the largest federally recognized tribes in the United States.
