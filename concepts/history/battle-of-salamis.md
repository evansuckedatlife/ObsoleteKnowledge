# Battle of Salamis

## summary

The *Battle of Salamis* (480 BC) was a decisive naval engagement where the allied Greek fleet, commanded by the Athenian *Themistocles*, decisively defeated the vastly larger Persian navy in the Saronic Gulf. The victory ended Persian hopes of conquering Greece and established naval dominance as the foundation of Athenian power.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 480 BC in the narrow straits around the island of Salamis, near Athens.
- *Themistocles* deliberately lured the Persian fleet into the confined waters where numbers meant less than maneuverability.
- The Greek alliance fielded around 370 triremes (warships); the Persians brought over 1,200 vessels.
- The narrow straits prevented the Persians from using their numerical advantage; Greek ships were faster and better coordinated.
- The Persians lost approximately 200–300 ships; the Greeks lost around 40.
- *Xerxes I*, watching from the shore, reportedly despaired and began planning his retreat to Asia.
- The victory secured Greek independence and made Athens the preeminent naval power of the eastern Mediterranean.
