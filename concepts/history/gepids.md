# Gepids

## summary

The *Gepids* were a Germanic people closely related to the *Goths* who initially ruled the region of Pannonia (modern Hungary) and the Balkans before falling under Hunnic domination. Though they resisted the *Huns* and briefly recovered independence after *Attila's* death, they were eventually destroyed by the *Ostrogoths* and absorbed into the successor kingdoms of the early medieval Balkans.

## you gotta know

- Originated as a branch of the Gothic peoples and settled in the Carpathian region and Pannonia, controlling key territorial passages in central Europe.
- Were conquered and held as tributaries by the *Huns* under *Attila*, forced to provide troops and acknowledge Hunnic overlordship.
- Briefly regained independence after the Hunnic empire collapsed in 453, ruling a kingdom in Pannonia under kings like *Ardaric*.
- Were eventually defeated and destroyed by the *Ostrogoths* under *Theodoric the Great* in the 480s–490s, losing their territorial independence entirely.
- Gradually disappeared from historical records as a distinct people, absorbed into the Ostrogothic kingdom and later into successor Balkan peoples.
- Represent one of the lesser-known Germanic peoples whose history was heavily shaped by the more powerful forces of the *Huns*, *Ostrogoths*, and later Byzantine expansion.
- Controlled strategic Balkan and Pannonian territory that made them important to the military and political calculations of larger powers.
