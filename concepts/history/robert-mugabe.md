# Robert Mugabe

## summary

*Robert Mugabe* was Zimbabwe's founding president, leading the nation from Rhodesian white-minority rule to independence in 1980. A nationalist who fought a brutal liberation war, he initially governed with pragmatism and reconciliation but transformed into an authoritarian figure, presiding over economic collapse and repression during his 37-year tenure.

## you gotta know

- Led the Zimbabwe African National Union (ZANU) guerrilla movement against Rhodesian white-minority rule during the 1970s Chimurenga War, becoming the dominant liberation figure.
- Elected Prime Minister at independence in 1980 and declared "reconciliation" with white Rhodesians and minorities, earning international credibility and initially governing with relative democratic restraint.
- Later pivoted toward authoritarianism, banning opposition parties, deploying secret police, and purging rivals, while promoting black land reclamation from white farmers—popular domestically but economically disruptive.
- Consolidated presidential power (becoming President in 1987), weakened parliament, and ran increasingly aggressive land seizures from the 1990s onward that destabilized agriculture and the economy.
- Presided over hyperinflation, currency collapse, and widespread poverty in the 2000s while enriching his family and loyalists, becoming a symbol of postcolonial authoritarianism and corruption.
- Remained in power until 2017, when the military ousted him, and died in exile in Singapore in 2019.
