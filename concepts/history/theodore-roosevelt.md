# Theodore Roosevelt

## summary

Theodore Roosevelt (1858–1919) was the 26th President of the United States and a transformative figure of the Progressive Era. He ascended to the presidency following McKinley's assassination in 1901 and served until 1909, pioneering trust-busting, conservation, and an aggressive foreign policy rooted in American imperial ambition. After leaving office, he split the Republican Party by forming the Progressive (Bull Moose) Party, permanently reshaping American politics.

## you gotta know

- Became president at 42, the youngest person ever to assume the office; known for his vigorous personality, cowboy past, and "strenuous life" philosophy.
- Launched massive antitrust suits against large corporations, earning the nickname "trust-buster"; dissolved the *Northern Securities Company* and challenged Standard Oil.
- Established the *Panama Canal* by supporting Panamanian independence from Colombia and securing canal rights; the canal became the signature engineering achievement of his presidency.
- Created the *Panama Corollary* to the Monroe Doctrine, asserting U.S. right to intervene in Latin American affairs for "hemispheric stability," justifying military occupations across the region.
- Won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1906 for mediating an end to the Russo-Japanese War, becoming the first American to receive the honor.
- Founded the conservation movement; established 5 national parks, 18 national monuments, 51 federal bird reserves, and 4 national game preserves, protecting ~230 million acres of public land.
- Left office in 1909 and became disillusioned with his successor, William Howard Taft; challenged Taft for the 1912 Republican nomination, lost, and split the party by forming the Progressive ("Bull Moose") Party.
- His third-party run split the Republican vote, allowing Democrat Woodrow Wilson to win with only 42% of the popular vote; a powerful legacy of intra-party division.
