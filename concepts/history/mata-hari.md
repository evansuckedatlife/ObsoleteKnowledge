# Mata Hari

## summary

*Mata Hari* was a Dutch exotic dancer and courtesan who became a German spy during World War I, though her true allegiances remain murky. She was executed by France in 1917, accused of espionage that may have caused thousands of deaths, making her history's most infamous female spy and a symbol of seduction as a tool of statecraft.

## you gotta know

- Born *Margaretha Geertruida Zelle* in the Netherlands; reinvented herself as "Mata Hari" (Eye of the Dawn in Malay) to perform exotic dances in Paris.
- Became a courtesan and lover to numerous French, British, and German military officers and politicians, gathering intelligence through intimate relationships.
- Worked as agent *H-21* for German intelligence while simultaneously accepting money from France and Britain, playing multiple sides during World War I.
- Accused of passing French military secrets to Germany, allegedly contributing to French defeats that cost thousands of lives.
- Court-martialed and executed by firing squad in Paris in February 1917 on espionage charges, though historians debate whether she was truly a significant spy or a scapegoat.
- Her trial and execution became a sensational international scandal, cementing her legend as the archetypal spy-courtesan.
