# The Atlantic

## summary

*The Atlantic* (originally *The Atlantic Monthly*), founded in 1857, is an influential American magazine of literature, politics, culture, and social commentary. Positioned as a forum for intellectual debate and serious journalism, it has published essays, fiction, and reportage by major American writers and thinkers.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1857 in Boston as *The Atlantic Monthly*; reflects New England intellectual and abolitionist traditions.
- Early contributors included *Ralph Waldo Emerson*, *Henry David Thoreau*, *Julia Ward Howe* (author of "The Battle Hymn of the Republic"), and other major figures.
- Published *Oliver Wendell Holmes* and *Nathaniel Hawthorne*; became a venue for literary establishment voices.
- Known for long-form journalism, essays on politics and culture, and serious fiction; typically avoids celebrity journalism.
- Editorial stance has emphasized progressive politics, intellectual rigor, and humanistic values throughout its history.
- Shifted to monthly publication and expanded reach in the twenty-first century; maintains reputation for sophisticated political analysis.
- Famous for publishing important political essays, including defenses of abolition and contemporary debates on democracy and rights.
