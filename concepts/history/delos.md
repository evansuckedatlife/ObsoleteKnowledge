# Delos

## summary

*Delos*, a small island in the Aegean Sea at the center of the Cyclades, was a sacred sanctuary dedicated to the birthplace of *Apollo* and *Artemis*. Though tiny and barren, Delos held immense religious significance across the Greek world and later served as the treasury of the *Delian League*, Athens's maritime alliance.

## you gotta know

- Sacred birthplace of *Apollo* and *Artemis*, the divine twins born to *Leto* on the island; Greek pilgrims traveled from across the Mediterranean to worship at the sanctuary.
- According to mythology, the island was once drifting until *Poseidon* fixed it in place so that Leto could find refuge to give birth, making it the holiest of sanctuaries.
- Housed the *Temple of Apollo*, featuring a famous archaic marble statue of the god, and attracted visitors seeking divine favor and purification.
- Served as the treasury and religious center of the *Delian League* (478–404 BCE), an Athenian-led naval alliance against the Persians; tribute was stored in the temple of Apollo.
- Contained an ancient harbor and became a major trading post and emporium, despite its small size, due to its sacred status and central Aegean location.
- The island housed the tombs and monuments of pilgrims, making it not only a religious destination but also a burial ground for the pious.
- Virtually abandoned by the 2nd century CE and fell into obscurity until archaeological excavations in the 19th and 20th centuries revealed its former splendor.

## connections

- [[apollo]] — god born on the island and honored at its primary sanctuary.
- [[artemis]] — Apollo's twin sister, also born on Delos and honored there.
- [[leto]] — mother of Apollo and Artemis, who gave birth on the sacred island.
