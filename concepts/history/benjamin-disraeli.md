## summary

*Benjamin Disraeli* (1804–1881) was the first Prime Minister of Jewish descent and a master of political theatre who transformed the Conservative Party into a vehicle for romantic nationalism and imperial expansion. His vision of "Tory democracy" married paternalism with imperial grandeur, and his cultivation of Queen Victoria made him one of the most influential advisors of the Victorian era.

## you gotta know

- Rose from a background as a novelist and social critic to lead the Conservative Party, embodying the self-made intellectual outsider.
- Famously courted Queen Victoria after Prince Albert's death, positioning himself as her favorite minister and reshaping the Crown's public role.
- Made the Suez Canal purchase in 1875, buying the British government's stake from the debt-ridden Egyptian khedive and securing Britain's waterway to India—a coup of imperial strategy and dramatic political theater.
- Proclaimed Victoria "Empress of India" in 1876, transforming the Crown into a symbol of global dominion that delighted the Queen and magnified British prestige.
- Coined the term "one nation" to describe his vision of a hierarchical but paternalist society united across class divisions—conservative in structure but responsive to working-class needs.
- Served as PM twice (1868 and 1874–1880), using his second term to consolidate Conservative strength and expand British imperial reach.
