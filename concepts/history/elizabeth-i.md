# Elizabeth I

## summary

Elizabeth I was Queen of England and Ireland from 1558 to 1603, the last Tudor monarch. Her reign, known as the Elizabethan Era, was a period of cultural flourishing that saw the works of Shakespeare and Marlowe, the expansion of English exploration, and the establishment of a stable Protestant Church. Her victory over the Spanish Armada cemented her status as a legendary leader.

## you gotta know

- She ordered the execution of her cousin, *Mary, Queen of Scots*, in 1587 after uncovering the Babington Plot to assassinate her.
- Her forces defeated the *Spanish Armada* in 1588, a victory celebrated in her famous speech to the troops at Tilbury.
- She established the moderate Protestant Church of England through the *Elizabethan Religious Settlement* of 1559.
- She never married and had no children, earning her the title of the *Virgin Queen* and bringing an end to the Tudor line.
- She faced several Catholic conspiracies to depose her, including the Ridolfi Plot and the Babington Plot.

## connections

- [[henry-viii]] — her father, who executed her mother Anne Boleyn and declared Elizabeth illegitimate.
- [[james-i]] — her successor (James VI of Scotland), who peacefully united the English and Scottish crowns upon her death.
