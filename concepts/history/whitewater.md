# Whitewater

## summary

*Whitewater* refers to an investigation into the Clintons' involvement in the *Whitewater Development Corporation*, a failed real-estate venture in *Arkansas* during the late *1970s*. The independent counsel investigation, initiated in *1994*, expanded far beyond the original real-estate question to encompass multiple inquiries, including the *Lewinsky scandal*, and became emblematic of partisan suspicion and the politicization of criminal investigations.

## you gotta know

- In *1978*, *Bill* and *Hillary Clinton* invested in *Whitewater Development Corporation*, a land development project in *Arkansas* with partners *Jim* and *Susan McDougal*; the venture lost money and eventually failed.
- When *Bill Clinton* became president, documents related to *Whitewater*'s finances were subpoenaed; questions arose about whether the Clintons had improperly benefited or whether savings-and-loan executive *James McDougal* had received favorable regulatory treatment.
- In *1994*, *Attorney General Janet Reno* appointed an independent counsel, *Robert B. Fiske Jr.* (later replaced by *Kenneth Starr*), to investigate the land deal and related matters involving the Clintons and the *McDougals*.
- The investigation lasted four years and cost roughly *$70 million*; no charges were brought against *Bill* or *Hillary Clinton* regarding *Whitewater*, though *James and Susan McDougal* were convicted of fraud related to other schemes.
- The independent counsel's mandate expanded to include investigations into White House personnel practices (*Travel Gate*, *FBI-File Gate*) and eventually the *Lewinsky scandal*—a scope creep that fueled accusations of political bias.
- Starr's final report, released in *1998*, found insufficient evidence to prosecute the Clintons on *Whitewater*-related charges, though the investigation had consumed years and significant resources.
- The scandal intensified public perception of partisan weaponization of the criminal justice system and contributed to the broader polarization of the *1990s*.
