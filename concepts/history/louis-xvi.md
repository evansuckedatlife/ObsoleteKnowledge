## summary

*Louis XVI* (1754–1793), the last Bourbon king of France, inherited the throne at a moment of fiscal crisis and deep social discontent. His indecisiveness, the unpopularity of his queen Marie Antoinette, and his failed attempts at reform precipitated the French Revolution and his own execution by guillotine.

## you gotta know

- Ascended to the throne in 1774 at age 19, inheriting a treasury emptied by his predecessor Louis XV's wars and extravagances.
- Called the Estates-General in 1789, which transformed into the National Assembly and authored the Declaration of the Rights of Man; his failure to support reform cost him the faith of reformers.
- Attempted to flee France with Marie Antoinette in the Flight to Varennes (1791), which destroyed his credibility and sealed the revolution's turn toward radicalism.
- Was tried for treason and executed by guillotine on January 21, 1793, declaring his innocence from the scaffold.
- His death galvanized European monarchies against the French Republic and inspired the cascade of monarchist uprisings and foreign invasion.
- His younger brother, the comte de Provence, escaped and claimed the throne-in-exile as Louis XVIII.
