# Battle of Gaugamela

## summary

The *Battle of Gaugamela* (331 BC) was the definitive clash between *Alexander the Great* and the Persian King *Darius III*, fought on the plains of Mesopotamia. Alexander's cavalry tactics and discipline shattered the Persian army, effectively ending the Achaemenid Empire and making Alexander master of the known world.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 331 BC near the village of Gaugamela in northern Mesopotamia (modern Iraq).
- *Alexander III* commanded approximately 47,000 Macedonian troops; *Darius III* fielded a vastly larger force, possibly 100,000+ troops plus war elephants.
- Darius positioned his army on open ground to maximize numbers and deployed elephants and scythed chariots—weapons unfamiliar to Macedonians.
- Alexander's cavalry, led by *Companion Cavalry*, executed a decisive oblique attack, targeting Darius himself and shattering Persian cohesion.
- The Persian center collapsed in panic when Darius fled; disciplined Macedonian phalanxes finished the rout.
- *Darius III* escaped but was killed months later in flight, ending effective Persian resistance.
- The victory ended the Achaemenid Dynasty and left Alexander's Macedonian Empire as the undisputed superpower.
