# Danton

## summary

*Georges Danton* (1759–1794) was a charismatic revolutionary leader who rose to prominence during the early radical phase of the French Revolution, but later advocated for moderation and mercy. His conflict with Robespierre over the scale of the Terror made him a target; he was tried and executed during the height of the Reign of Terror in April 1794.

## you gotta know

- A founding member of the Cordeliers Club, one of the revolution's most influential radical societies, and later a rival power base to Robespierre's faction.
- Served on the Committee of Public Safety (1793) but clashed with Robespierre over the scale of executions, arguing that terror should have limits and clemency should prevail.
- Advocated for ending the Terror and negotiating peace with foreign enemies, a position Robespierre branded as treason and weakness.
- Attempted to flee France in late 1793 but returned voluntarily, a fatal political miscalculation that sealed his arrest.
- Tried alongside *Camille Desmoulins*, a journalist and friend, in March–April 1794, on charges of plotting against the republic.
- Executed by guillotine on April 5, 1794, reportedly mocking the tribunal and proclaiming his final words to carry his decapitated head to show the crowd.
- His death marked the radicals' purge of moderate voices and the Terror's apex.
