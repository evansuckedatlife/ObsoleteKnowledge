# Battle of the Nile

## summary

The Battle of the Nile (also called the Battle of Aboukir Bay) on August 1, 1798, was a crushing naval defeat for the French in Egypt, where British Admiral Horatio Nelson's fleet destroyed most of the French squadron supporting Napoleon's Egyptian campaign. The loss isolated the French expeditionary force and ultimately forced its withdrawal, demonstrating British naval superiority and the fragility of Napoleon's reach beyond continental Europe.

## you gotta know

- Fought on August 1, 1798, in Aboukir Bay near the mouth of the Nile River between 13 British ships and 13 French ships of the line plus supporting vessels.
- Napoleon had invaded Egypt in 1798 to strike at British interests; the French fleet under Admiral Brueys anchored in a shallow defensive formation in the bay.
- Nelson's fleet attacked both sides of the French line simultaneously, a daring tactic that achieved local superiority and prevented the French from concentrating their firepower.
- The French flagship *L'Orient*, a massive 120-gun ship, caught fire and exploded, killing Admiral Brueys and triggering a general British victory throughout the engagement.
- French losses were enormous: 11 ships captured, 2 sunk, over 3,000 killed and 2,000 captured; British losses were approximately 1,800 killed and wounded across the fleet.
- The destruction of French naval power in the Mediterranean left Napoleon's army in Egypt stranded and forced its withdrawal by 1801.
- The victory established Nelson as a national hero in Britain and demonstrated that French naval power could be decisively defeated even in distant waters.
