## summary

*Virginia Hall* was an American intelligence officer and spy who worked for the OSS (Office of Strategic Services) during World War II, coordinating with the French Resistance to sabotage Nazi operations. Despite losing her leg in a hunting accident, she became one of the most effective American spies of the war and a decorated veteran.

## you gotta know

- Born in Baltimore; lost her left leg below the knee in a hunting accident in 1933, but refused to be deterred from a life of action and service.
- Worked for the U.S. State Department in the 1930s before joining the OSS in London; posed as a journalist and later as a French widow to infiltrate occupied France and coordinate Resistance activities.
- Operated behind German lines in occupied France under the code name *Marie* (and later *Diane*), organizing sabotage operations, gathering intelligence, and running networks of Resistance fighters against Nazi occupation.
- The Gestapo became intensely interested in hunting her, calling her "the one-legged woman" and marking her for assassination; she survived and escaped France in 1944.
- Returned to occupied France after D-Day with the OSS, continuing to gather intelligence and coordinate operations until the German surrender in May 1945.
- Received the Distinguished Service Cross (the military's second-highest decoration for valor) for her wartime service; continued working in intelligence for the CIA after the war until her retirement.
