# Amelia Earhart

## summary

*Amelia Earhart* set numerous women's aviation records in the 1920s and 1930s, became the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic, and disappeared over the Pacific Ocean in 1937 during an attempt to circumnavigate the globe. Her courage and skill broke gender barriers in aviation, and her mysterious disappearance captured the world's imagination for decades.

## you gotta know

- First woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean, in 1932, nearly five years after Lindbergh.
- Set women's altitude record, speed records, and cross-country distance records throughout the 1930s.
- Flew the twin-engine *Lockheed Electra* on a round-the-world flight attempt with navigator Fred Noonan in 1937.
- Disappeared over the Pacific near Howland Island on July 2, 1937; the cause remains officially unknown.
- Her final radio transmission indicated she was searching for Howland Island but couldn't find it.
- Pioneered women's entry into aviation and challenged gender stereotypes through her writing and lectures.
- Vanished at age 39; her disappearance spawned decades of speculation and search efforts.

## connections

- [[charles-lindbergh]] — transatlantic pioneer; she followed his 1927 route five years later.
- [[bessie-coleman]] — earlier Black aviator whose courage preceded Earhart's record-breaking flights.
- [[wiley-post]] — fellow speed and endurance record-setter who also died in a flying accident.
