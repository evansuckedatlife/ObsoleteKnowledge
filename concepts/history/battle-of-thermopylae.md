# Battle of Thermopylae

## summary

The *Battle of Thermopylae* (480 BC) saw a small Greek coalition led by Spartan King *Leonidas* make a legendary stand against the invading Persian army of *Xerxes I*. Though ultimately defeated, the Greeks' dogged defense at a narrow mountain pass inflicted heavy Persian casualties and bought time for other Greek city-states to organize resistance.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 480 BC at the narrow coastal pass of Thermopylae in central Greece.
- *Leonidas I* commanded roughly 7,000 Greek hoplites, with approximately 300 Spartans forming the core.
- *Xerxes I* commanded an enormous Persian force—ancient sources claim over 100,000, though historians debate the true number.
- The narrow terrain neutralized the Persians' numerical advantage, allowing Greek phalanx discipline to dominate.
- After three days of fierce combat, a traitor revealed a mountain path allowing the Persians to outflank the Greek position.
- The Spartans, rather than retreat, chose to stand and die, covering the Greek withdrawal with their lives.
- *Leonidas* and roughly 1,100 Greeks perished, while Persian losses reportedly reached 20,000.
