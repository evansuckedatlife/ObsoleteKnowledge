# Meriwether Lewis

## summary

*Meriwether Lewis* was a 19th-century American explorer who co-led the *Lewis and Clark Expedition*, a landmark American journey of exploration commissioned by *President Thomas Jefferson*. His expedition traversed the newly-acquired *Louisiana Territory*, descended the *Columbia River* to the Pacific Ocean, and mapped the northwestern United States while documenting indigenous peoples, flora, and fauna. His legacy was shadowed by depression, financial troubles, and an early death under mysterious circumstances.

## you gotta know

- Selected by President *Thomas Jefferson* to lead an expedition exploring the *Louisiana Purchase* after the 1803 acquisition from France.
- Co-commanded the *Corps of Discovery* with *William Clark*, departing from *St. Louis* in 1804 and reaching the Pacific Ocean in 1805, a journey of over 8,000 miles.
- And Clark spent winters with the *Mandan Indians* of the *Upper Missouri River* and employed *Sacagawea*, a *Shoshone* woman, as translator and guide—a relationship that facilitated peaceful contact with western tribes.
- Documented comprehensive natural history observations, including specimens and descriptions of previously-unknown plant and animal species of the American West.
- The expedition established American claims to the *Oregon Territory* and paved the way for westward expansion; detailed maps and journals proved invaluable for future settlers.
- After returning in 1806, Lewis was appointed Governor of the *Louisiana Territory* but struggled with depression and financial responsibility for expedition expenses the government was slow to reimburse.
- Died under mysterious circumstances in 1809 at *Grinder's Stand* in Tennessee, officially recorded as suicide, though some historians question the circumstances.
