# Zaire → Democratic Republic of the Congo

## summary

The *Democratic Republic of the Congo* underwent a dramatic renaming to *Zaire* in 1971 under dictator *Mobutu Sese Seko* as part of an "Africanization" campaign. The country reverted to *Democratic Republic of the Congo* in 1997 after Mobutu's fall, reclaiming its original post-colonial name. The rebranding to Zaire—derived from a Bantu word for "river"—was meant to sever ties with European colonialism, though it became synonymous with decades of authoritarian rule and resource extraction.

## you gotta know

- The region was the *Congo Free State* (1885–1908), personally ruled by Belgian King *Leopold II*, notorious for brutal exploitation and millions of deaths.
- Belgium annexed the territory as the *Belgian Congo* (1908–1960), developing mining and infrastructure but maintaining colonial extraction.
- The nation gained independence in 1960 as the *Democratic Republic of the Congo* (or *Congo-Kinshasa* to distinguish it from the *Republic of the Congo*).
- *Mobutu Sese Seko* seized power in 1965 and consolidated authoritarian rule; in 1971, he renamed the country *Zaire*, claiming to embrace indigenous African identity.
- The name *Zaire* derives from a Bantu word meaning "river," referencing the mighty *Congo River*, but became tainted by association with Mobutu's kleptocratic regime.
- After Mobutu's fall in 1997, *Laurent-Désiré Kabila* restored the original name *Democratic Republic of the Congo*, symbolically rejecting the authoritarian era.
