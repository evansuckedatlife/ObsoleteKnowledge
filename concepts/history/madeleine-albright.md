# Madeleine Albright

## summary

*Madeleine Albright* was Secretary of State under President Bill Clinton from 1997 to 2001, the first female to hold the office in American history. A fierce advocate for humanitarian intervention and NATO expansion into Eastern Europe, Albright navigated the post-Cold War era by positioning the United States as the guarantor of a liberal international order while confronting ethnic cleansing in the Balkans and the rise of global terrorism.

## you gotta know

- First female Secretary of State; brought a personal understanding of authoritarian regimes—she fled fascist Czechoslovakia as a child.
- Championed NATO expansion into Poland, Hungary, and the Czech Republic, incorporating former Soviet satellites and alarming Moscow.
- Advocated forcefully for military intervention in the Bosnian War (1992–1995) and the Kosovo War (1998–1999), framing it as humanitarian obligation.
- Articulated America's role as an "indispensable nation," asserting that the United States must lead in shaping the post-Cold War global order.
- Pursued engagement with China despite the 1989 Tiananmen Square massacre, balancing human rights concerns with strategic trade interests.
- Worked to contain Iraq through sanctions and no-fly zones after the Gulf War, though Saddam Hussein remained in power throughout her tenure.

## connections

- [[bill-clinton]] — the president under whom Albright served; her interventionist stance aligned with his humanitarian ambitions.
- [[bosnian-war]] — she pushed for American military involvement to halt ethnic cleansing.
- [[kosovo-war]] — her advocacy led to NATO bombing of Serbian positions, a controversial but ultimately decisive intervention.
- [[nato-expansion]] — her signature achievement, reshaping European security architecture in ways that echoed through subsequent decades.
