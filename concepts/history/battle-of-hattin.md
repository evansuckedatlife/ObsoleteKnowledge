## summary

The *Battle of Hattin* was fought on 4 July 1187 in the Levant, where *Saladin*'s Muslim forces decisively defeated the combined armies of the *Kingdom of Jerusalem*. The victory gave *Saladin* control of the Holy Land, forcing the Christian Crusaders into their third major mobilisation and becoming a watershed moment in the long conflict between Christianity and Islam for territorial dominance in the eastern Mediterranean.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the village of Hattin, west of the Sea of Galilee; *Saladin*'s forces numbered roughly 12,000 while the Crusader army was estimated at 20,000.
- King *Guy of Lusignan* led the Crusaders; he was lured into a devastating trap where *Saladin* cut off his retreat to water in the Horns of Hattin, a desolate, rocky terrain.
- The Crusaders, dehydrated and exhausted, were broken in a final assault and suffered catastrophic losses; many knights and nobles were executed after surrender.
- The defeat forced the Crusaders to abandon their strongest fortifications in the Holy Land, including the city of Jerusalem, which fell to *Saladin* shortly after.
- The battle prompted the Third Crusade (1189–1192), led by *Richard the Lionheart*, though even this massive effort failed to recover Jerusalem permanently.
- Hattin demonstrated *Saladin*'s superior strategy, logistics, and psychological understanding, cementing his reputation as one of history's greatest military commanders.
