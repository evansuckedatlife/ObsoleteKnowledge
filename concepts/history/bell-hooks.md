# bell hooks

## summary

*bell hooks* (1952–2023), born *Gloria Jean Watkins*, was an American writer and critical theorist who brought rigorous analysis of race, class, gender, and sexuality to feminist discourse. Her work, spanning theory, pedagogy, and popular culture, made her one of the most influential Black feminist intellectuals of the late 20th century.

## you gotta know

- Published *Ain't I a Woman: Black Women and Feminism* (1981), analyzing the erasure of Black women from mainstream feminist movements and white women's complicity in racism.
- Developed concepts of *intersectionality* and *interlocking oppressions* long before the term was canonized, showing how racism, sexism, and poverty operate together.
- Pioneered *engaged pedagogy* and *teaching as a practice of freedom*, arguing that education is inherently political and can be a site of liberation.
- Wrote 30+ books spanning academic theory, essays, poetry, and popular cultural analysis, making complex ideas accessible to broad audiences.
- Insisted on lowercasing her name (bell hooks) as a rejection of conventional capitalization and authority; emphasized humility and collectivity in intellectual work.
- Her work reframed feminism from a white middle-class movement to a coalition struggle for all marginalized people.
