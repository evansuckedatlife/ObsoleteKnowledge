# Martin Luther King Jr.

## summary

*Martin Luther King Jr.* was the preeminent leader of the American Civil Rights Movement, whose strategy of nonviolent resistance transformed racial segregation and secured federal protections for voting rights and public accommodations. His 1963 "I Have a Dream" speech became the defining voice of the movement, and his assassination in 1968 catalyzed further legislation and cemented his legacy as America's greatest advocate for racial equality.

## you gotta know

- Led the Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955–1956), which desegregated public transportation and launched his national prominence.
- Delivered the "I Have a Dream" speech during the 1963 March on Washington, which drew over 250,000 people and aired globally.
- Won the *Nobel Peace Prize* in 1964 at age 35, the youngest male recipient at the time.
- Championed nonviolent civil disobedience, modeled on *Mohandas Gandhi's* methods, explicitly rejecting violence even when confronted by police brutality.
- Spearheaded the Selma to Montgomery marches (1965), where state troopers attacked peaceful protesters on the Edmund Pettus Bridge in an incident that shocked the nation.
- His efforts contributed directly to passage of the *Civil Rights Act of 1964* and the *Voting Rights Act of 1965*.
- Assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee on April 4, 1968 by James Earl Ray; his death sparked both mourning and renewed civil rights activism.
