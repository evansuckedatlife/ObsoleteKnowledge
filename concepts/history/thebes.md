# Thebes

## summary

*Thebes*, the major city of Boeotia in central Greece, rose from cultural obscurity to become a military superpower under *Epaminondas* in the 4th century BCE. Famous in mythology as the cursed city of *Oedipus* and the site of tragic cycles, Thebes challenged Athens and Sparta for hegemony through innovative tactics and became the first city to defeat Sparta in open battle.

## you gotta know

- Legendary home of *Oedipus*, the tragic king who unknowingly fulfilled a prophecy by killing his father and marrying his mother.
- Founded, according to myth, by *Cadmus*, a Phoenician hero, who sowed dragon's teeth from which armed warriors sprang from the earth.
- Housed the *Sacred Band of Thebes*, an elite regiment of 300 hoplites paired as lovers, whose bond was thought to strengthen their fighting power.
- *Epaminondas*, a brilliant military commander, developed the *oblique phalanx* tactic and defeated Sparta at *Leuctra* (371 BCE), shattering the myth of Spartan invincibility.
- Served as a center of Boeotian power and culture, situated between Athens and Sparta, often caught in their rivalry.
- Hosted the *Mysteries of Cabiri*, secret religious rites, and was an intellectual center in its own right.
- Destroyed and enslaved by *Alexander the Great* in 335 BCE for resisting Macedonian rule, becoming a cautionary example of resistance.
