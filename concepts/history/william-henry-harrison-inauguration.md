# William Henry Harrison's Inauguration

## summary

*William Henry Harrison's* inauguration on March 4, 1841, was the longest presidential inaugural address in American history, spanning nearly two hours. Yet it proved brief in legacy: Harrison died merely 31 days into his term, the shortest presidency in US history, leaving the oath and address as his primary legacy.

## you gotta know

- Took place March 4, 1841, with an inaugural address of approximately 8,445 words.
- Harrison was 68 years old, the oldest president to take office at that time.
- Delivered the address in cold, wet conditions without adequate winter clothing.
- Died of pneumonia or other complications on April 4, 1841—31 days into his presidency.
- The address championed the cause of *manifest destiny* and westward expansion.
- Emphasized limits on executive power and the supremacy of Congress, opposing Jacksonian expansion of presidential authority.
- John Tyler, his vice president, became the first to assume the presidency mid-term, establishing that succession precedent.
