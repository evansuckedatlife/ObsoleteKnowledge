# The Lewinsky Scandal

## summary

The *Lewinsky scandal* erupted when President *Bill Clinton*'s sexual relationship with White House intern *Monica Lewinsky* became public in *1998*. After initially denying the affair under oath, *Clinton* admitted to "inappropriate relations"; his subsequent impeachment for perjury and obstruction of justice, and acquittal by the Senate, became a watershed moment in American political polarization.

## you gotta know

- Between *1995* and *1997*, President *Clinton* engaged in a sexual relationship with *Monica Lewinsky*, a 22–24-year-old White House intern; the relationship occurred in and near the Oval Office.
- When questioned under oath in a deposition for the *Paula Jones* sexual harassment lawsuit (*January 1998*), *Clinton* denied the relationship and claimed he had never had sexual relations with *Lewinsky*.
- A recorded conversation between *Lewinsky* and *Linda Tripp* (obtained by independent counsel *Kenneth Starr*) provided evidence of the affair; *Clinton* later admitted to "inappropriate relations."
- Independent counsel *Starr* released a graphic report detailing the affair; *Clinton* was impeached on *December 19, 1998* by the House on charges of perjury and obstruction of justice.
- The *Senate* voted *45–55* to acquit *Clinton* on the perjury charge and *50–50* on obstruction (both fell short of the required *67* votes for conviction), allowing him to remain in office.
- The scandal bitterly divided the nation along partisan lines and became a catalyst for intensifying congressional acrimony and public distrust of institutions.
- *Clinton*'s approval rating remained high throughout the scandal, complicating the impeachment narrative and highlighting the gap between public and elite opinion.
