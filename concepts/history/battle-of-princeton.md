# Princeton

## summary

On *January 3, 1777*, George Washington's Continental Army routed a British force at Princeton, New Jersey. Coming just a week after the triumph at Trenton, this victory reversed American military momentum and forced the British to consolidate around New York City, ceding most of New Jersey to American control. The back-to-back victories rescued the Revolution at its darkest hour and restored faith in Washington's command.

## you gotta know

- The Americans attacked a British regiment near Princeton College in the early morning, achieving surprise through a bold flank march around the main British position.
- British casualties included about 100 killed and wounded; Americans lost approximately 40, but inflicted severe damage on the British garrison.
- The victory proved Washington's strategic judgment and offensive capability—qualities in doubt after months of retreat.
- British general William Howe, commanding in New York, was forced to withdraw interior garrisons and consolidate his position.
- Combined with Trenton, these victories allowed Washington to winter at Morristown and restore army discipline and morale.
- The broader campaign (Trenton-Princeton) became a legend of American courage and improvisation, crucial to sustaining the cause.
