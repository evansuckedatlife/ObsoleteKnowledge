# Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

## summary

*Mustafa Kemal Atatürk* (1881–1938) was the founder and first president of the modern Turkish Republic, transforming the Ottoman Empire's remnants into a secular, nationalist state through sweeping military victories, legal reforms, and cultural modernization. His name—meaning "father of the Turks"—reflects his towering role in Turkish national identity.

## you gotta know

- Led Turkish forces to victory in the Turkish War of Independence (1919–1923) against occupying Allied powers and Greek invasion.
- Abolished the Ottoman sultanate in 1922 and established the Turkish Republic in 1923, declaring himself president.
- Implemented radical secularizing reforms: closed religious schools, replaced Islamic law with European legal codes, separated religion from state affairs.
- Adopted the Latin alphabet in place of Arabic script (1928), modernizing education and literacy despite fierce opposition.
- Changed the nation's name from *Ottoman Empire* to *Turkey*, promoting Turkish nationalism and a modern, Western-oriented identity.
- Granted women's suffrage and legal equality (1934), ahead of many European nations.
- Died in 1938, revered as the architect of modern Turkey; his remains lie in the *Anıtkabir*, a monument in Ankara.
