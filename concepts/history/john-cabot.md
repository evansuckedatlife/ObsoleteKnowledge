# John Cabot

## summary

*John Cabot* was a Venetian-born explorer who sailed under the English flag to explore the North American coast in 1497, establishing England's claim to portions of the continent. Though his expedition lasted only a few weeks and his precise landing site remains uncertain, his voyage created the foundation for British colonial expansion in North America centuries later.

## you gotta know

- Sailed westward from Bristol in 1497 under the sponsorship of *King Henry VII* of England, five years after Columbus.
- His ship, the *Matthew*, carried a crew of only about 18 men and was vastly smaller than Columbus's fleet.
- Reached land somewhere along the North American coast—likely *Newfoundland*, *Nova Scotia*, or *Maine*—though contemporary accounts are vague and his exact landfall remains disputed.
- Returned to England claiming to have discovered a new continent and believing he had reached Asia, the most northern part of the world.
- Received £10 from Henry VII upon return, and promised future support; his second expedition in 1498 never returned, and details of his fate remain unclear.
- His voyage gave England a claim to explore and eventually settle North America, used centuries later to justify English colonization.
- Descendants of his name (Cabot) were prominent in exploring and claiming territories for the English crown in the following centuries.
