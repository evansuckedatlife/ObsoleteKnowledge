# Battle of Agincourt

## summary

The *Battle of Agincourt* was fought on 25 October 1415 in northern France during the *Hundred Years' War*, where a heavily outnumbered English army under *Henry V* defeated a much larger French force through superior tactics and discipline. The victory revived English claims to the French throne and became a defining moment in English martial legend.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the village of Agincourt in the Pas-de-Calais, with English forces numbering roughly 6,000 against 20,000–30,000 French troops.
- English victory was secured by the disciplined use of the longbow, which devastated the French cavalry and heavily armoured knights before they could engage.
- The heavy mud of the battlefield, combined with the weight of French armour, created a bottleneck that negated the French numerical advantage and allowed the English archers to dominate.
- *Henry V* personally led the charge and maintained strict military discipline; after capturing thousands of French nobility, he ordered their execution to prevent a counterattack.
- The victory gave England a temporary upper hand in the Hundred Years' War and emboldened Henry's claims to the French crown.
- The battle became iconic in English culture, immortalised in Shakespeare's *Henry V* as a triumph of will and martial virtue.
