# Margaret Sanger

## summary

*Margaret Sanger* (1879–1966) was an American birth control activist who devoted her life to making contraception legal, accessible, and normalized. Working as a nurse in poor neighborhoods, she witnessed the human cost of uncontrolled fertility and made reproductive freedom the cornerstone of women's liberation.

## you gotta know

- Worked as a nurse on the Lower East Side of New York and treated women suffering from dangerous self-induced abortions and complications of repeated pregnancies.
- Opened the first birth control clinic in America in Brooklyn in 1916; was arrested and imprisoned for distributing contraceptives in violation of the Comstock Laws.
- Coined the term *birth control*, reframing contraception from moral transgression to public health necessity and women's right.
- Founded what became Planned Parenthood, an organization providing contraception, sex education, and reproductive healthcare to millions of women.
- Traveled internationally to promote family planning, particularly in the developing world, though her later advocacy became complicated by concerns about race and coercion.
- Her work was foundational to the sexual revolution and women's liberation movements; without contraceptive access, feminists argued, women could not be truly free.
