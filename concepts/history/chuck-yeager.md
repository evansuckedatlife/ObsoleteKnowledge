# Chuck Yeager

## summary

*Chuck Yeager* became the first pilot to break the sound barrier, flying the Bell X-1 aircraft on October 14, 1947, at Mach 1.07 near Edwards Air Force Base, California. His achievement marked a watershed moment in aviation history, proving that supersonic flight was possible and opening a new era of aeronautical exploration.

## you gotta know

- Broke the sound barrier in the rocket-powered *Bell X-1* at 45,000 feet over the Mojave Desert.
- Had broken two ribs the day before the flight but flew anyway, strapping himself in by hand.
- Flight achieved Mach 1.07, crossing the sound barrier by a narrow margin to ensure safe passage.
- Remained with the U.S. Air Force for decades, serving as a test pilot and instructor at Edwards Air Force Base.
- Flew the X-1A to Mach 2.44 in 1953, becoming the first to exceed twice the speed of sound.
- Survived ejecting from an F-104 Starfighter in 1963 over China; was rescued by a American officer.
- Became a cultural icon, featured prominently in Tom Wolfe's *The Right Stuff* and the film adaptation.
