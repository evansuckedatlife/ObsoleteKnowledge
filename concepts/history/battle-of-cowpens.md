# Cowpens

## summary

On *January 17, 1781*, American general *Daniel Morgan* defeated British cavalry colonel *Banastre Tarleton* at the Cowpens, South Carolina. Morgan's tactical use of militia and cavalry—positioning them to trade fire then retreat behind Continental regulars—shattered the British attack and forced Tarleton's surrender of nearly 800 troops. The victory galvanized the Southern theater and demonstrated American tactical sophistication late in the war.

## you gotta know

- Morgan deliberately used two lines of militia as a screen, with orders to fire and fall back to regulars—a maneuver that trapped Tarleton's charge.
- The British lost approximately 850 men (killed, wounded, captured); American casualties were fewer than 80.
- Tarleton's cavalry charge, a signature tactic for the British in the South, was decisively broken.
- Morgan's victory reinvigorated American resistance in the Carolinas and led to a series of American gains in the South.
- Called one of the finest tactical battles of the entire war, perfectly suited to the terrain and the opposing forces.
- Helped set conditions for the American victory at *Guilford Courthouse* two months later and the eventual British withdrawal from the Carolinas.
