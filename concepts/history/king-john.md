# John

## summary

John was King of England from 1199 until his death in 1216. His reign was marked by military failures, loss of English-held lands in France, and intense conflict with the Church and his own barons. These conflicts forced him to agree to the Magna Carta, a foundational document of constitutional governance.

## you gotta know

- He was forced by his rebellious barons to seal the *Magna Carta* at Runnymede in 1215.
- He earned the nickname *Lackland* because he received no significant landholdings from his father Henry II as a young prince, and later lost Normandy to Philip II of France.
- His dispute with Pope Innocent III over the appointment of Stephen Langton as Archbishop of Canterbury led to the Pope placing England under an interdict and excommunicating the king.
- His repudiation of the Magna Carta led directly to the *First Barons' War*, during which Prince Louis of France invaded England.
- He reportedly lost the Crown Jewels in the treacherous quicksand of the Wash shortly before dying of dysentery.

## connections

- [[henry-ii]] — his father, who favored him during the sibling conflicts with Richard and Henry the Young King.
- [[richard-i]] — his older brother, whom John attempted to depose while Richard was captured on his return from the Crusades.
