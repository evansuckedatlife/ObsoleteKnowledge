# Almohad Caliphate

## summary

The *Almohad Caliphate* (1121–1269 CE) was a North African Berber dynasty that emerged as a reformist reaction against the Almoravids, calling for a return to strict monotheistic doctrine and *tawhid* (God's oneness). Rising from the Atlas Mountains under *Muhammad ibn Tumart*, the Almohads became vastly more powerful than their predecessors, controlling both the Maghreb and Spain at their zenith before fragmenting into successor states.

## you gotta know

- Founded by *Muhammad ibn Tumart* (d. 1130 CE) as a theological and political movement emphasizing rigorous monotheism (*tawhid*), rejecting what he saw as the Almoravid drift from pure Islamic doctrine.
- *Abd al-Mu'min*, Tumart's successor, consolidated power and transformed the movement into an empire (1147 CE onward), conquering Almoravid territories across North Africa and Spain within a generation.
- Controlled *Spain* and the *Maghreb* simultaneously in the late 12th century, making them the geographically largest Berber empire, though Spain gradually slipped away to the Christian *Reconquista*.
- Patronized architecture and learning; the Almohads funded monumental mosques and the transmission of Greek philosophy through *Averroes* (Ibn Rushd) and *Maimonides*, despite their surface orthodoxy.
- Maintained a theocratic state apparatus where the caliph was both political and spiritual head, claiming descent from Prophet Muhammad to strengthen legitimacy.
- Fragmented after 1212 CE following military defeats in Spain; by 1269 CE, the Almohads had been replaced by smaller dynasties like the *Marinids* in the Maghreb and *Nasrids* in Granada.
