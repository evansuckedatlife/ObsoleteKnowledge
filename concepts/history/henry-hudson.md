# Henry Hudson

## summary

*Henry Hudson* was an English navigator who conducted multiple Arctic and North American expeditions seeking the fabled *Northwest Passage*. His voyages mapped unknown regions of northern Canada and the Arctic, though he never found the passage he sought. His fate remains shrouded in mystery—abandoned by a mutinous crew in *Hudson Bay*, he disappeared into the Canadian wilderness never to be seen again.

## you gotta know

- Conducted four major exploration expeditions between 1607 and 1611, alternating between exploring the Arctic and seeking a passage along the North American coast.
- His first two expeditions (1607, 1608) pushed into the high Arctic, reaching remarkably far north and documenting ice conditions and geography unknown to Europeans.
- His third expedition (1609) was conducted under Dutch sponsorship; he sailed along the Hudson River (later named for him) in what is now New York, seeking a passage to Asia.
- His fourth and final expedition (1610–1611) sailed into *Hudson Bay* in modern-day Canada, exploring its coasts in search of the western passage; crew members grew desperate as supplies dwindled.
- In June 1611, mutineers cast Hudson, his young son, and several loyal crew members adrift in a small boat on *Hudson Bay*; they were never found.
- The *Hudson Bay* and *Hudson River* are named in his honor, though he never found the passage he sought.
- His maps and notes, preserved by surviving crew members, provided valuable intelligence for future Arctic and North American exploration.
