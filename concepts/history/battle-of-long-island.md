## summary

On *August 27, 1776*, the first major engagement of the Revolutionary War saw British general William Howe crush an American force under George Washington near Brooklyn, New York. Washington had divided his army between Long Island and Manhattan; Howe outflanked the Long Island contingent and inflicted about 1,000 American casualties and nearly 1,000 captured. Only Washington's skillful evacuation across the East River in fog prevented total disaster and preserved his army to continue the war.

## you gotta know

- Also known as the *Battle of Brooklyn*; part of Howe's campaign to capture New York City and establish a base of operations.
- The Americans held a series of defensive lines on Long Island but were outflanked by a large British force moving around their left.
- American militia broke and fled when the British appeared unexpectedly from their rear; regular troops fought a delaying action.
- Washington's evacuation of 9,000+ troops across the East River under cover of darkness and fog prevented encirclement and destruction.
- British casualties were relatively light (fewer than 400), making it a lopsided American defeat in raw numbers.
- The defeat forced Washington to evacuate Manhattan and retreat across New Jersey, a period of strategic crisis for the Continental cause.
