# House of Valois

## summary

The **House of Valois** ruled France from 1328 to 1589, presiding over some of medieval Europe's grandest and bloodiest conflicts—the *Hundred Years' War*, the *Wars of Religion*, and the *Italian Wars*. Though overshadowed by external warfare and internal religious strife, the Valois patronized the arts and learning, helping usher in the French Renaissance before their line was extinguished by the rise of the *House of Bourbon*.

## you gotta know

- Founded when *Philip VI* ascended the throne in 1328 after the extinction of the *Capetian line*; ruled France for over 260 years.
- Fought the *Hundred Years' War* (1337–1453) with England over the French succession; saw the campaigns of *Joan of Arc*, who rallied France to military victories.
- *Francis I* (r. 1515–1547) embodied Renaissance kingship—a patron of art and learning who commissioned the *Châteaux of the Loire Valley* and rivaled *Henry VIII* in cultural ambition and martial prowess.
- Fought the *Italian Wars* against the *Habsburgs* for control of territories in Italy; a prolonged struggle that weakened both powers.
- Later Valois kings faced the *Wars of Religion* (1562–1598), brutal civil conflicts between Catholics and Huguenots (French Protestants) that fractured the realm.
- *Catherine de Medici*, an Italian queen, wielded enormous power during the minority and weakness of later Valois kings; orchestrated the *St. Bartholomew's Day Massacre* (1572).
- The dynasty ended with *Henry III*, assassinated in 1589; the crown passed to *Henry IV* of Bourbon, uniting the two houses.
