# Francis I

## summary

*Francis I* (1494–1547), the first French Renaissance king, was a patron of arts and letters who made France a cultural power and rival to the Holy Roman Empire. His reign balanced military ambition against patronage of humanist scholars and artists including Leonardo da Vinci.

## you gotta know

- Reigned 1515–1547; declared himself "Father and Restorer of Letters" and welcomed scholars, artists, and printers to his court, spreading Renaissance ideals across France.
- Invited *Leonardo da Vinci* to live and work in France, cementing his reputation as a patron and maker of culture.
- Waged the Italian Wars against *Charles V* and the Holy Roman Empire, competing for dominance in Italy and European prestige; captured and ransomed at the Battle of Pavia (1525).
- Built and expanded Châteaux like Fontainebleau, architectural masterpieces that became showcases of Renaissance taste and royal power.
- Negotiated the Concordat of Bologna (1516) with the Pope, granting the French crown significant control over church appointments and revenues in France.
- His reign marked France's transition from medieval to Renaissance culture and established it as a major artistic and intellectual center.
