# Jimmy Doolittle

## summary

*Jimmy Doolittle* was a pioneering aviator and military officer who made innovations in instrument flight, set numerous speed records in the 1920s and 1930s, and led the famous *Doolittle Raid* on Tokyo in 1942. His career spanned peacetime aviation advancement and combat leadership, making him one of the most decorated pilots in American history.

## you gotta know

- Conducted the first outside loop in an airplane in 1913, demonstrating advanced aerobatic techniques.
- Set world speed record of 296.3 mph in 1922 in the Gee Bee racer, dominating air racing competitions.
- Pioneered instrument-only flight through clouds, advancing blind flying techniques essential to modern aviation.
- Led the *Doolittle Raid* on Tokyo in April 1942, launching B-25 bombers from an aircraft carrier in retaliation for Pearl Harbor.
- Became a major general in the U.S. Army Air Forces, commanding air operations in North Africa and Europe.
- Survived ejecting over North Africa and was rescued after days of travel through enemy territory.
- Remained active in aviation throughout his life, demonstrating the Bell X-1 and consulting on experimental aircraft.
