# Bush v. Gore

## summary

*Bush v. Gore* (2000) resolved the disputed 2000 presidential election by halting Florida's recount, effectively handing the presidency to George W. Bush. The decision remains controversial for appearing to substitute judicial judgment for electoral processes and for its narrow reasoning limited to the case itself.

## you gotta know

- The 2000 presidential election hinged on Florida's 25 electoral votes; the race was extraordinarily close and several counties used punch-card ballots with ambiguous votes.
- Florida's Supreme Court ordered a manual recount of disputed ballots; Bush and his lawyers sought federal intervention to stop it.
- The U.S. Supreme Court voted 5–4 to stop the recount, reasoning that the lack of uniform recount standards violated the *Equal Protection Clause*.
- Chief Justice *William Rehnquist* and two other justices also argued that the decision should apply only to this case, a rare self-limiting precedent.
- The halted recount left Bush with a narrow certified victory in Florida, securing the presidency.
- Criticized for apparent result-oriented reasoning: the Court applied *Equal Protection* in a way inconsistent with prior doctrine, seemingly to reach a predetermined outcome.
