# McClure's Magazine

## summary

*McClure's Magazine*, founded in 1893 by *S. S. McClure*, became the flagship publication of the muckraking movement, exposing corporate corruption, political graft, and social injustice. Its bold investigative journalism helped catalyze Progressive Era reforms and demonstrated the power of magazines to drive social change.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1893; pioneered the ten-cent magazine price point, achieving mass circulation of over *400,000*.
- Famous for *muckraking* — aggressive investigative journalism exposing corruption; term coined partly through McClure's work.
- Published *Ida B. Tarbell's* groundbreaking series on Standard Oil's monopolistic practices (1902–1904), which contributed to its breakup.
- Published *Lincoln Steffens'* investigations of municipal corruption in "The Shame of the Cities."
- Known for serializing fiction alongside hard-hitting journalism; attracted major writers including *Willa Cather*.
- Editorial mission was explicit: expose wrongdoing and mobilize public opinion toward reform.
- Declined after 1910s as muckraking fell out of favor and other magazines captured the investigative mantle.
