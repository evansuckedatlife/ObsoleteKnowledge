# Ghaznavid Dynasty

## summary

The *Ghaznavid Dynasty* (977–1186 CE) was a Turkish military empire centered in *Ghazni* (in present-day Afghanistan), which conquered much of *Central Asia* and northern *India*, introducing Islam to the Indian subcontinent. Under the warrior-king *Mahmud of Ghazni*, the Ghaznavids became fearsome raiders and administrators, amassing wealth through Indian plunder and establishing a Turkic cultural foothold that would eventually lead to Muslim rule of India.

## you gotta know

- Established by *Alptigin* in 977 CE in Ghazni; rose to prominence under his successor *Sabuktigin*, who conquered Khorasan and the Hindu Kush region.
- *Mahmud of Ghazni* (r. 998–1030 CE) became the dynasty's most celebrated ruler, launching seventeen devastating raids into northern India, plundering temples and establishing Islam's military presence on the subcontinent.
- Conquered the rich commercial cities of *Samarkand* and *Bukhara*, making Ghazni a wealthy intellectual and cultural center that rivaled Baghdad in influence.
- Patronized Persian poetry and literature; the poet *Firdawsi* composed the *Shahnameh* (Book of Kings) under Ghaznavid patronage, creating one of Islam's greatest literary epics.
- Operated as successors to the *Abbasid* caliphate and the earlier *Samanid* dynasty, maintaining the fiction of Abbasid authority while exercising independent imperial power.
- Weakened by *Seljuk* pressure from the west and the rise of the *Ghurid* dynasty in Afghanistan; finally collapsed in 1186 CE, though Ghaznavid governors remained in India for another generation.
