# John Quincy Adams

## summary

*John Quincy Adams* was Secretary of State under President James Monroe from 1817 to 1825, during which he orchestrated some of the nation's most consequential territorial and diplomatic achievements. He skillfully navigated the complex geopolitics of the Western Hemisphere, securing continental expansion while articulating American principles of non-intervention through the Monroe Doctrine.

## you gotta know

- Negotiated the *Treaty of Ghent* (1814) ending the War of 1812, which preserved American independence though settling few substantive disputes.
- Orchestrated the *Florida Cession* (1819), acquiring Spanish Florida and solidifying American control of the southeastern frontier.
- Authored the *Monroe Doctrine* (1823), declaring the Western Hemisphere off-limits to European colonialism—a cornerstone of American foreign policy for a century.
- Signed the *Rush-Bagot Agreement* (1817), which demilitarised the Great Lakes and set a precedent for peaceful border settlement with Britain.
- Negotiated the *Convention of 1818*, establishing the shared U.S.–Canada border along the 49th parallel in the Northwest.
- Believed in American exceptionalism but opposed territorial conquest; supported gradual westward expansion through diplomacy rather than force.
- Later elected President (1825–1829); served in the House of Representatives after his presidency, the only ex-president to do so.

## connections

- [[james-monroe]] — the president under whom Adams served; Monroe's name is inscribed on Adams' greatest achievement.
- [[thomas-jefferson]] — a predecessor as Secretary of State; shared the vision of continental expansion.
- [[daniel-webster]] — succeeded him as a leading statesman and later became Secretary of State.
- [[monroe-doctrine]] — the geopolitical framework he defined for the hemisphere.
