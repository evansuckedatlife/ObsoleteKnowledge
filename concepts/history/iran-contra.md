# Iran-Contra

## summary

The *Iran-Contra* affair was a covert operation during the *Reagan* administration in which officials secretly facilitated arms sales to *Iran* (despite a government embargo) and illegally funneled the profits to fund the *Contras*, right-wing rebels fighting the *Sandinista* government in *Nicaragua*. When exposed in *1986*, the scandal revealed deliberate circumvention of congressional law and executive accountability.

## you gotta know

- Between *1985* and *1987*, senior officials including *National Security Advisor* *John Poindexter* and *National Security Council* aide *Oliver North* secretly negotiated arms sales to *Iran*, a state sponsor of terrorism.
- The arms sales violated a U.S. embargo on *Iran* imposed after the *1979 Iranian Revolution*; the Reagan administration falsely claimed it was seeking better relations with Iranian moderates.
- Proceeds from the *Iran* arms sales—approximately *$30 million*—were illegally diverted to fund the *Contras*, guerrilla fighters opposing Nicaragua's *Sandinista* government.
- Congress had explicitly forbidden military aid to the *Contras* via the *Boland Amendment* (*1982–1987*); the administration circumvented this constitutional constraint through classified operations.
- *Oliver North* admitted to destroying evidence and obstructing Congress; his superior *John Poindexter* and other officials misled Congress during testimony.
- A special prosecutor appointed in *1986* indicted multiple officials; most received presidential pardons in *December 1992*, days before President *Reagan* left office.
- The scandal raised urgent questions about executive power, congressional oversight, and the rule of law in foreign policy.

## connections

- [[ronald-reagan]] — the president under whose administration the scheme was authorized.
