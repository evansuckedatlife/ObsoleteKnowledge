# Battle of Actium

## summary

The *Battle of Actium* (31 BC) was a decisive naval battle off the Greek coast where *Octavian* defeated the combined forces of *Mark Antony* and *Cleopatra VII*. The victory eliminated Octavian's last rival for power, ushering in the Roman Empire and making Octavian (*Augustus*) history's first emperor.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 31 BC in the waters off Actium in northwestern Greece.
- *Octavian* commanded around 400 warships under his admiral *Agrippa*; *Mark Antony* and *Cleopatra* mustered approximately 500 vessels.
- Despite superior numbers, Antony's fleet was weakened by supply problems and desertions; many of his Egyptian contingents remained unreliable.
- Agrippa's tactical superiority and superior ship handling allowed the Octavian fleet to win several engagements and eventually break Antony's center.
- Antony and Cleopatra fled to Egypt; their remaining allies surrendered to Octavian.
- The two leaders eventually committed suicide as Octavian closed in, leaving him sole ruler of the Roman world.
- The battle effectively ended the Roman Republic and inaugurated the *Principate*—the era of imperial rule lasting 500 years.

## connections

- [[octavian]] — the future *Augustus*, who used victory to consolidate absolute power.
- [[mark-antony]] — the triumviral colleague whose defeat ended the Second Triumvirate.
- [[cleopatra]] — the Egyptian queen whose alliance with Antony sealed her fate.
- [[roman-empire]] — the political system that Actium's outcome directly created.
