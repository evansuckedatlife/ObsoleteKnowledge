# William I

## summary

William I, also known as William the Conqueror, was the first Norman king of England, reigning from 1066 until his death in 1087. Previously the Duke of Normandy, he launched the Norman Conquest of England, culminating in his victory at the Battle of Hastings. His reign reshaped English society, politics, and language, introducing feudalism and Norman French to the elite.

## you gotta know

- He ordered the compilation of the *Domesday Book* in 1086, a comprehensive survey of landholdings and resources in England.
- He defeated the last Anglo-Saxon king, Harold Godwinson, at the *Battle of Hastings* in 1066.
- To suppress rebellions in the north of England, he conducted the brutal campaign of destruction known as the *Harrying of the North*.
- He commissioned the construction of the *White Tower*, which became the central keep of the Tower of London.
- He was crowned at Westminster Abbey on Christmas Day, 1066, amid a riot that broke out when guards mistook the crowd's cheers for an assassination attempt.
