## summary

*Eddie Rickenbacker* was the United States' top fighter ace in World War I with 26 confirmed aerial victories, a record unmatched in American aviation at the time. He later became a race car driver, airline executive, and war hero, embodying the American aviator archetype of skill, courage, and entrepreneurial spirit.

## you gotta know

- Achieved 26 confirmed kills in World War I, making him America's leading ace—far ahead of his contemporaries.
- Flew the *Spad XIII*, a single-seat fighter aircraft that he mastered for combat operations.
- Survived being shot down and crashing behind enemy lines during the war.
- Transitioned to automobile racing before World War I, setting speed records and winning races.
- Became president of Eastern Air Lines, building it into a major American carrier.
- Survived 24 days adrift in the Pacific after his B-17 ditched in 1942; his story became legendary.
- Continued public life into his 80s, remaining an iconic figure of American aviation.
