# Romer v. Evans

## summary

*Romer v. Evans* (1996) is the Supreme Court decision that struck down Colorado's Amendment 2, which had prohibited all laws protecting LGBTQ people from discrimination. The 6–3 ruling established that laws targeting a class of people for disfavor violate equal protection and marked the Court's first major victory for LGBTQ rights at the federal level.

## you gotta know

- Decided May 20, 1996 by a 6–3 margin, with Justice Anthony Kennedy writing the majority opinion.
- Invalidated Colorado's Amendment 2, which had been approved by voters in 1992 and prohibited the state and its municipalities from enacting protections based on sexual orientation.
- The majority held that the amendment had "the apparent aim" of imposing a broad, undifferentiated disability on a single group.
- Kennedy's opinion stated that such a law cannot be justified by any rational basis test and violates the equal protection guarantee of the Fourteenth Amendment.
- The amendment had overturned existing LGBTQ protections in Denver, Boulder, and Aspen, and sought to prevent future ones statewide.
- Dissenters, including Justice Scalia, argued that the amendment was a legitimate expression of a moral disapproval of homosexuality.
- Opened the door to subsequent equal protection challenges to anti-LGBTQ laws and policies.
