# Golda Meir

## summary

*Golda Meir* (1898–1978) was the fourth and first female prime minister of Israel, serving from 1969 to 1974. A labor socialist and early Zionist immigrant to Palestine, she became a defining figure in Israeli politics and the nation's security doctrine, symbolizing female leadership in a male-dominated geopolitical arena.

## you gotta know

- Emigrated to Palestine from the Soviet Union (via the United States) in 1921; worked as a kibbutznik and labor organizer.
- Served as minister of labor and later foreign minister before becoming prime minister at age 71.
- Led Israel during the *Yom Kippur War* (1973), a sudden Egyptian-Syrian offensive that shocked the nation; Israel recovered and won, but the conflict eroded her popularity.
- Promoted Labour Party policies of government ownership, collective farms, and generous welfare; shaped Israel's social-democratic model.
- Oversaw the *Munich Massacre* (1972) at the Olympic Games and authorized *Operation Wrath of God*, a covert retaliation campaign.
- Resigned in 1974 after the Yom Kippur War inquiry; her political downfall reflected public disillusionment.
- Remained a cultural icon: modest, direct, and uncompromising, epitomizing Israeli pragmatism and toughness.
