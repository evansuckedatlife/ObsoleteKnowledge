# Obergefell v. Hodges

## summary

*Obergefell v. Hodges* (2015) is the landmark Supreme Court decision that legalized same-sex marriage nationwide, striking down all state bans under the Fourteenth Amendment's Due Process and Equal Protection Clauses. The 5–4 ruling, written by Justice Anthony Kennedy, affirmed that marriage is a fundamental right and that states cannot discriminate based on sexual orientation.

## you gotta know

- Decided June 26, 2015 by a 5–4 margin, with Justice Kennedy writing the majority opinion.
- Overturned the 1996 precedent *Defense of Marriage Act* (DOMA) and every state constitutional ban on same-sex marriage.
- The majority held that marriage is a fundamental right under the Fourteenth Amendment and that denial of that right to same-sex couples violates both due process and equal protection.
- Justice Kennedy's majority opinion emphasized the dignity and autonomy of same-sex couples and the harm caused by state-enforced exclusion.
- Dissented by Justices Scalia, Thomas, Alito, and Chief Justice Roberts, who argued the issue should be resolved through democratic process, not judicial mandate.
- Immediately enabled same-sex couples to marry and claim spousal benefits in all 50 states and territories.
- Built on the momentum of *United States v. Windsor* (2013), which struck down DOMA's federal provisions.
