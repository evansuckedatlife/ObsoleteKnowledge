# Iroquois (Haudenosaunee)

## summary

The *Iroquois* (Haudenosaunee or Hodenosaunee, meaning "People of the Longhouse") are a confederacy of six nations in the northeastern woodlands—*Mohawk*, *Oneida*, *Onondaga*, *Cayuga*, *Seneca*, and *Tuscarora*—renowned for their democratic governance system, longhouse culture, and military prowess. Their confederacy served as a model for democratic institutions and profoundly influenced colonial and early American political thought.

## you gotta know

- Organized as the *Haudenosaunee Confederacy*, governed by the *Great Law of Peace*, a constitution predating the U.S. Constitution and incorporating concepts of separation of powers and checks and balances.
- *Hiawatha* and *Deganawidah* (the Great Peacemaker) are credited with founding the confederacy, consolidating five warring nations into a unified political structure around 1142 (exact date disputed).
- Lived in communal longhouses housing entire matrilineal extended families; women held significant agricultural and political power, including the right to remove leaders.
- Maintained diplomatic and military alliances; *Joseph Brant* led Iroquois forces alongside the British during the American Revolution, seeking to preserve their lands against U.S. expansion.
- Divided in allegiances during the Revolutionary War; some allied with Britain, others with the colonists, resulting in fragmentation of the confederacy.
- Today comprise several nations across upstate New York, Ontario, and Quebec; the Haudenosaunee Confederacy continues as a sovereign entity with its own government and passports.
