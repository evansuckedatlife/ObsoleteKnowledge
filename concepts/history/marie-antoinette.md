# Marie Antoinette

## summary

*Marie Antoinette* (1755–1793), wife of *Louis XVI*, was an Austrian-born queen whose perceived extravagance and foreign origins made her a symbol of royal excess and betrayal. Despised by revolutionaries and the common people alike, she was tried for treason and executed by guillotine in October 1793, less than nine months after her husband's death.

## you gotta know

- Married *Louis XVI* in 1770 as part of a diplomatic alliance between France and Austria, but struggled with the rigid etiquette and isolation of court life.
- Became increasingly unpopular as fiscal crisis deepened, blamed for encouraging the king's resistance to reform and for alleged extravagances in dress and entertainment.
- The Flight to Varennes (1791), her failed escape attempt alongside Louis XVI, destroyed her image as a French queen and sealed the revolution's radicalization.
- Allegedly encouraged royalist conspiracies and secret negotiations with foreign powers, fueling charges that she was a traitor selling France to Austria.
- Separated from her family during imprisonment; her young son *Louis-Charles* (the dauphin) was taken from her and indoctrinated by revolutionaries.
- Tried for high treason in October 1793; prosecutors also leveled charges of incest with her son, designed to dehumanize her in public eyes.
- Executed by guillotine on October 16, 1793, reportedly maintaining composure and dignity on the way to the scaffold.
