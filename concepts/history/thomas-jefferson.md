# Thomas Jefferson

## summary

*Thomas Jefferson* (1743–1826) was the primary architect of American political philosophy and the nation's third president, but he is most remembered as the author of the Declaration of Independence (1776). A Virginia planter, Enlightenment polymath, and champion of agrarian democracy, he shaped the young republic's foreign policy as Secretary of State (1790–1793) and later doubled its territory through the Louisiana Purchase as president. His tenure marked the triumph of republican ideals and westward expansion, though he left unresolved the profound contradiction between his democratic vision and his ownership of enslaved people.

## you gotta know

### Founding Ideology
- Drafted the Declaration of Independence (1776), articulating the revolutionary philosophy that all men are created with inalienable rights to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.
- Grounded the Declaration in Enlightenment thought and popular sovereignty, replacing divine right with the consent of the governed as the sole basis of legitimate government.
- Served as Secretary of State under *George Washington* (1790–1793), establishing the diplomatic protocols and foreign policy stance of the early republic.

### The Presidential Vision
- Elected president in 1800, defeating *Alexander Hamilton*'s Federalist faction and inaugurating an era of Republican (democratic-leaning) dominance.
- Negotiated the *Louisiana Purchase* (1803) with France, doubling the nation's territory and securing the Mississippi River trade routes vital to western expansion.
- Commissioned the *Lewis and Clark Expedition* (1804–1806) to explore and map the newly acquired western territories, laying claim to the Pacific Northwest.
- Championed the *Embargo Act* (1807) to punish British impressment of American sailors and violations of neutral maritime rights, though the policy proved economically disastrous.

### Political Philosophy
- Believed in the agrarian ideal—a nation of independent small farmers, not urban factories—as the moral foundation of republican virtue and self-governance.
- Opposed *Alexander Hamilton*'s vision of a strong central bank and industrial economy, fearing consolidated power would corrupt republican institutions.
- Founded the University of Virginia (chartered 1819), designing its curriculum and architecture to embody his educational and enlightened ideals.

## context

Jefferson embodied the Age of Enlightenment's confidence that reason, education, and natural rights could transform human society. His vision of America as a republic of independent farmers spread across an ever-expanding continent became the dominant ideology of 19th-century westward expansion and popular democracy. Yet his legacy is irrevocably shadowed by his enslaved people—over 600 during his lifetime—a contradiction he acknowledged but never resolved, exposing the limits of Enlightenment universalism in the hands of men bound to slave labor.
