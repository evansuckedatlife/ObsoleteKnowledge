# Napoleon Bonaparte

## summary

*Napoleon Bonaparte* (1769–1821) rose from Corsican obscurity to become the French Revolution's greatest military hero and ultimately its usurper. His military genius and ambition allowed him to consolidate revolutionary gains into the Napoleonic Empire, transforming European politics and establishing a new model of state power that transcended both ancien régime and radical revolution.

## you gotta know

- Rose to prominence as a military officer during the revolution's wars, building a reputation for tactical brilliance and unwavering loyalty to the republican government.
- Played a key role suppressing the royalist Insurrection of 13 Vendémiaire (October 1795), earning the government's trust and advancing his career.
- Led successful campaigns in Italy (1796–1797) and Egypt (1798–1799), expanding French territory and his own legendary status.
- Engineered the Coup of 18 Brumaire (November 1799), overthrowing the ineffective Directory and installing himself as First Consul—marking the revolution's end as a popular movement.
- Consolidated revolutionary gains (legal equality, meritocracy, centralized administration) while eliminating revolutionary democracy and restoring order through autocracy.
- Crowned himself *Emperor* in December 1804, symbolizing the final transformation of revolutionary ideals into personal empire.
- His wars and conquests redrew the map of Europe and spread revolutionary institutions (civil codes, abolition of feudalism) across the continent.
