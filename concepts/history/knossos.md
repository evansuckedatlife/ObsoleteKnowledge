# Knossos

## summary

*Knossos*, the grand palace complex on Crete, was the ceremonial and administrative center of the *Minoan civilization*, Europe's first advanced society dating to roughly 2000–1450 BCE. Its intricate design with labyrinthine corridors, vibrant frescoes, and advanced plumbing inspired the later myth of the Minotaur's maze and speaks to a sophisticated culture that flourished centuries before Classical Greece.

## you gotta know

- Largest Bronze Age palace in the Mediterranean, with a complex floor plan spanning multiple levels connected by staircases and corridors that inspired the Greek myth of the *Labyrinth*.
- Built by the *Minoan* civilization, Europe's first advanced culture, which dominated Crete and the Aegean islands from ~2000 BCE until its decline around 1450 BCE.
- Ruled by *King Minos*, according to legend, who demanded a tribute of youths to feed the *Minotaur*, a monster confined in the maze beneath the palace.
- Decorated with colorful *frescoes* depicting natural scenes—dolphins, flowers, processions—revealing a culture sophisticated in art and aesthetics.
- Featured advanced engineering: a sophisticated water and drainage system, storage magazines (*silos*), and evidence of early written script (Linear A), undeciphered to this day.
- Seat of *Ariadne* and *Theseus*'s fateful meeting; Theseus slayed the Minotaur and escaped the Labyrinth with Ariadne's help using a thread.
- Excavated and reconstructed by Sir *Arthur Evans* (1900 onwards), whose dramatic restorations brought the palace to life, though some restoration choices remain debated.
