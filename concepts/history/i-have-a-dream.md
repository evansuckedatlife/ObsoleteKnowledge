## summary

Martin Luther King Jr.'s defining oration delivered during the 1963 March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, it articulates the nonviolent vision of racial equality and justice at the heart of the American Civil Rights Movement. The speech's rhythmic cadence, biblical imagery, and repeated refrain became the rallying cry for a generation and remains the most iconic statement of the civil rights era.

## you gotta know

- Delivered on *August 28, 1963* from the steps of the *Lincoln Memorial* during the March on Washington.
- Contains King's repeated refrain: *"I have a dream"* — visualizing a future of equality and racial harmony.
- Drawn from the well of American founding ideals: King holds the nation to its own promise of *"life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness"*.
- Employs extended biblical and patriotic metaphors — *"the promissory note"*, *"the jailhouse of segregation"* — grounding moral urgency in familiar imagery.
- King envisions a day when his children will be judged *"not by the color of their skin but by the content of their character"*.
- The speech crystallized opposition to Jim Crow segregation and institutional racism, becoming the symbolic center of the Civil Rights Movement.
- Delivered extemporaneously in parts; the final "dream" section departed from the prepared text and drew on earlier King sermons.
