# Battle of Borodino

## summary

The Battle of Borodino on September 7, 1812, was the largest and bloodiest engagement of the Napoleonic Wars, fought between Napoleon's invading Grande Armée and the Russian army under Field Marshal Mikhail Kutuzov near Moscow. Though the French technically held the field, the Russians withdrew in good order and the French gained no strategic advantage; the battle is often seen as the turning point of the Russian campaign.

## you gotta know

- Fought on September 7, 1812, between approximately 130,000 French troops and 120,000 Russian forces along the Kolocha River west of Moscow.
- The battle was primarily a positional struggle; Kutuzov established a defensive line on high ground and repelled repeated French assaults, particularly the successful French capture of the Raevsky Redoubt after brutal hand-to-hand combat.
- Combined casualties exceeded 70,000—among the highest of any single battle—with no decisive outcome; both armies were severely mauled.
- Despite holding the battlefield, Napoleon did not pursue the retreating Russian army; Kutuzov preserved his force to continue the campaign.
- One week later, the French entered Moscow to find it largely abandoned and set ablaze by the Russians; the victory proved strategically hollow.
- The failure to decisively defeat Russia at Borodino and the subsequent loss of Moscow marked the beginning of the collapse of the Grande Armée.
- The winter retreat from Moscow became a catastrophe; of the original 400,000-plus troops, fewer than 100,000 survived.
