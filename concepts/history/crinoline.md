# Crinoline

## summary

The *crinoline* is a stiffened petticoat or hoop skirt made of horsehair fabric or flexible steel hoops, worn primarily from the 1850s through the 1880s to create a full, bell-shaped silhouette. Cheaper and more flexible than the rigid farthingale of earlier centuries, the crinoline became the defining understructure of Victorian fashion, creating the iconic wide skirts that defined an entire era.

## you gotta know

- Originally a stiff fabric made from horsehair and linen ("crin" = horsehair), which was bulky but effective; later replaced by flexible steel hoops that were lighter and more comfortable.
- The steel-hoop crinoline, patented in 1856, revolutionized construction by allowing wearers to achieve extreme width (skirts could exceed 6 feet in circumference) with relative flexibility.
- At its peak around 1860, the crinoline was so wide that women struggled to pass through doorways, sit in carriages, or navigate stairs—the silhouette was functionally impractical but fashionable beyond utility.
- Wore in layers: typically 3–5 petticoats layered over the crinoline frame, with the outermost skirt draped and trimmed to create volume and silhouette.
- Democratized fashionable silhouette: cheaper than elaborate tailoring, the crinoline allowed middle-class women to approximate aristocratic width and status.
- Subject to safety hazards: crinolines were dangerously flammable, and women routinely suffered burns from candles and fires catching the skirt; crinoline-related accidents were common causes of injury.
- Gradually declined in the 1870s–1880s as the bustle redirected volume toward the rear, and modern fashion movements began rejecting extreme silhouettes.

## connections

- [[farthingale]] — earlier hooped understructure that created similar dramatic silhouettes.
- [[corset]] — rigid companion garment worn with crinolines to create the Victorian hourglass ideal.
- [[bustle]] — successor garment that replaced the full crinoline skirt with rear projection.
- [[victoria]] — British queen during the peak of crinoline fashion and dominance.
