# Fall of Constantinople

## summary

The *Fall of Constantinople* on 29 May 1453 marked the final collapse of the *Byzantine Empire* when Ottoman forces under *Mehmed II* conquered the ancient city after a 53-day siege. The conquest ended more than a thousand years of imperial continuity, symbolised the triumph of Islam in the eastern Mediterranean, and is widely considered the end of the medieval period and the beginning of the modern era.

## you gotta know

- The Ottoman siege lasted 53 days, from 6 April to 29 May 1453; the Byzantine garrison, numbering perhaps 5,000–7,000, defended against an Ottoman force of roughly 50,000–80,000.
- *Mehmed II* (known as Mehmed the Conqueror) directed the siege using innovative cannon and other artillery to breach the famed Theodosian Walls that had protected the city for over a thousand years.
- The last Byzantine Emperor, *Constantine XI Palaiogus*, died fighting on the walls, symbolising the end of the dynasty and empire; his body was never found.
- The conquest was achieved through a combination of artillery, naval blockade, and psychological pressure; *Mehmed* offered generous terms to those who surrendered, encouraging capitulation.
- The fall of Constantinople triggered a wave of Byzantine scholar emigration to Western Europe, carrying precious manuscripts and classical knowledge that fuelled the European Renaissance.
- The city became Istanbul, capital of the Ottoman Empire, and remained under Ottoman rule for nearly 500 years, fundamentally reshaping the religious and political map of the Mediterranean.
