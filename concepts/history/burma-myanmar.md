# Burma → Myanmar

## summary

*Burma* was the name of the Southeast Asian nation during and after British colonial rule, derived from the Bamar ethnic majority. In 1989, Myanmar's military junta (the State Law and Order Restoration Council, or SLORC) unilaterally renamed the country *Myanmar*, claiming it was more inclusive of the nation's ethnic minorities. The change remained controversial internationally, with many Western governments and opposition groups refusing to recognize it, viewing it as an illegitimate imposition by an authoritarian regime.

## you gotta know

- *Burma* comes from *Bamar*, the name of the dominant ethnic group who comprise roughly 68% of the population.
- The military junta renamed the country to *Myanmar* in 1989, claiming the new name included all ethnic groups (Shan, Karenni, Kachin, etc.), though many saw it as a nationalist assertion.
- Britain colonized Burma in three phases (1824–1826, 1852–1853, 1885–1886), finally annexing it fully as a province of British India in 1886.
- The name change coincided with the junta's violent suppression of pro-democracy protests in 1988, making it symbolically linked to authoritarian rule.
- *Aung San Suu Kyi*, the opposition leader and Nobel laureate, was imprisoned by the regime; many Western democracies rejected "Myanmar" as a sign of non-recognition.
- The spelling "Myanmar" reflects the Burmese transliteration preference, while "Burma" was the English colonial convention; the junta demanded the change universally.

## connections

- [[burmese-people]] — the Bamar ethnic majority whose name originated "Burma," and whose identity is central to the nation's cultural fabric.
- [[state-law-and-order-restoration-council]] — the military junta that issued the unilateral 1989 renaming decree.
- [[aung-san-suu-kyi]] — the pro-democracy leader imprisoned by the regime, symbolizing resistance to the junta's authoritarian control.
- [[british-burma]] — the colonial entity that ruled from 1886 until independence in 1948.
- [[southeast-asia]] — the region where Myanmar/Burma remains geopolitically pivotal and ethnically diverse.
