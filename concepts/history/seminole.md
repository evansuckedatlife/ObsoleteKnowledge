# Seminole

## summary

The *Seminole* are a Southeast nation formed in the 18th century from Creek and other Southeastern peoples in the Florida peninsula, known for their distinctive patchwork clothing, cypress-dome settlements, and fierce resistance during the *Seminole Wars*. Successfully defying forced removal longer than most Eastern tribes, they resettled in Oklahoma and Florida reservations where their culture thrives today.

## you gotta know

- Name derives from Spanish *cimarrón* ("wild" or "runaway"), referring to people who separated from Creek Nation; absorbed Creek, Yamassee, and escaped slaves fleeing oppression.
- Constructed unique dwellings called "chickees"—open-sided, thatched-roof structures adapted to Florida's swampy terrain and hot climate.
- Created distinctive clothing traditions, especially women's patchwork skirts and woven sashes, made with colorful traded textiles.
- Resisted forced removal in the *Seminole Wars* (1817–1858); *Osceola* led guerrilla resistance against Andrew Jackson and U.S. forces in the *Second Seminole War* (1835–1842).
- A group of Seminoles, led by *Billy Bowlegs*, successfully evaded forced removal; their descendants remain in the Florida Everglades as the Seminole Tribe of Florida.
- Today two federally recognized Seminole nations exist: the Seminole Tribe of Florida and the Seminole Nation of Oklahoma; both maintain strong tribal governments and cultural traditions.
