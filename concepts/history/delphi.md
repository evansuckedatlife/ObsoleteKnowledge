# Delphi

## summary

*Delphi*, nestled high in the slopes of Mount Parnassus in central Greece, was the sacred heart of the Greek world and home to the most renowned oracle in antiquity. Kings, generals, and common folk journeyed to the Temple of *Apollo* to consult the *Pythia*, the priestess who delivered cryptic prophecies that shaped the course of Greek history.

## you gotta know

- Built as a sanctuary to *Apollo* after he slew the serpent *Python*, establishing a pilgrimage site of unparalleled religious authority across the Greek world.
- The *Oracle of Delphi* spoke through the *Pythia*, a priestess in ecstatic trance, delivering prophecies in verse that were cryptic and often doubled-edged.
- Inscribed above the temple entrance: *"Know thyself"* and *"Nothing in excess,"* axioms of Greek philosophy and ethics.
- Hosted the *Pythian Games*, celebrated every four years and second only to the *Olympic Games* in prestige, featuring athletic contests and musical competitions.
- Served as a pan-Hellenic sanctuary where Greeks of all city-states gathered, transcending political rivalries and reinforcing cultural unity.
- Housed the *Omphalos* (navel of the world), a conical stone marking the center of the earth in Greek cosmology.
- The oracle's influence declined with the rise of Christianity; the sanctuary was closed by Roman emperors and the Pythia's final prophecy was recorded in the 4th century CE.
