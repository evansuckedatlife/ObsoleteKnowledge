# Simón Bolívar

## summary

**Simón Bolívar** (1783–1830) was a Venezuelan military leader and statesman who liberated six nations from Spanish colonial rule through a series of successful military campaigns across South America. Known as "El Libertador," he envisioned a unified continent but ultimately failed to establish lasting political unity despite his military victories.

## you gotta know

- Led the independence movements of Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia (named after him), and Panama, earning the title *El Libertador*.
- Won decisive victories at the Battle of Boyacá (1819) and the Battle of Carabobo (1821), turning the tide against Spanish forces.
- Declared himself dictator of Gran Colombia and attempted to establish a continental federation of newly independent nations.
- Founded Bolivia in 1825 and presided over the drafting of its constitution.
- Disillusioned by political fragmentation and internal conflicts, he resigned from power in 1830 and died in exile in Colombia.
- His vision of a unified South American state proved unrealistic; Gran Colombia dissolved shortly after his departure.
