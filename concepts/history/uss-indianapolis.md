# USS Indianapolis

## summary

**USS Indianapolis** (CA-35), a heavy cruiser, sank on July 30, 1945, after delivering atomic bomb components to Tinian Island just days before the Hiroshima strike. Torpedoed by a Japanese submarine, she went down in twelve minutes, and of her 1,196 crew, only 316 survived the sinking and subsequent four days adrift, making it one of the Navy's greatest peacetime disasters.

## you gotta know

- Completed her secret mission delivering uranium and bomb components for the *Little Boy* atomic bomb to Tinian Island on July 26, 1945, four days before Hiroshima.
- Torpedoed by Japanese submarine *I-58* on July 30, 1945, at 00:14 hours; sank in 12 minutes, giving the crew minimal time to abandon ship.
- The sinking itself killed approximately 300 crew; the remaining 900 spent four days in the ocean where shark attacks, dehydration, and exposure claimed 600 more, one of the highest casualty rates in naval history.
- The disaster remained classified for years because of its connection to the atomic bomb, delaying rescue efforts and investigations.
- The ship's captain, Charles B. McVay III, was court-martialed in a controversial decision that haunted him for decades; he was exonerated posthumously in 2001.
- Her fate is immortalized in survivor accounts and memoirs, including the book and film *Jaws*, which popularized the famous monologue about the Indianapolis.
