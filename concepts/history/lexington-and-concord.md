## summary

On *April 19, 1775*, British regulars marched from Boston to seize colonial munitions stored in Concord, Massachusetts. Local militia confronted them at Lexington, where shots fired—"heard round the world"—killed eight Americans, then again at Concord where the British were routed. This first pitched battle of the Revolutionary War galvanized the colonies and transformed armed resistance into open revolution.

## you gotta know

- British troops encountered *Minutemen* at *Lexington Green* at dawn; disputed gunfire left eight colonists dead and the British line scattering.
- Hundreds of armed militia converged on the retreating British at *Concord Bridge*, pinning them down and forcing a long, costly retreat to Boston.
- The British lost 273 men (killed, wounded, missing); Americans lost 95, but the psychological victory was enormous.
- Paul Revere's midnight ride ("The British are coming!") warned the colonists and allowed militia to mobilize in time.
- The incident shattered any remaining hope for peaceful reconciliation between Britain and the colonies.
- Occurred before the Declaration of Independence and before Congress appointed Washington commander-in-chief.
