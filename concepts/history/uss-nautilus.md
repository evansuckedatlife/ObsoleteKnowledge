# USS Nautilus

## summary

**USS Nautilus** (SSN-571), the world's first nuclear-powered submarine, launched in 1954 and revolutionized undersea warfare with her unlimited underwater endurance. In 1958, she became the first vessel to transit under the North Pole, proving the capability of submarines to operate in Arctic waters and establishing their strategic importance throughout the Cold War.

## you gotta know

- Commissioned in 1954 with a pressurized water reactor, allowing her to remain submerged indefinitely limited only by crew provisions, not battery capacity.
- Her nuclear propulsion made existing diesel-electric submarines instantly obsolete and ushered in the submarine-dominant nuclear deterrent era.
- Traveled more than 62,000 miles during her first deployment without surfacing, demonstrating the revolutionary reach of nuclear propulsion.
- Made her famous trans-Arctic voyage in August 1958, traveling 6,000 miles submerged under the ice pack to emerge at the North Pole, a feat that captured global imagination.
- Served throughout the Cold War as a ballistic missile test platform and attack submarine, accumulating over 500,000 miles of underwater travel before decommissioning in 1980.
- Now a museum ship berthed in Groton, Connecticut, at the Naval Submarine Base, drawing thousands of visitors annually to witness the vessel that transformed underwater naval power.
