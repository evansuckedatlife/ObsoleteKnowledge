# Benito Juárez

## summary

*Benito Juárez* was a Zapotec Mexican president who led the country through the Reform War and French intervention, ultimately restoring the republic in 1867. He championed liberal democratic principles and indigenous rights, transforming Mexico from monarchy back to a federal republic.

## you gotta know

- Rose from a poor Zapotec background in Oaxaca to become Mexico's first indigenous president.
- Led the *Reform War* (1858–1861) against conservative forces, establishing liberal governance and secular authority over the church.
- During the *French intervention* (1861–1867), resisted the foreign-imposed emperor *Maximilian I* and reclaimed Mexico City.
- Issued the *Laws of Reform* (*Leyes de Reforma*), stripping the church of land and political power to build a secular state.
- Known for his motto *"Respect for the rights of others is peace"* — a founding principle of Mexican liberalism.
- Won reelection in 1867 and 1871, implementing educational reform and modernisation before his death in 1872.

## connections

- [[maximilian-i]] — the Austrian archduke imposed as emperor during the French invasion; Juárez defeated him.
- [[lázaro-cardenas]] — later Mexican president who continued land reform and indigenous advocacy.

- [[mexico-independence]] — the republic Juárez fought to restore after foreign intervention.
