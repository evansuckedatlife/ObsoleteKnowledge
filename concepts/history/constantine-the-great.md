# Constantine the Great

## summary

Constantine the Great was a Roman emperor who ruled from AD 306 to 337. He emerged victorious from the civil wars following the collapse of the Tetrarchy to become the sole ruler of the Roman Empire. His reign was a major turning point in history, as he became the first Roman emperor to convert to Christianity and moved the imperial capital to a newly founded city in the East.

## you gotta know

- Before the *Battle of the Milvian Bridge* against Maxentius, he supposedly saw a cross in the sky with the words *in hoc signo vinces* ("in this sign, you will conquer").
- Along with Licinius, he issued the *Edict of Milan* in AD 313, which granted religious tolerance to Christianity and ended official state persecution.
- He convened the *First Council of Nicaea* in AD 325 to address the Arian controversy, resulting in the Nicene Creed, which affirmed that the Son is of the same substance as the Father.
- He founded a new imperial capital on the site of ancient Byzantium, naming it *Constantinople* (modern-day Istanbul), shifting the empire's centre of gravity eastward.
- He delayed his official baptism until he was on his deathbed.

## connections

- [[diocletian]] — his predecessor who established the Tetrarchic system that Constantine eventually dismantled.
- [[licinius]] — his co-emperor in the East whom he eventually defeated in civil war to become sole ruler.
- [[julian-the-apostate]] — his nephew and future emperor who would briefly attempt to return the empire to paganism.
