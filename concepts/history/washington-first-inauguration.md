# Washington's First Inauguration

## summary

*George Washington's* first inauguration in April 1789 marked the inauguration of the first president of the United States. Reluctant to take power but convinced of his duty, Washington traveled from Mount Vernon to New York City and accepted the presidency, setting precedents through his measured leadership that would define the office for generations.

## you gotta know

- Took place on April 30, 1789, in New York City, the nation's capital at the time.
- Washington was the only president unanimously elected by the Electoral College, receiving every elector's vote.
- Despite his reluctance, he accepted the position, viewing it as a duty to preserve the newly formed nation.
- Approximately 10,000 people gathered to witness the ceremony on Wall Street.
- His inaugural address, relatively brief, emphasized divine providence and warned against party factionalism.
- Set the unwritten tradition of two-term presidencies (honored until Franklin D. Roosevelt broke it).
- Established many presidential customs and dignified conduct standards that shaped executive expectations.
