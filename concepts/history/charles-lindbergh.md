# Charles Lindbergh

## summary

*Charles Lindbergh* made the first solo nonstop transatlantic flight on May 20–21, 1927, piloting the *Spirit of St. Louis* from New York to Paris in 33.5 hours. His feat electrified the world, made him an instant international celebrity, and inaugurated the modern age of long-distance aviation.

## you gotta know

- Flew the single-engine *Ryan Monoplane*, nicknamed the *Spirit of St. Louis*, across the Atlantic in 1927.
- Competed for the Orteig Prize, a $25,000 reward offered since 1919 for the first transatlantic flight.
- Navigated by celestial observation and dead reckoning, fighting sleep throughout the 33-hour flight.
- Became the first living person to be widely recognized worldwide; his fame set the modern template for celebrity.
- Toured the U.S. afterward, promoting commercial aviation and encouraging its rapid expansion.
- Later married aviator Anne Morrow; she became a pioneering pilot and author in her own right.
- His reputation was damaged by his isolationist views and admiration for Nazi Germany in the 1930s.
