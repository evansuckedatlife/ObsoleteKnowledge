# William Ewart Gladstone

## summary

*William Ewart Gladstone* (1809–1898) was the dominant Liberal statesman of the Victorian era and a moralist in politics, championing free trade, electoral reform, and Irish autonomy. Serving as Prime Minister four times across nearly 60 years of parliamentary life, he embodied the conscience of the Liberal cause and challenged the Conservatives at every turn.

## you gotta know

- Served as PM four times (1868–1874, 1880–1885, 1886, 1892–1894), the second-longest tenure in British history behind only Asquith and Campbell-Bannerman combined.
- Began his career as a high Tory and moved steadily toward radical Liberalism, emerging as a champion of electoral reform, Irish tenant rights, and free trade.
- Made Irish Home Rule the centerpiece of his final decades in power, pushing through three Home Rule bills (1886, 1893, 1895) that split both his party and the nation over Irish independence.
- Reformed education, expanded the franchise repeatedly, and championed the secret ballot—transforming the electorate and making Britain more democratic.
- Articulated a moral theology of politics, seeing government as an instrument of conscience and justice rather than mere interest—a vision that alienated many traditional Conservatives.
- Retired from Parliament at 89 after his final term, having reshaped the boundaries of Liberal political philosophy.
