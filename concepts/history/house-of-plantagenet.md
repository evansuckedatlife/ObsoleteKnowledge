# House of Plantagenet

## summary

The **House of Plantagenet** ruled England from 1154 to 1485, the longest-reigning English royal house. Named for the yellow broom flowers (*planta genista*) worn in the hat of their founder *Geoffrey of Anjou*, the Plantagenets produced strong medieval warrior-kings and gave England the *Magna Carta*, common law traditions, and Parliament itself—but declined into the fratricidal *Wars of the Roses* before their extinction.

## you gotta know

- Founded by *Henry II* in 1154; descended from Norman conquerors; controlled England and large territories in France (*Angevin Empire*).
- *King John* was forced by rebellious barons to seal *Magna Carta* (1215), a foundational document limiting royal power and establishing the rule of law.
- *King Henry III* and his son *Edward I* expanded English law, centralized governance, and conquered Wales; Edward I also campaigned in Scotland, claiming overlordship.
- *Richard the Lionheart* (r. 1189–1199), a Crusader king, gained legendary status but bankrupted the realm; rarely set foot in England during his reign.
- Presided over the birth of *Parliament* as an institution where the crown consulted with the nobility and common representatives on taxation and law.
- The dynasty fragmented into rival factions—the *House of York* (white rose) and the *House of Lancaster* (red rose)—who fought the *Wars of the Roses* (1455–1487).
- *Richard III* (r. 1483–1485), the last Plantagenet king, was killed at *Bosworth Field*, ending the dynasty and making way for the *House of Tudor*.
