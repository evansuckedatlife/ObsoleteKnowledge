# Idi Amin

## summary

*Idi Amin* was Uganda's military dictator (1971–1979), notorious for his brutality, arbitrary violence, and economic destruction. A brutal autocrat whose regime killed an estimated 300,000 people through executions, torture, and persecution, he became a symbol of African despotism and was eventually overthrown by Tanzanian-backed forces.

## you gotta know

- Rose from military ranks to seize power in a 1971 coup, overthrowing President Milton Obote and promising reform but quickly descending into random terror and ethnic persecution.
- Created secret police and execution squads that tortured and murdered political opponents, intellectuals, clergy, and ethnic minorities, particularly the Acholi and Lango peoples.
- Expelled Uganda's entire Asian (Indian and Pakistani) population in 1972, confiscating their property and destabilizing the economy through discriminatory nationalist ideology and personal greed.
- Declared himself "President for Life" and adopted increasingly grandiose titles; ruled through fear, ethnic favoritism (concentrating power among his Kakwa ethnic group), and economic collapse.
- Invaded and occupied the Tanzanian Kagera salient in 1979, triggering military intervention by Julius Nyerere's Tanzania that ousted him after eight years of terror.
- Fled into exile in Libya and Saudi Arabia, dying in hiding in Jidda, Saudi Arabia, in 2003, remembered as one of Africa's most brutal postcolonial dictators.
