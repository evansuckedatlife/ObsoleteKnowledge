# Henry II

## summary

Henry II was King of England from 1154 to 1189, initiating the Plantagenet dynasty. Through marriage and conquest, he ruled the vast Angevin Empire, which spanned England, parts of Ireland, and over half of modern France. His reign is noted for significant legal reforms, including the development of English common law, as well as his dramatic conflict with the Church.

## you gotta know

- His conflict with the Archbishop of Canterbury led to the assassination of *Thomas Becket* in Canterbury Cathedral after the king reportedly asked, "Who will rid me of this turbulent priest?"
- He was married to the powerful and wealthy *Eleanor of Aquitaine*, though she later supported their sons in a major rebellion against him.
- He laid the foundation for the English common law system through the *Assize of Clarendon* in 1166, which established grand juries.
- He issued the *Constitutions of Clarendon* in 1164 in an attempt to restrict clerical privileges and submit rogue priests to royal courts.
- His later years were plagued by the Great Revolt of 1173–1174, led by his sons Henry the Young King, Richard, and Geoffrey.
