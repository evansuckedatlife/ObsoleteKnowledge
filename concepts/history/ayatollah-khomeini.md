# Ayatollah Khomeini

## summary

*Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini* (1902–1989) was the supreme leader and principal architect of the Iranian Islamic Revolution, transforming Iran from a secular autocracy into an Islamic theocracy. His concept of *wilayat al-faqih* (rule by the jurist) established a new form of governance—the Islamic Republic—that reshaped regional geopolitics and Islamic political thought.

## you gotta know

- Spent 15 years exiled in Iraq and France (1964–1979) for opposing the *Shah's* Western-oriented reforms; became a symbol of Shiite resistance.
- Returned to Iran in February 1979 after the shah's fall; the *Islamic Revolution* installed him as supreme leader (the highest authority in Iran).
- Established the concept of *wilayat al-faqih*, enshrining the rule of a senior Islamic jurist—himself—above elected officials and the constitution.
- Declared war on the *Great Satan* (the United States) and the *Little Satan* (Israel); sponsored anti-Israeli and anti-American movements across the region.
- Initiated the *Iran-Iraq War* (1980–1988) in response to Iraqi invasions; the conflict became a bloody stalemate that killed hundreds of thousands.
- Issued a *fatwa* (religious edict) against author *Salman Rushdie* in 1989 over alleged blasphemy, establishing a precedent for religious condemnation of intellectuals.
- Died in June 1989; his legacy shaped Iran's political system and inspired Shiite movements in Iraq, Lebanon, and beyond.
