# Mary Wollstonecraft

## summary

*Mary Wollstonecraft* (1759–1797) was an English writer and philosopher whose *A Vindication of the Rights of Woman* (1792) became foundational to feminist thought. She argued that women were not naturally inferior to men but were denied education and independence, and that a rational society required granting women equal access to education and civic participation.

## you gotta know

- Published *A Vindication of the Rights of Woman*, the first major philosophical treatise demanding women's educational and political equality.
- Rejected the notion that women were meant by nature for domestic submission; argued that women's subordination was imposed by law and custom.
- Insisted that women needed education not to ornament themselves for men, but to become independent rational beings and citizens.
- Lived an unconventional life: worked as a governess, lady's companion, and teacher before becoming a writer, defying 18th-century expectations of female propriety.
- Traveled to revolutionary France during the Terror and had a daughter out of wedlock with American diplomat *Gilbert Imlay*, challenging sexual and social norms.
- Died at 38 from complications of childbirth following her marriage to philosopher *William Godwin*; her daughter became Mary Shelley, author of *Frankenstein*.
