# Adlai Stevenson Sr.

## summary

**Adlai Stevenson Sr.** was the twenty-third Vice President under Grover Cleveland (1893–1897) and a Illinois Democrat who represented the agrarian and free-silver wing of his party during the turbulent 1890s. Though the McKinley era eclipsed his political fortunes, Stevenson remained a prominent Democratic elder before his death in 1914.

## you gotta know

- Vice President during Cleveland's second term, a period marked by the Panic of 1893, severe economic depression, and labor unrest including the Pullman Strike.
- Represented the free-silver wing of the Democratic Party, opposing Cleveland's commitment to the gold standard—a tension that deepened party divisions.
- Served as a U.S. Congressman from Illinois, bringing agrarian and populist sympathies to national politics that made him a natural running mate for Cleveland despite their differences.
- Was an early supporter of expansionist foreign policy and the Spanish-American War, positions that foreshadowed American imperial ambitions.
- Ran unsuccessfully for Governor of Illinois and remained a fixture in Illinois Democratic politics, where he built a significant political machine.
- His grandson, Adlai Stevenson II, became a prominent 20th-century Democrat and two-time presidential candidate, reviving the family name in politics.
- Served as a U.S. Minister to Belgium late in his career, representing American interests in the years before World War I.
