# Battle of the Coral Sea

## summary

Fought in May 1942 between American and Japanese naval forces in the Coral Sea east of Australia, this battle marked the first naval engagement in which the opposing ships never came within sight of each other—all damage dealt by carrier-based aircraft. Though tactically inconclusive, the U.S. victory prevented a Japanese amphibious invasion of Port Moresby and halted Tokyo's southern expansion.

## you gotta know

- Occurred May 4–8, 1942; first naval battle fought entirely by carrier aircraft, establishing air power as the dominant naval weapon.
- U.S. carrier *Lexington* sunk and *Yorktown* damaged; Japanese light carrier *Shoho* sunk and fleet carrier *Shokaku* heavily damaged.
- Japanese called off their Port Moresby invasion operation (*Operation Mo*) due to carrier losses and operational uncertainty.
- Commanded on the American side by Admiral Frank Jack Fletcher in the *Yorktown*; Japanese force under Admiral Takao Kurita.
- Strategic impact: halted Japanese expansion southward and boosted American morale after the shock of Pearl Harbor.
- Neither side achieved a decisive tactical advantage; each side lost approximately equal tonnage and aircraft.
