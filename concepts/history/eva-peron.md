# Eva Perón

## summary

**Eva Perón** (1919–1952), known as "Evita," was an Argentine actress and wife of Juan Perón who became a powerful political figure in her own right. She championed workers' rights and social welfare, becoming a beloved symbol of Peronism and Argentine nationalism before her death from cancer at age 33.

## you gotta know

- Rose from poverty in rural Argentina to become a radio and film actress before marrying Juan Perón in 1945.
- Created the Eva Perón Foundation, which distributed money and aid to Argentina's poor, funding schools, hospitals, and housing.
- Campaigned for women's suffrage and universal voting rights, which were granted in 1947 under her influence.
- Earned the title "Spiritual Leader of the Nation" and maintained immense popularity despite lacking formal governmental power.
- After her death, her embalmed body was publicly displayed, and she became an iconic figure in Argentine culture and politics.
- Her life inspired the musical *Evita* by Andrew Lloyd Webber, cementing her status as a global cultural icon.
