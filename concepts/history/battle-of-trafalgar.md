# Battle of Trafalgar

## summary

The naval Battle of Trafalgar on October 21, 1805, off the coast of Spain, was fought between the combined French and Spanish fleets and the British Royal Navy under Admiral Horatio Nelson. Though Nelson died in the victory, his triumph destroyed Napoleon's hope of invading Britain and established British naval supremacy for over a century.

## you gotta know

- Fought on October 21, 1805, off Cape Trafalgar in the Strait of Gibraltar between 27 British ships and 33 French and Spanish vessels.
- Napoleon had gathered a Grande Armée at Boulogne to invade Britain; the battle removed any possibility of a successful crossing by eliminating French naval power.
- Nelson broke conventional line-of-battle tactics, dividing his fleet into two columns and cutting directly through the Franco-Spanish formation to overwhelm them in close combat.
- Nelson was mortally wounded during the battle by a sniper's bullet but lived long enough to hear of his victory; he became a British national hero and martyr.
- Combined French and Spanish losses were approximately 22 ships destroyed or captured, and over 12,000 casualties; British losses were around 1,600 casualties and no ships lost.
- The victory eliminated any realistic French naval threat to Britain and freed the Royal Navy to enforce a blockade of French ports.
- Trafalgar marked the beginning of the end of the age of sail and the rise of British global naval dominance.
