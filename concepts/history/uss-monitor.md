## summary

**USS Monitor** was the first ironclad warship built for the Union Navy, a revolutionary vessel with a rotating gun turret and low profile that fought the Confederate ironclad *CSS Virginia* to a stalemate at the Battle of Hampton Roads in 1862. Her radical design ended the era of wooden warships and ushered in the age of armored, steam-powered naval warfare.

## you gotta know

- Designed by Swedish engineer John Ericsson and launched in January 1862 with unprecedented innovations: an armored rotating turret housing two 11-inch guns and a low hull barely above the waterline.
- Fought *CSS Virginia* (*Merrimack*) at the Battle of Hampton Roads on March 9, 1862, the first clash between ironclad warships, which ended in a draw but proved the Monitor's superiority in maneuverability.
- Her turret design became the standard for battleship construction for the next century, fundamentally changing naval architecture worldwide.
- Sank in a storm off Cape Hatteras, North Carolina in December 1862 while under tow, though several Union monitors built on her design served through the Civil War.
- The battle at Hampton Roads is credited with making wooden warships obsolete overnight and accelerating the global shift to ironclad navies.
- Wreck discovered in 1973 and designated as the first National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration marine sanctuary.
