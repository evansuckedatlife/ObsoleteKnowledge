# Porfirio Díaz

## summary

*Porfirio Díaz* was a Mexican military general and statesman who ruled the country for 30 years (1876–1911), bringing modernisation and foreign investment but at the cost of authoritarian control and deep social inequality. His regime's collapse triggered the Mexican Revolution.

## you gotta know

- Became president through the *Plan of Tuxtepec* (1876), establishing the principle of *no reelection*—then violated it repeatedly.
- The *Porfiriato* (1876–1911) brought railroad expansion, industrialisation, and foreign investment, but concentrated wealth among a small elite.
- Suppressed indigenous peoples and campesinos, consolidating land into the hands of large haciendas and foreign corporations.
- Maintained order through the *Rurales*, a federal police force used to crush banditry and political opposition.
- By 1910, his ageing and apparent indefinite rule triggered the *Mexican Revolution* when reformer *Francisco Madero* challenged him.
- Fled Mexico in May 1911 and died in exile in France in 1915, watching the revolution he sparked unfold from afar.
