# Henry Kissinger

## summary

*Henry Kissinger* was National Security Adviser and then Secretary of State under President Richard Nixon from 1969 to 1977, architecting a grand strategy of geopolitical realism that sought to accommodate Soviet power, open diplomatic relations with China, and extricate the United States from Vietnam. His approach—rooted in the theory of *realpolitik*—prioritised strategic interests over ideological consistency, reshaping American foreign policy for the post-Cold War era.

## you gotta know

- Negotiated the *Paris Peace Accords* (1973), formally ending American involvement in the Vietnam War, though the agreement ultimately favoured North Vietnam.
- Orchestrated the secret opening to China (1971), culminating in President Nixon's historic visit and the establishment of diplomatic relations (1979), a geopolitical triumph.
- Pursued a policy of *détente* with the Soviet Union, seeking to manage Cold War tensions through trade, arms control agreements, and cultural exchange.
- Championed *realpolitik*—foreign policy based on national interest rather than ideology—a philosophy that justified support for authoritarian allies and non-interventionism in certain regions.
- Supported the 1973 coup in Chile that overthrew President Salvador Allende, prioritising anti-communist stability over democratic principle.
- Won the Nobel Peace Prize (1973) for his Vietnam negotiations, though the war's ultimate outcome undermined the acclaim.
