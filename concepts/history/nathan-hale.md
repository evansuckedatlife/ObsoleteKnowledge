## summary

*Nathan Hale* was an American patriot and spy for the Continental Army during the Revolutionary War. Executed by the British in 1776, he became a martyr to the cause of independence and an icon of patriotic sacrifice, famous for his purported last words: "I only regret that I have but one life to lose for my country."

## you gotta know

- Born in Connecticut; a Yale-educated schoolteacher who volunteered as a spy for General George Washington after the Battle of Long Island in August 1776.
- Operated behind British lines in New York, gathering intelligence about British troop strengths, supplies, and positions to assist the American cause.
- Captured by the British, possibly betrayed by a Loyalist cousin or caught with incriminating papers; court-martialed and hanged as a spy at dawn on September 22, 1776.
- His final words, reportedly "I only regret that I have but one life to lose for my country," became rallying cry for American independence, though the exact wording was recorded by witnesses after his death.
- Became the first American intelligence casualty and a symbol of patriotic duty, inspiring spy recruitment and sacrifice narratives throughout American history.
- Never married or had children; his brief life and noble death were overshadowed by the Revolution itself, leaving him a legend rather than a well-documented historical figure.
