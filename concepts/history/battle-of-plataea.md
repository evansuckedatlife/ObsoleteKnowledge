# Battle of Plataea

## summary

The *Battle of Plataea* (479 BC) was the final major land engagement of the Persian Wars, where a united Greek coalition decisively defeated the Persian general *Mardonius* near the city of Plataea in central Greece. The victory, combined with the concurrent naval Battle of Mycale, definitively ended Persian attempts to conquer mainland Greece.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 479 BC near Plataea in Boeotia, Greece.
- The Greek coalition, commanded by the Spartan regent *Pausanias*, mustered approximately 40,000 hoplites from multiple city-states, primarily Sparta and Athens.
- *Mardonius*, commanding the remaining Persian forces after *Xerxes* returned to Asia, fielded roughly 60,000 troops.
- The initial Greek advance was broken back by Persian cavalry and mounted archers, forcing a strategic retreat.
- *Pausanias* rallied the Spartan phalanx, which charged directly at Mardonius's position; the Persian general fell in hand-to-hand combat.
- The rout of the Persian center caused general collapse; estimates suggest 43,000 Persian casualties versus roughly 1,600 Greek deaths.
- The victory liberated Greek cities in Ionia and the Aegean, establishing Greek autonomy and cultural dominance for the next two centuries.
