# Battle of Zama

## summary

The *Battle of Zama* (202 BC) saw the Roman general *Scipio Africanus* decisively defeat *Hannibal* on African soil, ending the *Second Punic War* and confirming Rome as the Mediterranean's supreme power. The victory reversed Carthage's fortunes and made Scipio the greatest military hero of the Republican era.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 202 BC near Zama in North Africa (modern Tunisia).
- *Scipio Africanus* commanded approximately 30,000 Roman and allied troops; *Hannibal* fielded roughly 40,000, including Numidian cavalry.
- Scipio devised a tactical response to Hannibal's infantry—spacing his legions to allow the Carthaginian elephants to pass through harmlessly.
- Hannibal's traditionally superior cavalry was neutralized when *Massinissa's* Numidian riders, allied with Rome, attacked the Carthaginian horse.
- The resulting infantry clash saw Roman discipline and training overcome Carthaginian numbers; Hannibal's army was routed.
- Hannibal himself escaped but recognized Carthage's doom; he fled to Asia Minor.
- Carthage sued for peace, ceding Spain, paying indemnities, and sinking into long decline while Rome ascended.
