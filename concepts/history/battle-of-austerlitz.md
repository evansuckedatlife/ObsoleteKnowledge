# Battle of Austerlitz

## summary

The Battle of Austerlitz on December 2, 1805, was one of Napoleon's greatest tactical masterpieces, in which he defeated a combined Austro-Russian army near the town of Austerlitz in what is now the Czech Republic. Called the "Battle of the Three Emperors" because Napoleon, Tsar Alexander I, and Holy Roman Emperor Francis II all commanded on the field, it cemented French continental dominance and led directly to Austrian surrender.

## you gotta know

- Fought on December 2, 1805, between approximately 73,000 French troops and 85,000 Austro-Russian forces.
- The Austro-Russian coalition sought to trap Napoleon; he feigned weakness on his right flank to draw them south, then attacked their now-exposed center.
- Soult's corps smashed through the Austro-Russian center at the Pratzen Heights, splitting and routing the Allied army.
- Russian and Austrian cavalry reserves could not recover the day; combined losses exceeded 26,000, with thousands captured.
- The Treaty of Pressburg (Bratislava), signed weeks later, forced Austria to cede territory and Italy to France and Bavaria; it dissolved the Holy Roman Empire.
- Napoleon's victory secured his control of northern Europe and forced Britain to rely on naval and colonial power, rather than Continental allies.
- The battle is studied in military academies as a model of maneuver warfare and is often cited as the peak of Napoleonic tactical genius.
