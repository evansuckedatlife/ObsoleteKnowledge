# Richard Nixon

## summary

Richard Nixon (1913–1994) was the 37th President of the United States, serving from 1969 until his resignation in 1974. A Cold War strategist who pursued détente with the Soviet Union and opened diplomatic relations with China, he was also a polarizing figure whose presidency ended in disgrace due to the Watergate scandal. He represents both the summit of Cold War realpolitik and the depth of presidential abuse of power.

## you gotta know

- Built his career as a staunch anti-Communist; lost the 1960 presidential election to JFK and the 1962 California gubernatorial race, declaring his political death ("you won't have Nixon to kick around anymore").
- Returned to politics in 1968 with a "law and order" message; won the presidency with a narrow plurality, promising to end the Vietnam War "with honor" while pursuing aggressive bombing campaigns.
- Expanded the war into Cambodia (1970), sparking massive protests and the Kent State shooting; the war under Nixon killed tens of thousands and expanded beyond Vietnam without Congressional approval.
- Pioneered *détente* with the Soviet Union, signing the Strategic Arms Limitation Treaty (SALT I) and reducing Cold War tensions through diplomatic engagement.
- Opened relations with Communist China in 1971, a stunning reversal of Cold War orthodoxy that increased U.S. flexibility in the Cold War triangle.
- Created the *silent majority* political narrative, appealing to working-class and middle-class Americans he claimed the liberal establishment ignored; used targeted media and messaging to polarize the nation.
- Engaged in systematic abuse of power: compiled an *enemies list*, authorized the burglary of the Democratic National Committee headquarters at Watergate (June 1972), and orchestrated a cover-up that unraveled through 1973–1974.
- Resigned in August 1974 when impeachment and conviction became certain; the only president to resign from office, replaced by Vice President Gerald Ford.
