# Walter Mondale

## summary

**Walter Mondale** was the forty-second Vice President under Jimmy Carter (1977–1981) and a prominent figure in American liberalism whose brief presidential campaign in 1984 resulted in one of the worst electoral defeats in U.S. history. A veteran senator from Minnesota, Mondale embodied the values of the Great Society and New Deal liberalism during the Reagan era.

## you gotta know

- Vice President during the Iran hostage crisis and Soviet invasion of Afghanistan, during which the Carter administration took hardline stances on Cold War issues.
- Ran for president in 1984 and lost 49 of 50 states to Ronald Reagan, winning only Minnesota and the District of Columbia—the worst electoral performance by a major-party candidate since 1972.
- Notably promised in his campaign to raise taxes, an unusual stance that contributed to his decisive defeat and became a cautionary tale in Democratic politics.
- Served as U.S. Senator from Minnesota and was a leader of the Democratic Party's progressive wing, advocating for civil rights, social programs, and arms control.
- Later served as U.S. Ambassador to Japan and Norway, and ran again for Senate in 2002 at age 74, unsuccessfully seeking his old seat.
- Known for his intellectual seriousness and moral clarity on civil rights and opposition to the Vietnam War during his Senate career.
- Remained active in elder statesman circles, advocating for progressive causes and international diplomacy well into old age.
