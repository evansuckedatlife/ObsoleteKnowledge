# USS Constellation

## summary

**USS Constellation** (CV-64), a Kitty Hawk-class aircraft carrier commissioned in 1961, served through the Vietnam War and Cold War as one of America's premier power-projection platforms. Her long career spanned five decades of Cold War operations, forward deployments to the Mediterranean and Pacific, and active participation in multiple Cold War crises.

## you gotta know

- Commissioned in 1961 as the third ship of the Kitty Hawk-class, optimized for sustained power projection in distant theaters.
- Conducted multiple combat deployments during the Vietnam War, launching thousands of air sorties against North Vietnamese targets and providing sustained air support to allied forces.
- Participated in Cold War forward presence missions throughout her career, maintaining American naval supremacy in the Mediterranean and Western Pacific.
- Served as a flagship for multiple carrier strike groups, commanding task forces and demonstrating American commitment to regional allies from NATO to Japan.
- Distinguished for exceptional crew training programs and material condition maintenance, earning the Naval Efficiency Award multiple times during her operational career.
- Decommissioned in 2003 after 42 years of service and converted to a museum ship in San Diego, where her flight deck hosts historical exhibits and veteran gatherings.
