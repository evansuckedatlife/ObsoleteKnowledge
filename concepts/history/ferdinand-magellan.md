# Ferdinand Magellan

## summary

*Ferdinand Magellan* was a Portuguese navigator who led the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe, departing in 1519 under the Spanish flag. Though he died in the *Philippines* before completing the journey, the voyage he initiated proved the Earth's true size and demonstrated that the world was far larger than previously believed, with vast unexplored oceans beyond the Atlantic.

## you gotta know

- Initially served the Portuguese crown in the Indian Ocean trade routes, then shifted allegiance to Spain after a dispute with the Portuguese king.
- Proposed to the Spanish crown that he could reach the *East Indies* (modern Indonesia) by sailing west, crossing the Atlantic and then an unknown ocean beyond South America.
- Left Spain in 1519 with five ships and approximately 270 men; discovered the strait at the southern tip of South America, later named the *Strait of Magellan*, allowing passage from the Atlantic to the Pacific.
- Named the *Pacific Ocean* for its apparent calm waters (in contrast to the rocky strait).
- Sailed across the vast expanse of the Pacific without major sightings of land, suffering terrible hunger and disease; his crew called it the *Gran Mar Desconocido* (Great Unknown Sea).
- Reached the *Philippines* in 1521, where he was killed in the *Battle of Mactan* while attempting to convert and colonize the local population.
- Only one of his five ships, the *Vittoria*, returned to Spain in 1522 under the command of *Juan Sebastián Elcano*, with only 18 survivors—but they completed history's first circumnavigation.
