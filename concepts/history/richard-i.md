# Richard I

## summary

Richard I, widely known as Richard the Lionheart, was King of England from 1189 to 1199. Famed for his military prowess, he spent the vast majority of his reign abroad, principally leading the Third Crusade and defending his continental territories. Although a legendary figure of chivalry, his campaigns left England heavily taxed and financially drained.

## you gotta know

- He was a key commander of the *Third Crusade*, winning major victories at Acre and Arsuf but failing to recapture Jerusalem from Saladin.
- On his return from the Crusade, he was captured by Duke Leopold of Austria and ransomed for a massive sum that nearly bankrupted the English treasury.
- He spent less than a year of his ten-year reign in England, famously declaring he would sell London if he could find a buyer to fund his crusade.
- He died from an infected crossbow wound received while besieging the minor castle of Châlus-Chabrol in France.
- He was succeeded by his younger brother John, who had tried to usurp the throne during Richard's captivity.
