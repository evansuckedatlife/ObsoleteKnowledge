# Marcus Aurelius

## summary

Marcus Aurelius was a Roman emperor who ruled from AD 161 to 180, often considered the last of the "Five Good Emperors." He is equally famous as a practitioner of Stoic philosophy, writing deeply personal reflections while on military campaigns. His reign was dominated by almost constant warfare against Germanic tribes and the devastating effects of the Antonine Plague.

## you gotta know

- He authored the *Meditations*, a collection of personal writings in Greek outlining his Stoic philosophy and beliefs about duty and self-restraint.
- He spent much of his reign fighting the *Marcomannic Wars* along the Danube frontier against invading Germanic tribes.
- His reign was severely disrupted by the *Antonine Plague*, a devastating pandemic (possibly smallpox) brought back by troops returning from campaigns in the East.
- He initially co-ruled the empire alongside his adoptive brother *Lucius Verus* until Verus's death in 169.
- His death marked the end of the *Pax Romana*, as he was succeeded by his erratic and cruel biological son Commodus.

## connections

- [[commodus]] — his biological son and successor, whose disastrous reign broke the tradition of meritocratic adoption and ended the era of the "Five Good Emperors."
- [[lucius-verus]] — his co-emperor during the first eight years of his reign.
- [[antoninus-pius]] — his adoptive father and predecessor.
