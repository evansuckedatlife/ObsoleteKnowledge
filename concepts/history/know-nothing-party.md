# Know-Nothing Party

## summary

The Know-Nothing Party, officially the American Party, was a nativist political movement of the 1850s that opposed immigration and the political influence of Roman Catholics. Driven by fears of Irish and German immigration and anti-Catholic prejudice, it achieved temporary prominence by winning several governorships and House seats before collapsing as the slavery crisis overshadowed its agenda and more moderate Republicans co-opted its electoral base.

## you gotta know

- The party took its name from the response members gave when questioned about its secret rituals: "I know nothing," hence the nickname.
- The Know-Nothings opposed Irish and German Catholic immigration, viewing Catholics as loyal to the Pope rather than the United States and threats to Protestant American culture.
- In the 1856 presidential election, Know-Nothing candidate Millard Fillmore (a former Whig president) won 21% of the popular vote and 8 electoral votes, the party's peak showing.
- The party demanded stricter naturalization laws, longer waiting periods for citizenship, and bans on foreign-born citizens holding office.
- Internal divisions over slavery—the party tried to avoid the issue to maintain Northern and Southern support—weakened it as the sectional crisis intensified.
- By 1860, the party had effectively dissolved; its anti-immigrant program was abandoned, though nativist sentiment resurged periodically (Ku Klux Klan revivals, 1920s restriction era).
