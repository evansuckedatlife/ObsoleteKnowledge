## summary

In *September-October 1781*, American and French forces under George Washington surrounded British general *Lord Cornwallis* at Yorktown, Virginia. Trapped between American infantry, French Navy ships blocking escape by water, and no hope of relief, Cornwallis surrendered on *October 19, 1781*, ending major combat operations. Yorktown effectively sealed the British defeat and led to peace negotiations that granted American independence.

## you gotta know

- Cornwallis had retreated to Yorktown with roughly 7,000 men; Washington and French general *Comte de Rochambeau* converged with approximately 17,000 troops and artillery.
- The French Navy's victory at the *Battle of the Capes* prevented British reinforcement or evacuation by sea.
- American and French artillery bombarded the British defenses; the Allies captured a series of outer fortifications.
- Cornwallis surrendered approximately 7,000 soldiers, the largest British capitulation of the war and a decisive blow to Britain's ability to continue fighting.
- British losses and the scale of surrender shocked Parliament; peace negotiations began within months.
- The victory was as much a French triumph (Rochambeau's army, the naval blockade) as an American one, cementing Franco-American alliance.
