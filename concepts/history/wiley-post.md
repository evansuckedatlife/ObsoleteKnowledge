# Wiley Post

## summary

*Wiley Post* was a pioneering aviator of the 1930s who set multiple speed and distance records, designed the first practical pressure cabin for high-altitude flight, and circumnavigated the globe. His rapid progression from postal pilot to record-setter exemplified the daring spirit of aviation's golden age before his death in 1935.

## you gotta know

- Circumnavigated the world in 1931 in eight days and 16 hours, the fastest around-the-world flight at the time.
- Set multiple transcontinental speed records, including a 1934 flight from Los Angeles to New York in under 7 hours.
- Designed the first practical pressure cabin to enable high-altitude flight; his innovations extended altitude records.
- Flew the *Lockheed Vega*, a fast monoplane that became his signature aircraft for record attempts.
- Wore a patch over one eye, a distinctive feature from an earlier accident that didn't slow his career.
- Died in a 1935 plane crash near Point Barrow, Alaska, with humorist Will Rogers aboard.
- His influence on high-altitude flight and aircraft design outlasted his brief career.
