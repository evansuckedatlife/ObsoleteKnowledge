# New Deal

## summary

The New Deal was Franklin Delano Roosevelt's (FDR) comprehensive program of federal legislation and initiatives, enacted between 1933 and 1939, to address the economic crisis and social hardship of the *Great Depression*. It fundamentally expanded the role of the federal government through "alphabet agencies"—relief programs (e.g., the *Civilian Conservation Corps*), recovery initiatives (e.g., the *National Recovery Administration*), and reforms (e.g., *Social Security*)—intended to provide immediate relief, stimulate economic recovery, and restructure the American economic and social system. The New Deal represented a dramatic shift from laissez-faire capitalism and established the modern welfare state.

## you gotta know

### the three Rs: Relief, Recovery, Reform
- *Relief*: immediate aid to the poor and unemployed. The *Federal Emergency Relief Administration* (FERA) and later the *Works Progress Administration* (WPA) employed millions in public works projects.
- *Recovery*: stimulating economic growth through government spending and regulation. The *National Recovery Administration* (NRA) sought to coordinate business recovery; the *Tennessee Valley Authority* (TVA) managed regional economic development.
- *Reform*: structural changes to prevent future depressions. *Social Security* provided unemployment insurance, old-age pensions, and aid to the disabled; the *Securities and Exchange Commission* (SEC) regulated stock markets; the *National Labor Relations Board* (NLRB) protected workers' right to unionize.

### major agencies and programs
- *Civilian Conservation Corps* (CCC): employed young men in conservation and public lands projects.
- *Works Progress Administration* (WPA): the largest relief program, employing millions in public works, art, theater, and writing projects.
- *Tennessee Valley Authority* (TVA): a regional development corporation managing hydroelectric power and economic development in the Southeast.
- *Social Security Act* (1935): provided old-age pensions, unemployment insurance, and aid to the disabled.
- *National Labor Relations Act* (1935): protected workers' right to organize and bargain collectively.
- *Securities Exchange Act* (1934): regulated stock markets and created the SEC.

### constitutional and political impact
- The New Deal expanded federal power dramatically; the Supreme Court initially struck down some New Deal legislation as unconstitutional, but reversed course after FDR's "Court-packing" threat.
- The *Wickard v. Filburn* case (1942) greatly expanded Congress's power under the Commerce Clause.
- The New Deal created a permanent expansion of federal bureaucracy and welfare provisions.
- It established the *Fair Deal* and later *Great Society* as successors to progressive reform.
