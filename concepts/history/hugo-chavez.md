# Hugo Chávez

## summary

**Hugo Rafael Chávez Frías** (1954–2013) was a Venezuelan military officer and politician who led a Bolivarian Revolution, transforming Venezuela into an anti-American, socialist-oriented state. He served as president for 14 years and became a polarizing figure for his charismatic populism and radical redistributionist policies.

## you gotta know

- Led a failed military coup in 1992, which earned him fame among Venezuela's poor; released from prison, he ran for president and won in 1998.
- Implemented the *Bolivarian Revolution*, a sweeping program of constitutional rewriting, wealth redistribution, and expanded state control of oil revenues.
- Used Venezuela's vast oil wealth to fund social programs (healthcare, education, housing) and regional aid to allied Latin American nations.
- Survived a coup attempt in 2002 and consolidated executive power by purging political opponents from the judiciary and military.
- Moved Venezuela toward authoritarianism through supermajority control of congress and manipulation of referenda; dissidents were imprisoned and media outlets were shut down.
- Maintained mass support among the poor through charismatic leadership and populist rhetoric; polarized the educated middle class.
- Died in office in 2013; his successor Nicolás Maduro continued and radicalized his policies, leading to Venezuela's economic collapse.

## connections

- [[salvador-allende]] — Chávez explicitly modeled himself after Allende but avoided his democratic vulnerability by consolidating power.
- [[juan-peron]] — similar populist and redistributionist approach to politics.
- [[augusto-pinochet]] — Chávez positioned himself as antithetical to Pinochet's neoliberalism and human-rights violations.
