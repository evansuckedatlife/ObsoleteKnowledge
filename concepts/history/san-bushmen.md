# San (Bushmen)

## summary

The *San*, historically called *Bushmen*, are the indigenous hunter-gatherer peoples of southern Africa, predating the Bantu and European arrivals by thousands of years. They possess one of the oldest continuous cultures and are renowned for their exceptional tracking knowledge, distinctive language click consonants, and remarkable rock art spanning tens of thousands of years.

## you gotta know

- Inhabited *southern Africa* (modern-day South Africa, Botswana, Namibia, and surrounding regions) for at least 20,000 years before Bantu migrations and European colonization.
- Speak *Khoisan* languages featuring distinctive *click consonants* (four to five click types) produced by tongue, teeth, or palate; these sounds distinguish their languages from surrounding African language families.
- Practiced specialized hunting and gathering with unmatched tracking skills; accumulated deep ecological knowledge of game animals, water sources, and edible plants across diverse landscapes.
- Created extraordinary rock art (*rock paintings and engravings*) spanning 40,000+ years; depict animals, human figures, and spiritual visions; many sites remain sacred.
- Developed *trance dance* rituals for healing, spiritual communication, and community bonding; shamanic practitioners (*shamans* or *healers*) held high status.
- Faced severe marginalization and violence under colonial rule and post-colonial governments; many were displaced from ancestral lands and confined to marginal territories.
- Population today numbers roughly 100,000–150,000 across southern Africa; language and cultural preservation efforts are ongoing.
