# Franks

## summary

The *Franks* were a Germanic people who rose from the Rhine frontier to become the dominant power in Western Europe, establishing kingdoms that evolved into the Merovingian and Carolingian empires. Unlike other Germanic peoples whose kingdoms crumbled, the Franks built a lasting political and cultural legacy that shaped medieval Europe and eventually established the foundations of France and the Holy Roman Empire.

## you gotta know

- Originated along the Rhine as Roman foederati (allied troops) before expanding into Gaul after the empire's collapse.
- Under *Clovis I*, unified Frankish territories and defeated the last Roman forces in Gaul, establishing Frankish dominance and converting to Catholic Christianity to gain Roman provincial support.
- The *Merovingian dynasty* ruled for three centuries, maintaining Roman administrative structures while gradually consolidating Germanic warrior culture.
- The *Carolingian dynasty*, particularly *Charlemagne*, transformed the Franks into an empire that revived imperial authority and patronized learning, creating a cultural renaissance.
- Developed a sophisticated feudal system and maintained written law codes that preserved both Roman and Germanic legal traditions.
- Unlike the *Ostrogoths*, *Visigoths*, and *Vandals*, the Frankish kingdom endured and evolved into the medieval kingdoms of France and Germany.
- Frankish military innovation, including heavy cavalry and the stirrup, became dominant in medieval warfare.

## connections

- [[charlemagne]] — the greatest Frankish king; created the first Western empire since Rome.
- [[merovingian]] — the dynasty that first unified the Franks under *Clovis I*.
- [[carolingian]] — the dynasty that transformed Frankish power into a continental empire.
- [[gaul]] — the Roman province they conquered and made the center of their kingdom.
- [[clovis]] — founder of Frankish dominance; his conversion to Catholicism legitimized Frankish rule.
