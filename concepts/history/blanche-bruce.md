# Blanche Bruce

## summary

*Blanche Kelso Bruce* (1841–1898) was the first African American elected to a full six-year term in the United States Senate, representing Mississippi from 1875 to 1881. A planter, educator, and skilled orator, he navigated the turbulent final years of Reconstruction with pragmatism and dignity.

## you gotta know

- Second Black U.S. Senator overall, but first to win a full term; elected by the Mississippi state legislature in 1874.
- Born enslaved in Virginia; educated in the North and became a freedmen's school principal before entering politics and acquiring plantation land.
- Advocated for civil rights within the Senate chamber, giving notable speeches defending the rights of freedmen and Indigenous peoples.
- Served as register of the *Treasury* under President James Garfield, one of the highest federal offices held by a Black official in the 19th century.
- Witnessed the collapse of Republican power in Mississippi as *Reconstruction* ended and white Democrats regained control of state government.
- Later returned to prominence in Republican circles and was appointed minister to *Brazil*, making him a rare Black diplomatic representative of the era.
