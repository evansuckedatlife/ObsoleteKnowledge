# Bernardo O'Higgins

## summary

**Bernardo O'Higgins Riquelme** (1778–1842) was a Chilean military leader and statesman who, working alongside José de San Martín, liberated Chile from Spanish colonial rule and served as the nation's first supreme director. He is revered as the founder of the Chilean republic.

## you gotta know

- Son of an Irish-born Spanish official and a Chilean creole; raised in Spain before returning to South America to join the independence movement.
- Commanded Chilean forces alongside San Martín during the crossing of the Andes (1817) and the decisive Battle of Maipú (1818).
- Served as Supreme Director of Chile from 1818 to 1823, establishing the legal and institutional foundations of the Chilean state.
- Implemented land redistribution, weakened the Catholic Church's political influence, and abolished aristocratic titles to promote republicanism.
- Exiled himself to Peru in 1823 when internal political opposition mounted; Chile's rulers rejected his more radical reforms.
- Remained a revered figure in Chilean history despite not achieving all his goals; his name is honored as the "Father of Chile."
