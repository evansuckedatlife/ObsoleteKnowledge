## summary

*Robert E. Lee* was the commanding general of the Confederate Army of Northern Virginia and the most celebrated military figure of the American Civil War. Though his brilliant tactical maneuvers and inspired leadership of the South's best-equipped army prolonged the war and won him the devotion of his soldiers, his ultimate defeat by Ulysses S. Grant marked the end of the Confederacy and slavery in America. His legacy remains contested: celebrated by the South as a noble warrior defending his homeland, condemned by critics as the military arm of the slaveholding rebellion.

## you gotta know

- Born in Virginia in 1807 into an old planter aristocracy; graduated from West Point and served with distinction in the Mexican-American War, establishing himself as one of the U.S. Army's most promising officers.
- Turned down Lincoln's offer to command the Union armies, instead accepting command of Virginia's forces and eventually the Confederacy's principal army, the *Army of Northern Virginia*.
- Won a series of stunning tactical victories despite being consistently outnumbered: at the *Second Battle of Bull Run* (1862), at *Chancellorsville* (1863, his greatest victory despite the loss of his trusted subordinate *Stonewall Jackson*), and in the *Peninsula Campaign*.
- Invaded the North twice—after Antietam in 1862 and leading to Gettysburg in 1863—attempting to bring the war to Union territory and secure European recognition for the Confederacy; both invasions were repulsed.
- Fought the defensive *Siege of Petersburg* (1864–65) for nearly a year against Grant's overwhelming numbers, demonstrating tactical mastery in the face of inevitable attrition.
- Surrendered at *Appomattox Court House* to Ulysses S. Grant in April 1865, effectively ending the war; treated his defeated army with dignity and counseled reconciliation afterward.
- After the war, served as president of Washington College (now Washington and Lee University), declining further military positions and working toward sectional healing.

## connections

- [[ulysses-s-grant]] — his counterpart and eventual conqueror; their respect for each other transcended their opposing causes.
- [[thomas-stonewall-jackson]] — his greatest subordinate and trusted lieutenant, mortally wounded at Chancellorsville in the moment of his greatest victory.
- [[james-longstreet]] — his "Old Warhorse," chief lieutenant whose tactical conservatism sometimes clashed with Lee's aggression.
- [[american-civil-war]] — the consuming struggle he personified, his every campaign defining the war's trajectory.
- [[confederate-states-of-america]] — the nation whose military cause he became the face of, despite earlier service to the United States.
- [[battle-of-gettysburg]] — his greatest and most consequential defeat, marking the turning point of the war.
- [[appomattox-court-house]] — the site of his surrender, symbolizing the end of the Confederacy and slavery.
- [[virginia]] — his beloved home state, whose honor he believed he was defending.
