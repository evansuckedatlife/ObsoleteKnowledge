# House of Bourbon

## summary

The **House of Bourbon** rose to power in France in 1589 and became Europe's greatest dynasty of the 17th and 18th centuries, epitomized by *Louis XIV*, the "Sun King." Bourbon monarchs ruled France, Spain, and parts of Italy with absolute authority and baroque splendor, leaving an indelible mark on European culture, art, and political theory before revolutionary upheaval swept them away.

## you gotta know

- Founded when *Henry IV* ascended to the French throne in 1589 after the Wars of Religion; first Bourbon king brought internal peace to France.
- *Louis XIV* (r. 1643–1715), the "Sun King," epitomized absolute monarchy—his motto "L'État, c'est moi" (I am the state) defined centralized royal power for centuries.
- Built the *Palace of Versailles*, the grandest royal palace in Europe, as a seat of power and an instrument of control over the nobility.
- Fought the *War of Spanish Succession* (1701–1714) to establish a Bourbon on the Spanish throne; successful but weakened France's regional dominance.
- Ruled Spain from 1700 onwards as a cadet branch; Spanish Bourbons remained on the throne into the modern era.
- *Marie Antoinette* and *Louis XVI*, the last Bourbon monarchs of France, were executed during the French Revolution (1789–1799).
- Their court culture—fashion, art, literature, and manners—became the model for European aristocracy.
