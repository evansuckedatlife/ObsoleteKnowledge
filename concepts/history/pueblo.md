# Pueblo

## summary

The *Pueblo* peoples are a diverse group of Southwestern sedentary cultures inhabiting the Rio Grande Valley and adjacent regions of present-day New Mexico and Arizona, renowned for their multi-story adobe dwellings, sophisticated irrigation agriculture, pottery, and kivas (ceremonial chambers). Among North America's oldest continuously inhabited settlements, they developed unique governance systems and preserved indigenous traditions through centuries of Spanish colonial and U.S. expansion.

## you gotta know

- Comprise multiple distinct Keresan-, Tanoan-, Uto-Aztecan-, and Siouan-speaking peoples; named "Pueblo" (town) by Spanish conquistadors for their settlement pattern of compact villages.
- Constructed distinctive pueblo architecture: multi-story adobe structures with communal kivas (circular ceremonial chambers) and coordinated agricultural terracing systems.
- Developed advanced pottery traditions with distinctive geometric and figurative designs; maintained complex ceremonial calendars and kachina (spirit-ancestor) traditions.
- Under Spanish colonial rule (1598–1821), forced to adopt Christianity and labor systems; *Popé* led the *Pueblo Revolt* (1680), a coordinated uprising that temporarily expelled Spanish colonizers from the region.
- Practiced democratic governance through pueblo councils; agricultural surplus and trade networks connected them across the Southwest and into distant regions.
- Today about 20 Pueblo nations exist as federally recognized tribes; each maintains distinct governance, language, and cultural traditions; Pueblo communities are among the most culturally conservative in the United States.
