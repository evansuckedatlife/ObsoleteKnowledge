# Christopher Columbus

## summary

*Christopher Columbus* was a Genoese navigator whose 1492 voyage across the Atlantic Ocean reached the Caribbean islands, initiating sustained European contact with the Americas. Though not the first European to reach the New World, his expedition opened the door to the Age of Exploration and permanent colonial settlement, reshaping global history despite his brutal governance of the colonies he administered.

## you gotta know

- Sailed under the Spanish flag, commanding three ships (*Niña*, *Pinta*, *Santa María*) on his first transatlantic voyage, which lasted 70 days.
- Believed he could reach *Asia* by sailing west across the Atlantic, underestimating the Earth's circumference and unaware of the Americas' existence.
- Landed in the *Bahamas* in 1492 and subsequently explored islands in what he called the *West Indies*, never realizing he had discovered continents unknown to Europeans.
- Made four voyages across the Atlantic between 1492 and 1502, always maintaining he had reached Asia.
- Established the colony of *La Isabela* on *Hispaniola* in 1494, the first European settlement in the Caribbean; his rule was marked by harsh treatment of indigenous peoples and colonists alike.
- Enslaved thousands of indigenous people and was eventually arrested and sent back to Spain in chains in 1500 due to reports of his violent governance.
- His legacy is deeply contested: celebrated as a discoverer who opened the age of exploration, but also recognized as a brutal colonizer whose arrival initiated centuries of indigenous suffering.
