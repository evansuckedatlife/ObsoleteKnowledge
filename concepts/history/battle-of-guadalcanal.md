# Battle of Guadalcanal

## summary

Fought August 1942 to February 1943, Guadalcanal was the first major ground offensive launched by the Allies in the Pacific. American forces, seeking to prevent Japanese consolidation of the Solomon Islands, secured the airfield and gradually overcame determined Japanese resistance in a grueling six-month campaign that tested American logistics and resolve.

## you gotta know

- Campaign lasted six months; island airfield codenamed *Henderson Field* became the strategic prize.
- American forces initially landed with one Marine division under Major General Alexander Vandegrift; reinforced by Army units.
- Japanese commander General Hyakutake reinforced the garrison repeatedly, leading to numerous failed counteroffensives; Japanese lost over 24,000 soldiers.
- American losses: approximately 1,600 killed and 4,200 wounded; sustained by superior logistics and reinforcement capacity.
- Naval engagements (*Battle of the Coral Sea, Savo Island, Tassafaronga*) raged throughout; both sides suffered carrier and destroyer losses.
- U.S. victory established a foothold for future advances up the Solomon Islands chain toward the Philippines.
- Demonstrated American industrial advantage—the ability to replace ships, aircraft, and troops faster than Japan.
