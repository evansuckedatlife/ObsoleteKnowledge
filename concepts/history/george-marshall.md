# George Marshall

## summary

*George Marshall* was Secretary of State under President Harry S. Truman from 1947 to 1949, architecting the postwar recovery of Europe through the Marshall Plan and establishing the foundational diplomatic architecture of the Cold War. As a five-star general and statesman, Marshall embodied American commitment to reconstructing a democratic Europe and containing Soviet expansion through multilateral institutions and economic partnership.

## you gotta know

- Authored the *Marshall Plan* (1948–1952), a $12 billion program to rebuild war-devastated Western Europe—one of history's most effective foreign aid initiatives.
- Won the Nobel Peace Prize (1953) for his vision of European recovery and collective security.
- Oversaw the founding of the *North Atlantic Treaty Organisation* (NATO, 1949), the first peacetime military alliance in American history.
- Navigated American diplomacy in the early *Cold War*, supporting the creation of the State of *Israel* (1948) while managing Arab relations.
- Advocated for the *Truman Doctrine*, providing military and economic aid to nations resisting Soviet expansion, particularly Greece and Turkey.
- Believed in the possibility of negotiated settlement but recognised the incompatibility of American democracy and Soviet totalitarianism.
