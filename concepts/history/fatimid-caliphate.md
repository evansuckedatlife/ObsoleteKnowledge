# Fatimid Caliphate

## summary

The *Fatimid Caliphate* (909–1171 CE) was a Shiite dynasty centered in North Africa and later Egypt, which claimed descent from *Fatima* (Muhammad's daughter) and challenged the Sunni Abbasid caliphate's legitimacy for over 250 years. At their height, the Fatimids controlled Egypt, Sicily, and the Mediterranean trade routes, becoming one of the medieval world's wealthiest powers before being overtaken by the Ayyubid dynasty.

## you gotta know

- Named after *Fatima*, Muhammad's daughter, and claimed her line carried divine right to rule, a fundamental Shiite doctrine that delegitimized Sunni leadership.
- Rose in *Tunisia* under *Abdallah al-Mahdi* in 909 CE, spreading westward through North Africa before conquering *Egypt* in 969 CE and establishing *Cairo* as their capital.
- Founded *Al-Azhar University* in Cairo, one of Islam's oldest and most prestigious institutions, which remains a center of Islamic learning to this day.
- Controlled the *Red Sea* and *Mediterranean* trade routes, accumulating vast wealth from spice, silk, and strategic commerce, making Cairo a glittering cosmopolitan center.
- Practiced relative religious tolerance toward Christians, Jews, and other minorities within their realm, fostering a pluralistic urban culture distinct from Sunni neighbors.
- Weakened by Crusader invasions in the Levant and internal administrative decay; finally fell to *Saladin's* Ayyubid dynasty in 1171 CE, which restored Sunni orthodoxy to Egypt.
