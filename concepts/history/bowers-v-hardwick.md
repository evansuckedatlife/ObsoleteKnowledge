# Bowers v. Hardwick

## summary

*Bowers v. Hardwick* (1986) is the controversial Supreme Court decision that upheld Georgia's anti-sodomy law, denying that the Constitution protects a right to private consensual same-sex conduct. The 5–4 ruling became a symbol of judicial opposition to LGBTQ rights and was overturned 17 years later by *Lawrence v. Texas*.

## you gotta know

- Decided June 30, 1986 by a 5–4 margin, with Justice Byron White writing the majority opinion.
- Upheld a Georgia statute criminalizing sodomy between consenting adults, even in private.
- The majority rejected the claim that a constitutional right to privacy extends to same-sex conduct, distinguishing it from prior privacy cases like *Roe v. Wade*.
- Justice White's opinion stated that "none of the rights announced in [prior cases] bear any resemblance to the claimed constitutional right of homosexuals to engage in acts of sodomy."
- Chief Justice Burger's concurrence called homosexuality "anathema" to centuries of moral teachings.
- Dissenters, including Justice Blackmun, argued the case was ultimately about the right to be let alone in intimate matters.
- Became widely condemned by LGBTQ advocates and civil rights organizations as a major setback, galvanizing opposition to discriminatory laws.
