## summary

In *September-October 1777*, American forces commanded by *Horatio Gates* defeated British general *John Burgoyne's* army near Saratoga, New York. Burgoyne's strategy to divide the colonies by invading from Canada failed when he became surrounded and outnumbered. His surrender convinced France that the Americans could win, transforming French financial and military support into open alliance and global war.

## you gotta know

- Part of a larger British strategy to capture the Hudson River Valley and isolate New England.
- Burgoyne's supply lines were cut; he faced a growing American force and was unable to retreat safely.
- The climactic phase saw two major engagements: Freeman's Farm (*September 19*) and Bemis Heights (*October 7*).
- Burgoyne surrendered on *October 17, 1777* with approximately 5,800 men, the first major American victory against a full British army in the field.
- *Marquis de Lafayette*, a French volunteer, fought alongside American forces and impressed Congress.
- The victory triggered French diplomatic recognition of American independence and the formal Franco-American alliance (*February 1778*), bringing French warships and soldiers into the war.
