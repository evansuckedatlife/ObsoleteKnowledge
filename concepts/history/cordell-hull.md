# Cordell Hull

## summary

*Cordell Hull* was Secretary of State under President Franklin D. Roosevelt from 1933 to 1944, making him the longest-serving Secretary of State in American history. A devoted internationalist, Hull championed free trade, multilateral cooperation, and the nascent United Nations, working to navigate the treacherous waters of the Great Depression and World War II while attempting to keep the United States on a path toward peace and democratic collective action.

## you gotta know

- Served as Secretary of State for 12 years (1933–1944), the longest tenure of any person in that office.
- Negotiated *Reciprocal Trade Agreements*, reducing tariffs and promoting free trade during the isolationist 1930s.
- Championed the *Good Neighbor Policy* toward Latin America, renouncing interventionism and strengthening hemispheric ties.
- Pushed for American participation in the United Nations, laying diplomatic groundwork throughout World War II despite initial isolationist opposition.
- Worked to maintain American neutrality in the 1930s while gradually building support for aid to Britain and the Soviet Union after 1941.
- Won the Nobel Peace Prize (1945) for his role in founding the United Nations—the capstone to his lifelong commitment to international cooperation.
