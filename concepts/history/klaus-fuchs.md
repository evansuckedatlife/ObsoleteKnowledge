## summary

*Klaus Fuchs* was a German-born British physicist who worked on the Manhattan Project and transmitted atomic bomb secrets to the Soviet Union during World War II. His exposure in 1950 triggered the FBI's investigation that led to the arrests of Julius and Ethel Rosenberg, making him the catalyst for Cold War espionage investigations.

## you gotta know

- Fled Nazi Germany as a Communist sympathizer in 1933; worked as a theoretical physicist in Britain before being recruited by British intelligence to work on the American atomic bomb project.
- Recruited by Soviet intelligence in 1941 while working in Britain; continued spying after joining the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos, providing detailed technical information about bomb design and uranium enrichment.
- Transmitted hand-written notes and technical sketches to his Soviet handler, giving Moscow access to the most advanced scientific secrets of the war; his information accelerated Soviet atomic development by years.
- Confessed to his espionage in 1950 after the FBI began investigating Soviet penetration of the atomic program; his cooperation led directly to the identification and arrest of Julius Rosenberg and others.
- Imprisoned in Britain for nine years; released in 1959 and moved to East Germany, where he worked as a physicist for the communist state until his death in 1988.
- Become a turning point in Cold War espionage: his exposure initiated the FBI's Cambridge spy investigation, which expanded to uncover Aldrich Ames decades later.
