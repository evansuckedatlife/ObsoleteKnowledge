# Charles I

## summary

Charles I was King of England, Scotland, and Ireland from 1625 until his execution in 1649. His belief in the divine right of kings and his constant power struggles with Parliament led directly to the English Civil War. Following the defeat of the Royalist forces, he was tried, convicted of high treason, and executed, resulting in the brief abolition of the monarchy.

## you gotta know

- He was executed by beheading in 1649 outside the *Banqueting House* in Whitehall, London, following his trial by a High Court of Justice.
- He ruled without Parliament for eleven years (1629–1640), a period known as the *Personal Rule* or the "Eleven Years' Tyranny."
- His attempts to levy the controversial *Ship Money* tax during peacetime to fund the navy without parliamentary consent outraged the public.
- He triggered the Bishops' Wars by attempting to force the Anglican Book of Common Prayer on the Presbyterian Church of Scotland.
- He was defeated in the *English Civil War* by the Parliamentarian New Model Army led by generals like Thomas Fairfax and Oliver Cromwell.
