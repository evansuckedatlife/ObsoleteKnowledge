# Aboriginal Australians

## summary

The *Aboriginal Australians* are the indigenous peoples of Australia, with continuous cultural presence spanning at least 65,000 years, making them among the world's oldest living cultures. They developed sophisticated kinship systems, sacred sites, and land management practices including controlled burning, and maintained a deep spiritual connection to the continent codified in Dreamtime narratives.

## you gotta know

- Have inhabited *Australia* for at least 65,000 years, with some evidence suggesting 100,000+ years of presence, making their culture extraordinarily ancient.
- Developed complex kinship systems and social structures organizing hundreds of distinct language groups and nations with independent governance and law systems.
- Practiced *Dreamtime* (or *Dreaming*), a spiritual philosophy encoding creation narratives, law, geography, and relationships to land in sacred stories and song cycles.
- Used *dot painting* (acrylic dots on canvas) as a modern artistic practice to represent Dreamtime narratives and sacred geography, gaining international recognition in the 20th century.
- Practiced sophisticated land management including *cool burning* (controlled fires) to shape ecosystems and improve hunting.
- Created detailed rock art across the continent, some sites showing continuous cultural use for thousands of years.
- Speak or spoke over 250 distinct languages; many are extinct or critically endangered; revival efforts are ongoing.
