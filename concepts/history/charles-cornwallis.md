# Charles Cornwallis

## summary

*Charles Cornwallis* was the senior British general in the later stages of the American Revolution and the commander of British forces at *Yorktown*, where he surrendered in 1781, effectively ending the war. An able tactician and professional soldier, he was ultimately defeated by French naval superiority and the coordination of American and French forces rather than by British military inferiority.

## you gotta know

- Took command of the southern theater after the British capture of Charleston and pursued an aggressive strategy of invasion and control, seeking to rally loyalists and defeat the Continental Army in decisive battle.
- Was frustrated by *Nathanael Greene's* strategy of evasion and attrition, winning pitched battles such as *Camden* (1780) but losing control of the region as his supply lines were cut and his army weakened.
- Retreated into Virginia in 1781, eventually marching to *Yorktown* in pursuit of *Lafayette's* small force, only to find himself trapped by Washington's approach from the north and a French fleet blockading his escape by sea.
- Surrendered at *Yorktown* (October 1781) after a brief siege, effectively ending major combat operations and securing American independence.
- Was respected even by the Americans as an honorable opponent and a capable professional; he did not blame the soldiers under him for the defeat but attributed it to lack of naval support and strategic overextension.
- After the war, served as Governor-General of India and continued his career with distinction, dying as a respected elder statesman of the British Empire.
