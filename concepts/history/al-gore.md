# Al Gore

## summary

**Al Gore** was the forty-fifth Vice President under Bill Clinton (1993–2001) and a senator from Tennessee whose environmental advocacy and technological interests set him apart among vice presidents. Though he lost a contested presidential election in 2000 to George W. Bush, Gore later became the world's most prominent climate change advocate, shifting his legacy from electoral politics to environmental activism.

## you gotta know

- Vice President during the longest period of economic expansion in American history and the push toward government surpluses in the late 1990s.
- Lost the 2000 presidential election in one of the closest and most controversial elections in American history, decided by the Supreme Court's intervention in *Bush v. Gore*.
- Authored *An Inconvenient Truth* (2006), a book and documentary film that brought climate science to popular audiences and earned him the Nobel Peace Prize in 2007.
- Championed technological innovation and the information superhighway as part of Clinton's modernization agenda, helping shape early internet policy.
- Served as a U.S. Senator from Tennessee before his vice presidency, where he advocated for environmental protection and arms control.
- Created an activist organization called the Climate Reality Project, establishing himself as the world's leading voice on climate change and renewable energy.
- Nearly ran for president again in 2008 but ultimately declined, focusing instead on environmental and humanitarian causes.
