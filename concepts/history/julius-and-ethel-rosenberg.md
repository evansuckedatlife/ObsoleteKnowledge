# Julius and Ethel Rosenberg

## summary

*Julius and Ethel Rosenberg* were American Communist spies who passed atomic bomb secrets to the Soviet Union during the Cold War. Executed by electric chair in 1953, they became martyrs to some and traitors to others, their trial and death a defining moment of anti-Communist hysteria in 1950s America.

## you gotta know

- Julius, an electrical engineer at Los Alamos, was recruited by Soviet intelligence and ran a spy ring that transmitted classified nuclear research to Moscow during World War II and after.
- Ethel, Julius's wife, typed up handwritten notes from her brother David Greenglass (another spy) detailing uranium enrichment procedures and bomb design, though her exact level of involvement remains contested.
- Arrested in 1950 during the FBI's counterintelligence sweep following Klaus Fuchs's exposure; convicted under the Espionage Act based on testimony from Greenglass and others.
- Sentenced to death in April 1951; their execution on June 19, 1953 made them the first American civilians executed for espionage, drawing international protest and conspiracy theories that persist today.
- Some historians argue Ethel was largely peripheral to the espionage and was prosecuted partly to pressure Julius into confession; declassified Soviet files confirm Julius's guilt but have done little to resolve Ethel's role.
