# 303 Creative v. Elenis

## summary

*303 Creative v. Elenis* (2023) is the Supreme Court decision that granted a website designer a religious exemption from Colorado's public accommodation law, allowing her to refuse service to same-sex couples. The 6–3 ruling, written by Justice Neil Gorsuch, expanded religious liberty protections by holding that compelled speech claims receive heightened First Amendment protection in public accommodations disputes.

## you gotta know

- Decided June 30, 2023 by a 6–3 margin, with Justice Neil Gorsuch writing the majority opinion.
- The designer, Lorie Smith, sought an exemption from Colorado's Public Accommodations Act to refuse to design wedding websites for same-sex couples based on her religious beliefs.
- Gorsuch's majority held that the compelled speech doctrine (forcing someone to express a message) trumps the state's compelling interest in preventing discrimination.
- The decision expanded the reach of *Masterpiece Cakeshop* by directly addressing whether a business can be exempted from serving same-sex customers on religious grounds.
- Dissenters (Justices Sotomayor, Kagan, and Jackson) argued the ruling privileged religious conscience over the lived dignity of LGBTQ people seeking equal access to goods and services.
- Applied strict scrutiny to the compelled speech claim rather than rational basis review to the public accommodations law, fundamentally shifting the balance.
- Marked a significant doctrinal win for religious liberty advocates and a setback for LGBTQ equality under public accommodations law.
