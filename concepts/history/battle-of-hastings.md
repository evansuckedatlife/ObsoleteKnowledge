# Battle of Hastings

## summary

The *Battle of Hastings* was fought on 14 October 1066 in southeastern England, deciding the succession to the English throne after King Edward the Confessor's death. *William the Conqueror*, Duke of Normandy, defeated *Harold Godwinson*, the Saxon king who claimed the throne, fundamentally transforming English governance, language, and culture through Norman rule that lasted centuries.

## you gotta know

- Fought on 14 October 1066 near Hastings in East Sussex; the Norman victory effectively ended Anglo-Saxon dominance in England.
- *Harold Godwinson*, the Anglo-Saxon king, was killed in the battle—accounts say he took an arrow to the eye, symbolising the end of Saxon rule.
- *William the Conqueror* invaded from Normandy with roughly 7,000 troops to claim the English throne, which he believed Edward the Confessor had promised him.
- The Normans employed cavalry charges and archers in disciplined formation, tactics superior to the defensive shield-wall used by the Saxon infantry.
- The Norman victory at Hastings led to William's coronation and the *Norman Conquest*, reshaping English institutions, law, and language with French-Norman influence.
- The battle is immortalised in the *Bayeux Tapestry*, an 11th-century embroidered narrative that depicts events from William's invasion, including the battle itself.
