# Salvador Allende

## summary

**Salvador Gossens Allende** (1908–1973) was a Chilean physician and socialist politician who became Latin America's first democratically elected Marxist president. His attempt to build socialism within a democratic framework was cut short by a military coup, and he died during the takeover.

## you gotta know

- Elected president of Chile in 1970 on a socialist platform, promising to redistribute wealth and nationalize major industries.
- Attempted to implement land reform, nationalized copper mines, and expanded social welfare programs to benefit the poor.
- Faced severe economic challenges including inflation, currency devaluation, and capital flight by domestic and foreign investors.
- Refused to resign when the military moved against him; he died (by suicide or in combat) during the coup on September 11, 1973.
- His overthrow marked the beginning of Augusto Pinochet's 17-year dictatorship and became a watershed moment for the Cold War in Latin America.
- Remains a symbolic figure for the global Left and a cautionary tale about the fragility of democratic socialism.
