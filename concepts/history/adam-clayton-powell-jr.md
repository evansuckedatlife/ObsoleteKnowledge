# Adam Clayton Powell Jr.

## summary

*Adam Clayton Powell Jr.* (1908–1972) was a flamboyant and fearless congressman from Harlem who used his House seat and pulpit to challenge racial segregation and poverty. A minister, legislator, and cultural icon, he left an outsized imprint on mid-20th-century American politics despite constant institutional opposition.

## you gotta know

- Elected to the *House of Representatives* in 1944 from New York's Harlem district; served until 1967 and again 1969–1972.
- Pastor of the *Abyssinian Baptist Church* in Harlem, one of the nation's most prominent Black congregations, giving him a dual power base in church and legislature.
- Chaired the *House Committee on Education and Labor* (1961–1967), the first Black chairmanship of a major committee, and pushed through landmark anti-poverty legislation.
- Known for bringing his mistress to Europe on congressional junkets and for flamboyant lifestyle; politically vindicated on charges but personally vilified by white media.
- Expelled from the House in 1967 on ethical grounds (later ruled unconstitutional by the Supreme Court), a career climax that divided Black leadership.
- Coined the phrase *"Keep the faith, baby,"* which became a rallying cry for Black activism; his defiant rhetoric and legislative victories inspired younger civil rights figures.
