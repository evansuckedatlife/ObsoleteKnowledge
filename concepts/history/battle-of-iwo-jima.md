# Battle of Iwo Jima

## summary

Fought February–March 1945, the Battle of Iwo Jima saw American forces assault one of the most heavily fortified Japanese island positions in the Pacific. The 35-day campaign to capture the small volcanic island cost the U.S. Marines nearly 7,000 lives and resulted in an iconic photograph of the flag-raising atop Mount Suribachi that became an enduring symbol of American sacrifice.

## you gotta know

- Island located 1,200 km south of Tokyo; strategic as a base for fighter escort for B-29 bombers raiding Japan.
- Japanese garrison of approximately 21,000 soldiers commanded by General Tomoshiro Kamatube; dug into fortified cave positions and tunnels.
- American assault force: nearly 70,000 Marines under General Holland Smith; three Marine divisions landed on black sand beaches.
- Iconic photograph of Marines raising the American flag atop Mount Suribachi, taken by Joe Rosenthal; won the Pulitzer Prize.
- Fiercest fighting in the Pacific; U.S. casualties: 6,821 killed, over 19,000 wounded; Japanese lost 18,000+ (very few surrendered).
- Secured by March 26, 1945; provided forward base for fighter-escort operations and emergency airfield for damaged B-29s.
- Tactical American victory; demonstrated the cost of assaulting heavily defended Japanese territories.
