# David Ben-Gurion

## summary

*David Ben-Gurion* (1886–1973) was the founding prime minister of Israel and principal architect of its establishment as a Jewish state. His leadership shaped Israel's early years through military strategy, immigration policy, and nation-building, cementing Zionist ideals into a functioning state.

## you gotta know

- Led the *Yishuv* (Jewish community in Palestine) through negotiations with Britain and preparation for statehood in the 1930s and 1940s.
- Declared Israeli independence on May 14, 1948, and served as prime minister (1948–1953, 1955–1963).
- Organized the 1948 Arab-Israeli War, forging a fledgling Jewish state through military victory despite vastly outnumbered forces.
- Pioneered the *Law of Return* (1950), granting citizenship to all Jews, shaping Israel's demographic and cultural identity.
- Championed mass immigration (*aliyah*) and agricultural settlement; promoted the kibbutz movement as a model for nation-building.
- Advocated for universal military conscription and an expansionist security doctrine that shaped regional tensions.
- Retired to Kibbutz Sde Boker in the Negev, symbolizing the pioneer ethos he championed.
