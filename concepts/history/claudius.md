# Claudius

## summary

Claudius was the fourth Roman emperor, ruling from AD 41 to 54. Afflicted with a limp and slight deafness from a young age, he was largely dismissed by his family as a fool and kept out of public life. However, his physical ailments likely saved him from the purges of his predecessors, and upon Caligula's assassination, he was unexpectedly declared emperor, proving to be an able administrator and overseeing a major expansion of the empire.

## you gotta know

- He successfully oversaw the Roman conquest of *Britain* in AD 43, achieving what Julius Caesar and Caligula had failed to do.
- He was found cowering behind a curtain by the Praetorian Guard after Caligula's murder, and they famously proclaimed him emperor on the spot.
- His third wife, *Valeria Messalina*, engaged in scandalous affairs and even went through a public marriage ceremony with another man before Claudius had her executed.
- He was allegedly murdered by his fourth wife (and niece) *Agrippina the Younger*, who served him a dish of poisoned mushrooms.
- He was a prolific scholar and historian, famously writing extensive treatises on Etruscan and Carthaginian history, though these works are now lost.

## connections

- [[caligula]] — his erratic nephew and predecessor, whose assassination paved the way for Claudius's rise to power.
- [[nero]] — his stepson and successor, placed on the throne through the machinations of Claudius's last wife.
- [[agrippina-the-younger]] — his ambitious niece and final wife, who likely murdered him to secure power for her son.
