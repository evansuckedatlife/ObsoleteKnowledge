# Glenn Curtiss

## summary

*Glenn Curtiss* was an aviation pioneer and aircraft manufacturer who competed with the Wright Brothers for aviation supremacy in the early 1900s. He set speed records, designed innovative aircraft, and founded one of the first aircraft manufacturing companies, though his legal battles with the Wrights defined much of his career.

## you gotta know

- Set world speed record of 46.5 mph in 1909, dominating early aviation competitions.
- Designed the *Pusher* biplane with innovative wing-warping control, directly competing with the Wrights' design.
- Won the Gordon Bennett Cup in 1909, a prestigious international aviation race.
- Founded Curtiss Aeroplane Company, one of the first successful aircraft manufacturers in America.
- Engaged in decades-long patent litigation with the Wright Brothers over aircraft control mechanisms.
- Developed the hydrofoil and amphibious aircraft, expanding aviation's scope beyond land-based flight.
- Designed the *Curtiss Jenny*, which became the primary trainer aircraft for World War I American pilots.
