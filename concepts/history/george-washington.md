# George Washington

## summary

*George Washington* was the commander-in-chief of the Continental Army and the central figure of the American Revolution. A Virginia planter-turned-general, he held the revolutionary cause together through years of defeat and hardship, ultimately securing independence and becoming the nation's first president, earning him the epithet "Father of His Country."

## you gotta know

- Led the Continental Army from 1775 to 1783, outlasting the initial British advantages in training, resources, and naval power through strategic patience and dogged determination.
- Achieved a stunning victory at *Trenton* (December 1776) by crossing the ice-choked *Delaware River* in a surprise dawn attack, turning the tide of morale after months of defeats.
- Endured the brutal winter of 1777–78 at *Valley Forge* (Pennsylvania), where his army nearly froze and starved but held together under Washington's leadership.
- Adopted a strategy of avoiding decisive battle except when odds were favorable, preserving his army as the revolution's moral center even in retreat.
- At *Yorktown* (1781), coordinated with French forces under *Rochambeau* to trap British General *Cornwallis*'s army, securing the final victory of the war.
- Voluntarily relinquished command in 1783 and returned to private life, an unprecedented act that shocked European monarchies and boosted the revolution's republican credentials.

## connections

- [[nathanael-greene]] — southern theater commander who wore down British forces in the Carolinas while Washington fought in the North.
- [[marquis-de-lafayette]] — French aristocrat and trusted aide who fought without pay and secured crucial French support.
- [[henry-knox]] — his chief artillery officer and later first Secretary of War; instrumental in professionalizing the army.
- [[baron-von-steuben]] — Prussian officer who drilled and trained the Continental Army at Valley Forge.
- [[cornwallis|Charles Cornwallis]] — his chief British opponent; defeated at Yorktown, ending the war.
- [[american-revolution]] — the cause Washington embodied and whose success hinged on his perseverance.
