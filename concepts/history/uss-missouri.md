# USS Missouri

## summary

**USS Missouri** (BB-63), the last Iowa-class battleship, served as the flagship where Japan formally surrendered on September 2, 1945, ending World War II in the Pacific. Her decks became the setting for history's most momentous naval ceremony, symbolizing American naval dominance and the conclusive defeat of Japan.

## you gotta know

- Commissioned in 1944 and arrived in the Pacific just in time to participate in the bombardment of Japanese homeland targets in the war's final months.
- Hosted the formal Japanese surrender ceremony on September 2, 1945, with General Douglas MacArthur and Admiral Chester Nimitz presiding as Japanese officials signed the Instrument of Surrender.
- Served throughout the Cold War and Korean War, later modernized with missile systems and radar, extending her operational life well into the 1950s and beyond.
- Decommissioned in 1955 but retained in reserve, eventually becoming a museum ship berthed in Pearl Harbor and later in Missouri, where she remains a major tourist attraction.
- Known affectionately as "Mighty Mo" by her crew, her participation in the surrender ceremony gave her an outsized place in American collective memory of World War II's conclusion.
- Her presence at Japan's formal capitulation made her the symbolic capstone to the Pacific War and the literal stage for one of the twentieth century's pivotal moments.
