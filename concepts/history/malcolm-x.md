## summary

*Malcolm X* was a Muslim minister and human rights activist who challenged the integrationist strategy of the Civil Rights Movement, advocating instead for Black nationalism, self-determination, and armed self-defense. His fiery rhetoric and uncompromising stance on racial dignity made him an iconic figure and a controversial counterpoint to *Martin Luther King Jr.*, and his posthumously published autobiography remains foundational to understanding Black radical thought.

## you gotta know

- Joined the *Nation of Islam* while imprisoned, becoming one of its most charismatic ministers and the organization's spokesman by the late 1950s.
- Called for Black separatism and the creation of an independent Black nation within America, rejecting integration as a goal.
- Advocated "by any means necessary" — a phrase that signaled willingness to use force in self-defense, contrasting sharply with King's nonviolence.
- Briefly met with *Martin Luther King Jr.* in 1964 but their ideological differences remained unbridged.
- Left the Nation of Islam in 1964 and founded the *Organization of Afro-American Unity*, which took a broader pan-African and internationalist stance.
- Traveled to Mecca and Africa in 1964, experiences that moderated his earlier strict separatism and expanded his vision of Black liberation.
- Assassinated on February 21, 1965 in New York by Nation of Islam members, though questions about the full circumstances persist.
