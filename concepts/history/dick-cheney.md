# Dick Cheney

## summary

**Dick Cheney** was the forty-sixth Vice President under George W. Bush (2001–2009) and one of the most powerful and controversial vice presidents in American history. A former Secretary of Defense and Wyoming congressman, Cheney shaped post-9/11 national security policy and became a leading architect of the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq.

## you gotta know

- As Vice President, wielded unprecedented influence over foreign and defense policy, effectively co-president in Bush's first term after the 9/11 attacks.
- Advocated strongly for the 2003 invasion of Iraq, claiming Iraq possessed weapons of mass destruction that investigations later failed to confirm.
- Championed an expansive interpretation of executive power and authorized controversial interrogation practices including waterboarding.
- Shot hunting companion Harry Whittington in a quail-hunting accident in 2006, an incident that became a cultural touchstone for media satire.
- Served as Secretary of Defense under President George H. W. Bush during the first Gulf War and supervised Operation Desert Storm.
- Previously served in the House of Representatives from Wyoming and was a powerful minority whip, leading House Republican operations.
- Left office with low approval ratings due to his association with unpopular wars and remained a controversial figure in conservative politics afterward.
