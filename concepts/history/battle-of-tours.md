# Battle of Tours

## summary

The *Battle of Tours* was fought in 732 CE between the Frankish forces of *Charles Martel* and a Muslim Umayyad army led by *Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi*, halting the northward expansion of Islamic forces into Western Europe. The Frankish victory shaped the religious and political future of Europe and established the Franks as the dominant power in the region.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the city of Tours in central France; the Umayyad force, estimated at 80,000–120,000, was opposed by *Charles Martel*'s Frankish army of roughly 30,000.
- The Frankish infantry, fighting in close formation, repelled repeated cavalry charges by the Umayyad mounted forces over the course of several days of skirmishing.
- *Charles Martel* (Charles the Hammer) earned his nickname and reputation through this victory, which strengthened his political position and established Frankish military dominance.
- The defeat halted rapid Islamic northward expansion and ensured that Western Europe would remain predominantly Christian rather than fall under Umayyad control.
- The battle is often cited as a pivotal moment in world history, marking the limits of Islamic territorial expansion into Europe and securing Christian hegemony in the West.
- Following his success, *Charles Martel* founded the Carolingian dynasty, which would produce Charlemagne and reshape European feudalism and governance.
