# Aldrich Ames

## summary

*Aldrich Ames* was a CIA analyst and Moscow station chief who became the most damaging mole in American intelligence history. For nine years, he sold secrets to the Soviet Union and Russia, compromising dozens of intelligence operations and costing the lives of at least a dozen American assets abroad before his arrest in 1994.

## you gotta know

- Joined the CIA in 1962; had access to classified information about American intelligence operations, agents, and spy techniques across decades of Cold War espionage.
- Began spying for the Soviet Union in 1985 while working at the CIA's Arlington headquarters; motivated initially by financial desperation (failing marriage, mortgage debt) and a desire for attention.
- Provided the Soviets with detailed information about CIA operations, agent identities, and technical methods, earning approximately $2.7 million in payments while exposing dozens of American assets.
- His compromises led to the execution or imprisonment of at least ten CIA assets in the Soviet Union, undermining American intelligence gathering during a critical period.
- Remained undetected for nine years, living in relative comfort while colleagues and superiors overlooked signs of his treason; the FBI finally arrested him in February 1994 after an intensive investigation.
- Convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment without parole; he remains incarcerated, having never shown remorse for the agents he betrayed.
