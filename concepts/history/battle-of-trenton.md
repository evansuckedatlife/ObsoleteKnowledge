## summary

On *December 26, 1776*, after a devastating campaign and retreat across New Jersey, George Washington led approximately 2,400 troops across the icy Delaware River to attack a Hessian garrison at Trenton. The dawn assault routed the surprised garrison, capturing nearly 900 soldiers. This unexpected victory boosted American morale when it was at its nadir and demonstrated Washington's tactical boldness, buying time for the cause when all seemed lost.

## you gotta know

- Occurred on the day after Christmas, when the Hessian garrison (German soldiers hired by Britain) was off-guard and celebrating.
- Washington's daring night crossing of the Delaware River in freezing conditions is iconic—immortalized in famous paintings.
- The American force achieved complete surprise, surrounded the Hessian camp, and forced the garrison's surrender in less than an hour.
- American casualties were minimal (fewer than ten killed); Hessian losses included nearly 900 captured and 30 killed or wounded.
- The victory ended a string of American defeats and retreats that had threatened the Continental cause.
- Immediately followed by American victories at *Second Trenton* (January 2, 1777) and *Princeton* (January 3), reviving the army's credibility.
