# Apache

## summary

The *Apache* are a southwestern Athabaskan people traditionally inhabiting Arizona, New Mexico, and parts of Texas, Colorado, and Mexico. Organized into autonomous bands and known for their skilled horsemanship, raiding traditions, and fierce resistance to U.S. and Spanish colonial domination, they maintained independence longer than most Plains and Southwestern nations.

## you gotta know

- Athabaskan-speaking peoples distantly related to the Navajo; divided into autonomous bands including the *Western Apache*, *Chiricahua*, *Mescalero*, *Jicarilla*, and others, each with their own leaders and territories.
- Practiced mixed economy of hunting, gathering, agriculture, and—after acquiring horses—raiding and warfare against Spanish and Mexican settlements.
- *Geronimo* (Goyathlay) led the Chiricahua Apache in a prolonged resistance campaign (1876–1886), conducting guerrilla warfare across the Southwest and Mexico before surrendering in 1886.
- Subjected to forced relocations, including the *San Carlos Reservation* concentration; endured military campaigns and decimation of their buffalo hunting grounds.
- Apache scouts were enlisted by the U.S. Army during the Indian Wars, serving as guides and fighters in campaigns against their own people.
- Today six federally recognized Apache nations exist; strong cultural traditions and language preservation efforts continue across their reservations.
