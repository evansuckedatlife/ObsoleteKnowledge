# We Shall Fight on the Beaches

## summary

Winston Churchill's defiant address to Parliament on *June 4, 1940*, delivered after the Allied evacuation from Dunkirk, steels British resolve against Nazi Germany when military prospects were bleak. The speech crystallizes Churchill's rhetorical power: building from loss to an unbreakable will to resist, it became the sound of Britain's refusal to surrender and remains a touchstone of wartime leadership.

## you gotta know

- Delivered to Parliament on *June 4, 1940*, just days after the British Expeditionary Force's withdrawal from Dunkirk.
- Churchill declares: *"We shall defend our island, whatever the cost may be, we shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds..."* — a cascading affirmation of resistance across every terrain.
- Transmutes military catastrophe (Dunkirk evacuation) into moral victory: the retreat is reframed as a successful deliverance of troops.
- The speech concludes: *"this shall be our finest hour"* — suggesting that endurance itself confers historic greatness.
- Employs biblical and classical rhetoric; the catalogue of resistance sites echoes ancient rallying cries.
- Audiences reported being moved to tears; the speech became the emotional armor of the British public during the German threat.
- Later broadcast on radio, amplifying its reach beyond Parliament.
