# Roma

## summary

The *Roma* (sometimes called *Romani* or *Gypsies*, the latter now considered a slur) are a dispersed ethnic group originating in the Indian subcontinent who migrated to Europe beginning in the medieval period. They speak Romani languages derived from Sanskrit and have maintained distinct cultural, linguistic, and social traditions across European nations despite centuries of persecution, discrimination, and forced assimilation.

## you gotta know

- Originated in northern India and began migration to Europe around the 10th–11th centuries, eventually dispersing across Europe, Asia, and the Americas; largest populations are in Europe.
- Speak *Romani*, an Indo-Aryan language related to Sanskrit and Indian languages; multiple dialects exist reflecting different migration histories; many Roma today are bilingual or multilingual.
- Historically practiced occupations including metalwork, music, performance, and trade; some groups practiced pastoral herding (*Roma shepherds*); economic niches varied by region and historical period.
- Maintain distinctive *Romani culture* including music traditions (*Romani music* influenced European classical and folk genres), elaborate kinship systems, and social customs around marriage and honor.
- Experienced systematic persecution across European history including forced segregation, enslavement in some regions, and devastating genocide during the Holocaust (estimated 500,000+ Roma killed).
- Face ongoing discrimination, marginalization, and exclusion in modern European societies; denied access to education, employment, and housing; subject to police harassment and hate crimes.
- Language and cultural preservation efforts have expanded since the 1970s; Roma activism and political organization increasingly advocate for rights, recognition, and historical accountability.
