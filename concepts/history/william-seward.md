# William Seward

## summary

*William Seward* was Secretary of State under President Abraham Lincoln from 1861 to 1869, playing a crucial role in securing British and French neutrality during the Civil War while articulating a vision of American expansion. His post-war diplomacy advanced Union interests in the Western Hemisphere and the Pacific, culminating in the purchase of Alaska—an acquisition widely ridiculed but vindicated by the 20th century.

## you gotta know

- Served as Lincoln's Secretary of State throughout the Civil War, preventing European intervention that might have doomed the Union.
- Secured the *Alaska Purchase* (1867), acquiring vast Alaskan territory from Russia for $7.2 million—dismissed as "Seward's Folly" but proven strategically vital.
- Opposed slavery expansion into western territories, a stance that made him a Republican leader and contributed to sectional tensions.
- Nearly died in an assassination attempt by Confederate sympathiser Lewis Payne on the same night Lincoln was assassinated (April 14, 1865).
- Promoted American hegemony in the Caribbean and North Pacific through commercial treaties and strategic acquisitions.
- Believed the United States was destined to dominate the North American continent and extend influence across the oceans.
