# Comanche

## summary

The *Comanche* are a Great Plains and Southwest nation, historically inhabiting Texas, Oklahoma, Colorado, and Kansas, renowned as skilled mounted warriors and master horsemen. Organized into independent bands and leading a mobile, buffalo-hunting culture, they fiercely resisted U.S. expansion before confinement to reservations in the 19th century.

## you gotta know

- Shoshonean-speaking people; tradition holds they split from the Shoshone and migrated south to the Plains around 1700, acquiring horses and transforming into formidable mounted raiders.
- Organized into autonomous bands (*Yamparika*, *Kotsoteka*, *Quahadi*, *Penateka*) each led by independent chiefs; decisions made by consensus in band councils.
- Became legendary for horsemanship and cavalry tactics; mastered raiding warfare against Spanish, Mexican, and American settlements and rival tribes.
- *Quanah Parker*, half-Comanche and half-American, led the last band of free Comanche warriors, holding out in the *Palo Duro Canyon* until surrendering in 1875.
- Hunted buffalo extensively; the buffalo's near-extinction and confinement to the Comanche Reservation (Indian Territory, now Oklahoma) devastated their traditional economy.
- Today about 15,000 Comanche are enrolled in the Comanche Nation of Oklahoma; the Comanche language and cultural traditions remain strong among tribal members.
