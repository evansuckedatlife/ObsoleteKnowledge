# Huns

## summary

The *Huns* were a nomadic people from Central Asia whose invasions under *Attila* in the 5th century posed an existential threat to both the Eastern and Western Roman empires, forcing Germanic peoples to migrate and reshape the political order of Europe. Though their empire collapsed within a generation of Attila's death, they accelerated the fall of Rome and the rise of Germanic kingdoms.

## you gotta know

- Originated in the steppes of Central Asia and migrated westward, becoming expert cavalry warriors whose superior mobility revolutionized military tactics.
- Under *Attila*, united the fragmented Hunnic tribes and built an empire spanning from the Rhine to Central Asia, extracting tribute from both Roman empires.
- Defeated the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire at the *Battle of Adrianople in 378* (though this was primarily Gothic forces employed against Rome, showing Hunnic influence on Germanic migration patterns).
- *Attila* invaded Gaul in 451 and was defeated by a Romano-Frankish-Visigothic coalition at the *Battle of the Catalaunian Plains*, one of the rare unified Roman response against barbarian invasion.
- Sacked many Roman cities and demanded ruinous tribute, hastening the financial and military collapse of the Western Empire.
- Held Germanic peoples as tributaries and vassals, controlling the *Ostrogoths* and influencing the migrations of *Visigoths* and other groups.
- Their empire fragmented rapidly after *Attila's* death in 453; the Huns ceased to be a major historical force within decades, absorbed or displaced by Germanic successor kingdoms.

## connections

- [[attila]] — the greatest and most famous Hunnic leader; his invasions defined an era.
- [[roman-empire]] — their primary adversary and victim; they hastened its collapse.
- [[gothic]] — Germanic peoples they dominated and whose migrations they triggered.
- [[central-asia]] — their homeland and the source of their military traditions.
