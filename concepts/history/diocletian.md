# Diocletian

## summary

Diocletian was a Roman emperor who ruled from AD 284 to 305. Coming to power and ending the chaotic Crisis of the Third Century, he fundamentally reorganised the empire's administration and military. To manage the vast and embattled Roman state, he instituted the Tetrarchy, dividing power among four co-emperors, and eventually became the first Roman emperor to voluntarily abdicate the throne.

## you gotta know

- He established the *Tetrarchy* ("rule of four"), splitting the empire into Eastern and Western halves, each governed by an *Augustus* (senior emperor) and a *Caesar* (junior emperor).
- He initiated the *Diocletianic Persecution*, the empire's last, largest, and bloodiest official persecution of Christianity.
- He attempted to curb massive inflation by issuing the *Edict on Maximum Prices*, which fixed prices for goods and services but proved largely unenforceable.
- He voluntarily retired to a massive, heavily fortified palace in *Spalatum* (modern Split, Croatia) to grow cabbages.
- After his abdication, the Tetrarchic system quickly collapsed into a series of civil wars among the remaining leaders and their sons.

## connections

- [[maximian]] — his trusted general and appointed co-emperor (Augustus of the West) during the Tetrarchy.
- [[constantine-the-great]] — the future emperor who eventually emerged from the collapse of the Tetrarchy to reunite the empire.
