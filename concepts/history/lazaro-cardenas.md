# Lázaro Cárdenas

## summary

*Lázaro Cárdenas* was a Mexican president (1934–1940) who implemented the social revolution promised by the Constitution of 1917. He distributed millions of acres to peasants, nationalised foreign oil companies, and championed indigenous rights and labour reform.

## you gotta know

- Distributed over 49 million acres of land to peasant communities, the largest agrarian reform in Mexican history—fulfilling *Emiliano Zapata's* vision.
- Exiled former president *Plutarco Elías Calles*, consolidating executive power and ending the era of strongmen controlling from the shadows.
- Nationalised the oil industry in 1938, seizing foreign-held petroleum assets and establishing the state monopoly *PEMEX*.
- Created the *ejido* system of communal agricultural land, making rural properties collective holdings for peasant communities.
- Promoted indigenous education and culture, creating schools and supporting indigenous autonomy.
- His presidency marked the full realisation of revolutionary ideals and the consolidation of the *Institutional Revolutionary Party* (PRI).
