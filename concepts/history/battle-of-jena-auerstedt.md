# Battle of Jena-Auerstedt

## summary

The Battle of Jena-Auerstedt on October 14, 1806, was actually two simultaneous battles fought by Napoleon's army against the Prussian forces along two parallel fronts near these towns in central Germany. The French victories, achieved through maneuver and concentration of force, destroyed the Prussian army in a single day and demonstrated Napoleon's tactical mastery; Prussia sued for peace within weeks.

## you gotta know

- Fought on October 14, 1806, as part of Napoleon's campaign to consolidate control of the German Confederation after the Treaty of Pressburg.
- At Jena, Napoleon personally commanded against the Prussian main force under General Friedrich Hohenlohe; despite being outnumbered, tactical brilliance and superior coordination won the day.
- At Auerstedt, Marshal Davout and his 27,000 troops defeated the Prussian king's own command of 60,000 soldiers in a stunning rearguard action.
- Prussian casualties were approximately 25,000 killed or wounded and over 15,000 captured; French losses were about 10,000.
- Frederick William III fled to East Prussia; within weeks, Prussia signed the Treaty of Tilsit and lost nearly half its territory and population.
- The destruction of the Prussian army at Jena-Auerstedt was so complete that Prussia did not fully recover for eight years.
- The battle exemplified Napoleon's use of central position and interior lines—holding the interior of a triangle to defeat separated enemies sequentially.
