# Cheyenne

## summary

The *Cheyenne* (Tsitsistas, meaning "the People") are a Great Plains and Mountain nation originally inhabiting the Upper Plains (present-day Wyoming, Colorado, and Montana), known for their warrior traditions, democratic governance through councils of chiefs, and fierce resistance to U.S. westward expansion and buffalo hunting disruption.

## you gotta know

- Algonquian-speaking people; tradition holds they migrated from Minnesota woodlands to the Plains around 1500, acquiring horses and transforming into mounted warriors.
- Organized into democratic tribal councils governed by a *Council of Forty-Four*, where leadership required consensus and decisions reflected collective wisdom.
- Divided into Northern and Southern bands; maintained strong warrior societies, particularly the *Dog Soldiers*, elite warriors who policed the tribe and led hunts and raids.
- Fought U.S. expansion and were at the *Battle of Little Bighorn* (1876) alongside Lakota and other allied tribes against *Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer*.
- *Black Kettle*, a peace advocate, led efforts to find accommodation; *Sand Creek Massacre* (1864) killed hundreds of peaceful Cheyenne and Arapaho women and children, shattering peace efforts.
- Confined to reservations in Oklahoma and Montana after the Plains Wars; today the Cheyenne and Arapaho Tribes (Oklahoma) and Northern Cheyenne Tribe (Montana) maintain strong cultural traditions and language programs.
