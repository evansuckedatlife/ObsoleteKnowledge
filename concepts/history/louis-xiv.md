# Louis XIV

## summary

*Louis XIV* (1638–1715), called the Sun King, was the longest-reigning European monarch and the embodiment of absolute monarchy. He centralized royal power, built Versailles as the ultimate seat of government, and made France the dominant military and cultural force in Europe for his era.

## you gotta know

- Reigned for 72 years (1643–1715), the longest documented reign of any European sovereign; assumed personal rule in 1661 after Cardinal Mazarin's death.
- Built the Palace of Versailles outside Paris, a vast architectural statement that became the seat of government and where the nobility were compelled to attend; it symbolized his power and the grandeur of France.
- Declared "L'État, c'est moi" (I am the state), embodying the divine-right absolutism that defined his reign and influenced monarchs across Europe.
- Revoked the Edict of Nantes (1685), ending religious tolerance for Protestants and triggering the flight of Huguenots, which weakened France economically.
- Waged numerous wars of expansion, including the War of Spanish Succession, extending French borders but leaving the treasury depleted.
- Patronized the arts and sciences lavishly; the court at Versailles became the model for elegance and culture across Europe.
- Took the sun as his personal symbol, representing the divine source of all life and power radiating from himself as king.
