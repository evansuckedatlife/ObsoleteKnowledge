# Bunker Hill

## summary

On *June 17, 1775*, American militia fortified *Breed's Hill* (near Bunker Hill) overlooking Boston Harbor. British commander William Howe attacked with frontal assaults, and the Americans—short on ammunition—inflicted staggering casualties before being forced to retreat. Though technically a British victory, the heavy losses boosted colonial morale and proved American regulars could stand against professional troops.

## you gotta know

- The Americans were commanded by *Israel Putnam*, who issued the famous order: "Don't fire until you see the whites of their eyes" (or variants thereof).
- British forces made three costly uphill charges to overrun the American entrenchments.
- British casualties were over 1,000 (40% of their force); American losses approximately 450, with many surrounded or out of ammunition.
- The British captured the hill and Boston, but the lopsided casualty ratio shook their confidence in easy victory.
- Occurred in June 1775, three months after Lexington and Concord, before the Declaration of Independence.
- Demonstrated that colonial militia could inflict real damage on the world's premier military power.
