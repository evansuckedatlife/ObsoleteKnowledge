# Trajan

## summary

Trajan was a Roman emperor who ruled from AD 98 to 117. As the second of the "Five Good Emperors," he was a highly successful soldier-emperor who presided over the greatest military expansion in Roman history, leading the empire to attain its maximum territorial extent by the time of his death.

## you gotta know

- He led successful campaigns to conquer *Dacia* (modern-day Romania), incorporating its vast gold mines into the empire.
- His victory in Dacia is famously commemorated by *Trajan's Column*, a massive triumphal column in Rome featuring a continuous spiralling bas-relief depicting the wars.
- He expanded the empire's eastern borders by annexing the Nabataean kingdom to form the province of Arabia Petraea, and later conquered parts of the Parthian Empire, including Armenia and Mesopotamia.
- He was officially declared by the Senate as *optimus princeps* ("the best ruler").
- He implemented the *Alimenta*, an extensive welfare program that helped orphans and poor children throughout Italy.

## connections

- [[hadrian]] — his successor, a fellow Spaniard who consolidated Trajan's conquests and pulled back from some of his eastern expansions.
- [[nerva]] — his predecessor, who adopted the popular general Trajan as his heir to avoid a civil war.
- [[apollodorus-of-damascus]] — his chief architect, who designed Trajan's Forum, Trajan's Column, and the massive bridge across the Danube.
