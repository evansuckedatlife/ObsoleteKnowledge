# Agustín de Iturbide

## summary

*Agustín de Iturbide* was a Mexican general and statesman who completed the independence war begun by *Miguel Hidalgo*, crowned himself emperor, and established Mexico as a sovereign nation. His reign was brief but transformative, bridging colonial and modern Mexico.

## you gotta know

- Initially fought *for* Spain against Hidalgo's rebels, but switched sides and unified independent factions under the *Plan of Iguala* (1821).
- The *Plan of Iguala* promised independence, monarchy, and religious unity, uniting conservatives and liberals around a common cause.
- Crowned himself *Emperor Agustín I* in 1822, making Mexico an empire rather than a republic.
- His empire lasted only two years; he was forced to abdicate in 1823 as republicans rejected monarchy.
- Exiled to Europe, he attempted to return to Mexico in 1824 and was captured and executed by firing squad in Tamaulipas.
- Despite his brief reign, he secured Mexican independence and established the nation's first government.
