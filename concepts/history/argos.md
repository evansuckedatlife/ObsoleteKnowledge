# Argos

## summary

*Argos*, one of the oldest cities in Greece located in the northeastern Peloponnese, was a major Mycenaean power and later a rival to Sparta in Spartan-dominated Laconia. Though politically overshadowed by stronger neighbors, Argos maintained cultural significance, hosting the *Heraion*, one of the oldest Doric temples, and cultivating a legacy as the realm of great heroes.

## you gotta know

- Legendary birthplace of *Heracles*, the greatest of Greek heroes, born to *Alcmene* and *Zeus* in the palace of Amphitryon.
- Ruled by *Diomedes* during the *Trojan War*, a fierce warrior second only to *Achilles*, who fought valiantly against Troy's forces and stood against gods on the battlefield.
- Home to the *Heraion* (Temple of Hera), one of the earliest and greatest Doric temples, built around 500 BCE, housing a renowned statue by Polycletus.
- Founded, according to legend, by *Danaus*, a figure fleeing Egypt with his 50 daughters, establishing the city and the dynasty that produced kings and heroes.
- Served as a center for bronze-working and craftsmanship, producing high-quality pottery and metal goods that were traded throughout the Mediterranean.
- Rival to nearby *Sparta*, located in the same region but politically opposed, leading to periodic conflicts over regional dominance.
- Named "Argive" in Greek literature, a term used broadly for Greeks themselves—*Argive* soldiers refer to Greeks in general in Homeric poetry.
