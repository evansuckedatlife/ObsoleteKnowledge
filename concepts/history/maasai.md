# Maasai

## summary

The *Maasai* are a Nilotic pastoralist people of East Africa inhabiting the semi-arid savannas of Kenya and Tanzania, renowned for their cattle-herding culture, elaborate ceremonial traditions, and distinctive material culture. They have maintained strong cultural identity and traditional governance systems despite colonial pressures and modernization, though climate change and land restrictions now pose existential challenges to pastoral lifeways.

## you gotta know

- Inhabit the *East African* savannas across Kenya and Tanzania, particularly the *Maasai Mara* and surrounding semi-arid grasslands suited to pastoral nomadism.
- Speak *Maa*, a Nilotic language; also widely use Swahili and English; language preservation efforts are ongoing as younger generations increasingly adopt national languages.
- Practice pastoral transhumance, traditionally herding cattle (*Maasai cattle*) across vast territories following seasonal rainfall and grass growth; cattle are central to wealth, status, and social relationships.
- Practice *age-set* systems organizing males into cohorts (*morans*) with distinct roles and responsibilities; initiation ceremonies mark transitions between age grades.
- Maintain distinctive material culture including red ochre and shea butter adornment, *shuka* (red cloth), beaded jewelry with precise color and pattern symbolism encoding identity and status.
- Perform warrior dances including the *adumu* (jumping dance) and *kilumi* (warrior dances); these remain central to celebrations and ceremonial life.
- Governed traditionally by councils of elders (*laibons* and *sopai*) practicing consensus decision-making; this system coexists with modern state governance.
