# Vicente Fox

## summary

*Vicente Fox* was a Mexican businessman and politician who served as president (2000–2006), becoming the first non-PRI president in 71 years. His election marked the end of single-party rule and a democratic transition, though his administration faced economic and security challenges.

## you gotta know

- Rose through the *Coca-Cola* corporation before entering politics as governor of Guanajuato state (1995–1999).
- Led the centre-right *Partido Acción Nacional (PAN)* to victory in the 2000 presidential election, breaking the *PRI's* seven-decade grip on power.
- Campaigned as a reformer promising economic growth, anti-corruption measures, and modernisation.
- His presidency saw Mexico's integration deepening through *NAFTA*, but faced persistent drug-violence escalation and slower-than-promised economic growth.
- Promoted free-market economics and privatisation, differentiating himself from the PRI's state-interventionist model.
- Left office with mixed popularity; his successor *Felipe Calderón* (also PAN) continued the party's rightward trajectory.
