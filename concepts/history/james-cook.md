# James Cook

## summary

*Captain James Cook* was an 18th-century British naval officer whose methodical voyages charted vast regions of the *Pacific Ocean*, including *Australia*, *New Zealand*, and numerous Pacific islands. His expeditions combined precise cartography, scientific observation, and relatively humane treatment of indigenous peoples, setting new standards for exploration. Though celebrated as a great explorer and scientist, his voyages also facilitated European colonization and disease transmission that devastated Pacific island populations.

## you gotta know

- Began his career as a Royal Navy surveyor and participated in mapping the *St. Lawrence River* during the *Seven Years War*, gaining fame for accurate charts.
- Commanded three great *Pacific* expeditions (1768–1779) aboard the *HMS Endeavour*, *HMS Resolution*, and *HMS Discovery*, sponsored by the British Admiralty and the *Royal Society*.
- On his first voyage, sailed to *Tahiti* to observe the transit of Venus, then charted the coasts of *New Zealand* and *Australia*, the first detailed European maps of these regions.
- Proved *New Zealand* was two islands (not a peninsula of a larger continent) and that *Australia* was not the fabled southern continent—fundamentally revising geographic knowledge.
- Discovered and named the *Hawaiian Islands* in his third voyage, where he was killed in 1779 during a dispute with Hawaiian islanders over the theft of a ship's boat.
- Instituted practices that prevented scurvy and other shipboard diseases, significantly lowering death rates on long voyages—a major contribution to maritime exploration and naval health.
- Brought back extensive scientific collections, botanical specimens, and detailed journals that influenced European scientific understanding of the Pacific world.
