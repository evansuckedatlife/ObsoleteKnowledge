# Vasco da Gama

## summary

*Vasco da Gama* was a Portuguese explorer who established the first direct sea route from Europe to Asia by sailing around Africa, arriving in India in 1498. His voyage opened a new era of European maritime dominance and trade, bypassing the overland Silk Road routes and allowing Portugal to establish a lucrative Asian spice empire that would define the Age of Exploration's early decades.

## you gotta know

- Commissioned by *King Manuel I* of Portugal to find a sea route to Asia, following earlier Portuguese expeditions that had been slowly exploring Africa's coastline.
- Sailed south and east around Africa, rounding the *Cape of Good Hope* in 1488 (first achieved by *Bartolomeu Dias*), then crossed the Indian Ocean to reach *Calicut* on India's southwest coast in 1498.
- The voyage took approximately 10 months from Portugal to India; the return voyage took even longer, with crew suffering from scurvy and tropical disease.
- Opened direct trade between Europe and Asia, allowing Portuguese merchants to bypass Muslim middlemen in the *Mediterranean* and *Red Sea* trade routes.
- Returned to Portugal as a hero in 1499, establishing Portugal's supremacy in the Asian spice trade; the profits from this monopoly funded Portuguese expansion for decades.
- Made two subsequent voyages to India, using military force to establish Portuguese trading posts and dominance over regional powers.
- His success inspired both admiration and fear: European rivals raced to find alternative routes to Asia, and local rulers viewed Portuguese encroachment with increasing alarm.
