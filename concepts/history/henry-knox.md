## summary

*Henry Knox* rose from a Boston bookseller to become the chief artillery officer of the Continental Army and one of the war's most technically proficient commanders. Self-taught in military science and engineering, he revolutionized American artillery tactics and later served as the nation's first Secretary of War, establishing the foundation for American military professionalism.

## you gotta know

- Joined the Continental Army as a volunteer and quickly impressed *George Washington* with his mastery of artillery theory, earning promotion to Major General despite his lack of formal military training.
- Designed and supervised the construction of fortifications and siege works throughout the war, including the crucial defensive positions at *Valley Forge* and the siege works at *Yorktown*.
- Reorganized and standardized American artillery practices, transforming cannons from inaccurate support weapons into precision instruments that could decide battles.
- Played a critical role at *Yorktown* (1781), where his artillery barrage helped force the British surrender and end the war.
- Marched with Washington into defeated New York and served as his trusted aide-de-camp, becoming one of the general's most loyal subordinates.
- After the war, became the first Secretary of War under the Constitution, where he modernized American military organization and founded the artillery corps that persists today.
