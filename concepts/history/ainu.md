# Ainu

## summary

The *Ainu* are the indigenous people of Hokkaido and surrounding islands in what is now Japan, with a distinct language, culture, and spiritual tradition. They were historically marginalized and assimilated into Japanese society but have experienced cultural and political revival, especially since the 1990s, with formal recognition of their indigenous status by the Japanese government in 2008.

## you gotta know

- Inhabit *Hokkaido*, the northern island of Japan, as well as parts of *Honshu* and the *Kuril Islands*; their traditional territory extended across a larger region before Japanese expansion.
- Speak *Aynu Itak* (the Ainu language), an isolate not reliably linked to any other language family; it is critically endangered with only a few thousand fluent speakers remaining.
- Practice *Sintu* (or *Kamuy Moshir*), a polytheistic spiritual tradition centered on *kamuy* (spirits/gods) inhabiting nature; bears, salmon, and fire hold particular sacred significance.
- Historically practiced hunting, fishing, and gathering with sophisticated knowledge of Hokkaido's ecosystems; traded furs and other goods with Japanese and Russian merchants.
- Created distinctive oral literature including *yukar* (verse chants) and *uwepeker* (prose narratives) preserving history and spiritual knowledge.
- Experienced systematic discrimination and forced assimilation under Japanese rule, including language suppression in schools; officially recognized as indigenous by Japan in 2008.
- Practice *inau* carving (wooden ritual sticks) for spiritual ceremonies and *attush* weaving (bark cloth) as traditional crafts.
