# Louis XIII

## summary

*Louis XIII* (1601–1643), called the Just, reigned during one of France's most transformative periods. Though he began his reign as a weak young king, the dominance of his minister *Cardinal Richelieu* made France the primary power in European politics, establishing the foundations of Louis XIV's absolutism.

## you gotta know

- Reigned 1610–1643; his reign was dominated by *Cardinal Richelieu*, who effectively ruled France as chief minister and architect of royal absolutism.
- Survived the regency of his mother *Marie de' Medici*, who initially held power after Henry IV's assassination; his adult authority was often shadowed by court intrigue.
- Richelieu centralized authority, eliminated the power of the nobility, and built the foundations of the modern French state apparatus.
- Intervened in the Thirty Years' War on the side of Protestant powers to contain the power of the Habsburg dynasty, establishing France as the arbiter of European conflict.
- Issued decrees against the dueling nobility, weakening their feudal prerogatives and subordinating them to crown authority.
- His death in 1643 left a 4-year-old heir, *Louis XIV*, under regency; but Richelieu's institutional legacy ensured continuity and laid the groundwork for Louis XIV's absolute reign.
