# Lakota / Sioux

## summary

The *Lakota* (Sioux) are a Great Plains nation originally spanning the Dakotas, Nebraska, and Montana, renowned for their horse culture, warrior traditions, and resistance to U.S. expansion. Led by figures like *Sitting Bull* and *Crazy Horse*, they fought to preserve their buffalo-hunting way of life but were eventually confined to reservations by the late 19th century.

## you gotta know

- Divided into three branches: Lakota (western), Dakota (central), and Nakota (northern); the term "Sioux" is an exonym derived from French renderings of Ojibwe names.
- Became masters of mounted warfare after acquiring horses; organized into bands governed by consensus under hereditary and earned leadership.
- *Sitting Bull* and *Crazy Horse* led resistance to the Fort Laramie Treaties and the U.S. invasion during the Black Hills gold rush; defeated Lieutenant Colonel *George Armstrong Custer* at the *Battle of Little Bighorn* (1876).
- The Fort Laramie Treaty (1868) guaranteed the Black Hills as Lakota territory; the U.S. broke the treaty when gold was discovered in the 1870s.
- Confined to reservations and subjected to forced assimilation policies, including the *Indian Boarding Schools* that separated children from families and culture.
- Today comprise several federally recognized tribes in South Dakota, North Dakota, Montana, and Nebraska; the Lakota language is still spoken and revitalization efforts are ongoing.
