# Robespierre

## summary

*Maximilien Robespierre* (1758–1794) was the architect of the Reign of Terror, wielding unprecedented power during the French Revolution as a leading member of the Committee of Public Safety. His zealous prosecution of "enemies of the state" and idealistic vision of a virtuous republic made him the revolution's most infamous figure before his own execution by guillotine.

## you gotta know

- Rose from relative obscurity as a deputy to the Estates-General to become the dominant force of the radical phase of the revolution by 1793.
- Orchestrated the trial and execution of *King Louis XVI* in January 1793, framing it as necessary to save the republic from monarchist betrayal.
- As a leading member of the Committee of Public Safety (1793–1794), he presided over the Reign of Terror—mass arrests, trials, and executions that killed tens of thousands.
- Championed the *Festival of the Supreme Being* (June 1794), a quasi-religious civic ceremony reflecting his belief in virtue and national rebirth.
- Demanded the execution of rivals like *Danton* and *Hébert*, accusing them of betraying revolutionary ideals for personal gain or laxity.
- Fell from power during the Thermidorian Reaction of July 1794 when the Convention turned against him; he was executed without trial the next day.
- His legacy epitomizes the revolution's descent into ideological terror and the dangers of unchecked revolutionary fervor.
