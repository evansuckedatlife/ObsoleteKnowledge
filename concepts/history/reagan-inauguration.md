# Reagan's Inauguration

## summary

*Ronald Reagan's* inaugural address on January 20, 1981, marked a decisive political realignment toward conservatism and anti-communist assertiveness. At 69, Reagan became the oldest president inaugurated at the time, declaring that "government is not the solution to our problem; government is the problem"—a statement that defined his presidency and the rightward shift of American politics.

## you gotta know

- Took place January 20, 1981, amid economic malaise ("stagflation") and Cold War tensions.
- Reagan declared that "government is not the solution to our problem; government is the problem," signaling his commitment to deregulation and reduced federal spending.
- Emphasized American economic and military strength as antidotes to Soviet communism.
- His inaugural parade cost over $16 million, the most expensive to that date, reflecting his popularity among conservatives.
- Promised a "new beginning" in American domestic and foreign policy, departing sharply from the Carter years.
- Spoke directly to Soviet leaders in ideological terms, rejecting détente and calling for renewed American resolve.
- Set the stage for his presidency's defining themes: tax cuts, military buildup, and anti-communist interventionism.
