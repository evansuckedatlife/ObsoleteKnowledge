# Battle of Teutoburg Forest

## summary

The *Battle of Teutoburg Forest* (9 AD) was a catastrophic Roman defeat where Germanic tribes led by *Arminius* annihilated three entire Roman legions in the forests of what is now Germany. The disaster halted Roman expansion into Germanic lands and made the Rhine the permanent frontier of the Western Empire.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 9 AD in the Teutoburg Forest in northern Germany (modern North Rhine-Westphalia).
- Roman legate *Quinctilius Varus* commanded approximately 15,000–20,000 troops from three legions (*XVII*, *XVIII*, *XIX*) plus auxiliaries.
- *Arminius*, a Germanic chieftain who had served in the Roman army and knew Roman tactics, united the tribal confederacy and planned an ambush.
- As Varus's column marched through forested terrain en route to winter quarters, the Germanic forces attacked from all sides, preventing Roman cohesion.
- Over three days of fighting, the Romans were nearly exterminated; Varus himself committed suicide rather than face capture.
- Only a handful of survivors and some scattered units escaped; the three legions ceased to exist.
- Roman Emperor *Augustus* abandoned plans to conquer the Germanic territories; the Rhine became Rome's Germanic frontier for four centuries.

## connections
