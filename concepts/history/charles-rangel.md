# Charles Rangel

## summary

*Charles Rangel* (1930–2016) was a Long Island politician and longtime Harlem congressman who chaired the *House Ways and Means Committee*, overseeing U.S. tax and trade policy. A Korean War veteran and consummate insider, he accumulated legislative seniority and power across five decades.

## you gotta know

- Elected to the House from Harlem in 1970; served 46 consecutive years until 2017, making him one of the longest-serving members in congressional history.
- First Black chairman of the *House Ways and Means Committee* (2007–2011), the most powerful fiscal committee in the House, controlling tax and tariff legislation.
- A decorated *Korean War* veteran whose military service shaped his commitment to veterans' benefits and labor protections.
- Championed the *Earned Income Tax Credit*, an anti-poverty program that became one of the largest federal social-welfare initiatives.
- Known for constituent service and political machine-building in Harlem; navigated ethics investigations in the 2010s without losing his seat.
- Mentored younger Black legislators and served as a bridge between old-guard civil rights politicians and post-movement Democratic leadership.
