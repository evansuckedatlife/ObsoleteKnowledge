# Almoravid Dynasty

## summary

The *Almoravid Dynasty* (1056–1147 CE) was a Berber military movement that emerged from the Sahara Desert, conquered the western Maghreb (North Africa) and Islamic Spain, and restored puritanical Islamic orthodoxy to a fragmented region. Though their rule was geographically vast and militarily powerful, the Almoravids were eventually overthrown by a more zealous reformist movement, the *Almohads*, whom they had inadvertently inspired.

## you gotta know

- Founded by *Abdallah ibn Yasin*, a puritanical Islamic reformer, as a desert monastic movement (*al-murabitun*, "those bound by religious duty"), uniting fractious Saharan Berber tribes under strict Islamic law.
- Conquered the *Maghreb* under *Yusuf ibn Tashfin* in the 1070s, establishing a capital at *Marrakesh* (1071 CE), which became one of the Islamic world's most magnificent cities.
- Intervened in Islamic *Spain* at the invitation of embattled *taifa* (petty) kings, halting the Christian *Reconquista*; won the *Battle of Sagrajas* (1086 CE) but ultimately annexed Spanish territories rather than protecting their allies.
- Enforced a literalist, fundamentalist interpretation of Islam that suppressed philosophical inquiry and theological innovation, earning them reputation as rigid and intolerant.
- Controlled the lucrative *trans-Saharan* gold trade from West Africa, accumulating wealth that funded monumental architecture in Marrakesh and Córdoba.
- Succumbed to the *Almohad movement* (another Berber reformist dynasty) by 1147 CE, who denounced the Almoravids as insufficiently rigorous and captured their territories.
