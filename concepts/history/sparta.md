# Sparta

## summary

*Sparta* is an austere warrior society located in the Peloponnese that dominated the classical Greek world through military might and iron discipline rather than intellectual or artistic achievement. Built on a rigid caste system with helots (enslaved subjects) toiling below a warrior aristocracy, Sparta became Athens's great rival and the supreme land power of ancient Greece.

## you gotta know

- Governed by a *dual monarchy*—two hereditary kings ruling jointly—along with a council of elders, a unique system among Greek city-states.
- Subjected boys to *Agoge*, a brutal state-sponsored military training regime from age 7, stripping them of individuality and forging them into efficient soldiers.
- Grounded in a warrior ethos: *Spartans* valued death in battle above surrender, summarized by the phrase *"Come back with your shield, or on it."*
- Defeated the Persian Empire at *Thermopylae* (480 BCE) where *Leonidas* and 300 hoplites held the pass against *Xerxes'* vast army.
- Emerged victorious in the *Peloponnesian War* (431–404 BCE) against Athens, becoming the dominant power in Greece for several decades.
- Employed a powerful navy of *triremes* (three-banked warships) and forged alliances through the *Peloponnesian League*.
- Deliberately remained isolated from arts and philosophy, viewing such pursuits as weaknesses; produced no dramatists or major philosophers of note.
