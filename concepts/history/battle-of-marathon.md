# Battle of Marathon

## summary

The *Battle of Marathon* (490 BC) was a pivotal clash between the Athenian and Eretrian forces against invading Persian armies. The Greeks' unexpected victory against numerically superior forces proved that the Persian Empire was not invincible and became a defining moment in Athenian identity and Western history.

## you gotta know

- Fought in 490 BC near the town of Marathon, approximately 40 km northeast of Athens.
- The Persian King *Darius I* sought to punish Athens for supporting the Ionian Revolt and expand his empire into Greece.
- Athenian commander *Miltiades* devised tactics that exploited terrain and the Persian weakness in close combat.
- The Greeks fielded roughly 10,000 hoplites against a Persian force estimated at 20,000–40,000 soldiers.
- The decisive tactic was a heavy infantry phalanx charge that broke the Persian center, causing a rout.
- The Persian fleet retreated to Asia Minor without launching a landing on the mainland.
- Legend holds that a runner named *Philippides* ran the 40 km from Marathon to Athens to announce victory, spawning the modern marathon race.
