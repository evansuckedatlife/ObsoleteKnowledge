## summary

**USS Constitution**, the oldest naval vessel still in commission, is a 44-gun wooden frigate launched in 1797 that earned the nickname "Old Ironsides" during the War of 1812 for her seeming invulnerability to cannon fire. She fought Barbary pirates and defeated the British frigate *HMS Guerrière*, becoming a symbol of American naval power and ingenuity in the early republic.

## you gotta know

- Launched in 1797 and designed by Joshua Humphreys with a revolutionary hull construction using diagonal bracing and thick oak planking.
- Served in the Barbary Wars (1801–1805) against North African pirate states threatening American merchant ships.
- In 1812, defeated the British frigate *HMS Guerrière* in a dramatic single-ship action where British cannon balls reportedly bounced off her hull, giving her the nickname "Old Ironsides."
- Remained in active service through the Mexican-American War and Civil War era before being mothballed and restored as a national memorial.
- Still berthed in Boston Harbor and remains the oldest commissioned warship in the world, fully restored and occasionally taken on ceremonial cruises.
- Her design influenced frigate construction globally and proved American shipwright innovations in durability and speed.
