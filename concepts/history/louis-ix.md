# Louis IX

## summary

*Louis IX* (1214–1270), canonized as Saint Louis, was a French king revered for his piety, justice, and military ambition. He led two major crusades and expanded French territory, becoming the ideal of Christian kingship and one of France's most celebrated monarchs.

## you gotta know

- Canonized in 1297 by Pope Boniface VIII; the only French king to be declared a saint, cementing his reputation for personal righteousness and moral authority.
- Led the Seventh Crusade (1248–1254) to recapture the Holy Land; captured by Saracens and ransomed, he later launched the Eighth Crusade (1270) where he died of plague in Tunis.
- Reformed the French legal system, hearing grievances personally under an oak tree at Vincennes; his codification of law and pursuit of impartial justice enhanced the crown's authority.
- Acquired the Crown of Thorns relic in 1239, built the Sainte-Chapelle in Paris to house it, and made France a pilgrimage destination for Christendom.
- Expanded French territory through the Treaty of Paris (1259) with Henry III of England, confirming French control of Normandy and other territories.
- His reign (1226–1270) consolidated Capetian power and made France the preeminent medieval Christian kingdom.
