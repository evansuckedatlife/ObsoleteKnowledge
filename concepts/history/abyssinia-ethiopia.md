# Abyssinia → Ethiopia

## summary

*Abyssinia* was the classical and colonial-era name for the ancient Christian empire in the Horn of Africa, derived from Greek *Abyssinos* ("those from the depths"). The nation officially adopted *Ethiopia* as its primary name during the reign of Emperor *Haile Selassie*, particularly after resisting Italian invasion in the 1930s. "Ethiopia" comes from *Geez* (the ancient Semitic language of the region), meaning "land of burnt faces," and represents a reclamation of indigenous identity over Greco-European nomenclature.

## you gotta know

- The name *Abyssinia* derives from Greek terminology used by ancient and medieval geographers to describe the African highlands.
- Ethiopia is one of the world's oldest Christian nations, adopting Christianity as its state religion in the 4th century under *Axumite* rule.
- *Haile Selassie* (1892–1975) was the reforming emperor who modernized Ethiopia and elevated the nation's status during the League of Nations era.
- Italy invaded Ethiopia in 1935 under Mussolini, but Emperor Haile Selassie rallied international support; Ethiopia emerged victorious at the 1936 *Battle of Adwa*, repelling invasion.
- The nation officially began using "Ethiopia" in international forums during the 1930s–1950s, and it became the standard designation after independence confirmation.
- Ethiopia was never fully colonized, unlike nearly all other African nations, preserving its ancient *Ge'ez* script, language, and Orthodox Christian traditions.
