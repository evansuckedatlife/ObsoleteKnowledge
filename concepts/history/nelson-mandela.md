# Nelson Mandela

## summary

*Nelson Mandela* was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and statesman who became the country's first Black president, serving from 1994 to 1999. Imprisoned for 27 years by the apartheid regime, he emerged as a symbol of resistance and reconciliation, winning the Nobel Peace Prize and steering South Africa through democratic transition with his doctrine of forgiveness.

## you gotta know

- Imprisoned on Robben Island from 1962 to 1990, spending much of his time in a limestone quarry, becoming a symbol of resistance to apartheid and of endurance.
- Joined the African National Congress (ANC) in 1944 and rose to prominence as a lawyer and political organizer before his arrest.
- Released in 1990 by President F.W. de Klerk amid international pressure, and immediately negotiated South Africa's transition from apartheid.
- Elected president of South Africa in the first multiracial democratic elections in 1994, creating the Truth and Reconciliation Commission to address past atrocities without revenge.
- Known for his philosophy of Ubuntu—"I am because we are"—and his commitment to forgiveness over retribution, setting a global model for transitional justice.
- Served a single term (1994–1999) and stepped down voluntarily, contrasting with many African leaders who consolidated power indefinitely.
