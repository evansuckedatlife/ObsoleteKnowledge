# Suebi

## summary

The *Suebi* were a Germanic people who invaded the Iberian Peninsula in the 5th century, establishing a kingdom in the northwest that rivaled the *Visigoths* for dominance. Though ultimately subdued by Visigothic expansion, the Suebi left a cultural imprint on Galicia and northern Iberia and demonstrated the complexity of early medieval Germanic politics in the peninsula.

## you gotta know

- Invaded Iberia around 409–410, settling primarily in Galicia and the northwestern regions of the peninsula, establishing an independent kingdom.
- Competed directly with the *Visigoths* for control of the peninsula; their existence as a rival power fragmented Iberian politics for over a century.
- Gradually Romanized and Christianized despite their Germanic origins, maintaining legal codes and administrative structures influenced by Roman models.
- Were progressively conquered and absorbed by the expanding *Visigothic* kingdom under *Leovigild* and his successors, losing political independence by the early 6th century.
- Their kingdom, though short-lived as an independent state, represented the kind of regional fragmentation typical of post-Roman Europe where multiple Germanic peoples competed for territory.
- Left few written records, making them less prominent in historical memory than the *Visigoths* or *Franks*, though their settlement influenced the cultural development of Galicia.
- Demonstrate the complexity of "barbarian" migration: the Suebi were neither simply destructive invaders nor wholly assimilated settlers, but participants in a complex reorganization of Mediterranean civilization.
