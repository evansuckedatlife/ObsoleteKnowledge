# Anwar Sadat

## summary

*Anwar Sadat* (1918–1981) was the third president of Egypt and a pivotal figure in Middle Eastern diplomacy, transforming Egypt's foreign policy from Soviet alignment to a peace treaty with Israel—a controversial gambit that earned him both international acclaim and assassination at the hands of domestic extremists.

## you gotta know

- Served as vice president under *Gamal Abdel Nasser* and succeeded him in 1970; adopted the title "Infitah" (opening) to signal economic and political reform.
- Launched the *Yom Kippur War* in 1973 as a tactical offensive against Israel, reclaiming Egyptian pride after the 1967 *Six-Day War* defeat.
- Shifted Egypt's geopolitical alignment from the Soviet Union to the United States, seeking Western aid and investment.
- Signed the *Camp David Accords* (1978) with Israeli Prime Minister *Menachem Begin*, brokered by U.S. President *Jimmy Carter*; the first Arab-Israeli peace treaty.
- Signed a formal *Egyptian-Israeli Peace Treaty* in 1979, for which he won the Nobel Peace Prize (shared with Begin); this isolating him from Arab states and the Soviet bloc.
- Was assassinated on October 6, 1981, by members of the *Islamic Jihad*, an extremist group opposed to his Western alignment and Israeli peace.
- Viewed by historians as a visionary pragmatist; controversial in the Arab world but celebrated in the West for ending decades of war.
