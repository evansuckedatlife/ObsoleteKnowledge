## summary

*Jomo Kenyatta* was Kenya's first president and founding father, leading the nation from British colonial rule to independence in 1964. A nationalist politician, writer, and cultural theorist, he became an icon of African independence while consolidating power through authoritarian governance and nation-building.

## you gotta know

- Spent 15 years in Britain (1931–1946) where he wrote *Facing Mount Kenya*, an anthropological study of Kikuyu culture that became a manifesto of African cultural pride and independence.
- Imprisoned by the British from 1952 to 1959 for his alleged involvement in the Mau Mau Rebellion, a violent anti-colonial uprising in Kenya.
- Became president of the independent Kenya in 1964 and remained in power for 15 years, building a centralized nation-state and consolidating Kikuyu political dominance.
- Adopted moderate, pro-Western policies, maintaining close ties to Britain and the West rather than pursuing the radical pan-Africanism of contemporaries like Nkrumah.
- Accumulated significant personal wealth and presided over patronage networks that benefited his family and ethnic group, setting a pattern for postcolonial African leadership.
- Died in office in 1978, leaving a stable but ethno-centric and authoritarian political legacy.
