# José de San Martín

## summary

**José de San Martín** (1778–1850) was an Argentine military leader who liberated Chile and Peru from Spanish rule, earning the title "Protector of Peru." A more conservative figure than Bolívar, he prioritized military success over political idealism and eventually withdrew from public life.

## you gotta know

- Commanded the *Army of the Andes*, which crossed the Andes mountains to liberate Chile from Spanish control in 1817.
- Declared Peru's independence in 1821 and served as Protector until internal political conflicts forced his resignation.
- Met Bolívar in Peru; the two disagreed on strategy, with San Martín favoring a constitutional monarchy while Bolívar pushed for republicanism.
- Spent his final years in voluntary exile in France, rejecting offers of power and refusing to meddle in South American politics.
- Known for his professional military demeanor and emphasis on institutional order over personality-driven leadership.
