# George III

## summary

George III was King of Great Britain and Ireland from 1760 until his death in 1820. His long reign was marked by global conflicts, including the Seven Years' War and the Napoleonic Wars, and most famously by the loss of the American colonies in the American Revolution. In his later years, he suffered from severe, recurring mental illness, leading to the establishment of the Regency.

## you gotta know

- He was the reigning British monarch during the *American Revolutionary War*, during which the colonies declared independence from his crown.
- He suffered from a recurring and eventually permanent mental illness (now hypothesized by some to be porphyria), which inspired the play and film *The Madness of King George*.
- His eldest son ruled as Prince Regent starting in 1811 due to George's complete incapacity, initiating the *Regency era*.
- His reign saw the formal unification of Great Britain and Ireland through the *Act of Union 1800*, creating the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland.
- He was nicknamed *Farmer George* due to his keen interest in agriculture, farming, and simple domestic life.
