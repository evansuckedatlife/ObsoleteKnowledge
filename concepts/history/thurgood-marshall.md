## summary

*Thurgood Marshall* was a pioneering civil rights lawyer who became the first African-American Supreme Court Justice, using the law as a weapon against segregation and discrimination. As chief counsel of the *NAACP Legal Defense Fund*, he argued *Brown v. Board of Education* (1954) and a string of landmark cases that dismantled the legal architecture of Jim Crow, fundamentally transforming American society.

## you gotta know

- Led the legal challenge to segregation as head of the *NAACP Legal Defense and Educational Fund*, developing a strategy to chip away at the "separate but equal" doctrine.
- Argued *Brown v. Board of Education* before the Supreme Court in 1954, successfully challenging school segregation as inherently unequal and unconstitutional.
- Had argued 32 cases before the Supreme Court by 1967, winning 29 of them — an extraordinary trial record that established him as a constitutional master.
- Appointed to the U.S. Court of Appeals in 1961 by President Kennedy, and to the Supreme Court in 1967 by President Lyndon Johnson.
- Served 24 years as a Supreme Court Justice (1967–1991), writing opinions that expanded civil rights, criminal procedure protections, and voting rights.
- Faced fierce Southern opposition and death threats during his civil rights career, yet remained committed to nonviolent legal struggle.
