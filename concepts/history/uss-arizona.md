## summary

**USS Arizona** (BB-39), a Pennsylvania-class battleship, sank in 102 seconds during the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941, killing 1,177 of her 1,377-person crew. She became the most iconic American casualty of the attack and remains a war grave at the bottom of Pearl Harbor, her loss symbolizing the surprise and devastation of the morning.

## you gotta know

- Launched in 1915 and served in the Pacific Fleet as one of America's most advanced capital ships before Pearl Harbor.
- Hit by a high-altitude bomb that penetrated her forward magazine, triggering a catastrophic explosion and fire that engulfed the ship in minutes.
- Lost 1,177 men (more than any other ship at Pearl Harbor) with most entombed in the wreck, making her the costliest ship loss of the entire Pacific War in terms of crew fatalities.
- Left resting on the harbor bottom, with her superstructure still visible above the waterline, serving as a submarine memorial and national monument.
- The USS Arizona Memorial, built over her remains in 1962, has become one of America's most visited war monuments and a pilgrimage site for those honoring Pearl Harbor casualties.
- Her loss steeled American resolve to prosecute the Pacific War and inflict eventual victory over Japan.
