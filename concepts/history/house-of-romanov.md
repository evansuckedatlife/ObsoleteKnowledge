# House of Romanov

## summary

The **House of Romanov** ruled Russia for over three centuries (1613–1918), transforming it from a provincial Muscovite state into one of the world's largest and most powerful empires. Under towering figures like *Peter the Great* and *Catherine the Great*, the Romanovs modernized Russia, expanded its territories across three continents, and made it a major player in European power politics—until revolution and civil war swept the dynasty away.

## you gotta know

- Began with *Michael Romanov*, elected Tsar in 1613 after the chaos of the *Time of Troubles*; ruled for over 300 years until the *Russian Revolution*.
- *Peter the Great* (r. 1682–1725) westernized Russia, built the city of *St. Petersburg* as a "window to the West," modernized the military, and expanded territory; visited Western Europe incognito to learn.
- *Catherine the Great* (r. 1762–1796), an enlightened despot, further expanded Russian territory through wars with the Ottoman Empire and Poland, making Russia a great power.
- Ruled an autocracy where the Tsar was the supreme authority; centralized bureaucracy and military were instruments of imperial control.
- Expanded Russian territory from Eastern Europe to Siberia and the Pacific; controlled vast multiethnic domains of Slavs, Caucasians, Central Asians, and others.
- *Nicholas II* (r. 1894–1917), the last Tsar, faced revolution born of modernization, war defeats (Russo-Japanese War, World War I), and social unrest; abdicated in March 1917.
- The family was executed by Bolsheviks in July 1918, ending the dynasty and any hope of restoration.
