# William Pitt the Younger

## summary

*William Pitt the Younger* (1759–1806) was Britain's youngest Prime Minister at 24 and dominated Parliament through the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars. His tenure (1783–1801, 1804–1806) made him the architect of Britain's fiscal system and the principal strategist against Napoleon, establishing the tax infrastructure that funded the Empire for a century.

## you gotta know

- Became PM at age 24 and served for nearly 19 years total, second only to Walpole in longevity.
- Reformed the tax system, introducing the income tax temporarily during the Napoleonic Wars to finance military campaigns.
- Advocated for parliamentary reform and Catholic emancipation, though he could not achieve either during his lifetime.
- Orchestrated the Third Coalition against Napoleon (1805) and subsidized allied powers, spending vast sums to contain French expansion.
- His phrase upon hearing of Napoleon's victory at Austerlitz — "roll up the map of Europe" — captured the strategic despair of the moment.
- Died in office in 1806, worn down by the strain of wartime leadership.
