## summary

*Lawrence v. Texas* (2003) is the Supreme Court decision that decriminalized consensual same-sex relations nationwide, striking down anti-sodomy laws in 14 states. The 6–3 ruling directly overturned the 1986 precedent *Bowers v. Hardwick*, affirming LGBTQ rights to privacy and establishing that criminal sodomy statutes violate the Fourteenth Amendment.

## you gotta know

- Decided June 26, 2003 by a 6–3 margin, with Justice Kennedy writing the majority opinion.
- Explicitly overturned *Bowers v. Hardwick*, calling that decision "not correct when decided and is not correct today."
- Struck down a Texas anti-sodomy law that criminalized consensual same-sex conduct but not identical conduct between heterosexual couples.
- The majority held that the liberty interest protected by the Due Process Clause encompasses the right to engage in consensual same-sex conduct.
- Justice Scalia's dissent warned that the ruling would logically lead to recognition of same-sex marriage (a prediction that proved accurate).
- Invalidated sodomy laws in 13 other states that still had them on the books, though most had been unenforced.
- Marked a dramatic reversal from *Bowers*, signaling the Court's evolving view on LGBTQ equality over 17 years.
