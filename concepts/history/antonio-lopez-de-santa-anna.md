# Antonio López de Santa Anna

## summary

*Antonio López de Santa Anna* was a Mexican military general and politician who served as president multiple times, alternating between liberal and conservative factions. His defeats in the Texas Revolution and Mexican-American War cost Mexico nearly half its territory.

## you gotta know

- Served as Mexican president at least 11 times between 1833 and 1855, embodying the country's political instability in the 19th century.
- Defeated Texan rebels at the *Alamo* (1836) but was himself captured after his loss at *San Jacinto*, leading to Texan independence.
- Led Mexican forces in the *Mexican-American War* (1846–1848), suffering defeats that forced Mexico to cede the Southwest and California.
- Signed the *Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo*, losing nearly half of Mexican territory—the greatest territorial loss in the nation's history.
- Returned to power repeatedly despite military failures, embodying the ambitions and weaknesses of post-independence Mexico.
- Died in obscurity in Mexico City in 1876, a symbol of a era's military and political chaos.
