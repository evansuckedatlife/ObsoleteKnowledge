# Battle of Crécy

## summary

The *Battle of Crécy* was fought on 26 August 1346 in northern France, where *Edward III* of England's forces decisively defeated a much larger French army led by *Philip VI*. The English victory, secured largely through the devastating effectiveness of the longbow, marked a turning point in medieval warfare and bolstered English claims during the *Hundred Years' War*.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the village of Crécy-en-Ponthieu in Picardy; English forces of about 10,000 faced a French army estimated at 40,000 or more.
- The English made effective use of the longbow, which could fire arrows at a much higher rate and penetrate armour more reliably than crossbows or traditional medieval arms.
- The French suffered devastating losses in cavalry and heavily armoured knights who charged repeatedly against the English positions without success.
- *Edward III*'s son, the *Black Prince*, fought prominently in the battle and became a celebrated military figure across Europe.
- The battle demonstrated that disciplined infantry with ranged weapons could overcome the feudal cavalry charge, a fundamental shift in medieval military tactics.
- The French losses, including the death of many nobles and thousands of soldiers, weakened France's power and encouraged English territorial expansion in France.
