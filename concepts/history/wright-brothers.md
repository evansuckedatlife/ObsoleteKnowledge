# Wright Brothers

## summary

*Orville* and *Wilbur Wright*, American bicycle mechanics, achieved the first powered, controlled, heavier-than-air flight on December 17, 1903, near Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, launching the age of aviation. Their methodical approach to aerodynamics through wind-tunnel testing established the discipline of flight engineering and set the blueprint for all aircraft development to follow.

## you gotta know

- First powered flight lasted 12 seconds and covered 120 feet in their aircraft, the *Flyer*.
- Conducted extensive wind-tunnel experiments on airfoil shapes and control systems before attempting powered flight.
- Developed the three-axis control system—pitch, roll, yaw—still fundamental to aircraft design.
- Held patents on airplane design; spent years defending them against competitors, including Glenn Curtiss.
- Wilbur died in 1912; Orville continued aviation work and lived to see jet aircraft.
- Flew the *Flyer II* in 1904 and the *Flyer III* in 1905, making longer and more controlled flights.
- Their work was initially dismissed or downplayed in Europe, where French and German aviators claimed primacy.

## connections

- [[glenn-curtiss]] — rival whose aircraft designs and patent disputes spawned decades of litigation.
- [[charles-lindbergh]] — flew the *Spirit of St. Louis* inspired by the principles the Wrights pioneered.
- [[jimmy-doolittle]] — carried their aeronautical legacy into the age of speed and competition flying.
