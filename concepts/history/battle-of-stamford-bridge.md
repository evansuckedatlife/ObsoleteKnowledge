# Battle of Stamford Bridge

## summary

The *Battle of Stamford Bridge* was fought on 25 September 1066 in Yorkshire, England, where King *Harold Godwinson* defeated an invading Norwegian army led by King *Harold Hardrada*. Though a decisive English victory, the battle exhausted *Harold*'s forces and left him fatally vulnerable to the Norman invasion that followed just three weeks later, inadvertently contributing to the Norman Conquest.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the village of Stamford Bridge on the River Derwent in Yorkshire; *Harold Godwinson*'s Saxon forces routed the invading Norwegian and Viking mercenary army led by *Harold Hardrada*.
- *Harold Hardrada*, legendary Viking warrior-king of Norway, was killed in the battle—accounts describe him as an extremely formidable opponent despite his advanced age.
- The Saxon victory came through superior numbers and the element of surprise; the Norwegian forces, caught off-guard while foraging, were driven into the river and slaughtered.
- *Harold Godwinson*'s force, though victorious, suffered significant losses and was severely weakened by the battle's casualties and the exhausting 300-mile march north and back to the south coast.
- The battle is often called the last Viking invasion of England, symbolising the end of the Viking Age and Norse dominance in northern Europe.
- Ironically, *Harold*'s depleted forces were unprepared when *William the Conqueror*'s Normans landed just three weeks later, contributing to the fateful English defeat at Hastings.
