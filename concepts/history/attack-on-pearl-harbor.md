# Attack on Pearl Harbor

## summary

On December 7, 1941, the Japanese Imperial Navy launched a surprise attack on the United States naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, sinking eight battleships and killing over 1,100 sailors. The assault galvanized American public opinion and led to the U.S. declaration of war against Japan the following day, formally drawing the nation into World War II.

## you gotta know

- Carried out by 353 Japanese aircraft from six aircraft carriers, led by Commander Mitsuo Fuchida; coordinated from a depth of ~275 km northeast of the island.
- Destroyed or damaged eight battleships, three cruisers, and 188 aircraft; total casualties exceeded 1,200 American military personnel.
- President Franklin D. Roosevelt called it "a date which will live in infamy" in his address to Congress on December 8, 1941.
- The attack aimed to neutralize the U.S. Pacific Fleet and buy Japan time to consolidate control over Southeast Asia and the Pacific.
- Japanese planners achieved tactical surprise by treating Pearl Harbor as the main target rather than escaping after; the Americans received no warning despite some radio intercepts.
- U.S. aircraft carrier fleet was at sea during the attack, preserving the most valuable warships and enabling American recovery.
- Unified American political will for war; even isolationist politicians voted for the declaration, shifting the nation's entire strategic posture.
