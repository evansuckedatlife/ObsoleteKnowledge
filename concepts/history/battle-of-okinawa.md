# Battle of Okinawa

## summary

The longest and costliest of the Pacific island campaigns, Okinawa (April–June 1945) saw American forces battle a Japanese garrison defending an island that would serve as a staging ground for the invasion of the Japanese home islands. With over 100,000 total casualties, the devastating battle reinforced American military planners' expectation that a direct invasion of Japan would be catastrophically expensive, contributing to the decision to use atomic weapons.

## you gotta know

- Campaign lasted 82 days; Japanese garrison of approximately 77,000 troops commanded by General Masao Yoshida.
- American forces: approximately 183,000 from Army, Navy, and Marine Corps under General Simon Buckner Jr.
- U.S. casualties: 7,613 killed, 31,000 wounded; Japanese casualties: 62,000+ killed (most refused to surrender).
- Japanese also mobilized 150,000 civilian conscripts as militia; widespread civilian deaths estimated at 50,000 or more.
- Japanese deployed massive *kamikaze* attacks; U.S. Navy suffered 4,907 deaths and numerous ships damaged.
- Shuri Castle, the ancient seat of Okinawan royal power, became the centerpiece of Japanese defenses; captured late in the campaign.
- Strategic importance: final large island before Japanese home islands; U.S. viewed it as necessary staging area for invasion operations.
- Anticipated even greater losses for invasion of Honshu; influenced American decision-makers' calculations on weapon alternatives.
