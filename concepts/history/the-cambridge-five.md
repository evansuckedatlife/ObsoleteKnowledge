# The Cambridge Five

## summary

*The Cambridge Five* were a ring of British intelligence officers and diplomats who were secretly recruited by Soviet intelligence while at Cambridge University in the 1930s. They systematically betrayed British and American secrets throughout the Cold War, making them history's most damaging espionage network against the Western alliance.

## you gotta know

- Recruited at Cambridge in the 1930s by Soviet intelligence operatives who exploited intellectual idealism and left-wing sympathies among elite British students.
- The five comprised *Kim Philby* (head of MI6 counterintelligence), *Donald Maclean* (Foreign Office), *Guy Burgess* (British intelligence and Foreign Office), *Anthony Blunt* (MI5), and *John Cairncross* (Foreign Office and later GCHQ).
- Operated simultaneously in British intelligence, the Foreign Office, and liaison positions with American intelligence, giving Moscow comprehensive access to Western strategic thinking and agent networks.
- Exposed NATO plans, atomic bomb research, diplomatic communications, and the identities of Western intelligence officers and assets, shaping Soviet policy and military planning for over two decades.
- Three members—Philby, Maclean, and Burgess—defected to Moscow in the 1950s-60s; Blunt and Cairncross remained in the West until their exposure, with Blunt only being publicly named after his death.
- Became the template for the modern "mole" in spy fiction and popular culture—the trusted insider whose loyalty conceals a deeper betrayal.
