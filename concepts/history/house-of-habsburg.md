# House of Habsburg

## summary

The **House of Habsburg** was one of Europe's most powerful and longest-reigning dynasties, ruling Austria, Spain, Hungary, and much of Central Europe from the 13th century until 1918. Rising from a small Swiss canton, the Habsburgs used strategic marriages to accumulate territories across three continents, and their imperial reach shaped European politics, religion, and culture for over six centuries.

## you gotta know

- Rose from obscurity in Switzerland to become rulers of Austria by the 14th century; expanded through shrewd marriage alliances rather than conquest.
- Produced the *Holy Roman Emperors* continuously from 1438 until the empire's dissolution in 1806.
- Ruled the *Spanish Empire* from 1516 to 1700 under *Charles V* and his successors, controlling Spain, the Netherlands, parts of Italy, and Spanish America.
- *Maria Theresa* (1717–1780) modernized the Austrian state, reformed its military, and engaged in the War of Austrian Succession to retain Habsburg territories against Prussia.
- The *Austro-Hungarian Empire* (1867–1918) was their final and second-largest realm, stretching across Central and Eastern Europe with the dual monarchy of Austria and Hungary.
- Ruled Catholic Central Europe; played a central role in the Counter-Reformation and the Thirty Years' War.
- The assassination of *Archduke Franz Ferdinand* in 1914 triggered World War I and ultimately led to the dynasty's collapse in 1918.
