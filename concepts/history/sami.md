# Sámi

## summary

The *Sámi* are the indigenous people of northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula, traditionally inhabiting territories spanning present-day Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia. They developed a specialized pastoral culture centered on reindeer herding and maintained distinct languages, traditional dress, and spiritual practices despite centuries of marginalization and assimilation pressure from surrounding states.

## you gotta know

- Inhabit *Sápmi*, their traditional territory across northern *Norway*, *Sweden*, *Finland*, and Russia's *Kola Peninsula*; state borders divide their homeland.
- Speak *Sámi languages*, an Uralic language family with nine distinct languages; *Northern Sámi* is the most widely spoken; many regional varieties are endangered.
- Historically practiced *reindeer herding*, a specialized pastoral tradition requiring deep knowledge of Arctic ecology, animal behavior, and seasonal migration routes; herding remains culturally significant.
- Wear distinctive traditional dress (*gákti*) featuring bright colors, intricate embroidery, and silver jewelry; regional variations reflect different Sámi communities.
- Practice *Sámi joik*, a unique vocal tradition of wordless singing expressing emotions, places, and relationships with nature; distinct from Nordic folk singing.
- Experienced systematic assimilation policies in the 19th–20th centuries; Nordic states suppressed language, culture, and land rights in schools and official institutions.
- Gained increasing political recognition since the 1970s; established parliaments in Norway, Sweden, and Finland; ongoing struggles over land rights and environmental protection.
