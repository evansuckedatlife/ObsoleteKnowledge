# Battle of Waterloo

## summary

The decisive defeat of Napoleon on June 18, 1815, near the village of Waterloo in present-day Belgium, ended his Hundred Days return to power and marked the definitive close of the Napoleonic Wars. Allied forces under the Duke of Wellington and Prussian General Blücher defeated the French army in a brutal engagement that determined the final shape of European politics for a generation.

## you gotta know

- Fought on June 18, 1815, between Napoleon's reconstituted Grande Armée and a coalition of British, Dutch, Belgian, and Prussian forces.
- The French aimed to defeat the Allied armies separately before they could consolidate; Napoleon attacked the British-Dutch-Belgian center under Wellington throughout the day.
- British infantry squares repelled repeated cavalry charges; the Duke of Wellington famously held the ridge and refused to advance until the Prussians could fully commit.
- Prussian Field Marshal Blücher's army, which arrived on the French right flank in the afternoon, turned the battle decisively against Napoleon.
- French casualties were around 25,000; Allied casualties comparable, but the French could not replace losses and the Allies could.
- Following Waterloo, Napoleon abdicated for a second time and was exiled to the remote island of Saint Helena in the South Atlantic.
- The battle established Wellington's military reputation and ended the era of French revolutionary and Napoleonic dominance of continental Europe.
