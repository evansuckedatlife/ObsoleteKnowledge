# Burgundians

## summary

The *Burgundians* were a Germanic people who settled in the Rhône valley and eastern Gaul in the 5th century, establishing a kingdom that became a sophisticated cultural and political power in early medieval Burgundy. Though eventually absorbed into the Frankish realm, they left an indelible mark on the region and contributed distinctive institutions to medieval European civilization.

## you gotta know

- Originally settled along the Rhine as Roman foederati before migrating to the Rhône valley and establishing their kingdom in the 440s.
- Under early kings like *Gundioc*, expanded their territory and created a stable kingdom that balanced Frankish and Eastern Roman (Byzantine) influences.
- Converted to Christianity relatively early and maintained strong ties to Roman law and civilization, making them less culturally alien to the Roman population than some Germanic peoples.
- Developed *Lex Burgundionum* (*Law of the Burgundians*), a written legal code that blended Roman and Germanic traditions and influenced later medieval legal systems.
- Created a regional aristocracy that became the foundation of Burgundian nobility, giving the region a distinctive feudal character that persisted for centuries.
- Fell gradually to Frankish expansion and were incorporated into the Merovingian kingdom; their territories became the Duchy of Burgundy in the Carolingian system.
- The region of Burgundy retained cultural and administrative distinctiveness well into the High Middle Ages, partly due to their early development of local institutions.
