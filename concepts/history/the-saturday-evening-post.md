# The Saturday Evening Post

## summary

*The Saturday Evening Post* is an American weekly magazine founded in 1821 that became the most widely circulated periodical in the United States during the early twentieth century. Known for its wholesome family content, fiction by major authors, and especially Norman Rockwell's iconic cover illustrations, it epitomized mid-century American optimism and values.

## you gotta know

- Founded in *Philadelphia* in 1821; became the longest-continuously published magazine in American history (until 1969 suspension, later revived).
- Circulation peaked at over *6 million* copies per week in the 1930s, making it the best-selling magazine of its era.
- Employed *Norman Rockwell* as cover illustrator from 1916 to 1963, commissioning over 300 paintings that became defining images of American identity.
- Published serial fiction and short stories by major authors including *F. Scott Fitzgerald*, *Sinclair Lewis*, and *Ring Lardner*.
- Famous advertising pages shaped consumer culture; ads were as eagerly awaited as editorial content.
- Adopted the tagline "America's Greatest Weekly" and explicitly targeted the middle-class family market.
- Known for conservative, patriotic editorial stance during World War II and the Cold War.
