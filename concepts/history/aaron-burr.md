# Aaron Burr

## summary

**Aaron Burr** was the third Vice President of the United States under Thomas Jefferson (1801–1805), and a central figure in early American politics whose political career was derailed by his infamous duel with Alexander Hamilton. He remains one of the most controversial and enigmatic figures of the founding era.

## you gotta know

- Shot and killed Alexander Hamilton in a dawn duel in Weehawken, New Jersey in 1804, over insults related to Hamilton's opposition to Burr's political ambitions.
- Served as Vice President under Thomas Jefferson but was isolated from power and sidelined by the president's Republican faction.
- Ran for the New York gubernatorial office and the U.S. Senate, but his political career was destroyed by the Hamilton duel and subsequent scandal.
- Attempted to establish an independent empire in the western frontier, allegedly scheming to separate territories from the United States and place himself at the head of a new nation.
- Was tried for treason (1807) for his alleged conspiracy to invade Spanish territory and establish a western republic, but was acquitted.
- Fled to Europe after his acquittal to escape creditors and political enemies, spending years in exile before returning to America to practice law.
- Had a complex relationship with New York politics and the founding generation; his grandaughter married a prominent statesman.
