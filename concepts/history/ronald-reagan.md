# Ronald Reagan

## summary

Ronald Reagan (1911–2004) was the 40th President of the United States, serving from 1981 to 1989, and a transformative conservative figure who reshaped American politics and foreign policy. An actor-turned-politician, he championed free-market economics (Reaganomics), military strength against the Soviet Union, and a rollback of the welfare state. His presidency coincided with the final phase of the Cold War and the Soviet Union's economic collapse, making him a hero to American conservatives.

## you gotta know

- Former Hollywood actor and Democratic supporter who became a Republican in the 1960s; governed California (1967–1975) as a conservative; built a coalition of social conservatives, anti-Communists, and free-market advocates.
- Elected president in 1980 on a platform of tax cuts, deregulation, and aggressive anti-Communism; defeated the incumbent Jimmy Carter with 51% of the vote and won a landslide re-election in 1984.
- Implemented *Reaganomics*: massive tax cuts for the wealthy (on the theory that growth would "trickle down"), deregulation, and cuts to social programs; the economy recovered from 1982 onward, though inequality increased.
- Pursued a muscular anti-Communist foreign policy worldwide: supported right-wing insurgencies in Nicaragua, Angola, and Afghanistan (arming the Mujahideen against the Soviets); intervened in Grenada and Lebanon.
- *Iran-Contra scandal*: his administration secretly sold arms to Iran (despite an embargo) and funneled proceeds to Contra insurgents in Nicaragua, violating Congressional restrictions; the scandal was contained, but the illegality was proven.
- Met Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev four times; initially hostile ("evil empire"), he shifted to engagement as Gorbachev pursued reform (*glasnost* and *perestroika*); their summits reduced Cold War tensions and enabled arms control agreements.
- Delivered the famous *"Tear Down This Wall"* speech at the Brandenburg Gate (1987), calling on Gorbachev to remove the Berlin Wall—a symbolic moment preceding the Cold War's end.
- Won strong approval ratings and cultivated a "Morning in America" image, using optimistic rhetoric about American renewal.
