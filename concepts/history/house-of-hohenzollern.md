# House of Hohenzollern

## summary

The **House of Hohenzollern** rose from minor German nobility to rule Prussia and, ultimately, a unified German Empire spanning Central Europe. Through military genius, administrative reform, and aggressive statecraft, the Hohenzollerns transformed Prussia into Europe's mightiest power in the 18th and 19th centuries before leading Germany into World War I and abdication in 1918.

## you gotta know

- Originated in Swabia; acquired the *Margraviate of Brandenburg* in the 15th century, which became the kernel of Prussian power.
- *Frederick the Great* (r. 1740–1786) transformed Prussia into a major European power through military victories, Enlightened reforms, and the conquest of Silesia from Austria.
- Developed the *Prussian military system*—disciplined, efficient, and feared throughout Europe; the army was the state and the state was the army.
- *Otto von Bismarck* (Chancellor, 1862–1890) unified the fragmented German states through war and diplomacy, creating the *German Empire* (1871) under King *William I*.
- *Kaiser Wilhelm II* (r. 1888–1918), the last German emperor, presided over imperial expansion, naval rivalry with Britain, and Germany's ill-fated plunge into World War I.
- Abdicated in November 1918 as Germany faced military defeat and revolution; the dynasty ended with the proclamation of the *Weimar Republic*.
- Their cultural patronage enriched Prussia and Germany; supported Prussian militarism, efficiency, and Enlightened absolutism.
