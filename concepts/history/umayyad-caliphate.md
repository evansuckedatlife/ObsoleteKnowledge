# Umayyad Caliphate

## summary

The *Umayyad Caliphate* (661–750 CE) was the first hereditary Islamic dynasty, centered in Damascus and ruled by Arab aristocracy, which expanded Islamic rule across North Africa and into Spain and Central Asia. Despite its military success and the flowering of early Islamic art and architecture, the Umayyad line was overthrown by the *Abbasid Revolution*, which promised a more inclusive Islamic state.

## you gotta know

- Founded by *Mu'awiyah I* after defeating *Ali ibn Abi Talib*, shifting from elective to dynastic succession and moving the capital from Medina to *Damascus*.
- Conquered *North Africa* (670s–690s), *Spain* (711–715), and *Central Asia* up to the borders of China, making it one of history's largest empires by geographic extent.
- Defeated at the *Battle of Tours* (732 CE) in France—halting further Islamic expansion into Western Europe, a turning point in medieval history.
- Patronized early Islamic art, calligraphy, and the construction of the *Dome of the Rock* in Jerusalem, establishing enduring aesthetic standards.
- Arab dominance bred resentment among non-Arab converts (*mawali*) and Shiite communities, creating internal tensions that weakened the dynasty.
- Overthrown in 750 CE by the *Abbasid Revolution*, which promised a more merit-based caliphate; the last Umayyad surviving prince fled to Spain and established the *Umayyad Emirate of Córdoba*.
