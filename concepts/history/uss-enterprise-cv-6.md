## summary

**USS Enterprise** (CV-6), the legendary Yorktown-class aircraft carrier nicknamed "Big E," was the most-decorated ship of World War II, participating in nearly every major naval engagement in the Pacific and surviving damage that sank other ships. Her combat record, from Pearl Harbor through the final raids on Japan, made her the archetype of American naval power in the aircraft carrier age.

## you gotta know

- Commissioned in 1938 and one of the few American carriers in the Pacific on December 7, 1941; escaped Pearl Harbor damage by being at sea during the attack.
- Participated in every major Pacific carrier battle: the Coral Sea (May 1942), Midway (June 1942), the Eastern Solomons (August 1942), and Santa Cruz (October 1942).
- At Midway, her air group contributed significantly to the sinking of four Japanese fleet carriers, the battle that reversed the war's trajectory in the Pacific.
- Survived multiple bomb and torpedo hits throughout the war, including at Santa Cruz where she suffered 70 casualties and massive damage but remained operational.
- Earned 20 battle stars—more than any other American warship—and launched nearly 40,000 combat sorties during her service.
- Decommissioned in 1947 and scrapped in 1960, denying her the memorial status given to USS Arizona and USS Missouri, a decision many deemed unjust for her distinguished record.
