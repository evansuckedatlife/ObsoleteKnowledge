# Ladies' Home Journal

## summary

*Ladies' Home Journal*, founded in 1883, was the first magazine explicitly designed and edited for women, reaching unprecedented circulation and shaping American domesticity, consumer culture, and women's roles. Under editor *Edward Bok*, it became the best-selling magazine of its era and influenced household practices, fashion, and social attitudes nationwide.

## you gotta know

- Founded in 1883 by *Cyrus Curtis*; expanded to millions of copies under editor *Edward Bok* (1889–1919).
- Circulation peaked at over *2 million* in 1911, making it the best-selling American magazine of its time.
- Editorial content focused on home, hearth, fashion, cooking, childcare, and women's domestic and social roles.
- Published fiction by major authors; *Louisa May Alcott*, *Sarah Orne Jewett*, and others wrote for it.
- Famous for its advertising — showcasing consumer goods (appliances, clothing, beauty products) directly to women shoppers.
- Edward Bok pioneered the *professional woman editor* role and used his platform for modest social reform: public health, women's education, and moral causes.
- Shaped American standards for home decoration, cooking, fashion, and women's social expectations throughout the twentieth century.
