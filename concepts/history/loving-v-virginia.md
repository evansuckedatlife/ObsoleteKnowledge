# Loving v. Virginia

## summary

*Loving v. Virginia* (1967) struck down all remaining state laws criminalizing interracial marriage, ruling that such prohibitions violated the *14th Amendment*'s guarantees of equal protection and due process. Richard and Mildred Loving, a Black woman and white man, had been convicted in Virginia and exiled from the state; the Supreme Court's unanimous decision eliminated the last major Jim Crow tool for enforcing racial separation.

## you gotta know

- *Richard and Mildred Loving* were a married couple prosecuted under Virginia's Racial Integrity Act for their marriage; they were exiled from the state for 25 years.
- Chief Justice *Earl Warren* wrote that marriage is a fundamental liberty and that restricting it by race violates equal protection under the *14th Amendment*.
- Sixteen states still had miscegenation laws on the books at the time; the decision invalidated all of them at once.
- The Lovings' case became a symbol of both personal freedom and the waning days of Jim Crow legal scaffolding.
- One of the broadest civil rights victories of the era, with no sustained dissent and little organized opposition.
- Established that the right to marry is a fundamental constitutional protection that may not be abridged on racial grounds.
