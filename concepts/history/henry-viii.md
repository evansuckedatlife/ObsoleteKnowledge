# Henry VIII

## summary

Henry VIII was King of England from 1509 to 1547. He is best known for initiating the English Reformation, separating the Church of England from papal authority, and marrying six times in his quest for a male heir. His reign transformed England's religious landscape, centralising royal power and dissolving the monasteries.

## you gotta know

- He passed the *Act of Supremacy* in 1534, declaring himself the Supreme Head of the Church of England.
- He dissolved and confiscated the wealth of Catholic monasteries in England, Wales, and Ireland in a campaign known as the *Dissolution of the Monasteries*.
- He had six wives, famously summarized by the mnemonic "divorced, beheaded, died, divorced, beheaded, survived."
- His chief minister *Thomas Cromwell* helped engineer the annulment of his first marriage to Catherine of Aragon, but was later executed by the king.
- He was granted the title *Fidei Defensor* ("Defender of the Faith") by Pope Leo X in 1521, a title English monarchs still bear despite the later split with Rome.
