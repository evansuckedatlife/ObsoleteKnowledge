# Kwame Nkrumah

## summary

*Kwame Nkrumah* was Ghana's first president and a driving force behind African independence and pan-Africanism. He led Ghana to independence in 1957, making it the first sub-Saharan African colony to achieve self-rule, and championed a vision of continental unity that inspired liberation movements across Africa.

## you gotta know

- Led Ghana (then Gold Coast) to independence from Britain in 1957, becoming Africa's first sub-Saharan nation to break free from colonial rule and setting a template for other independence movements.
- Founder of the Organisation of African Unity (OAU) in 1963, promoting pan-Africanism and continental solidarity against imperialism.
- Pursued ambitious industrialization and nation-building projects, building the Volta River hydroelectric dam and expanding education and infrastructure across Ghana.
- Introduced a one-party socialist system and concentrated presidential power, eventually dissolving the legislature and ruling increasingly authoritatively.
- Overthrown in a military coup in 1966 while visiting China, exiled for a decade, and died in Romania in 1972 without returning to Ghana.
- Spent his last years writing on African socialism and revolutionary theory, positioning himself as an ideological leader of the postcolonial struggle.
