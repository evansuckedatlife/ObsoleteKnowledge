# Haile Selassie

## summary

*Haile Selassie I* was Emperor of Ethiopia from 1930 until his overthrow in 1974, the last ruling member of Ethiopia's Solomonic dynasty. A modernizer and pan-Africanist who championed African sovereignty and dignity, he became a spiritual icon in Rastafarianism while defending his nation against Italian fascism and colonialism.

## you gotta know

- Crowned emperor in 1930, taking the throne name Haile Selassie ("Might of the Trinity") after decades of modernizing Ethiopian government and military as regent and then heir.
- Defended Ethiopia's independence and sovereignty against Italian invasion in 1935–1936, appealing to the League of Nations in a dramatic speech that galvanized anti-colonial sentiment worldwide.
- Fled Ethiopia during the Italian occupation (1936–1941) and lived in exile in Britain, returning after British-led forces liberated the country, rebuilding the nation into a respected regional power.
- Founded the Organisation of African Unity (OAU) in Addis Ababa in 1963, positioning Ethiopia as the symbolic capital and himself as a continental leader.
- Became a divinity figure in Rastafarianism, with Jamaican and Caribbean followers revering him as the incarnation of God (Jah), though he was Coptic Christian and never embraced the faith.
- Overthrown in a Marxist military coup in 1974 and died in captivity in 1975, ending the Solomonic dynasty's 3,000-year reign.
