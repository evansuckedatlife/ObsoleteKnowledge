## summary

*Louis-Antoine de Saint-Just* (1767–1794) was a radical revolutionary and Robespierre's closest political ally, serving as a key architect of the Reign of Terror on the Committee of Public Safety. His speeches advocating total revolutionary purity and his zealous prosecution of enemies of the state made him one of the revolution's most feared figures before his execution alongside Robespierre in July 1794.

## you gotta know

- Emerged as a young radical deputy to the National Convention with a gift for oratory and an uncompromising vision of revolutionary virtue.
- Became a dominant force on the Committee of Public Safety (1793–1794), alongside Robespierre, signing arrest warrants and calling for purges of suspected enemies.
- Delivered influential speeches calling for the execution of *King Louis XVI*, framing it as necessary to eliminate the revolution's enemies.
- Orchestrated the elimination of the *Enragés* (radical extremists), the *Hébertists*, and the *Dantonists*, using terror as an instrument of revolutionary purification.
- Authored the *Ventôse Decrees* (February–March 1794), which attempted to redistribute émigrés' and suspects' property to the poor, embodying radical redistributive ideals.
- Fell from power alongside Robespierre during the Thermidorian Reaction of July 1794, arrested without support from the Convention.
- Executed by guillotine on July 28, 1794 (9 Thermidor), having made no attempt to defend himself or recant his beliefs.
