# Yasser Arafat

## summary

*Yasser Arafat* (1929–2004) was the founder and longtime leader of the Palestine Liberation Organization and the principal architect of Palestinian nationalism as a political movement. His journey from guerrilla militant to peace negotiator—culminating in the Oslo Accords—made him the defining face of the Palestinian cause for nearly five decades, though his legacy remains deeply contested.

## you gotta know

- Founded the *Fatah* faction (1959), an armed nationalist movement; became chairman of the *Palestine Liberation Organization* (PLO) in 1969.
- Led the PLO's guerrilla campaign against Israel from the 1960s through the 1980s, operating from bases in Jordan, Lebanon, and Tunisia.
- Was expelled from Jordan (1970–1971), then from Lebanon after the 1982 Israeli invasion; relocated the PLO to Tunis.
- Signed the *Oslo Accords* (1993) with Israeli Prime Minister *Yitzhak Rabin*, recognizing Israel and accepting a limited Palestinian self-governance in the West Bank and Gaza.
- Became president of the Palestinian National Authority (1996–2004), the elected leadership of Palestinian territories.
- Remained a polarizing figure: to supporters, a nationalist freedom fighter; to critics, complicit in terrorism and corruption.
- Died in Paris in November 2004 amid the Second Intifada; his legacy shapes Palestinian political identity to this day.
