# Ayyubid Dynasty

## summary

The *Ayyubid Dynasty* (1169–1260 CE) was a Kurdish military clan founded by *Saladin* (*Salah ad-Din al-Ayyubi*), who unified Egypt and the *Levant* and became Islam's most celebrated warrior-hero by defeating the Crusaders at the *Battle of Hattin*. Though Saladin's successors could not maintain his unified empire, the Ayyubids established lasting Islamic governance in Egypt and inspired centuries of Islamic historical memory.

## you gotta know

- Founded by *Ayyub ibn Shadi*, a Kurdish military officer; rose to power under his son *Saladin*, who served as vizier to the Fatimid caliph before seizing control in 1171 CE.
- *Saladin* (r. 1169–1193 CE) is the dynasty's defining figure—a commander who united fractious Islamic emirates, crushed the Crusaders at the *Battle of Hattin* (1187 CE), recaptured *Jerusalem*, and became a legendary symbol of Islamic chivalry and resistance.
- Ruled *Egypt*, *Syria*, *Palestine*, *Yemen*, and parts of *Mesopotamia*, creating a vast unified realm that restored Sunni Islam and ended Shiite Fatimid rule.
- Practiced relative magnanimity toward defeated enemies and religious minorities; after reconquering Jerusalem, Saladin permitted Christian inhabitants safe passage rather than ordering a massacre, earning respect across Christendom.
- Saladin's heirs fragmented the empire almost immediately after his death, with his nephew *Al-Adil* and subsequent sons dividing territories among themselves.
- Replaced by the *Mamluk Sultanate* (a slave-soldier military regime) in Egypt by 1250 CE, though Ayyubid princes lingered as nominal rulers in Syria until 1260 CE.
