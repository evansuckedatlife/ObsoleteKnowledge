## summary

The *Battle of Manzikert* was fought on 26 August 1071 in eastern Anatolia (modern Turkey), where the Seljuk Sultan *Alp Arslan*'s forces defeated the Byzantine Emperor *Romanus IV Diogenes*. The catastrophic loss ceded control of Anatolia to the Turks and fundamentally weakened the Byzantine Empire, triggering events that would reshape the medieval Middle East and eventually prompt the Christian Crusades.

## you gotta know

- Fought near the fortress city of Manzikert in Armenian territory; the Byzantine army was estimated at 30,000–40,000 while the Seljuk force was roughly 15,000.
- *Romanus IV Diogenes* led the Byzantine forces into what became an ambush; the Seljuks feigned retreat and then surrounded the Byzantine army, breaking it utterly.
- The emperor was captured and ransomed, returning to Constantinople in shame; his defeat severely damaged imperial prestige and weakened his position at court.
- The loss of Anatolia—historically the heart of the Byzantine Empire and its primary source of soldiers and resources—shifted the balance of power in the eastern Mediterranean.
- The Byzantine request for military aid against the Turks, sent to the Pope in the West, was a key factor in prompting the First Crusade (1096–1099).
- Manzikert marked the beginning of Turkish-Muslim dominance in Anatolia and the permanent reduction of the Byzantine Empire to a shrinking coastal state.
