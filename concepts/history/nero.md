# Nero

## summary

Nero was the fifth Roman emperor and the last of the Julio-Claudian dynasty, ruling from AD 54 to 68. Coming to power at age sixteen, his reign was marked by extravagant spectacles, artistic pretensions, and increasing brutality, including the murders of his mother and his first two wives. His loss of political support led to a rebellion, prompting him to commit suicide and plunging the empire into a period of civil war.

## you gotta know

- He famously "fiddled while Rome burned" during the *Great Fire of Rome* in AD 64, though the fiddle did not yet exist and he actually sang and played the lyre.
- He used the cleared land from the Great Fire to construct his massive, opulent golden palace, the *Domus Aurea*.
- He blamed the early Christians for the Great Fire, ordering them to be thrown to the beasts or burned as human torches in his gardens.
- He ordered the assassination of his own mother, *Agrippina the Younger*, after a failed attempt involving a collapsible boat.
- During his reign, the British Celtic queen *Boudica* led a massive but ultimately unsuccessful revolt against Roman rule.
- His dying words were reportedly *Qualis artifex pereo* ("What an artist dies in me!").

## connections

- [[claudius]] — his predecessor and adoptive father, whom Nero's mother allegedly poisoned.
- [[agrippina-the-younger]] — his mother, who schemed to put him on the throne but whom he eventually had murdered to consolidate power.
- [[seneca-the-younger]] — his stoic tutor and early advisor, whom Nero later forced to commit suicide following the Pisonian conspiracy.
- [[vespasian]] — the general he dispatched to crush the First Jewish-Roman War, who would later emerge as emperor after the Year of the Four Emperors.
