# Benedict Arnold

## summary

*Benedict Arnold* was a trusted American general during the Revolutionary War who secretly negotiated with the British to surrender West Point and its strategic position. His attempt to betray the Continental Army in 1780 was thwarted, but his name became synonymous with treason in American history.

## you gotta know

- One of the most capable and aggressive American commanders in the Revolution; helped capture Fort Ticonderoga (1775) and played a crucial role in the victory at Saratoga (1777), which boosted American morale and French support.
- Passed over for promotion and recognition despite his military successes, growing resentful of Congress and the broader Revolutionary cause; financial insolvency worsened his bitterness.
- Secretly negotiated with British spy John André beginning in 1779, eventually agreeing to surrender West Point (the fortress controlling the Hudson River) in exchange for money and a British commission.
- The plot was exposed when André was captured with incriminating documents; André was executed as a spy, but Arnold escaped to British lines and lived the rest of his life in exile in Canada and Britain.
- Fought for the British in the remaining years of the war, but never regained the status or fortune he had hoped for; died in obscurity in 1801.
- His name became a permanent epithet for betrayal in American culture—calling someone "a Benedict Arnold" meant calling them a traitor.
