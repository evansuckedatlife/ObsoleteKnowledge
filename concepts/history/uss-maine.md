# USS Maine

## summary

**USS Maine**, an American armored cruiser that exploded in Havana harbor in February 1898, became the catalyst for the Spanish-American War when American media and officials blamed Spain, rallying the nation with the cry "Remember the Maine!" The ship's destruction, whatever its true cause, launched America into its first foreign war.

## you gotta know

- Sank in Havana harbor on February 15, 1898, with 266 crew members killed in the explosion; initial cause unknown but widely blamed on Spanish sabotage or a Spanish mine.
- The disaster was seized upon by American newspapers, particularly William Randolph Hearst's press, as justification for war against Spain and colonial intervention in Cuba.
- Congress declared war on Spain less than two months after the explosion, and the conflict lasted only ten weeks, resulting in American acquisition of Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines.
- Modern analysis (1976 investigation) suggested the explosion was likely an internal boiler or ammunition magazine failure, not Spanish action, though debate persists among historians.
- Her name and the slogan "Remember the Maine" became synonymous with American jingoism and imperial expansion in the 1890s.
- The wreck was salvaged in 1912 and later sunk in deep water as a memorial.
