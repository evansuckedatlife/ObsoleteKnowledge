# Juan Perón

## summary

**Juan Domingo Perón** (1895–1974) was an Argentine military officer and politician who founded Peronism, a nationalist and populist movement that dominated Argentine politics for decades. He served as president three times and created a distinctive political ideology blending labour advocacy, nationalism, and state intervention.

## you gotta know

- Rose to prominence as Labour Minister under Argentina's military junta (1943–1945), building a coalition with labour unions that became his base of support.
- Elected president in 1946 on a populist platform emphasizing workers' rights, social welfare, and economic nationalism.
- Married actress Eva Perón, who became a beloved public figure and championed his labour policies before her death in 1952.
- Ousted in a military coup in 1955; returned to power in 1973 and served until his death, when his second wife Isabel assumed the presidency.
- Peronism became an enduring political force in Argentina, surviving his death and influencing the nation's politics into the 21st century.
