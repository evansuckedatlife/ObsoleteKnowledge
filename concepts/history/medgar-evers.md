# Medgar Evers

## summary

*Medgar Evers* was a field organizer for the *NAACP* in Mississippi and one of the most effective civil rights leaders of the 1960s, orchestrating campaigns for desegregation and voting rights in one of the most hostile environments in the South. His assassination in 1963 at the hands of a white supremacist made him a martyr to the movement and galvanized national attention on the violence faced by frontline activists.

## you gotta know

- Served as the *NAACP* state field secretary for Mississippi from 1954 onward, making him the face of the organization in the state's most dangerous terrain.
- Led campaigns to desegregate the University of Mississippi in 1962, supporting *James Meredith* through court battles and subsequent enrollment.
- Organized voter registration drives throughout Mississippi, personally recruiting and training volunteers despite severe risks.
- Mentored younger activists and SNCC workers, providing strategic guidance and emotional support through years of constant danger.
- Assassinated on June 12, 1963 outside his home by Byron De La Beckwith, a white supremacist; the murder occurred just after President Kennedy's civil rights address to the nation.
- His death brought Mississippi's racial violence to the center of national attention and accelerated calls for federal intervention in the South.
- His murderer evaded conviction for over 30 years before being retried and convicted in 1994, a delayed justice that became symbolic of the long struggle for accountability.
