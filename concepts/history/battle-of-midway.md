# Battle of Midway

## summary

Fought June 4–7, 1942, the Battle of Midway stands as the turning point of the Pacific War. American naval forces, warned by intelligence of Japanese intentions, ambushed the enemy fleet and sank four irreplaceable fleet carriers while losing only one, permanently shifting naval superiority to the United States and ending Japanese strategic initiative in the Pacific.

## you gotta know

- Centered on Midway Atoll, roughly equidistant between Hawaii and Japan's earlier conquests; the battle lasted four days of intense carrier warfare.
- American victory: sank four Japanese fleet carriers (*Akagi*, *Kaga*, *Soryu*, *Hiryu*) and one heavy cruiser; U.S. lost one fleet carrier (*Yorktown*).
- Japanese forces commanded by Admiral Isoroku Yamamoto; American forces under Admiral Chester Nimitz and tactical commanders including Admiral Frank Jack Fletcher.
- Decided by American codebreakers who decrypted Japanese plans, allowing Nimitz to position his carriers in ambush before the Japanese learned of their location.
- Destroyed the cream of Japanese naval aviation—experienced pilots who were irreplaceable; subsequent Japanese carriers carried less-trained aircrews.
- End of Japanese expansion; thereafter, Japan fought a defensive war of attrition across the Pacific.
