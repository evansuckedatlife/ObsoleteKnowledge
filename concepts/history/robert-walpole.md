# Robert Walpole

## summary

*Robert Walpole* (1676–1745) is recognized as Britain's first Prime Minister, dominating Parliament for over two decades under George I and George II. His long tenure established the principle that the Crown's chief minister must command the confidence of the House of Commons, setting the template for constitutional monarchy that endured for centuries.

## you gotta know

- Served as First Lord of the Treasury for nearly 21 years (1721–1742), the longest tenure of any British Prime Minister.
- Rose to prominence as a Whig during the South Sea Bubble crisis, steering clear of financial disaster that ruined rivals.
- Cultivated George I and George II by accepting the Crown's reduced role in day-to-day governance, allowing the monarch to cede power to Cabinet ministers.
- Mastered the art of parliamentary patronage and corruption, rewarding allies with titles, pensions, and sinecures to maintain a reliable majority.
- Promoted financial stability and avoided costly foreign wars, earning the epithet "the Minister of Peace."
- Resignation in 1742 over the War of Jenkins's Ear marked the beginning of the end of his dominance.
