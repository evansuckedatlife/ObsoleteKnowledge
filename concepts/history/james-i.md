# James I

## summary

James I (r. 1603–1625) was King of England and Ireland, having already ruled Scotland as James VI since 1567. The first Stuart monarch of England, he succeeded Elizabeth I and unified the crowns of England, Scotland, and Ireland. His reign saw the beginning of English colonization of the Americas, but was also marked by theological conflicts and disputes with Parliament.

## you gotta know

- He was the target of the *Gunpowder Plot* in 1605, a Catholic conspiracy led by Robert Catesby and Guy Fawkes to blow up the House of Lords.
- He commissioned the *King James Version* of the Bible, which was published in 1611 and became a cornerstone of English literature and liturgy.
- He was the first monarch to call himself the "King of Great Britain," attempting to politically unify England and Scotland.
- He authored *Daemonologie*, a treatise on witchcraft and demonology, and *Basilikon Doron*, a book of instructions for his son on kingship.
- His reign saw the establishment of *Jamestown*, the first permanent English settlement in the Americas, in 1607.

## connections

- [[elizabeth-i]] — his predecessor, who had executed his mother Mary, Queen of Scots, but named him her successor.
- [[charles-i]] — his son and successor, whose reign would end in the English Civil War.
