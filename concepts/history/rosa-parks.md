# Rosa Parks

## summary

*Rosa Parks* was an African-American civil rights activist whose refusal to surrender her bus seat to a white passenger in Montgomery, Alabama on December 1, 1955 sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott and became a pivotal moment in the Civil Rights Movement. Known as the "Mother of the Civil Rights Movement," her act of quiet defiance initiated the cascade of events that established *Martin Luther King Jr.* as a national leader.

## you gotta know

- Arrested on December 1, 1955 in Montgomery for violating the city's segregation ordinances, despite being seated in the "colored" section of the bus.
- Her trial was the catalyst for the Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955–1956), which lasted 381 days and paralyzed the city's transit system.
- Was not an accidental activist but a seasoned *NAACP* member with prior activism; her act was partly planned as a test case against segregation.
- Suffered harassment and death threats after her arrest, and eventually moved to Detroit seeking safety and new opportunities.
- Authored her autobiography *Rosa Parks: My Story* (1992), which brought her reflections on courage and principle to wider audiences.
- Became an elder stateswoman of the movement; she was celebrated internationally and received the *Presidential Medal of Freedom* in 1996.
