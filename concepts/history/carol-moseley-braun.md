# Carol Moseley Braun

## summary

*Carol Moseley Braun* (1947–) was the first Black woman elected to the United States Senate, representing Illinois from 1993 to 1999. A lawyer and diplomat, she broke two historic barriers simultaneously—gender and race—in one of the upper chamber's most significant electoral breakthroughs.

## you gotta know

- First African American woman U.S. Senator, elected in 1992 in a stunning upset against an incumbent Republican senator.
- Served on the Senate Judiciary Committee during the *Clarence Thomas confirmation hearings*, questioning witnesses on issues of sexual harassment and race.
- Ran for the Democratic presidential nomination in 2004, expanding the visible range of Black female political ambition on a national stage.
- Later served as U.S. Ambassador to *New Zealand* and *Samoa*, representing American diplomacy in the Pacific.
- A former prosecutor and Illinois state legislator, she pioneered a legislative voice centered on civil rights, education, and economic justice.
- Her 1992 election came amid a surge of women's political organizing and backlash against the Clarence Thomas hearings, making her victory a watershed moment.
