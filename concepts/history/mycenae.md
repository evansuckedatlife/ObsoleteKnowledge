# Mycenae

## summary

*Mycenae*, a fortress city in the Peloponnese, was the dominant political and military power of the Late Bronze Age (1600–1100 BCE) and served as the legendary seat of *Agamemnon*. Its imposing *Lion Gate*, massive stone walls, and chamber tombs reveal a highly organized civilization that dominated the eastern Mediterranean before declining mysteriously around 1100 BCE.

## you gotta know

- Ruled by *Agamemnon*, the leader of the Greek forces in the *Trojan War* according to Homer and later tradition, making it the symbolic capital of the Greeks at Troy.
- Featured the famous *Lion Gate*, a monumental entrance with two lions rampant flanking the doorway, a symbol of Mycenaean power and architectural achievement.
- Built with *Cyclopean* masonry—massive, roughly-hewn stone blocks fitted together without mortar—suggesting divine (or superhuman) construction to later Greeks.
- Home to the *Treasury of Atreus* (or Tomb of Agamemnon), a *tholos* tomb of extraordinary engineering, featuring a corbelled vault covering a vast underground chamber.
- Headed the *Mycenaean civilization*, a Bronze Age culture (1600–1100 BCE) that unified the Greek mainland, established trade networks, and influenced the Aegean world.
- Fell around 1100 BCE amid the *Bronze Age Collapse*, a mysterious period of widespread destruction and upheaval across the Mediterranean.
- Rediscovered by archaeologist *Heinrich Schliemann* in the 1870s, who excavated shaft graves and proclaimed he had found the tomb of Agamemnon.
