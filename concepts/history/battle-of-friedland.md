# Battle of Friedland

## summary

The Battle of Friedland on June 14, 1807, was Napoleon's decisive victory over the Russian army under General Levin Bennigsen near the East Prussian town of Friedland. The French destroyed much of the Russian force and forced Russia to seek peace; within weeks, the Treaty of Tilsit ceded Russian Polish territory and brought Russia into alliance with France against Britain.

## you gotta know

- Fought on June 14, 1807, between approximately 73,000 French troops and 60,000 Russian forces near Friedland in East Prussia.
- The Russian army, attempting to cross back over the Neman River, was caught in the act by Napoleon's rapidly advancing columns; Bennigsen was forced into a desperate battle with limited options.
- French artillery and coordinated infantry attacks repeatedly shattered Russian formations; waves of cavalry charges by the French Imperial Guard broke the Russian center repeatedly.
- Russian casualties were approximately 20,000 killed and wounded, plus 14,000 captured; French losses were around 8,000 but operationally decisive.
- The defeated Russian army fell back toward the Polish border; with Prussia already defeated and Britain unable to offer direct support, Tsar Alexander I sued for peace.
- The Treaty of Tilsit, signed two weeks later, forced Russia to cede Poland and the Ionian Islands, to reduce army size, and to secretly agree to anti-British trade measures.
- Friedland brought the Fourth Coalition War to an end and temporarily placed all of continental Europe under French domination or alliance.
