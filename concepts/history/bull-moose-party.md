# Bull Moose Party

## summary

The Bull Moose Party, officially the Progressive Party, was a U.S. political party founded by former President Theodore Roosevelt in 1912 after his rupture with the Republican Party. Running as the party's presidential candidate on a platform of social reform and progressive regulation of business, Roosevelt finished second in the 1912 election, displacing the Republican incumbent William Howard Taft and allowing Democrat Woodrow Wilson to win the presidency.

## you gotta know

- Roosevelt formed the party after losing the Republican nomination to incumbent President Taft, splitting the conservative GOP vote and guaranteeing a Democratic victory.
- The party's symbol was the bull moose—Roosevelt famously claimed to be "as strong as a bull moose"—and it attracted progressive reformers, many labor supporters, and women's suffrage advocates.
- Roosevelt's 1912 campaign platform included workplace safety regulations, child labor restrictions, conservation initiatives, and antitrust enforcement; he received over 27% of the popular vote, the highest third-party showing until Ross Perot in 1992.
- Running on the slogan "A Chicken in Every Pot," the party promised expansive federal intervention in the economy to aid working people.
- Though Roosevelt lost, the Progressive Party's split with the Republicans cemented a realignment: Wilson's victory gave Democrats control, ending a generation of Republican dominance since the Civil War.
- The party largely dissolved after 1912, though it ran a second time in 1916 (again with Roosevelt), winning far fewer votes as Republicans reunified.
