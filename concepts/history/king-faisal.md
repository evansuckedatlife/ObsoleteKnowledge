# King Faisal

## summary

*Faisal ibn Abdulaziz Al Saud* (1906–1975) was the third king of Saudi Arabia and a transformative figure in modern Middle Eastern politics, using the kingdom's vast oil wealth to modernize the nation and project Saudi influence across the Islamic world. His role in the 1973 oil embargo made him a symbol of Arab economic power against Western support for Israel.

## you gotta know

- Became crown prince in 1958 and king in 1964; consolidated authority from his elder half-brother by promising modernization and religious credibility.
- Oversaw Saudi Arabia's transformation into an oil superpower; used petroleum revenues to fund military expansion, infrastructure, and religious institutions.
- Led the oil embargo of 1973–1974 against the United States and other Western nations in retaliation for their support of Israel in the *Yom Kippur War*, demonstrating OPEC's geopolitical clout.
- Promoted *Wahhabism*, the puritanical Islamic doctrine, as Saudi Arabia's state religion; exported it through educational and missionary networks across the Islamic world.
- Championed Arab unity and pan-Islamic solidarity while maintaining strong ties to the United States, a balancing act that defined Saudi foreign policy.
- Expanded the kingdom's military, patronized cultural projects, and advanced women's education (cautiously, within Islamic constraints).
- Assassinated in March 1975 by his nephew, Faisal ibn Musaed, in a palace shooting; the motive remains disputed, but the deed shocked the region.
