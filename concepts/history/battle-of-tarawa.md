# Battle of Tarawa

## summary

Fought November 20–23, 1943, the Battle of Tarawa was the first major amphibious assault by American forces against a heavily fortified Japanese atoll in the Central Pacific. The three-day battle to capture the tiny atoll of Betio resulted in a strategic American victory but demonstrated the devastating cost of island assaults, with over 1,000 American lives lost and nearly all Japanese defenders killed.

## you gotta know

- Battle fought on Betio, a small islet within Tarawa Atoll in the Gilbert Islands; Japanese garrison approximately 4,500 troops under Rear Admiral Keiji Shibasaki.
- American landing force: 35,000 Marines of the 2nd Marine Division under Major General Julian Smith; launched amphibious assault operation *Galvanic*.
- Heavily fortified Japanese position: concrete blockhouses, anti-tank gun emplacements, barbed wire, and mined beaches created killing zones.
- U.S. casualties: 1,009 killed, 2,101 wounded in three days of intense close-quarters combat; Japanese casualties: nearly total destruction of garrison.
- Iconic for the intensity of hand-to-hand fighting amid fortified positions; Marines forced to clear pillboxes methodically under fire.
- Achieved dominance in Central Pacific, opening direct route toward Japan; secured by November 23.
- Shocked American public with casualty photographs; demonstrated that island-hopping would exact a terrible price.
