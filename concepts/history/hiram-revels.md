## summary

*Hiram Rhodes Revels* (1827–1901) was the first African American to serve in the United States Senate, representing Mississippi during Reconstruction. A clergyman and educator, he embodied the political ascent of freed people and Northern Republican leadership in the postwar South.

## you gotta know

- First Black U.S. Senator, elected by the Mississippi state legislature in 1870; served until 1871.
- A *Methodist* clergyman and educator who founded educational institutions for freedmen before entering politics.
- Originally from North Carolina, he was selected by Republican Reconstruction delegates to represent a temporarily reshaped Mississippi electorate.
- Senate Democrats challenged his eligibility (questioning whether he had been a citizen the requisite nine years before election), but Republicans secured his seat.
- His brief single term occurred during the height of *Radical Reconstruction* when Republican-controlled state governments in the South appointed or elected freedmen to office.
- Later became president of *Alcorn University*, a historically Black land-grant college he helped establish.

## connections

- [[reconstruction-era]] — the period of federal oversight and political reorganization when Southern freedmen briefly wielded electoral power.
- [[blanche-bruce]] — his fellow Black Mississippi senator, elected in 1874 and serving a full six-year term.
- [[united-states-senate]] — the chamber he pioneered Black representation in.
- [[mississippi]] — the state whose legislature elected him despite fierce Democratic opposition.
