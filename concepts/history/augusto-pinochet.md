# Augusto Pinochet

## summary

**Augusto Ugarte Pinochet** (1915–2006) was a Chilean military officer who orchestrated a coup against Salvador Allende in 1973 and ruled Chile as dictator for 17 years. His regime was marked by severe human-rights abuses, disappearances, torture, and economic neoliberal reforms that reshaped the nation.

## you gotta know

- Led the military coup on September 11, 1973, that overthrew Salvador Allende's democratically elected socialist government.
- Established the Junta, a military ruling council, and served as its leader; declared himself president in 1974 and ruled until 1990.
- Consolidated power through secret police (DINA) that carried out widespread torture, extrajudicial killings, and forced disappearances of political opponents.
- Implemented neoliberal economic policies, privatizing state industries and replacing Allende's socialism with a free-market model.
- Stepped down in 1990 after losing a plebiscite; remained Commander-in-Chief of the army until 1998.
- Indicted for human-rights crimes in Spain and Chile after leaving office; died in 2006 before facing full prosecution.
