# Arthur Wellesley, Duke of Wellington

## summary

*Arthur Wellesley, Duke of Wellington* (1769–1852) was the paramount British military figure of the Napoleonic Wars, defeating French forces across Europe and finally Napoleon himself at Waterloo. Though his tenure as Prime Minister (1828–1830) was brief and unpopular, his military legacy dominated British national consciousness for decades.

## you gotta know

- Earned the title "The Iron Duke" for his disciplined tactics and relentless pursuit of French armies in the Peninsula War (1808–1814) and at Waterloo (1815).
- Defeated Napoleon at Waterloo in 1815 alongside Prussian allies, ending the Emperor's Hundred Days and cementing Britain's role as the guarantor of European order.
- As PM, reluctantly supported Catholic emancipation in 1829, recognizing that denial risked Irish rebellion; the move alienated his Tory base and hastened his departure.
- Was deeply unpopular as a peacetime PM, seen as an aristocratic relic out of step with reform; his government fell in 1830 over the refusal to extend the franchise.
- Remained a towering figure in British life, repeatedly called upon for public service and revered as the embodiment of martial virtue and national defense.
- Died in 1852 and received a state funeral of unparalleled splendor.
