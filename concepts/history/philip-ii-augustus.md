# Philip II Augustus

## summary

*Philip II* (1165–1223), called Augustus, transformed France from a minor feudal kingdom into the dominant European power. Through shrewd alliances, military victories, and the systematic annexation of Norman and Angevin territories, he tripled the crown's lands and wealth.

## you gotta know

- Reigned 1180–1223; his 43-year reign saw the shift of power from feudal lords to the French crown, establishing a proto-national monarchy.
- Crusaded to the Holy Land with Richard the Lionheart (Third Crusade, 1191) but quarreled with the English king and returned early to seize his lands.
- Defeated John of England at the Battle of Bouvines (1214), the decisive victory that confirmed French supremacy over the English-held territories in France.
- Systematically conquered Normandy, Anjou, Maine, and other lands held by the English crown, reducing English power in France from dominance to a toehold.
- Organized the royal administration, expanding the royal demesne and revenues through careful management of monasteries and conquered lands.
- His victories founded the territorial base that would make France the major European power for centuries.
