# Brandywine

## summary

On *September 11, 1777*, British general William Howe outmaneuvered George Washington's Continental Army near Brandywine Creek in Pennsylvania. Howe sent part of his force to pin the Americans while secretly marching the bulk of his army around their flank to assault them from behind. The Americans were routed and forced to retreat, but the army escaped destruction and Howe's casualties were significant enough that he did not pursue aggressively.

## you gotta know

- Howe's strategy was to divide his force and execute a wide flanking march around Washington's left flank while pinning the center with a frontal attack.
- Washington, receiving conflicting intelligence about the British movements, was slow to respond and failed to secure his left flank adequately.
- American forces retreated in disorder after the flank attack broke their line; total American casualties were approximately 300 killed and over 400 captured.
- British and Hessian losses exceeded 500, making it a costly victory for Howe despite his tactical success.
- The defeat forced Washington to abandon Philadelphia, which Howe occupied a few weeks later.
- Despite the American loss, Washington's army remained intact and operational, and Howe failed to decisively end the war.
