# Saddam Hussein

## summary

*Saddam Hussein* (1937–2003) was the dictator of Iraq from 1979 to 2003, ruling through the Ba'athist Party with authoritarian brutality. His military ambitions sparked two major wars—against Iran and against a U.S.-led coalition—that devastated Iraq and redefined Middle Eastern geopolitics in the late 20th century.

## you gotta know

- Rose through the Ba'athist Party, becoming vice chairman in 1968 and consolidating power over the next decade before formally assuming the presidency in 1979.
- Launched the *Iran-Iraq War* (1980–1988), invading his neighbor over border disputes and regional hegemony; the conflict became a stalemate lasting eight bloody years and killing roughly 1 million.
- Used chemical weapons against Iran and against Kurdish civilians in northern Iraq; represented one of the worst instances of chemical warfare since World War I.
- Annexing Kuwait in 1990, triggering the *Gulf War* (1991), in which a U.S.-led coalition expelled Iraqi forces and humiliated Saddam's military.
- Maintained a brutal secret police and military apparatus; suppressed Shiite and Kurdish minorities through massacres and displacement.
- Remained in power through the 1990s under UN sanctions and weapons inspections, defying international pressure until the 2003 U.S. invasion.
- Captured in December 2003, tried for crimes against humanity, and executed by the Iraqi government in December 2006.
