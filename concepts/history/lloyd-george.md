# David Lloyd George

## summary

*David Lloyd George* (1863–1945) was the last Liberal Prime Minister and a decisive wartime leader who mobilized Britain for total war and then grappled with the peace. His tenure (1916–1922) as PM saw him reshape the state apparatus, challenge the German Empire, and navigate the treacherous postwar settlement—a period of unmatched political turbulence and innovation.

## you gotta know

- Rose from a Welsh background and radical Liberal politics to become PM at 52, breaking the aristocratic mold that had previously dominated the office.
- Reorganized the British government for total war as PM, centralizing procurement, propaganda, and strategy in a small War Cabinet that sidelined traditional ministers.
- Negotiated the Treaty of Versailles (1919) as one of the "Big Three" alongside Woodrow Wilson and Georges Clemenceau, helping to shape the postwar order and the League of Nations.
- Created the Irish Free State in 1922, effectively granting dominion status to most of Ireland after centuries of conflict and partition—a historic reversal of Conservative policy.
- Promoted social reform through Lloyd George's People's Budget of 1909 (as Chancellor), which taxed wealth to fund old-age pensions and National Insurance, presaging the modern welfare state.
- Lost office in 1922 to a Conservative revolt, signaling the Liberal Party's terminal decline and the rise of Labour as the principal opposition to the Tories.
