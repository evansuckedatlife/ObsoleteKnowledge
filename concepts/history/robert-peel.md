# Robert Peel

## summary

*Robert Peel* (1788–1850) was a reforming Conservative who modernized British policing and shattered his own party over free trade. His repeal of the Corn Laws in 1846, made urgent by the Irish Potato Famine, saved lives but split the Tories and secured the principle of free trade that governed Britain into the 20th century.

## you gotta know

- Founded the Metropolitan Police in 1829 as Home Secretary, establishing the modern model of civilian policing; London constables became known as "Bobbies" after him.
- Repealed the Corn Laws in 1846 to alleviate the Irish Potato Famine, breaking with protectionist Tory doctrine and putting humanitarian need above party loyalty.
- His repeal of the Corn Laws destroyed the Conservative Party's cohesion; protectionists split off, and the free-trade faction evolved into the modern Liberal Party.
- Served as PM twice: 1834–1835 and 1841–1846, using both terms to reposition the Tories as a party of progressive order rather than landed aristocracy.
- Articulated a vision of Conservatism grounded in gradual, lawful reform to prevent violent revolution.
- Died following a riding accident in 1850, mourned as a statesman who had put principle before party survival.
