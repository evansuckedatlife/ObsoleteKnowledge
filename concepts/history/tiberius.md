# Tiberius

## summary

Tiberius was the second Roman emperor, ruling from AD 14 to 37. The stepson of Augustus, he was a brilliant and successful general in his youth, securing the empire's northern frontiers. However, his reign was characterised by growing paranoia, treason trials, and his eventual withdrawal to the island of Capri, leaving the ruthless Praetorian Prefect Sejanus in charge of Rome.

## you gotta know

- He retreated from Rome in AD 26 to live in a villa on the island of *Capri*, from which he ruled mostly by correspondence for the rest of his life.
- He left the day-to-day administration of Rome to *Sejanus*, the ambitious commander of the Praetorian Guard, before eventually ordering his execution for treason.
- It was during his reign that *Jesus* of Nazareth was crucified in the province of Judaea by the governor Pontius Pilate.
- He was Augustus's stepson through his mother, Livia, and was only adopted as heir after the deaths of Augustus's preferred choices, Gaius and Lucius Caesar.
- Upon his death, the Roman crowds reportedly rejoiced and cried "To the Tiber with Tiberius!"

## connections

- [[augustus]] — his stepfather and predecessor, who forced him to divorce his beloved wife Vipsania to marry his daughter Julia.
- [[caligula]] — his grand-nephew, adopted grandson, and successor, who spent time with Tiberius on Capri.
- [[jesus]] — the religious figure executed in Judaea during Tiberius's imperial reign.
- [[germanicus]] — his popular adopted son and successful general whose suspicious death cast a long shadow over Tiberius's rule.
